How To Treat Damaged Curly Hair Naturally A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Aug 18, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to maintain its health and vibrancy. Whether you’re dealing with split ends, breakage, or frizz, damaged curly hair can be a real challenge to fix. Fortunately, there are natural remedies that can help you treat and prevent hair damage without resorting to harsh chemicals or expensive salon treatments. In this article, we’ll show you how to treat damaged curly hair naturally, step by step.

How to Treat Damaged Curly Hair Naturally

The first step in treating damaged curly hair naturally is to identify the cause of the damage. Common causes of hair damage include:

  • Excessive heat styling
  • Chemical treatments (such as coloring or relaxing)
  • Poor nutrition
  • Environmental factors (such as sun exposure or pollution)

Once you’ve identified the cause of the damage, you can take steps to address it using natural remedies. Here are some of the best ways to treat damaged curly hair naturally:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

One of the easiest and most effective ways to treat damaged curly hair is to use a deep conditioning treatment. You can make your own deep conditioner at home using natural ingredients like avocado, coconut oil, and honey. Simply apply the treatment to your hair, cover it with a shower cap, and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

2. Apply a Protein Treatment

Protein is essential for healthy hair, and applying a protein treatment can help strengthen and repair damaged strands. You can use ingredients like eggs, yogurt, or even gelatin to create a protein treatment at home. Apply the treatment to your hair, leave it on for 30 minutes, and rinse it out thoroughly.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Excessive heat styling can damage curly hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. To prevent further damage, try to avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays can damage hair just as they can damage skin, so it’s important to protect your hair from the sun. Wear a hat or scarf when you’re outside, or use a hair product that contains UV protection.

5. Eat a Healthy Diet

What you eat can have a big impact on the health of your hair. Make sure you’re getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins A and C in your diet to keep your hair strong and healthy.

Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Damaged Curly Hair Naturally

If you’re ready to start treating your damaged curly hair naturally, follow these steps:

Step 1: Identify the Cause of the Damage

Take a look at your hair care routine and identify any habits that could be causing damage to your hair. Once you know the cause of the damage, you can take steps to address it.

Step 2: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair once a week to help repair and strengthen damaged strands.

Step 3: Apply a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair once a month to help rebuild and strengthen damaged strands.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and when you do use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or scarf when you’re outside, or use a hair product that contains UV protection.

Step 6: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, iron, and vitamins A and C to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining healthy curly hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush
  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair

FAQs About Treating Damaged Curly Hair Naturally

Q: How often should I use a deep conditioning treatment?

A: You should use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help repair and strengthen damaged strands.

Q: Can I use a protein treatment too often?

A: Yes, using a protein treatment too often can actually cause more damage to your hair. Aim to use a protein treatment once a month to help rebuild and strengthen damaged strands.

Q: How can I prevent split ends?

A: To prevent split ends, avoid using heat styling tools and be gentle when combing or brushing your hair. You can also get regular trims to remove any split ends that have already formed.

Curly Hair Care: Best Practices

Here are some best practices for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair
  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz
  • Avoid washing your hair every day
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to help keep your curls moisturized

By following these tips and using natural remedies to treat and prevent hair damage, you can keep your curly hair looking healthy, vibrant, and beautiful.
