Are Curly Hair Tarantulas Docile A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Aug 03, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're considering getting a curly hair tarantula as a pet, you might be wondering about their temperament. Are they docile creatures that can be handled without fear of bites? In this guide, we'll explore the answer to that question and provide tips for caring for your curly hair tarantula.

Description

Curly hair tarantulas, also known as Brachypelma albopilosum, are a species of tarantula native to Central America. They are relatively small, with a leg span of about 5 inches, and are known for their striking curly hair. They are often kept as pets due to their calm nature and ease of care.

How to Care for a Curly Hair Tarantula

Curly hair tarantulas are relatively easy to care for and require minimal maintenance. Here are the basic steps you'll need to follow:

1. Housing

You'll need to provide your tarantula with a tank or enclosure that is at least 3 times the leg span of your spider. A 10-gallon tank is a good size for a curly hair tarantula. Make sure the enclosure is escape-proof and has plenty of hiding spots, such as pieces of bark or fake plants.

2. Substrate

The substrate in your tarantula's enclosure should be at least 2 inches deep and made of a material that can hold moisture, such as coconut fiber or sphagnum moss. You can also add a small water dish for your spider to drink from.

3. Temperature and Humidity

Curly hair tarantulas thrive in temperatures between 70-80°F and require a humidity level of around 60%. You can achieve this by misting the enclosure daily and placing a damp sponge in the water dish.

4. Feeding

Curly hair tarantulas are carnivorous and should be fed a diet of live insects, such as crickets or mealworms. Feed your spider once or twice a week, depending on their appetite.

5. Handling

While curly hair tarantulas are generally docile creatures, they should not be handled unless necessary. If you do need to handle your tarantula, make sure to do so gently and with extreme care. Always supervise children when handling tarantulas.

Step by Step Guide to Caring for a Curly Hair Tarantula

Here is a step by step guide to caring for your curly hair tarantula:

Step 1: Set up your tarantula's enclosure

Make sure the enclosure is escape-proof and has plenty of hiding spots for your spider.

Step 2: Add substrate

Add at least 2 inches of substrate to the enclosure. Choose a material that can hold moisture.

Step 3: Maintain temperature and humidity

Keep the enclosure at a temperature between 70-80°F and maintain a humidity level of around 60%.

Step 4: Feed your tarantula

Feed your spider live insects once or twice a week, depending on their appetite.

Step 5: Handle with care

If you need to handle your tarantula, do so gently and with extreme care. Always supervise children when handling tarantulas.

Tips for Caring for a Curly Hair Tarantula

Here are some additional tips for caring for your curly hair tarantula:

  • Do not house multiple tarantulas together, as they may fight.
  • Do not feed your tarantula wild-caught insects, as they may contain pesticides or other harmful substances.
  • Do not handle your tarantula if they are in the process of molting, as this can be a stressful time for them.
  • Keep your tarantula's enclosure clean and free of debris.

FAQs

Are curly hair tarantulas docile?

Yes, curly hair tarantulas are generally docile creatures that can be handled with care.

Do curly hair tarantulas bite?

While curly hair tarantulas are not aggressive and rarely bite, they do have the ability to do so if they feel threatened.

What should I do if my tarantula bites me?

If you are bitten by a tarantula, seek medical attention immediately. Tarantula bites are usually not serious, but they can cause an allergic reaction in some people.

Hair Care for Curly Hair Tarantulas

Curly hair tarantulas require minimal hair care, as their curly hair is largely for decoration. However, you should avoid handling your spider by their hair, as this can be painful for them. If you do need to remove debris from your tarantula's hair, use a soft brush or a damp cloth.

In conclusion, curly hair tarantulas are generally docile creatures that can make great pets with proper care and handling. Follow the steps outlined in this guide to ensure your tarantula stays healthy and happy.
