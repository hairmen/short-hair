How To Cut Your Own Hair Straight Across The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a great way to save money and get the style you want. In this guide, we’ll go over how to cut your own hair straight across the back, including everything from the tools you’ll need to the steps you should follow. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced haircutter, this guide will help you achieve a professional-looking cut.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You’ll Need
  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Straight Across the Back
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Your Cut

Tools You’ll Need

Before you begin cutting your hair, it’s important to have the right tools on hand. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors or hair cutting shears
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A hair tie or clip

Make sure your scissors are sharp and specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a smooth, even cut.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Straight Across the Back

Follow these steps to cut your own hair straight across the back:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be harder to cut evenly.
  2. Part your hair down the middle and clip or tie one side out of the way.
  3. Comb out any tangles.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and pull it straight up. This will be your guide for the length of the rest of your hair.
  5. Use your scissors to cut the hair straight across at the length you want. Be sure to cut slowly and carefully, checking your progress in the mirror as you go.
  6. Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting them to match the length of your guide section.
  7. Once you’ve finished one side, repeat the process on the other side.
  8. Check the back of your head in the mirror to ensure the length is even on both sides.
  9. Trim any uneven sections until you’re happy with the overall length.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Remember, it’s always better to cut less hair than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more, but you can’t undo a cut that’s too short.

Tips for a Successful Cut

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you cut your own hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly to ensure an even cut.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go.
  • If you’re unsure about cutting your own hair, consider starting with a small trim or consulting a professional stylist.
  • Remember that hair grows back, so don’t be too hard on yourself if your first attempt isn’t perfect.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair straight across the back without a guide?

While it’s possible to cut your hair straight across the back without a guide, it’s not recommended. Using a guide section helps ensure that your hair is cut evenly and at the desired length.

How often should I cut my hair?

The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair’s length and texture, as well as your personal preferences. As a general rule, it’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Your Cut

After you’ve cut your own hair, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a hair mask or treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own hair straight across the back and achieve a professional-looking cut. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and be patient with yourself. Happy cutting!
