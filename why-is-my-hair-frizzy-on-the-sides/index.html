Why Is My Hair Frizzy On The Sides

Written by Robby Jul 27, 2023 · 3 min read

Frizzy hair can be a real problem, especially when it seems to be concentrated on the sides of your head. There are a number of reasons why your hair might be frizzy on the sides, from weather conditions to hair care mistakes. In this article, we’ll explore some of the most common causes of frizz and what you can do to prevent it.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

Frizzy hair on the sides can be caused by a number of factors, including humidity, hair care mistakes, and even genetics. When your hair becomes frizzy, it can be difficult to manage and style, leaving you feeling frustrated and self-conscious. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth and healthy.

How to Prevent Frizzy Hair on the Sides

Step by Step

Here are some steps you can take to prevent frizzy hair on the sides:

  1. Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.
  2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, as sulfates can be harsh on your hair and scalp.
  3. Apply a smoothing serum or oil to your hair before styling to help tame frizz.
  4. Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too frequently, as this can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  5. Avoid brushing your hair too vigorously, as this can create friction and lead to frizz.
  6. Consider getting regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can contribute to frizz.
  7. Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

Tips for Managing Frizzy Hair on the Sides

In addition to preventing frizz, there are also steps you can take to manage it when it does occur:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as this can help prevent breakage and frizz.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel after washing, as this can create friction and lead to frizz.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to help nourish and hydrate your hair, which can help prevent frizz.
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or hairstyles that can create friction and lead to frizz.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase, as these materials can help prevent friction and keep your hair smooth and frizz-free.

FAQ

Q: Can genetics play a role in frizzy hair?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in the texture and thickness of your hair, which can contribute to frizz.

Q: Can weather conditions affect frizz?

A: Yes, humidity can be a major contributor to frizz, as it can cause your hair to absorb moisture and swell.

Q: Can using the wrong hair products contribute to frizz?

A: Yes, using hair products that contain harsh ingredients like sulfates and alcohol can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

Hair Care for Frizzy Hair on the Sides

If you’re struggling with frizzy hair on the sides, it’s important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and hydrated. This may involve using gentle hair care products, avoiding harsh styling tools, and taking steps to manage frizz when it does occur. With the right care and attention, you can keep your hair looking smooth, healthy, and frizz-free.
