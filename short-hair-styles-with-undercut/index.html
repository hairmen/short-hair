Short Hair Styles With Undercut The Ultimate Guide

If you're looking for a bold and edgy hairstyle, you might want to consider getting an undercut. This style features a shaved section of hair underneath longer hair on top, which can create a striking contrast and add dimension to your look. In this guide, we'll explore different short hair styles with undercut, how to achieve the look, step-by-step instructions, tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice.

Short Hair Styles with Undercut

There are many different ways to rock an undercut, and the style can be customized to suit your preferences and face shape. Here are some popular short hair styles with undercut:

1. Pixie Undercut

A pixie cut with an undercut is a trendy and low-maintenance option. The shaved section can be hidden when you wear your hair down or revealed when you pull your hair up into a ponytail or bun. This style works well for oval or heart-shaped faces.

2. Bob Undercut

A bob haircut with an undercut is a fresh and modern take on a classic style. The shaved section can be placed at the nape of the neck, the temple, or the side of the head, depending on your preference. This style works well for round or square-shaped faces.

3. Mohawk Undercut

A mohawk with an undercut is a bold and daring style that can make a statement. The shaved section can be wider or narrower, depending on your preference. This style works well for any face shape.

How to Achieve the Look

If you're interested in getting an undercut, you'll need to visit a hair salon or barber. Here's what to expect:

1. Consultation

Your stylist will discuss your desired style and placement of the undercut. They will also assess your hair type, texture, and thickness to determine the best approach.

2. Sectioning

Your stylist will section off the hair on top of your head and clip it out of the way. They will then use clippers to shave the undercut section to your desired length.

3. Styling

Your stylist will style the longer hair on top of your head as desired, using products and tools to create volume, texture, or sleekness.

Step-by-Step Instructions

If you're interested in trying to do an undercut at home, here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Gather Supplies

You will need clippers, a comb, hair scissors, and a mirror.

2. Section Hair

Use a comb to section off the hair on top of your head and clip it out of the way.

3. Trim Sides

Use clippers to shave the sides and back of your head to your desired length.

4. Blend Edges

Use hair scissors to blend the edges of the shaved section with the longer hair on top, creating a seamless transition.

5. Style Hair

Style the longer hair on top of your head as desired, using products and tools to achieve your desired look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Undercut

Here are some tips for keeping your undercut looking fresh and stylish:

  • Visit a hair salon or barber every 4-6 weeks for touch-ups.
  • Use hair products that are appropriate for your hair type and style.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques to find what works best for you.
  • Consider adding color or highlights to enhance the dimension of your undercut.

FAQ

1. Will an undercut work for me?

An undercut can work for anyone, regardless of hair type, texture, or color. Your stylist can help you determine the best placement and length for your undercut based on your face shape and personal style.

2. How long does an undercut last?

An undercut can last for several weeks to several months, depending on how fast your hair grows and how often you get touch-ups.

3. Can I still wear my hair up with an undercut?

Yes, you can still wear your hair up with an undercut. The shaved section will be visible, but this can add an edgy and unique touch to your updo.

Hair Care Advice

To keep your hair healthy and strong, it's important to follow a consistent hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing hats or scarves.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins to promote hair growth and health.

Now that you have all the information you need about short hair styles with undercut, are you ready to try this bold and edgy look for yourself? Whether you go for a pixie, bob, or mohawk, an undercut can add a unique touch to your hairstyle and make you stand out from the crowd.
