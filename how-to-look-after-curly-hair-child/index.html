How To Look After Your Curly Hair Child A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jul 08, 2023 · 3 min read

Curly hair can be beautiful, but it requires a little extra care compared to other hair types. If you're a parent of a curly-haired child, you might have some questions about how to best take care of their hair. In this guide, we'll cover everything from how to detangle and style curly hair to common mistakes to avoid.

Table of Contents

  • How to Look After Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Curly Hair Care
  • Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair
  • FAQs about Curly Hair Care

How to Look After Curly Hair

Curly hair is prone to dryness and tangling, which means it needs a little extra attention. Here are some general tips for looking after curly hair:

  • Wash curly hair no more than once a week.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Detangle hair gently while wet using a wide-toothed comb or your fingers.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flatirons and blow dryers, as they can damage curly hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help keep hair moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide to Curly Hair Care

Here's a step-by-step guide to looking after your curly hair child:

  1. Wet hair thoroughly.
  2. Apply a sulfate-free shampoo and massage gently into the scalp.
  3. Rinse thoroughly.
  4. Apply a sulfate-free conditioner to the lengths of the hair, avoiding the scalp.
  5. Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to gently detangle the hair while the conditioner is still in.
  6. Rinse thoroughly.
  7. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to damp hair.
  8. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry hair on a low heat setting, or air-dry naturally.
  9. Once hair is dry, use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to gently separate and style curls.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your child's curly hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid hair breakage and frizz.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause pulling and breakage, such as tight ponytails and braids.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a month to help keep hair moisturized.
  • Protect hair from the sun by using a hat or head covering.

FAQs about Curly Hair Care

Here are some common questions parents have about looking after their child's curly hair:

How often should I wash my child's curly hair?

Curly hair should be washed no more than once a week to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.

What type of shampoo and conditioner should I use on curly hair?

Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.

Can I use heat tools on my child's curly hair?

Avoid using heat tools like flatirons and blow dryers, as they can damage curly hair.
