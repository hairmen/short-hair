Will My Hair Stop Falling Out After Bleaching

Written by Robby Oct 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Bleaching your hair is a popular trend among many people these days. However, it can cause some serious damage to your hair, including hair fall. If you have recently bleached your hair and are wondering whether your hair will stop falling out, then this article is for you. In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about hair fall after bleaching and some tips on how to prevent it.

Description

Bleaching your hair involves removing the natural color from your hair shafts. This process can cause your hair to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. As a result, you may experience hair fall after bleaching.

How to

If you are experiencing hair fall after bleaching, there are a few things you can do to prevent it. Here are some tips to help you out:

Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

After bleaching your hair, it is important to use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair. You can use a store-bought treatment or make your own at home using natural ingredients.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons can further damage your already weakened hair, causing it to fall out. Therefore, it is best to avoid heat styling as much as possible.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

After washing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will prevent unnecessary breakage and hair fall.

Step by Step

If you have already experienced hair fall after bleaching, here are some steps you can take to prevent further damage:

Step 1: Trim Your Ends

Bleaching can cause split ends, which can lead to further hair fall. Therefore, it is important to trim your ends regularly to prevent further damage.

Step 2: Use a Hair Serum

Hair serums are a great way to add moisture to your hair and prevent further damage. Look for serums that contain natural ingredients like argan oil or coconut oil.

Step 3: Avoid Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments like perms and relaxers can further damage your hair, causing it to fall out. Therefore, it is best to avoid such treatments until your hair has fully recovered from the bleaching process.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair fall after bleaching:

Tip 1: Avoid Tying Your Hair Too Tight

Tying your hair up too tight can cause tension on your hair shafts, leading to breakage and hair fall. Therefore, it is best to avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids.

Tip 2: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Therefore, it is important to eat a balanced diet that includes fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

Tip 3: Limit Your Use of Chemical Products

Chemical products like hair dyes and styling products can further damage your hair, causing it to fall out. Therefore, it is best to limit your use of such products as much as possible.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair fall after bleaching:

Q: Will my hair stop falling out after bleaching?

A: Yes, your hair will eventually stop falling out after bleaching. However, it may take some time for your hair to fully recover from the damage.

Q: Can I dye my hair after bleaching?

A: It is best to wait at least a few weeks after bleaching before dyeing your hair. This will give your hair enough time to recover from the damage.

Q: Can I prevent hair fall after bleaching?

A: Yes, you can prevent hair fall after bleaching by following the tips mentioned in this article, such as using a deep conditioning treatment and avoiding heat styling.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential, especially after bleaching. Here are some additional tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly can help remove dirt and oil buildup, which can weigh down your hair and make it look dull. However, be careful not to overwash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.

Tip 2: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent hair breakage and reduce frizz. Silk is a gentle material that does not tug on your hair like cotton does, causing less damage.

Tip 3: Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth. It is recommended to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

In conclusion, hair fall after bleaching is a common problem, but it can be prevented. By following the tips mentioned in this article and taking care of your hair, you can prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth.
