Do Bangs Work With Curly Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 13, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have curly hair, you may have wondered whether bangs would work for you. Bangs can be a great way to change up your look and add some dimension to your hairstyle, but they can also be tricky to style, especially with curly hair. In this article, we'll explore whether bangs work with curly hair and give you some tips on how to make them work for you.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Bangs can work with curly hair, but it's important to consider a few factors before taking the plunge. The type of curl you have, the shape of your face, and your personal style all play a role in whether bangs will work for you.

If you have tight, coiled curls, bangs may be difficult to style and may end up looking frizzy or messy. On the other hand, if you have looser curls or waves, bangs can be a great way to add some volume and texture to your hair.

When it comes to face shape, bangs can work with just about any shape, but the key is to choose a style that complements your features. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to choose side-swept bangs that help elongate your face, while if you have a square face, you may want to choose longer, wispy bangs that soften your features.

Finally, when it comes to personal style, you'll want to consider whether bangs fit with your overall look. Bangs can be edgy and modern, or they can be soft and romantic, so it's important to choose a style that matches your personality and fashion sense.

How to

If you've decided to give bangs a try with your curly hair, here are some tips on how to make them work for you:

1. Choose the Right Type of Bangs

As we mentioned earlier, the type of bangs you choose will depend on your face shape and personal style. If you're not sure what type of bangs will work for you, ask your stylist for recommendations.

2. Consider Your Curl Pattern

When it comes to curly hair, not all bangs are created equal. If you have tight, coiled curls, you may want to consider longer, side-swept bangs that blend in with the rest of your hair. If you have looser curls or waves, you can experiment with shorter, blunt bangs for a more dramatic look.

3. Use the Right Products

Curly hair requires extra moisture and care, and the same goes for bangs. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated, and use a curl-defining cream or gel to help define your curls and keep your bangs in place.

4. Style Your Bangs When Wet

The best time to style your curly bangs is when they're wet. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and then apply your styling products. Use your fingers to shape your bangs, and then let them air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry them gently.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style curly bangs:

1. Wet Your Hair

Start by wetting your hair in the shower or sink. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and then rinse your hair thoroughly.

2. Apply Your Styling Products

Apply a curl-defining cream or gel to your hair, focusing on your bangs. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly.

3. Shape Your Bangs

Use your fingers to shape your bangs, pulling them down and to the side for side-swept bangs or straight down for blunt bangs.

4. Let Your Hair Air Dry or Use a Diffuser

Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair gently. Avoid using a regular hair dryer on high heat, as this can cause frizz and damage to your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for styling bangs with curly hair:

1. Keep Your Hair Hydrated

Curly hair requires extra moisture, so be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

2. Trim Your Bangs Regularly

To keep your bangs looking their best, be sure to trim them regularly. You can do this yourself or ask your stylist to do it for you.

3. Experiment with Different Styles

Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and lengths of bangs to find the look that works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about styling bangs with curly hair:

Q: Will bangs make my hair look shorter?

A: Bangs can make your hair look shorter, but this depends on the length of your overall hairstyle. If you're concerned about your hair looking too short, consider choosing longer bangs that blend in with the rest of your hair.

Q: Can I straighten my curly bangs?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly bangs with a flat iron, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to avoid damage to your hair.

Q: Can I style my curly bangs with a blow dryer?

A: Yes, you can style your curly bangs with a blow dryer, but use a diffuser attachment on low heat to avoid frizz and damage to your hair.

Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for caring for your curly hair:

1. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, so use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair, so avoid using flat irons or curling irons on a regular basis. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.

So there you have it - bangs can work with curly hair, as long as you choose the right style and take care of your hair properly. With the tips and advice in this article, you'll be well on your way to rocking a fabulous new look!
