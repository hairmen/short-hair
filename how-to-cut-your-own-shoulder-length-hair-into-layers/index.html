How To Cut Your Own Shoulder Length Hair Into Layers

Written by Daniel Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new haircut, but don't want to spend money at the salon, cutting your own hair is a great option. Cutting your own hair may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a breeze. In this article, we'll walk you through how to cut your own shoulder length hair into layers.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Own Shoulder Length Hair into Layers
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs

Description

Layered hair is a popular hairstyle that can add volume and dimension to your hair. Layers can be added to any length of hair, but shoulder length hair is a great length to start with if you're new to cutting your own hair. By cutting your own hair into layers, you can save money and have the freedom to experiment with different styles.

How to Cut Your Own Shoulder Length Hair into Layers

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few essential tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Handheld mirror or full-length mirror

Make sure that you have a clear workspace with good lighting. It's also a good idea to wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own shoulder length hair into layers:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair and remove any tangles.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use hair ties or clips to separate your hair into two sections: top and bottom. The top section should start at the top of your ears and go straight across the back of your head.
  3. Begin cutting the bottom section. Start by cutting the hair from the back of your head. Use your fingers to gather a small section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the tips and working your way up. Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom section.
  4. Move on to the top section. Start at the crown of your head and work your way down. Use the same technique as before, cutting at an angle and working your way up.
  5. Blend the layers. Comb your hair and blend the top and bottom sections together by cutting any uneven pieces.
  6. Check your work. Use a handheld mirror or a full-length mirror to check the back of your head.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to control and less likely to make a mistake.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Go slow and be patient. Cutting your own hair takes time and practice.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair:

Can I cut my hair into layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can still cut your hair into layers if it's curly. Just make sure that you don't stretch your hair too much when cutting it.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim the hair again or go to a salon to have it fixed.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and style. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

By following these tips and techniques, you can cut your own shoulder length hair into layers and achieve a fresh new look without spending money at the salon.
