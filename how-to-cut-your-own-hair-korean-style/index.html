How To Cut Your Own Hair Korean Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 12, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon and spending a fortune on haircuts? Why not try cutting your own hair Korean style? This trend has been gaining popularity in recent years, and for good reason. Not only is it cost-effective, but it also allows you to customize your haircut according to your face shape and personal style. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair Korean style in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. Let's get started!

How to Cut Your Own Hair Korean Style

Korean style haircuts are known for their soft and natural look. They usually feature layers, side-swept bangs, and a slight V-shape at the back. To achieve this look, you'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips. It's also recommended to cut your hair when it's dry and styled as you normally would, so you can see the actual length and texture of your hair.

Related Keywords: Korean Haircut, DIY Haircut, Korean Style Haircut

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair Korean style:

1. Section your hair

Start by dividing your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

2. Cut the back

Take one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at a slight angle to create a V-shape. Repeat on the other side.

3. Cut the sides

Release one of the front sections and comb it forward. Use the back section as a guide and cut the hair at a slight angle towards the back. Repeat on the other side.

4. Add layers

If you want to add layers, take a small section of hair from the top of your head and comb it straight up. Hold it between your fingers and cut it at a slight angle. Continue doing this for the entire top section of your hair.

5. Trim the bangs

Finally, trim your bangs to your desired length. Korean style bangs are usually side-swept and end just above the eyebrows.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair Korean style:

  • Start with small cuts and trim gradually, as it's easier to cut more hair than to add it back.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head periodically.
  • Don't cut too much off at once, especially if you're not experienced in cutting hair.
  • Use hair clips to keep sections of hair separated and out of the way.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair requires some practice and patience.

FAQs

1. Can I cut my hair Korean style if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your hair Korean style if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that the final result may look slightly different due to the texture of your hair. It's best to consult with a professional stylist if you're not sure how to approach your curly hair.

2. Can I cut my hair Korean style if I have short hair?

Yes, you can still cut your hair Korean style even if you have short hair. However, the final result may look slightly different due to the length of your hair. It's best to adjust the angle of the cut and the length of the layers accordingly.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips for post-haircut care:

  • Use a nourishing hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Trim your hair every three to six months to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.
  • Use a silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and frizz while you sleep.

With these tips and steps, you can now confidently cut your own hair Korean style at home. Remember to be patient, take your time, and practice regularly. Who knows, you might just discover a new talent for hairstyling!
