How To Cut Your Own Hair In Layers In The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to give your hair a fresh new look without breaking the bank, cutting your own hair in layers in the back can be a great option. While it may seem daunting at first, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your hair in layers, as well as provide tips and answers to some frequently asked questions.

  3. Sectioning Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • Handheld mirror for the back of your head

It's important to use sharp hair cutting scissors, as dull scissors can lead to split ends and an uneven cut.

Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Brush out any tangles or knots, and make sure your hair is free of any styling products.

Next, decide on the length you want your layers to be. It's a good idea to start with a longer length, as you can always go back and cut more if needed.

Sectioning Your Hair

Once your hair is dry and tangle-free, use a comb to create a center part. Then, use the comb to section your hair into two equal parts, one on each side of your head. Clip or tie each section to keep them separate.

Starting with one section, use the comb to create a horizontal parting about an inch above the nape of your neck. Clip or tie the rest of the hair out of the way. This will be your first layer.

Cutting Your Hair

Take the first layer of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers at the length you want to cut it. Using the sharp hair cutting scissors, make a straight cut across the hair. Repeat this process for the rest of the layer.

Once you've finished the first layer, let down the next section of hair and repeat the process. Make sure to match the length of the second layer to the first layer.

Continue cutting and letting down sections of hair until you reach the top layer of your hair.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting your hair in layers, remove any clips or ties and comb through your hair to make sure it's evenly cut. Use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with a longer length and cut in small increments to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid split ends and an uneven cut.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're struggling to cut the back of your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair in layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your hair in layers if it's curly. However, keep in mind that curly hair can be more difficult to cut evenly. It's a good idea to cut your hair when it's dry and in its natural state to avoid any surprises when your hair dries.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. However, if you're cutting your own hair, you can adjust the frequency to suit your needs.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may need to seek professional help to fix it. However, small mistakes can often be fixed by trimming the surrounding hair to match.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair in layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and maintain your new cut. This includes:

  • Using a quality shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Trimming your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair

Cutting your own hair in layers in the back can be a fun and cost-effective way to switch up your look. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a beautiful, layered cut from the comfort of your own home.
