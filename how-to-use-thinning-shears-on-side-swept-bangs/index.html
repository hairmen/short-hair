How To Use Thinning Shears On Side Swept Bangs

Written by Bardi Jun 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Side swept bangs can be a great addition to any hairstyle, but they can also be a bit of a hassle to maintain. If you're looking for an easy way to thin out your bangs without sacrificing length, thinning shears might be just what you need. In this article, we'll go over how to use thinning shears on side swept bangs, step by step.

How-To

Before you begin, you'll need a few things:

  • Thinning Shears
  • Regular Shears
  • A Comb
  • Hair Clips

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you get a better idea of how your bangs fall naturally, and will make it easier to see where you need to thin them out.

Step 2: Section Off Your Bangs

Use your comb to section off your bangs from the rest of your hair. Clip the rest of your hair back so it's out of the way.

Step 3: Cut a Guide

Use your regular shears to cut a guide for your bangs. This is the length you want your bangs to be. Make sure to cut them straight across, and not angled.

Step 4: Cut with Thinning Shears

Once you have your guide, use your thinning shears to start thinning out your bangs. Start at the bottom and work your way up, making sure to only cut a little bit at a time.

Step 5: Blend

Use your regular shears to blend the thinned out sections with the rest of your bangs. This will help create a more natural look.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you're finished, check your work in a mirror. If you need to thin out any more sections, repeat the process until you're happy with the results.

Tips

  • Only thin out small sections at a time. It's easier to take more off than it is to put it back on.
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair. This can cause the hair to clump together and create an uneven cut.
  • Always use sharp shears. Dull shears can damage your hair and create split ends.

FAQ

What are thinning shears?

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade. They're used to thin out hair without changing the length.

Can I use thinning shears on curly hair?

Yes, but be careful. Thinning shears can create frizz and uneven cuts in curly hair. It's best to use them on straight or wavy hair.

Hair Care

To keep your side swept bangs looking their best, make sure to trim them every 4-6 weeks. Use a heat protectant when styling with hot tools, and avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair.

With these tips and steps, you should be able to use thinning shears on your side swept bangs with ease. Remember to always take your time and only cut a little bit at a time. Happy styling!
