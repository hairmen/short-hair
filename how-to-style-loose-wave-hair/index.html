How To Style Loose Wave Hair From Beachy To Glamorous

Written by Frank Aug 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of the same old straight or curly look? Loose waves are the perfect style to switch things up. Whether you're going for a beachy, boho look or a more polished and glamorous vibe, loose waves are versatile and easy to style. In this article, we'll give you step-by-step instructions on how to style loose wave hair, as well as tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to keep your waves looking their best.

How to Style Loose Wave Hair

Before you start styling, it's important to have the right tools and products on hand. You'll need a curling iron or wand, heat protectant spray, hair texturizer or sea salt spray, and a flexible hold hairspray. Once you have your supplies, you're ready to get started.

Option 1: Beachy Waves

For a beachy, casual look, follow these steps:

  1. Apply heat protectant spray to your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections and curl each section with a curling wand or iron. Hold the wand horizontally and wrap the hair around the barrel, leaving the ends out. Alternate the direction of the curls for a more natural look.
  3. Once you've curled all sections, spray a texturizing or sea salt spray all over your hair and scrunch it with your fingers.
  4. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep the waves in place.

Pro tip: For even more texture, braid your hair before curling and leave the braids in until your hair has cooled down.

Option 2: Polished Waves

If you want a more polished, glamorous look, follow these steps:

  1. Apply heat protectant spray to your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections and curl each section with a curling wand or iron. Hold the wand vertically and wrap the hair around the barrel, starting at the root and leaving the ends out.
  3. Once you've curled all sections, brush through your hair with a paddle brush to soften the waves.
  4. Finish with a flexible hold hairspray to keep the waves in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

To make it even easier, here's a step-by-step guide to styling loose wave hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with products that are formulated for your hair type.
  2. Towel-dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray.
  3. Blow dry your hair until it's about 80% dry.
  4. Divide your hair into sections and curl each section with a curling wand or iron, following the steps for either beachy or polished waves.
  5. If desired, apply a texturizing or sea salt spray to add texture and definition to your waves.
  6. Finish with a flexible hold hairspray to keep your waves in place.

Tips for Styling Loose Wave Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you perfect your loose wave style:

  • Start with clean, dry hair for best results.
  • Use a curling wand or iron with a barrel size that matches the length of your hair. For shorter hair, use a smaller barrel; for longer hair, use a larger barrel.
  • Alternate the direction of your curls for a more natural look.
  • If your waves are too tight, use a paddle brush to brush them out and soften them.
  • If your waves are too loose, use a smaller curling wand or iron and hold the curls for a few extra seconds.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling loose wave hair:

How do I keep my waves from falling out?

Use a flexible hold hairspray to keep your waves in place without making them stiff or crunchy. You can also try using a texturizing or sea salt spray to add grip and hold to your hair.

Can I style my hair in loose waves without heat?

Yes! You can create loose waves without heat by braiding your hair while it's damp and leaving the braids in until your hair is dry. When you take out the braids, you'll have natural-looking waves. You can also try using foam rollers or a headband to create waves without heat.

Hair Care for Loose Wave Hair

To keep your loose waves looking their best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle. Instead, aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair every day, as this can cause damage and breakage. Instead, try to limit heat styling to once or twice a week.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner before swimming.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style your loose wave hair with ease and confidence. Whether you're going for a casual beachy vibe or a more polished and glamorous look, loose waves are the perfect style to switch things up. So go ahead and give it a try!
