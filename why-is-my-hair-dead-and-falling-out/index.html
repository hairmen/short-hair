Why Is My Hair Dead And Falling Out

What is Hair?

Hair is a protein filament that grows from follicles found in the skin. It is made up of three layers – the medulla, cortex, and cuticle. The medulla is the innermost layer, the cortex is the middle layer, and the cuticle is the outermost layer. The cuticle is responsible for protecting the cortex and medulla from damage.

Why is Hair Dead and Falling Out?

Hair is technically dead once it grows out of the follicle. It has no nerves, blood vessels, or muscles. However, the hair follicle is alive and responsible for hair growth. Hair loss occurs when the hair follicle shrinks over time, eventually leading to hair that is shorter, thinner, and falls out more easily.

There are many reasons why hair may be dead and falling out, including:

  • Genetics
  • Hormonal changes
  • Stress
  • Poor nutrition
  • Chemical treatments
  • Heat styling
  • Medical conditions or medications

How to Prevent Hair Loss?

While some causes of hair loss are out of our control, there are steps we can take to prevent or slow down hair loss:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair
  • Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments
  • Reduce stress through exercise or relaxation techniques
  • Use gentle hair products and avoid overwashing
  • Consider using hair growth products or supplements

Hair Care Tips

Proper hair care is key to maintaining healthy hair and preventing hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Don't overwash or use hot water
  • Avoid brushing wet hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or use a heat protectant
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather

FAQ

Is hair loss normal?

It is normal to lose up to 100 hairs a day. However, if you are experiencing excessive hair loss or bald patches, it may be a sign of a medical condition.

Can hair loss be reversed?

Depending on the cause of hair loss, it may be reversible. However, some types of hair loss may be permanent.

What are some medical conditions that can cause hair loss?

Medical conditions that can cause hair loss include thyroid problems, alopecia areata, and lupus.

What are some medications that can cause hair loss?

Medications that can cause hair loss include chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and antidepressants.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss. Telogen effluvium is a type of hair loss caused by stress that usually resolves on its own.

By following proper hair care and seeking medical advice if necessary, we can prevent and slow down hair loss. Remember, healthy hair starts from the inside out.
