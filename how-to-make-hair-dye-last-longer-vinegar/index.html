How To Make Hair Dye Last Longer With Vinegar Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your hair dye fading faster than you'd like? If you've tried everything from color-safe shampoo to avoiding hot water, there's one natural ingredient that could make all the difference - vinegar. In this article, we'll show you how to make hair dye last longer with vinegar, and answer some frequently asked questions about hair care.

What is Vinegar?

Vinegar is a sour-tasting liquid that's made by fermenting alcohol or cider. It's a common ingredient in cooking, cleaning, and even hair care. Apple cider vinegar, in particular, is known for its health and beauty benefits.

How Does Vinegar Help Hair Dye Last Longer?

Vinegar has a low pH level, which can help seal the hair cuticle and lock in color. It also helps remove buildup from styling products and hard water, which can strip hair of color over time. Plus, vinegar is a natural conditioner that can leave your hair soft and shiny.

How to Make Hair Dye Last Longer with Vinegar: Step by Step

Now that you know the benefits of vinegar, here's how to use it to make your hair dye last longer:

Step 1: Shampoo Your Hair

Before using vinegar, shampoo your hair with a color-safe formula. This will help remove any dirt, oil, or buildup that could interfere with the vinegar treatment. Rinse thoroughly and gently towel-dry your hair.

Step 2: Mix Vinegar and Water

In a bowl or spray bottle, mix equal parts water and vinegar. If you have dry or sensitive hair, you can use more water and less vinegar. If you have oily or thick hair, you can use more vinegar and less water. You can also add a few drops of essential oil for a pleasant scent.

Step 3: Apply the Vinegar Solution

Apply the vinegar solution to your hair, making sure to saturate every strand from roots to ends. You can use a spray bottle or a bowl and brush. Massage the solution into your scalp and comb through your hair with a wide-toothed comb to distribute it evenly.

Step 4: Leave the Solution on Your Hair

Leave the vinegar solution on your hair for 5-10 minutes, depending on your hair type and the intensity of your dye. You can cover your hair with a shower cap or towel to keep the solution from dripping. Avoid getting the solution in your eyes or mouth.

Step 5: Rinse Your Hair with Cool Water

Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water, until the water runs clear. Avoid using hot water, which can open the hair cuticle and cause color to fade. You can follow up with a conditioner, if desired.

Tips for Making Hair Dye Last Longer

In addition to using vinegar, here are some tips for making your hair dye last longer:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid hot water and steamy showers
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Touch up your roots as needed

FAQ: Hair Care and Dyeing

How Often Should I Wash My Dyed Hair?

It's best to wash your dyed hair no more than twice a week, to avoid stripping the color. You can use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair fresh and voluminous.

What Should I Do If My Hair Dye Fades Too Fast?

If your hair dye fades too fast, you can try using a hair gloss or toner to refresh the color. You can also mix a little bit of hair dye with your conditioner, to deposit color as you condition your hair.

Is It Safe to Dye Your Hair During Pregnancy?

It's generally safe to dye your hair during pregnancy, as long as you take some precautions. You should avoid using bleach or ammonia-based dyes, as they can be harsh on your scalp and lungs. You should also dye your hair in a well-ventilated area and avoid leaving the dye on your scalp for too long.

Hair Care: Final Thoughts

Whether you're a natural blonde, a fiery redhead, or a bold brunette, taking care of your hair is essential to looking and feeling your best. By using vinegar to make your hair dye last longer, you can save time and money on touch-ups, and enjoy vibrant, healthy-looking hair.
