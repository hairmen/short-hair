How To Layer Thick Medium Length Hair A Guide For Relaxed Styling

Written by Frank Aug 18, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Layering thick medium length hair can be a great way to add movement, dimension, and texture to your locks. Whether you're looking to add volume to fine hair or reduce weight and bulk in thick hair, layering can be a versatile solution for many hair types. In this guide, we'll share step-by-step instructions on how to layer thick medium length hair, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you achieve your desired look.

  • How to Layer Thick Medium Length Hair
  • Step-by-Step Instructions

  • FAQs About Layering Thick Medium Length Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

How to Layer Thick Medium Length Hair

Before you start layering your hair, it's important to decide on the length and shape of your layers. You can choose to create long layers that blend in with your hair's length, or shorter layers that add more volume and movement. You can also opt for angled or choppy layers for a more edgy look. Once you've decided on the style you want, follow these general steps to layer your hair:

1. Wash and dry your hair:

Start with clean, dry hair that's free of any tangles or knots. You can use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils that may weigh your hair down.

2. Divide your hair into sections:

Use hair clips to separate your hair into sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up. This will help you keep track of which sections you've already cut and ensure that your layers are even.

3. Start cutting:

Using sharp scissors, start cutting small sections of hair at a time. Hold each section between your fingers and cut straight across to create a blunt line. You can then use a razor or thinning shears to add texture and reduce weight to your hair.

4. Repeat on each section:

Move up to the next section of hair and repeat the cutting process until you've layered all of your hair. Be sure to cut each section to the same length and shape to achieve a uniform look.

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Follow these detailed steps to layer your thick medium length hair:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils that may weigh your hair down. Rinse thoroughly and towel dry your hair until it's damp but not soaking wet.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use hair clips to separate your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Clip the top sections away from the bottom sections to keep them out of the way while you layer your hair.

Step 3: Start cutting

Take a small section of hair from the bottom section and hold it between your fingers. Cut straight across to create a blunt line. Repeat this process on each section of hair, working your way up from the bottom to the top.

Step 4: Check the length and shape

After you've cut all of your hair, check the length and shape of your layers in the mirror. Make any adjustments as needed to ensure that your layers are even and uniform.

Tips for Layering Thick Medium Length Hair:

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results when layering your hair:

1. Use sharp scissors:

Sharp scissors will give you a clean, precise cut and prevent split ends. Avoid using dull or rusty scissors, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.

2. Take your time:

Layering your hair can take time and patience, especially if you're doing it yourself. Don't rush the process, and take breaks if you need to. It's better to take your time and get it right than to rush and end up with uneven layers.

3. Start with small sections:

Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections. This will help you maintain control and ensure that your layers are even.

4. Consider professional help:

If you're unsure about layering your hair yourself, consider going to a professional stylist. They can help you achieve the look you want and ensure that your layers are even and uniform.

FAQs About Layering Thick Medium Length Hair:

Q: Will layering my hair make it look thinner?

A: Layering can actually make your hair look thicker and more voluminous, especially if you have fine or thin hair. Layers can add dimension and movement to your hair, making it look fuller and more textured.

Q: How often do I need to layer my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. For most people, layering every 6-8 weeks is sufficient to maintain the shape and style of their layers. However, if your hair grows quickly or you want to change your style, you may need to layer your hair more often.

Q: Can I layer my hair if it's already damaged or thinning?

A: Layering can be a good option for damaged or thinning hair, as it can help add volume and texture. However, if your hair is severely damaged or thinning, it's best to consult with a professional stylist to determine the best course of action.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair:

Here are some tips to help you care for your layered hair:

1. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner:

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type and concerns. Look for products that contain nourishing ingredients like keratin, biotin, and argan oil to help strengthen and protect your hair.

2. Avoid heat styling:

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can damage your hair and cause breakage. Try to limit your use of these tools, or use a heat protectant spray before styling to help minimize damage.

3. Trim your hair regularly:

To maintain the shape and style of your layers, it's important to trim your hair regularly. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as needed to keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb:

When combing or brushing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to help minimize tangles and breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

5. Protect your hair from the sun:

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. To protect your hair, wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray when spending time outdoors.

With these tips and instructions, you can confidently layer your thick medium length hair and achieve a stylish, effortless look. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and care for your hair properly to maintain healthy, vibrant locks.
