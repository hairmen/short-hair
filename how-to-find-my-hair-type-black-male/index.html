How To Find Your Hair Type As A Black Male

Written by William Sep 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Understanding your hair type is crucial when it comes to properly caring for your hair. Whether you have curly, wavy, or straight hair, knowing your hair type will help you choose the right products and styling techniques that work best for you. However, finding your hair type as a black male can be a bit challenging, especially if you have relaxed hair. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of determining your hair type and provide tips on how to take care of your hair based on your specific hair type.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Hair Type
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Determine Your Hair Type

There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Within each hair type, there are different subtypes that can also affect how your hair behaves.

When it comes to identifying your hair type, there are a few things you should look for:

Texture

Texture refers to the thickness or diameter of each individual strand of hair. To determine your hair texture, take a single strand of hair between your fingers and rub it up and down. If you can barely feel the strand, you have fine hair. If you can feel the strand but it's not too thick, you have medium hair. If the strand feels thick and coarse, you have coarse hair.

Curl Pattern

Curl pattern refers to the shape of the hair strand. To determine your curl pattern, look at your hair when it's wet and free from any styling products. If your hair is straight, you have type 1 hair. If your hair has a slight wave or curl, you have type 2 hair. If your hair has a defined curl pattern, you have type 3 hair. If your hair has a tight, coiled curl pattern, you have type 4 hair.

Porosity

Porosity refers to how easily your hair absorbs and retains moisture. To determine your hair's porosity, take a strand of hair and place it in a bowl of water. If the strand sinks to the bottom, you have high porosity hair. If the strand floats in the middle, you have medium porosity hair. If the strand floats on top, you have low porosity hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know what to look for, here's a step-by-step guide to help you determine your hair type:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Pat your hair dry with a towel so it's damp but not soaking wet.
  3. Take a single strand of hair and rub it up and down between your fingers to determine the texture.
  4. Look at your hair when it's wet and free from any styling products to determine the curl pattern.
  5. Take a strand of hair and place it in a bowl of water to determine the porosity.
  6. Repeat these steps in different areas of your head to ensure consistency.
  7. Once you've determined your hair type, research products and styling techniques that work best for your hair.

Tips for Hair Care

Now that you know your hair type, here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

Straight Hair

Straight hair is generally easy to manage and doesn't require a lot of maintenance. However, it's important to use a heat protectant when using heat styling tools and to avoid using too much product that can weigh down your hair.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair can be prone to frizz, so it's important to use a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum to keep your hair smooth. It's also best to air-dry your hair instead of using heat styling tools.

Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit more maintenance and moisture to maintain its shape. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner and a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls defined. It's also best to avoid using heat styling tools and to use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair.

Coily Hair

Coily hair is the most fragile hair type and requires a lot of moisture to prevent breakage. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner and a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized. It's also best to avoid using heat styling tools and to use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change due to hormonal changes or damage from heat styling or chemical treatments.

Do I need to use products specifically for my hair type?

Yes, using products specifically formulated for your hair type will help you achieve the best results and prevent damage.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Straight hair can be washed more frequently than curly or coily hair, which can be prone to dryness.

Should I use heat styling tools?

It's best to avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair. However, if you must use them, use a heat protectant and avoid using the highest heat setting.

By following these tips and properly caring for your hair based on your specific hair type, you can achieve healthy, luscious locks that look and feel great.
