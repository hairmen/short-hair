Why Is My Hair Curly Underneath But Frizzy On Top

Written by Eveline Nov 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who has curly hair, you may have noticed that your hair is curly underneath but frizzy on top. This can be a frustrating problem to deal with, and you may be wondering why it happens and what you can do about it. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and give you some tips on how to manage your curly/frizzy hair.

Description

Curly hair is often the result of genetics, and the type of curl you have can vary from loose waves to tight coils. The reason why your hair may be curly underneath but frizzy on top is due to the structure of your hair. The hair on top of your head is exposed to more environmental elements such as wind, humidity, and pollution, which can cause it to become frizzy. The hair underneath is protected by the hair on top and is not exposed to these same elements, which can cause it to retain its curl pattern.

Another reason why your hair may be curly underneath but frizzy on top is due to the way you style your hair. If you use heat styling tools such as flat irons or blow dryers on your hair, this can cause damage and breakage, which can lead to frizz. Additionally, if you brush your hair too much or use products that contain alcohol, this can also cause frizz.

How to Manage Curly/Frizzy Hair

Managing curly/frizzy hair can be a challenge, but there are several things you can do to keep your hair looking its best. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right products can make a big difference in how your hair looks and feels. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly/frizzy hair, such as shampoos, conditioners, and styling products. These products will help to enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can cause it to become dry and frizzy. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day, and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb will help to detangle your hair without causing damage or breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently combing through any knots or tangles.

4. Air Dry Your Hair

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, as these can cause damage and breakage. Instead, try to air dry your hair whenever you can. If you need to use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment and set it to the lowest heat setting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to manage your curly/frizzy hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Apply Conditioner

Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water. This will help to seal the cuticle and reduce frizz.

Step 4: Gently Squeeze Out Excess Water

Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out the excess water from your hair. Don't rub your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz.

Step 5: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair, such as a curl cream or gel. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 6: Air Dry Your Hair

Air dry your hair whenever possible. If you need to use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment and set it to the lowest heat setting.

Tips for Managing Curly/Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing your curly/frizzy hair:

  • Don't brush your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and cause frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What causes curly hair?

Curly hair is often the result of genetics. The shape of your hair follicle and the way your hair grows out of it determine the type of curl you have.

How can I make my hair less frizzy?

Using the right products, washing your hair less often, and avoiding heat styling tools can all help to reduce frizz. Additionally, using a wide-tooth comb and air drying your hair can also help.

Is it better to comb or brush curly hair?

It's generally better to use a wide-tooth comb on curly hair, as this will help to detangle your hair without causing damage or breakage. Brushing curly hair can cause frizz and breakage.

Hair Care for Curly/Frizzy Hair

If you have curly/frizzy hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some additional tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Protect your hair from chlorine by wearing a swim cap or applying a leave-in conditioner before swimming.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal clasps, as these can cause breakage.

By following these tips, you can manage your curly/frizzy hair and keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
