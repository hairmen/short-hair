How To Make Thin Hair Look Thicker In A Messy Bun

Written by Petter Sep 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have thin hair, you may feel like there's not much you can do to create a voluminous hairstyle. But don't worry, there are ways to make your hair look thicker, even in a messy bun. In this article, we'll show you step-by-step how to create a messy bun that gives the illusion of thicker hair. Let's get started!

Description

A messy bun is a popular hairstyle that's easy to create and perfect for those lazy hair days. However, if you have thin hair, a messy bun can sometimes look flat and lifeless. The good news is that there are ways to add volume to a messy bun, making it look fuller and thicker.

How to Make Thin Hair Look Thicker in a Messy Bun

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to add body and lift to your hair. Then, apply a volumizing mousse to your damp hair, focusing on the roots.

Step 2: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add even more volume. Flip your head upside down while blow-drying to give your hair extra lift.

Step 3: Tease Your Hair

Next, tease your hair at the crown and along the sides. This will create more texture and volume. Use a teasing comb or a boar bristle brush to gently backcomb your hair.

Step 4: Create a Messy Bun

Gather your hair into a ponytail and secure it with an elastic band. Then, twist your hair and wrap it around the base of the ponytail to create a messy bun. Secure the bun with bobby pins, making sure to leave some pieces of hair loose to create a more relaxed look.

Step 5: Add More Volume

If your bun still looks flat, use a volumizing powder or dry shampoo to add more texture and volume. Sprinkle the powder or dry shampoo onto the roots of your hair and massage it in with your fingers.

Tips

  • Use a hair donut to create a fuller-looking messy bun.
  • Experiment with different bun placements, such as a low bun or a side bun, to see what works best for your hair.
  • Accessorize your messy bun with a headband or hair scarf to add more dimension to your hairstyle.

FAQ

Can I create a messy bun with short hair?

Yes, you can still create a messy bun with short hair. However, it may not look as voluminous as longer hair. You can try using a hair donut or teasing your hair to add more volume.

Do I need to use a specific type of elastic band?

No, you can use any type of elastic band to secure your ponytail. However, a clear elastic band may be less noticeable in your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking thick and healthy, it's important to take care of it. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and try to avoid using heat styling tools too often. Additionally, make sure to get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

In conclusion, creating a messy bun that looks thick and voluminous is easy with the right techniques. Follow our step-by-step guide and use our tips to achieve the perfect messy bun for your hair type. With a little practice, you'll be able to create a beautiful, effortless hairstyle that's perfect for any occasion.
