How To Deal With Thick Short Hair Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Luffy Oct 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Thick short hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous and full of life, it can also be hard to manage and style. If you're struggling with thick short hair, don't worry! We've got you covered with tips and tricks to make your haircare routine a breeze.

How to

1. Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Choosing the right shampoo and conditioner can make a huge difference in managing your thick short hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for thick hair, and avoid those that contain sulfates and alcohol. These ingredients can dry out your hair and make it more difficult to manage.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims are essential for maintaining the health and shape of your hair. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep split ends at bay and maintain the shape of your style.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to hydrate and nourish your hair, making it easier to manage. Apply a small amount to damp hair and comb through to distribute evenly.

4. Invest in Quality Styling Tools

Investing in quality styling tools, such as a high-quality blow dryer and flat iron, can make a huge difference in the manageability of your hair. Look for tools with ceramic or tourmaline plates, as these will help to reduce frizz and damage to your hair.

Step by Step

1. Start with Clean Hair

Begin by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner designed for thick hair. Rinse thoroughly and gently towel dry.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair, making sure to distribute evenly. Comb through with a wide-toothed comb to detangle.

3. Blow Dry

Use a high-quality blow dryer with a concentrator attachment to blow dry your hair. Start by rough drying your hair with your fingers until it is about 80% dry, then use a round brush to smooth and shape your hair.

4. Style

Once your hair is dry, use a flat iron or curling iron to style as desired. Use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from damage.

Tips

- Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. - Use a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent tangles. - Consider getting a layered haircut to add movement and texture to your hair. - Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

FAQ

What can I do about frizz in my thick short hair?

To combat frizz, use a smoothing serum or oil on your hair before blow drying or styling. You can also use a boar bristle brush to smooth your hair and reduce frizz.

Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with thick short hair?

Absolutely! A ponytail can be a great option for keeping your hair out of your face and looking stylish. Use a hair tie that won't damage your hair, and consider adding a cute accessory like a scarf or headband.

How often should I wash my thick short hair?

This will depend on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people with thick short hair can get away with washing every 2-3 days. If you have an oily scalp, you may need to wash more frequently.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for managing thick short hair. Make sure to use products that are specifically designed for your hair type, and avoid using hot tools too frequently. Regular trims and deep conditioning treatments can also help to keep your hair healthy and manageable. With the right care and styling techniques, your thick short hair can be your best accessory!
