How To Cut Curtain Bangs At Home 2020 A Step By Step Guide

Curtain bangs have been a trend for a while now, and it seems like they're here to stay. These bangs are soft, face-framing, and can complement any hairstyle. If you're thinking of giving yourself a DIY haircut, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to cut curtain bangs at home in 2020.

How to Cut Curtain Bangs at Home

If you're wondering how to cut curtain bangs at home, don't worry. It's not as complicated as it looks. However, you need to prepare yourself and your hair before attempting to cut your bangs. Here's what you need to do:

1. Choose the Right Scissors

Cutting your hair with the right scissors is essential. Avoid using kitchen or craft scissors. Invest in a pair of hair-cutting scissors with sharp blades.

2. Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry. Don't blow-dry your hair, as it can affect the natural fall of your bangs.

3. Detangle Your Hair

Comb your hair and make sure there are no tangles or knots. Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid damaging your hair.

4. Section Your Hair

Section your hair and clip the top section away. You want to focus on the lower section of your hair first.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've prepared your hair, it's time to cut your bangs. Follow these steps:

1. Determine the Length of Your Bangs

Determine how long you want your bangs to be. Use your eyebrows as a guide. Place a comb vertically against the side of your nose, and the outer corner of your eye. Mark this spot with your fingers. This is where you'll start cutting your bangs.

2. Cut Your Bangs

Hold your hair between your index and middle fingers, and snip the hair below your fingers in a straight line. Don't cut your entire bangs at once. Cut small sections of hair at a time until you achieve the desired length.

3. Texturize Your Bangs

To make your bangs look more natural, you can texturize them. Hold the scissors vertically and make tiny cuts into your bangs, creating a feathered effect.

4. Style Your Bangs

Style your bangs by parting them in the middle or to the side. Use a hairdryer and a round brush to blow-dry them in the direction you want them to fall.

Tips for Cutting Curtain Bangs

Cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Cut Your Bangs Dry

Always cut your bangs when they're dry. Wet hair can shrink when it dries, and you might end up cutting too much hair.

2. Cut Small Sections of Hair

Cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much hair at once. It's easier to cut more hair than to put it back.

3. Start Longer

Start cutting your bangs longer than you think you need. You can always go shorter, but you can't go back once you've cut too much hair.

FAQs

1. How Often Should I Trim My Curtain Bangs?

You can trim your curtain bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

2. Can I Cut My Bangs When They're Wet?

No, it's best to cut your bangs when they're dry. Wet hair can shrink when it dries, and you might end up cutting too much hair.

3. Can I Use Regular Scissors to Cut My Bangs?

No, it's best to use hair-cutting scissors with sharp blades. Regular scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Bangs

Now that you've cut your bangs, it's important to take care of them. Here are some hair care tips for bangs:

1. Wash Your Bangs Separately

Wash your bangs separately from the rest of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot water.

2. Blow-Dry Your Bangs

Blow-dry your bangs in the direction you want them to fall. Use a round brush to create volume and a smooth finish.

3. Use Dry Shampoo

Use dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh in between washes. Spray it on your roots and massage it into your scalp.

That's it! You now know how to cut curtain bangs at home in 2020. Remember to take your time, cut small sections of hair, and have fun!
