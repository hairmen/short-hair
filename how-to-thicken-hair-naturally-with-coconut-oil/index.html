How To Thicken Hair Naturally With Coconut Oil

Written by Daniel Sep 30, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking for a natural way to thicken your hair? Look no further than coconut oil! This versatile oil has been used for centuries to promote hair growth and thickness. In this article, we'll discuss how to thicken hair naturally with coconut oil, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Thicken Hair Naturally with Coconut Oil
  2. Step-by-Step Instructions
  3. Tips for Using Coconut Oil to Thicken Hair
  4. Frequently Asked Questions
  5. Hair Care Tips for Thicker, Healthier Hair

How to Thicken Hair Naturally with Coconut Oil

Coconut oil is a natural emollient that can penetrate deep into the hair shaft, providing nourishment and hydration to each strand. Here's how to use coconut oil to thicken your hair:

1. Choose a high-quality coconut oil.

Look for unrefined, cold-pressed coconut oil, which retains more of its beneficial nutrients than refined coconut oil. You can find coconut oil at most health food stores, online retailers, and some grocery stores.

2. Warm up the coconut oil.

Scoop a few tablespoons of coconut oil into a microwave-safe bowl or a small saucepan. Heat the oil for 10-15 seconds in the microwave or on the stove until it is melted and warm but not hot.

3. Apply the coconut oil to your hair.

Starting at the roots and working your way to the ends, massage the warm coconut oil into your hair, making sure to cover every strand. You may want to section your hair to make sure you don't miss any spots. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the oil evenly.

4. Leave the coconut oil in your hair for at least 30 minutes.

You can leave the coconut oil in your hair for up to several hours, depending on your hair type and how much time you have. Cover your hair with a shower cap or a towel to keep the oil from getting on your clothes or furniture.

5. Rinse the coconut oil out of your hair.

Shampoo your hair as usual to remove the coconut oil. You may need to shampoo twice to get all the oil out. Follow up with a conditioner if desired.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here's a more detailed breakdown of how to use coconut oil to thicken your hair:

1. Wash your hair.

Start with clean, damp hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual, and then towel dry it until it is just damp.

2. Melt the coconut oil.

Place a few tablespoons of coconut oil in a small saucepan or microwave-safe bowl. Heat the oil for 10-15 seconds in the microwave or on the stove until it is melted and warm but not hot.

3. Apply the coconut oil to your hair.

Starting at the roots and working your way to the ends, use your fingers to massage the coconut oil into your hair. Make sure to cover every strand, and use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the oil evenly.

4. Cover your hair with a shower cap or towel.

To prevent the oil from getting on your clothes or furniture, cover your hair with a shower cap or towel. You can also wrap a warm towel around your head to help the oil penetrate deeper into your hair.

5. Leave the coconut oil in your hair for at least 30 minutes.

You can leave the coconut oil in your hair for up to several hours, depending on your hair type and how much time you have. The longer you leave it in, the more time the oil has to penetrate your hair shaft and nourish your strands.

6. Rinse the coconut oil out of your hair.

Shampoo your hair as usual to remove the coconut oil. You may need to shampoo twice to get all the oil out. Follow up with a conditioner if desired.

Tips for Using Coconut Oil to Thicken Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of your coconut oil hair treatment:

  • Use a small amount of coconut oil at first, and gradually increase the amount if your hair can handle it.
  • Don't apply too much coconut oil to your scalp, as it can clog your hair follicles and cause breakouts.
  • For best results, use coconut oil on your hair once a week or every other week.
  • Try mixing coconut oil with other natural ingredients, such as honey or avocado, for added nourishment.
  • Consider taking a biotin supplement, which can help promote hair growth and thickness from the inside out.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions and answers about using coconut oil to thicken hair:

Q: Can coconut oil make your hair greasy?

A: Yes, if you use too much coconut oil or don't rinse it out thoroughly, it can leave your hair looking and feeling greasy. Start with a small amount of coconut oil, and make sure to rinse it out completely.

Q: How often should I use coconut oil on my hair?

A: For best results, use coconut oil on your hair once a week or every other week.

Q: Can coconut oil help with hair loss?

A: While coconut oil can't cure hair loss, it can help promote healthy hair growth and thickness by nourishing your hair and scalp.

Hair Care Tips for Thicker, Healthier Hair

In addition to using coconut oil, there are several other things you can do to promote thicker, healthier hair:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools that can damage your hair.
  • Brush your hair gently, using a soft-bristled brush or a wide-tooth comb.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors.

By following these tips and using coconut oil regularly, you can achieve thick, healthy, beautiful hair naturally!
