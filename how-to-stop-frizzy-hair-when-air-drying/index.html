How To Stop Frizzy Hair When Air Drying

Written by Daniel Nov 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly or wavy hair, you know the struggle of trying to air dry your hair without it turning into a frizzy mess. Frizz can be caused by a number of factors, including humidity, heat damage, and improper hair care. In this blog post, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to stop frizzy hair when air drying.

  • How to Stop Frizzy Hair When Air Drying
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Frizz-Free Hair

How to Stop Frizzy Hair When Air Drying

The key to preventing frizz is to keep your hair hydrated and nourished. This means using the right hair products and taking care of your hair on a daily basis. Here are some steps to follow when air drying your hair to prevent frizz:

Step 1: Start with a Clean Slate

Before you air dry your hair, make sure it is clean and free of any product buildup. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any excess buildup and prepare your hair for air drying.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after you have washed it. This will help to reduce frizz and keep your hair hydrated. Make sure to apply the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Use a Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt to Dry Your Hair

Instead of using a regular towel to dry your hair, opt for a microfiber towel or t-shirt. These materials are gentler on your hair and will help to reduce frizz. Gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair without rubbing it vigorously.

Step 4: Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair to help define your curls or waves. This will also help to lock in moisture and reduce frizz. Use a small amount of product and apply it evenly throughout your hair.

Step 5: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Once you have applied your styling product, let your hair air dry naturally. Avoid touching your hair too much while it is drying as this can cause frizz. Instead, let it dry on its own and style it once it is completely dry.

Tips for Frizz-Free Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve frizz-free hair when air drying:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush to detangle your hair while it is wet.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as hair dryers, flat irons, and curling irons as these can cause heat damage and frizz.
  • Protect your hair from humidity by using a humidity-resistant hairspray or serum.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause frizz.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about stopping frizzy hair when air drying:

Q: Can I air dry my hair overnight?

A: It is not recommended to air dry your hair overnight as this can cause tangles and breakage. It is best to air dry your hair during the day when you can keep an eye on it.

Q: How often should I use a leave-in conditioner?

A: You should use a leave-in conditioner every time you wash your hair to keep it hydrated and nourished.

Q: Can I still use hair oil if I am air drying my hair?

A: Yes, you can still use hair oil if you are air drying your hair. Just make sure to use a small amount and apply it evenly throughout your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you manage frizz:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep it looking healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve frizz-free hair when air drying. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you will see results over time.
