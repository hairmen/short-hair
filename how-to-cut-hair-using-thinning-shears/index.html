How To Cut Hair Using Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to add texture and volume to your hair without sacrificing length, thinning shears might be the answer. Thinning shears are a type of scissors with teeth that allow you to remove bulk and create layers in your hair. In this article, we'll walk you through how to cut hair using thinning shears in a few easy steps.

How to Cut Hair Using Thinning Shears

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections with clips or ties. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up. This will help you keep track of what you've already cut and ensure that you're cutting evenly.

Step 3: Choose the Right Thinning Shears

Make sure you're using the right type of thinning shears for your hair type. If you have thick hair, you'll need a pair of shears with more teeth. If you have fine hair, you'll need a pair with fewer teeth.

Step 4: Hold the Shears Properly

Hold your thinning shears with your thumb and middle finger in the holes and your index finger on the blade. This will give you more control and help you cut more precisely.

Step 5: Cut Your Hair

Start by cutting small sections of hair at a time. Hold the section you want to cut between your fingers and slide the thinning shears down the hair shaft. Make sure you're only cutting a small amount of hair at a time to avoid any mistakes.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

Check your progress as you go to make sure you're cutting evenly and creating the texture you want.

Step 7: Finish Up

Once you've finished cutting all the sections of your hair, style it as desired. You should notice a more textured and voluminous look to your hair.

Tips for Cutting Hair Using Thinning Shears

- Always cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid any mistakes.

- Make sure you're using the right type of thinning shears for your hair type.

- Hold the shears properly to give you more control and precision.

- Check your progress as you go to make sure you're cutting evenly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

A: Yes, but you'll need to make sure you're using the right type of thinning shears for your hair type.

Q: Can thinning shears damage your hair?

A: If used improperly, thinning shears can damage your hair. Make sure you're only cutting a small amount of hair at a time and checking your progress as you go.

Q: How often should you use thinning shears on your hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much texture you want. It's best to consult with a professional stylist to determine how often you should use thinning shears on your hair.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears

After using thinning shears on your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and texture. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and maintain its shape.

Cutting your hair using thinning shears can give you a more textured and voluminous look without sacrificing length. Follow these easy steps and tips to achieve the perfect cut.
