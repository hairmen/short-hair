Why Does My Hair Get So Greasy When I Straighten It

Written by Robby Jun 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've ever straightened your hair and noticed it getting greasy much faster than usual, you're not alone. This can be a frustrating experience, especially when you've put in the effort to get your hair looking sleek and smooth. In this article, we'll explore why your hair may be getting greasy after straightening, and what you can do to prevent it.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Straighten Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Greasy Hair

Description

When you straighten your hair, you're essentially applying heat and pressure to your hair strands to change their shape. This can cause your hair's natural oils, or sebum, to spread out more evenly along the hair shaft. When sebum is spread out like this, it can make your hair look greasy and flat much faster than usual.

How to Straighten Your Hair

If you're new to straightening your hair, here are some basic steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any buildup or impurities in your hair, and make it easier to straighten.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Before you start straightening, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help prevent heat damage and keep your hair looking healthy.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to straighten each section evenly.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, use a flat iron to straighten each section of hair from root to tip. Be sure to move the flat iron slowly and evenly through each section.

Step 5: Finish with a Serum

Once you've straightened all of your hair, finish with a smoothing serum or oil to add shine and tame any flyaways.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to straightening your hair:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start straightening, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also a good idea to detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or brush.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas that are prone to damage.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. For best results, divide your hair into at least four sections: two in the front and two in the back.

Step 4: Straighten Each Section

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, use a flat iron to straighten each section of hair from root to tip. Be sure to move the flat iron slowly and evenly through each section, and avoid going over the same section multiple times.

Step 5: Finish with a Serum

Once you've straightened all of your hair, finish with a smoothing serum or oil to add shine and tame any flyaways.

Tips for Preventing Greasy Hair

Here are some tips for preventing greasy hair after straightening:

  • Wash your hair less frequently. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can cause your scalp to produce more oil to compensate.
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to absorb excess oil.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much. The oils from your hands can transfer to your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Avoid using too much product. Overloading your hair with styling products can weigh it down and make it look greasy.
  • Avoid applying heat directly to your scalp. This can cause your scalp to produce more oil.

FAQ

Why does my hair get greasy after straightening?

Straightening your hair can cause your natural oils to spread out more evenly along the hair shaft, which can make your hair look greasy much faster than usual.

What can I do to prevent greasy hair after straightening?

Wash your hair less frequently, use a dry shampoo in between washes, avoid touching your hair too much, and avoid using too much product. You can also try applying heat protectant only to the ends of your hair, and avoid applying heat directly to your scalp.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some general hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins.

By following these tips, you can help keep your hair healthy and prevent it from getting greasy after straightening.
