How To Do Your Hair 50S Style

Written by Petter Jul 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a fan of the retro 50s era and want to recreate the iconic hairstyles that defined the decade? Look no further. In this article, we will guide you through how to do your hair 50s style, step by step. From the classic victory rolls to the glamorous bouffant, we’ve got you covered. Read on for tips, tricks, and hair care advice.

Description

The 50s was a time of glamour, where women’s fashion and beauty were highly stylized. The hairstyles of the era were no exception, characterized by volume, curls, and intricate updos. Whether you’re attending a retro-themed party or just want to add a vintage touch to your everyday look, the 50s hairstyle is a fun and stylish choice.

How to

First, decide on which 50s hairstyle you want to recreate. Below are some popular options:

  • Victory Rolls
  • Bouffant
  • Poodle Cut
  • Pin Curls
  • Beehive

Once you’ve chosen your style, follow these steps:

Victory Rolls

1. Start with clean, dry hair. Part your hair down the middle.

2. Take a small section of hair from the front and roll it towards your head, securing it with bobby pins.

3. Repeat on the other side, making sure the rolls are equal in size and shape.

4. Use hairspray to hold the rolls in place.

Bouffant

1. Start by teasing the crown of your hair to create volume.

2. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section.

3. Gather the hair at the crown and secure it with bobby pins.

4. Brush the sides of your hair back and secure with bobby pins at the nape of your neck.

5. Finish with hairspray.

Poodle Cut

1. Start with a short, curly bob.

2. Part your hair on one side and sweep the longer side across your forehead.

3. Pin the shorter side back behind your ear.

4. Finish with hairspray.

Pin Curls

1. Start with damp hair.

2. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around your finger, rolling it towards your scalp.

3. Pin the curl in place with a bobby pin.

4. Repeat all over your head.

5. Allow the curls to dry completely.

6. Remove the bobby pins and gently brush out the curls.

7. Finish with hairspray.

Beehive

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

2. Tease the crown of your hair to create volume.

3. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section.

4. Gather the hair at the crown and twist it into a bun, securing it with bobby pins.

5. Brush the sides of your hair back and secure with bobby pins at the nape of your neck.

6. Finish with hairspray.

Tips

- Use hot rollers or a curling iron to create curls for most 50s hairstyles.

- Backcombing or teasing the hair is essential for adding volume to many 50s hairstyles.

- Use bobby pins that match your hair color for a seamless look.

- Hairspray is your friend – use it generously to hold your hairstyle in place.

FAQ

Q: Can I do 50s hairstyles on short hair?

A: Yes, many 50s hairstyles were designed for short hair, such as the poodle cut and the pixie cut.

Q: Do I need to use heat styling tools?

A: Not necessarily. You can create curls using pin curls or foam rollers and achieve volume through teasing.

Hair Care

When recreating 50s hairstyles, it’s important to take care of your hair. Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools and avoid excessive teasing or backcombing, which can cause breakage. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Now that you know how to do your hair 50s style, it’s time to channel your inner Marilyn Monroe and rock that retro look with confidence!
