What Is The Difference Between A Temp Fade And A Taper

Written by Oliver Sep 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you planning to get a new haircut? If you are, then you might be wondering what the difference is between a temp fade and a taper. Both haircuts are popular choices for men and can help you achieve a stylish and modern look. In this article, we will discuss the key differences between a temp fade and a taper.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A temp fade is a type of haircut that features a gradual fade from the sides to the back of the head. The hair on the sides is cut short and gradually fades into longer hair on the top of the head. The fade typically starts above the ears and ends at the temple. This type of haircut is popular among men who want a stylish and modern look.

A taper, on the other hand, is a type of haircut that features a gradual decrease in hair length from the top of the head down to the neckline. The hair on the sides and back is gradually tapered shorter, while the hair on top of the head remains longer. This type of haircut is versatile and can be worn in a variety of styles.

How to

If you're looking to get a temp fade or a taper, it's important to find a skilled barber or hairstylist who can help you achieve your desired look. Here are some tips on how to get a great temp fade or taper:

1. Find a skilled barber or hairstylist

Look for a barber or hairstylist who has experience cutting hair in the style you want. Ask for recommendations from friends or family members, or read online reviews to find a skilled professional.

2. Communicate your desired look

Be clear and specific about the type of fade or taper you want. Bring in pictures or examples of the style you're looking for to help your barber or hairstylist understand your vision.

3. Maintain your haircut

To keep your temp fade or taper looking fresh, you'll need to maintain it regularly. This may include getting touch-ups every few weeks or using styling products to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Step by step

Here are some general steps that a barber or hairstylist may follow when giving you a temp fade or taper:

1. Choose the right clipper guard

The barber or hairstylist will choose the right clipper guard for the length you want your hair to be on the sides and back of your head.

2. Start with the sides

The barber or hairstylist will start by cutting the hair on the sides of your head, gradually fading the length as they move up towards the temple.

3. Blend the top

Next, the barber or hairstylist will blend the hair on top of your head with the fade on the sides, using scissors or clippers to create a seamless transition.

4. Taper the back

If you're getting a taper, the barber or hairstylist will gradually taper the hair on the back of your head, creating a neat and tidy look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain your temp fade or taper:

1. Use the right styling products

Choose the right styling products for your hair type and the style you want to achieve. This may include pomade, wax, or gel.

2. Brush and comb regularly

To keep your hair looking neat and tidy, brush and comb it regularly. This will also help distribute any styling products you're using.

3. Schedule regular touch-ups

To keep your temp fade or taper looking fresh, schedule regular touch-ups with your barber or hairstylist. This may be every few weeks or as needed, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

FAQ

What is a high taper?

A high taper is a type of taper haircut that features a shorter length on the sides and back of the head, with longer hair on top. The fade may start higher up on the head than a traditional taper.

What is a low fade?

A low fade is a type of fade haircut that features a gradual decrease in hair length from the top of the head down to the neckline. The fade typically starts below the temple.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

1. Wash regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.

2. Use heat protectant

If you use heat styling tools, such as a hair dryer or flat iron, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Trim regularly

To prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair, get regular trims every few months.

So, now that you know the difference between a temp fade and a taper, you can choose the right haircut for your style and preferences. Whether you want a modern and edgy look or a classic and versatile style, a skilled barber or hairstylist can help you achieve your desired look.
