How To Curl Thick Straight Hair And Make It Stay

Written by Christine Aug 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have thick and straight hair that just won't hold a curl? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people with thick straight hair struggle with getting curls to stay in place. In this article, we'll cover some tips and tricks for curling thick straight hair and making it stay.

Table of Contents

  • How to prepare your hair for curling
  • Which curling tools to use
  • How to curl your hair
  • How to make your curls stay
  • Tips for styling your curls
  • FAQ

How to Prepare Your Hair for Curling

Before you start curling your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to add texture and volume to your hair, which will make it easier to curl.

Once you've washed your hair, apply a heat protectant spray to your hair. This will help to protect your hair from damage caused by the heat of curling tools.

Which Curling Tools to Use

There are many different types of curling tools available, so it can be difficult to know which one to use. For thick straight hair, we recommend using a curling iron with a barrel size of 1.5 inches or larger. This will help to create loose, beachy waves that will look great on thick hair.

If you prefer tighter curls, you can use a smaller barrel size. Just be aware that smaller barrels can be more difficult to use on thick hair.

How to Curl Your Hair

To curl your hair, start by dividing it into sections. Use clips to keep each section of hair separated.

Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron. Hold the curling iron in place for about 10 seconds, then release the curl. Repeat this process until you've curled all of your hair.

How to Make Your Curls Stay

The key to making your curls stay is to use the right products. Start by applying a light-hold hairspray to your hair before you start curling. This will help to give your hair some texture and hold.

After you've curled your hair, apply a strong-hold hairspray to each section of hair. This will help to keep your curls in place all day long.

Tips for Styling Your Curls

Once you've curled your hair, there are many different ways you can style it. For a casual look, you can simply leave your curls loose and tousled. For a more formal look, you can brush out your curls to create soft waves.

You can also experiment with different hairstyles, such as half-up, half-down styles or braids.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I want to curl it?

A: It's best to wash your hair every other day if you want to curl it. This will help to preserve your hair's natural oils, which will make it easier to curl.

Q: Can I curl my hair without heat?

A: Yes, you can curl your hair without heat using a variety of techniques. Some popular options include braiding your hair while it's damp or using foam rollers.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Start by using a high-quality shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type.

Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. If you do use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

Finally, be sure to get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can curl your thick straight hair and make it stay all day long. With a little practice, you'll be able to create beautiful, bouncy curls that will turn heads wherever you go.
