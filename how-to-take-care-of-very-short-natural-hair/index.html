How To Take Care Of Very Short Natural Hair Tips And Tricks

If you have very short natural hair, you know that it requires a different type of care than longer hair. Short hair can be more prone to breakage and damage, but with the right techniques, you can keep your hair healthy and looking great. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for taking care of very short natural hair.

How to Take Care of Very Short Natural Hair

Whether you have a TWA (teeny weeny afro) or a slightly longer cut, taking care of very short natural hair is essential. Here are some key things to keep in mind:

Shampoo Regularly

Even though your hair is short, it still needs to be cleansed regularly. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Moisturize, Moisturize, Moisturize

Short hair can be more prone to dryness and breakage, so it's essential to keep it moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair hydrated and protected.

Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep. This will help prevent breakage and keep your hair looking its best.

Trim Regularly

Even though your hair is short, it still needs to be trimmed regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Avoid Heat

Avoid using heat tools like flat irons or curling irons on your short natural hair, as they can cause damage and breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to taking care of very short natural hair:

Step 1: Shampoo your hair

Wet your hair thoroughly and apply a small amount of sulfate-free shampoo. Massage your scalp gently, then rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Condition your hair

Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-toothed comb to distribute the product evenly.

Step 3: Moisturize your hair

Apply a moisturizing cream or oil to your hair, focusing on the ends. This will help keep your hair hydrated and protected.

Step 4: Protect your hair at night

Use a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep.

Step 5: Trim your hair

Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Tips for Caring for Very Short Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of very short natural hair:

Use a wide-toothed comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-toothed comb to avoid pulling and tugging on your hair.

Avoid tight hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles like braids or weaves, as they can cause damage and breakage.

Protect your hair from the sun

If you're going to be spending time in the sun, wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from UV damage.

Drink plenty of water

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my very short natural hair?

A: Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week, depending on how oily your scalp gets.

Q: Can I use heat tools on my very short natural hair?

A: It's best to avoid using heat tools on your short natural hair, as they can cause damage and breakage.

Q: How can I prevent breakage on my very short natural hair?

A: To prevent breakage, make sure to keep your hair moisturized, avoid tight hairstyles, and protect your hair at night.

Hair Care Routine for Very Short Natural Hair

Here's a simple hair care routine for very short natural hair:

  • Shampoo once or twice a week with a sulfate-free shampoo
  • Condition with a leave-in conditioner or oil
  • Moisturize with a cream or oil
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet
  • Trim every 6-8 weeks

With these tips and tricks, you can keep your very short natural hair healthy and looking great. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and don't hesitate to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
