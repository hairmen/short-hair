How To Do Lace Front Edges A Step By Step Guide

If you're new to wearing lace front wigs, you may be wondering how to achieve natural-looking edges that blend seamlessly with your hairline. The good news is that with the right tools and techniques, it's easier than you think! In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps for achieving flawless lace front edges.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Do Lace Front Edges

Lace front wigs are designed to mimic the look of natural hair, but achieving a seamless hairline can be tricky. The key to natural-looking edges is to use a combination of tweezing and hair products to create a gradual transition from the wig to your skin. Here's what you'll need:

  • Lace front wig
  • Tweezers
  • Scissors
  • Edge control product
  • Small brush or toothbrush
  • Blow dryer

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepping Your Wig

The first step is to prep your wig by cutting the lace. Use scissors to carefully trim the excess lace around the hairline, leaving about 1/4 inch of lace. Be sure to cut in a zigzag pattern to create a natural-looking hairline.

Step 2: Tweezing

Next, use your tweezers to pluck individual hairs from the hairline. This will create a more natural-looking hairline by mimicking the look of baby hairs. Be careful not to overpluck, as this can create bald spots in your wig.

Step 3: Applying Edge Control

Once you've tweezed your wig to your liking, it's time to apply edge control. Use a small brush or toothbrush to apply a small amount of edge control along your hairline, blending it into your skin. Be sure to use a product that matches your skin tone for the most natural look.

Step 4: Blow Drying

To set your edges, use a blow dryer on low heat to dry the edge control. This will help to smooth down any flyaways and keep your edges in place.

Tips for Perfecting Your Lace Front Edges

  • Use a light hand when tweezing your wig. It's better to underpluck than to overpluck.
  • Be sure to match your edge control product to your skin tone for the most natural look.
  • Use a small brush or toothbrush to apply edge control for more precise application.
  • Be patient! It may take a few tries to get your edges looking just right.

FAQs about Lace Front Edges

Q: How often should I touch up my edges?

A: This will depend on how often you wear your wig and how well you take care of it. Generally, you should touch up your edges every few days to keep them looking fresh.

Q: Can I use regular hair gel for my edges?

A: While you can use regular hair gel for your edges, it may not provide the same level of hold as a dedicated edge control product. Look for a product specifically designed for edges for the best results.

Q: How do I prevent my edges from frizzing?

A: To prevent frizz, be sure to use a small amount of product and avoid touching your edges too much once they're styled. You can also use a silk or satin scarf to protect your edges while you sleep.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Lace Front Wig

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the longevity and appearance of your lace front wig. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your wig, as this can damage the hair fibers.
  • Store your wig properly when not in use to prevent tangling and matting.
  • Use a wig cap to protect your natural hair and scalp from friction and sweat.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve flawless lace front edges that blend seamlessly with your natural hairline. Happy styling!
