How To Cut Senior Women s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a hair stylist looking to learn how to cut senior women's hair? With age, hair texture and style change, and as a stylist, it's important to know how to cater to your senior clients. In this guide, we will take you through the steps to cutting senior women's hair and provide you with tips on how to do it right.

Description

Senior women's hair has unique needs as it changes with age. As we age, our hair becomes finer, drier, and loses its elasticity. It's essential to know how to cut senior women's hair to create a style that is easy to maintain and suits their face shape and hair texture.

How to Cut Senior Women's Hair

Step 1: Consultation

Before starting, have a consultation with your client to understand their hair type, texture, and any challenges they may have with their hair. This is also a good time to discuss the desired length and style.

Step 2: Wash and Dry

Wash your client's hair and dry it thoroughly. This will help you see the natural fall of their hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Section the hair into four parts – top, sides, and back. Use clips to hold each section in place. This will make the cutting process easier and more efficient.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start with the back section and cut the hair using a razor or scissors, depending on the desired style. Keep in mind that senior women's hair is delicate, so avoid using too much tension or pressure when cutting. Cut the hair in small sections and check the length after each cut to ensure you're on the right track.

Step 5: Move to the Sides

Once you've cut the back section, move on to the sides. Use the same technique as before, cutting in small sections and checking the length after each cut.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top section and cut the hair to the desired length. Keep in mind that senior women's hair is often thinner on top, so avoid removing too much hair.

Step 7: Blend and Style

Blend the hair by using scissors to remove any uneven strands. Style the hair using a blow dryer, straightener, or curling iron, depending on the desired look.

Tips

  • Use a razor or thinning shears to add texture and volume to thin hair.
  • Consider using a shorter style to make hair appear fuller and thicker.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down the hair and make it look greasy.
  • Encourage your senior clients to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep their hair healthy and hydrated.

FAQ

What is the best haircut for senior women?

The best haircut for senior women depends on their face shape, hair texture, and personal style. However, shorter hairstyles are often recommended, as they can make hair appear fuller and thicker. A pixie cut, bob, or shaggy layers are popular choices.

How often should senior women get their hair cut?

Senior women should get their hair cut every six to eight weeks to maintain the shape of their haircut and keep their hair healthy and free from split ends.

Hair Care

To keep senior women's hair healthy and looking its best, encourage them to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Avoid using harsh shampoos and conditioners, and use a heat protectant spray when using styling tools. Finally, protect hair from the sun's harmful rays by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

By following these steps and tips, you can confidently cut senior women's hair and provide them with a style that is easy to maintain and suits their needs.
