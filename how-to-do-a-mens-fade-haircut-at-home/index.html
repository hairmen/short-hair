How To Do A Mens Fade Haircut At Home

Written by Robby Sep 18, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to save money and time on your haircuts, learning how to do a fade haircut at home is a great option. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and professional-looking cut without leaving your house. In this article, we'll go through the steps to achieve a mens fade haircut at home, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first step to achieving a mens fade haircut at home is to gather the necessary tools. You will need a set of clippers with various guard sizes, a comb, scissors, and a handheld mirror. It's also helpful to have a cape or towel to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Before starting the haircut, it's important to have a clear idea of the type of fade you want. There are several types of fades, including low, medium, and high fades. You can also choose between a tapered or skin fade. Research different styles and find one that suits your preferences.

Once you have your tools and a clear idea of the style you want, you can begin the haircut. It's essential to start with clean, dry hair. Use the clippers and the longest guard size to trim the hair on top of your head. Work in small sections and use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head.

Step by Step

Follow these steps to achieve a mens fade haircut at home:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use the clippers and the longest guard size to trim the hair on top of your head.
  3. Switch to a shorter guard size and trim the sides and back of your head.
  4. Blend the top and sides of your hair with scissors and a comb.
  5. Use the clippers without a guard to create a bald fade around the sides and back of your head.
  6. Blend the bald fade with the longer hair on top using scissors and a comb.
  7. Clean up the neckline and sideburns with the clippers and a smaller guard size.
  8. Check your work in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great mens fade haircut at home:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use the comb and scissors to blend the hair for a natural look.
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired to avoid mistakes.
  • Use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head frequently.
  • Watch tutorials or get advice from a professional if you're unsure about the process.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about mens fade haircuts:

How often should I get a mens fade haircut?

The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and style. Generally, fades need to be maintained every two to four weeks.

What's the difference between a low, medium, and high fade?

The difference is the location of the fade on the sides of the head. Low fades start near the ear, medium fades start halfway up the sides, and high fades start near the top of the head.

What's the difference between a tapered and skin fade?

The difference is the length of the hair at the bottom of the fade. Tapered fades have longer hair at the bottom, while skin fades are completely bald at the bottom.

Hair Care

After your mens fade haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy. Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and frizziness.

Learning how to do a mens fade haircut at home can save you both time and money. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and professional-looking cut in the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps, tips, and hair care advice to achieve a great mens fade haircut at home.
