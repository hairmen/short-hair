How To Stop Hair Falling Out After Covid

Written by Frank Nov 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss after recovering from Covid-19? You're not alone. Many people have reported hair loss as a side effect of the virus. But don't worry, we've got some tips and tricks to help you stop your hair from falling out.

Table of Content

  1. How to Stop Hair Falling Out After Covid
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips to Prevent Hair Loss
  4. FAQs

How to Stop Hair Falling Out After Covid

First and foremost, it's important to understand that hair loss after Covid-19 is normal and temporary. It's caused by stress on the body from the virus, and it should stop within a few months. However, there are some things you can do to help speed up the process and prevent further hair loss.

1. Take Care of Your Scalp

Your scalp needs to be healthy in order for your hair to grow properly. Make sure you're using a gentle shampoo that's free of sulfates and parabens. You can also try using a scalp treatment, such as tea tree oil or aloe vera, to soothe any irritation or inflammation.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

What you eat can have a big impact on your hair health. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E. These nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth. You can also take a daily multivitamin to ensure you're getting all the nutrients you need.

3. Reduce Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it's important to find ways to manage your stress levels. This can include practicing yoga or meditation, getting regular exercise, or talking to a therapist.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can damage your hair and contribute to hair loss. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and when you do, use a heat protectant spray.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to help stop your hair from falling out:

  1. Take care of your scalp by using a gentle shampoo and a scalp treatment.
  2. Eat a balanced diet that's rich in protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E.
  3. Reduce stress by practicing yoga, meditation, or exercise.
  4. Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.
  5. Consider taking a daily multivitamin to ensure you're getting all the nutrients you need.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, which can pull on your hair and cause it to fall out.
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments, such as perms and relaxers, which can damage your hair and contribute to hair loss.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can lead to hair breakage and hair loss.
  • Avoid smoking and limit your alcohol intake, as both can contribute to hair loss.

FAQs

Q: Will my hair grow back after Covid-19?

A: Yes, in most cases, your hair will grow back within a few months after recovering from Covid-19. However, it's important to take care of your hair and scalp to help speed up the process.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can contribute to hair loss. When you're stressed, your body produces hormones that can affect your hair growth cycle.

Q: Are there any supplements that can help prevent hair loss?

A: Yes, some supplements, such as biotin and saw palmetto, may help prevent hair loss. However, it's important to talk to your doctor before starting any new supplements.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair no more than every other day to prevent stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair and scalp, you can help prevent hair loss after Covid-19 and promote healthy hair growth.
