Is It Permissible To Use Hair Relaxer In Islam

As a Muslim, it's important to follow the guidelines set forth in the Quran and Hadith regarding personal care and hygiene. One common question that arises is whether or not it's permissible to use hair relaxer in Islam. In this article, we'll explore the topic in depth and provide you with everything you need to know.

What is a Hair Relaxer?

A hair relaxer is a chemical treatment that is applied to curly or kinky hair to straighten it. It works by breaking down the protein bonds in the hair, which allows it to be reshaped. Hair relaxers can be used on all types of hair, but they are most commonly used by people with African or Caribbean hair.

Is it Halal or Haram?

There is no clear consensus on whether or not hair relaxers are halal or haram in Islam. Some scholars argue that it is permissible to use hair relaxers as long as they do not contain any haram ingredients. Others argue that manipulating the hair in such a way is haram and goes against the natural state that Allah created.

It is important to note that there is no mention of hair relaxers specifically in the Quran or Hadith, so it is up to the individual to make their own decision based on their interpretation of Islamic teachings.

How to Use Hair Relaxer

If you decide to use hair relaxer, it's important to follow the instructions carefully and take the necessary precautions to avoid damaging your hair or scalp.

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  2. Apply petroleum jelly or a protective cream around your hairline and ears to prevent the relaxer from getting on your skin.
  3. Section your hair and apply the relaxer to each section, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Leave the relaxer on for the recommended amount of time (usually 10-20 minutes).
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water and apply a neutralizing shampoo to stop the chemical process.
  6. Condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent breakage.

Tips for Using Hair Relaxer

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when using hair relaxer:

  • Do a patch test before applying the relaxer to your entire head to make sure you're not allergic to any of the ingredients.
  • Don't apply the relaxer to your scalp or leave it on for longer than recommended, as this can cause burns or damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioner regularly to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools (like flat irons or curling irons) on your hair after relaxing, as this can cause damage.
  • Consider getting your hair professionally relaxed by a licensed stylist to minimize the risk of damage.

FAQ

Can I use hair relaxer if I wear hijab?

Yes, you can still use hair relaxer if you wear hijab. Just be sure to take extra care when applying the relaxer around your hairline to avoid getting it on your hijab.

Can I use hair relaxer if I'm pregnant?

It is generally not recommended to use hair relaxer if you're pregnant, as the chemicals can be harmful to your baby. If you must use hair relaxer, talk to your doctor first and be sure to take extra precautions to avoid getting the relaxer on your skin.

Can I use hair relaxer if I have damaged hair?

It is not recommended to use hair relaxer if you have damaged hair, as it can make the damage worse. Instead, focus on repairing your hair with deep conditioning treatments and avoiding heat styling tools.

Hair Care After Relaxing

After relaxing your hair, it's important to take extra care to keep it healthy and moisturized. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, or use a heat protectant spray if you must.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can further damage it.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, the question of whether or not it's permissible to use hair relaxer in Islam is a complex one. While there is no clear consensus, it's important to make an informed decision based on your own interpretation of Islamic teachings and take the necessary precautions to avoid damaging your hair or scalp.
