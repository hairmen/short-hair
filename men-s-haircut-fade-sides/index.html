Men s Haircut Fade Sides A Guide To The Perfect Look

Are you looking for a trendy and stylish men's haircut that will make you stand out in a crowd? Look no further than the fade sides haircut. This hairstyle has been popular for years, and it's not going away anytime soon. In this guide, we'll take you through everything you need to know about the men's haircut fade sides, from how to get it to hair care tips to maintain its look.

How to Get a Men's Haircut Fade Sides

The men's haircut fade sides is a versatile hairstyle that can be worn by all hair types and lengths. The key to achieving this look is to have a skilled barber who knows how to create the perfect fade. Here's how to get it:

Step 1: Choose the Right Length

Before your barber starts cutting your hair, you need to decide on the length of your fade. The fade can start from the skin or gradually blend into a longer length. You can choose between a low, mid, or high fade depending on your preference and face shape.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clippers

Your barber will use different clippers to achieve the perfect fade. A shorter clipper will be used for the sides and back of your head, while a longer clipper will be used for the top. Make sure your barber has the right equipment to achieve the perfect fade.

Step 3: Communicate with Your Barber

Communication is key when it comes to getting the perfect men's haircut fade sides. Make sure you communicate with your barber about the length and style you want. Your barber should also be able to recommend the best fade for your face shape.

Tips for Maintaining the Men's Haircut Fade Sides

Now that you have the perfect men's haircut fade sides, it's important to maintain its look. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking sharp:

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

Using the right hair products is essential for maintaining the men's haircut fade sides. Use a pomade or wax to style your hair and keep it in place throughout the day. Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Tip 2: Schedule Regular Haircuts

To maintain the perfect fade, you need to schedule regular haircuts every two to three weeks. This will prevent your hair from growing out and ruining the fade. Your barber can also trim any stray hairs and touch up the fade.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from Damage

Protecting your hair from damage is important for maintaining the men's haircut fade sides. Avoid using hot tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, as they can damage your hair and make it look frizzy. Use a heat protectant spray if you must use hot tools.

FAQ

Q: Is the men's haircut fade sides suitable for all face shapes?

Yes, the men's haircut fade sides is suitable for all face shapes. Your barber can recommend the best fade for your face shape.

Q: Can I style the men's haircut fade sides differently?

Yes, you can style the men's haircut fade sides differently depending on your preference. You can slick it back, comb it over, or add texture to the top.

Q: Can I get the men's haircut fade sides if I have curly hair?

Yes, the men's haircut fade sides is suitable for all hair types, including curly hair. Your barber can recommend the best fade for your hair type.

Hair Care

The men's haircut fade sides is a stylish and trendy hairstyle that requires regular maintenance. Follow these hair care tips to keep your hair looking sharp:

Tip 1: Shampoo and Condition Regularly

Shampoo and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Tip 2: Avoid Over-washing

Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils and make it look dry and frizzy. Wash your hair every two to three days to keep it clean and healthy.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protecting your hair from the sun is important for maintaining its health and preventing damage. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF when you're outside for extended periods.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve the perfect men's haircut fade sides and maintain its look for weeks to come. Remember to communicate with your barber and use the right products to keep your hair looking sharp.
