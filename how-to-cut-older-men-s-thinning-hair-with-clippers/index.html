How To Cut Older Men s Thinning Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 29, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're an older man with thinning hair, you may be wondering how to cut your hair with clippers. Clippers are a great tool for cutting hair at home, especially when you can't get to a barber. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair with clippers, as well as some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Description

Thinning hair is a natural part of aging, and many older men experience it. However, this doesn't mean that you have to live with a bad haircut. With the right tools and techniques, you can cut your hair at home and still look great.

How to

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few supplies. You'll need a pair of clippers, a comb, and a mirror. You may also want to have a towel or cape to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Once you have your supplies, follow these steps:

Step 1: Choose the Right Clipper Guard

The first step in cutting your hair is to choose the right clipper guard. The guard determines how short your hair will be cut. If you're unsure which guard to use, start with a longer guard and work your way down until you find the right length.

Step 2: Start at the Sides

Start cutting your hair at the sides of your head. Use the clipper to trim the hair from the bottom of your ear to the top of your head. Work your way around the sides of your head, making sure to keep the clipper level.

Step 3: Move to the Back

Once you've finished the sides, move to the back of your head. Use the clipper to trim the hair from the nape of your neck to the top of your head. Again, make sure to keep the clipper level.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Now it's time to cut the top of your head. Use the comb to lift the hair and the clipper to trim it to the desired length. Work your way from the front of your head to the back, making sure to keep the clipper level.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the top of your head, blend the hair from the sides and back. Use the clipper to trim any stray hairs and create a seamless transition between the different sections of your hair.

Step 6: Clean Up

Finally, use the clipper to clean up any stray hairs around your ears and the nape of your neck. Use the comb to brush away any loose hairs and check for any uneven spots.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down until you find the right length
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head
  • Don't press too hard with the clipper – let it do the work
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Consider asking a family member for help if you're unsure

FAQ

Can I cut my hair without a guard?

We don't recommend cutting your hair without a guard, as it can result in uneven and patchy hair. Always use a guard when cutting your hair with clippers.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always use a longer guard to even out any uneven spots or wait for your hair to grow back.

How often should I cut my hair?

How often you cut your hair depends on your personal preference and hair growth rate. We recommend cutting your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain a neat and tidy look.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as hairdryers and straighteners, too often
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to promote hair growth
  • Consider using a hair growth serum or supplement

Cutting your hair with clippers can be a great way to save time and money. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut at home. Follow our step-by-step guide, use our tips and tricks, and take care of your hair to maintain a healthy and stylish look.
