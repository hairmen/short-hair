How To Thin Out Bangs With Thinning Shears A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Oct 04, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're tired of your bangs looking too bulky and heavy, thinning them out with thinning shears can be a great solution. However, it's important to know the proper technique to avoid any mishaps. In this guide, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to thin out bangs with thinning shears.

How to Thin Out Bangs with Thinning Shears

Before we get into the step-by-step process, let's first talk about what thinning shears are and why they're useful. Thinning shears are a type of scissors with razor-sharp teeth that cut some hairs while leaving others intact. This allows you to remove bulk from your hair without compromising the overall length. Here's how to thin out bangs with thinning shears:

Step 1: Start with Dry Hair

It's important to work with dry hair when using thinning shears. Wet hair can stretch and look longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven results. Blow-dry your bangs straight before starting.

Step 2: Section Your Bangs

Using a comb, section off the front part of your hair that you want to thin out. Clip the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Hold Your Thinning Shears Correctly

Thinning shears have a specific way of being held. You want to hold them with the teeth pointing upwards and the handle pointing downwards. This allows the teeth to do the cutting while the handle guides the shears.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting at the roots, take a small section of hair in your non-dominant hand and use your thinning shears to cut into the hair. Work in small sections and don't cut too much hair at once. Remember, you can always go back and cut more if needed.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After cutting a few sections, step back and check your progress in a mirror. Make sure your bangs still look balanced and even. If you need to make any adjustments, go back and cut a bit more.

Tips for Thinning Out Bangs with Thinning Shears

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when thinning out your bangs:

  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's easier to go back and cut more if needed.
  • Use sharp thinning shears. Dull shears can damage your hair and lead to split ends.
  • Work in small sections for more control.
  • Step back and check your progress frequently to avoid any mishaps.

FAQ About Thinning Out Bangs with Thinning Shears

Can you thin out bangs with regular scissors?

You can technically thin out bangs with regular scissors, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors can create uneven layers and lead to split ends.

How often should you thin out your bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how thick your bangs are. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good time frame for thinning out your bangs.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Bangs with Thinning Shears

After thinning out your bangs, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a nourishing hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Thinning out your bangs with thinning shears can be a great way to achieve a more manageable and stylish look. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a perfect result every time.
