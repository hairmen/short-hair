Starting Dreads With Natural Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Are you interested in starting dreadlocks with your natural hair but don't know where to begin? Look no further! This article will provide you with all the information you need to know about starting dreads with natural hair. From the initial process to hair care tips, we've got you covered.

Description

Dreadlocks, commonly known as dreads or locks, are a hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are formed by letting your hair grow and tangle together. Starting dreads with natural hair is a process that involves allowing your hair to lock together over time. This process can take anywhere from several months to a few years, depending on your hair type, length, and maintenance routine.

How to Start Dreads with Natural Hair

Starting dreads with natural hair involves two main methods: the freeform method and the twist and rip method.

The Freeform Method

The freeform method involves letting your hair grow and tangle naturally without any manipulation. This method is ideal for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle and do not want to spend time twisting or braiding their hair. With this method, your hair will form its own unique pattern and shape over time.

The Twist and Rip Method

The twist and rip method involves sectioning your hair and twisting each section to create a dreadlock. This method is ideal for those who want more control over the size and shape of their dreads. This method can be done with or without the use of a dread wax or locking gel.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with natural hair using the twist and rip method:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly with a residue-free shampoo.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using a comb or your fingers.
  3. Twist each section of hair in one direction until it tightens and begins to lock.
  4. Rip the twisted hair in the opposite direction to create a knot.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 for each section of hair.
  6. Use a dread wax or locking gel (optional) to help the hair lock together.
  7. Allow your hair to dry naturally without using a hair dryer.
  8. Repeat the twisting and ripping process every few weeks until your dreads have fully formed.

Tips for Starting and Maintaining Dreads

Here are some tips to help you start and maintain your dreads:

  • Be patient. The process of starting dreads with natural hair takes time and requires patience.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup in your dreads.
  • Regularly wash your hair to keep your scalp clean and healthy.
  • Avoid over-twisting or over-manipulating your dreads, as this can cause damage to your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads with natural hair:

Can I start dreads with short hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with short hair. However, it may take longer for your dreads to fully form.

Do I need to use products to start dreads?

No, you do not need to use products to start dreads. However, using a dread wax or locking gel can help the hair lock together more quickly.

Can I wash my dreads?

Yes, you can wash your dreads. In fact, it's important to regularly wash your hair to keep your scalp clean and healthy.

Hair Care for Dreads

Here are some tips for caring for your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using conditioner, as it can loosen your dreads.
  • Use a light oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, to moisturize your scalp.
  • Avoid using heavy products or excessive heat on your dreads.
  • Retwist your dreads every few weeks to maintain their shape and prevent unraveling.

Starting dreads with natural hair is a process that requires patience and dedication. With the right techniques and hair care routine, you can achieve beautiful, healthy dreads. Whether you choose the freeform method or the twist and rip method, remember to take good care of your hair and enjoy the journey!
