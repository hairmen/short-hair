Best Blonde Hair Color For Over 60 A Guide To Choosing The Right Shade

Written by William Nov 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're over 60 and looking for a hair color that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural beauty, blonde may be the way to go. However, with so many shades of blonde to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which one is best for you. In this guide, we'll explore the best blonde hair color for over 60 and offer tips on how to maintain your new look.

Description

As we age, our skin tone changes, and our hair may lose its natural pigment. Blonde hair can brighten up your face and give you a youthful glow. However, not all blonde shades work for everyone. It's important to choose a hue that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural features. The best blonde hair color for over 60 is one that is not too bright or too dark but falls somewhere in between.

How to Choose the Right Shade

Choosing the right blonde shade for your skin tone can be a bit tricky. Here are some tips to help you decide:

Consider Your Skin Tone

If you have a warm skin tone, choose a blonde shade with golden or honey undertones. If you have a cool skin tone, choose a blonde shade with ashy or silver undertones. If you're not sure what your skin tone is, hold up different shades of blonde to your face to see which one complements your skin the best.

Look at Your Natural Hair Color

If you have light hair, you may be able to go for a lighter blonde shade. If you have dark hair, you may need to lighten your hair first before going blonde. A professional hair stylist can help you determine the best course of action.

Consider Your Lifestyle

If you're an active person who spends a lot of time outdoors, you may want to choose a low-maintenance blonde shade that won't require frequent touch-ups. If you have a more relaxed lifestyle, you may be able to go for a higher-maintenance shade.

Step by Step Guide

Once you've chosen the right blonde shade for you, it's time to make the change. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Consult with a Professional Stylist

A professional stylist can help you determine the best blonde shade for your skin tone and natural hair color. They can also help you achieve the look you want without damaging your hair.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

If you have dark hair, you may need to lighten it first before going blonde. This may require multiple sessions and can be damaging to your hair if not done correctly. Your stylist will prepare your hair for the lightening process and ensure that your hair remains healthy.

Step 3: Apply the Blonde Hair Color

Once your hair is prepped, your stylist will apply the blonde hair color. They will ensure that the color is applied evenly and that the shade complements your skin tone.

Step 4: Rinse and Style Your Hair

After the color is applied, it's time to rinse and style your hair. Your stylist will ensure that your hair is moisturized and styled to your liking.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Look

Now that you have your new blonde hair color, it's important to maintain it. Here are some tips to help you keep your hair looking its best:

Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for color-treated hair. This will help keep your hair looking vibrant and healthy.

Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can damage your hair and cause your color to fade more quickly. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Get Regular Trims

Regular trims will help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. This will also help your color look more vibrant.

FAQ

Can I Dye My Hair Blonde at Home?

We recommend that you have your hair dyed blonde by a professional stylist. Lightening your hair can be damaging if not done correctly, and a stylist can ensure that your hair remains healthy.

How Often Should I Get My Hair Colored?

This will depend on your hair type, the shade of blonde you choose, and your lifestyle. Generally, you should plan on getting your hair colored every 4-6 weeks to maintain your new look.

Hair Care

To keep your new blonde hair color looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips to help you keep your hair healthy:

Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

A deep conditioning treatment can help keep your hair moisturized and prevent damage. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair looking healthy.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can damage your hair and cause your color to fade more quickly. Wear a hat or use a hair protectant spray when you're spending time outdoors.

Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers. These can damage your hair and cause your color to fade more quickly.

In conclusion, choosing the right blonde hair color for over 60 can be a bit overwhelming, but with the right guidance and care, you can achieve a beautiful new look. Consult with a professional stylist, choose a shade that complements your skin tone, and take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
