How To Cut Layers In Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting layers in your hair is a great way to add volume, texture, and movement to your hairstyle. It’s also a popular choice for those who want to switch up their look without going for a drastic chop. In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps of cutting layers in your hair, along with some tips and FAQs to ensure you get the best results possible.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers in Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair
  • FAQs about Cutting Layers in Hair
  • Hair Care after Cutting Layers

How to Cut Layers in Hair

Before you start cutting layers in your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off your hair. It’s also a good idea to have a mirror set up so you can see the back of your head.

The first thing you need to do is decide how many layers you want and where you want them to start. If you’re not sure, it’s best to start with a few long layers around your face and gradually work your way up to shorter layers at the crown of your head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section off your hair

Using your comb, section off your hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start cutting

Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and cut at a slight angle to create a soft, layered effect. Repeat this process with each section, gradually working your way up to the top section.

Step 4: Blend the layers

Once you’ve cut all the sections, it’s time to blend the layers together. Take a comb and run it through your hair to blend the layers and create a seamless look.

Step 5: Check the length

Once you’ve blended the layers, check the length of your hair. If you need to trim any stray hairs or adjust the length, do so now.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up, rather than cutting large chunks of hair all at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut and avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don’t cut too much off at once. It’s better to start with a little and gradually cut more if needed.
  • If you’re not confident in your cutting skills, consider going to a professional stylist for a consultation.

FAQs about Cutting Layers in Hair

Can I cut layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, it’s important to keep in mind that curly hair tends to bounce up after it’s cut, so you may need to cut the layers longer than you want them to be.

How often should I cut my layered hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers lose their shape. As a general rule, it’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your layers.

What’s the best way to style layered hair?

Layered hair looks great when it’s styled with loose waves or curls. You can use a curling iron or hot rollers to create soft, bouncy curls, or scrunch your hair with a curl-enhancing product for a more natural look.

Hair Care after Cutting Layers

After cutting layers in your hair, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Use a shampoo and conditioner that’s suited for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. You can also use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair nourished and hydrated.

Cutting layers in your hair is a great way to add some dimension and movement to your hairstyle. With the right tools and some basic know-how, you can achieve a layered look that’s perfect for you.
