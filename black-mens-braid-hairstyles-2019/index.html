In this article, we will be discussing the latest trends and styles for black men's braid hairstyles in 2019, as well as tips on how to achieve them and how to care for your hair.

Trends for Black Men's Braid Hairstyles in 2019

One of the biggest trends for black men's braid hairstyles in 2019 is the use of intricate patterns and designs. These can range from simple cornrows to more complex styles like box braids and twist braids. Another popular trend is the use of hair accessories like beads and rings to add some flair and personality to your look.

Another trend that has been gaining popularity is the use of natural hair colors like black, brown, and dark blonde. This trend is all about embracing your natural hair texture and color, and creating a style that is unique to you.

How to Achieve Black Men's Braid Hairstyles

The first step in achieving black men's braid hairstyles is to determine which style you want to go for. There are many different styles to choose from, so it's important to do your research and find one that suits your face shape and personal style.

Once you have chosen a style, you will need to gather the necessary tools and supplies. This will typically include a comb, hair gel or pomade, and hair ties or rubber bands.

Step-by-Step Guide to Creating Black Men's Braid Hairstyles

Here is a step-by-step guide to creating black men's braid hairstyles:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair to remove any dirt or buildup. Towel-dry your hair until it is damp but not soaking wet.
  2. Using a comb, part your hair into sections. The size of the sections will depend on the style you are going for.
  3. Apply hair gel or pomade to each section to help keep the hair in place and make it easier to braid.
  4. Begin braiding each section of hair according to the style you have chosen. This may involve creating simple cornrows, twist braids, or box braids.
  5. Continue braiding until all sections of hair have been braided.
  6. Secure the ends of each braid with a hair tie or rubber band.
  7. Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade to your scalp to help keep your hair in place and prevent frizz.

Tips for Maintaining Black Men's Braid Hairstyles

Here are some tips for maintaining black men's braid hairstyles:

  • Keep your scalp clean and moisturized by washing your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like curling irons and straighteners, as these can damage your hair and cause breakage.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your braids, as this can cause them to loosen and fall out.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent your hair from drying out and becoming frizzy while you sleep.

FAQs About Black Men's Braid Hairstyles

Here are some frequently asked questions about black men's braid hairstyles:

Q: How long can I keep my braids in?

A: This will depend on the style of braids you have and how well you care for them. Generally, braids can be left in for anywhere from two to six weeks.

Q: Can I wash my hair while I have braids in?

A: Yes, you can wash your hair while you have braids in. It's important to use a mild shampoo and to be gentle when washing your hair to avoid loosening the braids.

Q: Can I get braids if my hair is short?

A: Yes, you can still get braids if your hair is short. However, you may need to have some length added with extensions in order to achieve the desired style.

Hair Care Tips for Black Men's Braid Hairstyles

Here are some additional hair care tips for black men's braid hairstyles:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heavy oils and products that can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or applying a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

With the right care and maintenance, black men's braid hairstyles can be a stylish and practical choice for any man. Whether you opt for simple cornrows or more complex twist braids, these styles are sure to turn heads and make a statement.
