How To Get Red Undertones Out Of Blonde Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Nov 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of seeing red undertones in your blonde hair? This can be a common problem, especially if you have naturally dark hair or have recently dyed your hair blonde. But don't worry, there are several ways to get rid of those pesky red tones and achieve the perfect blonde shade. In this article, we'll discuss how to get red undertones out of blonde hair, step by step.

What Causes Red Undertones in Blonde Hair?

Before we dive into the solution, it's important to understand what causes red undertones in blonde hair. The most common cause is the natural pigment in dark hair. When you bleach dark hair to make it blonde, the pigment can turn reddish-orange, which can give your blonde hair a warm, brassy tone. Another cause could be the type of dye you're using, as some blonde dyes have warmer undertones than others.

How to Get Red Undertones Out of Blonde Hair

1. Use a Purple Shampoo

One of the easiest ways to get rid of red undertones in blonde hair is by using a purple shampoo. Purple is opposite to yellow on the color wheel, which means it can neutralize yellow and warm undertones in your hair. When you use a purple shampoo, it can help cancel out any brassiness or red tones in your blonde hair.

2. Tone Your Hair

If your blonde hair has a lot of red undertones, you may need to tone it. Toning your hair involves using a hair dye that's the opposite of the undertones you want to get rid of. For example, if you have red undertones, you'll need a green-toned dye. You can either go to a salon to get your hair toned or do it yourself at home with a toning kit.

3. Use a Hair Mask

Another way to get rid of red undertones in blonde hair is by using a hair mask. Hair masks are deep conditioning treatments that can help nourish your hair and neutralize any warm tones. Look for a hair mask that's specifically designed for blonde hair and has ingredients like keratin and amino acids.

4. Try a Color-Depositing Treatment

A color-depositing treatment is a temporary hair dye that can help neutralize any red or warm undertones in your blonde hair. You can find color-depositing treatments in shades like blue, purple, and green, which can help cancel out any unwanted tones. Just be sure to follow the instructions carefully, as some color-depositing treatments can be quite strong.

Tips for Maintaining Blonde Hair

Now that you know how to get rid of red undertones in blonde hair, it's important to maintain your blonde locks. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

1. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause your blonde hair to become dry and brittle. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that's designed for blonde hair to keep your locks looking healthy and shiny.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your blonde hair and cause it to become dry and frizzy. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your blonde hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair in top condition.

FAQ

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on my blonde hair?

Yes, you can use regular shampoo on your blonde hair, but it's best to use a shampoo that's specifically designed for blonde hair. Regular shampoo can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.

Q: How often should I wash my blonde hair?

It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash your hair every day or every other day. If you have dry hair, you may only need to wash your hair once or twice a week.

Hair Care for Blonde Hair

Taking care of blonde hair requires a little extra effort, but it's worth it for the beautiful results. Here are a few tips for caring for your blonde locks:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Blonde hair can become dry and brittle, so it's important to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized. Look for a treatment that's specifically designed for blonde hair and has ingredients like keratin and amino acids.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can cause your blonde hair to become dry and damaged, so be sure to protect your hair with a hat or scarf when you're outside. You can also use a UV protectant spray to shield your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

3. Avoid Chlorine

Chlorine can turn blonde hair green, so it's best to avoid swimming in chlorine pools. If you must swim in a chlorinated pool, be sure to wear a swim cap or rinse your hair with fresh water after you swim.

By following these tips and using the methods we've discussed, you can get rid of red undertones in your blonde hair and keep your locks looking healthy and beautiful.
