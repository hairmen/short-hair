Pixie Hair Styles For Over 60S A Guide To Looking Fabulous

Written by Frank Oct 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and low-maintenance hairdo that's perfect for women over 60, you should definitely consider a pixie cut. This timeless haircut is versatile, easy to style, and can help you look and feel younger. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to rock a pixie hair style and answer some of the most common questions about this trendy look.

Why Pixie Hair Styles are Popular Among Women Over 60?

Pixie hair styles have been popular for decades, and for good reason. They're incredibly versatile, and can be customized to suit any face shape or hair texture. For women over 60, pixie cuts are especially popular because they're easy to maintain, require minimal styling, and can help to make them look and feel younger.

How to Choose the Right Pixie Hair Style for You?

The key to choosing the right pixie hair style is to find one that complements your face shape and hair texture. If you have a round face, for example, you may want to opt for a pixie with longer layers around the crown to help elongate your face. If you have fine hair, you may want to choose a choppy pixie with lots of texture to add volume and dimension.

Step-by-Step Guide to Creating a Pixie Hair Style

If you're ready to take the plunge and try out a pixie hair style, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Talk to your hairstylist: Before you make any drastic changes to your hair, it's important to talk to your hairstylist about what kind of pixie cut would work best for you. They can help you choose the right length, texture, and style based on your hair type and face shape.
  2. Prepare your hair: If you have long hair, you may need to cut it short before getting a pixie cut. It's also a good idea to wash and condition your hair before your appointment to make sure it's clean and easy to work with.
  3. Cut your hair: Once you're in the salon, your hairstylist will cut your hair to the desired length and style. They may use scissors or clippers, depending on the look you're going for.
  4. Style your hair: Once your hair is cut, your hairstylist will style it using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron. They may also use styling products like mousse, gel, or hairspray to help your hair hold its shape.

Tips for Maintaining Your Pixie Hair Style

While pixie hair styles are low-maintenance, they do require some upkeep to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips for maintaining your pixie cut:

  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking fresh and neat.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Invest in styling products like pomade or wax to help you create different looks.
  • Consider getting highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your hair.

FAQs About Pixie Hair Styles for Over 60s

Here are some of the most common questions people have about pixie hair styles for women over 60:

  • Are pixie hair styles difficult to maintain?
  • No, pixie hair styles are actually very low-maintenance. They require minimal styling and can be easily maintained with regular trims.
  • Do I need to have a certain face shape to rock a pixie cut?
  • No, pixie hair styles can be customized to suit any face shape. Your hairstylist can help you choose a cut that complements your features.
  • Can I still wear hair accessories with a pixie cut?
  • Yes, you can still wear hair accessories like headbands, clips, and barrettes with a pixie cut. You may need to experiment with different styles to find ones that work best with your hair length and texture.

Caring for Your Pixie Hair Style

Caring for your pixie hair style is easy. You'll want to keep it clean and conditioned by washing it regularly and using a good quality shampoo and conditioner. You should also avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and dryness. Instead, let your hair air dry whenever possible or use a diffuser on your blow dryer to minimize heat damage. Finally, don't forget to schedule regular trims with your hairstylist to keep your pixie cut looking fresh and stylish.
