How To Get Messy Hair Guys A Relaxed Guide

Are you tired of your hair looking too neat and tidy? Do you want to achieve that effortless, messy hair look that guys seem to effortlessly pull off? You've come to the right place! How to Get Messy Hair Guys

First things first, let's talk about what we mean by "messy hair." Messy hair is a casual, laid-back hairstyle that looks effortless and undone. It's all about creating texture and movement in your hair. Here are the steps to achieve the look:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you can style your hair, you need to make sure it's clean. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. Avoid using conditioner, as it can weigh down your hair and make it harder to create the messy look.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Next, apply a styling product to your hair. We recommend using a matte paste or clay, as these products will give you a natural, textured look. Work the product through your hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends.

Step 3: Create Texture

Now it's time to create some texture in your hair. Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create some volume. You can also use a comb or brush to create some definition and separation.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

To keep your hair in place and add some extra texture, finish with a light-hold hairspray. Spray a small amount over your hair and use your fingers to tousle it one last time.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of how to get messy hair guys can rock, let's break it down step-by-step:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. Avoid using conditioner, as it can weigh down your hair and make it harder to style.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair until it's damp. Don't rub your hair vigorously, as this can cause damage and frizz.

Step 3: Apply Styling Product

Apply a dime-sized amount of styling product to your hair. We recommend using a matte paste or clay, as these products will give you a natural, textured look. Work the product through your hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends.

Step 4: Create Texture

Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create some volume. You can also use a comb or brush to create some definition and separation.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your hair in place and add some extra texture. Spray a small amount over your hair and use your fingers to tousle it one last time.

Tips for Getting Messy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect messy hair look:

  • Use a hairdryer on a low heat setting to add more volume and texture to your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it harder to style.
  • Consider getting a haircut that's easy to style and maintain, such as a short, textured cut.

FAQs

How often should I wash my hair to achieve the messy look?

We recommend washing your hair every 2-3 days to maintain its natural oils and texture.

What products should I use for messy hair?

We recommend using a matte paste or clay for a natural, textured look. Avoid using products with a shiny finish, as they can make your hair look greasy.

Can I achieve the messy look with long hair?

Absolutely! Messy hair looks great on all hair lengths, from short to long.

Hair Care for Messy Hair

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to follow a good hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Apply a conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve the perfect messy hair look in no time. Remember, it's all about creating texture and movement in your hair, so don't be afraid to experiment and have fun with it. Happy styling!
