How To Start Dreads With Short Natural Hair

If you have short natural hair and are thinking about starting dreadlocks, you may be wondering where to begin. Starting dreadlocks with short hair can be a bit challenging, but with the right techniques and patience, you can achieve the look you want. In this article, we will discuss how to start dreads with short natural hair, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Start Dreads with Short Natural Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Instructions

  4. FAQs about Starting Dreads with Short Hair

How to Start Dreads with Short Natural Hair

The first step to starting dreadlocks with short hair is to let your hair grow out a bit. This will make it easier to twist and lock your hair. You should aim to have at least 2-3 inches of hair before starting your dreadlocks.

Once your hair has grown out, you can start the process of twisting and locking your hair. There are several methods you can use to start your dreadlocks, including the backcombing method, the twist and rip method, and the crochet method.

The backcombing method involves teasing the hair at the roots and then backcombing it towards the scalp. This method can be quite time-consuming and may not work well on short hair.

The twist and rip method involves twisting small sections of hair and then ripping them apart. This method can be more effective on short hair, as it doesn't require as much length to start the locks.

The crochet method involves using a crochet hook to pull hair through itself, creating knots that will eventually form into dreadlocks. This method can be very effective on short hair, but it can also be quite damaging if not done properly.

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Here is a step-by-step guide to starting dreadlocks with short natural hair:

  1. Let your hair grow out to at least 2-3 inches in length.
  2. Choose a method for starting your dreadlocks (backcombing, twist and rip, or crochet).
  3. Section your hair into small, manageable sections.
  4. Start at the roots and work your way down, twisting and knotting each section of hair.
  5. Use a hair clip or rubber band to secure the end of each section.
  6. Repeat this process until all of your hair is locked.
  7. Use a residue-free locking gel or wax to help hold the locks in place.
  8. Keep your hair clean and free of buildup by washing it regularly.
  9. Be patient and allow your hair to naturally lock over time.
  10. Enjoy your new dreadlocks!

Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair:

Here are some tips to help you start your dreadlocks with short natural hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair before starting.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils, as they can make it harder for your hair to lock.
  • Consider using a locking accelerator spray to help speed up the locking process.
  • Be patient and allow your hair to naturally lock over time, as forcing it can cause damage.
  • Try to avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can also cause damage.

FAQs about Starting Dreads with Short Hair:

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with short natural hair:

  • How long does it take for dreadlocks to form? It can take anywhere from a few months to a year or more for your hair to fully lock into dreadlocks.
  • Can you start dreadlocks with very short hair? Yes, you can start dreadlocks with very short hair, but it may take longer for them to fully form.
  • What is the best method for starting dreadlocks with short hair? The twist and rip method is often the best method for starting dreadlocks with short natural hair.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks:

Proper hair care is important for maintaining healthy dreadlocks. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils, as they can cause buildup and make it harder for your hair to lock.
  • Use a residue-free locking gel or wax to help hold your locks in place.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage.
  • Be gentle when washing and styling your hair to avoid pulling or damaging your locks.

Starting dreadlocks with short natural hair can be a bit challenging, but with the right techniques and patience, you can achieve the look you want. Follow these tips and instructions to start your dreadlocks and maintain healthy, beautiful locks for years to come.
