How To Style Short Natural African Hair At Home

Written by Bardi Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Short natural African hair can be both beautiful and versatile. However, styling it can be a bit of a challenge, especially if you're used to longer hair. But with the right tools and techniques, you can create amazing hairstyles right at home. In this article, we'll share with you some tips and tricks on how to style short natural African hair at home.

How to Style Short Natural African Hair at Home

Before you start styling your short natural African hair, make sure you have the right tools and products. Here are some of the things you'll need:

  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Styling cream or gel
  • Hair clips
  • Bobby pins
  • Hair elastics

1. Wash your hair

The first step in styling your short natural African hair is to wash it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and make sure to rinse thoroughly. Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your hair and scalp. Instead, use lukewarm or cool water.

2. Detangle your hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots. Be gentle to avoid pulling or breaking your hair.

3. Apply styling cream or gel

Apply a small amount of styling cream or gel to your hair. Work it through your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly. This will help to define your curls and keep your hair in place.

4. Section your hair

Section your hair using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and style. Start by creating a part down the middle of your head, and then divide each side into two or three sections.

5. Style your hair

There are many different styles you can create with short natural African hair. Here are some ideas:

  • Bantu knots: Twist small sections of hair into tight knots, and secure them with bobby pins or hair elastics. Leave the knots in overnight, and then unravel them in the morning for beautiful, defined curls.
  • Twist-out: Divide your hair into small sections, and twist each section tightly. Leave the twists in overnight, and then unravel them in the morning for a beautiful, textured look.
  • Braids: Braid your hair into small or medium-sized sections, and secure the ends with hair elastics. Leave the braids in for a few days, and then unravel them for a beautiful, textured look.
  • Frohawk: Create a mohawk-style look by slicking the sides of your hair down with gel or cream, and leaving the middle section in a curly, voluminous fro.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style short natural African hair at home:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb.
  3. Apply a small amount of styling cream or gel to your hair.
  4. Section your hair using hair clips.
  5. Style your hair using one of the techniques described above.
  6. Finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Styling Short Natural African Hair

Here are some tips to help you style your short natural African hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your hair and scalp.
  • Be gentle when detangling your hair to avoid pulling or breaking it.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Apply styling cream or gel evenly throughout your hair to define your curls and keep your hair in place.
  • Section your hair to make it easier to work with and style.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Q: Can I style my short natural African hair without using heat?

A: Yes, there are many ways to style short natural African hair without using heat. Try using twists, braids, or Bantu knots to create beautiful, textured styles.

Q: How often should I wash my short natural African hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair once or twice a week, depending on your hair type and how much product you use.

Hair Care Tips for Short Natural African Hair

Here are some tips to keep your short natural African hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk bonnet or using a satin pillowcase.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or relaxers on your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can create beautiful styles for your short natural African hair right at home. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and experiment with different styles to find what works best for you!
