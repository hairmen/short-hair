Why Is My Hair Falling Out Bad

Written by Robby Dec 01, 2023 · 3 min read
Why Is My Hair Falling Out Bad? | Tips and Hair Care

Are you experiencing excessive hair fall lately? Losing a few strands of hair every day is normal, but if you're shedding more than usual, it could be a sign of an underlying problem. In this article, we'll discuss the reasons behind hair fall and how to prevent it.

Table of Contents

  • Reasons for Hair Fall
  • Prevention Tips
  • Hair Care Routine
  • FAQs

Reasons for Hair Fall

There are various reasons why your hair might be falling out excessively. Here are some of the most common causes:

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes during pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid issues can lead to hair fall. This happens due to the imbalance of hormones in the body.

Stress

Stress is one of the major causes of hair fall. When you're under stress, your body releases cortisol, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair fall.

Poor Nutrition

Your hair needs essential vitamins and minerals to grow and remain healthy. A diet lacking in nutrients can cause hair fall.

Chemical Treatments

Repeated use of chemicals, such as hair dyes, relaxers, and perms, can weaken the hair and lead to hair fall.

Medical Conditions

Medical conditions such as scalp infections, alopecia, and dermatitis can cause hair fall.

Prevention Tips

Here are some tips to prevent hair fall:

Eat a Balanced Diet

Make sure your diet includes essential nutrients such as protein, iron, and vitamins. Include leafy greens, nuts, and fish in your diet.

Reduce Stress

Practice stress-reducing activities such as meditation, yoga, or exercise.

Avoid Chemical Treatments

Avoid using chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes, relaxers, and perms.

Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle hair products that do not contain harsh chemicals.

Hair Care Routine

A good hair care routine can help prevent hair fall. Here are some steps to follow:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep your scalp clean and healthy.

Use a Conditioner

Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.

Massage Your Scalp

Massage your scalp regularly to increase blood flow and stimulate hair growth.

FAQs

1. Can hair fall be reversed?

Yes, hair fall can be reversed with proper care and treatment.

2. How much hair fall is normal?

It's normal to lose around 50-100 strands of hair every day.

3. Can stress lead to hair fall?

Yes, stress can lead to hair fall due to the release of cortisol in the body.

4. How long does it take for hair to grow back after hair fall?

It can take a few months for hair to grow back after hair fall.

In conclusion, hair fall can be caused by various factors, including hormonal changes, stress, poor nutrition, chemical treatments, and medical conditions. To prevent hair fall, follow a balanced diet, reduce stress, avoid chemical treatments, and use gentle hair products. A good hair care routine, including regular washing, conditioning, and scalp massage, can also help prevent hair fall. If your hair fall persists, consult a doctor or dermatologist.
