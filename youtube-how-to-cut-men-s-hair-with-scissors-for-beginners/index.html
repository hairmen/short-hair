Welcome to our beginner's guide on how to cut men's hair with scissors. Cutting hair can be intimidating, especially when you're new to it. But with the right tools and techniques, you can learn to cut hair like a pro. In this tutorial, we'll show you how to use scissors to give a simple haircut to a male client. We'll cover everything from preparing the hair to finishing the haircut. Let's get started!

Description

Before you start cutting men's hair with scissors, it's important to have a few things on hand. You'll need a pair of hairdressing scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. You'll also need a cape or towel to drape over your client's shoulders to prevent hair from falling on their clothes. It's also helpful to have a mirror so your client can see their haircut as you work.

How To Cut Men's Hair With Scissors For Beginners

When cutting men's hair with scissors, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be harder to cut and style, so make sure your client's hair is completely dry before you start. If your client has long hair, you may need to trim it with clippers or scissors before you begin.

Step 1: Section The Hair

Using your comb, section your client's hair into four parts: top, sides, back, and nape. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate and make it easier to cut the hair.

Step 2: Cut The Hair On The Sides

Starting with the sides, comb the hair down and use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Use your comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle to create a natural-looking finish. Repeat on the other side.

Step 3: Cut The Hair On The Back

Next, move on to the back of the head. Comb the hair straight down and use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Again, use your comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle for a natural-looking finish.

Step 4: Cut The Hair On The Top

Now it's time to cut the hair on the top of the head. Comb the hair straight up and use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Use your fingers to pull the hair forward and cut it at an angle to create a textured, layered look.

Step 5: Blend The Hair

Use your scissors to blend the hair on the sides and the back of the head. Use your comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle to create a seamless transition between the different sections of hair.

Step 6: Finish The Haircut

Once you've cut all the hair to the desired length, it's time to finish the haircut. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a more polished look. Use your comb to check for any uneven areas and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips For Cutting Men's Hair With Scissors

Here are a few tips to help you cut men's hair with scissors like a pro:

  • Start with clean, dry hair.
  • Use hairdressing scissors, not regular scissors.
  • Section the hair to make it easier to cut.
  • Use your comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle.
  • Blend the hair to create a seamless transition between sections.
  • Trim any stray hairs to create a polished look.

FAQs About Cutting Men's Hair With Scissors

What kind of scissors should I use to cut men's hair?

You should use hairdressing scissors, which are specifically designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors can damage the hair and make it harder to cut.

Do I need to wet the hair before cutting it with scissors?

No, it's best to cut hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be harder to cut and style, and it can also make the hair look shorter than it actually is.

How do I know what length to cut the hair?

You should ask your client what length they want their hair to be. It's also helpful to have pictures or examples of different hairstyles to show your client so they can choose the look they want.

Hair Care After Cutting Men's Hair With Scissors

After cutting men's hair with scissors, it's important to give your client some hair care tips. Advise them to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and to avoid using heat styling tools too often. Encourage them to come back for regular trims to keep their hair looking neat and tidy.

Cutting men's hair with scissors can be a little intimidating at first, but with practice, you'll soon be able to give great haircuts. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
