Why Is Top Layer Curly Hair Frizzy

Written by Robby Nov 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look gorgeous and voluminous, it can also be prone to frizz. One of the most common complaints from those with curly hair is that the top layer tends to be particularly frizzy. In this article, we'll explore why this happens and what you can do to combat it.

Description: Understanding Frizz

Before we dive into why the top layer of curly hair tends to be frizzy, it's important to understand what causes frizz in the first place. Essentially, frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of the hair becomes raised, allowing moisture to enter and disrupt the hair's natural pattern. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, heat damage, and over-manipulation of the hair.

How to: Combatting Frizz

If you're dealing with frizz in your curly hair, don't worry â there are plenty of things you can do to combat it. Here are a few tips:

Step 1: Moisturize

One of the main causes of frizz is dryness, so it's important to keep your hair moisturized. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week and consider incorporating a leave-in conditioner into your routine.

Step 2: Avoid Over-Manipulation

The more you touch your hair, the more likely it is to become frizzy. Avoid over-brushing or combing your hair, and try to minimize heat styling as much as possible.

Step 3: Use the Right Products

Not all hair products are created equal, and some can actually make frizz worse. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, and avoid anything that contains alcohol or sulfates.

Tips: Taming the Top Layer

Now that you understand the basics of frizz, let's talk about why the top layer of curly hair tends to be particularly prone to it. Here are a few tips for taming that pesky top layer:

Tip 1: Use a Diffuser

When blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat more evenly and prevent the top layer from becoming too frizzy.

Tip 2: Try a Silk Scarf

Wrap a silk scarf around your head at night to help protect your hair and prevent the top layer from becoming too frizzy while you sleep.

Tip 3: Consider a Haircut

If your top layer is particularly unruly, consider getting a haircut that will help distribute the volume more evenly throughout your hair.

FAQ: Common Questions About Curly Hair

Q: Why does curly hair frizz more than straight hair?

A: Curly hair is naturally more prone to frizz because the cuticle layer is more raised, making it easier for moisture to enter and disrupt the hair's natural pattern.

Q: Can I use a straightening iron to combat frizz?

A: While a straightening iron can temporarily smooth out frizz, it can also cause heat damage and make the problem worse in the long run. It's better to focus on using products and techniques that are specifically designed for curly hair.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It varies depending on your hair type and personal preferences, but most people with curly hair find that washing every 2-3 days is sufficient. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.

Hair Care: Taking Care of Your Curly Hair

Ultimately, the key to combatting frizz in the top layer of curly hair is to take good care of your hair. Make sure you're using the right products, avoiding over-manipulation, and keeping your hair moisturized. And remember â embracing your natural curls is always the best option!
