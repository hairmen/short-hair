How To Cut Hair With Clippers For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of spending money at a hair salon every few weeks? Or maybe you just want to try out a new hairstyle? Whatever your reason, cutting your own hair with clippers can be a satisfying and cost-effective solution. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut hair with clippers for beginners, including tips and hair care advice.

How to Cut Hair with Clippers

Cutting hair with clippers is a quick and easy process. Clippers are electric devices that come with various guard sizes to help you achieve different hair lengths. Here are the basic steps:

  1. Choose the right guard size for the length you want to cut.
  2. Attach the guard to the clippers.
  3. Turn on the clippers and start cutting from the bottom of your hairline, moving upwards.
  4. Follow the natural curve of your head to ensure an even cut.
  5. Repeat the process until you've cut all the hair to the desired length.
  6. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers may have missed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, let's dive into a more detailed step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

You'll need a few items to get started:

  • Clippers
  • Guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Cape or towel
  • Mirror

Step 2: Choose the Right Guard Size

Guard sizes range from 0.5mm to 25mm, with each size representing a different hair length. It's important to choose the right size to achieve the desired length. Start with a longer guard and work your way down until you find the right length.

Step 3: Prepare Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair before cutting it. Comb it out and remove any tangles. If possible, have someone else help you by holding the mirror or cape/towel.

Step 4: Attach the Guard to the Clippers

Most clippers have a lever or button that allows you to detach and attach the guards. Slide the guard onto the clippers until it clicks into place. Make sure it's secure before turning on the clippers.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Begin at the bottom of your hairline, moving the clippers upwards. Follow the natural curve of your head to ensure an even cut. Don't rush and take your time. You can always go back and cut more if needed.

Step 6: Repeat the Process

Repeat the process until you've cut all the hair to the desired length. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want a shorter length.

Step 7: Trim Any Longer Hairs with Scissors

Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers may have missed. Comb out the hair and trim any uneven parts.

Tips for Cutting Hair with Clippers

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to achieve the desired length.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.
  • Take your time and don't rush.
  • Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers may have missed.
  • Ask someone for help to hold the mirror or cape/towel.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Clippers are designed specifically for cutting hair and come with guard sizes to help you achieve the desired length.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go back and cut more or even out the hair with scissors. If you're unsure, start with a longer guard and work your way down.

Hair Care after Cutting with Clippers

After cutting your hair with clippers, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a comb or brush to style it.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to keep it healthy and shiny.
  • Trim it regularly to maintain the desired length.

Cutting your hair with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Just remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully.
