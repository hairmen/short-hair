How To Cut Hair Easily At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your hair at home can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional hairdresser. However, with the right tools, technique, and a little bit of guidance, you can easily achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of how to cut hair easily at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare
  • How to Choose the Right Tools
  • How to Section Your Hair
  • How to Cut Your Hair
  • Tips for Different Hair Types
  • How to Clean Up
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Prepare

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare yourself and your workspace. Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow-drying it. This will make it easier to cut and style.

Next, find a well-lit area in your home with a mirror. Make sure you have all the necessary tools and products on hand, including scissors, a comb, sectioning clips, and a spray bottle filled with water.

How to Choose the Right Tools

When it comes to cutting hair, having the right tools is crucial. Use a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, which are specifically designed for cutting hair. Avoid using regular household scissors, as they can damage the hair and cause split ends.

Additionally, choose a comb that is wide-toothed and sturdy, as this will help you section your hair and create clean lines. Sectioning clips are also helpful for keeping your hair in place while you work.

How to Section Your Hair

To achieve an even haircut, it's important to section your hair. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: the top, sides, and back. Use sectioning clips to keep each section in place while you work.

Within each section, you can create smaller subsections using your comb. This will help you achieve more precision and control while you cut.

How to Cut Your Hair

Begin by cutting the hair in the back section. Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up. Take small sections of hair and hold them between your fingers, pulling them straight down. Use your scissors to cut at the desired length.

Continue working your way up the back section, then move on to the sides and top sections. Use your comb to create clean lines and ensure that the length is consistent throughout.

Tips for Different Hair Types

Depending on your hair type, you may need to adjust your cutting technique. For example, if you have curly hair, it's best to cut it dry rather than wet, as this will help you see how the curls will fall.

If you have straight hair, it's important to create texture by cutting small sections at an angle. This will prevent your hair from looking too blunt and flat.

How to Clean Up

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to clean up any stray hairs. Use a vacuum or broom to sweep up any hair on the floor, and wipe down your scissors and tools with a disinfectant wipe.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Q: Can I cut my own bangs?

A: Yes, but it's important to be cautious and start with small sections. It's always better to cut less than to cut too much.

Hair Care Tips

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. Additionally, consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily cut your hair at home and achieve a salon-worthy look. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun experimenting with different styles!
