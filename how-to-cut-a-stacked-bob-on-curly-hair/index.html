How to Cut a Stacked Bob on Curly Hair

A stacked bob is a haircut that is shorter in the back and longer in the front. It is a great option for those who want to add some volume and movement to their hair. If you have curly hair, you can still rock a stacked bob. Here are the steps to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.

Step 2: Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.

Step 3: Start cutting the back section. Using a comb, pull the hair straight down and cut it to the desired length. For a stacked bob, the back should be shorter than the front.

Step 4: Move on to the front sections. Pull the hair straight down and cut it at an angle. The length should gradually get longer towards the front.

Step 5: Blend the back and front sections by cutting the sides at an angle. This will create a seamless transition from the back to the front.

Step 6: Use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk from your hair. This will give your curls more movement and prevent your hair from looking too heavy.

Step 7: Style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron to define your curls or leave them natural.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Tip 1: Cut your hair when it is dry. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so cutting it wet might result in a shorter length than you intended.

Tip 2: Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. A comb will help you detangle your curls without causing frizz.

Tip 3: Cut your hair in small sections. This will help you achieve a more precise cut and prevent any mistakes.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my curly hair at home?

A: Yes, you can. However, if you are not confident in your cutting skills, it is best to go to a professional.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. As a general rule, it is recommended to get a trim every 8-12 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Tip 1: Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Tip 2: Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls moisturized.

Tip 3: Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to avoid frizz.

Cutting a stacked bob on curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a beautiful and trendy look. Remember to take care of your curls by following our hair care tips. Good luck!
