Should You Go Darker Or Lighter With Hair Color As You Age

Written by Frank Sep 17, 2023 · 5 min read

As we age, our hair naturally changes, losing pigment and becoming more fragile. This can leave us wondering whether we should stick with our natural color or switch things up with a new shade. The question of whether to go darker or lighter with hair color as you age is a common one, and there’s no one-size-fits-all answer. However, there are a few factors to consider when making your decision.

Why Does Hair Change as We Age?

As we age, our hair loses melanin, the pigment that gives it color. This can result in graying hair that can be coarse and wiry. Additionally, aging hair can become thinner and more fragile, making it more prone to breakage and damage. While these changes are natural, they can leave many people feeling self-conscious about their hair.

How to Decide Whether to Go Darker or Lighter

When it comes to choosing a hair color, there are a few factors to consider. Here are some tips:

Consider Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone can play a big role in determining which hair color will look best on you. If you have warm undertones, you may want to go for a warmer, darker color. If you have cool undertones, a cooler, lighter color may suit you better.

Think About Your Natural Color

When choosing a new hair color, it’s important to consider your natural color. If you’re going darker, you may want to stick with a shade that’s only a few shades darker than your natural color. If you’re going lighter, you may need to bleach your hair, which can be damaging.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle can also play a role in determining which hair color will work best for you. If you have a high-maintenance lifestyle, you may want to stick with a low-maintenance hair color that doesn’t require frequent touch-ups. If you’re looking for a dramatic change, you may need to invest more time and money in your hair color.

Step-by-Step Guide to Changing Your Hair Color

If you’ve decided to change your hair color, here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose Your Color

Start by choosing a hair color that will work with your skin tone and natural color.

Step 2: Gather Your Supplies

You’ll need hair dye, gloves, a brush, and a bowl for mixing the dye. You may also want to invest in a color-safe shampoo and conditioner.

Step 3: Protect Your Skin

Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline and on your ears to protect your skin from the dye.

Step 4: Mix the Dye

Follow the instructions on the hair dye package to mix the dye.

Step 5: Apply the Dye

Apply the dye to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down. Use the brush to distribute the dye evenly.

Step 6: Wait

Follow the instructions on the package for how long to wait before rinsing out the dye.

Step 7: Rinse and Condition

Rinse out the dye with warm water, then apply the conditioner that came with the dye. Rinse out the conditioner with cool water.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Color

Once you’ve changed your hair color, there are a few things you can do to keep it looking its best:

Use Color-Safe Products

Invest in a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help preserve your new hair color.

Avoid Heat

Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair and cause your color to fade more quickly.

Protect Your Hair

Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun and wind, which can cause damage and fading.

FAQ About Changing Hair Color As You Age

Is It Safe to Dye Your Hair?

Dyeing your hair is generally safe, but it can be damaging if done too frequently or if you don’t follow the instructions carefully.

Can I Dye My Hair at Home?

Yes, you can dye your hair at home, but be sure to follow the instructions carefully and use caution when handling the dye.

How Often Should I Touch Up My Hair Color?

This will depend on the type of hair color you have and how quickly your hair grows. Generally, you’ll need to touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Aging Hair

In addition to choosing the right hair color, there are a few things you can do to keep your aging hair looking its best:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won’t strip your hair of its natural oils.

Condition Regularly

Use a conditioner after every shampoo to help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Avoid Heat

Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair and make it more fragile.

Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Ultimately, the decision to go darker or lighter with your hair color as you age is a personal one. Consider your skin tone, natural color, and lifestyle when making your decision, and be sure to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
