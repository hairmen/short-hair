How To Cut Guys Hair Fade At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to save money on haircuts or just want to try your hand at cutting hair, learning how to cut a guy's hair fade at home can be a great option. Not only will you be able to customize the style to your liking, but you'll also have the satisfaction of knowing that you did it yourself. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieving a great fade haircut right in your own home.

Description

A fade haircut is a popular style for men that involves cutting the hair shorter and shorter as it gets closer to the bottom of the head. This creates a gradient effect that looks great on a variety of hair types and lengths. The key to achieving a good fade is to blend the different lengths smoothly, so that there are no harsh lines or visible transitions.

How to Cut Guys Hair Fade at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, you'll need to make sure you have all the necessary tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards in various sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Barber brush or soft-bristled brush
  • Mirror
  • Styling product (optional)

Step 2: Wash and Dry the Hair

To get the best results, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition the hair as usual, then towel dry it or use a blow dryer to get it completely dry. This will make it easier to see the different lengths and textures of the hair as you cut.

Step 3: Choose the Length and Guard Size

Decide on the length you want for the top of the hair, and choose a clipper guard that will achieve that length. Then, choose a guard size that is one or two sizes shorter than that for the sides and back of the head. For example, if you want the top to be 1 inch long, choose a #4 guard for the sides and back.

Step 4: Start Cutting the Sides and Back

Starting at the bottom of the head, use the longer guard size to cut the hair in an upward motion. You want to create a line where the shorter hair will start, so be sure to cut all the way around the head at this length. Then, switch to the shorter guard size and use it to cut the hair up to the line you just created. Use an upward motion and blend the hair as you go, being careful not to create any harsh lines.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Using scissors or clippers with the appropriate guard, cut the hair on top to the desired length. Be sure to blend the hair at the crown of the head so that there are no visible lines.

Step 6: Blend the Top and Sides

Using scissors or clippers without a guard, blend the hair between the top and sides. This will create a seamless transition between the different lengths.

Step 7: Clean Up the Neckline and Sideburns

Use the clippers or scissors to clean up the hair at the neckline and sideburns. You can create a straight line or a curved line, depending on your preference.

Step 8: Style the Hair (Optional)

If you like, you can use a styling product to give the hair some texture or hold. Just be sure not to use too much, as this can weigh down the hair and make it look greasy.

Tips

  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting hair can be tricky, so it's important to go slowly and carefully.
  • Use the mirrors to check your work from different angles.
  • Ask the person whose hair you're cutting for feedback throughout the process.
  • Watch online tutorials or read guides to get a better understanding of the technique.

FAQ

How long does a fade haircut last?

A fade haircut can last anywhere from 2-4 weeks, depending on how quickly the hair grows and how well it was blended.

Can you cut a fade with scissors?

Yes, you can cut a fade with scissors, but it may be more difficult to achieve a smooth blend without the use of clippers.

Do you need to wet the hair before cutting a fade?

No, you don't need to wet the hair before cutting a fade. In fact, it's better to start with dry hair so you can see the different lengths and textures more clearly.

Hair Care

To keep your fade looking fresh, it's important to maintain it regularly. This means getting it trimmed every few weeks and using a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy and moisturized. You can also use a styling product to enhance the texture and hold of the hair.

Learning how to cut guys hair fade at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a great-looking haircut that will save you time and money in the long run. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and practice regularly to perfect your skills.
