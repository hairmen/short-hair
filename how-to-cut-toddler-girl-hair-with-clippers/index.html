How To Cut Toddler Girl Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your toddler girl's hair with clippers can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. With the right tools and techniques, you can give your little one a stylish haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut toddler girl hair with clippers.

How to

Before you start cutting your toddler girl's hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Clippers guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Spray Bottle
  • Towel

Once you have all the tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Get Your Toddler Girl Ready

Make sure your toddler girl is comfortable and not fidgety. You can give her a toy or a snack to keep her occupied. Cover her with a towel to protect her clothes from hair clippings.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clippers Guard

The clippers guard number determines the length of the hair. Choose the right guard depending on the length you want to cut. Start with a longer guard and gradually go shorter if needed.

Step 3: Wet the Hair

Spray the hair with water using a spray bottle. Wetting the hair makes it easier to cut and reduces the chances of hair getting caught in the clippers.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start by cutting the hair on the sides and back of the head, moving the clippers in an upward motion. Make sure to use the guard and keep the clippers level to the head.

Step 5: Move to the Top

Once you finish cutting the sides and back, move to the top of the head. Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it using the clippers.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

Use scissors to blend the hair on the top and sides. This will give a more natural look to the haircut.

Step 7: Trim the Bangs

If your toddler girl has bangs, use scissors to trim them. Hold the hair between two fingers and cut it straight across.

Step 8: Clean Up

Use a brush to remove any hair clippings from your toddler girl's head and clothes.

Step 9: Style the Hair

Use a comb or your fingers to style your toddler girl's hair. You can add bows, clips or headbands for a cute and stylish look.

Tips

Here are some tips to make the haircutting process smoother:

  • Use a high-quality clipper and guard set.
  • Start with a higher guard number and work your way down.
  • Keep the clippers level to the head.
  • Use scissors to blend the hair for a more natural look.
  • Cut in a well-lit area.
  • Keep your toddler girl occupied with toys or snacks.

FAQ

How often should I cut my toddler girl's hair?

It depends on how fast her hair grows and the length of the haircut. On average, you can cut your toddler girl's hair every 6-8 weeks.

Can I cut my toddler girl's hair when she's asleep?

It's not recommended to cut your toddler girl's hair when she's asleep as it can be dangerous. She might move suddenly and get injured.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my toddler girl's hair?

If you make a mistake, don't worry. Hair grows back. You can take your toddler girl to a professional hairdresser to fix the mistake.

Hair Care

After the haircut, make sure to wash your toddler girl's hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair and avoid using a hair dryer as it can damage the hair.

Cutting your toddler girl's hair with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience. Follow these steps and tips to give your little one a stylish haircut from the comfort of your own home.
