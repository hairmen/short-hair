How To Style Thinning Natural Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Oct 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning hair can be a challenge for anyone, but it can be especially difficult for those with natural hair. Fortunately, there are plenty of styling options for those with thinning natural hair. Whether you're dealing with hair loss due to aging, genetics, or other factors, there are ways to style your hair so that it looks full and healthy. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for styling thinning natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Thinning Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Style Thinning Natural Hair

When it comes to styling thinning natural hair, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Choose styles that add volume to your hair.
  • Avoid styles that pull on your hair too tightly.
  • Use products that are gentle on your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles until you find what works for you.

Here are some styles to consider:

Braids

Braids are a great option for those with thinning natural hair. Not only do they add volume, but they can also help protect your hair from further damage. Try a simple three-strand braid, or experiment with more intricate styles like box braids or cornrows.

Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a classic natural hairstyle that can be a great option for those with thinning hair. They add volume to your hair and can be done on both short and long hair. Plus, they're a great way to keep your hair off your face.

Twist-Outs

A twist-out is a simple yet stylish way to add volume to your hair. Simply twist your hair in sections, allow it to dry, then unravel the twists for a full, voluminous look.

Wash-and-Go

A wash-and-go is a low-maintenance style that can be a great option for those with thinning hair. Simply wash your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner, and go. This style works best on hair that has some curl or wave to it.

Step-by-Step

Here's a step-by-step guide for creating a twist-out:

  1. Start with clean, detangled hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections.
  3. Apply a styling product (such as a curl cream or gel) to each section.
  4. Twist each section tightly and secure the ends.
  5. Allow your hair to dry completely (you can use a diffuser to speed up the process).
  6. Untwist each section and fluff your hair to create volume.

Tips

Here are some tips for styling thinning natural hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, which can further damage your hair.
  • Avoid styles that require tight braiding or pulling, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as this is less damaging than using a brush.
  • Experiment with different styles and products until you find what works for you.

FAQ

Q: Can I still straighten my hair if it's thinning?

A: It's best to avoid using heat styling tools on thinning hair, as this can further damage your hair. If you really want to straighten your hair, consider using a heat protectant spray and a low-heat setting on your styling tool.

Q: Will wearing a wig or weave damage my hair?

A: Wearing a wig or weave can be a great option for those with thinning hair, as it can help protect your hair from further damage. However, it's important to choose a wig or weave that is gentle on your hair and to avoid styles that are too tight or heavy.

Hair Care

In addition to styling your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips for caring for thinning natural hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for thinning hair.
  • Avoid using hot water, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Styling thinning natural hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can create a full, voluminous look that you'll love. Whether you prefer braids, twists, or a wash-and-go, there are plenty of options to choose from. Remember to take care of your hair by using gentle products and eating a healthy diet, and you'll be on your way to healthy, beautiful hair in no time.
