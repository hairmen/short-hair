How To Cut Your Short Hair Yourself Diy Haircut Tips And Tricks In 2023

Written by Frank Aug 20, 2023 · 4 min read
How to Cut Your Short Hair Yourself in 2023 | DIY Haircut Tips and Tricks

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially when it comes to short hairstyles. But with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable! In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut your short hair yourself, including tips and tricks for a successful DIY haircut. Whether you're looking to save money, avoid a trip to the salon, or simply enjoy the satisfaction of cutting your own hair, we've got you covered.

How to Cut Your Short Hair Yourself

The first step in cutting your short hair yourself is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror to see the back of your head. It's also a good idea to have some hair clips or elastics to help section off your hair.

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to decide on the length and style you want for your haircut. Look up some inspiration pictures online or in magazines, and consider your face shape and hair texture when choosing a style.

When you're ready to start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut accurately, and any styling products in your hair can make it harder to see the individual strands.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you're prepared, let's get started on the actual cutting process. Follow these steps to cut your short hair yourself:

  1. Start by sectioning off your hair. Use hair clips or elastics to create four sections: two on the top of your head, and two on the bottom. This will make it easier to cut your hair in even sections.
  2. Starting with the bottom sections, take a small section of hair between your fingers and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to trim the ends, cutting straight across. Repeat this process for all the bottom sections.
  3. Once the bottom sections are trimmed, move on to the top sections. Use your comb to lift a small section of hair straight up, and use your scissors to trim the ends. Work your way around your head, taking small sections and cutting them to the desired length.
  4. Finally, check the back of your head using the handheld mirror. If you notice any uneven spots, trim them carefully to even out your haircut.

DIY Haircut Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to keep in mind when cutting your short hair yourself:

  • Start with small cuts, and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut!
  • Invest in a good pair of haircutting scissors. Using regular household scissors can result in uneven cuts and damaged hair.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're struggling to see the back of your head, ask a friend or family member for assistance.
  • Take breaks if you need them. Cutting your hair can be tiring and time-consuming, so don't hesitate to take a break and come back to it later.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your short hair yourself:

Is it easy to cut your own short hair?

While cutting your own hair can be challenging, it's definitely doable with the right tools and techniques. It may take some practice and patience, but you can achieve a great DIY haircut.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic! You can always trim the other side to match or visit a professional stylist to fix the issue.

How often should I cut my short hair?

The frequency of your haircuts will depend on your individual hair growth and style. As a general rule, short haircuts should be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

Hair Care after Cutting Your Hair

After you've successfully cut your short hair yourself, it's important to take care of your new haircut. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated, and consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish your strands. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and prevent it from growing.

Cutting your short hair yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools, techniques, and mindset, you can achieve a great DIY haircut that you'll love. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and enjoy the process!
