How To Frame My Face With Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 23, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Welcome to our blog post on how to frame your face with bangs! Bangs are a great way to change up your look and add some extra style to your hair. However, it can be tricky to know how to properly frame your face with bangs. In this guide, we'll go over everything you need to know about framing your face with bangs, including how to, tips, FAQs, and hair care. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Frame Your Face with Bangs
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Perfect Bangs

How to Frame Your Face with Bangs

If you're new to bangs, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. The key is to choose a style that complements your face shape and hair type. Some popular bang styles include:

  • Straight bangs
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Wispy bangs
  • Textured bangs

When choosing a bang style, consider your face shape. For example, if you have a round face, you might want to opt for side-swept bangs to elongate your face. If you have a square face, wispy bangs can soften your features.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've chosen your bang style, it's time to frame your face. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use a comb to part your hair where you want your bangs to begin.
  2. Section off your bangs. Use a comb to separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.
  3. Cut your bangs. Use hair-cutting scissors to trim your bangs to the desired length. Start by cutting a little bit at a time and check your progress in the mirror.
  4. Style your bangs. Use a round brush and a blow dryer to style your bangs. If you want straight bangs, use a flat iron to smooth them.

Tips for Perfect Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when framing your face with bangs:

  • Don't cut your bangs too short. It's better to start with longer bangs and trim them as needed.
  • Choose a bang style that complements your face shape and hair type.
  • Use a sharp pair of hair-cutting scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Blow dry your bangs in the direction you want them to lay.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using a flat iron on your bangs.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about framing your face with bangs:

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look unkempt. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Can I cut my own bangs?

While it's possible to cut your own bangs, it's best to leave it to the professionals if you're not experienced. A stylist can help you choose the best bang style for your face shape and hair type.

What if I don't like my bangs?

If you don't like your bangs, don't worry! They will grow out eventually. In the meantime, you can style them in different ways to make them less noticeable.

Hair Care for Bangs

Now that you've framed your face with bangs, it's important to take care of them. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your bangs regularly to avoid oil buildup.
  • Blow dry your bangs after washing to prevent them from sticking to your forehead.
  • Avoid touching your bangs throughout the day to prevent oil buildup.
  • Use a dry shampoo between washes to keep your bangs looking fresh.

That's it! We hope you found this guide helpful. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to frame your face with bangs like a pro.
