How To Increase Volume In Fine Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Oct 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you have fine hair that lacks volume? You're not alone. Many people struggle with fine hair that falls flat and lacks body. Fortunately, there are several ways to increase volume in fine hair, from choosing the right products to using the right styling techniques. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you achieve the voluminous locks you've always wanted.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Increase Volume in Fine Hair

There are several things you can do to increase volume in fine hair:

1. Choose the right products

The right hair products can make a big difference when it comes to adding volume to fine hair. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for fine hair. These products are formulated to add body and lift to your hair without weighing it down.

2. Blow dry your hair upside down

One easy way to add volume to fine hair is to blow dry it upside down. This technique helps to lift the roots of your hair, creating volume and body. Use a round brush to add even more volume as you blow dry your hair.

3. Use a volumizing mousse or spray

Volumizing mousses and sprays can help to add body and lift to fine hair. Apply a small amount of mousse to your roots before blow drying your hair, or spray a volumizing spray onto your roots and gently massage it in with your fingertips.

4. Tease your hair

Teasing your hair can help to add volume and texture. To tease your hair, take small sections of hair and backcomb them with a teasing comb. Be sure to tease gently and avoid pulling too hard on your hair, as this can cause damage.

5. Use hot rollers

Hot rollers are a great way to add volume and curl to fine hair. Roll small sections of hair onto the hot rollers, then let them cool before removing them. Use your fingers to gently separate the curls and create volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to increasing volume in fine hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated to add volume to fine hair.

Step 2: Towel dry your hair.

Gently towel dry your hair, then apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots.

Step 3: Blow dry your hair upside down.

Use a blow dryer to dry your hair upside down, using a round brush to add even more volume.

Step 4: Tease your hair.

Take small sections of hair and tease them gently with a teasing comb.

Step 5: Use hot rollers.

Roll small sections of hair onto hot rollers and let them cool before removing them. Use your fingers to gently separate the curls and add volume.

Tips for Increasing Volume in Fine Hair

Here are some additional tips for increasing volume in fine hair:

1. Avoid heavy hair products.

Heavy hair products can weigh down fine hair, making it look flat and lifeless. Stick to lightweight products that are designed for fine hair.

2. Use a clarifying shampoo.

Buildup from hair products can also weigh down fine hair. Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup and keep your hair looking light and voluminous.

3. Embrace your natural texture.

If you have fine hair, you might be tempted to straighten it to create the illusion of volume. However, embracing your natural texture can actually make your hair look thicker and more voluminous.

FAQs

Q: Can cutting my hair help to increase volume?

A: Yes, a layered haircut can help to create the illusion of volume in fine hair.

Q: What's the best way to style fine hair for maximum volume?

A: Blow drying your hair upside down and using hot rollers are both great ways to add volume to fine hair.

Q: Can coloring my hair help to add volume?

A: Yes, adding highlights or lowlights can help to create the illusion of volume in fine hair.

Hair Care Tips for Fine Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for fine hair:

1. Don't over-wash your hair.

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it look flat and lifeless. Stick to washing your hair every other day or every few days.

2. Don't brush your hair too much.

Brushing your hair too much can cause breakage and make your hair look flat. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

3. Protect your hair from heat damage.

Using heat styling tools can cause damage to fine hair. Protect your hair by using a heat protectant spray before using hot rollers or a blow dryer.

By following these tips and tricks, you can increase volume in your fine hair and achieve the full, voluminous locks you've always wanted.
