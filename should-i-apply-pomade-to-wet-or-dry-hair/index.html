Should I Apply Pomade To Wet Or Dry Hair

Written by Daniel Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and sleek hairstyle, pomade can be a great option. But when it comes to applying pomade, one of the most common questions is whether to apply it to wet or dry hair. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of each method and help you decide which is best for you.

  2. Applying Pomade to Wet Hair
  3. Applying Pomade to Dry Hair

How to Apply Pomade

Pomade is a styling product that can help you achieve a wide range of hairstyles, from slicked-back looks to messy, textured styles. Before you apply pomade, it's important to choose the right product for your hair type and desired style. Some pomades are better suited for thin or fine hair, while others work best on thick or curly hair.

Once you have the right product, start by washing and drying your hair. If you're applying pomade to wet hair, towel dry your hair so that it's slightly damp. If you're applying pomade to dry hair, make sure your hair is completely dry.

Applying Pomade to Wet Hair

One of the advantages of applying pomade to wet hair is that it can help you achieve a sleek and polished look. When you apply pomade to wet hair, it can help to smooth down any flyaways or frizz, and give your hair a uniform sheen.

However, applying pomade to wet hair can also make it harder to achieve a textured or messy look. The pomade may weigh down your hair or make it look greasy if you apply too much. Additionally, if you have thick or curly hair, it may take longer for the pomade to set and dry.

Applying Pomade to Dry Hair

Applying pomade to dry hair can be a better option if you're looking for a more natural or textured look. When you apply pomade to dry hair, you can better control the amount of product you use and how you distribute it throughout your hair.

However, if you have thin or fine hair, applying pomade to dry hair can make it look limp or greasy. Additionally, if you apply too much product or don't distribute it evenly, it can create clumps or patches in your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

To apply pomade to your hair, follow these simple steps:

  1. Choose the right product for your hair type and desired style.
  2. Wash and dry your hair, making sure it's either slightly damp or completely dry.
  3. Scoop a small amount of pomade onto your fingers.
  4. Rub your fingers together to warm up the pomade.
  5. Distribute the pomade evenly throughout your hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends.
  6. Use a comb or your fingers to style your hair as desired.

Tips for Applying Pomade

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of your pomade:

  • Start with a small amount of product and add more as needed.
  • Apply pomade in layers for better control and distribution.
  • Experiment with different amounts and application methods to find what works best for your hair.
  • Don't apply pomade to your scalp, as it can clog pores and cause breakouts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I apply pomade to wet and dry hair?

Yes, you can apply pomade to both wet and dry hair. However, each method has its own advantages and disadvantages, so it's important to choose the one that's best for your hair type and desired style.

How much pomade should I use?

Start with a small amount of pomade, about the size of a dime. You can always add more if needed, but using too much can make your hair look greasy or weighed down.

What's the best pomade for my hair type?

The best pomade for your hair type depends on whether you have thin, fine hair, thick, curly hair, or something in between. Talk to your stylist or do some research to find a pomade that's right for you.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy, shiny, and strong. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a conditioner to help nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons too often.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support hair growth and strength.

By following these tips and experimenting with different pomade application methods, you can achieve a stylish and polished look that suits your hair type and personal style.
