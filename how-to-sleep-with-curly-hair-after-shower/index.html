How To Sleep With Curly Hair After Shower Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Aug 21, 2023 · 5 min read

Having curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it’s beautiful and unique, it can also be a challenge to manage, especially when it comes to sleeping. If you’ve ever woken up with a head full of frizz or tangles, you know exactly what we’re talking about. But don’t worry, we’ve got you covered. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to sleep with curly hair after shower.

  • How to Sleep with Curly Hair After Shower

How to Sleep with Curly Hair After Shower

If you want to maintain your curls while you sleep, you need to take some extra steps before you hit the hay. Here’s what you need to do:

1. Detangle Your Hair

You should never go to bed with tangled hair, especially if you have curly hair. It will only make things worse in the morning. So, before you go to bed, make sure to detangle your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush specifically designed for curly hair. Start at the ends and work your way up, gently removing any knots or tangles.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After you’ve detangled your hair, you should apply a leave-in conditioner. This will help to keep your curls hydrated and defined while you sleep. Make sure to apply the conditioner evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a cotton pillowcase can cause friction and lead to frizz and tangles. So, if you want to maintain your curls, you should use a silk or satin pillowcase. These materials are much gentler on your hair and will help to prevent breakage.

4. Pineapple Your Hair

The pineapple method involves pulling your hair up into a loose and high ponytail at the crown of your head. This will help to preserve your curls and prevent them from getting squished while you sleep. Make sure to use a scrunchie or a hair tie that won’t leave a dent in your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to sleep with curly hair after shower:

Step 1: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush specifically designed for curly hair to gently detangle your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and tangles.

Step 4: Pineapple Your Hair

Pull your hair up into a loose and high ponytail at the crown of your head using a scrunchie or a hair tie.

Tips for Sleeping with Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for sleeping with curly hair:

1. Don’t Sleep with Wet Hair

Wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage, so make sure to give your hair enough time to dry before you go to bed.

2. Use a Silk or Satin Bonnet

If you don’t want to pineapple your hair, you can use a silk or satin bonnet instead. This will help to protect your curls while you sleep.

3. Refresh Your Curls in the Morning

If your curls are looking a little flat in the morning, you can refresh them by using a curl refreshing spray or mist. Simply spray it onto your hair and scrunch your curls to bring them back to life.

FAQs

Q: Can I sleep with my hair down if I have curly hair?

A: While it’s possible to sleep with your hair down if you have curly hair, it’s not recommended. Your curls are more likely to get tangled and frizzy if you sleep with your hair down.

Q: Can I use a regular pillowcase if I don’t have a silk or satin one?

A: While it’s not ideal, you can use a regular pillowcase if you don’t have a silk or satin one. Just make sure to detangle your hair and apply a leave-in conditioner before you go to bed.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

If you want to maintain your curls and keep them looking their best, it’s important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Shampoo and Conditioner Specifically Designed for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires extra moisture, so make sure to use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, and argan oil.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and lead to frizz and breakage. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

3. Use a Deep Conditioner

Once a week, use a deep conditioner to nourish and hydrate your curls. Leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

4. Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Curly hair doesn’t need to be washed every day. In fact, overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils and lead to dryness and frizz. Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week.

By following these tips and tricks, you can sleep with curly hair after shower and wake up with beautiful, defined curls. Happy sleeping!
