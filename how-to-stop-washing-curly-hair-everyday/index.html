How To Stop Washing Curly Hair Everyday

Written by Luffy Nov 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that washing it every day can be a real struggle. Not only can it be time-consuming, but it can also strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. In this blog post, we will discuss how to stop washing curly hair every day and provide you with some tips and tricks to keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful.

Why Wash Curly Hair Less Often?

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair because the natural oils produced by the scalp have a harder time making their way down the hair shaft. When you wash your hair every day, you are stripping it of those natural oils, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Additionally, curly hair tends to be more delicate than straight hair, so excessive washing can damage it even further.

How to Stop Washing Curly Hair Everyday

The first step in stopping the daily washing of your curly hair is to transition to washing it every other day. This will give your hair a chance to produce and maintain its natural oils, which will keep it healthy and hydrated. To help your hair adjust to this new routine, you can try using a dry shampoo on the days when you don't wash it. This will help absorb any excess oil and keep your curls looking fresh.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you transition to washing your curly hair every other day:

  1. Start by washing your hair every other day instead of every day.
  2. Use a dry shampoo on the days when you don't wash your hair.
  3. Gradually increase the number of days between washes until you are only washing your hair once or twice a week.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you stop washing your curly hair every day:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Invest in a good leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and healthy between washes.
  • Try using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners, as they can further damage your hair.

FAQ

How often should I wash my curly hair?

You should aim to wash your curly hair once or twice a week to allow your hair to maintain its natural oils and stay hydrated.

What should I do on the days when I don't wash my hair?

You can use a dry shampoo to absorb any excess oil and keep your curls looking fresh. You can also try refreshing your curls with a water-based leave-in conditioner.

What kind of shampoo should I use on my curly hair?

You should use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for curly hair.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some general hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it's dry.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can stop washing your curly hair every day and keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful.
