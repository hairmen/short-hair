What Causes Newborns To Lose Hair Understanding The Reasons Behind It

Written by Eveline Nov 05, 2023 Â· 3 min read

As new parents, it can be alarming to see your newborn's hair falling out. However, it is a common occurrence and usually nothing to worry about. In this article, we will discuss the reasons behind hair loss in newborns, how to care for their hair, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  1. Causes of Newborn Hair Loss
  2. How to Care for Your Newborn's Hair
  3. Frequently Asked Questions

Causes of Newborn Hair Loss

One of the most common causes of newborn hair loss is hormonal shifts. During pregnancy, the hormones in a mother's body can affect the baby's hair growth, making it thicker and more luscious. However, after birth, the baby's body experiences a hormonal shift, causing the hair to fall out. This type of hair loss is usually temporary and is known as telogen effluvium.

In addition to hormonal shifts, hair loss in newborns can also be caused by friction. Newborns often rub their heads against bedding, car seats, or other surfaces, which can cause hair to fall out. This type of hair loss is also temporary and will often grow back on its own.

Another cause of hair loss in newborns is cradle cap. This is a common condition that causes flaky, scaly patches on the scalp. Although it can cause hair loss, it is usually not a serious condition and can be treated with gentle shampooing and brushing.

How to Care for Your Newborn's Hair

While hair loss in newborns is often temporary and nothing to worry about, there are some steps you can take to care for your baby's hair:

1. Be Gentle

When washing your baby's hair, use a gentle shampoo and be careful not to rub too hard. Use a soft washcloth or your hands to gently massage the scalp and rinse thoroughly with warm water.

2. Brush Regularly

Brushing your baby's hair regularly can help remove any cradle cap flakes and stimulate hair growth. Use a soft-bristled brush and be gentle to avoid tugging on the hair.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid putting your baby's hair in tight ponytails or braids, as this can cause hair to break and fall out. Instead, let their hair flow naturally or use a soft headband or clip to keep it out of their face.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is hair loss in newborns normal?

Yes, hair loss in newborns is normal and usually nothing to worry about. It is often caused by hormonal shifts or friction and will usually grow back on its own.

2. How long does hair loss in newborns last?

Most cases of hair loss in newborns are temporary and will last a few months. However, if the hair loss is caused by a medical condition, it may take longer to grow back.

3. Can I prevent hair loss in my newborn?

While you cannot prevent hair loss in your newborn, you can take steps to care for their hair and promote healthy growth. Be gentle when washing and brushing their hair, avoid tight hairstyles, and talk to your pediatrician if you have any concerns.

Overall, hair loss in newborns is a common occurrence and usually nothing to worry about. By understanding the causes and following a few simple steps to care for your baby's hair, you can help promote healthy growth and development.
