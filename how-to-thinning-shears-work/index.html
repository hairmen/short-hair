How To Use Thinning Shears A Guide

Written by Christine Jun 14, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool for those looking to add texture and volume to their hair. They are also useful in removing bulk from thick hair or creating a more natural look. In this article, we will discuss how to thinning shears work and provide step-by-step instructions on how to use them.

How Do Thinning Shears Work?

Thinning shears work by removing a certain amount of hair at a time. The blades of the scissors have teeth on them that are designed to cut some of the hair while leaving some behind. This results in a textured look that can make hair appear thinner or more voluminous.

It's important to note that thinning shears should only be used on dry hair. Wet hair is more prone to damage and can result in uneven cuts.

How To Use Thinning Shears

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into small, manageable sections. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas. Use clips to hold the sections in place.

Step 2: Hold The Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears like regular scissors, with your fingers in the holes. The teeth should be facing up towards the ceiling.

Step 3: Cut The Hair

Starting at the top of the section, make small cuts with the thinning shears. Make sure to only cut a small amount of hair at a time to avoid an uneven finish. Work your way down the section, cutting small pieces until you reach the ends.

Step 4: Blend The Hair

After you have cut the first section, release the clip and let down another section. Use the thinning shears to blend the cut section with the uncut section. This will create a more natural look.

Step 5: Repeat

Continue this process until you have thinned out all of the sections. Be sure to take breaks and check your progress in the mirror to ensure that you are achieving the desired look.

Tips For Using Thinning Shears

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger sections as you become more comfortable with the shears.
  • Use a comb to separate the hair and make sure you are only cutting the desired section.
  • Don't use thinning shears on already thin or fine hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Keep the blades clean and sharp for the best results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Use Thinning Shears On Curly Hair?

Yes, you can use thinning shears on curly hair. However, it's important to take extra care to ensure that the cuts are even and don't create any unwanted frizz. Use a light hand and make sure to blend the sections well.

How Often Should I Use Thinning Shears?

This will depend on your hair type and desired look. If you have thick hair, you may want to use thinning shears every few months to remove bulk. If you have fine hair, you may only want to use them occasionally for texture.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears

After using thinning shears, it's important to care for your hair properly. Use a deep conditioning treatment to help repair any damage that may have occurred during the cutting process. Avoid using heat styling tools and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair. With proper care, your hair will look healthy and voluminous.

Thinning shears can be a great tool for achieving your desired hairstyle. By following these tips and using them correctly, you can create a natural, textured look that will make your hair look its best.
