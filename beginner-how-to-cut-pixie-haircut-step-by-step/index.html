Beginner How To Cut Pixie Haircut Step By Step A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking of cutting your hair short and trying out a pixie haircut? Pixie haircuts are a popular choice for women who want to go for a stylish and low-maintenance look. However, the thought of cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're a beginner. But fear not, with a little patience and practice, cutting a pixie haircut can be done at home. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut a pixie haircut, along with some tips and hair care advice.

How to Cut a Pixie Haircut

Before we delve into the step-by-step guide, let's first discuss the tools you'll need to cut a pixie haircut. You'll need a pair of scissors, a comb, and a clip to hold your hair in place. You may also want to invest in a pair of hair cutting shears, as they are designed specifically for cutting hair and will give you a more precise cut.

It's also important to note that you should cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be deceiving and make your hair look shorter than it actually is.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let's get into the step-by-step guide for cutting a pixie haircut:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the top, back, and two sides.
  2. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way.
  3. Take a small section of hair at the nape of your neck and comb it straight down.
  4. Hold the hair between your index and middle finger and cut it to your desired length.
  5. Continue cutting small sections of hair at the back until you've achieved the desired length.
  6. Release the top section of your hair and comb it forward.
  7. Hold a small section of hair between your index and middle finger, and cut it to the same length as the back.
  8. Continue cutting small sections of hair at the top until you've achieved the desired length.
  9. Move on to the sides, and comb the hair straight down.
  10. Hold a small section of hair between your index and middle finger, and cut it to your desired length.
  11. Continue cutting small sections of hair on both sides until you've achieved the desired length.
  12. Blend the top and sides together by combing the hair towards the back and cutting any longer pieces that stick out.
  13. Use your scissors to add texture and layers, if desired.

And there you have it, a basic pixie haircut!

Tips for Cutting a Pixie Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a pixie haircut:

  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Use your fingers as a guide to cutting your hair, instead of relying solely on the comb.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting shears for a more precise cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're struggling to reach certain parts of your head.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a pixie haircut:

  • Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?
  • Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have curly hair. Just make sure to cut your hair when it's dry, as curly hair can shrink when it dries.
  • What if I make a mistake?
  • Don't panic if you make a mistake. Hair grows back, and you can always visit a hair stylist to fix any mistakes.
  • How often should I trim my pixie haircut?
  • It's recommended to trim your pixie haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

Hair Care Advice

Now that you've cut your pixie haircut, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some hair care tips to keep your pixie haircut looking great:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Invest in a good quality leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.

And there you have it, a comprehensive guide on how to cut a pixie haircut step by step. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance look right at home.
