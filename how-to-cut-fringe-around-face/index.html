How To Cut Fringe Around Face Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 19, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you thinking of changing your hairstyle? Cutting fringe around your face is a great way to update your look without a major haircut. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut fringe around your face. We’ll also provide you with some tips on how to maintain your new fringe and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Fringe Around Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Fringe
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Fringe

How to Cut Fringe Around Face

Before we start, it’s important to note that cutting fringe is not something you should do on a whim. It requires some planning, so make sure you’re committed to the look before you start. Here are the steps you should follow:

Step 1: Choose the Right Fringe Style

There are several types of fringe styles to choose from, including blunt, side-swept, and wispy. You should choose a style that complements your face shape and hair type. If you’re not sure what style to choose, consult a hairstylist.

Step 2: Gather the Right Tools

You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section your hair. Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to section the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape. The base of the triangle should be your forehead, and the point of the triangle should be at the top of your head.

Step 4: Cut Your Fringe

Start cutting your fringe in the middle of the triangle, then work your way outwards. Cut small sections at a time, and be sure to keep the scissors at an angle to create a soft, natural look. Don’t cut too much at once, as you can always trim more later.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you’ve finished cutting your fringe, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments, and trim any stray hairs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to cut fringe around your face:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair and blow-dry it straight. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair.

Step 2: Choose Your Fringe Style

Choose the fringe style you want to achieve. You can find inspiration online or consult with a hairstylist.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair. Take a section of hair from the middle of your forehead, and comb it straight down. This will be your guide for the length of your fringe. Use hair clips to secure the rest of your hair away from your face.

Step 4: Cut Your Fringe

Start cutting your fringe in the middle of your forehead. Use small, snipping motions to create a soft, natural look. Work your way outwards, cutting small sections at a time. Keep the scissors at an angle to create a blended look.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments, and trim any stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Fringe

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect fringe:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to be conservative and trim more later.
  • Keep the scissors at an angle to create a natural look.
  • Keep your hair dry and straight when cutting your fringe.
  • Consult a hairstylist if you’re not sure what style to choose.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I cut my fringe?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and the style of your fringe. Generally, you should trim your fringe every four to six weeks to maintain the length and shape.

Can I cut my fringe myself?

Yes, you can cut your fringe yourself, but it’s important to be careful and follow the steps outlined in this article.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. You can always trim more hair or visit a hairstylist to fix the problem.

Hair Care for Fringe

Here are some tips on how to care for your new fringe:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your fringe clean.
  • Blow-dry your fringe straight to maintain the shape.
  • Use a small amount of hair product, such as a styling cream or serum, to keep your fringe in place.
  • Avoid touching your fringe too much, as this can make it oily and greasy.
  • Visit a hairstylist regularly to maintain the shape and length of your fringe.

By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a beautiful new look with fringe around your face. Remember to take your time, be careful, and have fun!
