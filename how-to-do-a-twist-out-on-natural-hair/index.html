How To Do A Twist Out On Natural Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Aug 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a fun and easy way to switch up your natural hair routine, a twist out might just be the way to go. This timeless hairstyle can be achieved with just a few simple steps, and it's perfect for anyone who wants to add some definition and volume to their curls. In this guide, we'll take you through the process of how to do a twist out on natural hair, step by step.

Description

A twist out is a hairstyle created by twisting sections of your natural hair, allowing them to set, and then unraveling them to reveal defined curls or waves. This style works best on natural hair that is at least shoulder length and has a curl pattern that can hold a twist. Twist outs can be done on wet or dry hair, depending on your preference, and can last for several days with proper maintenance.

How to Do a Twist Out on Natural Hair

The following steps will guide you through the process of doing a twist out on natural hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that work well for your hair type. It's important to detangle your hair thoroughly while you're conditioning it, as this will make the twisting process easier later on.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After you've rinsed out your conditioner, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair. This will help to moisturize your hair and make it easier to twist.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. The number of sections you create will depend on the thickness and length of your hair.

Step 4: Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product to each section of your hair. This can be a gel, cream, or butter, depending on your preference. Make sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair to ensure that each twist is well-defined.

Step 5: Twist Your Hair

Start twisting each section of hair from the roots to the ends. Make sure to keep the twists tight and smooth, as this will help to create a defined curl pattern.

Step 6: Allow Your Hair to Set

Once you've twisted all of your hair, allow it to set for several hours or overnight. This will give your hair time to dry and set in the twist pattern.

Step 7: Unravel Your Twists

Carefully unravel each twist, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. Avoid pulling your hair too hard or aggressively, as this can cause frizz and damage.

Step 8: Fluff and Style Your Hair

Once you've unraveled all of your twists, use your fingers to fluff and separate your curls. You can also use a pick or wide-tooth comb to add volume to your hair. Style your hair as desired, and enjoy your beautiful twist out!

Tips for Achieving the Perfect Twist Out

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when doing a twist out on natural hair:

  • Use products that work well for your hair type to ensure that your twists are well-defined and long-lasting.
  • Twist your hair tightly and smoothly to create a defined curl pattern.
  • Allow your hair to set for several hours or overnight to ensure that the twists are fully dry.
  • Be gentle when unraveling your twists to avoid damaging your hair or causing frizz.
  • Use a pick or wide-tooth comb to add volume to your hair and create a fuller look.

FAQs

How long will my twist out last?

A twist out can last for several days with proper maintenance. To extend the life of your twist out, you can use a satin bonnet or pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep, and refresh your curls with a moisturizing spray or leave-in conditioner.

Can I do a twist out on short hair?

While twist outs work best on hair that is at least shoulder length, you can still achieve a similar look on shorter hair by creating smaller twists and using a styling product that adds volume and definition to your curls.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Twist Out

After you've achieved the perfect twist out, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure that your curls stay defined and healthy:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or moisturizing spray.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin bonnet or pillowcase to prevent breakage and tangling.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and taking care of your natural hair, you can achieve a beautiful and long-lasting twist out that will turn heads wherever you go!
