How To Thin Out Thick Curly Hair With Scissors

Written by Frank Oct 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to manage. One of the best ways to make your hair more manageable is to thin it out. Thinning out your hair will help reduce the bulk and make it easier to style. If you're wondering how to thin out thick curly hair with scissors, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll show you step-by-step how to thin out your hair with scissors.

Description

Thinning out your hair is a process of removing excess hair to create a more balanced look. It involves using scissors or a razor to cut some of the hair, leaving behind a lighter and more manageable hairstyle. Thinning out your hair is especially important if you have thick, curly hair that tends to get frizzy and unmanageable.

How to Thin Out Thick Curly Hair with Scissors

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Before you start thinning out your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, then let it air dry or blow-dry it until it's completely dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin out your hair.

Step 2: Section your hair

Once your hair is dry, use a comb to section it into four equal parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section of hair to keep it out of the way while you work on the other sections.

Step 3: Start thinning out your hair

Take a section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Start at the bottom of the section and use your scissors to cut small pieces of hair at a time. Make sure to cut at an angle and not straight across. This will create a more natural look and prevent your hair from looking too blunt.

Continue thinning out your hair section by section until you've thinned out all four sections. Make sure to check the back of your head to ensure you've thinned out all areas evenly.

Step 4: Trim the ends

Once you've thinned out your hair, it's time to trim the ends. Use your scissors to cut the ends of your hair at an angle to create layers. This will give your hair more movement and create a more natural look.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.

- Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.

- Don't over-thin your hair, as it can make it look too wispy.

FAQ

Q: Can I thin out my hair with a razor instead of scissors?

A: Yes, you can use a razor to thin out your hair. However, it's important to use a razor specifically designed for hair, as using a regular razor can damage your hair.

Q: How often should I thin out my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

Thinning out your hair can be a great way to make it more manageable, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on your blow dryer to prevent frizz. Regular trims can also help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
