How To Cut Layered Hair Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and go for a shorter, layered look, you might be wondering how to cut your hair at home. While cutting your own hair can be daunting, with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to cut layered hair short in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layered Hair Short
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layered Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Layered Hair Short

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's talk about what you'll need to get started. Here are the tools you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Spray bottle with water or hair detangler

Now that you have your tools ready, let's move on to the step-by-step guide.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair, making it easier to cut.

2. Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips or elastics to secure each section.

3. Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers at the desired length you want to cut. Make sure the hair is evenly distributed between your fingers.

4. Use your scissors to cut the hair at the length you're holding. It's important to use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and damage to your hair.

5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 on the other sections of your hair, making sure to cut at the same length.

6. Once you've cut the length of your hair, you can start adding layers. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle. This will create a layered effect.

7. Repeat step 6 on the other sections of your hair, making sure to cut at the same angle.

8. After you've finished adding layers, you can blend them by taking small sections of hair and cutting at a slight angle. This will help create a seamless transition between the layers.

9. Once you're satisfied with the length and layers of your hair, you can trim any uneven pieces or flyaways.

10. Finally, style your hair as desired and enjoy your new short, layered look!

Tips for Cutting Layered Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can appear longer than it actually is when dry.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring and it's important to take breaks to avoid mistakes.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can cut your hair if it's curly. However, it's important to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is when dry.

Q: Can I cut my hair if it's thin?

A: Yes, you can cut your hair if it's thin. Layering can actually help create the illusion of thicker hair.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain a healthy and vibrant look. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Get regular trims every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Cutting your hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a short, layered look that's perfect for any occasion. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and take breaks if you need to. Happy cutting!
