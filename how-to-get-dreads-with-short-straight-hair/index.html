Description

Dreadlocks, or simply dreads, are a popular hairstyle that involves allowing hair to mat and lock into a series of rope-like strands. While traditionally associated with African and Caribbean cultures, dreads have become popular all over the world. However, getting dreads with short, straight hair can be a bit of a challenge. This article will provide step-by-step instructions on how to get dreads with short straight hair, as well as tips for maintaining and caring for your new hairstyle.

How To

The first step to getting dreads with short straight hair is to grow your hair out to at least 3 inches in length. This will give you enough hair to work with and allow the dreads to form properly.

Next, you will need to decide on a method for creating your dreads. There are several methods you can use, including backcombing, twisting, and interlocking. Each method has its own pros and cons, so do your research and choose the one that works best for you.

Once you have decided on a method, it's time to start creating your dreads. This can be a time-consuming process, so be patient and take breaks as needed. You may also want to enlist the help of a friend or professional stylist.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to creating dreads with short straight hair:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly and let it air dry.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Take a section of hair and start backcombing or twisting it from the roots to the ends.
  4. Once you have backcombed or twisted the entire section, use a dread wax or locking gel to hold the hair in place.
  5. Repeat this process with each section of hair until you have created all of your desired dreads.
  6. Once your dreads are in place, use a hairdryer to help set them in place.
  7. Use a crochet hook to tighten any loose spots in your dreads.
  8. Finally, use a residue-free shampoo to wash your dreads and keep them clean.

Tips

Here are some tips for getting and maintaining dreads with short straight hair:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup in your dreads.
  • Be patient â it may take several months for your dreads to fully form.
  • Consider using a professional stylist for best results.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid frizz and damage to your dreads while you sleep.
  • Be gentle when washing and styling your dreads to avoid breakage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting dreads with short straight hair:

  • How long does it take to get dreads with short straight hair? It can take several months to a year for dreads to fully form, depending on the method you use and how often you maintain them.
  • Can I get dreads with relaxed hair? Yes, it is possible to get dreads with relaxed hair, but it may take longer for them to form and you may need to be extra gentle with your hair to avoid breakage.
  • Do I need to use wax or gel to create dreads? No, you don't have to use wax or gel to create dreads, but they can help hold the hair in place and make the dreads form more quickly.

Hair Care

Once you have your dreads in place, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or hair products that can cause buildup in your dreads.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons on your dreads, as they can cause damage.
  • Be gentle when washing and styling your dreads to avoid breakage.
  • Consider visiting a professional stylist for regular maintenance and upkeep.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to get dreads with short straight hair and maintain them for years to come. Whether you're looking for a bold new style or just want to try something different, dreads are a great option for anyone with the patience and dedication to make them work.
