Hairstyles In The 60S And 70S A Blast From The Past

Written by Frank Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

The 60s and 70s were decades of experimentation and freedom, and this was reflected in the hairstyles of the time. From the bouffant to the afro, there was no shortage of unique and iconic hairstyles that defined the era. In this blog post, we will dive into the most popular hairstyles of the 60s and 70s, how to recreate them, tips to perfect your look, and FAQ's to help you achieve the perfect retro hairdo. Let's get started!

Popular Hairstyles of the 60s and 70s

The 60s and 70s were a time of great change, and the hairstyles of the time reflected this. Here are some of the most popular hairstyles of the era:

The Bouffant

The bouffant was a popular hairstyle in the 60s, characterized by a large, puffy style that was often teased and styled with hairspray. To achieve this look, start by backcombing the top section of your hair to create volume. Then, smooth the top layer of your hair over the backcombed section, and secure with bobby pins. Finish with a generous amount of hairspray to hold the style in place.

The Beehive

The beehive was another iconic hairstyle of the 60s, characterized by a high, dome-shaped style that was often accessorized with hairpins and ribbons. To achieve this look, start by backcombing the top section of your hair to create height. Then, smooth the top layer of your hair over the backcombed section, and secure with bobby pins. Finish with a decorative hairpin or ribbon to add a touch of glamour.

The Afro

The afro was a popular hairstyle in the 70s, characterized by a large, rounded style that was often worn by African Americans as a symbol of black pride. To achieve this look, start by washing and conditioning your hair to create volume. Then, use a wide-tooth comb to gently tease your hair outwards, creating a rounded shape. Finish with a generous amount of afro pick to define your curls, and you're good to go!

How to Recreate these Iconic Hairstyles

Recreating these iconic hairstyles may seem daunting at first, but with a bit of practice and patience, you can achieve the perfect retro look. Here are some step-by-step instructions to help you recreate these iconic hairstyles:

The Bouffant

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair to create volume.
  2. Blow-dry your hair, lifting the roots to create volume.
  3. Section off the top part of your hair, and backcomb the roots to create height.
  4. Smooth the top layer of your hair over the backcombed section, and secure with bobby pins.
  5. Use a generous amount of hairspray to hold the style in place.

The Beehive

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair to create volume.
  2. Blow-dry your hair, lifting the roots to create volume.
  3. Section off the top part of your hair, and backcomb the roots to create height.
  4. Smooth the top layer of your hair over the backcombed section, and secure with bobby pins.
  5. Finish with a decorative hairpin or ribbon to add a touch of glamour.

The Afro

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair to create volume.
  2. Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment to enhance your curls.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently tease your hair outwards, creating a rounded shape.
  4. Finish with a generous amount of afro pick to define your curls.

Tips to Perfect Your Retro Look

Here are some tips to help you perfect your retro look:

  • Use a fine-tooth comb to create a more polished look.
  • Use hairspray to hold your style in place.
  • Accessorize with hairpins and ribbons to add a touch of glamour.
  • Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair to enhance your curls.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some FAQ's to help you achieve the perfect retro hairdo:

Q: How can I make my hair look fuller?

A: Backcombing your hair and using volumizing products can help create the illusion of fuller hair.

Q: How can I make my curls last longer?

A: Using a curl-enhancing product and avoiding excessive heat styling can help your curls last longer.

Q: Can I wear a bouffant or beehive to a formal event?

A: Yes! These hairstyles are perfect for formal events and can add a touch of glamour to any outfit.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and clean.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

And there you have it! A complete guide to recreating the iconic hairstyles of the 60s and 70s. Whether you're attending a retro-themed party or just looking to switch up your hairstyle, these looks are sure to turn heads.
