How To Repair Bleached Damaged Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Sep 13, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you dealing with bleached damaged curly hair? If you are, don’t worry, because we have got you covered. In this article, we will guide you through the process of repairing bleached damaged curly hair. We will discuss how to, step by step guides, tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care tips to help you recover your beautiful curls.

How to Repair Bleached Damaged Curly Hair

Bleaching your hair can be a fun and exciting experience, but it can also leave your hair dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. If you’re dealing with bleached damaged curly hair, don’t worry, because there are ways to repair it. The first step is to assess the damage and determine the best course of action.

Assess the Damage

The first thing you should do is assess the damage to your hair. Look for signs of dryness, breakage, and split ends. If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to cut it and start fresh. However, if the damage is not too severe, you can repair your hair with the following steps.

Step by Step Guide to Repair Bleached Damaged Curly Hair

Step 1: Moisturize Your Hair

The first step in repairing bleached damaged curly hair is to moisturize your hair. Use a deep conditioner or hair mask to hydrate your hair. Leave the conditioner or mask on for 20-30 minutes, then rinse it off with cool water.

Step 2: Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help repair damaged hair by strengthening the hair shaft. Look for a protein treatment that contains keratin or collagen. Follow the instructions on the package and rinse it off with cool water.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can further damage your hair. Avoid using them until your hair has fully recovered.

Step 4: Trim Your Hair

If you have split ends or damaged hair, you should trim your hair. This will prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth. You can trim your hair yourself or visit a professional stylist.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair by using a silk or satin pillowcase. These fabrics are gentle on your hair and will prevent breakage. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to protect your hair from damage.

Tips to Repair Bleached Damaged Curly Hair

Tip 1: Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfate-free shampoos are gentle on your hair and won’t strip it of its natural oils. Look for a shampoo that contains natural ingredients such as aloe vera or coconut oil.

Tip 2: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentle on your hair and will prevent breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

Tip 3: Avoid Overwashing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness. Try to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Tip 4: Wear Protective Styles

Protective styles such as braids or twists can help protect your hair from damage. They also promote healthy hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to repair bleached damaged curly hair?

The time it takes to repair bleached damaged curly hair depends on the severity of the damage. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to fully recover.

Can I use a hair straightener on my bleached damaged curly hair?

No, you should avoid using heat styling tools on your bleached damaged curly hair. They can further damage your hair and prevent it from recovering.

Can I dye my bleached damaged curly hair?

You should avoid dyeing your bleached damaged curly hair until it has fully recovered. Dyeing your hair can further damage it and prevent it from recovering.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Bleached Damaged Curly Hair

Tip 1: Limit Your Use of Chemicals

Chemicals such as hair dyes and relaxers can damage your hair. Try to limit your use of these products.

Tip 2: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair. Wear a hat or use a scarf to protect your hair from the sun.

Tip 3: Use a Heat Protectant

If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

Tip 4: Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can promote healthy hair growth. Eat foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals such as spinach, avocados, and salmon.

So there you have it, a complete guide on how to repair bleached damaged curly hair. Follow these steps and tips to promote healthy hair growth and recover your beautiful curls.
