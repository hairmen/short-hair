Why Does My Hair Get Frizzy After I Straighten It

Written by Bardi Aug 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Frizzy hair is a common problem for many women, especially those with curly or wavy hair. It can be frustrating to spend time straightening your hair, only to have it turn frizzy shortly after. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your hair might be getting frizzy after straightening it and provide tips to help you keep your hair smooth and sleek.

  • Is Heat Damage the Culprit?

What Causes Frizzy Hair?

Frizzy hair occurs when the hair cuticle is raised and the hair becomes dry and brittle. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Humidity
  • Heat damage
  • Chemical damage
  • Using the wrong hair products
  • Over-styling

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you may be more prone to frizz since your hair cuticles are naturally more raised and the hair is more porous.

Is Heat Damage the Culprit?

Using heat to straighten your hair can cause damage to your hair cuticles, especially if you use high heat settings or straighten your hair frequently. Over time, this can cause your hair to become dry and brittle, leading to frizz and breakage.

To prevent heat damage, make sure to use a heat protectant spray before straightening your hair and try to limit your use of heat styling tools.

How Humidity Affects Your Hair

Humidity is a major cause of frizzy hair since it can cause the hair cuticles to expand and let in moisture. When this happens, the hair becomes frizzy and unmanageable.

To combat humidity, try using a smoothing hair serum or oil before straightening your hair. This can help to seal the hair cuticles and prevent moisture from getting in.

Using the Right Products

Using the wrong hair products can also contribute to frizzy hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type and that contain moisturizing ingredients like argan oil or shea butter.

You should also avoid using products that contain alcohol or sulfates since these can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

Proper Straightening Technique

The way you straighten your hair can also impact how frizzy it becomes. Make sure to use a high-quality flat iron with ceramic plates, which will distribute heat evenly and reduce the risk of heat damage.

When straightening your hair, work in small sections and use a comb to help guide the hair through the flat iron. This will help to create a smooth, sleek look and reduce frizz.

Hair Care Tips to Keep Your Hair Smooth

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your hair smooth and frizz-free. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy, beautiful hair:

  • Wash your hair no more than 2-3 times per week
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel instead of rubbing it
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it is already damaged?

A: It is best to wait until your hair is healthy before using heat styling tools. If you must straighten your hair, use a low heat setting and a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It is best to limit your use of heat styling tools to once or twice a week to minimize damage to your hair.

Q: Can I straighten my hair without using heat?

A: Yes, there are several methods for straightening your hair without using heat, including using a straightening brush or using rollers to create a sleek look.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can keep your hair smooth and frizz-free even after straightening it.
