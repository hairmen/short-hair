What Causes Thick Coarse Hair

Written by Eveline Aug 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick and coarse hair can be a blessing or a curse depending on how you look at it. Some people love their thick, voluminous hair, while others struggle to manage it. If you belong to the latter group, you may be wondering what causes thick coarse hair. In this article, we'll explore the different factors that contribute to thick and coarse hair, and provide you with tips on how to manage it.

  2. Ethnicity
  3. Hormones

Genetics

One of the primary factors that determine the thickness and texture of your hair is genetics. If your parents or grandparents have thick, coarse hair, there's a good chance that you will too. Similarly, if you have thin, fine hair, it's likely that your family members have the same hair type.

If you're unhappy with your hair's thickness and texture, there's not much you can do to change it. However, you can work with what you have and find ways to manage your hair effectively.

Ethnicity

Another factor that affects hair texture is ethnicity. People of African descent tend to have thick, coarse hair, while people of Asian descent generally have straight, silky hair. People of European descent often have hair that falls somewhere in between.

Of course, these are just generalizations, and there are always exceptions. However, if you're looking to understand why your hair is the way it is, your ethnicity can be a good place to start.

Hormones

Hormones can also play a role in hair texture. For example, during puberty, both boys and girls experience an increase in androgens, which can lead to thicker, coarser hair. Women going through menopause may also experience changes in hair texture as their hormone levels shift.

If you've noticed a change in your hair texture, it may be worth speaking to your doctor to rule out any underlying hormonal issues.

Nutrition

Your diet can also affect the health and texture of your hair. If you're not getting enough nutrients, your hair may become dry, brittle, and more prone to breakage. On the other hand, a diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

Some foods that are particularly beneficial for hair health include:

  • Salmon
  • Eggs
  • Spinach
  • Avocado
  • Sweet potatoes
  • Almonds

Haircare

The way you care for your hair can also impact its texture. If you're using harsh shampoos or styling products, or if you're using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons too frequently, your hair may become dry, damaged, and more prone to breakage.

To keep your hair healthy and smooth, try using gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, and limit your use of heat styling tools. You can also try incorporating a deep conditioning treatment into your haircare routine once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Styling

The way you style your hair can also contribute to its thickness and texture. If you're constantly pulling your hair back into tight ponytails or braids, or if you're using hair extensions or weaves, your hair may become more prone to breakage and damage.

Instead, try experimenting with different hairstyles that don't put as much stress on your hair. For example, you could try wearing your hair down more often, or experimenting with loose braids or buns.

FAQ

Can you change the texture of your hair?

While you can't change your hair's natural texture, you can make it appear smoother and more manageable with the right styling products and techniques. For example, using a smoothing serum or oil can help tame frizz and add shine to your hair.

Is thick hair harder to manage than thin hair?

Thick hair can be more challenging to manage than thin hair, as it can be heavier and more prone to tangling. However, with the right haircare routine and styling techniques, you can keep your thick hair looking healthy and beautiful.

What's the best way to wash thick, coarse hair?

When washing thick, coarse hair, it's important to use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to massage the shampoo into your scalp thoroughly, and rinse your hair completely to avoid any product buildup. After washing, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while it's still wet.

How often should I trim my thick, coarse hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair growth. However, if you're trying to grow your hair out, you may be able to stretch out the time between trims a bit longer.

In conclusion, thick and coarse hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, ethnicity, hormones, diet, and haircare and styling habits. By understanding the root cause of your hair texture, you can take steps to manage it effectively and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
