How To Get Dreads With Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

One of the most common misconceptions about dreadlocks is that you need long hair to achieve them. But that's not true at all! You can get dreads with short hair too, and we're here to tell you how. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of getting dreads with short hair, from start to finish. So if you're ready to rock some fierce dreads, let's get started!

How to Get Dreads with Short Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's talk about the basics of getting dreads with short hair. The first thing you need to understand is that the length of your hair doesn't matter as much as the texture does. If you have straight hair, it's going to be more difficult to achieve dreads than if you have curly or kinky hair. That being said, it's not impossible to get dreads with straight hair, it just requires more patience and effort.

The second thing you need to understand is that there are different methods for getting dreads with short hair. Some people opt for a more natural method, while others prefer to use products and tools to help speed up the process. We'll cover both methods in this guide, so you can choose the one that works best for you.

Step-by-Step Guide

Method 1: Natural Method

The natural method involves simply letting your hair grow and form into dreads over time. Here's how to do it:

  1. Stop brushing or combing your hair. This will allow your hair to start to tangle and knot naturally.
  2. Separate your hair into sections. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate. The smaller the sections, the faster your hair will form into dreads.
  3. Be patient. Depending on your hair texture, it can take anywhere from a few months to a year or more for your hair to fully dread.
  4. Once your hair has fully dreaded, you can start to maintain them by palm rolling, twisting, or using a crochet hook to tighten any loose hairs.

Method 2: Product and Tool Method

If you don't have the patience to wait for your hair to naturally dread, you can use products and tools to help speed up the process. Here's how:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair.
  2. Section your hair into small sections. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate.
  3. Apply a locking gel or wax to each section. This will help your hair to knot and tangle more easily.
  4. Use a dread comb or crochet hook to backcomb each section. This will help to create knots and tangles in your hair.
  5. Once your hair has fully dreaded, you can start to maintain them by palm rolling, twisting, or using a crochet hook to tighten any loose hairs.

Tips for Getting Dreads with Short Hair

Regardless of which method you choose, there are a few tips that can help you get the best results:

  • Be patient. Getting dreads takes time, especially if you have short hair.
  • Keep your hair clean and well-moisturized. This will help your hair to knot and tangle more easily.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol or other drying ingredients, as this can make it harder for your hair to dread.
  • Wear a durag or headwrap at night to help protect your dreads while you sleep.
  • Consider getting your dreads professionally maintained every few months to keep them looking neat and healthy.

FAQs

Can you get dreads with short hair?

Yes, you can get dreads with short hair! While it may take longer for your hair to fully dread, it's definitely possible.

How long does it take to get dreads with short hair?

It depends on your hair texture and the method you use. With the natural method, it can take anywhere from a few months to a year or more for your hair to fully dread. With the product and tool method, you can typically achieve dreads in a matter of hours or days.

Can you wash your hair with dreads?

Yes, you can and should wash your hair with dreads! It's important to keep your hair clean and well-moisturized to help it knot and tangle more easily.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you've got your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking neat and healthy. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your dreads.
  • Keep your scalp clean and well-moisturized.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreads.

Now that you know how to get dreads with short hair, it's time to start your journey to fierce and fabulous locks. Whether you opt for the natural method or the product and tool method, just remember to be patient and take good care of your hair. With a little effort and a lot of love, you'll have beautiful dreads in no time!
