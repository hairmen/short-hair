How To Thin Out A Curly Wig With Scissors

Written by Daniel Oct 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly wigs are a great way to change up your look, but sometimes they can be too thick and heavy. If you want to achieve a more natural and lightweight look, thinning out your curly wig with scissors is a great option. In this article, we will guide you through the steps of how to thin out a curly wig with scissors and provide tips on how to care for your wig afterwards.

How to Thin Out a Curly Wig with Scissors

Thinning out a curly wig with scissors is a simple process that can be done at home. All you need is a pair of sharp scissors and a comb. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Detangle the Wig

Before you start thinning out your wig, make sure it is detangled. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently comb through the wig, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. This will ensure that you don't accidentally cut any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Section the Wig

Next, you'll want to section the wig into smaller parts. Use clips to separate the top, middle, and bottom sections of the wig. This will make it easier to thin out the hair evenly.

Step 3: Start Thinning Out the Wig

Take a small section of hair and hold it up. Use the scissors to cut into the hair vertically, making small snips. Be sure to cut at an angle, rather than straight across. This will create a more natural look. Repeat this process until you have thinned out the hair to your desired level.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you have thinned out the hair, it's time to blend the layers. Use the scissors to lightly cut into the ends of the hair, creating a more natural and blended look. Be careful not to cut off too much hair or create any uneven layers.

Step 5: Style the Wig

Finally, style the wig as desired. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to create defined curls, or leave the hair natural for a more relaxed look.

Tips

Thinning out a wig can be tricky, so here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent any fraying or damage to the hair.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to ensure even results.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as it's easier to thin out more later than to add hair back in.
  • Be careful not to cut too close to the roots, as this can create a choppy look.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about thinning out curly wigs:

Can I thin out a synthetic wig?

Yes, you can thin out a synthetic wig with scissors, but be sure to use caution and work slowly. Synthetic hair is more delicate than human hair and can easily be damaged.

How often should I thin out my wig?

This depends on how often you wear your wig and how quickly it becomes thick and heavy. As a general rule, you can thin out your wig every 2-3 months.

Can I thin out my wig while wearing it?

No, you should never try to thin out your wig while wearing it. This can result in uneven cutting and damage to the hair.

Hair Care

After thinning out your wig, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wig comb or brush to gently detangle the hair.
  • Wash the wig every 6-8 wears with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Store the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on synthetic wigs, as they can melt the hair.

Thinning out a curly wig with scissors is a great way to achieve a more natural and lightweight look. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve beautiful, natural-looking curls that will turn heads.
