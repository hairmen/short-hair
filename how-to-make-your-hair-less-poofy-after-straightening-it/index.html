How To Make Your Hair Less Poofy After Straightening It

Written by Oliver Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking like a puffball after straightening it? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with the same problem. In this article, we will go over some tips and tricks on how to make your hair less poofy after straightening it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Your Hair Less Poofy After Straightening It
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Keep Your Hair Less Poofy
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Make Your Hair Less Poofy After Straightening It

The first step to making your hair less poofy after straightening it is to use the right products. Apply a smoothing serum or cream to your hair before straightening it. This will help to smooth out any frizz and keep your hair looking sleek.

Another trick is to use a paddle brush while straightening your hair. This will help to distribute the heat evenly and prevent any hot spots that could cause frizz. Make sure to brush your hair in small sections to ensure that each section is straightened properly.

After straightening your hair, let it cool down before touching it. This will help to set the style and prevent any frizz from forming. If you need to touch up any areas, use a flat iron on low heat and only touch up the areas that need it.

Step by Step Guide

1. Apply a smoothing serum or cream to your hair before straightening it.

2. Use a paddle brush while straightening your hair.

3. Brush your hair in small sections.

4. Let your hair cool down before touching it.

5. Use a flat iron on low heat to touch up any areas that need it.

Tips to Keep Your Hair Less Poofy

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it more prone to frizz.

2. Don't wash your hair every day. Over-washing can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

4. Avoid using hot tools on high heat. Use the lowest heat setting that will still straighten your hair.

5. Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to limit straightening your hair to once or twice a week to prevent damage.

Q: Can I use hairspray to keep my hair less poofy?

A: While hairspray can help to keep your hair in place, it can also make it more stiff and crunchy. Instead, try using a lightweight hair oil or serum to smooth down any frizz.

Q: Will a hair mask help to make my hair less poofy?

A: Yes, a hair mask can help to hydrate your hair and make it less prone to frizz. Look for a mask that contains ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter.

Hair Care Tips

1. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before washing it.

2. Don't rub your hair with a towel after washing it. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water.

3. Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools on your hair.

4. Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep it healthy.

5. Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your hair less poofy after straightening it. Remember to use the right products, brush your hair in small sections, and let it cool down before touching it. With a little bit of effort, you can have sleek, smooth hair that lasts all day.
