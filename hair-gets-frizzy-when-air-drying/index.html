Why Does Hair Get Frizzy When Air Drying

Written by Bardi Oct 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever noticed that your hair tends to get frizzy whenever you let it air dry? This can be especially frustrating when you're trying to achieve a sleek and polished look. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide some tips for keeping your hair smooth and frizz-free.

Description

Frizz occurs when the cuticles of your hair become raised and allow moisture to enter the hair shaft. This can happen for a variety of reasons, but one of the most common causes is humidity. When the air is humid, the moisture in the air can penetrate your hair and cause it to swell, which in turn leads to frizz.

When you let your hair air dry, you're exposing it to the natural moisture in the air. This can be a problem if you live in a humid climate or if it's a particularly humid day. Additionally, if your hair is damaged or has been chemically treated, it may be more prone to frizzing when it air dries.

How to Prevent Frizz When Air Drying

Fortunately, there are some things you can do to prevent frizz when air drying your hair. Here are some tips:

Step by Step

  1. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are formulated to smooth and tame frizz.
  2. After washing your hair, gently squeeze out any excess water with a towel. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as this can cause damage and lead to frizz.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum to your hair while it's still damp. This will help to seal the cuticles and prevent moisture from entering the hair shaft.
  4. Use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and lead to frizz.
  5. If you have curly or wavy hair, consider using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help dry your hair without causing frizz.
  6. If you don't have a diffuser, you can also try plopping your hair. This involves wrapping your hair in a t-shirt or microfiber towel and letting it air dry. The fabric will help to absorb excess moisture and prevent frizz.
  7. Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying. The more you touch it, the more likely it is to become frizzy.
  8. Once your hair is completely dry, you can use a small amount of anti-frizz serum or oil to smooth down any flyaways.
  9. If you need to go outside on a humid day, consider wearing a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the moisture in the air.
  10. Finally, remember to be patient. Air drying your hair takes longer than blow drying, but it's a healthier option for your hair in the long run.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to the tips above, there are some general hair care practices that can help to prevent frizz:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too frequently, as this can cause damage and lead to frizz.
  • Consider getting a haircut that's specifically designed to reduce frizz, such as a layered cut or a blunt bob.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Finally, be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling it too tight when you style it, and try to use soft hair ties that won't cause damage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about frizzy hair:

Q: Can I blow dry my hair to prevent frizz?

A: Blow drying your hair can be a good way to prevent frizz, but it's important to use a heat protectant spray and a diffuser attachment to avoid causing damage to your hair.

Q: Are there any natural remedies for frizzy hair?

A: Some people find that applying coconut oil or argan oil to their hair can help to tame frizz. However, it's important to use these oils sparingly, as too much can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Q: Should I use a straightening iron to combat frizz?

A: While a straightening iron can be effective at smoothing down frizz, it's important to use it sparingly and to use a heat protectant spray to avoid causing damage to your hair.

Conclusion

If you're tired of dealing with frizzy hair when you let it air dry, there are plenty of things you can do to prevent it. By following the tips in this article and taking good care of your hair, you can achieve a smooth and polished look that will last all day.
