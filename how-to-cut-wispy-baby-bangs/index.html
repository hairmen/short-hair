How To Cut Wispy Baby Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle, adding wispy baby bangs could be just the thing you need. This trendy style is perfect for framing your face and giving your look a fresh new update. But how do you cut wispy baby bangs at home? In this article, we'll guide you through the process step-by-step, so you can achieve the perfect look with ease.

How to Cut Wispy Baby Bangs

Cutting wispy baby bangs can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, you can do it at home. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle with water

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut wispy baby bangs:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair straight down over your forehead, and separate the section you want to cut into a triangle shape.
  2. Use the comb to separate the section into smaller pieces. This will help you see exactly where you need to cut.
  3. Take a small piece of hair between your fingers and hold it up at a 45-degree angle. Use the scissors to cut the hair in a diagonal line, starting from the middle and cutting towards the outside.
  4. Repeat this process, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Be sure to keep the hair at a 45-degree angle and to only cut a little at a time.
  5. Once you've cut all the way across, check your work in the mirror. If you want your bangs to be shorter or more wispy, you can always go back and cut more.
  6. Finish by styling your bangs however you like. You can blow dry them straight, or use a curling iron to add some texture.

Tips for Cutting Wispy Baby Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting wispy baby bangs:

  • Start with longer bangs and gradually cut more off. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they're wet. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, so it's best to wait until your hair is dry before cutting.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and make your bangs look uneven.
  • Take your time. Cutting your own bangs can be nerve-wracking, but rushing through the process can lead to mistakes.
  • Be patient. It may take a few tries to get the perfect wispy baby bangs, so don't get discouraged if it doesn't look right the first time.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting wispy baby bangs:

How often should I trim my wispy baby bangs?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. As a general rule, aim to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Can I cut wispy baby bangs with curly hair?

Yes, you can! Just be sure to cut your bangs when your hair is dry and in its natural state, so you can see exactly how it will fall.

What if I mess up my bangs?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to have them fix it, or you can try to style your bangs in a way that hides the mistake.

Hair Care Tips for Wispy Baby Bangs

Here are some tips for keeping your wispy baby bangs looking fresh and healthy:

  • Wash your bangs every 2-3 days to keep them clean and oil-free.
  • Blow dry your bangs using a round brush to add volume and bounce.
  • Use a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your bangs too often, as this can cause damage and split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut wispy baby bangs like a pro. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll end up with the perfect look for you.
