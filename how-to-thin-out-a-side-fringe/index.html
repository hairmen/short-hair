How To Thin Out A Side Fringe A Step By Step Guide In Relaxed English

If you have a side fringe that's looking a little too thick or heavy, don't worry! Thinning out your fringe is a simple process that can be done at home with just a pair of hair cutting scissors. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to thin out a side fringe in relaxed English language, and provide you with some tips and tricks for maintaining your newly thinned out fringe.

How to Thin Out a Side Fringe

Thinning out a side fringe involves removing some of the hair from the fringe to create a more wispy, textured look. This can be done using a pair of hair cutting scissors, which are specifically designed for trimming hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before starting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the natural fall of your fringe and determine where to start thinning it out.

Step 2: Section Off Your Fringe

Use a comb to section off your fringe from the rest of your hair. This will make it easier to see which areas need to be thinned out.

Step 3: Determine Where to Thin Out Your Fringe

Take a look at your fringe and determine which areas are too thick or heavy. These are the areas where you'll want to thin out your fringe.

Step 4: Use Hair Cutting Scissors to Thin Out Your Fringe

Starting at the bottom of your fringe, use your hair cutting scissors to create small, vertical snips. These snips should be made at a slight angle, and should be about 1/4 inch apart. Continue snipping until you reach the top of your fringe, making sure to focus on the areas that need to be thinned out the most.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've finished thinning out your fringe, take a step back and check your progress. If you're happy with the look, you're done! If not, you can continue snipping until you achieve your desired look.

Tips for Thinning Out a Side Fringe

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. It's always easier to take more hair off than to put it back on.
  • Make sure you're using hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can damage your hair.
  • Don't thin out your fringe too much, as this can create a choppy, uneven look.
  • When in doubt, seek the help of a professional hair stylist.

FAQs

Q: Can I thin out my fringe if it's already too thin?

A: If your fringe is already too thin, we recommend avoiding further thinning it out. Instead, try adding some volume by using a volumizing spray or styling product.

Q: How often should I thin out my fringe?

A: This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule of thumb, we recommend thinning out your fringe every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and texture.

Hair Care After Thinning Out a Side Fringe

After thinning out your fringe, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and texture. Here are some tips for caring for your hair after thinning out your side fringe:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as hair dryers and flat irons, as these can damage your hair and undo the effects of thinning out your fringe.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing your hair when it's wet.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair nourished and healthy.

Thinning out a side fringe is a simple process that can be done at home with just a pair of hair cutting scissors. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a more textured, wispy look that adds a touch of edge to your hairstyle. Happy snipping!
