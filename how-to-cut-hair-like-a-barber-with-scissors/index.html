How To Cut Hair Like A Barber With Scissors

Written by William Jun 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to cut your hair like a barber with scissors, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we'll go through step-by-step instructions on how to achieve a great haircut using only scissors. We'll also provide tips on how to care for your hair afterwards to keep it looking great.

Description:

Cutting hair with scissors is an art form that requires skill and practice. It's important to have the right tools and techniques to achieve the desired results. In this blog post, we'll provide you with everything you need to know to cut hair like a barber with scissors. You'll learn about the different types of scissors, how to choose the right ones, and how to use them effectively. We'll also go through the different haircutting techniques and styles, so you can choose the one that suits you best. By the end of this post, you'll have all the knowledge and skills needed to give yourself a great haircut with scissors.

How to:

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also helpful to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair.

Step 1: Choose the right scissors

The first step to cutting your hair like a barber is to choose the right scissors. There are different types of scissors, so it's important to choose the ones that are best for your hair type and the style you want to achieve. For example, if you have thick hair, you'll need scissors with larger blades. If you want to achieve a layered look, you'll need scissors with shorter blades. Look for scissors with sharp blades and a comfortable grip.

Step 2: Prepare your hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare it. Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it. Then, comb your hair to remove any tangles. If your hair is too dry, spray it with water to dampen it. This will make it easier to cut and style.

Step 3: Section your hair

Now it's time to section your hair. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: front, left side, right side, and back. Use clips to hold each section in place. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and achieve the style you want.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair from the back section, starting at the nape of your neck. Take small sections of hair and cut them using your scissors. Use your comb to hold the hair in place as you cut. Work your way up to the crown of your head, then move on to the side sections. Cut the hair on the sides in the same way, using your comb to hold the hair in place as you cut. Finally, move on to the front section, again using your comb to hold the hair in place as you cut.

Step 5: Check the Length

After you've cut all the sections of your hair, check the length. Use your comb to lift small sections of hair and check that they're all the same length. If there are any uneven areas, go back and trim them until your hair is even all around.

Tips:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use your comb to hold the hair in place as you cut to ensure an even cut.
  • Check the length frequently to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Take breaks if you need to rest your arms or hands.

FAQ:

What types of scissors should I use to cut my hair?

The type of scissors you use will depend on your hair type and the style you want to achieve. Look for scissors with sharp blades and a comfortable grip. If you have thick hair, you'll need scissors with larger blades. If you want to achieve a layered look, you'll need scissors with shorter blades.

How can I ensure an even cut?

Use your comb to hold the hair in place as you cut to ensure an even cut. Take small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once, and check the length frequently to avoid cutting too much hair.

Hair Care:

After you've cut your hair, it's important to care for it properly to keep it looking great. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Conclusion:

Cutting your hair like a barber with scissors is an achievable goal with the right tools and techniques. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you'll be able to achieve a great haircut that suits your style. Remember to choose the right scissors, prepare your hair properly, and take your time to ensure an even cut. With practice, you'll be able to master the art of cutting hair with scissors like a pro.
