How To Hide Unnatural Hair Color At Work

Written by Eveline Jul 08, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Having an unnatural hair color can be a fun way to express yourself, but it can also be a challenge when it comes to the workplace. While some companies have relaxed policies on hair color, others may require a more professional appearance. If you're looking for ways to hide your unnatural hair color at work, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to blend in with the crowd and maintain a professional appearance.

Table of Contents

  • How to Hide Unnatural Hair Color at Work
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Hiding Unnatural Hair Color at Work
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Unnatural Hair Color

How to Hide Unnatural Hair Color at Work

If you're looking to hide your unnatural hair color, here are some ideas:

Wear a Hat or Headband

One way to hide your hair color is to wear a hat or headband. Choose a neutral color that will blend in with your outfit and won't draw attention to your hair. This is a great option if you work in a casual office or if your job requires you to be outside.

Style Your Hair Differently

If you have long hair, try wearing it in a bun or ponytail. This will help to conceal your hair color and give you a more professional look. You can also try parting your hair differently or adding some volume to your hair to draw attention away from the color.

Use Temporary Hair Color

If you're not ready to commit to a permanent hair color change, try using a temporary hair color spray or chalk. These products will wash out easily and won't damage your hair. Choose a color that is close to your natural hair color to blend in more easily at work.

Try Hair Accessories

You can also try using hair accessories to hide your hair color. For example, you can wear a headscarf, hair clip, or a hairband with a bow. This will help to draw attention away from your hair color and give you a more polished look.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to hide your unnatural hair color at work:

  1. Choose a hairstyle that will help to conceal your hair color.
  2. Wear a hat, headband, or hair accessory to draw attention away from your hair color.
  3. Use a temporary hair color spray or chalk to blend in with your natural hair color.
  4. Make sure your hair is well-maintained and styled neatly.
  5. Choose clothing that will complement your hairstyle and help you look professional.

Tips for Hiding Unnatural Hair Color at Work

Here are some additional tips for hiding your unnatural hair color at work:

  • Keep your hair clean and well-maintained to avoid drawing attention to it.
  • Avoid wearing bright or unusual clothing that will clash with your hair color.
  • Be confident and act professionally to avoid drawing attention to your hair.
  • Consider talking to your employer about their policy on hair color to see if there is any room for negotiation.

FAQs

Can I dye my hair back to its natural color?

Yes, you can dye your hair back to its natural color if you want to avoid drawing attention to your hair. Make sure to choose a professional hair color and follow the instructions carefully.

Can I wear a wig to work?

Yes, you can wear a wig to work if you want to hide your hair color. Choose a wig that looks natural and is similar in color to your natural hair.

What if my employer has a strict policy on hair color?

If your employer has a strict policy on hair color, you may need to consider changing your hair color back to a natural shade or finding a new job that is more accepting of your hair color.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Unnatural Hair Color

If you want to maintain your unnatural hair color, here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair color vibrant.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often to prevent the color from fading.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause the color to fade.
  • Consider using a hair mask or treatment to keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage.

In conclusion, hiding your unnatural hair color at work doesn't have to be a challenge. With a few simple tricks and some confidence, you can blend in with the crowd and maintain a professional appearance. If you're not ready to commit to a permanent hair color change, try using temporary hair color products or accessories to help conceal your hair color. And remember, always be confident and act professionally to avoid drawing attention to your hair.
