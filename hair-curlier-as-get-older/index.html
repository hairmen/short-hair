Hair Curlier As You Get Older Why It Happens And How To Embrace It

Written by Robby Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, our hair goes through many changes, one of which is becoming curlier. This phenomenon is not uncommon, but it can be surprising and overwhelming for those who have had straight hair all their life. In this blog post, we will discuss why hair becomes curlier as you get older, how to enhance your natural curls, and tips on how to care for your curly hair.

Why Does Hair Become Curlier as You Get Older?

One of the main reasons why hair becomes curlier as you age is due to hormonal changes. As we get older, our bodies produce less estrogen, which is responsible for keeping hair straight. With less estrogen, hair becomes drier and more brittle, causing it to curl and frizz.

Another reason why hair becomes curlier as you age is due to changes in the hair follicles. As we age, the shape of the hair follicles can change, resulting in a curlier texture.

How to Enhance Your Natural Curls

If you are embracing your natural curls, there are a few things you can do to enhance them:

1. Use a Curl-Enhancing Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair can help enhance your curls and reduce frizz. Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil.

2. Avoid Brushing Your Hair When Dry

Brushing curly hair when it is dry can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it is wet.

3. Use a Diffuser When Blow-Drying

A diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help enhance your curls and reduce frizz. Use a low heat setting and scrunch your hair while blow-drying to enhance your natural curls.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Curly Hair

If you are new to styling curly hair, follow these simple steps:

1. Start with Clean, Conditioned Hair

Wash your hair with a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner.

2. Apply a Curl-Defining Cream or Gel

Apply a curl-defining product to your hair while it is still wet. Use a quarter-sized amount and distribute it evenly throughout your hair.

3. Use a Diffuser to Dry Your Hair

Attach a diffuser to your blow dryer and set it on low heat. Scrunch your hair while you blow-dry to enhance your natural curls.

4. Finish with a Curl-Enhancing Spray

Once your hair is dry, finish with a curl-enhancing spray to lock in your curls and reduce frizz.

Tips for Caring for Your Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair requires a few extra steps to keep it healthy and looking its best:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.

2. Moisturize Your Hair Regularly

Curly hair is naturally dry, so it's important to moisturize it regularly. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week and apply a leave-in conditioner daily.

3. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce frizz and prevent your curls from becoming tangled while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I Straighten My Curly Hair?

Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant and avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.

2. How Often Should I Wash My Curly Hair?

How often you wash your curly hair depends on your hair type and texture. Some people can go a week without washing their hair, while others need to wash it every other day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

3. Can I Color My Curly Hair?

Yes, you can color your curly hair, but it's important to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent color fading and damage.

In conclusion, embracing your natural curls can be a fun and exciting journey. By following these tips and taking care of your curly hair, you can enhance your natural curls and keep them looking healthy and beautiful.
