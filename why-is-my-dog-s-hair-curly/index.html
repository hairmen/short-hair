Why Is My Dog s Hair Curly

Written by Christine Jun 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Curly hair in dogs can be a charming characteristic, but many pet owners may wonder why their dog's hair is curly. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind curly hair in dogs, how to care for it, and some frequently asked questions.

What Causes Curly Hair in Dogs?

Curly hair in dogs is typically caused by genetics. Certain breeds are more likely to have curly hair, such as Poodles, Bichon Frises, and Portuguese Water Dogs. These breeds have been selectively bred to have curly or wavy hair for specific purposes, such as water retrieving or hunting.

However, even dogs without a curly-haired breed in their lineage can have curly hair due to a genetic mutation. This mutation can affect the texture of the hair, making it curly or wavy.

How to Care for Curly Dog Hair

Caring for curly dog hair requires some extra attention compared to straight-haired dogs. Here are some tips:

Regular Grooming

Curly hair can easily become matted and tangled, so regular grooming is essential. Brush your dog's hair at least once a week to prevent tangles and mats from forming. Use a slicker brush to gently remove any knots, and a comb to detangle the hair.

Use the Right Shampoo

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it's important to use a shampoo that won't strip the natural oils from their coat. Look for a shampoo that is specifically formulated for curly or dry hair. Avoid using human shampoo, as it can be too harsh for your dog's coat.

Avoid Over-Bathing

While regular grooming is important, over-bathing can dry out your dog's coat and make it more prone to tangles and mats. Aim to bathe your dog once every 4-6 weeks, or as needed if they get particularly dirty.

FAQ

Can I Straighten My Dog's Curly Hair?

No, it's not recommended to straighten your dog's hair. Straightening tools can be too hot for your dog's delicate skin, and the chemicals used in straightening products can be harmful if ingested.

Can Curly Hair Cause Skin Irritation?

Curly hair can sometimes cause skin irritation if it's not properly groomed. Mats and tangles can create a breeding ground for bacteria and yeast, which can lead to skin infections. Regular grooming can help prevent these issues.

Conclusion

In conclusion, curly hair in dogs is typically caused by genetics, and caring for it requires some extra attention. Regular grooming, using the right shampoo, and avoiding over-bathing can all help keep your dog's curly hair healthy and beautiful.
