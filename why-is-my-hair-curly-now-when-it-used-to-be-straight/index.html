Why Is My Hair Curly Now When It Used To Be Straight

Written by Christine Aug 10, 2023 · 3 min read

Table of Content:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Have you ever wondered why your hair has suddenly become curly when it used to be straight? The answer could be due to a variety of reasons, including genetic factors, hormonal changes, and damage from heat styling or chemical treatments.

While some people are born with naturally curly hair, others can experience a change in texture due to hormonal fluctuations. For example, pregnancy, menopause, and certain medications can all affect the texture of your hair.

Additionally, damage from heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can cause your hair to become curly over time. Chemical treatments like perms or relaxers can also alter the structure of your hair, leading to a change in texture.

How to

If you're wondering how to embrace your newfound curls, there are several things you can do to enhance and define your natural texture:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage
  • Apply a curl-enhancing product to damp hair to help define your curls
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray if you must use them

Step by step

If you're new to styling curly hair, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing product, like a curl cream or mousse, to your hair
  3. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair, or air dry if possible
  4. Once your hair is dry, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently separate and define your curls
  5. Finish with a light hold hairspray or oil to add shine and hold

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you care for and style your curly hair:

  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Consider getting a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your curls looking healthy and bouncy
  • Experiment with different styling techniques, like plopping or diffusing, to find what works best for your hair

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about why hair can change from straight to curly:

Can stress cause hair to become curly?

While stress can certainly affect your hair's health and appearance, it's unlikely to cause a permanent change in texture.

Can using a diffuser make hair curly?

A diffuser can help enhance and define natural curls, but it won't create curls where there aren't any.

Can hair go from curly to straight naturally?

It's possible for hair to change texture naturally over time, but it's not common for curly hair to become completely straight without the use of heat styling tools or chemical treatments.

Hair Care

Whether your hair is naturally curly or you've recently experienced a change in texture, it's important to care for your locks properly. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, beautiful curls:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your curls
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner
  • Consider using a silk or satin hair wrap or bonnet to protect your curls while you sleep

By following these tips and embracing your natural texture, you can rock your new curly hair with confidence!
