How To Style My Very Short African Hair

If you have very short African hair, you know how challenging it can be to style. However, with the right techniques and products, you can create a variety of stylish and trendy looks. This article will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to style your very short African hair, as well as tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Style Very Short African Hair

Styling very short African hair can be challenging, but there are many options available. You can choose from a variety of styles, including twists, braids, and natural curls. The key is to use the right products and techniques to achieve the desired look.

Products to Use

The first step in styling very short African hair is to use the right products. You will need a good moisturizer and a styling gel or cream. Look for products that are specifically designed for African hair, as these will provide the best results.

Techniques to Use

Once you have the right products, you'll need to use the right techniques to style your hair. For twists and braids, start with clean, dry hair, and use a comb to section it into small sections. Apply a small amount of moisturizer to each section, then twist or braid the hair tightly. Use a styling gel or cream to keep the twists or braids in place.

For natural curls, use a curl defining cream or gel, and apply it to damp hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair, then scrunch it up to create curls. Allow the hair to air dry, then use a pick or comb to separate the curls and create volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to style your very short African hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Pat dry with a towel, but do not rub.
  2. Apply a small amount of moisturizer to your hair, and use a comb to detangle it.
  3. Section your hair into small sections, and apply a styling gel or cream to each section.
  4. Twist or braid each section tightly, and secure with a small hair tie or clip.
  5. Allow your hair to air dry, or use a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment to dry your hair.
  6. Once your hair is dry, remove the hair ties or clips, and use your fingers to separate the twists or braids.
  7. Apply a small amount of oil to your hair, and use a pick or comb to style it as desired.

Tips for Styling Short African Hair

Here are some tips to help you style your very short African hair:

  • Use the right products for your hair type.
  • Be gentle when detangling your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk bonnet or scarf.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly to keep it healthy and hydrated.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling very short African hair:

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how often you use styling products. Generally, you should wash your hair every 7-10 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: How can I add volume to my hair?

A: You can add volume to your hair by using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and by blow drying your hair upside down with a diffuser attachment.

Q: How can I prevent breakage?

A: To prevent breakage, avoid using harsh chemicals, heat styling tools, and tight hairstyles. Be gentle when detangling your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers instead of a brush.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips for keeping your very short African hair healthy:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Now that you have all the information you need, you can start experimenting with different styles for your very short African hair. Remember to be patient and gentle, and your hair will look healthy and beautiful.
