10 Ways To Put Thin Hair Up

Are you struggling to put your thin hair up? Having thin hair can be frustrating, especially when you want to try out different hairstyles. But don't fret! There are plenty of ways to put your thin hair up that are easy and stylish. In this article, we'll cover 10 different ways to put thin hair up that will give you the full and voluminous look you desire.

Section 1: The Classic Ponytail

The classic ponytail is a go-to hairstyle for many women with thin hair. It's simple, easy, and can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. To make your ponytail look fuller, try teasing the crown of your hair before tying it back. This will give the illusion of more volume.

How to:

  1. Brush your hair to smooth out any tangles.
  2. Tease the crown of your hair using a comb or teasing brush.
  3. Gather your hair into a ponytail, making sure to include the teased section at the crown.
  4. Secure the ponytail with an elastic band.
  5. Take a small section of hair from the ponytail and wrap it around the elastic band to hide it.
  6. Secure the wrapped section of hair with a bobby pin.
  7. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips:

  • Use a clear elastic band to make the wrapped section of hair look seamless.
  • Pull on the sides of the ponytail to loosen it up and make it look more voluminous.
  • Add a hair accessory, such as a ribbon or scrunchie, to dress up the look.

FAQ:

  • Can I do a high ponytail with thin hair?
  • Yes, you can! Just make sure to tease the crown of your hair to create more volume.
  • What if my hair is too short for a ponytail?
  • You can still create a half-up half-down style by gathering the top section of your hair and securing it with a clip or elastic band.

Section 2: The Messy Bun

The messy bun is another easy hairstyle that looks great on thin hair. It's perfect for those days when you don't have a lot of time to style your hair but still want to look put together. The key to a good messy bun is to embrace the imperfections and make it look effortless.

How to:

  1. Brush your hair to smooth out any tangles.
  2. Gather your hair into a high ponytail and secure it with an elastic band.
  3. Twist the ponytail around the base of the elastic band to create a bun.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins, making sure to hide them underneath the hair.
  5. Pull on the sides of the bun to loosen it up and make it look more messy.
  6. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips:

  • Leave some pieces of hair out around your face to frame it.
  • Use a scrunchie instead of an elastic band for a more casual look.
  • Add a hair accessory, such as a headband or scarf, to dress up the look.

FAQ:

  • Can I do a low messy bun with thin hair?
  • Yes, you can! Just gather your hair into a low ponytail and follow the same steps to create the bun.
  • What if my hair is too slippery to hold a messy bun?
  • Try using texturizing spray or dry shampoo to give your hair more grip.

Section 3: The Top Knot

The top knot is a stylish and trendy hairstyle that's perfect for thin hair. It's a great way to keep your hair out of your face while still looking chic. To make your top knot look fuller, try teasing your hair before putting it up.

How to:

  2. Tease the crown of your hair using a comb or teasing brush.
  3. Gather your hair into a high ponytail and secure it with an elastic band.
  4. Twist the ponytail around the base of the elastic band to create a bun.
  5. Secure the bun with bobby pins, making sure to hide them underneath the hair.
  6. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips:

  • Leave some pieces of hair out around your face to frame it.
  • Add a hair accessory, such as a bow or scrunchie, to dress up the look.
  • Use a hair donut to create a more voluminous bun.

FAQ:

  • Can I do a low top knot with thin hair?
  • Yes, you can! Just gather your hair into a low ponytail and follow the same steps to create the bun.
  • What if my hair is too short for a top knot?
  • You can still create a mini top knot by gathering the top section of your hair and securing it with a small elastic band.

Section 4: The French Twist

The French twist is a classic and elegant hairstyle that's perfect for special occasions. It's a great way to show off your neck and shoulders while still keeping your hair up and out of the way. To make your French twist look fuller, try teasing your hair before twisting it up.

How to:

  1. Brush your hair to smooth out any tangles.
  2. Tease the crown of your hair using a comb or teasing brush.
  3. Gather your hair into a low ponytail at the nape of your neck.
  4. Twist the ponytail up towards the crown of your head, tucking the ends under.
  5. Secure the twist with bobby pins, making sure to hide them underneath the hair.
  6. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips:

  • Use a hair comb to help secure the twist.
  • Leave some pieces of hair out around your face to frame it.
  • Add a hair accessory, such as a jeweled clip or barrette, to dress up the look.

FAQ:

  • Can I do a high French twist with thin hair?
  • Yes, you can! Just make sure to tease the crown of your hair to create more volume.
  • What if my hair is too slippery to hold a French twist?
  • Try using texturizing spray or dry shampoo to give your hair more grip.
