Description

Splat bleach is a popular hair bleach that is commonly used to lighten hair. However, many people are concerned about the side effects of using bleach on their hair, including hair loss. In this article, we will discuss whether Splat bleach makes your hair fall out and what you can do to prevent this from happening.

How to Use Splat Bleach

Splat bleach is a strong hair bleach that can lighten your hair by several shades. Before using this product, it is important to follow the directions carefully to avoid damaging your hair or scalp. Here are the steps to using Splat bleach:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before applying Splat bleach, it is important to prepare your hair by washing it thoroughly and removing any tangles or knots. You should also apply a protective oil or cream to the ends of your hair to prevent them from becoming too dry or damaged.

Step 2: Mix the Bleach

Next, you should mix the bleach according to the instructions on the package. Be sure to wear gloves and mix the bleach in a well-ventilated area to avoid inhaling any fumes.

Step 3: Apply the Bleach

Using a brush or applicator, apply the bleach to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to apply the bleach evenly and avoid overlapping the bleach on previously bleached hair.

Step 4: Wait and Rinse

After applying the bleach, you should wait for the recommended amount of time before rinsing it out. This will vary depending on the level of lightening you want to achieve. Once you have rinsed out the bleach, you should apply a conditioner to your hair to restore moisture and prevent damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you are new to bleaching your hair or are using Splat bleach for the first time, it can be helpful to follow a step-by-step guide to ensure that you are using the product correctly. Here is a step-by-step guide to using Splat bleach:

Step 1: Choose the Right Shade

Splat bleach comes in several shades, so it is important to choose the right one for your hair color and the level of lightening you want to achieve. If you are unsure which shade to choose, consult a professional hairstylist or do a strand test before applying the bleach to your entire head.

Step 2: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start bleaching your hair, you should gather all the supplies you will need, including gloves, a brush or applicator, a mixing bowl, and a protective cream or oil. You should also wear old clothing or a cape to avoid getting bleach on your clothes.

Step 3: Prepare Your Hair

Before applying the bleach, you should prepare your hair by washing it thoroughly and removing any tangles or knots. You should also apply a protective cream or oil to the ends of your hair to prevent them from becoming too dry or damaged.

Step 4: Mix the Bleach

Next, you should mix the bleach according to the instructions on the package. Be sure to wear gloves and mix the bleach in a well-ventilated area to avoid inhaling any fumes.

Step 5: Apply the Bleach

Using a brush or applicator, apply the bleach to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to apply the bleach evenly and avoid overlapping the bleach on previously bleached hair.

Step 6: Wait and Check

After applying the bleach, you should wait for the recommended amount of time before checking the color. You can check the color by wiping a small section of hair with a damp towel or by rinsing a small section of hair with water. If the color is not light enough, you can reapply the bleach and wait a bit longer.

Step 7: Rinse and Condition

Once you have achieved the desired level of lightening, you should rinse out the bleach thoroughly and apply a conditioner to your hair. This will help restore moisture and prevent damage.

Tips Before Bleaching Your Hair

Bleaching your hair can be a fun way to change up your look, but it is important to take some precautions to prevent damage and hair loss. Here are some tips to keep in mind before bleaching your hair:

  • Do a strand test before applying bleach to your entire head.
  • Avoid washing your hair for a few days before bleaching to allow natural oils to build up.
  • Apply a protective cream or oil to the ends of your hair to prevent damage.
  • Use a lower volume developer if you have fine or damaged hair.
  • Don't overlap the bleach on previously bleached hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about using Splat bleach:

Does Splat bleach make your hair fall out?

Splat bleach can cause hair breakage or damage if it is not used correctly, but it is unlikely to cause hair loss. To avoid damage, be sure to follow the instructions carefully and avoid leaving the bleach on for too long.

How often can I bleach my hair with Splat bleach?

You should wait at least a month between bleachings to allow your hair to recover and prevent damage. If your hair is already damaged or dry, you should wait even longer between bleachings.

Can I dye my hair after using Splat bleach?

Yes, you can dye your hair after using Splat bleach. However, you should wait at least a week before dyeing your hair to allow it to recover and avoid further damage.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it is important to take extra care to prevent damage and keep your hair healthy. Here are some tips for caring for your hair after bleaching:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to restore moisture.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or blow drying your hair too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid coloring your hair too often to prevent further damage.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to restore moisture and prevent breakage.

In conclusion, Splat bleach is a strong hair bleach that can lighten your hair by several shades. While it can cause hair breakage or damage if not used correctly, it is unlikely to cause hair loss. By following the instructions carefully and taking extra care to protect your hair, you can achieve the desired level of lightening without damaging your hair or scalp.
