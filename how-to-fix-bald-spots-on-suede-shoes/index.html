How To Fix Bald Spots On Suede Shoes

Written by Petter Jul 20, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're a fan of suede shoes, you may have experienced the frustration of discovering bald spots on your beloved footwear. These bald spots can be caused by a number of things, including friction, water damage, or even just general wear and tear. But don't worry, there are ways to fix bald spots on suede shoes and restore them to their former glory. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of fixing bald spots on suede shoes, step by step.

Description

Suede shoes are a popular choice for their soft texture and stylish appearance. However, they can be quite delicate and are prone to bald spots. These spots can be caused by a number of factors, including friction from walking, water damage, or even just general wear and tear. If you've noticed bald spots on your suede shoes, don't despair – there are ways to fix them and restore your shoes to their former glory.

How to Fix Bald Spots on Suede Shoes

Fixing bald spots on suede shoes is a relatively simple process that you can do at home. Here's how:

Step 1: Clean the Shoes

Before you begin fixing the bald spots, it's important to clean the shoes thoroughly. Use a suede brush or a soft cloth to remove any dirt or debris from the surface of the shoes. This will ensure that the shoe is clean and free of any contaminants that could interfere with the repair process.

Step 2: Identify the Bald Spots

Next, identify the bald spots on the shoes. These spots will be areas where the suede is worn away, leaving a smooth and shiny surface. Take note of the size and location of each bald spot, as this will help you determine the best course of action for repairing them.

Step 3: Use a Suede Eraser

Once you've identified the bald spots, use a suede eraser to gently rub the surface of the shoe. This will help to remove any remaining debris and rough up the surface of the suede. Be sure to use a light touch, as applying too much pressure can cause further damage to the shoe.

Step 4: Brush the Suede

After using the suede eraser, use a suede brush to brush the surface of the shoe. This will help to restore the texture of the suede and prepare it for the next step in the repair process.

Step 5: Apply Suede Dye

Now it's time to apply suede dye to the bald spots. Choose a dye that matches the color of your shoes as closely as possible. Apply the dye to the bald spots using a small brush or a cotton swab, being careful not to get any dye on the surrounding suede. Allow the dye to dry completely before moving on to the next step.

Step 6: Brush the Suede Again

After the dye has dried, use a suede brush to brush the surface of the shoe once again. This will help to blend the dye into the surrounding suede and restore the texture of the shoe.

Step 7: Apply Suede Protector

Finally, apply a suede protector to the entire surface of the shoe. This will help to prevent future bald spots and protect the shoe from water and other elements that could cause damage. Follow the instructions on the suede protector carefully to ensure that it is applied correctly.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when fixing bald spots on suede shoes:

  • Be gentle when using the suede eraser and brush to avoid causing further damage to the shoe.
  • Choose a suede dye that matches the color of your shoes as closely as possible.
  • Apply the suede dye in thin layers, allowing each layer to dry completely before applying the next.
  • Test the suede protector on a small, inconspicuous area of the shoe before applying it to the entire surface.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing bald spots on suede shoes:

Can I use a different type of dye to fix bald spots on my suede shoes?

No, it's important to use a suede dye specifically designed for use on suede shoes. Using a different type of dye could cause damage to the shoe or result in an uneven finish.

Can I fix bald spots on suede shoes myself?

Yes, fixing bald spots on suede shoes is a relatively simple process that you can do at home. However, if you're unsure about the process or don't feel comfortable doing it yourself, you can always take your shoes to a professional.

How can I prevent bald spots from occurring on my suede shoes?

You can prevent bald spots from occurring on your suede shoes by avoiding excessive friction, keeping them dry, and using a suede protector to help prevent damage.

Hair Care

Just like your hair, your suede shoes need proper care and maintenance to stay healthy and looking their best. Here are a few tips for caring for your suede shoes:

  • Regularly brush your suede shoes to remove dirt and debris.
  • Avoid wearing suede shoes in wet or snowy conditions.
  • Use a suede protector to help prevent damage from water and other elements.
  • Store your suede shoes in a cool, dry place to prevent mold and mildew.

By following these tips, you can keep your suede shoes looking great for years to come.
