Best Volumizing Mousse For Fine Hair 2020

Written by Bardi Aug 31, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have fine hair, you know how difficult it can be to get the volume you want. That's why choosing the right volumizing mousse is so important. But with so many options on the market, it can be overwhelming to decide which one to go for. In this article, we'll be discussing the best volumizing mousse for fine hair in 2020. Read on to find out more.

Description

Volumizing mousse is a hair product that is designed to add volume and body to fine hair. It is usually applied to damp hair before blow-drying, and can help to create the appearance of thicker, fuller hair. There are many different types of volumizing mousse on the market, each with its own unique formula and benefits.

How to Choose the Best Volumizing Mousse for Fine Hair

When choosing a volumizing mousse for fine hair, there are a few key factors to consider:

1. Formula

Look for a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down. Avoid mousse that contains heavy oils or waxes, as these can make your hair look greasy and flat.

2. Hold

The level of hold you need will depend on your hair type and the style you're going for. If you have fine hair, look for a mousse with a medium hold, as this will help to add volume without making your hair stiff or crunchy.

3. Ingredients

Look for mousse that contains ingredients that are beneficial for fine hair, such as protein, keratin, and vitamins. These can help to strengthen and protect your hair, while also adding volume and body.

Step by Step: How to Apply Volumizing Mousse to Fine Hair

Applying volumizing mousse to fine hair is easy. Follow these steps:

1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.

Start by using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to add volume and body to your hair before you even apply the mousse.

2. Towel-dry your hair.

Gently towel-dry your hair until it is damp. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with the towel, as this can cause damage and breakage.

3. Apply the mousse.

Dispense a small amount of mousse into your hand, and then distribute it evenly through your hair. Focus on the roots and mid-lengths, as these are the areas that need the most volume.

4. Blow-dry your hair.

Use a blow dryer and a round brush to dry your hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends, lifting your hair as you dry to create volume.

Tips for Using Volumizing Mousse on Fine Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your volumizing mousse:

1. Use a small amount.

A little goes a long way with volumizing mousse. Start with a small amount and add more as needed.

2. Don't apply too close to the scalp.

If you apply too much mousse too close to the scalp, it can create a greasy, weighed-down look. Focus on the mid-lengths and ends instead.

3. Avoid touching your hair too much.

The more you touch your hair, the more volume you will lose. Once you've styled your hair, try to avoid touching it too much throughout the day.

FAQ

Q: Can volumizing mousse be used on all hair types?

A: Yes, volumizing mousse can be used on all hair types. However, it is especially beneficial for fine hair, as it can help to create the appearance of thicker, fuller hair.

Q: How often should I use volumizing mousse?

A: You can use volumizing mousse as often as you like. However, it is important to avoid using too much product, as this can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

Q: Can I use volumizing mousse on dry hair?

A: Volumizing mousse is designed to be used on damp hair, as this is when it is most effective. However, if you want to add some volume to dry hair, you can try using a dry shampoo or texturizing spray instead.

Hair Care for Fine Hair

Using the right hair care products is essential for maintaining healthy, voluminous hair. Here are some tips for caring for fine hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo.

Choose a shampoo that is specifically formulated for fine hair, and avoid using products that contain harsh sulfates or surfactants.

2. Condition regularly.

Conditioner can help to add moisture and nourishment to fine hair. Look for a lightweight, volumizing conditioner that won't weigh your hair down.

3. Avoid heat styling.

Heat styling can cause damage and breakage, which can make fine hair look even thinner. Try to air-dry your hair as much as possible, and avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons.

4. Protect your hair from the sun.

The sun's UV rays can cause damage to your hair, making it look dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains UV protection to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Overall, the best volumizing mousse for fine hair in 2020 is one that is lightweight, contains beneficial ingredients, and provides a medium hold. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can achieve the full, voluminous hair you've always wanted.
