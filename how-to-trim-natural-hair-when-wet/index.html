How To Trim Natural Hair When Wet A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Trimming natural hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're new to the process. But it's an essential part of maintaining healthy, strong hair. And while many people prefer to trim their hair when it's dry, trimming it when wet can be easier and more effective. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of trimming natural hair when wet, and share some tips and tricks along the way.

How to Trim Natural Hair When Wet

Trimming natural hair when it's wet can be a great option because the hair is more pliable and easier to manipulate. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely saturated with water before you begin trimming.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb or your fingers, separate your hair into manageable sections. Start with one section at a time, and clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush, gently detangle your hair from the ends to the roots. This will help prevent breakage and make it easier to trim your hair evenly.

Step 4: Trim Your Hair

Using a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, trim your hair one section at a time. Start by taking small sections of hair and trimming just the ends. Be sure to cut straight across and avoid cutting at an angle, which can create split ends.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've finished trimming one section, check to see if it's even with the other sections. If not, make small adjustments until it's even.

Step 6: Repeat

Continue these steps until you've trimmed all of your hair.

Tips for Trimming Natural Hair When Wet

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when trimming your natural hair when wet:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Trim your hair in a well-lit area.
  • Trim your hair in small sections to ensure even results.
  • Don't try to trim too much hair at once.
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure a good trim.

FAQ

Can I trim my natural hair when it's dry?

Yes, you can. However, trimming your natural hair when it's wet can be easier and more effective because the hair is more pliable.

How often should I trim my natural hair?

It's recommended to trim your natural hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Should I trim my hair myself or go to a professional?

It's up to you. If you're comfortable trimming your own hair, go for it. However, if you're new to the process or have a lot of hair to trim, it may be best to go to a professional stylist.

Hair Care

To keep your natural hair healthy and strong, it's important to follow a good hair care routine. This includes washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can maintain healthy, beautiful natural hair for years to come.
