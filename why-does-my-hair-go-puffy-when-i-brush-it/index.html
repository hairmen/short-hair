Why Does My Hair Go Puffy When I Brush It

Written by Bardi Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever experienced brushing your hair and suddenly, it goes from smooth and silky to puffy and frizzy? It can be frustrating, especially when you're about to go out and your hair just won't cooperate. In this article, we'll discuss the reasons why your hair goes puffy when you brush it and what you can do to prevent it from happening.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

When you brush your hair, you create friction between the brush and your hair strands. This friction can cause your hair cuticles to lift up, creating a puffy and frizzy look. The more you brush your hair, the more the cuticles lift up, making your hair even puffier. This is especially true if you have curly or wavy hair, as the natural texture of your hair can add to the puffiness.

How To

To prevent your hair from going puffy when you brush it, there are a few things you can do:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with flexible bristles. This will help to minimize the amount of friction created when you brush your hair.
  • Brush your hair gently, using slow and smooth strokes. Avoid brushing your hair aggressively, as this can cause the cuticles to lift up even more.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair serum before brushing your hair. This will help to smooth down the cuticles and reduce the amount of frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it's more prone to damage and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before brushing it.

Step by Step

To brush your hair without making it puffy, follow these steps:

  1. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Take a section of your hair and hold it with one hand.
  4. Using a wide-tooth comb or a brush with flexible bristles, gently brush the section of hair with the other hand.
  5. Repeat until you've brushed all of your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to prevent your hair from going puffy when you brush it:

  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it more prone to puffiness.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can contribute to frizz and puffiness.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about why hair goes puffy when you brush it:

  • Q: Does brushing your hair too much cause it to go puffy?
    A: Yes, brushing your hair too much can cause the cuticles to lift up and make your hair puffy and frizzy.
  • Q: Does hair texture affect how puffy your hair gets when you brush it?
    A: Yes, if you have curly or wavy hair, it's more prone to puffiness because of the natural texture of your hair.
  • Q: Can using the wrong brush make your hair go puffy?
    A: Yes, using a brush with stiff bristles or a brush that's too small can create more friction and make your hair go puffy. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with flexible bristles instead.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to prevent your hair from going puffy when you brush it. Here are some additional tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle.
  • Pat your hair dry with a soft towel instead of rubbing it vigorously.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a hair serum with UV protection.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can prevent it from going puffy when you brush it. Remember to be gentle with your hair and use the right tools and products to keep it healthy and looking its best.
