Short Haired Dog Sheds A Lot How To Manage It

Written by Petter Jul 28, 2023 · 4 min read

If you own a short haired dog, you might be surprised to learn that they shed a lot. While it might seem like a minor inconvenience, excessive shedding can lead to allergies, mess, and an overall unpleasant living environment. In this article, we’ll give you tips and tricks on how to manage the shedding and keep your home clean and tidy.

Description

Short haired dogs, such as Beagles, Boxers, and Dalmatians, shed a lot. Shedding is a natural process where dogs lose their old, damaged, or excess hair. This is especially true during the spring and fall when dogs shed their winter and summer coats. However, some dogs shed more than others, and short haired dogs are no exception. Their shedding can be attributed to their genetics, health, diet, and environment.

How to Manage It

Managing a short haired dog’s shedding requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some tips:

Brush Your Dog Regularly

Brushing your dog’s coat regularly is the most effective way to manage shedding. Use a slicker brush, a deshedding tool, or a rubber curry brush to remove loose hair from your dog’s coat. Brush your dog outdoors or in a room with easy-to-clean floors to avoid getting hair all over your home.

Bathe Your Dog Occasionally

Bathing your dog occasionally can also help manage shedding. Use a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner to keep your dog’s coat healthy and shiny. However, be careful not to bathe your dog too often, as it can dry out their skin and cause more shedding.

Feed Your Dog a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can also affect your dog’s shedding. Feed your dog high-quality dog food that contains essential nutrients such as omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids, biotin, and zinc. These nutrients can improve your dog’s coat health and reduce shedding.

Keep Your Home Clean

Keeping your home clean is crucial when managing a short haired dog’s shedding. Vacuum your floors, furniture, and rugs regularly to remove hair. Use lint rollers or pet hair removers to remove hair from your clothes and upholstery. Wash your dog’s bedding and toys frequently to keep them clean and hair-free.

Step by Step

Here are the step-by-step instructions on how to manage a short haired dog’s shedding:

  1. Brush your dog’s coat regularly using a slicker brush, a deshedding tool, or a rubber curry brush.
  2. Bathe your dog occasionally using a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner.
  3. Feed your dog high-quality dog food that contains essential nutrients such as omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids, biotin, and zinc.
  4. Keep your home clean by vacuuming, using lint rollers or pet hair removers, and washing your dog’s bedding and toys frequently.

Tips

Here are some additional tips on how to manage a short haired dog’s shedding:

  • Use a high-velocity dryer to blow out loose hair from your dog’s coat.
  • Use a shedding blade to remove stubborn clumps of hair from your dog’s coat.
  • Consult with your veterinarian if you notice excessive shedding or bald patches on your dog’s coat.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about short haired dog shedding:

Q: Do all short haired dogs shed a lot?

A: No, not all short haired dogs shed a lot. Shedding can vary depending on the breed, genetics, health, diet, and environment.

Q: How often should I brush my short haired dog?

A: You should brush your short haired dog at least once a week, but more frequently during shedding seasons.

Q: Can diet affect a short haired dog’s shedding?

A: Yes, a healthy diet can improve a short haired dog’s coat health and reduce shedding.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential when managing a short haired dog’s shedding. Here are some tips:

  • Use a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner to keep your dog’s coat healthy and shiny.
  • Trim your dog’s nails regularly to prevent them from scratching their skin and causing more shedding.
  • Check your dog’s ears and teeth regularly to ensure they are healthy and clean.

Managing a short haired dog’s shedding requires time, effort, and patience. However, with the right tools, tips, and tricks, you can keep your home clean and your dog healthy and happy.
