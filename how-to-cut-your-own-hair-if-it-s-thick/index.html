How To Cut Your Own Hair If It s Thick A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts? Do you have thick hair that seems impossible to manage? If so, you're not alone. Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair if it's thick.

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous and beautiful, it can also be challenging to manage. If you have thick hair, you know that it can take longer to style and can be more prone to frizz and tangles. Additionally, thick hair can be difficult to cut if you don't have the right tools or experience. However, by following the tips and techniques in this article, you can learn how to cut your own hair and achieve a sleek and stylish look.

How to Cut Your Own Hair if it's Thick

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own hair is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a comb or brush, hair clips, and a handheld mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while you cut it.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need to wash and dry it. This will make it easier to cut and will ensure that your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. Once your hair is clean, blow-dry it straight to make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair into four parts: two on the top and two on the bottom. Use hair clips to secure each section, leaving one section loose to work with.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer of your hair, comb out any tangles and hold the ends between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut off any dead or split ends, being careful not to cut off too much.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, release the top section of your hair and repeat the process. Comb out any tangles and hold the ends between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut off any dead or split ends, blending them with the bottom layer.

Step 6: Check for Symmetry

After you've finished cutting both layers of your hair, use your handheld mirror to check for symmetry. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is even and balanced.

Step 7: Add Layers (Optional)

If you want to add layers to your hair, release one section at a time and cut the ends at an angle. This will create a layered effect and add volume to your hair.

Step 8: Trim Your Bangs (Optional)

If you have bangs, you can trim them by holding them between your fingers and cutting them straight across. Alternatively, you can angle them slightly for a softer look.

Tips

- Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

- Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.

- Cut your hair when it's dry to avoid any surprises once it's dry.

- Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring and time-consuming, so don't rush.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your hair if it's curly. However, keep in mind that curly hair will look shorter when it's dry than when it's wet, so be conservative with your cuts.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always make adjustments or seek the help of a professional stylist.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type. Additionally, avoid using heat tools too often, as they can damage your hair. If you do use heat tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage. Finally, consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.

Conclusion

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can learn how to cut your own hair if it's thick and achieve a sleek and stylish look.
