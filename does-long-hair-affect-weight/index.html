Does Long Hair Affect Weight The Truth About This Hair Myth

Written by Robby Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who loves having long hair, you might have heard the myth that having long hair can add extra weight to your body. But is there any truth to this claim? In this article, we'll take a closer look at this myth and help you understand whether or not there's any scientific evidence to support it.

Does Long Hair Really Add Weight?

First things first, it's important to understand that your hair is made up of dead cells and doesn't have any metabolic activity. This means that your hair doesn't contribute to your body's weight in any significant way.

According to experts, the average person's hair weighs between 3-5% of their total body weight. So, even if you have long and thick hair, it's unlikely that it would add more than a few ounces to your overall weight.

Factors That Can Affect Your Hair's Weight

While your hair itself doesn't have any impact on your body weight, there are some factors that can affect how heavy your hair feels on your head. These include:

Hair Density

If you have a lot of hair, it might feel heavier on your head simply because of its density. However, this doesn't mean that your hair is actually adding weight to your body.

Hair Length

The longer your hair is, the more weight it will have. However, as we mentioned earlier, the weight of your hair is unlikely to be significant enough to affect your overall body weight.

Hair Products

Using heavy hair products like oils, serums, and gels can make your hair feel heavier on your head. However, this is just a temporary effect that doesn't contribute to your body weight in any way.

How to Care for Long Hair

Now that we've established that long hair doesn't actually affect your body weight, let's talk about how to care for your long locks. Here are some tips:

Regular Trims

Getting regular trims is important for keeping your hair healthy and preventing split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, even if you're trying to grow your hair out.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help prevent breakage and keep your hair looking healthy.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to limit your use of these tools as much as possible.

Use a Heat Protectant

If you do use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage to your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does cutting your hair make it grow faster?

No, cutting your hair doesn't actually make it grow faster. However, getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair look and feel healthier.

How often should you wash long hair?

How often you should wash your hair depends on your hair type and personal preference. However, most experts recommend washing your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from getting too dry.

Does brushing your hair make it grow faster?

No, brushing your hair doesn't actually make it grow faster. However, it can help distribute your hair's natural oils and make it look shinier and healthier.

The Bottom Line

So, does long hair affect weight? The answer is no. While your hair might feel heavy on your head, it doesn't actually contribute to your body weight in any significant way. However, taking good care of your hair is important for keeping it healthy and looking its best.
