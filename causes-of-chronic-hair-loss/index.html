Causes Of Chronic Hair Loss Understanding The Culprits Behind Thinning Hair

Written by Bardi Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss that seems to never go away? If so, you may have chronic hair loss. This type of hair loss can be frustrating and overwhelming, but understanding the causes can help you take the necessary steps to address it. In this article, we will discuss the common causes of chronic hair loss and provide tips on how to manage it.

Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances are a leading cause of chronic hair loss. The most common hormonal imbalance that causes hair loss is androgenetic alopecia or female/male pattern baldness. This condition causes hair follicles to shrink, leading to thinning hair and eventual hair loss. Other hormonal imbalances that can cause chronic hair loss include thyroid disorders, polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), and pregnancy.

Nutrition Deficiencies

Nutrition deficiencies can also be a cause of chronic hair loss. If your body is lacking in essential nutrients like protein, iron, and vitamin D, it can lead to weak and thinning hair. A diet low in protein can cause hair to enter the resting phase prematurely, leading to hair loss.

Stress

Stress can have a significant impact on your hair health. When you are stressed, your body produces cortisol, a hormone that can interfere with hair growth. Stress can also cause you to pull your hair out, leading to further hair loss. If you are experiencing chronic stress, it's important to find ways to manage it to prevent hair loss.

Medications

Sometimes, chronic hair loss can be a side effect of medications. Chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and antidepressants are just a few examples of medications that can cause hair loss. If you are experiencing hair loss and are taking medication, talk to your doctor to see if there are any alternatives or if the dosage can be adjusted.

Genetics

Genetics can also play a role in chronic hair loss. If you have a family history of hair loss, you may be more likely to experience it yourself. Androgenetic alopecia, for example, is a genetic condition that is passed down through families.

How to Manage Chronic Hair Loss

If you are experiencing chronic hair loss, there are steps you can take to manage it. The first step is to identify the cause of your hair loss. Once you know the underlying cause, you can take steps to address it. For example, if your hair loss is due to a nutrition deficiency, you can adjust your diet to include more protein, iron, and vitamin D. If your hair loss is due to stress, you can practice relaxation techniques like yoga or meditation.

Hair Care Tips

Proper hair care can also help manage chronic hair loss. Avoid using harsh chemical treatments like relaxers and perms, which can damage hair and cause it to break. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner and avoid over-styling your hair. If you must use heat tools, use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant.

FAQ

1. Can chronic hair loss be reversed?

It depends on the cause of your hair loss. If the underlying cause is addressed, hair loss can sometimes be reversed. For example, if your hair loss is due to a nutrition deficiency and you start eating a more balanced diet, your hair may start to grow back. However, if your hair loss is due to genetics, it may not be reversible.

2. Can stress cause permanent hair loss?

Stress can cause hair loss, but it's usually temporary. Once the stress is managed or resolved, hair will usually grow back. However, if stress is chronic, it can lead to permanent hair loss.

3. How long does it take for hair to grow back after chronic hair loss?

It depends on the individual and the underlying cause of the hair loss. Hair typically grows about half an inch per month, so it can take several months to a year for hair to fully grow back.


Chronic hair loss can be a challenging condition to deal with, but with the right approach, it can be managed. By identifying the underlying cause of your hair loss and taking steps to address it, you can start to see improvements in your hair health and overall well-being. Remember to be patient and consistent in your efforts to manage chronic hair loss.
