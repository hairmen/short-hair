How To Use Thinning Scissors On Men s Hair

Written by Daniel Jul 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to add texture and volume to men's hair, thinning scissors can be a great tool to use. Thinning scissors are specifically designed to remove bulk from the hair without changing the overall length, making them ideal for creating a natural, effortless look. In this article, we'll go over how to use thinning scissors on men's hair step by step, along with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you get the best results.

  2. Step by Step Guide

  4. FAQs About Using Thinning Scissors on Men's Hair
  5. Hair Care Tips for Men's Hair

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Men's Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that thinning scissors should only be used on dry hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to work with, and you may end up removing too much hair if you're not careful. Here's how to use thinning scissors on men's hair:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into manageable sections. Use clips to hold the hair in place, and work on one section at a time. This will make it easier to see what you're doing and prevent you from accidentally thinning out too much hair.

Step 2: Hold the Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinning scissors with your dominant hand, with your thumb in the lower hole and your ring and middle fingers in the upper hole. This will give you the most control over the scissors and allow you to make precise cuts.

Step 3: Start at the Mid-Length

Begin by cutting the hair at the mid-length. Make sure you're only cutting a small section of hair at a time, and keep the scissors at a slight angle to the hair. This will help you remove bulk without creating visible lines or gaps in the hair.

Step 4: Work Your Way Up

Once you've worked your way through the mid-length of the hair, start cutting at the roots and work your way up towards the mid-length. Again, make sure you're only removing a small section of hair at a time, and keep the scissors at a slight angle to the hair.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

As you work, check your progress in a mirror to make sure you're not removing too much hair. It can be easy to get carried away with thinning scissors, so take your time and be patient.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide to using thinning scissors on men's hair:

  1. Section the hair into manageable sections.
  2. Hold the thinning scissors correctly with your dominant hand.
  3. Start cutting at the mid-length of the hair.
  4. Work your way up towards the roots.
  5. Check your progress in a mirror as you work.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors on Men's Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors on men's hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean, precise cuts.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, or you risk creating visible lines or gaps in the hair.
  • Work slowly and carefully, checking your progress as you go.
  • Use a comb to guide the scissors and ensure even cutting.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on very short hair, as it can create a choppy, uneven look.

FAQs About Using Thinning Scissors on Men's Hair

Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

Thinning scissors can be used on most hair types, but they may not be suitable for very short hair or hair that is already thin or fine. It's best to consult with a stylist if you're unsure whether thinning scissors are right for your hair.

How often should you use thinning scissors on men's hair?

This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. In general, it's best to use thinning scissors every few months to maintain a natural, textured look.

Can thinning scissors damage the hair?

Thinning scissors are designed to remove bulk without damaging the hair, but using them incorrectly or too often can cause damage. It's important to use sharp scissors and work slowly and carefully to avoid damaging the hair.

Hair Care Tips for Men's Hair

Here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors on men's hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Using thinning scissors on men's hair can be a great way to add texture and volume without changing the overall length. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a natural, effortless look that's perfect for any occasion.
