How To Make A Double Chin Look Smaller With Makeup

Written by Eveline Sep 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of feeling self-conscious about your double chin? While there are exercises and treatments that can help reduce the appearance of a double chin, there's also a simple makeup technique that can make a big difference. In this article, we'll show you how to make a double chin look smaller with makeup.

Description

A double chin, also known as submental fat, is a common issue that affects both men and women. It's caused by an accumulation of fat under the chin, which can be due to genetics, weight gain, or aging. While there are surgical and non-surgical treatments available, many people prefer a more natural approach. Makeup can be a great way to camouflage a double chin and create the illusion of a more defined facial structure.

How to

Step 1: Prep your skin

Before applying makeup, it's important to prepare your skin. Start by cleansing your face with a gentle cleanser and moisturizing with a lightweight, oil-free moisturizer. This will help create a smooth, even base for your makeup.

Step 2: Apply foundation

Choose a foundation that matches your skin tone and apply it evenly all over your face and neck. Make sure to blend it well, especially around your jawline and under your chin. This will help create a seamless transition between your face and neck, making your chin appear smaller.

Step 3: Use contouring

Contouring is a makeup technique that involves using darker shades to create the illusion of shadows and depth. To make your double chin look smaller, use a contouring powder or cream that's one or two shades darker than your skin tone. Apply it under your chin, along your jawline, and on the sides of your nose. This will help create the illusion of a more defined jawline and a slimmer face.

Step 4: Highlight

Highlighting is the opposite of contouring and involves using lighter shades to bring out your best features. To make your double chin look smaller, use a highlighter that's one or two shades lighter than your skin tone. Apply it to the center of your chin, the tip of your nose, and the tops of your cheekbones. This will help draw attention away from your chin and towards your other features.

Step 5: Blend

Once you've applied your contouring and highlighting, it's important to blend it well. Use a makeup sponge or brush to blend the darker and lighter shades together, creating a seamless, natural look.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you make your double chin look smaller with makeup:

  • Use a matte foundation and powder to help minimize shine and create a more natural look.
  • Avoid using shimmery or glittery makeup on your chin, as this can draw attention to the area.
  • Apply your makeup in natural light to ensure that it looks as natural as possible.
  • Consider using a neck cream or serum to help firm and tighten the skin under your chin over time.

FAQ

Can makeup really make a double chin look smaller?

Yes, with the right techniques, makeup can help create the illusion of a more defined jawline and a slimmer face. While it won't actually reduce the amount of fat under your chin, it can make it less noticeable.

What's the best type of contouring product to use?

It depends on your skin type and personal preference. Some people prefer powder contouring products, while others prefer cream or liquid formulas. Experiment with different products to find what works best for you.

Do I need to use both contouring and highlighting?

No, you can use one or the other, or both. It depends on your personal preference and the look you're trying to achieve.

Hair Care

In addition to makeup, your hairstyle can also help create the illusion of a more defined jawline. Consider getting a haircut that's shorter around your chin, or try wearing your hair up in a high ponytail or bun. This will help draw attention upwards towards your face, rather than downwards towards your chin.

With these simple makeup and hair tips, you can make your double chin look smaller and feel more confident in your appearance. Give them a try and see what works best for you!
