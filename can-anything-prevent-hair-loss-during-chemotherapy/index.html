Can Anything Prevent Hair Loss During Chemotherapy

Written by Frank Aug 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Chemotherapy is a treatment that is used to destroy cancer cells, but unfortunately, it also affects healthy cells in the body, including hair follicles. This often leads to hair loss, which can be a distressing side effect for many patients. While there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy, there are some steps you can take to reduce the severity of the side effect.

How to Prevent Hair Loss During Chemotherapy

It's important to note that not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss. Some drugs only cause thinning of the hair, while others cause complete hair loss. If you are concerned about hair loss during chemotherapy, talk to your doctor about the drugs you will be receiving and their potential side effects.

While there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy, there are a few things you can do to reduce the severity of the side effect:

  • Use a scalp cooling system - this is a device that cools the scalp during chemotherapy, which can reduce blood flow to the hair follicles and make them less susceptible to damage.
  • Use gentle hair care products - avoid using harsh shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that can damage your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling - blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners can further damage your hair, making it more likely to fall out.
  • Try a wig or head covering - many cancer patients choose to wear wigs or head coverings during chemotherapy to help them feel more comfortable and confident.

Scalp Cooling System

A scalp cooling system is a device that is placed on the head during chemotherapy. The device circulates cold water through a cap that is worn on the head, which helps to reduce blood flow to the hair follicles. This can make the hair follicles less susceptible to damage from chemotherapy drugs.

Scalp cooling systems have been shown to be effective in reducing hair loss during chemotherapy, but they are not available at all treatment centers. If you are interested in using a scalp cooling system, talk to your doctor about your options.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you are interested in using a scalp cooling system during chemotherapy, here is a step-by-step guide:

  1. Talk to your doctor - your doctor can help you determine if a scalp cooling system is right for you.
  2. Choose a treatment center - not all treatment centers offer scalp cooling systems, so you will need to find one that does.
  3. Schedule your treatment - scalp cooling systems are typically used 30 minutes before chemotherapy and for 90 minutes after chemotherapy.
  4. Prepare for your treatment - bring a hat or scarf to wear during your treatment, as the cap can be cold and uncomfortable.
  5. Use the cooling system - the cap will be placed on your head and connected to the cooling system. The cap will be cold, but it should not be painful.

Tips for Managing Hair Loss During Chemotherapy

If you do experience hair loss during chemotherapy, here are some tips to help you manage the side effect:

  • Be gentle with your hair - use a soft brush or comb and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles - tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can pull on your hair and increase hair loss.
  • Protect your scalp - wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold.
  • Consider a wig or head covering - many cancer patients find that wearing a wig or head covering can help them feel more comfortable and confident.

FAQs

Does all chemotherapy cause hair loss?

No, not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss. Some drugs only cause thinning of the hair, while others cause complete hair loss. Talk to your doctor about the drugs you will be receiving and their potential side effects.

Are scalp cooling systems effective?

Yes, scalp cooling systems have been shown to be effective in reducing hair loss during chemotherapy. However, they are not available at all treatment centers.

Can I still use hair care products during chemotherapy?

Yes, you can still use hair care products during chemotherapy, but it's important to choose gentle products that won't damage your hair.

Hair Care During Chemotherapy

If you do experience hair loss during chemotherapy, it's important to take good care of your scalp and any remaining hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use gentle hair care products - choose shampoos and conditioners that are formulated for sensitive scalps.
  • Avoid heat styling - blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners can further damage your hair.
  • Be gentle with your hair - use a soft brush or comb and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.
  • Protect your scalp - wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold.

While there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy, there are steps you can take to reduce the severity of the side effect. Talk to your doctor about your options and take good care of your scalp and any remaining hair.
