How To Cut Your Hair So It s Straight At The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. One of the most challenging parts of cutting your hair is getting it straight at the back. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your hair so it's straight at the back, with tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect cut every time.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a great way to save money and have more control over your style. However, it can be difficult to achieve a straight cut at the back of your head, especially if you're working alone. This guide will show you how to cut your hair so it's straight at the back, with step-by-step instructions and helpful tips to make the process easier.

How to Cut Your Hair So It's Straight at the Back: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror or a mirror positioned behind you. Make sure your scissors are clean and sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair before you start cutting. This will give you a better idea of how your hair falls naturally, and will help you to see any split ends or damaged areas that need to be trimmed.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using your comb, divide your hair into sections. Start at the top of your head and work your way down, using clips to secure each section as you go. This will help you to see the different layers of your hair and make it easier to cut each section evenly.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Use your hair cutting scissors to trim the ends, holding the scissors at an angle to create a slightly rounded shape. Work your way around the back of your head, cutting each section of hair to the same length.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Use your handheld mirror or mirror positioned behind you to check your progress. Look for any uneven areas or sections that need to be trimmed further. If you notice any areas that need to be re-cut, repeat steps 4 and 5 until you achieve the desired length.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

After cutting the back of your hair, it's time to blend the layers. Unclip the top sections of your hair and use your scissors to blend the layers together. Hold the scissors at an angle and make small snips, working your way down each section of hair to create a smooth blend.

Step 7: Finish the Cut

Once you've blended the layers, it's time to finish the cut. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven areas, and check your progress in the mirror to make sure everything looks even.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve a great haircut at home:

  • Start with small snips. It's always better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use a comb to help you guide your scissors and create an even cut.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in an uneven cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your hair at home:

Q: Can I cut my own hair if I have curly or wavy hair?

A: Yes, you can still cut your own hair if you have curly or wavy hair. However, be aware that cutting curly or wavy hair can be tricky, and it's best to start with small snips and work your way up to a longer cut.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: This will depend on the length and style of your hair, as well as your personal preference. As a general rule, most people should aim to cut their hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and trimmed.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

A: While it's possible to use regular household scissors to cut your hair, it's not recommended. Household scissors are often dull and can cause split ends and uneven cuts. Invest in a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors for the best results.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for caring for your hair after a cut:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep it looking healthy.

Cutting your own hair can be a rewarding experience, but it takes practice and patience to get it right. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut at home and save money on salon visits. Good luck!
