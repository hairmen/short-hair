Is It Bad To Thin Out Thick Curly Hair

Written by Frank Jun 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it looks amazing, it can be tough to manage and style. One solution that people often turn to is thinning out their hair. This involves cutting some of the hair to reduce its thickness and create a more manageable style. But is it bad to thin out thick curly hair? Let's explore this topic in more detail.

Description

Thinning out thick curly hair involves using special scissors to cut the hair at different lengths. This process can help to remove some of the bulk and make the hair more manageable to style. However, it's important to understand that thinning out hair is not the same as getting a haircut. A haircut involves cutting the hair to a specific length, while thinning out hair involves cutting some of the hair to reduce its thickness.

How To Thin Out Thick Curly Hair

Thinning out thick curly hair should be done by a professional hairstylist who has experience working with curly hair. The hairstylist will use special thinning shears to cut the hair at different lengths, creating a more layered and textured look. It's important to communicate with your hairstylist about the look you want to achieve and how much hair you want to be thinned out.

Step by Step Process to Thin Out Thick Curly Hair

Here are the steps involved in thinning out thick curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Blow-dry your hair or let it air-dry naturally.
  3. Section your hair into small sections using hair clips.
  4. Take one section of hair and comb through it to remove any tangles.
  5. Use the thinning shears to cut the hair at different lengths, starting from the bottom of the hair and working your way up.
  6. Repeat this process for each section of hair.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out thick curly hair:

  • Always go to a professional hairstylist who has experience working with curly hair.
  • Communicate with your hairstylist about the look you want to achieve and how much hair you want to be thinned out.
  • Don't thin out your hair too much, as this can make it look wispy and uneven.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQ

Is it bad to thin out thick curly hair?

Thinning out thick curly hair is not necessarily bad, but it should be done by a professional hairstylist who has experience working with curly hair. If done incorrectly, it can damage your hair and make it look uneven.

Will thinning out my hair make it easier to manage?

Thinning out your hair can make it easier to manage, as it can reduce the amount of bulk and create a more textured and layered look. However, it's important to communicate with your hairstylist about the look you want to achieve and how much hair you want to be thinned out.

How often should I thin out my hair?

How often you should thin out your hair depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to thin it out. It's best to consult with your hairstylist to determine the best schedule for thinning out your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind if you have thick curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as this can damage your hair.

Overall, thinning out thick curly hair can be a great way to create a more manageable style. However, it's important to go to a professional hairstylist who has experience working with curly hair and to communicate your desired look. With the right care and maintenance, you can keep your thick curly hair looking healthy and beautiful.
