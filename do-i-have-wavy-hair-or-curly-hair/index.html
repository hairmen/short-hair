Do I Have Wavy Hair Or Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Sep 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you often wonder if you have wavy hair or curly hair? It can be confusing to differentiate between the two, especially if you have never paid attention to your hair type. In this article, we will help you determine if you have wavy or curly hair and provide you with some tips on how to care for your hair type.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Determine Your Hair Type
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Caring for Wavy and Curly Hair

Description

Wavy hair and curly hair are two distinct hair types that can be easily confused. Wavy hair has a looser curl pattern, while curly hair has a tighter curl pattern. Wavy hair typically forms an "S" shape, while curly hair forms a more defined spiral or coil. Understanding your hair type is essential in determining the best hair care routine and products to use.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

There are a few ways to determine if you have wavy hair or curly hair. One way is to examine your hair after washing and air-drying it. If your hair forms loose waves, you have wavy hair. If your hair forms tight spirals or coils, you have curly hair.

Another way to determine your hair type is to do the hair strand test. Take a strand of hair and stretch it. If your hair stretches without breaking and has a slight curve, you have wavy hair. If your hair stretches without breaking and forms a tight coil, you have curly hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you are still unsure about your hair type, follow these steps to determine your hair type:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Gently pat your hair dry with a towel.
  3. Let your hair air dry without brushing or styling it.
  4. Observe the texture and shape of your hair once it is dry.

If your hair forms loose waves, you have wavy hair. If your hair forms tight spirals or coils, you have curly hair.

Tips for Caring for Wavy and Curly Hair

Wavy hair and curly hair require different hair care routines and products. Here are some tips for caring for wavy and curly hair:

Wavy Hair

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Use a lightweight conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your natural waves.

Curly Hair

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Use a rich, creamy conditioner to nourish your curls.
  • Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb while it is wet to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls.

FAQs

What causes wavy hair?

Wavy hair is caused by genetics. It is often the result of having a mix of straight and curly hair genes.

Can wavy hair turn curly?

Wavy hair can sometimes turn curly due to hormonal changes, such as puberty or pregnancy.

What causes curly hair?

Curly hair is also caused by genetics. It is often the result of having several curly hair genes.

Hair Care Products for Wavy and Curly Hair

Using the right hair care products is essential in maintaining healthy wavy or curly hair. Here are some hair care products that are perfect for wavy and curly hair:

  • Sulfate-free shampoo
  • Lightweight conditioner
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Curl enhancing cream
  • Detangling spray

Now that you know whether you have wavy hair or curly hair, you can start taking care of your hair the right way. Remember to use the right hair care products and follow the tips provided in this article to keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
