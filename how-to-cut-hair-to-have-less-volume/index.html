How to Cut Hair to Have Less Volume

If you have thick and voluminous hair, it can be a challenge to manage. Fortunately, there are ways to cut your hair to have less volume. In this article, we will discuss step by step how to cut hair to have less volume, tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care tips.

  • Step by Step

How to Cut Hair to Have Less Volume

When you want to cut your hair to have less volume, you need to consider the shape and length of your hair. The right cut and style can help to reduce the volume of your hair, making it easier to manage. Here are some tips to help you cut your hair to have less volume:

1. Consider a Layered Cut

A layered cut can help to reduce the volume of your hair. Layers will add texture and movement to your hair, which can make it look thinner. Talk to your hairstylist about the best layering technique that will work for your hair type and length.

2. Avoid Blunt Cuts

Blunt cuts can make your hair look thicker and voluminous. If you want to reduce the volume of your hair, avoid blunt cuts. Instead, opt for softer cuts that will create a natural flow and movement in your hair.

3. Keep it Short

Shorter haircuts can help to reduce the volume of your hair. A shorter haircut will make your hair look lighter and less voluminous. If you're not ready for a short haircut, consider a shoulder-length cut that will still help to reduce the volume of your hair.

4. Thin Out Your Hair

Thinning out your hair can help to reduce the volume of your hair. Ask your hairstylist to use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk from your hair. This technique works well for thick and voluminous hair.

5. Consider Bangs

Bangs can help to reduce the volume of your hair, especially if you have a lot of hair around your face. Bangs will add a focal point to your hair, which can make it look thinner. Talk to your hairstylist about the best type of bangs that will work for your face shape and hair type.

Step by Step

Here are the step-by-step instructions to cut your hair to have less volume:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting from the back of your head to the front.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Using sharp scissors, start cutting your hair from the bottom of each section. Cut at an angle to create a layered look.

Step 4: Thin Out Your Hair

Use thinning shears to thin out your hair, especially around the crown of your head.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired using a hairbrush, hairdryer, or straightening iron.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you cut your hair to have less volume:

1. Use the Right Tools

Choose sharp scissors and thinning shears to cut your hair.

2. Consider Your Face Shape

When choosing a haircut, consider your face shape and hair type.

3. Consult with a Hairstylist

Consult with a hairstylist to get the best haircut for your hair type and face shape.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting hair to have less volume:

1. Will cutting my hair reduce its thickness?

Yes, cutting your hair can reduce its thickness, especially if you choose a layered cut or thinning technique.

2. Can I cut my hair at home?

It's best to consult with a hairstylist to get the best haircut for your hair type and face shape. However, if you have experience cutting hair, you can cut your hair at home.

3. How often should I cut my hair to reduce its volume?

You should cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and reduce its volume.

Hair Care

Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain your hair after cutting it to have less volume:

1. Use the Right Products

Use hair products that are designed for your hair type and texture.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as straightening irons and blow dryers, as they can damage your hair and make it look thicker.

3. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a hair protectant spray.

4. Brush Your Hair

Brush your hair regularly to distribute the natural oils and prevent tangles.
