Can You Curl Your Hair With A Fat Straightener

Written by Christine Oct 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to styling hair, there are a lot of tools and techniques to choose from. One popular method is using a fat straightener to create curls. If you're wondering whether this is possible, the answer is yes! In this article, we'll explore how to curl your hair with a fat straightener, as well as some tips and tricks for achieving the perfect look.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Curl Your Hair With a Fat Straightener
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

A fat straightener, also known as a wide or thick straightener, is a hair styling tool that is typically used to straighten hair. However, it can also be used to create loose, beachy waves or tight, bouncy curls. The key to using a fat straightener for curling is in the technique. By twisting and pulling the hair through the plates, you can create curls that last for hours.

How to Curl Your Hair With a Fat Straightener

To curl your hair with a fat straightener, you'll need a few key tools:

  • A fat straightener
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Sectioning clips
  • Hairspray or other styling products (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to curl your hair with a fat straightener:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is damp, it can be more difficult to achieve curls that last.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to minimize damage from the straightener.
  3. Section your hair into small sections using sectioning clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that all parts of your hair are curled.
  4. Starting with a small section of hair, clamp the straightener down near the roots.
  5. Twist the straightener away from your face or towards your face, depending on the direction you want your curls to go. Keep the straightener in this position for a few seconds, then release.
  6. Repeat this process with the rest of your hair, working in small sections and twisting the straightener in different directions to create a natural, tousled look.
  7. Once you've curled all of your hair, use a light hairspray or other styling product to set the curls in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks for curling your hair with a fat straightener:

  • Experiment with different twisting motions to create different types of curls. For example, twisting the straightener towards your face will create curls that frame your face, while twisting away from your face will create more natural-looking waves.
  • Don't clamp the straightener down too hard on your hair, as this can create creases or kinks in your curls.
  • Use a lower heat setting on your straightener to minimize damage to your hair.
  • If you want tighter curls, use smaller sections of hair.
  • If you want looser curls or waves, use larger sections of hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curling your hair with a fat straightener:

  • What type of hair is best for curling with a fat straightener? You can curl any type of hair with a fat straightener, but it may work best on hair that is shoulder-length or longer and has some natural texture or wave.
  • What's the difference between using a fat straightener and a curling iron? A fat straightener can create a more natural, tousled look than a curling iron, which can create tighter, more defined curls.
  • How long will the curls last? The longevity of your curls will depend on your hair type, the products you use, and the technique you use to curl your hair. In general, you can expect your curls to last for several hours or even all day.

Hair Care and Maintenance

To keep your hair healthy and looking great after curling with a fat straightener, here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a heat protectant spray every time you use heat styling tools, including your fat straightener.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray or other styling products, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to curl your hair with a fat straightener, you can experiment with different styles and techniques to create the perfect look. Remember to use a heat protectant spray, work in small sections, and don't be afraid to try different twisting motions to achieve your desired look. With a little practice and some patience, you can create gorgeous, bouncy curls that last all day.
