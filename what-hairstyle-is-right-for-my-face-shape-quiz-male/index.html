In this article, we'll be discussing what hairstyle is right for your face shape and provide you with a quiz to determine your face shape. With the right hairstyle, you'll be able to enhance your facial features and look your best!

Face Shape Quiz

Before we dive into the hairstyles, let's determine your face shape. Take this quiz to find out:

  1. What is the widest part of your face?
    • a. Forehead
    • b. Cheekbones
    • c. Jawline
  2. What is the shape of your jawline?
    • a. Round
    • b. Square
    • c. Pointed
  3. What is the length of your face?
    • a. Short
    • b. Medium
    • c. Long
  4. What is the shape of your forehead?
    • a. Round
    • b. Flat
    • c. Pointed
  5. What is the shape of your face?
    • a. Round
    • b. Oval
    • c. Square
    • d. Rectangle
    • e. Triangle
    • f. Diamond
    • g. Heart

Add up your answers to determine your face shape:

  • Mostly A's: Round Face Shape
  • Mostly B's: Oval Face Shape
  • Mostly C's: Square Face Shape
  • Mostly D's: Rectangle Face Shape
  • Mostly E's: Triangle Face Shape
  • Mostly F's: Diamond Face Shape
  • Mostly G's: Heart Face Shape

Round Face Shape

For those with a round face shape, it's best to go for hairstyles that add height and length to the face. This can be achieved with longer hairstyles and side-swept bangs. Avoid hairstyles that add width to the face such as blunt bangs and chin-length haircuts.

Hairstyles for Round Face Shape:

  • Short sides and long top
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Messy quiff
  • Layered haircut

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, congratulations! You have the most versatile face shape and can pull off almost any hairstyle. It's best to avoid hairstyles that cover up your face and opt for styles that showcase your facial features.

Hairstyles for Oval Face Shape:

  • Short sides and long top
  • Crew cut
  • Side part
  • Buzz cut

Square Face Shape

For those with a square face shape, it's best to go for hairstyles that soften the angles of the face. This can be achieved with longer hairstyles and messy textured haircuts. Avoid hairstyles that add width to the face such as blunt bangs and chin-length haircuts.

Hairstyles for Square Face Shape:

  • Messy textured haircuts
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Long layers
  • Short sides and long top

Rectangle Face Shape

If you have a rectangle face shape, it's best to go for hairstyles that add width to the face. This can be achieved with hairstyles that have volume on the sides and shorter hair on top. Avoid hairstyles that make the face look longer such as slicked-back hair and long hairstyles.

Hairstyles for Rectangle Face Shape:

  • Side-swept bangs
  • Short sides and long top
  • Medium length layered haircut
  • Textured crop

Triangle Face Shape

For those with a triangle face shape, it's best to go for hairstyles that add width to the forehead and temples. This can be achieved with hairstyles that have volume on top and shorter hair on the sides. Avoid hairstyles that add width to the jawline such as heavy sideburns and chin-length haircuts.

Hairstyles for Triangle Face Shape:

  • Textured crop
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Short sides and long top
  • Messy quiff

Diamond Face Shape

For those with a diamond face shape, it's best to go for hairstyles that add width to the forehead and chin. This can be achieved with hairstyles that have volume on top and shorter hair on the sides. Avoid hairstyles that add width to the cheekbones such as heavy sideburns and chin-length haircuts.

Hairstyles for Diamond Face Shape:

  • Side-swept bangs
  • Short sides and long top
  • Textured crop
  • Messy quiff

Heart Face Shape

For those with a heart face shape, it's best to go for hairstyles that add width to the forehead and temples. This can be achieved with hairstyles that have volume on top and shorter hair on the sides. Avoid hairstyles that add width to the chin such as heavy sideburns and chin-length haircuts.

Hairstyles for Heart Face Shape:

  • Side-swept bangs
  • Short sides and long top
  • Textured crop
  • Messy quiff

Hairstyle Tips

Now that you know what hairstyle is right for your face shape, here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Consider your hair type and texture when choosing a hairstyle.
  • Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best haircut for your face shape.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles and products to find what works best for you.
  • Don't be afraid to change up your hairstyle to keep things fresh and new.

FAQs

What face shape do I have?

You can determine your face shape by measuring the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, as well as the length of your face. Alternatively, you can take a quiz like the one provided in this article.

Can I pull off any hairstyle if I have an oval face shape?

While those with an oval face shape can pull off most hairstyles, it's still important to consider your hair type and texture, as well as your personal style preferences.

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a square face shape?

Avoid hairstyles that add width to the face, such as blunt bangs and chin-length haircuts.
