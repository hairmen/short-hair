How To Start Dreads On Natural Hair A Simple Guide

Written by Christine Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dreads or dreadlocks have become a popular hairstyle for many people around the world. It is a hairstyle that is not only trendy but also has a cultural significance. For those with natural hair, starting dreads can be a bit daunting, but with the right guidance, it can be an easy process. In this article, we will be discussing how to start dreads on natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare Your Hair for Dreading
  • How to Start Your Dreads
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads
  • Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
  • Hair Care for Your Dreads

How to Prepare Your Hair for Dreading

Before starting the process of dreading your natural hair, it is essential to prepare your hair for the process. Here are some things you should do:

1. Clean Your Hair

It is essential to have clean hair before starting the process of dreading. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up or residue from your hair.

2. Detangle Your Hair

It is important to detangle your hair before starting the process of dreading. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair.

3. Trim Your Ends

Trimming the ends of your hair can help prevent split ends and make the dreading process smoother.

How to Start Your Dreads

Now that you have prepared your hair, it is time to start the dreading process. Here is how to start your dreads on natural hair:

1. Choose Your Method

There are various methods of starting dreads, such as the palm rolling method, the two-strand twist method, and the backcombing method. Choose the method that works best for you.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using a rattail comb. The size of the sections will depend on your hair texture and the method you choose.

3. Start Dreading

Using your chosen method, start dreading each section of your hair. Be patient and take your time. It may take a few weeks or even months for your dreads to fully form.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Maintaining your dreads is essential to keep them healthy and looking good. Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

It is important to keep your dreads clean by washing them regularly. Use a residue-free shampoo to prevent build-up.

2. Retwist Your Dreads

Retwist your dreads every few weeks to help maintain their shape.

3. Protect Your Dreads

Protect your dreads while sleeping by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to prevent them from getting frizzy.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Can I start dreads on short hair?

Yes, you can start dreads on short hair. However, it may take longer for them to fully form.

2. How often should I wash my dreads?

It is recommended to wash your dreads every 1-2 weeks.

3. Can I dye my dreads?

Yes, you can dye your dreads. However, it is important to use a dye that is safe for dreadlocks and to deep condition your hair afterward.

Hair Care for Your Dreads

Taking care of your dreads is essential for their overall health and appearance. Here are some hair care tips for your dreads:

1. Oil Your Scalp

Use natural oils such as coconut oil or jojoba oil to moisturize your scalp and prevent dryness.

2. Avoid Using Wax

Avoid using wax on your dreads as it can cause build-up and prevent them from locking properly.

3. Trim Your Dreads

Trim the ends of your dreads every few months to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

In conclusion, starting dreads on natural hair can be a fun and exciting process. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can achieve the dreadlock hairstyle you desire. Remember to take care of your dreads to keep them healthy and looking their best.
