How To Trim My Own Side Swept Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of constantly going to the salon just to trim your side swept bangs? Well, you don't have to anymore! With our simple step-by-step guide, you can learn how to trim your own side swept bangs in the comfort of your own home. Read on to learn more!

Description: What are Side Swept Bangs?

Side swept bangs are a popular hairstyle where the hair is cut diagonally across the forehead, creating a soft, face-framing look. They are versatile and can work with many different hair types and face shapes. However, they do require maintenance and trimming every few weeks to keep them looking their best.

How to Trim My Own Side Swept Bangs

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to trim your own side swept bangs:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wet Your Bangs

Wet your bangs with water or a spray bottle to make them easier to work with. Comb them straight down over your forehead.

Step 3: Section Off Your Bangs

Clip the rest of your hair back, leaving only your bangs visible. Make sure they are combed straight down and sitting evenly across your forehead.

Step 4: Determine Your Desired Length

Decide how short you want your bangs to be. Keep in mind that it's always better to start longer and gradually cut more if needed.

Step 5: Begin Cutting

Starting at the center of your bangs, hold a small section between your fingers and cut straight across. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as you can always go back and trim more.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

After cutting a small section, let your bangs fall back into place and check for evenness. If one side is longer than the other, make small adjustments until they are even.

Step 7: Continue Cutting

Continue cutting small sections, working your way outwards from the center. Remember to check for evenness after each cut.

Step 8: Blend the Edges

Once you have reached the desired length, blend the edges by holding your scissors vertically and making small snips along the edge of your bangs. This will create a softer, more natural look.

Step 9: Style Your Bangs

Style your bangs as desired, using a blow dryer or flat iron if needed.

Step 10: Finish with Hair Spray

Finish with a light mist of hairspray to keep your bangs in place.

Tips for Trimming Your Own Side Swept Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when trimming your own side swept bangs:

  • Always start with clean, dry hair
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid any uneven cuts
  • Take your time and don't rush
  • Keep checking for evenness as you go
  • Start longer and gradually cut more if needed

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about trimming side swept bangs:

How often should I trim my side swept bangs?

You should trim your side swept bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking their best.

Can I trim my side swept bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still trim your side swept bangs if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut them when they are dry to avoid any shrinkage.

What if I make a mistake while trimming my bangs?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! You can always pin them back and wait for them to grow out, or visit a salon to have them fixed.

Hair Care for Side Swept Bangs

To keep your side swept bangs looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Trim your bangs regularly to prevent split ends

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can confidently trim your own side swept bangs and achieve a salon-worthy look at home. Happy trimming!
