How To Cut Hair In Long Layers Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair at home can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily. Long layers are a popular hairstyle that can add volume and movement to your hair. In this guide, we will show you how to cut hair in long layers yourself, without the need for a professional hairstylist.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Will Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Layers
  • Finishing Touches

  • FAQs

Tools You Will Need

Before you get started, you will need the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips

Make sure your scissors are sharp and designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve the desired look.

Preparation

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may lead to uneven cuts.

Next, decide where you want your layers to start. You can choose to have them start at your chin, collarbone, or even lower.

Sectioning Your Hair

Begin by parting your hair down the middle and brush it out. Then, separate your hair into four equal sections, two in the front and two in the back.

Take one of the back sections and split it in half horizontally, creating a top and bottom section. Secure the top section with a hair tie or clip.

Repeat this process with the other back section and then move on to the front sections.

Cutting Your Hair

Start with one of the bottom sections in the back. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, keeping it taut.

Using your scissors, cut a small amount off the ends of the hair. Remember, it's better to cut less at first and then go back and trim more if needed.

Continue cutting small sections of hair and working your way up the section. Once you've finished one section, release the top section and repeat on the other side.

Once you've finished the back sections, move on to the front. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the sections you created earlier as a guide.

Blending Your Layers

After you've cut all of your layers, it's time to blend them together. Take a comb and comb through your hair, making sure all of the sections are even.

If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim those sections until your hair is even.

Finishing Touches

You've now successfully cut your hair in long layers! To finish off your new look, you can blow dry and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections and work your way up to avoid cutting off too much hair at once.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. It's better to cut less at first and then go back and trim more if needed.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair in long layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your hair in long layers if it's curly. However, it's best to cut your hair when it's dry and in its natural state to avoid any surprises.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go back and trim any uneven sections. If you're really unhappy with the results, you can always visit a professional hairstylist for help.

Hair Care Tips

  • Trim your hair regularly to keep your layers looking fresh.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools on your hair.
  • Invest in a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Cutting your own hair in long layers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking hairstyle from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have great-looking hair in no time!
