Does Hair Thinning Stop After Accutane

Written by Petter Sep 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Accutane is a powerful oral medication used to treat severe acne. While it can be effective in clearing up acne, it is also known to cause several side effects, including hair thinning. If you have taken Accutane and are experiencing hair thinning, you may be wondering if it will ever stop. In this article, we will explore this topic in-depth and provide you with tips on how to care for your hair during and after taking Accutane.

What is Accutane?

Accutane is a brand name for the generic drug isotretinoin, which is used to treat severe acne that has not responded to other treatments. It is a powerful medication that works by reducing the amount of oil produced by the skin's sebaceous glands, which can help prevent clogged pores and acne breakouts.

Does Accutane Cause Hair Thinning?

Yes, Accutane is known to cause hair thinning in some people. Hair thinning is listed as one of the potential side effects of the medication, and it is thought to be caused by the drug's ability to reduce oil production in the scalp. This reduction in oil can lead to dryness, irritation, and inflammation of the scalp, which can cause hair to become thin and brittle.

Will Hair Thinning Stop After Accutane?

It is possible for hair thinning to stop after Accutane treatment is discontinued. However, it may take some time for your hair to return to its normal thickness and for any hair loss to be reversed. The amount of time it takes for this to happen can vary from person to person, but it is generally thought to take several months to a year.

If you are experiencing hair thinning after taking Accutane, it is important to talk to your doctor. They may be able to recommend treatments or products to help promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss.

How to Care for Your Hair During Accutane?

If you are currently taking Accutane, there are several things you can do to care for your hair and reduce the risk of hair thinning. These include:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or other harsh chemicals
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like flat irons and curling irons
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it is wet
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair health

By following these tips, you can help keep your hair healthy and reduce the risk of hair thinning while taking Accutane.

How to Care for Your Hair After Accutane?

Once you have completed your Accutane treatment, it is important to continue caring for your hair to promote growth and prevent further thinning. Some tips for caring for your hair after Accutane include:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like flat irons and curling irons
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or other harsh chemicals
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair health
  • Consider using hair growth products like minoxidil or biotin supplements

By following these tips, you can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further thinning after completing your Accutane treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Accutane cause permanent hair loss?

While hair thinning is a common side effect of Accutane, it is usually reversible and does not cause permanent hair loss. However, in rare cases, Accutane may cause permanent hair loss.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after Accutane?

The amount of time it takes for hair to grow back after Accutane can vary from person to person. It may take several months to a year for hair to return to its normal thickness and for any hair loss to be reversed.

Can I color my hair while taking Accutane?

It is generally not recommended to color your hair while taking Accutane. The medication can make your scalp more sensitive and increase the risk of irritation and damage to your hair.

Conclusion

While hair thinning is a potential side effect of Accutane, it is usually reversible and does not cause permanent hair loss. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can help care for your hair during and after Accutane treatment and promote healthy hair growth. If you are experiencing hair thinning, be sure to talk to your doctor, as they may be able to recommend treatments or products to help promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss.
