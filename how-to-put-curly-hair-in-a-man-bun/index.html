How To Put Curly Hair In A Man Bun A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have curly hair and are looking for a stylish way to keep it out of your face, a man bun might be just what you need. While putting curly hair in a man bun might seem intimidating at first, it's actually a fairly simple process that can be easily adapted to suit your hair type and personal style. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps involved in putting curly hair in a man bun, as well as provide some tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking great.

How to Put Curly Hair in a Man Bun: Step-by-Step

Before you begin, you'll need to gather a few supplies: a hair elastic, a comb or brush, and some hair product (optional). Once you have everything you need, follow these steps to create your man bun:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

The first step in putting curly hair in a man bun is to start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet or damp, it'll be harder to work with and won't hold its shape as well.

Step 2: Brush or Comb Your Hair

Next, use a brush or comb to detangle your hair and remove any knots or tangles. This will make it easier to work with your hair and create a smooth, even bun.

Step 3: Gather Your Hair into a Ponytail

Once your hair is brushed or combed out, use your hands to gather it into a ponytail at the crown of your head. You can make the ponytail as high or low as you like, depending on your personal style.

Step 4: Secure the Ponytail with a Hair Elastic

Use a hair elastic to secure the ponytail in place. Make sure the elastic is tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not so tight that it causes discomfort or pulling.

Step 5: Twist the Ponytail

Now that your hair is in a ponytail, it's time to start creating the bun. Begin by twisting the ponytail around itself to create a tight, spiral shape.

Step 6: Wrap the Twisted Ponytail Around the Elastic

Once the ponytail is twisted, wrap it around the hair elastic to create the bun. Tuck the ends of your hair under the bun to hide them and create a more polished look.

Step 7: Secure the Bun with Another Hair Elastic

Once the bun is in place, use another hair elastic to secure it in place. Make sure the elastic is hidden and doesn't create any bumps or lumps in your hair.

Step 8: Tame Flyaways

If you have any flyaways or loose strands of hair, use a bit of hair product (such as gel or hairspray) to tame them and create a more polished look.

Tips for Putting Curly Hair in a Man Bun

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when putting curly hair in a man bun:

  • Use a hair elastic that matches your hair color for a more seamless look.
  • Experiment with different bun styles (such as a messy bun or a low bun) to find the one that works best for you.
  • Don't be afraid to add hair accessories (such as a headband or scarf) to your man bun for a more stylish look.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid damaging your curls.

FAQ

Can I put my curly hair in a man bun if it's short?

Yes, you can still put short curly hair in a man bun! However, you may need to use more hair product to help your hair stay in place.

How do I prevent my curly hair from getting tangled in the bun?

To prevent tangling, make sure your hair is well-brushed or combed before putting it in a bun. You can also use a bit of hair product (such as leave-in conditioner) to help keep your curls smooth and tangle-free.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

This can vary depending on your hair type and personal preferences, but many people with curly hair find that washing their hair every 2-3 days is sufficient. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos or sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause breakage and damage.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools (such as flat irons or curling irons) on a regular basis, which can cause damage and frizz.

By following these tips and techniques, you should be able to put your curly hair in a man bun with ease and keep your hair healthy and looking great. Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you, and don't be afraid to ask a stylist for advice if you need it. With a little practice and patience, you'll be rocking a stylish man bun in no time!
