Can Your Hair Get Curlier As You Age

Written by William Aug 06, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

As we age, many changes happen in our bodies, including our hair. One question that people often ask is, can your hair get curlier as you age? The answer is yes, it's possible. In this article, we will explore why and how your hair can get curlier as you age, and provide some tips on how to care for your curly hair.

Description

As we age, our hair can undergo changes due to hormonal changes, genetics, and other factors. One of these changes is that our hair can become curlier. This can happen to both men and women, and it's more common in people with naturally wavy or curly hair.

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. The more oval-shaped the follicle, the curlier the hair will be. As we age, the shape of our hair follicles can change, which can cause our hair to become curlier.

How to

If you've noticed that your hair has become curlier as you age, there are a few things you can do to embrace your new curls and keep them looking their best.

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it's important to use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that's designed for curly hair.

2. Condition regularly

Curly hair needs moisture to stay healthy and defined. Use a deep conditioner once a week, and a regular conditioner every time you wash your hair.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair and cause it to lose its shape. Try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, and let your hair air dry whenever possible.

4. Use a diffuser

If you do need to blow dry your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat evenly and reduce frizz.

Step by Step

If you're new to caring for curly hair, here's a simple step-by-step routine you can follow:

1. Shampoo

Wet your hair and apply a sulfate-free shampoo. Massage your scalp gently and rinse thoroughly.

2. Condition

Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for a few minutes and then rinse thoroughly.

3. Detangle

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

4. Apply styling product

Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair, such as a curl cream or gel. Work it through your hair, scrunching as you go.

5. Air dry

Let your hair air dry, or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips for caring for curly hair:

1. Sleep on silk

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce frizz and prevent your curls from getting flattened overnight.

2. Don't over-wash

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can make curly hair even drier. Aim to wash your hair no more than every other day.

3. Embrace your curls

Curly hair can be beautiful and unique. Embrace your curls and experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curly hair:

1. What causes curly hair?

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. The more oval-shaped the follicle, the curlier the hair will be.

2. Can your hair get curlier as you age?

Yes, it's possible for your hair to get curlier as you age due to changes in the shape of your hair follicles.

3. How can I make my curls more defined?

Use a curl-enhancing product, such as a curl cream or gel, to help define your curls. Scrunch your hair while it's wet to encourage curl formation.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking their best. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid heat styling, and use products that are specifically designed for curly hair. Embrace your curls and have fun experimenting with different styles!
