How To Cut Thick Curly Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts at the salon? Do you have thick curly hair that can be difficult to manage? Cutting your own hair with clippers may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to cut thick curly hair with clippers in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Thick Curly Hair with Clippers

Cutting thick curly hair with clippers requires a different approach than cutting straight or thin hair. Clippers are designed to cut hair evenly and quickly, making them a great tool for people with thick hair. However, it’s important to use the right clipper guard and technique to avoid cutting too much hair or creating an uneven look.

What You’ll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clippers with adjustable guards
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your thick curly hair with clippers:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner, and then towel-dry or blow-dry it until it’s completely dry. Make sure there are no tangles or knots in your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using the comb, divide your hair into four sections: two on top of your head and two on the sides. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Choose Your Clipper Guard

Select the clipper guard based on how much hair you want to cut. For thick curly hair, it’s best to start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard if needed.

Step 4: Begin Cutting

Starting with the longest guard, begin cutting the hair on the sides of your head first. Use the clippers to cut from the bottom of your hairline and work your way up to the top. Use a comb to lift the hair and ensure you’re cutting evenly.

Repeat this process on the other side, and then move to the top of your head. Use the same guard and technique to cut your hair on top, making sure to cut in the direction of your hair growth.

Step 5: Blend and Fade

Once you’ve cut your hair with the longest guard, switch to a shorter guard and blend the hair at the sides and back of your head. Use a scissors to trim any long or uneven hairs, and then fade the hair from the sides to the top.

Step 6: Check for Any Uneven Spots

Use a mirror to check for any uneven spots or areas that need more trimming. Make sure to trim any hair that’s sticking out or not blending well with the rest of your hair.

Step 7: Clean Up

Once you’re finished cutting your hair, use a towel or cape to brush off any loose hair. Clean your clippers and scissors, and put away your tools.

Tips for Cutting Thick Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you cut your thick curly hair with clippers:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard if needed
  • Cut your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can be difficult to cut evenly
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and ensure you’re cutting evenly
  • Trim any long or uneven hairs with scissors
  • Fade the hair from the sides to the top for a more natural look

FAQs

Can I cut my hair with clippers without a guard?

No, it’s not recommended to cut your hair with clippers without a guard as this can result in uneven cutting and damage to your hair.

How often should I cut my thick curly hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair with clippers if it’s wet?

No, it’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry as wet hair can be difficult to cut evenly.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Curly Hair

To keep your thick curly hair looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it’s wet
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz

With these tips and techniques, cutting your own thick curly hair with clippers can be done successfully. Remember to take your time and use the right tools and guards for the best results. Happy cutting!
