How To Air Dry Hair And Still Have Volume

Written by Frank Sep 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're tired of using heat tools on your hair every day, air drying might seem like a great alternative. However, many people avoid air drying because they worry that it will leave their hair limp and lifeless. The good news is that there are plenty of ways to air dry your hair and still have volume. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for achieving voluminous air-dried hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Air Dry Hair and Still Have Volume
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Voluminous Air-Dried Hair

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to follow for voluminous air-dried hair:

Step 1: Start with clean hair

Air drying works best on clean hair. Make sure you shampoo and condition your hair before air drying.

Step 2: Use a towel to remove excess water

Use a soft towel to gently remove excess water from your hair. Don't rub your hair vigorously with the towel, as this can cause damage and frizz.

Step 3: Apply a volumizing product

Apply a volumizing product to your hair, such as mousse or a volumizing spray. Work the product through your hair evenly.

Step 4: Divide your hair into sections

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will help you achieve more volume and prevent your hair from becoming flat.

Step 5: Let your hair air dry

Let your hair air dry completely before removing the clips or hair ties. This can take several hours, so be patient.

Step 6: Fluff your hair

Once your hair is completely dry, remove the clips or hair ties and gently fluff your hair with your fingers. This will help boost volume and create a more natural-looking style.

Tips for Voluminous Air-Dried Hair

Here are some additional tips for achieving voluminous air-dried hair:

Use the right products

Choose products that are specifically designed for air-drying, such as mousse or a volumizing spray. These products will help create volume and prevent frizz.

Don't touch your hair too much

Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying, as this can cause frizz and flatten your hair.

Flip your hair over

Flip your hair over while it's drying to create more volume at the roots. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to create more volume.

Try a braided style

Braid your hair while it's still damp and let it air dry. When you take out the braid, you'll have natural-looking waves and more volume.

FAQs

Can I air dry my hair overnight?

Yes, you can air dry your hair overnight. Just make sure your hair is completely dry before you go to bed, as sleeping on damp hair can cause breakage and frizz.

Can I use a hair dryer on low heat to speed up the drying process?

Yes, you can use a hair dryer on low heat to speed up the drying process. Just make sure you use a diffuser attachment and don't apply too much heat to your hair.

What if I have naturally curly or wavy hair?

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you can still air dry your hair and achieve volume. Just make sure to use a product that's specifically designed for curly or wavy hair.

Hair Care for Air-Dried Hair

Here are some tips for caring for air-dried hair:

Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet. This will help prevent breakage and damage.

Avoid using heat tools

Avoid using heat tools on your hair as much as possible. If you do need to use a heat tool, use a heat protectant spray first.

Get regular trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. This will help your hair look fuller and more voluminous.

With these tips and tricks, you can air dry your hair and still have voluminous, healthy-looking locks. Give it a try and see how your hair responds!
