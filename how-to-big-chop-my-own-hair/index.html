How To Big Chop Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering chopping off all your relaxed hair and starting fresh with a natural look? The big chop is a brave and liberating decision, but it can also be intimidating. Fear not, with the right tools and guidance, you can successfully big chop your own hair at home. Here's our step-by-step guide on how to big chop your own hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to
  2. Step-by-Step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care

How to

Before you start, make sure you have the proper tools: scissors, a comb, a mirror, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also recommended to have a friend or family member to assist you.

Step 1: Make the Decision

The first step to big chopping your own hair is making the decision to do it. This means accepting that you will be cutting off all of your relaxed hair, leaving only your natural hair. This is a major change, and it's important to be mentally and emotionally prepared for it.

Step 2: Choose Your Length

Next, decide on the length you want your hair to be after the big chop. Some people prefer to have a short, close-cropped look, while others opt for a longer, tapered cut. This is a personal preference, and it's important to choose a length that you feel comfortable with.

Step-by-Step

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: front, back, left, and right. This will make it easier to cut your hair in even sections.

Step 4: Wet Your Hair

Spray your hair with water until it is damp. This will make it easier to cut and prevent breakage.

Step 5: Cut Your Hair

Starting with one section, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair as close to your scalp as possible. Continue cutting small sections of hair until all of the relaxed hair has been removed. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once all of the relaxed hair has been cut, you can style your hair as desired. Some people choose to leave their hair as is, while others prefer to add a little product or shape it with a styling tool.

Tips

Here are some tips to make your big chop experience a success:

  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Make sure you have the proper tools and a friend or family member to assist you.
  • Be mentally and emotionally prepared for the big chop.
  • Choose a length that you feel comfortable with.
  • Section your hair for even cutting.
  • Keep your hair damp to prevent breakage.
  • Don't be afraid to seek professional help if you need it.

FAQ

What if I don't like my hair after the big chop?

Remember that hair grows back. Give yourself time to adjust to your new look, and experiment with different styles and products to find what works for you.

Can I big chop my own hair if it's been chemically treated?

Yes, you can big chop your own hair even if it has been chemically treated. Just be sure to cut off all of the relaxed hair, leaving only your natural hair.

Hair Care

After the big chop, it's important to take care of your natural hair. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly.
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin or silk bonnet or pillowcase.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to big chop your own hair, you can confidently make the decision to start fresh with a natural look. Remember to be patient with yourself, take your time, and enjoy the journey to healthy, beautiful natural hair.
