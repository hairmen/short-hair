How To Use Thinning Scissors On Short Bob A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of having a bulky and heavy short bob haircut? Do you want to add texture and volume to your hair without sacrificing its length? If so, then thinning scissors could be the solution youâve been looking for. Thinning scissors are special tools that can help you remove some of the hairâs thickness without changing its length. In this guide, we will show you how to use thinning scissors on short bob haircuts to achieve a lighter and more manageable hairstyle.

Description

Thinning scissors are designed with a serrated edge on one blade and a smooth edge on the other. When the blades are closed, the teeth of the serrated blade fit between the teeth of the smooth blade, creating a gap that allows some of the hair to slip through. When the scissors are opened and closed, the teeth of the serrated blade cut some of the hair while leaving the rest intact. This helps to create a more natural-looking hairstyle with less bulk and weight.

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Short Bob: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before using thinning scissors, you should wash and dry your hair thoroughly. This will help you to see the natural fall and movement of your hair, which will be important when deciding which sections to thin out. Make sure that your hair is completely dry before you start using the scissors.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you should divide your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will help you to work on one section at a time and ensure that you donât miss any areas. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward.

Step 3: Hold the Scissors Properly

When using thinning scissors, you should hold them with the serrated blade facing upwards and the smooth blade facing downwards. This will ensure that the teeth of the serrated blade are facing the section of hair that you want to thin out.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

When cutting the hair with thinning scissors, you should only cut about 1/3 of the hair in each section. This will help you to remove some of the bulk without making the hair too thin or stringy. Start at the top of each section and work your way down to the bottom, using quick and gentle snips.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

After thinning out each section, you should use regular scissors to blend the hair and create a more natural-looking finish. This will help to remove any harsh lines or unevenness caused by the thinning scissors. You can also use a comb to check the balance and symmetry of the haircut.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors on Short Bob

* Use high-quality thinning scissors that are sharp and well-maintained.
* Work on small sections of hair at a time to avoid over-thinning.
* Use thinning scissors only on the mid-lengths and ends of the hair, not near the roots.
* Be patient and take your time to achieve the desired result.
* Consult a professional hairstylist if you are not confident in using thinning scissors on your own.

FAQ

Q: Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?
A: Thinning scissors can be used on most hair types, but they may not be suitable for very thin or fine hair as they can make the hair look stringy or sparse.

Q: Will thinning scissors make my hair look shorter?
A: No, thinning scissors will not change the length of your hair. They will only remove some of the thickness and weight, making your hair look lighter and more manageable.

Hair Care Tips for Short Bob Haircuts

* Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
* Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and smooth.
* Avoid using heat tools too often as they can damage your hair.
* Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.
* Use a hair serum or oil to add shine and nourishment to your hair.
* Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and pollution by wearing a hat or scarf.

Thinning scissors can be a great tool for achieving a more natural and textured short bob haircut.
