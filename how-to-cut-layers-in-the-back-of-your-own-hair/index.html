How To Cut Layers In The Back Of Your Own Hair

Written by Petter Oct 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair? Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you want to add layers in the back. But with a little patience and practice, you can achieve a layered look without leaving your home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting layers in the back of your own hair.

Description

Layers can add volume and texture to your hair. They can also make your face look more defined. Cutting layers in the back of your own hair can save you money and time. However, it requires careful planning and execution to achieve the desired look.

How to Cut Layers in the Back of Your Own Hair

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, wash and dry it thoroughly. Make sure your hair is free of tangles and knots. Use a comb to detangle your hair and a blow dryer to dry it completely.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into two parts. Use a comb to create a horizontal parting line from ear to ear. Clip the top section of your hair away from the bottom section. You will work on the bottom section first.

Step 3: Decide on the Length of Your Layers

Decide on the length of your layers. It's important to have a clear idea of where you want to cut your hair. Use a mirror to see the back of your head and visualize where you want to add layers.

Step 4: Cut the First Layer

Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your index and middle fingers and cut the hair at an angle. Use a sharp pair of scissors to make a clean cut. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 5: Cut the Rest of the Layers

Continue cutting the rest of the layers using the first layer as a guide. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle. Remember to cut less hair as you go up to create a gradual layering effect.

Step 6: Unclip the Top Section

Once you finish cutting the bottom section, unclip the top section and repeat the same process. Comb a small section of hair straight down, hold it between your fingers, and cut it at an angle. Use the previously cut layers as a guide to create a consistent look.

Tips

- Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
- Cut less hair than you think you need
- Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated
- Use a mirror to check the back of your head regularly

FAQ

Q: Can I cut layers in my own hair if it's curly?
A: Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, it's important to cut it when it's dry to avoid any surprises after it dries.

Q: What if I make a mistake?
A: If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always cut more hair to fix it. However, it's better to cut less than to cut too much.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?
A: It depends on your hair type and length. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, make sure to take care of it properly. Use a conditioner to keep it moisturized and a serum to avoid frizz. Avoid using heat styling tools frequently and use a heat protectant if you do. And most importantly, be patient with your hair. It takes time to grow and maintain healthy hair.
