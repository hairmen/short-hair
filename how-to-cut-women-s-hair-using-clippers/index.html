How To Cut Women s Hair Using Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for an easy way to cut women's hair, clippers are a great option. Not only are they quick and efficient, but they can also create a variety of styles that are difficult to achieve with scissors alone. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting women's hair using clippers, from choosing the right tools to finishing the look.

How To

Before we dive into the step-by-step process of cutting women's hair using clippers, let's take a look at the tools you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards (various sizes)
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Hair cape or towel
  • Hairbrush
  • Hair dryer (optional)

Once you have all of your tools together, you can begin the process of cutting women's hair using clippers.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to make sure your client's hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the final result looks neat and polished. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and towel dry the hair thoroughly. You can also use a hair dryer on a low heat setting to speed up the drying process.

Step 2: Choose the Right Guard

Clipper guards come in various sizes, and each size corresponds to a different length of hair. For a short buzz cut, choose a smaller guard (such as a #1 or #2), while a longer haircut will require a larger guard (such as a #6 or #8). Keep in mind that you can always switch to a shorter guard if needed, but you can't add length once it's been cut.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Using a comb, divide the hair into sections. Start at the back of the head, and work your way forward. Use clips to secure each section of hair, so it's out of the way while you work on other areas. This will help you keep track of what you've already cut, and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Step 4: Begin Cutting

Start by using the clippers without a guard to trim any longer hair around the neck and hairline. Then, attach the guard you've chosen and begin cutting the hair in the back of the head. Hold the clippers at a 45-degree angle, and work your way up the head in a straight line. Be sure to use even pressure and avoid pushing too hard, which can result in uneven cuts.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut the hair in the back of the head, it's time to blend the sides and top. Switch to a larger guard (if necessary) and work your way up the sides of the head, blending the shorter hair with the longer hair on top. Use the comb to lift sections of hair away from the head, and use the clippers to trim any excess hair.

Step 6: Detail the Hair

Once you've finished the bulk of the cutting, it's time to detail the hair. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. Pay close attention to the hairline, around the ears, and at the nape of the neck, as these areas often require extra attention.

Step 7: Clean Up

Once you're done cutting, it's important to clean up the hair. Use a hairbrush to remove any loose hairs, and use a towel or hair cape to wipe away any excess hair on the client's neck and shoulders.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting women's hair using clippers:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down, rather than starting with a shorter guard and trying to add length.
  • Use a comb to lift sections of hair away from the head, making it easier to cut evenly.
  • Take breaks as needed to rest your hands and avoid fatigue.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes and uneven cuts.

FAQ

Here are a few common questions about cutting women's hair using clippers:

Can you use clippers on wet hair?

It's generally best to cut hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly. However, some clippers are designed to be used on wet hair, so be sure to check the manufacturer's instructions before using them.

How often should you sharpen your clippers?

It's a good idea to have your clippers sharpened once a year, or more frequently if you use them frequently or notice that they're not cutting as smoothly as they used to.

Can you cut curly hair with clippers?

Yes, you can cut curly hair with clippers, but it's important to use the right guard and comb the hair carefully to avoid uneven cuts.

Hair Care

After cutting women's hair using clippers, it's important to take care of the hair to keep it looking healthy and polished. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heated styling tools too frequently. Regular trims can also help keep the hair looking neat and tidy.

Cutting women's hair using clippers can be a quick and easy way to achieve a variety of styles. With the right tools and techniques, you can create a polished and professional look that your clients will love.
