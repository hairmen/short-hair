How Do You Make Nappy Hair Curly

How Do You Make Nappy Hair Curly? | Step-by-Step Guide & Tips

If you have nappy hair, you might be wondering how to make it curly. Luckily, there are several ways to achieve curly hair that looks great and is easy to maintain. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about making nappy hair curly, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and answers to frequently asked questions.

How to Make Nappy Hair Curly

There are several methods for making nappy hair curly, including using heat tools, styling products, and protective styles. The method you choose will depend on your hair type, the look you're going for, and your personal preferences.

Heat Tools

One way to make nappy hair curly is by using heat tools, such as a curling iron or hot rollers. This method can give you defined curls that last for several days, but it can also cause damage to your hair if not done correctly. To use heat tools on nappy hair, follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent heat damage.
  3. Section your hair into small sections, using clips to keep them in place.
  4. Take one section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron or hot roller, holding for a few seconds.
  5. Repeat with each section of hair until your entire head is curled.
  6. Once your hair is curled, use a light hold hairspray to set the style.

Styling Products

Another way to make nappy hair curly is by using styling products, such as curl creams or gels. This method can give you more natural-looking curls that are easier to maintain, but it can also require more time and effort. To use styling products on nappy hair, follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a curl cream or gel to your hair, distributing it evenly from root to tip.
  3. Section your hair into small sections, using clips to keep them in place.
  4. Take one section of hair and twist it around your finger, starting at the root and working your way down to the end.
  5. Repeat with each section of hair until your entire head is twisted.
  6. Once your hair is twisted, allow it to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to speed up the drying process.
  7. Once your hair is dry, untwist each section and fluff with your fingers to create more volume.
  8. Use a light hold hairspray to set the style.

Protective Styles

Finally, you can make nappy hair curly by using protective styles, such as braids or twists. This method can give you long-lasting curls that are easy to maintain, but it can also require more time and effort. To use protective styles on nappy hair, follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into small sections, using clips to keep them in place.
  3. Take one section of hair and divide it into three equal sections.
  4. Braid or twist the three sections of hair together, starting at the root and working your way down to the end.
  5. Repeat with each section of hair until your entire head is braided or twisted.
  6. Once your hair is braided or twisted, allow it to set overnight or for several hours.
  7. Once your hair is set, carefully unravel each braid or twist to reveal your curly hair.
  8. Use a light hold hairspray to set the style.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to making nappy hair curly using styling products:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a curl cream or gel to your hair, distributing it evenly from root to tip.
  3. Section your hair into small sections, using clips to keep them in place.
  4. Take one section of hair and twist it around your finger, starting at the root and working your way down to the end.
  5. Repeat with each section of hair until your entire head is twisted.
  6. Once your hair is twisted, allow it to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to speed up the drying process.
  7. Once your hair is dry, untwist each section and fluff with your fingers to create more volume.
  8. Use a light hold hairspray to set the style.

Tips for Making Nappy Hair Curly

Here are some tips to help you make nappy hair curly:

  • Start with clean, well-conditioned hair to ensure the best results.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair before styling.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray if using heat tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use the lowest heat setting possible if using heat tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the ones that work best for your hair type.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to speed up the drying process and create more volume.
  • Don't over-manipulate your hair to prevent breakage and damage.
  • Protect your hair at night by wrapping it in a silk or satin scarf or using a satin pillowcase.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making nappy hair curly:

Can nappy hair be curly?

Yes, nappy hair can be curly. With the right styling products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls that are easy to maintain.

What is the best way to make nappy hair curly?

The best way to make nappy hair curly depends on your hair type, the look you're going for, and your personal preferences. Some methods include using heat tools, styling products, and protective styles.

How often should I make my nappy hair curly?

It's best to avoid making your nappy hair curly too often to prevent damage and breakage. Aim to make your hair curly once a week or less.

Hair Care for Nappy Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of nappy hair:

  • Wash your hair once a week or less to prevent dryness.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat tools on your hair to prevent damage.
  • Protect your hair at night by wrapping it in a silk or satin scarf or using a satin pillowcase.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to make nappy hair curly, it's time to experiment with different styling techniques and find the ones that work best for you. With a little patience and practice, you can achieve beautiful, curly hair that you'll love!
