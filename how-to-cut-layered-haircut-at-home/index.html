How To Cut Layered Haircut At Home Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of paying high prices for haircuts at the salon? Cutting your own hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a salon-quality layered haircut at home. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut layered haircut at home and provide tips for maintaining your new look.

How to Cut Layered Haircut at Home

Before you get started, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. Here's how to cut layered haircut at home:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it until it's completely dry. This will make it easier to see the layers and ensure an even cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle and clip each side into two sections: the top half and the bottom half. Then, clip the top two sections out of the way.

Step 3: Create the First Layer

Starting with the bottom section of hair, take a small section and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across, using the length of the previous cut as a guide. Continue this process until you've cut all of the bottom section of hair.

Step 4: Cut the Second Layer

Release the top section of hair and repeat the same process, using the first layer as your guide. Make sure to cut each section of hair at the same length to maintain the layered effect.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut both layers, remove the clips and blend the layers together by taking small sections of hair and cutting them at a slight angle towards the face. This will create a softer, more natural look.

Step 6: Trim the Ends

Finally, trim any uneven ends and make sure the layers are even on both sides. Use your comb to check for any stray hairs and trim them as needed.

Tips for Cutting Layered Haircut at Home

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect layered haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut less than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure the layers are even.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, start with a small trim and work your way up to a full haircut.
  • Consider using hair-thinning scissors to create a more natural, textured look.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your own hair if you have curly hair. Just be sure to cut each section of hair while it's dry, since curly hair can shrink when it dries.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always go to a salon and have a professional fix it. Alternatively, you can try styling your hair in a way that hides the mistake until it grows out.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for maintaining your new layered haircut:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, since they can dry out your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain your layers.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and empowering experience. With these tips, you can achieve a beautiful layered haircut at home and keep it looking great for weeks to come.
