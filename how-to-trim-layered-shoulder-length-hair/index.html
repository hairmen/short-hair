How To Trim Layered Shoulder Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have layered shoulder length hair, you know how important it is to keep those layers looking fresh and maintained. Whether you're trying to save money by trimming your own hair at home or just want to keep up with regular maintenance between salon visits, trimming your own hair can be a daunting task. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to trim your layered shoulder length hair.

Description

Layered shoulder length hair is a popular hairstyle for many women. It's versatile and easy to maintain, making it a great choice for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle that still looks great. However, to keep your hair looking its best, it's important to trim your hair regularly. Trimming your hair helps to get rid of split ends, which can make your hair look frizzy and damaged. It also helps to maintain the shape of your hairstyle, keeping it looking fresh and polished.

How to Trim Layered Shoulder Length Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start trimming your hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to hold your hair in place.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to trim your hair when it's clean and dry. Wash your hair as you normally would and let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start trimming.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections using hair clips. Start by sectioning off the top layer of your hair and clip it on top of your head. Then, take another section of hair from the back and clip it out of the way. Continue to section your hair until you have a small section of hair to trim.

Step 4: Trim Your Hair

Hold your hair between your fingers and trim the ends of your hair using your hair cutting scissors. Start by trimming a small amount of hair at a time and work your way up. Be sure to keep your scissors at an angle to create layers. Continue to trim your hair until you've trimmed all the sections of your hair.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished trimming your hair, check your work in the mirror. Look for any uneven spots or areas that need to be trimmed. If you notice any areas that need to be trimmed, repeat the trimming process until you're happy with the results.

Tips

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to prevent split ends
  • Trim your hair in a well-lit area
  • Trim small amounts of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much off
  • Keep your scissors at an angle to create layers
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as wet hair tends to look longer than it actually is

FAQ

How often should I trim my layered shoulder length hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and maintained.

Can I trim my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can trim your hair if it's curly. However, it's important to remember that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so be sure to trim less than you think you need to.

Should I trim my hair myself or go to a salon?

It's up to personal preference. If you feel comfortable trimming your own hair, go for it. If you're unsure or want a more drastic change, it's best to go to a salon.

Hair Care

Trimming your hair regularly is just one part of maintaining healthy hair. It's also important to take care of your hair by using the right products, avoiding heat styling tools, and protecting your hair from the sun. Be sure to use a conditioner after shampooing your hair and use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools. Additionally, consider getting regular trims from a professional stylist to keep your hair looking its best.
