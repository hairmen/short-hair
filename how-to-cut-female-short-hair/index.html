How To Cut Female Short Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Oct 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Short hair is all the rage these days, and for good reason. Not only is it low-maintenance and easy to style, but it can also be incredibly chic and stylish. However, cutting short hair can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut female short hair in a simple and straightforward way.

Description: What You Need to Know Before Cutting Short Hair

Before we get started with the actual cutting process, there are a few things you need to know. First, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, preferably ones specifically designed for cutting hair. You may also want to invest in a pair of thinning shears, which can help add texture and volume to your hair.

Second, it's important to have a clear idea of what you want your hair to look like. Do you want a blunt bob, a layered pixie, or something in between? It's a good idea to do some research and find pictures of hairstyles you like to show your stylist or to use as a guide if you're cutting your own hair.

How To Cut Female Short Hair: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you get a more accurate idea of how your hair will look when it's styled. If your hair is wet, it may look longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you'll need to section your hair. Start by dividing your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, divide each side into two sections, one at the top and one at the bottom. Clip the top sections out of the way for now.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Section

Begin by cutting the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers at the length you want it to be. Use your scissors to cut straight across. Continue working your way around your head, cutting small sections of hair at a time.

Step 4: Cut the Top Section

Once you've finished cutting the bottom section, unclip the top section of your hair. Again, take small sections of hair and cut them to the desired length. If you want a layered look, you can use your thinning shears to add texture and volume to the top section.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

After you've finished cutting, check your hair for evenness. One way to do this is to comb your hair straight down and check that the ends are all at the same length. If you notice any unevenness, you can go back and trim those sections until your hair is even.

Tips for Cutting Short Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting short hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use your fingers as a guide to ensure you're cutting at the right length.
  • When cutting layers, cut at an angle to create a natural-looking finish.
  • Don't be afraid to go back and trim sections that aren't even.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Cut My Own Short Hair?

Yes, you can cut your own short hair, but it's important to be careful and take your time. It's also a good idea to have someone else check your work to ensure it's even.

How Often Should I Cut My Short Hair?

It's recommended to get your short hair trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair requires minimal maintenance, but there are a few things you can do to keep it looking its best:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a texturizing spray or pomade to add volume and texture to your hair.

Conclusion

Cutting female short hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, whether you're doing it yourself or going to a stylist. By following these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a stylish and chic look that's easy to maintain and style. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
