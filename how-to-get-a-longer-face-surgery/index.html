How To Get A Longer Face Surgery Everything You Need To Know

Written by William Jul 01, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of feeling self-conscious about the shape of your face? Do you feel like your face is too round or too short? If so, you may be considering a longer face surgery. In this post, we’ll cover everything you need to know about getting a longer face surgery, including how to prepare, what the procedure entails, and how to care for your face post-surgery.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get a Longer Face Surgery
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Surgery
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Surgery

How to Get a Longer Face Surgery

The first step in getting a longer face surgery is to find a reputable plastic surgeon. Look for a surgeon who is board-certified and has experience performing facial surgeries. You can ask for recommendations from your primary care physician, friends, or family members who have undergone similar procedures.

Once you’ve found a surgeon, schedule a consultation to discuss your goals and expectations for the surgery. Your surgeon will evaluate your face and recommend the best procedure to achieve your desired results. They’ll also explain the risks and benefits of the surgery and answer any questions you may have.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s what you can expect during a longer face surgery:

  1. You’ll be given anesthesia to keep you comfortable during the procedure.
  2. Your surgeon will make incisions in your hairline or inside your mouth to access the bones in your face.
  3. The bones in your face will be carefully reshaped to create a longer, more symmetrical appearance.
  4. Your surgeon will close the incisions with sutures or surgical glue.
  5. You’ll be monitored in a recovery area until you’re ready to go home.

Tips for a Successful Surgery

Follow these tips to ensure a successful surgery and speedy recovery:

  • Quit smoking at least two weeks before your surgery to promote healing.
  • Avoid taking blood-thinning medications, such as aspirin or ibuprofen, for at least two weeks before your surgery.
  • Arrange for transportation to and from your surgery appointment.
  • Stock up on soft foods, such as soup or yogurt, to eat during your recovery.
  • Take pain medication as prescribed by your surgeon to manage any discomfort.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about longer face surgery:

How long does the surgery take?

The surgery can take anywhere from 1-3 hours, depending on the complexity of the procedure.

How long is the recovery period?

The recovery period can vary, but most patients can resume their normal activities within 2-3 weeks.

What are the risks associated with the surgery?

Like any surgery, there are risks involved with longer face surgery, including bleeding, infection, and scarring. Your surgeon will discuss the risks with you before the procedure.

Hair Care After Surgery

After your surgery, you’ll need to take special care of your hair to avoid damaging the incisions. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after the surgery.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and avoid scrubbing your scalp.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly or wearing tight hats or headbands.
  • Avoid swimming or exposing your hair to chlorinated water for at least 2-3 weeks after the surgery.

Getting a longer face surgery can be life-changing for many people. By following these tips and working with a reputable plastic surgeon, you can achieve the longer, more symmetrical face you’ve always wanted.
