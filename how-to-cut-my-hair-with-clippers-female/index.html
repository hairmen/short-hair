How To Cut Your Hair With Clippers As A Female A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to cut your hair at home with clippers, you might be feeling a little intimidated, especially if you're a woman. But don't worry, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of how to cut your hair with clippers as a female.

Table of Contents

  2. Preparing Your Hair
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair
  5. Blending Your Hair

  7. Tips for a Successful Haircut
  8. FAQs

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • A set of hair clippers
  • Clipper guards in various sizes
  • Shears or scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Make sure your clippers are fully charged or plugged in and that your blades are sharp and clean. You don't want to start cutting your hair with dull blades.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare your hair. Make sure your hair is clean and dry. If you have tangles, brush them out. If you have long hair, consider putting it up in a ponytail or bun to make it easier to work with.

Next, use your comb to part your hair where you want to start cutting. This will make it easier to section your hair and ensure an even cut.

Sectioning Your Hair

Now it's time to section your hair. Use your comb to make a part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then make a part from ear to ear, dividing your hair into four sections.

Clip up three of the sections, leaving one section down to start cutting. It's important to work in small sections so you can ensure an even cut and avoid missing any spots.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Start with the longest guard and work your way down to shorter guards until you reach your desired length. Use the comb to lift up a small section of hair and run the clippers over it, making sure to keep the clippers level and perpendicular to your head.

Work your way around your head, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Make sure to check your work in the mirror as you go to ensure an even cut.

Blending Your Hair

Once you've cut your hair to your desired length, it's time to blend the different sections together. Change to a smaller guard and use your clippers to blend the sections together, using a flicking motion to create a seamless transition.

Use your shears or scissors to trim any longer pieces or to add texture to your hair. Don't be afraid to take your time and make small adjustments as needed.

Cleaning Your Clippers

After you're done cutting your hair, it's important to clean your clippers. Use a small brush to remove any hair from the blades and then wipe them down with a cloth or paper towel. You can also use a disinfectant spray to sanitize your clippers.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use your comb to lift up small sections of hair for a more even cut.
  • Take your time and make small adjustments as needed.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're having trouble with a certain section.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair with clippers if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your hair with clippers if you have curly hair. Just make sure to work in small sections and use a comb to lift up the hair before you cut it.

What guard should I use for a shorter cut?

If you want a shorter cut, start with a #4 guard and work your way down to a #1 or #2 guard for a closer cut.

Can I use clippers to cut my own bangs?

Yes, you can use clippers to cut your own bangs. Just make sure to use a smaller guard and work in small sections.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

After you've cut your hair with clippers, it's important to take care of your new haircut. Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. You may also want to use a styling product to help control any flyaways or add texture to your hair.

Congratulations, you've successfully cut your hair with clippers as a female! With a little practice, you'll be able to achieve a salon-worthy cut at home.
