How To Make Simple Hairstyle For Short Hair

Description

Short hair can be a bit tricky to style, especially if you're used to having long hair. But fear not, there are plenty of simple hairstyles you can try out that will look great on shorter locks! In this blog post, we'll go over some easy hairstyles for short hair that you can do at home. These styles are perfect for everyday wear or special occasions, and they don't require a lot of time or effort to achieve.

How to

Before we get started, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a comb, bobby pins, and hair ties. If you want to add some extra volume to your hair, you can also use a volumizing mousse or hairspray.

1. The Half-Up Half-Down

The half-up half-down is a classic hairstyle that works well on short hair. To achieve this look, simply take the top section of your hair (starting at your temples) and pull it back into a small ponytail. Secure the ponytail with a hair tie or bobby pins, and you're done!

2. The Pixie Cut

If you have a pixie cut, you're already rocking a stylish and low-maintenance look. But if you want to switch things up, you can add some texture and volume to your hair by using a styling wax or pomade. Simply work a small amount of product through your hair with your fingers, and you'll have a chic and effortless look.

3. The Side Part

The side part is a simple and elegant hairstyle that works well on short hair. To achieve this look, simply part your hair on one side and tuck the shorter side behind your ear. You can add some volume to the longer side by using a volumizing mousse or hairspray.

4. The French Twist

The French twist is a classic hairstyle that can be adapted for short hair. To achieve this look, simply twist your hair up and tuck it under at the nape of your neck. Secure the twist with bobby pins, and you're done!

Step by step

Here are step-by-step instructions for each of the hairstyles mentioned above:

The Half-Up Half-Down:

  1. Take the top section of your hair (starting at your temples) and pull it back into a small ponytail.
  2. Secure the ponytail with a hair tie or bobby pins.

The Pixie Cut:

  1. Apply a small amount of styling wax or pomade to your hair.
  2. Work the product through your hair with your fingers.

The Side Part:

  1. Part your hair on one side.
  2. Tuck the shorter side behind your ear.
  3. Add volume to the longer side with a volumizing mousse or hairspray.

The French Twist:

  1. Twist your hair up and tuck it under at the nape of your neck.
  2. Secure the twist with bobby pins.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when styling short hair:

  • Use a comb to create a clean part.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair.
  • If you're short on time, use dry shampoo to refresh your hair instead of washing it.
  • Accessorize with hair clips, headbands, or scarves to add some extra flair to your hairstyles.

FAQ

Q: Can I still curl my short hair?

A: Yes, you can definitely curl short hair! Just make sure to use a smaller curling iron or wand, and focus on curling the ends of your hair for a soft and natural look.

Q: How often should I wash my short hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp gets. As a general rule, you should aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days. If you have very fine or oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently.

Hair Care

Short hair requires minimal maintenance, but it's still important to take care of your locks to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.

With these simple hairstyles and hair care tips, you'll be rocking your short hair with confidence in no time!
