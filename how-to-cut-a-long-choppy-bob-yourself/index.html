How To Cut A Long Choppy Bob Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and want to try a long choppy bob, but don't want to spend money at the salon, then you might want to consider cutting your hair yourself. While it may seem daunting, with the right tools, techniques, and guidance, cutting a long choppy bob can be done at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps on how to cut a long choppy bob yourself, as well as tips on how to maintain and care for your new hairstyle.

Description

A long choppy bob is a versatile haircut that features layers and texture, giving your hair a natural and effortless look. This hairstyle is perfect for those who want a change from their long locks but don't want to go too short. It is also easy to style and can be worn straight or with loose waves.

How to Cut a Long Choppy Bob Yourself

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

Before starting, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You will need sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while cutting.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it straight. It's important to have your hair dry before cutting to ensure accuracy.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Clip the top sections away and work on the bottom sections first.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Take a section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle, starting at the length you want and working your way down. Repeat this step with the rest of the hair at the back, making sure to keep the length consistent.

Step 5: Cut the Front

Release the top sections and comb them forward. Cut at an angle, starting at the length you want and working your way down. Make sure to blend the front with the back by cutting at the same angle as the back.

Step 6: Add Layers

To add layers and texture, take small sections of hair and cut at an angle, starting at the length you want and working your way down. Make sure to cut at different angles to create a choppy and layered look.

Step 7: Check for Balance

Once you've finished cutting, check for balance by comparing the length of each section. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is even.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, whether it's straight or with loose waves. You can also use a texturizing product to enhance the choppy and layered look.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will appear longer than it actually is when it dries.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair while cutting.
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure accuracy.

FAQ

Can I cut a long choppy bob myself?

Yes, with the right tools and guidance, cutting a long choppy bob can be done at home.

How do I know what length to cut?

It's best to start with a longer length and work your way down to ensure accuracy. You can also consult with a hairstylist for advice on what length would suit your face shape and hair type.

How often should I cut my long choppy bob?

To maintain the shape and style of your long choppy bob, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

To keep your long choppy bob looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. It's also a good idea to use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.

Cutting a long choppy bob yourself may seem intimidating, but with practice and patience, it can be done. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a stylish and effortless look without breaking the bank.
