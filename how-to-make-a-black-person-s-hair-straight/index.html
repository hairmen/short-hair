How To Make A Black Person s Hair Straight A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 13, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking to straighten your black hair, you've come to the right place. In this article, we will be sharing tips, tricks, and step-by-step instructions on how to make a black person's hair straight. Whether you have naturally curly hair or relaxed hair, we've got you covered. So, let's get started.

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Straightening Black Hair

How to Make a Black Person's Hair Straight

There are a few different methods you can use to make your black hair straight. These include:

  • Using a flat iron
  • Using a blow dryer and round brush
  • Using a chemical relaxer
  • Using a keratin treatment

Each of these methods has its own benefits and drawbacks, so it's important to choose the one that works best for your hair type and lifestyle.

Using a Flat Iron

A flat iron is a great tool for straightening black hair. It works by using heat to smooth out your hair strands, leaving them sleek and straight. Here's how to use a flat iron:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Dry your hair completely with a towel or blow dryer.
  3. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the flat iron.
  4. Divide your hair into sections and clip them out of the way.
  5. Starting at the bottom, take a small section of hair and clamp the flat iron down on it as close to the root as possible.
  6. Slowly glide the flat iron down the section of hair, pulling it straight as you go.
  7. Repeat this process with each section of hair until your whole head is straight.
  8. Apply a smoothing serum or oil to your hair to add shine and prevent frizz.

Make sure to choose a flat iron with adjustable heat settings so you can avoid damaging your hair.

Using a Blow Dryer and Round Brush

If you don't want to use a flat iron, you can also use a blow dryer and round brush to straighten your hair. Here's how:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Dry your hair with a towel until it's damp.
  3. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the blow dryer.
  4. Divide your hair into sections and clip them out of the way.
  5. Starting at the bottom, take a small section of hair and wrap it around a round brush.
  6. Use the blow dryer to direct hot air at the section of hair while you pull it straight with the brush.
  7. Repeat this process with each section of hair until your whole head is straight.
  8. Apply a smoothing serum or oil to your hair to add shine and prevent frizz.

This method takes a bit longer than using a flat iron, but it's less damaging to your hair.

Using a Chemical Relaxer

A chemical relaxer is a permanent way to straighten your hair. It works by breaking down the protein bonds in your hair, making it easier to straighten. However, it can be damaging to your hair and scalp if not done correctly. Here's how to use a chemical relaxer:

  1. Choose a relaxer that is designed for your hair type.
  2. Follow the instructions on the relaxer kit carefully.
  3. Apply the relaxer to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down.
  4. Leave the relaxer on for the amount of time specified in the instructions.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with water.
  6. Apply a neutralizing shampoo to your hair to stop the chemical process.
  7. Condition your hair as usual.

It's important to follow the instructions carefully and to avoid getting the relaxer on your scalp or in your eyes.

Using a Keratin Treatment

A keratin treatment is a semi-permanent way to straighten your hair. It works by coating the hair strands with a protein that makes them easier to straighten. Here's how to use a keratin treatment:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Dry your hair with a towel until it's damp.
  3. Apply the keratin treatment to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down.
  4. Leave the treatment on for the amount of time specified in the instructions.
  5. Blow dry your hair until it's completely dry.
  6. Use a flat iron to straighten your hair in small sections.

It's important to choose a keratin treatment that is designed for your hair type and to follow the instructions carefully.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to straightening your black hair:

  1. Choose the straightening method that works best for your hair type and lifestyle.
  2. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  3. Dry your hair completely with a towel or blow dryer.
  4. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the straightening method.
  5. Divide your hair into sections and clip them out of the way.
  6. Start at the bottom and work your way up, straightening each section of hair with your chosen method.
  7. Apply a smoothing serum or oil to your hair to add shine and prevent frizz.

Remember to take your time and be gentle with your hair to avoid damage.

Tips for Straightening Black Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when straightening your black hair:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from heat styling tools.
  • Choose a straightening method that works best for your hair type and lifestyle.
  • Be gentle with your hair to avoid breakage and damage.
  • Don't straighten your hair too often to avoid damage and breakage.
  • Use a smoothing serum or oil to prevent frizz and add shine.

FAQs

Can I straighten my natural hair?

Yes, you can straighten your natural hair. Use a flat iron or blow dryer and round brush to straighten your hair.

How often should I straighten my hair?

You should avoid straightening your hair too often to avoid damage and breakage. Aim to straighten your hair no more than once a week.

Can I use a chemical relaxer on my hair?

Yes, you can use a chemical relaxer on your hair. However, it can be damaging to your hair and scalp if not done correctly.

Hair Care for Straightened Hair

Here are some tips for caring for your straightened hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to avoid damage and breakage.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair once a week to keep it healthy and moisturized.
  • Use a smoothing serum or oil to prevent frizz and add shine.

By following these tips, you can keep your straightened hair looking healthy and beautiful.

In conclusion, there are several methods you can use to make a black person's hair straight. Whether you choose to use a flat iron, blow dryer and round brush, chemical relaxer, or keratin treatment, it's important to choose the method that works best for your hair type and lifestyle. By following our tips and step-by-step instructions, you can achieve sleek, straight hair that looks and feels amazing.
