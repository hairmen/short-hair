How To Get Your Hair To Not Lay Flat

Written by Eveline Nov 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your hair laying flat and lifeless? Do you want to add some volume and texture to your locks? Look no further! In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to get your hair to not lay flat, so you can achieve the full, bouncy hair you desire.

Table of Contents

  • Section 1: Understanding Your Hair Type
  • Section 2: Shampoo and Conditioner
  • Section 3: Blow-Drying Techniques
  • Section 4: Styling Products
  • Section 5: Haircuts and Layers
  • Section 6: Frequently Asked Questions
  • Section 7: Hair Care Tips

Section 1: Understanding Your Hair Type

Before attempting to add volume and texture to your hair, it's important to understand your hair type. Some hair types are naturally flat and fine, while others are coarse and thick.

If you have fine hair, it's essential to avoid using heavy products that can weigh your hair down. Instead, opt for lightweight products that add volume and texture, such as volumizing mousse or root-lifting spray.

If you have thick hair, you can use heavier products to add texture and volume. However, it's important not to use too much product, as this can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

Section 2: Shampoo and Conditioner

The right shampoo and conditioner can make a huge difference in adding volume and texture to your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed to add volume, such as volumizing shampoo and conditioner.

When it comes to applying conditioner, avoid applying it to your roots, as this can weigh your hair down. Instead, focus on applying it to the mid-lengths and ends of your hair.

Section 3: Blow-Drying Techniques

Blow-drying your hair in the right way can also add volume and texture. Start by blow-drying your hair upside down, as this will help to lift your roots and add volume.

As you blow-dry, use a round brush to lift your roots and create volume. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to create more texture in your hair.

Section 4: Styling Products

Styling products can also help to add volume and texture to your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed to add texture and volume, such as sea salt spray, dry shampoo, or texturizing spray.

When applying styling products, focus on the roots and mid-lengths of your hair, as this is where you want to add volume and texture. Avoid applying too much product, as this can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

Section 5: Haircuts and Layers

The right haircut and layers can also help to add volume and texture to your hair. If you have fine hair, consider getting layers to create the illusion of more volume.

If you have thick hair, consider getting a shorter haircut to remove some of the weight and create more body.

Section 6: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I wash my hair to add volume and texture?

A: It's best to wash your hair every two to three days, as washing it too frequently can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and damaged.

Q: Can I add volume and texture to my hair without using heat?

A: Yes! You can add volume and texture to your hair by braiding it while it's wet and letting it air dry. You can also twist your hair into a bun and let it air dry to create natural waves.

Section 7: Hair Care Tips

In addition to the tips mentioned above, there are a few other things you can do to keep your hair healthy and voluminous.

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too frequently, as this can damage your hair and make it look flat.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals, as this can promote healthy hair growth.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve the full, bouncy hair you desire. Remember, it's important to understand your hair type and use the right products and techniques to add volume and texture to your hair.
