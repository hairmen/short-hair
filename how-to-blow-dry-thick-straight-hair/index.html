How To Blow Dry Thick Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 10, 2023 · 4 min read
If you have thick straight hair, you know that it can be challenging to style. One of the best ways to achieve a sleek and polished look is by blow-drying your hair. However, if you don't know how to blow dry your hair correctly, it can cause damage and frizz. In this article, we'll show you how to blow dry thick straight hair in a relaxed English language.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Blow Dry Thick Straight Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's discuss the tools you'll need for blow-drying your hair. Tools:
  • Blow dryer with a concentrator nozzle
  • Round brush
  • Hair clips
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hair serum or oil

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing your hair with a hydrating shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair until it's damp, but not dripping wet.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the blow dryer.
  3. Section your hair into four parts using hair clips. Start with the bottom section and work your way up.
  4. Take a small section of hair and place the round brush underneath it. Use the concentrator nozzle on your blow dryer to direct the heat down the hair shaft, following the brush as you pull it through your hair.
  5. Repeat step 4 until your hair is dry.
  6. Move on to the next section and repeat steps 4 and 5.
  7. Once you've blow-dried all sections, turn the blow dryer to the cool setting and run it over your hair to set the style.
  8. Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to the ends of your hair to add shine and reduce frizz.
  9. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.
  10. Enjoy your sleek and polished blowout!

Tips for Blow Drying Thick Straight Hair

  • Use a high-quality blow dryer with a concentrator nozzle to direct the heat where you need it.
  • Don't use a brush that's too small, as it can create too much tension on the hair and cause breakage.
  • When you're blow-drying your hair, keep the nozzle at least six inches away from your hair to prevent heat damage.
  • If you have very thick hair, consider using a leave-in conditioner or detangler before blow-drying to make the process easier.
  • If you're in a hurry, you can rough-dry your hair with a towel before blow-drying to speed up the process.
  • Don't forget to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the blow dryer.
  • For extra volume, use a round brush to lift the roots of your hair as you blow-dry.

FAQs

Q: Should I blow dry my hair every day?
A: It's best to limit your blow-drying to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair. Q: Can I blow dry my hair without a round brush?
A: While a round brush can help create a smooth and polished look, you can still blow dry your hair without one. Just use your fingers to pull the hair taut as you direct the heat from the blow dryer. Q: How can I reduce frizz when blow-drying my hair?
A: Use a heat protectant spray and a hair serum or oil to reduce frizz. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to distribute the heat more evenly.

Hair Care for Thick Straight Hair

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, there are a few things you can do:
  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as it can cause damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers.
In conclusion, blow-drying your thick straight hair can be a great way to achieve a sleek and polished look. By following our step-by-step guide and using the right tools and products, you can achieve salon-quality results at home. Don't forget to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
