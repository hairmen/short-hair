Will Blow Drying My Hair Make It Frizzy Everything You Need To Know In 2023

Written by Oliver Jul 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who struggles with frizzy hair on a daily basis? Do you often wonder if blow drying your hair is making it worse? In this blog post, we'll answer the question "will blow drying my hair make it frizzy?" and provide you with tips and tricks on how to prevent frizz and maintain healthy hair.

  • Description
  • How to Prevent Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Blow Drying Your Hair

Description

The short answer to "will blow drying my hair make it frizzy?" is yes, blow drying can contribute to frizz if not done correctly. The heat from the blow dryer can strip the hair of its natural oils, causing it to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Additionally, if you do not use a heat protectant before blow drying, the heat can cause damage to the hair cuticle, leading to frizz and split ends.

However, that doesn't mean you have to avoid blow drying your hair altogether. By following a few simple steps, you can blow dry your hair without causing frizz or damage.

How to Prevent Frizzy Hair

The first step to preventing frizzy hair is to start with a good shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically designed for frizzy hair and contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter, which help to moisturize and nourish the hair.

After washing your hair, avoid rubbing it with a towel, as this can cause friction and lead to frizz. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.

Next, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair before blow drying. This will help to protect your hair from the heat and prevent damage to the cuticle.

Step-by-Step Guide to Blow Drying Your Hair

Now that you've prepared your hair for blow drying, it's time to get started. Follow these steps for a smooth and frizz-free blowout:

  1. Start by dividing your hair into sections. This will make it easier to blow dry and ensure that all of your hair is evenly dried.
  2. Using a round brush, gently pull your hair taut as you blow dry each section. This will help to smooth out the cuticle and prevent frizz.
  3. Keep the blow dryer moving constantly, and don't focus the heat on one section for too long. This can cause damage to your hair and lead to frizz.
  4. When you're finished blow drying, use the cool shot button on your blow dryer to set your style and seal the cuticle.

Tips to Keep Your Hair Healthy and Frizz-Free

In addition to following the steps above, there are a few other things you can do to keep your hair healthy and frizz-free:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often. These can cause damage to your hair and lead to frizz.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and lead to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

FAQs

Can blow drying cause hair damage?

Yes, blow drying can cause hair damage if not done correctly. The heat from the blow dryer can strip the hair of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and brittle. Additionally, if you do not use a heat protectant before blow drying, the heat can cause damage to the hair cuticle, leading to frizz and split ends.

What is the best heat protectant for blow drying?

The best heat protectant for blow drying is one that contains ingredients like silicone, which help to seal the cuticle and protect the hair from heat damage. Look for heat protectants that are specifically designed for blow drying and contain nourishing ingredients like argan oil or shea butter.

How often should I blow dry my hair?

It's best to avoid blow drying your hair every day, as this can cause damage and lead to frizz. Instead, try to limit blow drying to 2-3 times per week, and let your hair air dry on the other days.

Hair Care Products for Frizzy Hair

If you're struggling with frizzy hair, there are a few products you can use to help tame your locks:

  • Shampoo and conditioner for frizzy hair
  • Heat protectant spray or serum
  • Frizz-fighting hair oil
  • Anti-humidity hairspray
  • Deep conditioning treatment

By following these tips and using the right products, you can blow dry your hair without causing frizz or damage. So go ahead and enjoy a smooth and silky blowout!
