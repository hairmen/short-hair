How To Get Rid Of Face Framing Layers

Written by Petter Nov 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your face framing layers and want a new look? Removing face framing layers can be a process, but it is not impossible. In this article, we will discuss how to get rid of face framing layers and give you some tips on how to care for your hair after the process.

Description

Face framing layers are a popular hairstyle that adds depth and dimension to your hair. These layers can frame your face and accentuate your facial features. However, over time, you may want to change your hairstyle and remove these layers. Removing face framing layers can be done at home or by a professional hairstylist.

How to Get Rid of Face Framing Layers

There are a few ways to get rid of face framing layers, including:

1. Let Your Hair Grow Out

The easiest and most natural way to get rid of face framing layers is to let your hair grow out. You can trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy, but avoid cutting the face framing layers. Over time, your hair will grow out, and the layers will fade away.

2. Cut Your Hair Short

If you want a more drastic change, you can cut your hair short. A short haircut will remove the face framing layers and give you a fresh new look. However, make sure to choose a haircut that flatters your face shape and hair texture.

3. Get a Professional Haircut

If you are not confident in cutting your hair at home, you can go to a professional hairstylist. They can remove the face framing layers and give you a new haircut that fits your style and preferences. Make sure to communicate your desires clearly to the hairstylist to ensure they achieve the look you want.

Step by Step

If you decide to remove your face framing layers at home, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. This will ensure that your hair is clean and ready for the next step.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Using a pair of hair scissors, cut your hair in a straight line, avoiding the face framing layers. You can use a mirror to ensure that your hair is even on both sides.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

After cutting your hair, you can style it as desired. Use a hairbrush or a wide-tooth comb to style your hair and add volume.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get rid of face framing layers and care for your hair:

1. Be Patient

Removing face framing layers can take time, so be patient and let your hair grow out naturally. Avoid cutting your hair too short or too often, as this can damage your hair and slow down the growth process.

2. Use Hair Products

Use hair products that promote hair growth and strengthen your hair. Products that contain biotin, keratin, and vitamins can help nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

3. Protect Your Hair

Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and slow down the growth process. Use a heat protectant spray before using any styling tools to protect your hair from heat damage.

FAQ

Can I remove face framing layers at home?

Yes, you can remove face framing layers at home, but be careful not to cut your hair too short or unevenly. If you are not confident in cutting your hair, you can go to a professional hairstylist.

How long does it take to remove face framing layers?

It can take several months to a year for your hair to grow out and remove the face framing layers. Be patient and avoid cutting your hair too often.

How can I care for my hair after removing face framing layers?

Use hair products that promote hair growth and strengthen your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and protect your hair from heat damage. Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and avoid split ends.

Hair Care

After removing face framing layers, it is important to take care of your hair. Use hair products that promote hair growth and strengthen your hair, such as shampoos and conditioners that contain biotin and keratin. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray. Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and avoid split ends.

In conclusion, removing face framing layers can be a process, but it is not impossible. Whether you choose to let your hair grow out or get a new haircut, make sure to take care of your hair and use hair products that promote hair growth and strengthen your hair.
