How To Cut Layers In Your Hair Easy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle without losing too much length, adding layers is a great option. Layers can add volume and texture to your hair, making it look more dynamic and interesting. And the best part? Cutting layers in your hair is easy to do at home, even if you're not a professional hairstylist. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step by step, so you can achieve the look you want without breaking the bank.

Description

Cutting layers in your hair involves creating different lengths within your hair, so some sections are shorter than others. This creates depth and dimension in your hairstyle, making it look more interesting than if you had all one length. There are a few different techniques you can use to cut layers in your hair, but we'll focus on the easiest and most straightforward method for beginners.

How to Cut Layers in Your Hair Easy: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, you'll need a few basic supplies. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Clips to section your hair

Make sure you have everything you need before you start, so you don't have to stop halfway through and go looking for something.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's easier to cut hair when it's clean and dry, so start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. Once it's dry, comb it out so it's smooth and tangle-free.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using the comb, separate your hair into sections. You can start by parting your hair down the middle and then creating two more sections on either side. Use the clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin with one section of hair, and hold it straight out from your head. Use the comb to comb through the hair, and then use the scissors to snip off the ends. Start by cutting a small amount of hair at a time, and then work your way up to cutting more if you want a more dramatic look.

Step 5: Repeat

Continue cutting each section of hair, working your way around your head. Make sure you're cutting each section to the same length, so your layers are even.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Make sure you're cutting each section to the same length, so your layers are even.
  • Don't rush. Take your time and make sure you're happy with each section before moving on to the next one.
  • It's always a good idea to have someone else help you, especially if you're cutting the back of your head.

FAQ

Can I cut layers in my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair, but it can be trickier than cutting straight hair. Make sure you cut each section of hair when it's dry and in its natural state, so you can see how it will look when it's styled.

How often should I cut my layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to change up your hairstyle. If you're happy with the length of your hair, you can just trim the layers every few months to keep them looking fresh.

Hair Care

Cutting layers in your hair can be a fun and easy way to change up your hairstyle. However, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Make sure you're using a shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. Use a heat protectant spray if you're using a straightener or curling iron, and try to let your hair air dry as much as possible.

Cutting layers in your hair can be easy and fun with the right tools and a little bit of patience. Give it a try and see how you like the new look!
