Can Your Hair Still Grow If You Have Scalp Psoriasis

Written by Petter Sep 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Scalp psoriasis is a common skin condition that affects many people. It can cause red, itchy, and scaly patches on the scalp, which can be quite uncomfortable. But what about hair growth? Can your hair still grow if you have scalp psoriasis? In this article, we’ll explore this question and provide some helpful tips for managing scalp psoriasis to promote hair growth.

Description

Scalp psoriasis is a chronic skin condition that causes red, scaly patches on the scalp. It’s an autoimmune disorder that affects the skin cells, causing them to grow too quickly. This results in a buildup of skin cells on the scalp, which can be itchy, uncomfortable, and unsightly. Scalp psoriasis can also affect hair growth, as it can cause hair follicles to become inflamed and obstructed.

How to

If you have scalp psoriasis, there are several things you can do to promote hair growth:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

When you have scalp psoriasis, it’s important to use a gentle shampoo that won’t irritate your scalp. Look for a shampoo that’s specifically designed for sensitive skin, and avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or fragrances.

2. Moisturize your scalp

Keeping your scalp moisturized can help reduce the inflammation and itching associated with scalp psoriasis. Use a moisturizer that’s designed for the scalp, and apply it regularly to keep your skin hydrated.

3. Avoid scratching your scalp

Scratching your scalp can make scalp psoriasis worse, as it can lead to further inflammation and irritation. If you feel the urge to scratch, try using a cold compress or taking a cool shower to soothe your skin.

Step by step

Here are some step-by-step tips for managing scalp psoriasis:

1. Cleanse your scalp

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo that’s designed for sensitive skin. Massage the shampoo into your scalp, being careful not to scratch or irritate the affected areas. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

2. Apply a moisturizer

After shampooing, apply a moisturizer to your scalp to help soothe and hydrate your skin. Look for a product that’s specifically designed for the scalp and contains ingredients like aloe vera or coconut oil.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons can be harsh on your scalp and hair. Avoid using these tools as much as possible, and opt for air-drying or low-heat styling instead.

4. See a dermatologist

If your scalp psoriasis is severe or not responding to at-home treatments, it’s important to see a dermatologist. They can prescribe medications or recommend other treatments to help manage your symptoms and promote hair growth.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for managing scalp psoriasis:

1. Wear a hat

Wearing a hat can help protect your scalp from the sun and other environmental irritants that can aggravate scalp psoriasis.

2. Eat a healthy diet

A healthy diet can help promote hair growth and reduce inflammation in the body. Try to eat a balanced diet that’s rich in fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

3. Manage stress

Stress can worsen scalp psoriasis, so it’s important to find ways to manage stress in your life. Try practicing yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises to help reduce stress and promote relaxation.

FAQ

Q: Can scalp psoriasis cause hair loss?

A: Yes, scalp psoriasis can cause hair loss if it’s severe or left untreated. It can damage hair follicles and cause them to become inflamed, which can lead to hair loss.

Q: Is scalp psoriasis contagious?

A: No, scalp psoriasis is not contagious. It’s an autoimmune disorder that’s caused by an overactive immune system.

Q: Can scalp psoriasis be cured?

A: There is no cure for scalp psoriasis, but it can be managed with proper treatment and self-care.

Hair Care

If you have scalp psoriasis, it’s important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Don’t over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair every other day, or even less often if possible.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair and scalp than a brush. Use it to detangle your hair after washing, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

3. Trim your hair regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, or as needed to keep your hair looking its best.

In conclusion, scalp psoriasis can be a frustrating and uncomfortable condition, but it doesn’t have to affect your hair growth. With proper treatment and self-care, you can manage your symptoms and promote healthy hair growth. If you’re struggling with scalp psoriasis, don’t hesitate to reach out to a dermatologist for help.
