How To Keep Curly Hair Healthy When Swimming

Written by Oliver Oct 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Swimming is a great way to cool off during the hot summer months, but it can be damaging to your curly hair. Chlorine, saltwater, and other chemicals can cause your hair to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Fortunately, there are a few simple steps you can take to keep your curls healthy while still enjoying a dip in the pool or ocean.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wet your hair before swimming

Wetting your hair before entering the pool or ocean can help reduce the amount of chlorine or saltwater that your hair absorbs. This is because hair is like a sponge and can only absorb so much water. By wetting your hair beforehand, you are filling it up with clean water, leaving less room for damaging chemicals.

Step 2: Apply a leave-in conditioner

Before swimming, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help protect it from chlorine or saltwater damage. A good leave-in conditioner will form a barrier between your hair and the water, helping to prevent your curls from becoming dry and brittle.

Step 3: Wear a swim cap

If you have long hair, wearing a swim cap can be a good way to protect your curls from the damaging effects of chlorine or saltwater. Look for a swim cap that is made specifically for curly hair, as these tend to be more comfortable and less likely to cause hair breakage.

Step 4: Rinse your hair after swimming

After swimming, it's important to rinse your hair with clean water to remove any chlorine or saltwater that may have accumulated in your curls. Make sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to help restore moisture to your hair.

Tips for Keeping Your Curly Hair Healthy

Tip 1: Use a clarifying shampoo

If you swim frequently, it's a good idea to use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup from chlorine or saltwater. Look for a clarifying shampoo that is gentle and sulfate-free to avoid drying out your curls.

Tip 2: Avoid using heat styling tools

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners can be damaging to curly hair. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help enhance your natural curls.

Tip 3: Deep condition regularly

Deep conditioning your hair once a week can help keep your curls healthy and hydrated. Look for a deep conditioner that is formulated specifically for curly hair, as these tend to be more moisturizing.

Tip 4: Trim your hair regularly

Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can be especially damaging for curly hair. Make sure to visit a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair to avoid any unwanted frizz or unevenness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I still swim if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can still swim if you have curly hair! Just make sure to take the proper precautions to protect your curls from chlorine or saltwater damage.

Q: Should I use a sulfate-free shampoo?

A: Yes, using a sulfate-free shampoo can be beneficial for curly hair. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to breakage.

Q: Can I use a regular conditioner before swimming?

A: It's best to use a leave-in conditioner before swimming, as these are specifically formulated to protect your hair from chlorine or saltwater damage.

Curly Hair Care

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and hydrated. In addition to the tips mentioned above, here are a few more things you can do to care for your curls:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help reduce friction and prevent tangles while you sleep.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can be damaging and drying.

By following these tips and taking the proper precautions, you can keep your curly hair healthy and beautiful all year round!
