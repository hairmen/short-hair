What Helps Frizzy Curly Hair Tips Tricks And Products To Tame Your Mane

Written by Eveline Nov 15, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that frizz is a constant battle. Whether you have tight coils or loose waves, frizz can make your hair look dry, dull, and out of control. But don't worry – there are plenty of things you can do to help tame your frizz and enhance your curls. In this article, we'll go over some of the best tips, tricks, and products for managing frizzy curly hair.

How to Manage Frizzy Curly Hair

The first step in managing frizzy curly hair is understanding your hair type. Everyone's curls are different, so it's important to figure out what works for you. Some people have tighter curls that tend to be dry and prone to frizz, while others have looser waves that are more prone to getting weighed down.

Once you know your hair type, you can start experimenting with different styling techniques. Here are a few tips to get you started:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb or Detangling Brush

Curly hair is more prone to tangles, so it's important to use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently work through knots. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, taking care not to pull or tug too hard.

2. Apply Products on Wet Hair

Curly hair is naturally dry, so it's important to keep it hydrated. Apply styling products like leave-in conditioners, curl creams or gels, and oils to wet hair to help lock in moisture and prevent frizz.

3. Don't Over-Dry Your Hair

Blow-drying your hair can cause damage and make frizz worse. Instead, let your hair air-dry as much as possible. If you do use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment and dry on a low heat setting.

4. Use Silk or Satin Pillowcases

Regular cotton pillowcases can cause friction and breakage, which can lead to more frizz. Switch to silk or satin pillowcases to help protect your curls while you sleep.

Products for Frizzy Curly Hair

There are many products on the market specifically designed for frizzy curly hair. Here are a few that are worth trying:

1. Curl Creams

Curl creams are designed to define curls and combat frizz. Look for one that's silicone-free and contains nourishing ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil.

2. Leave-In Conditioners

Leave-in conditioners are a great way to hydrate and detangle curly hair. Look for one that's lightweight and won't weigh down your curls.

3. Anti-Frizz Serums

Anti-frizz serums can help smooth down flyaways and control frizz. Look for one that's alcohol-free and contains ingredients like argan oil or jojoba oil.

Tips for Taming Frizzy Curly Hair

Aside from using the right products and styling techniques, there are a few other things you can do to help tame frizzy curly hair:

1. Don't Touch Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz. Try to resist the urge to play with your curls throughout the day.

2. Use a Microfiber Towel

Regular towels can cause friction and damage to curly hair. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water after washing your hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can damage curly hair and make frizz worse. Try to avoid using them as much as possible.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to more frizz. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

FAQs About Frizzy Curly Hair

Q: Can I brush my curly hair?

A: Yes, but it's important to use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush and be very gentle to avoid causing frizz or breakage.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people with very dry hair may only need to wash once a week, while others with oilier hair may need to wash every other day. Experiment to find what works for you.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my curly hair?

A: Yes, but it's important to choose a shampoo and conditioner that's gentle and hydrating. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain nourishing ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter.

Hair Care for Frizzy Curly Hair

In addition to using the right styling techniques and products, there are a few other things you can do to help keep your frizzy curly hair healthy and happy:

1. Drink Plenty of Water

Drinking water can help keep your hair hydrated from the inside out. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water a day.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

A healthy diet can help keep your hair strong and nourished. Make sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and healthy fats.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can cause damage to curly hair and make frizz worse. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF when you're out in the sun.

By following these tips and using the right products, you can help manage frizzy curly hair and enhance your natural curls. With a little patience and experimentation, you'll soon have a head of happy, healthy curls!
