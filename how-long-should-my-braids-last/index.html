How Long Should My Braids Last

Written by Eveline Nov 14, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're considering getting braids, one of the most common questions you may have is, "how long should my braids last?" The answer to this question can vary depending on several factors, including the type of braids you have, how well you take care of them, and your hair type.

Types of Braids

There are several types of braids, including box braids, cornrows, micro braids, and Senegalese twists. The type of braids you have can affect how long they will last. Generally, box braids and Senegalese twists can last up to eight weeks, while cornrows and micro braids can last up to six weeks.

How to Make Braids Last Longer

If you want your braids to last as long as possible, there are several things you can do:

  • Wash your hair before getting braids. This will help remove any product buildup or dirt that could cause your braids to loosen faster.
  • Avoid getting your braids wet for the first few days after getting them. This will give them time to set and stay in place.
  • Wrap your hair at night with a satin scarf or bonnet to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your braids, as this can cause them to loosen and fall out.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're new to getting braids, here's a step-by-step guide to help you understand the process:

  1. Wash and condition your hair before getting braids.
  2. Choose the type of braids you want and the size you prefer.
  3. Divide your hair into sections and start braiding from the root to the end.
  4. Secure the end of the braid with a rubber band or hair tie.
  5. Repeat the process until your entire head is braided.
  6. Trim any stray hairs or flyaways.

Tips for Braid Care

Here are some additional tips to help you take care of your braids:

  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as this can cause buildup and make your braids look greasy.
  • Use a lightweight oil or serum to moisturize your scalp and prevent it from getting dry and itchy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as this can cause your braids to unravel and become frizzy.
  • Don't keep your braids in for too long. While it may be tempting to keep them in for several weeks, it's important to give your hair a break to prevent damage and breakage.

FAQs

How long should I wait before getting braids again?

You should wait at least two weeks before getting braids again. This will give your scalp time to heal and prevent damage.

Can I wash my braids?

Yes, you can wash your braids, but be gentle when doing so. Use a mild shampoo and avoid scrubbing your scalp too hard, as this can cause your braids to loosen.

What should I do if my braids start to unravel?

If your braids start to unravel, try to secure them with a rubber band or hair tie. If they continue to unravel, it may be time to take them out and get them redone.

Hair Care Tips for Braids

While braids can be a great protective style, it's important to take care of your hair while wearing them. Here are some additional hair care tips:

  • Moisturize your hair and scalp regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, as this can cause tension and breakage.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you can help ensure that your braids last as long as possible and keep your hair healthy and strong.
