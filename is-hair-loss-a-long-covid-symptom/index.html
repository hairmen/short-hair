Is Hair Loss A Long Covid Symptom

Written by Daniel Aug 31, 2023 · 3 min read

Table of Content:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Since the pandemic started, many people have experienced various symptoms related to Covid-19, such as cough, fever, and loss of taste and smell. But, as time goes by, there are some people who experience long-term effects of Covid-19, also known as long Covid. One of the symptoms that have been reported is hair loss. In this blog post, we will discuss whether hair loss is a long Covid symptom and what you can do about it.

How to

The first thing you need to do is to consult with your doctor or dermatologist. They can help you determine whether your hair loss is related to Covid-19 or other factors. Your doctor may recommend some tests to check your hormone levels, iron levels, and thyroid function.

Step by step

If your hair loss is related to Covid-19, there are some steps you can take to promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Here are some steps:

  1. Take care of your overall health. Eat a healthy diet, exercise regularly, and get enough sleep.
  2. Reduce stress. Stress can cause hair loss, so try to manage your stress levels through meditation, yoga, or other relaxation techniques.
  3. Take supplements. Your doctor may recommend supplements such as biotin, zinc, and iron to promote hair growth.
  4. Use gentle hair care products. Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools that can damage your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips that can help you prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  • Avoid wearing hats or headbands for long periods
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption

FAQ

Is hair loss a common symptom of Covid-19?

No, hair loss is not a common symptom of Covid-19. However, it has been reported as a long-term effect of Covid-19.

How long does hair loss last after Covid-19?

It varies from person to person. Some people may experience hair loss for a few months, while others may experience it for a year or more.

Can hair loss be reversed?

Yes, in most cases, hair loss can be reversed with proper treatment and hair care.

Hair Care

Here are some hair care tips that can help you prevent hair loss:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo
  • Condition your hair after washing
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair
  • Don't rub your hair vigorously with a towel, instead gently pat it dry
  • Avoid using hair dryers and other heat styling tools
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends

In conclusion, hair loss can be a long Covid symptom, but it is not a common symptom. If you are experiencing hair loss, consult with your doctor or dermatologist to determine the cause and get proper treatment. In addition, take care of your overall health, reduce stress, and use gentle hair care products to promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss.
