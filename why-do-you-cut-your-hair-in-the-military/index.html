Why Do You Cut Your Hair In The Military

Written by Frank Oct 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering joining the military, you may have heard about the strict grooming standards that are in place. One of the most noticeable of these standards is the requirement for short, cropped haircuts. In this article, we'll explore why the military has this requirement and what it means for those who serve.

Why Does the Military Require Short Haircuts?

The military has strict grooming standards for a variety of reasons. One of the main reasons is to promote uniformity and discipline. By requiring all service members to have short haircuts, the military is able to create a sense of unity and cohesion among the ranks.

Another reason for the short haircut requirement is practicality. Short haircuts are easier to maintain in the field and require less time and effort to style. This is important for service members who may be deployed to remote locations where access to grooming facilities is limited.

Finally, short haircuts are also a safety measure. In combat situations, long hair can become a liability. It can get caught in equipment or obscure vision, putting the service member and their unit at risk.

How to Get a Military Haircut

If you're joining the military, you'll need to get a military haircut before reporting for basic training. While you can go to a barber or stylist to get your haircut, many service members choose to do it themselves or have a fellow service member do it for them.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Hair for the Military

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

2. Use clippers to trim the sides and back of your head. Use a guard size of 1/8 inch or shorter.

3. Use scissors to trim the top of your head. Trim it to a length of 1/4 inch or shorter.

4. Use the clippers to clean up any stray hairs or uneven spots.

5. Use a comb to make sure your hair is even all around.

Tips for Maintaining Your Military Haircut

1. Trim your hair regularly to maintain its length and shape.

2. Keep your hair clean and free of product buildup.

3. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

4. Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage the hair and make it harder to maintain a short haircut.

FAQs About Military Haircuts

Q: Can women have short haircuts in the military?

A: Yes, women are also required to have short haircuts in the military.

Q: Can I have a beard or mustache in the military?

A: The military has strict guidelines for facial hair. Generally, service members are only allowed to have mustaches that do not extend past the corners of the mouth. Beards are generally not allowed, except for medical or religious reasons.

Hair Care Tips for Short Haircuts

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

2. Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as this can damage the hair and make it harder to maintain a short haircut.

3. Consider using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid breaking it.

In conclusion, the military requires short haircuts for a variety of reasons, including promoting uniformity and discipline, practicality, and safety. If you're planning to join the military, it's important to know how to maintain your haircut and keep your hair healthy.
