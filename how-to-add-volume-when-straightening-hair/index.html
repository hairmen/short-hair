How To Add Volume When Straightening Hair

Welcome to our guide on how to add volume when straightening hair! If you have naturally flat or thin hair, you may find that straightening your hair makes it even flatter. However, with a few simple tips and tricks, you can add volume to your hair while still achieving a sleek and straight look.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Add Volume When Straightening Hair
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Adding Volume
  4. FAQs

How to Add Volume When Straightening Hair

The key to adding volume when straightening hair is to use the right products and techniques. Here are a few tips to get you started:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Before you begin straightening your hair, make sure it is clean and free of any product buildup. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any excess oils, dirt, or product residue.

2. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

To add volume to your hair, use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products are formulated to give your hair a lift and create the appearance of thicker, fuller hair.

3. Apply a Volumizing Mousse

Apply a volumizing mousse to your hair before blow-drying. This will help to add volume and hold your style in place.

4. Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

To further add volume to your hair, blow-dry your hair upside down. This will lift the roots and create a fuller look.

5. Use a Round Brush

When straightening your hair, use a round brush to lift the roots and add volume. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends, pulling the brush through your hair as you go.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to adding volume when straightening your hair:

Step 1:

Start with clean, damp hair. Apply a volumizing mousse to your hair, focusing on the roots.

Step 2:

Blow-dry your hair upside down using a round brush.

Step 3:

Once your hair is dry, section it off using clips or hair ties.

Step 4:

Take a small section of hair and clamp the straightening iron at the roots.

Step 5:

Slowly glide the straightening iron down the length of your hair, pulling the brush through your hair as you go.

Step 6:

Repeat steps 4 and 5 for each section of hair.

Step 7:

Once you have straightened all of your hair, apply a light mist of hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Adding Volume

Here are a few additional tips for adding volume when straightening your hair:

1. Use a Root Lifting Spray

If you have very flat hair, try using a root lifting spray to add even more volume.

2. Try a Half-Up, Half-Down Style

To add volume to the crown of your head, try a half-up, half-down style.

3. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can create the appearance of thicker, fuller hair. Use a comb to tease the roots of your hair, then smooth the top layer with a brush.

FAQs

Q: Can I add volume to my hair without using heat?

A: Yes! Try using a volumizing dry shampoo or texturizing spray to add volume to your hair without heat.

Q: Will using too much product weigh my hair down?

A: Yes, using too much product can weigh your hair down. Use a small amount of product and focus on the roots of your hair.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It is best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

1. Use a Heat Protectant Spray

Before heat styling your hair, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

2. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims will help to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

3. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

We hope you found these tips helpful! With a little bit of practice, you can easily add volume to your hair when straightening it.
