What Is It Called When You Get Bald Spots On Your Head

Written by Luffy Oct 06, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Having bald spots on your head can be a troubling experience for anyone. While it is typically associated with men, women can also experience this condition. It is important to know what it is called, what causes it, and how to treat it. In this blog post, we will answer all your questions about bald spots on your head.

Description

The medical term for bald spots on your head is alopecia areata. This is an autoimmune condition that causes hair loss in round patches on the scalp. It can also affect other parts of the body, such as the eyebrows, eyelashes, and beard. Alopecia areata occurs when the immune system attacks hair follicles, causing them to shrink and stop producing hair.

How to

If you are experiencing bald spots on your head, it is important to see a dermatologist. They can diagnose alopecia areata and recommend treatment options. There is no cure for the condition, but there are treatments that can help slow down or stop hair loss and promote hair regrowth.

Step by step

The following are common treatments for alopecia areata:

  • Corticosteroids: These are anti-inflammatory medications that can be applied topically or injected into the scalp to reduce inflammation and promote hair regrowth.
  • Topical immunotherapy: This involves applying a chemical to the scalp to produce an allergic reaction, which can stimulate hair regrowth.
  • Minoxidil: This is a topical medication that can be applied to the scalp to promote hair regrowth.
  • Anthralin: This is a topical medication that can be applied to the scalp to promote hair regrowth.
  • Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy: This involves injecting a concentrated solution of platelets into the scalp to promote hair regrowth.

Tips

It is important to take care of your hair and scalp to prevent hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids, cornrows, and ponytails.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals, such as relaxers and hair dyes.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, such as iron and zinc.
  • Manage stress through exercise, meditation, or therapy.
  • Get enough sleep and avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.

FAQ

Can alopecia areata be cured?

There is no cure for alopecia areata, but there are treatments that can help slow down or stop hair loss and promote hair regrowth.

Is alopecia areata hereditary?

There is a genetic component to alopecia areata, but it is not always hereditary. It is more likely to occur in people who have a family history of the condition.

Can stress cause alopecia areata?

Stress can be a trigger for alopecia areata, but it is not the sole cause of the condition.

Hair Care

To take care of your hair and scalp, it is important to:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, too often.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to harsh chemicals, such as chlorine in swimming pools.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

Now that you know what it is called when you get bald spots on your head, you can take steps to prevent and treat the condition. Remember to seek medical attention if you notice any hair loss or changes in your hair.
