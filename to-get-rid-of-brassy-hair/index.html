How To Get Rid Of Brassy Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Aug 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who has dyed their hair blonde or has naturally blonde hair, you may have experienced the dreaded brassiness. Brassiness is a common hair problem that occurs when your hair takes on a yellow or orange tint. This can happen for a variety of reasons, such as sun exposure, hard water, or using the wrong hair products. But don't worry, there are ways to get rid of it! In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you get rid of brassy hair.

What Causes Brassy Hair?

Before we dive into how to get rid of brassy hair, let's first talk about what causes it. As we mentioned earlier, there are a few factors that can contribute to brassiness:

  • Sun exposure
  • Hard water
  • Using the wrong hair products
  • Not using enough toner

Now that we know what causes brassy hair, let's get into how to fix it.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Hair: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for anyone with blonde hair. The purple tones in the shampoo help to neutralize the yellow and orange tones in your hair, leaving you with a cooler, more ashy tone. Use the shampoo once or twice a week, depending on how often you wash your hair.

Step 2: Try a Toner

If your hair is still looking brassy after using purple shampoo, you may want to try a toner. Toners are a great way to get rid of any remaining yellow or orange tones in your hair. You can either get a toner done at a salon or use an at-home toner kit.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair, which can lead to even more brassiness. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray before using them.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can also cause brassiness in your hair. If you're going to be spending time outside, make sure to wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair.

Tips for Preventing Brassy Hair

Preventing brassiness in the first place is always the best option. Here are some tips to help you keep your blonde hair looking its best:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Choose hair products specifically made for blonde hair
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Brassy Hair

Q: Can brassy hair be fixed?

A: Yes! There are a variety of ways to fix brassy hair, including using purple shampoo, toners, and avoiding heat styling.

Q: How often should I use purple shampoo?

A: It depends on how often you wash your hair. If you wash your hair every day, you can use purple shampoo once or twice a week. If you only wash your hair once or twice a week, you can use it every time you wash your hair.

Q: Can I use toner at home?

A: Yes, there are toner kits available for at-home use. However, if you're unsure about how to use them, it's best to get a toner done at a salon.

Hair Care for Blonde Hair

Blonde hair requires a little extra care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips to help you care for your blonde hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent damage
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it healthy
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your blonde hair looking its best and avoid any unwanted brassiness. Remember, prevention is key, so make sure to take care of your hair and use the right products to keep it healthy and beautiful.
