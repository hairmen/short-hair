How To Cut Short Layers In Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how important it is to find the right haircut that complements your curls. Short layers can add volume and definition to your curls, making them look more bouncy and lively. However, cutting short layers in curly hair can be a bit tricky, and you need to be careful not to end up with a disaster. In this post, we'll guide you through the process of cutting short layers in curly hair, step-by-step.

How to Cut Short Layers in Curly Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut short layers in curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and detangle your hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and free of tangles. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for curly hair. After washing your hair, gently detangle it using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.

Step 2: Part your hair

Part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference. Use a rat-tail comb to make a clean part.

Step 3: Section your hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Clip each section with a hair clip.

Step 4: Start cutting

Take a small section of hair from the back and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and moving upwards. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the back to the front.

Step 5: Check the length

After cutting all the layers, check the length of your hair. If you want shorter layers, repeat the above process, cutting at a steeper angle. If you want longer layers, cut at a shallower angle.

Step 6: Blend the layers

Use a pair of blending shears to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless look and prevent any choppy edges.

Step 7: Style your hair

Style your hair as desired. You can use a diffuser to enhance your curls or a straightener to create a sleek look.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers in Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short layers in curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid making any big mistakes.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in uneven layers.
  • Don't cut your hair too short, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, seek advice from a professional stylist.

FAQs About Cutting Short Layers in Curly Hair

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself, but it's important to be careful and start with small sections of hair. If you're unsure, seek advice from a professional stylist.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut your curly hair every 3-4 months to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Will cutting short layers make my hair look thinner?

No, cutting short layers can add volume and definition to your curls, making them look more bouncy and lively.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.

Cutting short layers in curly hair can be a bit daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, bouncy look that complements your curls. Whether you're cutting your hair yourself or seeking advice from a professional stylist, remember to be patient and take it slow. Happy cutting!
