Hairstyles To Avoid For Diamond Face Shape

Written by Aprilia Oct 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a diamond face shape, then you know that it can be challenging to find the perfect hairstyle that complements your face. The diamond face shape is defined by a narrow forehead and jawline, with the cheekbones being the widest part of the face. To help you navigate the world of hairstyling, we've compiled a list of hairstyles to avoid for diamond face shape.

Layered Hairstyles

Layered hairstyles can be challenging for diamond-shaped faces because they can make the face look even longer. Instead, opt for straight, blunt cuts that fall just below the jawline. This will help to balance out the width of the cheekbones and make the face appear more proportionate.

Bangs

Bangs can be tricky for diamond-shaped faces because they can make the forehead look even narrower. Instead, try a side-swept fringe that falls just below the eyebrows. This will help to balance out the face and draw attention away from the narrow forehead.

Slicked-Back Hair

Slicked-back hair can be a bold and edgy look, but it's not the best choice for diamond-shaped faces. This hairstyle can make the face look longer and more angular, which can be unflattering. Instead, try a side part with loose waves or curls. This will help to soften the face and add volume to the cheekbones.

Volume at the Crown

Hairstyles that add volume at the crown can be tricky for diamond-shaped faces because they can make the face look longer. Instead, try a sleek and straight style that falls just below the jawline. This will help to balance out the width of the cheekbones and make the face appear more proportionate.

Long Hair

Long hair can be a beautiful look, but it's not always the best choice for diamond-shaped faces. Long hair can make the face look even longer and more angular, which can be unflattering. Instead, try a shoulder-length cut with a slight wave or curl. This will help to soften the face and add volume to the cheekbones.

Pixie Cut

The pixie cut can be a fun and edgy look, but it's not always the best choice for diamond-shaped faces. This hairstyle can make the face look longer and more angular, which can be unflattering. Instead, try a bob or lob that falls just below the jawline. This will help to balance out the width of the cheekbones and make the face appear more proportionate.

Updos

Updos can be a beautiful and elegant look, but they're not always the best choice for diamond-shaped faces. Updos can make the face look longer and more angular, which can be unflattering. Instead, try a half-up, half-down style with loose waves or curls. This will help to soften the face and add volume to the cheekbones.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a diamond face shape?

A diamond face shape is defined by a narrow forehead and jawline, with the cheekbones being the widest part of the face.

What hairstyles are best for diamond-shaped faces?

Straight, blunt cuts that fall just below the jawline are the best choice for diamond-shaped faces. Side-swept fringes and shoulder-length cuts with slight waves or curls are also good options.

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a diamond-shaped face?

Avoid layered hairstyles, bangs, slicked-back hair, volume at the crown, long hair, pixie cuts, and updos.

Hair Care Tips

Regardless of your face shape, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and flat irons.
  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using heat-styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.

By following these hair care tips and avoiding the hairstyles that don't flatter your diamond face shape, you'll be able to find the perfect look that makes you feel confident and beautiful.
