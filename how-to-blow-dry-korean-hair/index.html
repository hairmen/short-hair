How To Blow Dry Korean Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have Korean hair, you know that it can be challenging to style. Blow drying your hair is one of the best ways to achieve a sleek, polished, and voluminous look. In this guide, we will show you how to blow dry your Korean hair like a pro. We will cover everything from the tools you need to the techniques you should use. Read on to learn more!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Need
  • Understanding Your Hair Type
  • Preparation
  • Blow Drying Techniques
  • Tips & Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You Need

Before you start blow drying your Korean hair, you need to have the right tools. Here are the things you need:

  • A blow dryer with a concentrator nozzle
  • A round brush
  • A heat protectant spray
  • A detangling brush or comb
  • Hair clips to section your hair

Make sure that your blow dryer has different heat and speed settings. This will allow you to adjust the temperature and airflow according to your hair type.

Understanding Your Hair Type

Korean hair can come in different textures, thicknesses, and lengths. It is essential to understand your hair type so that you can choose the right products and techniques for blow drying. Here are some common Korean hair types:

  • Straight and fine
  • Straight and thick
  • Wavy and fine
  • Wavy and thick
  • Curly and fine
  • Curly and thick

Depending on your hair type, you may need to use a different brush or adjust the temperature and speed of your blow dryer.

Preparation

Before you start blow drying your Korean hair, you need to prepare it properly. Here are the steps you should follow:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Do not rub your hair vigorously with a towel. Instead, use a microfiber towel or a t-shirt to gently squeeze out the excess water.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair. This will help protect your hair from heat damage and make it easier to work with.
  3. Use a detangling brush or comb to remove any tangles or knots. Start from the ends of your hair and work your way up. Do not tug or pull your hair.
  4. Section your hair using hair clips. This will make it easier to blow dry your hair in sections.

Blow Drying Techniques

Now that you have prepared your hair, it's time to start blow drying. Here are the steps you should follow:

  1. Start with the bottom sections of your hair. Use a round brush to lift the roots of your hair and direct the airflow of your blow dryer downwards. Use the concentrator nozzle to control the airflow and prevent frizz.
  2. Move on to the middle sections of your hair. Use the same technique but direct the airflow of your blow dryer towards the ends of your hair. Use the round brush to create volume and bounce.
  3. Finish with the top sections of your hair. Use the same technique but direct the airflow of your blow dryer towards the roots of your hair. This will help create lift and volume at the crown of your head.
  4. Once you have blow dried all sections of your hair, use the cool shot button on your blow dryer to set your style. This will help lock in the shape and add shine.

Tips & Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks that you can use to blow dry your Korean hair:

  • Use a diffuser attachment if you have curly or wavy hair. This will help enhance your natural curls or waves without causing frizz.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to create extra volume at the roots.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to add extra shine and smoothness to your hair.
  • Do not use too much heat. High heat can damage your hair and cause breakage. Use the lowest heat setting that works for your hair type.
  • Do not blow dry your hair every day. Give your hair a break and let it air dry or use other styling techniques.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about blow drying Korean hair:

Can I blow dry my Korean hair if it is damaged?

If your hair is damaged, it is best to avoid blow drying it. Instead, let it air dry or use other gentle styling methods. If you must blow dry your hair, use a low heat setting and a heat protectant spray.

How often should I blow dry my Korean hair?

It is best to avoid blow drying your hair every day. Give your hair a break and let it air dry or use other styling methods. If you must blow dry your hair, limit it to 2-3 times a week.

What is the best brush to use for blow drying Korean hair?

The best brush to use for blow drying Korean hair is a round brush with natural bristles. This will help create volume and bounce without causing damage or frizz.

Hair Care Tips

Blow drying your Korean hair is just one part of your hair care routine. Here are some other tips that can help keep your hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair. This includes blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals. This will help nourish your hair from the inside out.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated and moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and healthy head of Korean hair. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and it will reward you with a stunning style!
