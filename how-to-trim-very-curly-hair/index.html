How To Trim Very Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 08, 2023 · 6 min read

Trimming very curly hair can be a daunting task, especially if you’re not familiar with the texture and patterns. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a clean and healthy-looking cut without damaging your hair. In this article, we’ll go over the steps on how to trim very curly hair, as well as some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to keep your curls looking their best.

  • How to Trim Very Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Trimming Very Curly Hair

How to Trim Very Curly Hair

Trimming very curly hair requires some special considerations compared to straight or wavy hair. One of the most important things to keep in mind is that curly hair tends to shrink up when dry, so you need to be extra careful not to cut off too much length. Here are the basic steps for trimming very curly hair:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start trimming, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural shape of your curls and avoid any surprises once your hair dries. If you need to detangle your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently work through any knots.

2. Section your hair

Divide your hair into several sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will help you focus on one area at a time and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair at once. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness and length of your hair, but aim for sections that are about 1-2 inches wide.

3. Use the right tools

When trimming curly hair, it’s important to use sharp scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Avoid using regular household scissors, as they can cause split ends and damage your hair. You may also want to use a comb or your fingers to guide your scissors and help you achieve the desired length.

4. Cut small sections at a time

Start by trimming a small section of hair at a time, focusing on the ends. You can always cut more later, but it’s harder to add length back once it’s gone. Use your fingers or a comb to hold the section of hair taut, and snip off any split ends or damaged hair. Try to cut at an angle to avoid a blunt, choppy look.

5. Check your progress

After you’ve trimmed a few sections, step back and check your progress in the mirror. Make sure the length is even and that the shape of your curls is intact. If you need to make any adjustments, go back and trim small sections as needed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basic steps for trimming very curly hair, let’s go over a more detailed guide:

1. Gather your tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the tools you need. You’ll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Wide-tooth comb or your fingers
  • Optional: spray bottle with water or leave-in conditioner

2. Wash and dry your hair

Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is prone to frizz, you may want to use a leave-in conditioner or a spray bottle with water to help tame your curls. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle any knots.

3. Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or ties. Aim for sections that are about 1-2 inches wide, depending on the thickness and length of your hair.

4. Start trimming

Choose a section of hair to start with, and use your fingers or a comb to hold it taut. Use your scissors to trim off any split ends or damaged hair, making sure to cut at an angle to avoid a blunt look.

5. Move on to the next section

Once you’ve finished trimming a section, move on to the next one. Try to keep the sections even in size, and work your way around your head. If you need to, use a mirror to check the back of your head.

6. Check your progress

After you’ve finished trimming all the sections, step back and check your progress in the mirror. Make any final adjustments as needed, but be careful not to over-trim your hair.

Tips for Trimming Very Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results when trimming very curly hair:

  • Trim your hair when it’s dry, as curly hair tends to shrink up when dry.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Use a comb or your fingers to guide your scissors and help you achieve the desired length.
  • Cut at an angle to avoid a blunt, choppy look.
  • Step back and check your progress frequently to avoid over-trimming.

FAQs

Can you trim curly hair when it’s wet?

While some people prefer to trim their curly hair when it’s wet, it’s generally better to trim it when it’s dry. This is because curly hair tends to shrink up when dry, so you may end up cutting off more length than you intended if you trim it when it’s wet.

How often should you trim very curly hair?

How often you should trim your very curly hair depends on how fast it grows and how prone it is to damage. As a general rule, aim to trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can you trim curly hair yourself?

Yes, you can trim your curly hair yourself, but it’s important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging your hair. If you’re not confident in your skills, it’s always better to go to a professional stylist.

Hair Care Advice for Very Curly Hair

Trimming your very curly hair is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. Here are some additional tips to help you keep your curls looking their best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as this can cause frizz and damage your curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

Trimming very curly hair may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a clean and healthy-looking cut. Remember to take your time, work in small sections, and check your progress frequently to avoid over-trimming. And don’t forget to take care of your curls with a healthy hair care routine!
