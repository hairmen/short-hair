How To Wear Long Hair In Your 60S

Written by William Jul 01, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're in your 60s and have long hair, you may be wondering how to style it in a way that's age-appropriate and flattering. In this article, we'll provide you with tips and tricks on how to wear long hair in your 60s, as well as hair care advice to keep your locks healthy and vibrant.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Long Hair in Your 60s
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Wearing Long Hair in Your 60s
  • FAQs

How to Wear Long Hair in Your 60s

Long hair can be a beautiful and youthful look, regardless of age. The key to wearing long hair in your 60s is to choose a style that flatters your face shape and hair texture. Here are some tips:

1. Layers

Layers can add volume and movement to long hair, making it look more youthful and fresh. Ask your stylist for long layers that frame your face and add dimension to your hair.

2. Bangs

Adding bangs to your long hair can instantly refresh your look and make you appear younger. Consider getting wispy, side-swept bangs or blunt bangs that hit just above your eyebrows.

3. Updos

Updos are a great way to get your hair out of your face and show off your neck and shoulders. Try a messy bun or a low ponytail for a relaxed, effortless look.

4. Beachy Waves

Beachy waves are a low-maintenance and stylish way to wear long hair. Use a curling iron or a sea salt spray to add texture and create loose waves that frame your face.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to create a simple and elegant updo for long hair:

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

Wash and condition your hair as usual, then let it air dry or blow dry it straight.

2. Create a low ponytail.

Gather your hair at the nape of your neck and secure it with an elastic band.

3. Twist and tuck.

Twist the ponytail into a loose knot and tuck the ends under to create a bun. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place.

4. Finish with hairspray.

Spray your hair with a light hold hairspray to keep the updo in place.

Tips for Wearing Long Hair in Your 60s

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when wearing long hair in your 60s:

1. Embrace your natural hair color.

Gray hair can be beautiful and chic, so don't be afraid to let your natural hair color shine through.

2. Use a volumizing spray.

As we age, our hair can become thinner and less voluminous. Use a volumizing spray or mousse to add fullness and body to your hair.

3. Invest in quality hair products.

Using high-quality hair products can make a big difference in the health and appearance of your hair. Look for products that are specifically formulated for mature hair.

FAQs

1. Can I wear my hair in a ponytail or braid?

Absolutely! Ponytails and braids can be a stylish and practical way to wear long hair in your 60s. Just make sure to keep the style loose and relaxed for a more youthful look.

2. Is it better to wear my hair up or down?

It all depends on your personal style and preference. Experiment with different updos and down hairstyles to see what looks best on you.

Hair Care Tips for Long Hair in Your 60s

Here are some hair care tips to keep your long hair looking healthy and vibrant:

1. Use a gentle shampoo.

Choose a shampoo that's gentle and sulfate-free to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

2. Avoid heat styling.

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause breakage. Try to air dry your hair whenever possible, and use heat-protectant products when you do use styling tools.

3. Get regular trims.

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and full.

Now that you know how to wear long hair in your 60s, it's time to experiment with different styles and find what works best for you. Remember to take care of your hair and embrace your natural beauty at any age.
