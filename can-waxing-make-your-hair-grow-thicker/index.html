Can Waxing Make Your Hair Grow Thicker

Written by Oliver Nov 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who likes to keep their body hair-free, you've probably tried waxing at least once. Waxing is a popular hair removal method that involves applying hot wax to the skin and then removing it quickly, along with the hair. While waxing can leave your skin smooth and hair-free for weeks, there's a common myth that it can make your hair grow back thicker and darker. In this article, we'll explore whether there's any truth to this myth.

Description

Waxing is a popular hair removal method that involves applying hot wax to the skin and then removing it quickly, along with the hair. This method can be used on various parts of the body, including the legs, arms, underarms, bikini area, and face. Waxing is preferred by many people because it removes hair from the root, which means that hair takes longer to grow back as compared to shaving or using hair removal creams.

How to Wax

Waxing at home can be a bit tricky, but it's definitely doable. Here's how you can do it:

  1. Choose the right type of wax. There are two types of wax: hard wax and soft wax. Hard wax is better for sensitive areas like the bikini area, while soft wax is better for larger areas like the legs and arms.
  2. Prepare your skin. Make sure that your skin is clean and dry before you start waxing. You can also apply some talcum powder to absorb any excess moisture.
  3. Apply the wax. Use a spatula to apply the wax in the direction of hair growth. Make sure that you apply a thin layer of wax.
  4. Remove the wax. Hold the skin taut and then remove the wax quickly in the opposite direction of hair growth.
  5. Repeat. If there are any hairs left, you can repeat the process.
  6. Clean up. Use some oil or lotion to remove any leftover wax and soothe the skin.

Step by Step Guide

If you're a beginner, here's a step-by-step guide on how to wax:

Step 1: Choose the Right Wax

As mentioned earlier, there are two types of wax: hard wax and soft wax. Hard wax is better for sensitive areas like the bikini area, while soft wax is better for larger areas like the legs and arms. Choose the right wax depending on the area you want to wax.

Step 2: Prepare Your Skin

Make sure that your skin is clean and dry before you start waxing. You can also apply some talcum powder to absorb any excess moisture.

Step 3: Apply the Wax

Use a spatula to apply the wax in the direction of hair growth. Make sure that you apply a thin layer of wax. Don't apply too much wax as it can make the process more painful.

Step 4: Remove the Wax

Hold the skin taut and then remove the wax quickly in the opposite direction of hair growth. Make sure that you remove the wax in one swift motion.

Step 5: Repeat

If there are any hairs left, you can repeat the process. However, avoid waxing the same area more than twice as it can irritate the skin.

Step 6: Clean Up

Use some oil or lotion to remove any leftover wax and soothe the skin. Avoid using soap or hot water on the waxed area for at least 24 hours.

Tips for Waxing

Here are some tips to make your waxing experience less painful and more effective:

  • Exfoliate your skin a day before waxing to remove dead skin cells and make it easier to remove hair.
  • Trim your hair to about 1/4 inch before waxing so that it's easier to remove.
  • Avoid waxing during your period as your skin is more sensitive during this time.
  • Apply some numbing cream or pain relief gel before waxing to reduce the pain.
  • Avoid waxing over sunburnt, irritated, or broken skin.

FAQs

Does waxing make your hair grow back thicker?

No, waxing does NOT make your hair grow back thicker. This is a common myth that has been debunked by experts.

How often should you wax?

You should wax every 4-6 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows. Waxing too often can irritate the skin and cause ingrown hairs.

Is waxing painful?

Yes, waxing can be painful, especially if you're a beginner. However, the pain reduces with time as your skin gets used to the process.

Can you wax at home?

Yes, you can wax at home. However, if you're a beginner, it's recommended that you get it done by a professional first to avoid any accidents.

Hair Care After Waxing

After waxing, it's important to take care of your skin to avoid any irritation or ingrown hairs. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid hot showers or baths for at least 24 hours after waxing.
  • Avoid wearing tight clothes or underwear that can rub against the waxed area.
  • Avoid sun exposure for at least 24 hours after waxing.
  • Apply some aloe vera or moisturizer to soothe the skin.
  • Exfoliate the waxed area gently after a few days to prevent ingrown hairs.

In conclusion, waxing does NOT make your hair grow back thicker. It's a safe and effective method of hair removal that can leave your skin smooth and hair-free for weeks. Just make sure that you follow the right technique and take care of your skin after waxing. Happy waxing!
