Top Laziest Dog Breeds

Written by Frank Sep 11, 2023 ¬∑ 6 min read

If you're looking for a furry companion who loves to sleep and lounge around, then a lazy dog breed might be the perfect match for you. In this article, we'll be discussing the top laziest dog breeds that are known for their laid-back personalities and low energy levels. So, grab a cup of coffee, sit back, and learn more about these adorable couch potatoes!

Bulldog

The Bulldog is a popular breed known for its wrinkly face, stocky build, and laid-back personality. They are often called "lazy" because of their tendency to sleep for hours on end. Bulldogs are also great apartment dogs because they don't need a lot of exercise and are content with just lounging around.

Hair Care Tips:

Bulldogs have short coats that require minimal grooming. They shed moderately, so brushing them once a week will help keep their coat healthy and shiny. Bathing them once a month is also enough to keep them clean.

Basset Hound

The Basset Hound is a scent hound with short legs and long ears. They are known for their love of food and their laid-back personalities. Basset Hounds are great family dogs because they are gentle and affectionate with children. They also don't require a lot of exercise, making them perfect for apartment living.

Hair Care Tips:

Basset Hounds have short, dense coats that shed moderately. Brushing them once a week will help keep their coat healthy and shiny. They only need to be bathed every few months.

Pug

Pugs are a small breed known for their wrinkly faces and charming personalities. They are often called "lap dogs" because they love to cuddle and snuggle with their owners. Pugs are also great with children and are easy to train. They don't require a lot of exercise, making them perfect for apartment living.

Hair Care Tips:

Pugs have short, sleek coats that shed moderately. Brushing them once a week will help keep their coat healthy and shiny. They only need to be bathed every few months.

Chow Chow

The Chow Chow is a fluffy breed known for its thick coat and blue-black tongue. They are independent and aloof, but also affectionate with their owners. Chow Chows don't require a lot of exercise and are content with just lounging around the house.

Hair Care Tips:

Chow Chows have a thick double coat that requires regular brushing to prevent matting and tangling. They shed heavily twice a year, so extra brushing during those times is necessary. They only need to be bathed every few months.

Bernese Mountain Dog

The Bernese Mountain Dog is a large breed known for its friendly and gentle nature. They are great family dogs and are especially good with children. Bernese Mountain Dogs don't require a lot of exercise, but they do need regular walks to keep them healthy and happy.

Hair Care Tips:

Bernese Mountain Dogs have a thick, long coat that requires daily brushing to prevent matting and tangling. They shed heavily twice a year, so extra brushing during those times is necessary. They only need to be bathed every few months.

Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

The Cavalier King Charles Spaniel is a small breed known for its affectionate and playful personality. They are great family dogs and are especially good with children. Cavalier King Charles Spaniels don't require a lot of exercise and are content with just lounging around the house.

Hair Care Tips:

Cavalier King Charles Spaniels have a long, silky coat that requires regular brushing to prevent matting and tangling. They also need to be bathed once a month to keep their coat clean and healthy.

Greyhound

The Greyhound is a large breed known for its speed and athleticism. However, they are also known for their love of lounging around and sleeping. Greyhounds are great apartment dogs because they don't require a lot of exercise and are content with just a few short walks a day.

Hair Care Tips:

Greyhounds have short, sleek coats that require minimal grooming. They shed moderately, so brushing them once a week will help keep their coat healthy and shiny. They only need to be bathed every few months.

Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small breed known for its long, silky coat and charming personality. They are great family dogs and are especially good with children. Shih Tzus don't require a lot of exercise and are content with just lounging around the house.

Hair Care Tips:

Shih Tzus have a long, silky coat that requires daily brushing to prevent matting and tangling. They also need to be bathed once a month to keep their coat clean and healthy.

English Mastiff

The English Mastiff is a giant breed known for its size and gentle nature. They are great family dogs and are especially good with children. English Mastiffs don't require a lot of exercise and are content with just lounging around the house.

Hair Care Tips:

English Mastiffs have a short, dense coat that requires minimal grooming. They shed moderately, so brushing them once a week will help keep their coat healthy and shiny. They only need to be bathed every few months.

Great Dane

The Great Dane is a giant breed known for its size and affectionate nature. They are great family dogs and are especially good with children. Great Danes don't require a lot of exercise and are content with just a few short walks a day.

Hair Care Tips:

Great Danes have a short, sleek coat that requires minimal grooming. They shed moderately, so brushing them once a week will help keep their coat healthy and shiny. They only need to be bathed every few months.

FAQ

Q: Are lazy dogs good for apartment living?

A: Yes, lazy dogs are great for apartment living because they don't require a lot of exercise and are content with just lounging around the house.

Q: Do lazy dogs shed a lot?

A: It depends on the breed. Some lazy dogs, like Bulldogs and Greyhounds, have short coats that shed moderately. Other breeds, like Bernese Mountain Dogs and Chow Chows, have thick coats that shed heavily twice a year.

Q: Do lazy dogs make good family pets?

A: Yes, lazy dogs make great family pets because they are usually gentle and affectionate with children.

Q: Do lazy dogs need to be exercised?

A: Yes, all dogs need some form of exercise to stay healthy and happy. However, lazy dogs don't require as much exercise as more active breeds.
