How to Shed Hair Less

Before we dive into the tips and tricks, let's understand why hair shedding happens in the first place. Hair shedding is a natural process where old hair falls out and new hair grows in its place. On average, we shed about 50-100 hairs per day. However, if you are shedding more than that, it could be due to various reasons such as:

  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Nutritional deficiencies
  • Stress
  • Medications
  • Tight hairstyles
  • Heat styling
  • Chemical treatments

Now that we know the reasons for excessive hair shedding let's move on to how to shed hair less.

1. Keep Your Scalp Clean and Healthy

A healthy scalp is essential for healthy hair growth. Use a mild shampoo to keep your scalp clean and free from dirt and oil. Avoid using hot water as it can strip your scalp of its natural oils. Also, avoid using too many hair products as they can clog your hair follicles and lead to hair fall.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure to include foods rich in protein, iron, zinc, and vitamins A, C, and E. These nutrients help strengthen your hair and prevent hair fall.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and buns can lead to hair fall. These hairstyles pull your hair tightly, leading to hair breakage and thinning. Opt for loose hairstyles that do not pull on your hair.

4. Limit Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and hair dryers can damage your hair and lead to hair fall. Limit your use of these tools and use a heat protectant spray before styling your hair.

5. Avoid Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments such as coloring, straightening, and perming can damage your hair and lead to hair fall. Avoid these treatments as much as possible, and if you must get them done, make sure to go to a professional hair stylist.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to shed hair less:

  1. Clean your scalp regularly with a mild shampoo.
  2. Eat a balanced diet rich in nutrients that promote healthy hair growth.
  3. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  4. Limit your use of heat styling tools.
  5. Avoid chemical treatments as much as possible.
  6. Consult a doctor if you suspect an underlying medical condition.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you shed hair less:

  • Massage your scalp regularly to improve blood circulation.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

FAQ

1. Is hair shedding normal?

Yes, hair shedding is a natural process. On average, we shed about 50-100 hairs per day.

2. What causes excessive hair shedding?

Excessive hair shedding can be caused by various factors such as hormonal imbalances, nutritional deficiencies, stress, medications, tight hairstyles, heat styling, and chemical treatments.

3. Can I prevent hair shedding?

You can minimize hair shedding by following a healthy hair care routine, eating a balanced diet, avoiding tight hairstyles, limiting heat styling, and avoiding chemical treatments as much as possible.

Hair Care

Here are some general hair care tips that can help promote healthy hair growth:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Condition your hair after every wash.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair.
  • Avoid using too many hair products.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to too much sunlight.

By following these tips and tricks, you can shed hair less and promote healthy hair growth. Remember, healthy hair starts with a healthy scalp, so make sure to take care of both.
