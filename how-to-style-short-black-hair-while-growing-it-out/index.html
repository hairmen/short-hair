How To Style Short Black Hair While Growing It Out

Written by Luffy Aug 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Short hair can be a fun and refreshing change, but growing it out can be a bit of a challenge. When it comes to styling short black hair while growing it out, there are a few things you need to keep in mind. In this post, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you style your hair while it's growing out.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Short Black Hair While Growing It Out
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Style Short Black Hair While Growing It Out

When growing out short black hair, it's important to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. This will help prevent breakage and damage as your hair grows. Here are a few styling tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat tools as much as possible to prevent damage
  • Try protective styles, such as braids or twists, to give your hair a break from styling
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently

When it comes to styling your hair, there are a few options that work well for short black hair that's growing out:

1. Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a great style for short hair that's growing out. To create this style, start with clean, moisturized hair. Divide your hair into small sections and twist each section tightly. Wrap the twisted section around itself to create a knot, and secure it in place with a bobby pin. Repeat this process until all of your hair is in knots. Leave the knots in overnight, and then unravel them in the morning for a curly, voluminous look.

2. Twist-Outs

Twist-outs are another great option for short black hair that's growing out. To create this style, divide your hair into small sections and twist each section tightly. Leave the twists in overnight, and then unravel them in the morning. Use your fingers to separate the twists and create a voluminous, curly look.

3. Head Wraps

If you're having a bad hair day, or if you just want to switch up your look, head wraps are a fun and stylish option. There are many different ways to wrap your hair, so experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to creating Bantu knots:

  1. Start with clean, moisturized hair
  2. Divide your hair into small sections
  3. Twist each section tightly
  4. Wrap the twisted section around itself to create a knot
  5. Secure the knot in place with a bobby pin
  6. Repeat this process until all of your hair is in knots
  7. Leave the knots in overnight
  8. Unravel the knots in the morning for a curly, voluminous look

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you style short black hair while growing it out:

  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you
  • Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to prevent breakage and damage
  • Invest in a good leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and your personal preferences. Some people prefer to wash their hair once a week, while others wash their hair every few days. Experiment with different washing schedules to find what works best for you.

Q: Can I use heat tools on my hair?

A: While it's best to avoid heat tools as much as possible, you can use them occasionally if you take the proper precautions. Always use a heat protectant spray, and avoid using the highest heat settings.

Hair Care

When growing out short black hair, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent breakage and damage. Here are a few tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat tools as much as possible
  • Try protective styles to give your hair a break from styling
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently
  • Invest in a good leave-in conditioner
  • Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol

By following these tips and tricks, you can style your short black hair while it's growing out and keep it healthy and moisturized. Experiment with different styles and have fun with your hair!
