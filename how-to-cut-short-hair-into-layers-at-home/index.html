How To Cut Short Hair Into Layers At Home

Written by Oliver Oct 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're tired of your short hair looking flat and lifeless, adding layers is a great way to give it some volume and movement. But going to a salon for a haircut can be expensive and time-consuming. Luckily, cutting short hair into layers at home is easier than you might think. In this article, we'll go over the steps you need to take to achieve a layered haircut that looks like it was done by a professional.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Short Hair into Layers

How to Cut Short Hair into Layers

Before we get started, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off your hair. If you have curly hair, it's best to cut your hair when it's dry so you can see how the curls will fall. If you have straight hair, you can cut it when it's wet or dry.

When cutting short hair into layers, it's essential to have a plan in mind. Think about how many layers you want and where you want them to fall. Layers can be cut at different lengths, so consider how short you want your hair to be overall.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your short hair into layers:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section.
  3. Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold it between your index and middle fingers, then cut the ends at an angle. This will create a soft, layered effect. Repeat until you've cut all the back sections.
  4. Move on to the left and right sections. Comb each section straight up and cut the ends at an angle. Make sure to match the length and angle of the back sections.
  5. Finally, move on to the top section. This is the trickiest part, as you want to create layers without making your hair too short. Comb the hair straight up and cut the ends at an angle, but be conservative with your cuts. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  6. Once you've cut all the sections, use your fingers to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless, natural look.

Tips for Cutting Short Hair into Layers

Here are some tips to keep in mind as you cut your short hair into layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can create uneven cuts and damage your hair.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to take a break if you need one.
  • If in doubt, go to a professional. If you're not confident in your ability to cut your hair into layers, it's better to go to a salon and get it done by a professional.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short hair into layers:

Can I cut my short hair into layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can. It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry so you can see how the curls will fall.

How many layers should I have?

This depends on your personal preference and the length of your hair. Short hair typically looks best with two to three layers.

Can I cut my hair into layers if it's already layered?

Yes, you can. Just make sure to match the length and angle of the existing layers.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Hair into Layers

After cutting your short hair into layers, you'll want to take care of it properly to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a heat protectant if you plan to use heat tools on your hair.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layered haircut.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut your short hair into layers at home like a pro. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and have a plan in mind before you start cutting. Good luck!
