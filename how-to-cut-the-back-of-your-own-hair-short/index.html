How To Cut The Back Of Your Own Hair Short

Written by Aprilia Sep 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money and time at the hair salon? Do you want to learn how to cut the back of your own hair short? You have come to the right place! In this article, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to achieve a professional-looking short haircut in the comfort of your own home. Read on to learn more!

Description

Cutting the back of your own hair short can be an intimidating task, especially if you have never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a chic and stylish look without breaking the bank. This article is designed to help you feel confident and comfortable while cutting your own hair.

How to Cut the Back of Your Own Hair Short

Before we begin, it is important to note that you should always start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be difficult to cut, and it may not provide an accurate representation of the final result. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You will need a few essential tools for cutting your own hair, including:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A handheld mirror
  • A hair tie or clip

Make sure that your scissors are sharp and clean, as dull or dirty scissors can damage your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by parting your hair down the middle and then dividing it into two sections. Use the hair tie or clip to secure one section, so it does not interfere with your cutting process.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Begin cutting the bottom layer of your hair by taking a small section of hair at the nape of your neck. Comb the hair straight down, and then use the handheld mirror to check the length. Cut the hair straight across, using the previous length as a guide. Repeat this process, working your way up the back of your head.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut the bottom layer, blend the layers together by taking small sections of hair from the top layer and cutting them to match the length of the bottom layer. Use the mirror to check the length and ensure that the layers blend seamlessly together.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Before you finish, check your work in the mirror to ensure that the hair is even and the layers are blended. Make any necessary adjustments until you are happy with the result.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind while cutting the back of your own hair short:

  • Work slowly and carefully, taking small sections at a time.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Check your work frequently to ensure that the length and layers are even.
  • If you are unsure about cutting your hair, start with a small section and work your way up.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting the back of your own hair short:

Can I cut my hair wet?

It is generally best to cut your hair when it is dry, as wet hair can be difficult to cut accurately.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and the length of your hair. Generally, it is recommended to cut your hair every 4 to 6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, do not panic. You can always make adjustments or seek professional help if necessary.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it is important to take good care of it to maintain a healthy and shiny appearance. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools, such as blow dryers or straighteners, too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends and breakage.

Cutting the back of your own hair short can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
