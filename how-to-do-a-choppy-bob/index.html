How To Do A Choppy Bob A Relaxed Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

In this year 2023, the choppy bob hairstyle is still in fashion. It's a versatile cut that can be customized to fit any face shape or hair texture. If you're looking to change up your look and try something new, a choppy bob might be the perfect option. In this article, we'll go over how to do a choppy bob, step by step, and give you some tips and tricks to make the process easier. We'll also answer some frequently asked questions and give you some hair care tips to keep your new cut looking fresh.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Do a Choppy Bob
  2. Step by Step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ

How to Do a Choppy Bob

The first step in doing a choppy bob is to decide on the length and style you want. A choppy bob can be anywhere from chin-length to shoulder-length, and can be straight or wavy. Once you have an idea of what you want, it's time to gather your tools.

Tools You'll Need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Thinning shears
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Hair dryer
  • Straightening or curling iron (optional)

Step by Step

Now that you have your tools, it's time to get started. Here's how to do a choppy bob:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it's damp. Use a spray bottle to mist your hair with water if it starts to dry out during the cutting process. Blow-dry your hair straight if you plan on keeping it straight, or use a curling iron or straightening iron to add waves or curls.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Part your hair down the middle or to one side, depending on your preference.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four equal parts, using hair clips to hold each section in place. Start by sectioning off the back of your head, then move to the sides and finally the front.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Start with the back section of your hair. Use your comb to separate a small section of hair, and then use your scissors to cut it at an angle. Repeat this process, working your way up to the crown of your head. Use your thinning shears to remove any excess bulk or weight.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up to the top. Use your thinning shears to remove any excess bulk or weight.

Step 6: Cut the Front

Finish with the front section of your hair. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up to the top. Use your thinning shears to remove any excess bulk or weight.

Step 7: Add Texture

Use your scissors to add texture to your hair by cutting small sections at different lengths. This will give your hair a choppy, edgy look.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Style your hair using a straightening or curling iron, if desired. Use a texturizing spray to add volume and hold to your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect choppy bob:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use your thinning shears to remove any excess bulk or weight.
  • Cut your hair at an angle to create a choppy, edgy look.
  • Add texture to your hair by cutting small sections at different lengths.
  • Use a texturizing spray to add volume and hold to your hair.

FAQ

Q: Will a choppy bob work for my hair type?

A: Yes! A choppy bob can be customized to work with any hair type, whether you have straight, wavy, or curly hair.

Q: How often do I need to get my choppy bob trimmed?

A: It's recommended that you get your choppy bob trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Q: Can I style my choppy bob differently every day?

A: Yes! A choppy bob is a versatile cut that can be styled in many different ways, from sleek and straight to tousled and wavy.

Hair Care

To keep your choppy bob looking fresh, here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it and cause split ends.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when styling your hair with heat tools.

Now that you know how to do a choppy bob, you can try this trendy hairstyle for yourself. With a little practice and some patience, you can achieve the perfect choppy bob that suits your face shape and hair texture. Remember to take care of your hair by using the right products and avoiding excessive heat, and your new cut will look great for weeks to come!
