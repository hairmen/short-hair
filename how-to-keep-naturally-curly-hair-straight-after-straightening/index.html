How To Keep Naturally Curly Hair Straight After Straightening

Written by Petter Sep 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours straightening your curly hair only to have it return to its natural state within a few hours? The struggle is real for those with naturally curly hair who want to keep it straight. But don't worry, there are ways to help your straightened hair stay smooth and sleek for longer periods of time. In this article, we'll go over how to keep naturally curly hair straight after straightening with easy steps and helpful tips.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Keep Naturally Curly Hair Straight After Straightening

The first step in keeping your naturally curly hair straight after straightening is to properly prepare your hair before applying heat. Use a heat protectant spray or serum on your hair before blow-drying and straightening to prevent heat damage and breakage.

Once you have straightened your hair, it's important to avoid any activities that could cause your hair to revert back to its natural state. This includes sweating, humidity, and touching or brushing your hair too much.

Another way to keep your hair straight is to use a hair oil or leave-in conditioner to help lock in moisture and keep your hair smooth and shiny. Avoid using heavy or greasy products that can weigh down your hair and make it look oily.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are some simple steps to follow to help keep your naturally curly hair straight after straightening:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair before blow-drying and straightening.
  3. Use a high-quality flat iron to straighten your hair in small sections.
  4. Avoid touching or brushing your hair too much after straightening to prevent frizz.
  5. Apply a hair oil or leave-in conditioner to keep your hair smooth and shiny.
  6. Avoid sweating or exposing your hair to humidity for at least 24 hours after straightening.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Straight

Here are some additional tips to help keep your naturally curly hair straight after straightening:

  • Avoid using heavy or greasy hair products that can weigh down your hair and make it look oily.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid tying your hair up in tight ponytails or buns, as this can cause kinks and waves to form.
  • Use a dry shampoo or texturizing spray to refresh your hair between washes.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment or other smoothing treatment to help keep your hair straight for longer periods of time.

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it's damaged or brittle?

A: It's not recommended to straighten hair that is already damaged or brittle, as this can cause further breakage and damage. Be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment to help repair and strengthen your hair before attempting to straighten it.

Q: How often should I straighten my curly hair?

A: It's best to avoid straightening your curly hair too often, as this can cause heat damage and breakage. Aim to straighten your hair no more than once or twice a week, and be sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum each time.

Hair Care for Naturally Curly Hair

Keeping your naturally curly hair healthy and strong is the key to achieving and maintaining straight hair. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or sulfates that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause heat damage and breakage.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.

By following these tips and using the right products, you can keep your naturally curly hair straight after straightening and achieve the sleek, smooth look you desire. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid any activities that could cause it to revert back to its natural state.
