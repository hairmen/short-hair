How To Cut Women s Short Hair Layered Haircut

Written by Frank Nov 11, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to switch up your look, a layered haircut can add dimension and movement to your short hair. But if you're not comfortable going to a salon or want to save money, you can try cutting your own hair at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut women's short hair layered haircut.

How to

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step by Step

1. Start with dry hair. Wet hair can shrink and make it difficult to accurately measure the length you want.

2. Section your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

3. Begin with the back section. Take a small section of hair, and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up.

4. Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle, using the previous section as a guide.

5. Move on to the side sections, following the same process.

6. Finally, cut the top section, holding the hair straight up and cutting at an angle.

7. Once you're done cutting, remove the hair clips and style your hair as desired.

Tips

- Take your time and cut small sections at a time.

- Use a mirror to check the back of your head.

- If you're unsure about cutting, start with a longer length and gradually cut more until you achieve the desired look.

FAQ

Q: How do I know if a layered haircut will suit me?

A: Layered haircuts can suit most face shapes and hair types. However, it's best to consult with a hairstylist if you're unsure.

Q: What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting?

A: Don't panic! Mistakes can be fixed by cutting a little more hair off or blending the hair with scissors. If the mistake is significant, it's best to seek the help of a professional stylist.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain a healthy and shiny appearance. You can use hair oils or serums to add moisture and prevent split ends. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair in the long run. Regular trims every 6-8 weeks can also help keep your layered haircut looking fresh.

With these tips and steps, you can confidently cut your own women's short hair layered haircut at home. Experiment with different angles and lengths to find the perfect look for you.
