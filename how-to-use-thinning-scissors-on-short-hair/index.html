How to Use Thinning Scissors on Short Hair

Thinning scissors are a type of hair cutting scissors that have teeth or notches on one blade. These teeth help to remove bulk and create texture in the hair. Here's how to use them on short hair:

1. Prepare the Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure the hair is clean and dry. Comb it straight and remove any tangles. If you're working with wet hair, make sure it's not too wet - it should be damp but not dripping.

2. Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

3. Hold the Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinning scissors with the teeth or notches facing up. This will ensure that the hair is being cut evenly and prevent any uneven or choppy sections.

4. Start Cutting

Starting at the top of the section, hold the hair between your fingers and slide the scissors down the hair shaft, opening and closing them as you go. This will remove some of the hair and create texture. Continue doing this until you've thinned out the whole section.

5. Check Your Progress

After you've thinned out a section, let it fall naturally and check your progress. Make sure that the hair still looks even and that you haven't created any choppy or uneven sections. If you need to, go back and thin out any areas that need it.

6. Repeat on Other Sections

Repeat the process on the other sections of hair until you've thinned out the entire head. Make sure that you're working in small sections and taking your time so that you don't miss any areas.

Step by Step

Here's a step by step guide to using thinning scissors on short hair:

  1. Prepare the hair
  2. Section the hair
  3. Hold the scissors correctly
  4. Start cutting
  5. Check your progress
  6. Repeat on other sections

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors on short hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always go back and thin out more if you need to.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on already thin or fine hair. This can make the hair look even thinner and more sparse.
  • Use a comb to help guide the scissors and ensure that you're cutting the hair evenly.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. This can create choppy or uneven sections.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning scissors on short hair:

Can you use thinning scissors on all types of hair?

Thinning scissors are best for thick or coarse hair. If you have thin or fine hair, using thinning scissors can make it look even thinner or more sparse.

How often should you use thinning scissors on short hair?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how you want it to look. Generally, you can use thinning scissors every 4-6 weeks to maintain your desired look.

Hair Care

After using thinning scissors on your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. This can damage the hair and make it look dry and brittle.
  • Get regular trims to keep the hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh the hair down and make it look greasy or flat.

Now that you know how to use thinning scissors on short hair, you can create a variety of textures and styles. Just remember to take your time and work in small sections to ensure that you get the look you want.
