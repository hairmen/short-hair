How To Know If A Fringe Will Look Good On You

Written by Robby Aug 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're thinking about getting a fringe or bangs, you may be wondering if it will suit you. A fringe can transform your look and give you a fresh new style, but it's important to know if it will flatter your face shape and hair type. In this article, we'll show you how to know if a fringe will look good on you and provide tips for maintenance and care.

Table of Content

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Considering Your Hair Type
  • Evaluating Your Forehead Size
  • Framing Your Face Features
  • Choosing the Right Length

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in knowing if a fringe will suit you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart-shaped, diamond, and oblong. Knowing your face shape will help you choose a fringe that complements your features.

If you have an oval face shape, you're in luck! Most fringes suit this face shape. If you have a round face, go for a side-swept fringe that's longer on the sides. For a square face, choose a soft, wispy fringe that's longer in the middle. If you have a heart-shaped face, opt for a wispy, layered fringe that's shorter in the middle. Diamond-shaped faces look great with a blunt, straight fringe. If you have an oblong face, go for a heavy, full fringe that cuts across the forehead.

Considering Your Hair Type

Your hair type is another important factor to consider when choosing a fringe. If you have fine hair, a wispy, layered fringe will add volume and texture. If you have thick hair, a blunt, heavy fringe will look great. If you have curly hair, a side-swept fringe will complement your curls.

Evaluating Your Forehead Size

Your forehead size is also something to consider. If you have a smaller forehead, go for a wispy, layered fringe that's shorter in the middle. If you have a larger forehead, a blunt, straight fringe will look great.

Framing Your Face Features

A fringe can also frame your face features. If you have a strong jawline, a side-swept fringe will soften it. If you have high cheekbones, a blunt, straight fringe will draw attention to them. If you have a small chin, a heavy, full fringe will balance out your features.

Choosing the Right Length

The length of your fringe is also important to consider. If you're getting a fringe for the first time, start with a longer fringe that can be styled in different ways. As you get used to it, you can gradually go shorter. If you're not sure about the length, ask your hairstylist for their opinion.

Maintenance and Care

A fringe requires regular maintenance and care to keep it looking great. Trim it every 4-6 weeks to keep it at the right length. Use a small amount of styling product to keep it in place. Wash it regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and blow dry it with a round brush to give it volume.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will a fringe suit my glasses?

Yes, a fringe can look great with glasses. Choose a fringe that complements your face shape and glasses style.

Can I style my fringe in different ways?

Yes, a fringe can be styled in different ways. Sweep it to the side, part it in the middle, or pin it back for a different look.

Can I get a fringe if I have curly hair?

Yes, a side-swept fringe can look great with curly hair. Make sure to choose a fringe length that complements your curls.

Can I cut my own fringe?

We recommend going to a professional hairstylist to get your fringe cut. They can give you the right length and shape that suits your face shape and hair type.

How do I know if a fringe is right for me?

By considering your face shape, hair type, forehead size, and face features, you can determine if a fringe will suit you. Consult with a hairstylist for their opinion and advice.

Conclusion

Getting a fringe can be a great way to update your look and add some personality to your style. By considering your face shape, hair type, forehead size, and face features, you can determine if a fringe will suit you. Remember to maintain and care for your fringe regularly to keep it looking great. Consult with a hairstylist for their opinion and advice, and have fun experimenting with different styles!
