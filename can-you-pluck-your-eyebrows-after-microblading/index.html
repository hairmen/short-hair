Can You Pluck Your Eyebrows After Microblading

Written by Aprilia Jul 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Microblading is a semi-permanent makeup technique that involves using a handheld tool to create small, hair-like strokes on the eyebrows. It's a popular choice for those who want to fill in sparse brows or create a more defined shape. But what happens if you need to pluck your eyebrows after microblading? In this article, we'll answer that question and provide some tips for taking care of your brows after the procedure.

  • Description
  • How To

Description

Microblading is a popular cosmetic procedure that involves tattooing small, hair-like strokes onto the eyebrows to create a more defined, natural-looking shape. The technique uses a handheld tool with tiny needles to deposit pigment into the skin. It's a semi-permanent procedure, which means that the results can last anywhere from 6 months to 2 years.

After microblading, it's important to take good care of your eyebrows to ensure that they heal properly and the pigment sets correctly. This includes avoiding certain activities that could cause the pigment to fade or become distorted, such as swimming or sun exposure. But what about plucking your eyebrows? Can you still do that after microblading?

How To

Yes, you can pluck your eyebrows after microblading, but it's important to be careful not to pluck any of the newly microbladed hairs. The microblading process creates tiny cuts in the skin, and plucking those hairs could disrupt the healing process and cause the pigment to fade or become distorted. It's best to wait until your eyebrows have fully healed (usually around 4-6 weeks) before plucking any stray hairs.

When you do pluck your eyebrows, be sure to use clean, sterile tweezers to avoid introducing any bacteria or infection into the area. It's also a good idea to avoid using any harsh chemicals or exfoliants on your eyebrows after microblading, as these can also disrupt the healing process and cause the pigment to fade.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for plucking your eyebrows after microblading:

  1. Wait until your eyebrows have fully healed (usually around 4-6 weeks).
  2. Use clean, sterile tweezers to pluck any stray hairs, being careful not to disturb the microbladed hairs.
  3. Avoid using any harsh chemicals or exfoliants on your eyebrows after plucking.
  4. Continue to take good care of your eyebrows by avoiding activities that could cause the pigment to fade or become distorted.

Tips

Here are some tips for taking care of your eyebrows after microblading:

  • Avoid swimming or sun exposure for at least 2 weeks after the procedure.
  • Avoid using any harsh chemicals or exfoliants on your eyebrows for at least 2 weeks after the procedure.
  • Avoid touching or scratching your eyebrows while they heal.
  • Apply a healing ointment or cream to your eyebrows as directed by your technician.
  • Avoid sweating excessively for at least 2 weeks after the procedure.

FAQ

How long does microblading last?

Microblading is a semi-permanent procedure, which means that the results can last anywhere from 6 months to 2 years.

Does microblading hurt?

Microblading can be uncomfortable, but most people find the procedure to be tolerable. Your technician will apply a numbing cream to the area to help minimize any pain or discomfort.

Can I wear makeup after microblading?

You should avoid wearing makeup on or around your eyebrows for at least 2 weeks after the procedure to allow your skin to fully heal.

Hair Care

In addition to taking care of your eyebrows after microblading, it's also important to take good care of your hair. This includes using gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, avoiding excessive heat styling, and getting regular trims to prevent split ends. Taking care of your hair can help it look and feel healthy and shiny.

Conclusion

Microblading is a great way to achieve fuller, more defined eyebrows, but it's important to take good care of them after the procedure. While you can pluck your eyebrows after microblading, it's important to be careful not to disturb the newly microbladed hairs. By following these tips and taking good care of your eyebrows and hair, you can maintain a beautiful, natural-looking appearance for years to come.
