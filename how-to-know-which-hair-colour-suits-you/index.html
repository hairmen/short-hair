How To Know Which Hair Colour Suits You

Written by Christine Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your current hair colour and wanting to change things up? Choosing a new hair colour can be exciting, but it can also be overwhelming. With so many options and factors to consider, it can be challenging to know which hair colour would suit you best. In this article, we will share helpful tips and steps to help you determine which hair colour is right for you.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step 2: Consider Your Eye Colour

  • Step 4: Take Inspiration from Your Favourite Celebrities

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step to finding the right hair colour is determining your skin tone. Your skin tone can be either warm, cool, or neutral. Warm skin tones have yellow or golden undertones, while cool skin tones have pink or blue undertones. Neutral skin tones have a mix of both warm and cool undertones.

If you have a warm skin tone, shades of red, copper, and golden blonde would look great on you. If you have a cool skin tone, ash blonde, platinum, and cool brown shades would complement your skin. Neutral skin tones can pull off both warm and cool tones.

Step 2: Consider Your Eye Colour

Your eye colour can also play a role in determining which hair colour suits you best. If you have warm-toned eyes like brown or hazel, warm hair colours like caramel and honey blonde would look great on you. If you have cool-toned eyes like blue or green, cool shades like ash brown and platinum blonde would complement your eyes.

Step 3: Think About Your Natural Hair Colour

If you're not looking for a drastic change, consider your natural hair colour when choosing a new shade. Staying within a few shades of your natural hair colour can make the transition easier and look more natural.

Step 4: Take Inspiration from Your Favourite Celebrities

Looking at your favourite celebrities who have similar skin tones and eye colours to you can give you inspiration for your new hair colour. You can also consult with a professional hairstylist who can recommend the best hair colours for your skin tone and eye colour.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour

  • Consider your lifestyle and how much maintenance the new hair colour will require.
  • Try on wigs or use virtual try-on tools to see how the new hair colour will look on you.
  • Start with subtle changes and work your way up to more dramatic colours.
  • Consider the season and how the new hair colour will look in different lighting.

FAQs

Can I dye my hair a lighter colour without bleaching it?

If you have dark hair, it's unlikely that you can dye your hair a lighter colour without bleaching it. Bleaching is necessary to lift the natural pigment in your hair before applying a lighter colour.

How often should I touch up my roots?

It depends on the rate at which your hair grows and the contrast between your natural hair colour and the dyed hair colour. Generally, roots should be touched up every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care after Colouring

After colouring your hair, it's essential to take care of your locks to maintain the vibrancy of the new hair colour. Here are some tips:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid hot tools and excessive sun exposure, as they can cause the colour to fade.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and shiny.

Choosing the right hair colour can be a fun and exciting process. By considering your skin tone, eye colour, natural hair colour, and taking inspiration from celebrities, you can find the perfect shade for you. Remember to take care of your hair after colouring to maintain the vibrancy of your new hair colour.
