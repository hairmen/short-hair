How to Cut My Own Hair Shoulder Length | Step-by-Step Guide

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle, cutting your own hair can be a great option. Not only does it save you time and money, but it also gives you the freedom to experiment with different looks. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut your own hair shoulder length.

Description

Cutting your own hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily. Shoulder length hair is a popular choice as it is versatile and easy to style. Whether you want a blunt cut or layers, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into two parts, using a comb to create a center part from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, divide your hair into two sections, securing each with hair clips.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with one section of hair, remove the hair clip and comb your hair straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle finger and cut the hair at a slight angle, following the length you want to achieve. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 4: Check for Balance

After cutting both sides, check for balance by bringing your hair to the front and looking in the mirror. Adjust any uneven sections by cutting small amounts at a time.

Step 5: Add Layers (Optional)

If you want to add layers, section your hair into smaller parts and cut at an angle. Start from the bottom and work your way up to the top, making sure to blend the layers in with the rest of your hair.

Step 6: Finish with Texturizing

To add texture, use your scissors to cut into the ends of your hair, creating small snips. This will give your hair a softer, more natural look.

Tips

- Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length.

- Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid split ends.

- Cut your hair when it's dry to avoid any shrinkage.

- Don't cut too much at once, as it can be difficult to fix if you make a mistake.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my hair wet?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be misleading and may result in a shorter cut than intended.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot tools too often. Regular trims will also help to prevent split ends and breakage.
