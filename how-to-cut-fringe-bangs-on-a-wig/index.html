How To Cut Fringe Bangs On A Wig A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old wig style? Want to switch things up and add some fringe bangs to your look? Cutting fringe bangs on a wig can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily and quickly. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of cutting fringe bangs on a wig and provide some tips and hair care advice to keep your new do looking fabulous.

How to Cut Fringe Bangs on a Wig

Before you begin, you’ll need a few essential tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A wig stand or mannequin head
  • A comb or brush
  • Hairspray or styling product (optional)

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prepare Your Wig

First, you’ll want to make sure your wig is dry and free of tangles. Place it on a wig stand or mannequin head and use a comb or brush to smooth out any knots or kinks. If your wig is synthetic, be sure to use a brush specifically designed for synthetic fibers to avoid damaging the hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to separate the section of hair you want to cut into fringe bangs. This section should be about 2-3 inches wide and should start above your eyebrows. Use hair clips to secure the rest of your wig out of the way.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Starting at the center of your sectioned hair, use your scissors to make small, vertical cuts into the hair. These cuts should be about 1/4 inch apart and should not go all the way through the hair. This will create a textured, layered look to your fringe bangs. Continue making vertical cuts across the entire section of hair until you’ve achieved your desired length.

Step 4: Blend Your Bangs

Once you’ve cut your fringe bangs, use your scissors to blend the edges into the rest of your wig. This will create a more natural look and prevent any harsh lines from showing. Use small, horizontal cuts to blend the edges of your bangs into the surrounding hair.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Finally, use a comb or brush to style your new fringe bangs. You can use hairspray or styling product to hold your bangs in place or create a specific look. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Tips for Cutting Fringe Bangs on a Wig

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting fringe bangs on a wig:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair and creating uneven cuts.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting fringe bangs can be a bit tricky, but with practice, you’ll become more confident and skilled.
  • Consider the shape of your face when deciding on the length and style of your bangs. Consult with a hairstylist or do some research online for inspiration.

FAQs

Can I cut fringe bangs on a curly wig?

Yes, you can cut fringe bangs on a curly wig. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when cut, so you may want to cut your bangs slightly longer than you would on straight hair.

How often should I trim my fringe bangs?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you wear your wig. As a general rule, you should trim your fringe bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and maintained.

Hair Care for Your New Fringe Bangs

To keep your new fringe bangs looking their best, be sure to take care of your wig properly. This means storing it on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use, using a wig brush or comb to prevent tangles, and washing it regularly with a wig shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using regular hair products on your wig, as they can damage the synthetic fibers.

Cutting fringe bangs on a wig can be a fun and easy way to switch up your style. With these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to create a fabulous new look in no time!
