What Is The Best Bleach For Blonde Hair

Written by Eveline Aug 28, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re looking to lighten your blonde hair, you may be wondering what the best bleach is for your locks. With so many options on the market, it can be tough to know which one to choose. In this article, we’ll explore the different types of bleach available and provide some tips for achieving the perfect blonde shade.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Choose the Best Bleach for Blonde Hair
  3. Step by Step Guide
  4. Tips for Using Bleach on Blonde Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care after Bleaching

Description

Bleach is a powerful chemical that can damage hair if not used correctly. It’s important to choose a bleach that is specifically designed for blonde hair, as this will help to minimize damage and ensure the best results. The best bleach for blonde hair will be gentle on your locks while still providing the lightening power you need to achieve your desired shade.

How to Choose the Best Bleach for Blonde Hair

When choosing a bleach for your blonde hair, consider the following factors:

  • Type of Bleach: There are two main types of bleach: powder and cream. Powder bleach is more powerful and can lift hair up to 8 levels, while cream bleach is more gentle and can lift hair up to 5 levels.
  • Strength: Bleach comes in different strengths, usually measured in volumes. 20 volume bleach is the most common and is suitable for most hair types. 30 or 40 volume bleach is more powerful and should only be used by experienced professionals.
  • Brand: Choose a reputable brand of bleach that is specifically designed for blonde hair. This will ensure that the product is safe and effective.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide for using bleach on blonde hair:

  1. Choose the right bleach for your hair type and desired shade.
  2. Section your hair and apply the bleach evenly, starting from the roots and working towards the ends.
  3. Leave the bleach on for the recommended amount of time, checking your hair regularly to ensure it doesn’t over-process.
  4. Rinse the bleach out thoroughly with warm water.
  5. Apply a toner if necessary to achieve your desired shade.
  6. Rinse your hair again and apply a deep conditioning treatment to help repair any damage caused by the bleach.

Tips for Using Bleach on Blonde Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when using bleach on blonde hair:

  • Always follow the instructions on the bleach carefully, and never leave it on for longer than recommended.
  • Avoid using bleach on hair that is already damaged or over-processed.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment after bleaching to help repair any damage and keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Consider visiting a professional stylist for the best results, especially if you’re looking to make a significant change to your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using bleach on blonde hair:

Can bleach damage my hair?

Yes, bleach is a powerful chemical that can damage your hair if not used correctly. It’s important to choose a bleach that is specifically designed for blonde hair and to follow the instructions carefully.

How long should I leave bleach on my hair?

The length of time you should leave bleach on your hair will depend on the type of bleach and the strength of the product. Follow the instructions carefully and check your hair regularly to ensure it doesn’t over-process.

Can I use bleach on dyed blonde hair?

Yes, you can use bleach on dyed blonde hair. However, it’s important to be aware that bleach can cause damage to already-processed hair, so it’s important to take extra care and follow the instructions carefully.

Hair Care after Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it’s important to take extra care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as these can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to help repair any damage caused by the bleach.
  • Avoid chlorine and salt water, as these can cause further damage to already-processed hair.

By following these tips and choosing the right bleach for your hair type, you can achieve the perfect shade of blonde without damaging your locks. Remember to always follow the instructions carefully and take extra care to keep your hair healthy after bleaching.
