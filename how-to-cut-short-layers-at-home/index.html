How To Cut Short Layers At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Oct 19, 2023 · 4 min read

Short layered haircuts are a popular choice for those looking to add volume and texture to their hair. And while going to a professional stylist is always an option, it's also possible to achieve a great layered haircut at home. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut short layers at home and provide tips for maintaining your new haircut.

How to Cut Short Layers at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a comb, and clips to keep your hair in place. It's also a good idea to use a mirror or two so you can see the back of your head while cutting.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair before you begin cutting. This will make it easier to see the natural texture and shape of your hair.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Using a comb, part your hair down the middle or to one side. This will help you create even layers on both sides of your head.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using clips, section off your hair into manageable sections. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, comb your hair straight down and trim the ends. This will create a solid base for your layers.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Next, release the middle section of hair and comb it straight down. Use the bottom layer as a guide and cut the hair to the same length.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, release the top layer of hair and comb it straight down. Use the middle layer as a guide and cut the hair to the same length. This will create the layered effect.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, it's time to blend them together. Use your scissors to make small, vertical cuts into the hair to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 8: Check for Evenness

After you've finished cutting, check for evenness by running your fingers through your hair. If you notice any uneven spots, trim them until your hair is even.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers at Home

Here are a few tips to help you achieve a great layered haircut at home:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always trim more hair, but you can't undo a bad cut.
  • Use a comb to hold your hair in place while cutting. This will help you create even layers.
  • Don't try to cut too much hair at once. It's better to make small, precise cuts.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so give yourself time to rest and recharge.

FAQ About Cutting Short Layers at Home

Can I cut short layers with regular scissors?

No, it's best to use haircutting scissors to achieve a clean, even cut.

Do I need to wet my hair before cutting?

No, it's best to cut dry hair so you can see the natural texture and shape.

How often should I cut my short layers?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly the layers lose their shape. As a general rule, you should aim to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Layers at Home

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your layers.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny.

Overall, cutting short layers at home can be a great way to save money and achieve a great haircut. With the right tools and technique, you can create a layered look that suits your face shape and personal style.
