How To Cut Your Hair Into A Choppy Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and want a low-maintenance yet stylish look, a choppy bob might be just what you need. This trendy haircut is perfect for those who want to add some edge to their hair without going too drastic. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your hair into a choppy bob in a few easy steps.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Cut Your Hair into a Choppy Bob

Before we begin, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a picture of the choppy bob you want as a reference.

Step by Step

1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut.

2. Divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to separate the top section from the bottom section.

3. Take a small section of hair from the back and cut it at an angle. This will create a choppy effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle until you've finished the back section.

4. Move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair and cut it at an angle, following the line of your cheekbone. Repeat on the other side.

5. Release the top section of your hair and cut it at an angle, starting from the crown of your head. Make sure to cut the hair slightly shorter towards the back.

6. Blend the layers together by cutting small sections of hair at an angle throughout your head. Make sure to keep checking the length and texture to ensure an even cut.

7. Once you're happy with the length, use a razor to create more texture and volume. Run the razor through the ends of your hair, making sure to not overdo it.

8. Style your hair as desired. A choppy bob looks great when styled with loose waves or messy curls. Use a texturizing spray to add more definition and hold.

Tips

  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut small sections at a time to avoid mistakes.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.
  • It's okay to make mistakes. Hair grows back, and you can always go to a professional if you're not happy with the results.

FAQ

Q: How long does a choppy bob last?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you take care of it. On average, a choppy bob can last between 6-8 weeks before needing a touch-up.

Q: Can I cut a choppy bob on curly hair?

A: Yes, a choppy bob looks great on curly hair. Just make sure to cut it when it's dry to see the natural texture of your hair.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your hair into a choppy bob, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using heat-styling tools too often. Regular trims will also help maintain the shape of your choppy bob.

So there you have it, a step by step guide on how to cut your hair into a choppy bob. Whether you're looking for a new hairstyle or just want to switch things up, a choppy bob is a great choice for anyone who wants a stylish yet low-maintenance look.
