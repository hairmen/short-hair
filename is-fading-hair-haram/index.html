Is Fading Hair Haram A Complete Guide In 2023

Are you wondering if fading hair is haram? There has been a lot of debate on this topic in recent years, especially in the Muslim community. In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about fading hair and whether it is considered haram or not.

Description

Fading hair is a popular hairstyle where the hair is gradually cut shorter towards the neck. This style has become increasingly popular in recent years and is commonly seen in the fashion industry. However, there has been a lot of debate on whether fading hair is considered haram in Islam.

In Islam, there are certain guidelines for hairstyles. Men are encouraged to keep their hair short and neat, while women are encouraged to cover their hair. However, there is no specific rule on fading hair. Some scholars believe that it is haram because it involves changing the natural state of hair, while others argue that it is permissible as long as it is not excessive and does not resemble the hairstyles of non-Muslims.

How to Fade Hair

Before we dive into the haram aspect of fading hair, let's take a look at how to achieve this hairstyle. Fading hair can be done at home with the right tools and technique. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Gather the Tools

You will need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Make sure the clippers come with different guard sizes to achieve different lengths.

Step 2: Choose the Length

Decide how short you want your hair to be. Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually. This will help you achieve the fading effect.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin with the longest guard size and cut the hair around the sides and back of your head. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly. Then, switch to a shorter guard size and repeat the process, gradually cutting the hair shorter towards the neck.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Use scissors to blend the longer hair with the shorter hair. This will help achieve a smoother transition between the lengths.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to fade hair:

  1. Gather the tools
  2. Wash and dry your hair
  3. Choose the length
  4. Start cutting with the longest guard size
  5. Repeat with a shorter guard size, gradually cutting the hair shorter towards the neck
  6. Blend the hair with scissors
  7. Repeat on the other side
  8. Trim the top of the hair
  9. Clean up the neckline and sideburns
  10. Style the hair as desired

Tips for Fading Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fading hair:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually
  • Use scissors to blend the hair for a smoother transition
  • Trim the top of the hair to achieve a more polished look
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head
  • Clean up the neckline and sideburns for a cleaner look

FAQs

Is fading hair haram in Islam?

There is no clear consensus on whether fading hair is haram in Islam. Some scholars believe that it is permissible as long as it is not excessive and does not resemble the hairstyles of non-Muslims, while others believe that it is haram as it involves changing the natural state of hair.

Can women fade their hair?

Women are encouraged to cover their hair in Islam, so fading hair may not be appropriate for them. However, there is no clear rule on this and it may vary depending on the interpretation of different scholars.

How often should I fade my hair?

This will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the fading effect. Generally, it is recommended to fade your hair every 2-3 weeks.

Hair Care After Fading

After fading your hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain a healthy look. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Trim the ends of your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Use hair products sparingly to avoid buildup

In conclusion, fading hair is a popular hairstyle that has become increasingly popular in recent years. While there is no clear consensus on whether it is haram in Islam, it is important to understand the guidelines for hairstyles in Islam and to make an informed decision. With the right tools and technique, fading hair can be achieved at home. Remember to take care of your hair after fading it to maintain a healthy look.
