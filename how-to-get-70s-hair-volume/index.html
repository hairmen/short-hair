How To Get 70S Hair Volume Relaxed English Language Guide

If you're looking to add some retro flair to your hairstyle, why not try out some 70s hair volume? This iconic look is all about big, bold hair that commands attention. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to achieve this look, including tips for hair care and maintenance. Let's get started!

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Get 70s Hair Volume

The key to achieving 70s hair volume is all in the styling. You'll need to create lots of lift and volume at the roots, while keeping the length of your hair relatively straight. Here's what you'll need:

  • Thickening spray or mousse
  • Blow dryer with diffuser attachment
  • Round brush
  • Hairspray

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to get 70s hair volume:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a thickening spray or mousse to the roots of your hair. Focus on the crown and top of your head.
  3. Flip your head upside down and blow dry your hair using a diffuser attachment. This will help to create lift and volume at the roots.
  4. Once your hair is about 80% dry, flip your head back up and use a round brush to smooth out the lengths of your hair. Roll the brush under at the ends to create a slight curl.
  5. Finish with a spritz of hairspray to help hold the volume.

Tips for Maintaining 70s Hair Volume

To keep your 70s hair volume looking its best, try these tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip away natural oils and make your hair limp. Instead, aim to wash every 2-3 days.
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to help absorb excess oil and add volume.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or styling products, as these can weigh down your hair and make it harder to achieve volume.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about 70s hair volume:

Can I achieve this look with shorter hair?

Yes! While longer hair can help to create more volume, you can still achieve this look with shorter hair. Just focus on creating lift at the roots and using a round brush to curl the ends.

Do I need to use a specific type of hairspray?

Not necessarily. Look for a hairspray that provides a strong hold without making your hair feel sticky or weighed down. Experiment with different products to find one that works for you.

Hair Care for 70s Hair Volume

Finally, here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it harder to maintain volume.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair and prevent dryness.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve 70s hair volume and keep it looking fabulous. Happy styling!
