How To Correct Bad Hair Coloring At Home A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Sep 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Bad hair coloring can happen to anyone. Whether it's a DIY disaster or a salon mishap, the result can be disastrous. But don't worry, there are ways to fix it at home without spending a fortune. In this article, we'll share our expert tips on how to correct bad hair coloring at home.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Correct Bad Hair Coloring at Home
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Keep in Mind
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Coloring

Description

Bad hair coloring can occur due to various reasons, such as choosing the wrong color, improper application, or using low-quality products. Sometimes, the result is too dark, too light, or uneven color. In some cases, the color may turn out too brassy or orange. No matter the reason, the good news is that there are ways to correct it at home.

How to Correct Bad Hair Coloring at Home

The first step to correcting bad hair coloring is to assess the damage. Determine the problem, whether it's too dark, too light, or uneven color. If the color is too dark, you may need to lighten it, and if it's too light, you may need to darken it. Here are some easy ways to fix bad hair coloring at home:

Color-Remover

If you've colored your hair too dark, you can use a color-remover to lighten it. A color-remover is a product that removes the pigment from the hair without damaging it. Follow the instructions on the package, but be careful not to over-process your hair.

Bleach

If the color is too light, you can use bleach to darken it. Bleaching your hair can be damaging, so it's important to use a high-quality product and follow the instructions carefully.

Color Correction

If your hair has uneven color, you can use a color correction kit to even it out. A color correction kit is a product that contains different shades to neutralize the unwanted tones.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to correct bad hair coloring at home:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and prepare your hair for the correction process.

Step 2: Determine the Problem

Determine the problem with your hair color, and choose the appropriate correction method.

Step 3: Apply the Product

Apply the product according to the instructions on the package. Be careful not to over-process your hair, as this can cause damage.

Step 4: Rinse and Condition

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water, and apply a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture and shine.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some tips to keep in mind when correcting bad hair coloring at home:

  • Always perform a strand test before applying any color or bleach to your hair.
  • Use high-quality products to minimize damage to your hair.
  • Follow the instructions on the package carefully.
  • Don't over-process your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment after the correction process to restore moisture and shine to your hair.

FAQs

Can I correct bad hair coloring at home?

Yes, you can correct bad hair coloring at home. However, it's important to follow the instructions carefully and use high-quality products to minimize damage to your hair.

How long should I wait before correcting bad hair coloring?

You should wait at least two weeks before correcting bad hair coloring to allow your hair to recover from the previous color treatment.

Can I use box dye to correct bad hair coloring?

It's not recommended to use box dye to correct bad hair coloring, as it can cause further damage to your hair. It's best to use a color-remover, bleach, or color correction kit designed for this purpose.

Hair Care After Coloring

After correcting bad hair coloring, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the color and keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for color-treated hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Correcting bad hair coloring at home can be a daunting task, but with the right products and techniques, it's possible to achieve great results. Follow our guide, and remember to take care of your hair after coloring to maintain healthy, vibrant locks.
