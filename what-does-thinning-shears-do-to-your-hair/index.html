What Does Thinning Shears Do To Your Hair

Written by Bardi Nov 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning shears may sound intimidating to some people, but they’re actually a useful tool in creating a variety of hairstyles. This tool can help you achieve a desired look and texture by removing excess bulk from the hair. In this article, we’ll explain what thinning shears are, how they work, and how to use them on your hair.

What Are Thinning Shears?

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that are designed to remove some of the hair’s thickness. They have two blades, one with teeth that are set apart from the other blade. This allows for some of the hair to be cut while allowing some of it to remain.

How Do Thinning Shears Work?

Thinning shears work by cutting some of the hair while leaving the rest of it intact. The teeth on one of the blades help to remove some of the bulk from the hair, making it lighter and easier to manage. This creates a textured and layered look in the hair.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Your Hair

Thinning shears can be used in a variety of ways to achieve different looks. Here are some steps on how to use them on your hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the areas that need thinning.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use clips to section your hair into smaller sections. This will make it easier to thin out specific areas.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears in your dominant hand and the comb in the other. Make sure the teeth of the thinning shears are facing upwards towards the ceiling.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Use the thinning shears to cut some of the hair in the section you’re working on. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up towards the roots. Use the comb to help guide the thinning shears through the hair. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 5: Repeat

Continue thinning out different sections of your hair until you achieve the desired look.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Your Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on your hair:

- Always start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. - Use the comb to help guide the thinning shears through the hair. - Don’t cut too much hair at once. - Use the thinning shears to create texture and movement in the hair. - Be careful not to thin out too much hair in one area, as this can create a patchy look.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Thinning Shears Damage Your Hair?

Thinning shears can cause damage to your hair if they’re not used correctly. It’s important to use them in moderation and not to thin out too much hair at once.

Can You Use Thinning Shears on Any Hair Type?

Thinning shears can be used on most hair types, but they’re especially useful for thick or coarse hair.

How Often Should You Use Thinning Shears on Your Hair?

It’s recommended to use thinning shears on your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the desired look.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears

After using thinning shears on your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

- Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized. - Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage. - Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair gently. - Get regular trims to maintain the desired look.

Conclusion

Thinning shears can be a great tool in creating a variety of hairstyles. They work by removing excess bulk from the hair, creating a textured and layered look. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can achieve the desired look and maintain healthy hair.
