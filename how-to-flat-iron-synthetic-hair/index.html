How To Flat Iron Synthetic Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 08, 2023 · 5 min read

Flat ironing synthetic hair can be a daunting task for many, but with the right technique and tools, it can be achieved without any damage to your hair. In this article, we will guide you step-by-step on how to flat iron synthetic hair and provide you with some tips and tricks to make the process easier. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Flat Iron Synthetic Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Synthetic Hair

How to Flat Iron Synthetic Hair

Before you begin, it's important to know that synthetic hair cannot withstand high heat like natural hair. Therefore, you need to be careful with the temperature of your flat iron. Most synthetic hair can handle heat up to 350°F, but it's best to check the packaging or consult with a professional to be sure.

Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your synthetic hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly and pat dry with a towel. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start flat ironing.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to protect it from heat damage. This will also help your hair stay straight for longer.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to flat iron each section and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 4: Flat Iron Your Hair

Set your flat iron to the appropriate temperature for your synthetic hair. Slowly glide the flat iron through each section of your hair, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Repeat until all sections of your hair are straight.

Step 5: Apply Finishing Products

Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to your hair to add shine and reduce frizz. Style your hair as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you flat iron your synthetic hair:

Step 1: Choose the Right Flat Iron

Choose a flat iron with ceramic plates that heat evenly and won't damage your hair. Avoid flat irons with metal plates as they can get too hot and melt your synthetic hair.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Brush or comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to flat iron your hair and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to flat iron each section and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 4: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to protect it from heat damage. This will also help your hair stay straight for longer.

Step 5: Set Your Flat Iron to the Right Temperature

Set your flat iron to the appropriate temperature for your synthetic hair. Most synthetic hair can handle heat up to 350°F, but it's best to check the packaging or consult with a professional to be sure.

Step 6: Flat Iron Your Hair

Slowly glide the flat iron through each section of your hair, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Use a comb to help guide the flat iron through your hair if necessary. Repeat until all sections of your hair are straight.

Step 7: Apply Finishing Products

Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to your hair to add shine and reduce frizz. Style your hair as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you flat iron your synthetic hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before flat ironing.
  • Don't flat iron wet or damp hair as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Don't use high heat as this can melt your synthetic hair.
  • Divide your hair into small sections to ensure that you don't miss any areas.
  • Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to add shine and reduce frizz.

FAQs

Can you flat iron synthetic hair?

Yes, you can flat iron synthetic hair, but you need to be careful with the temperature of your flat iron. Most synthetic hair can handle heat up to 350°F, but it's best to check the packaging or consult with a professional to be sure.

Can you use a regular flat iron on synthetic hair?

You can use a regular flat iron on synthetic hair, but make sure it has ceramic plates that heat evenly and won't damage your hair. Avoid flat irons with metal plates as they can get too hot and melt your synthetic hair.

How often should you flat iron synthetic hair?

You should avoid flat ironing your synthetic hair too often as it can damage your hair. Flat iron your hair only when necessary and use a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from heat damage.

Hair Care for Synthetic Hair

Here are some tips to help you take care of your synthetic hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water as it can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Store your synthetic hair in a cool, dry place to prevent tangling and damage.

Flat ironing synthetic hair can be a daunting task, but with the right technique and tools, it can be achieved without any damage to your hair. Follow these steps, tips, and tricks to get perfectly straight hair every time!
