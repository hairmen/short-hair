How To Tie Your Short Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have short natural hair, you may think that styling options are limited. However, there are plenty of ways to tie your hair that are both practical and stylish. In this article, we'll go over how to tie your short natural hair step-by-step, as well as some tips, FAQ, and hair care advice.

  • How to Tie Your Short Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Tying Short Natural Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Short Natural Hair

How to Tie Your Short Natural Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's briefly go over some of the best ways to tie short natural hair:

  • Pineapple
  • Puff
  • Twists or braids
  • Bantu knots
  • Braid-out or twist-out

These styles work best with short hair because they don't require a lot of length or manipulation. They also help to protect your hair and retain moisture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let's walk through how to tie your short natural hair in each of these styles:

Pineapple:

The pineapple is a popular and easy way to tie short natural hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Flip your head over and gather all of your hair at the top of your head.
  2. Secure your hair with a scrunchie or hair tie.
  3. Fluff the hair at the top of your head to create a voluminous look.
  4. Smooth any frizz or flyaways with a little bit of oil or gel.

The pineapple is great for preserving your curls and preventing them from getting flattened while you sleep.

Puff:

The puff is another simple style that works well for short natural hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Brush your hair upward and gather it at the crown of your head.
  2. Secure your hair with a hair tie or headband.
  3. Fluff out the hair to create a full, rounded look.
  4. Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands or to shape the puff.

The puff is perfect for those days when you want to keep your hair out of your face but still look stylish.

Twists or Braids:

Twists or braids are a great option for short natural hair because they can add texture and dimension to your hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Part your hair into sections.
  2. Twist or braid each section of hair.
  3. Secure the ends of each twist or braid with a small hair tie or clip.
  4. Use bobby pins to tuck any loose strands or to create a different style.

Twists or braids can be worn as a protective style or as a way to add some interest to your hair.

Bantu Knots:

Bantu knots are a fun and edgy way to tie short natural hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Part your hair into small sections.
  2. Twist each section of hair until it's tightly coiled.
  3. Wrap the coil around itself to create a small knot.
  4. Secure the knot with a hair tie or bobby pin.
  5. Repeat the process until all of your hair is in knots.

Bantu knots can be worn as a protective style or as a way to create a unique and bold look.

Braid-out or Twist-out:

A braid-out or twist-out is a great way to create volume and texture in short natural hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Section your hair into small sections.
  3. Twist or braid each section of hair.
  4. Let your hair air dry or sit under a hooded dryer.
  5. Undo the twists or braids and separate the hair to create volume.

A braid-out or twist-out can be worn as a standalone style or as a way to prepare your hair for a more complex style.

Tips for Tying Short Natural Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when tying short natural hair:

  • Use a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep.
  • Don't tie your hair too tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Experiment with different styles and accessories to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about tying short natural hair:

Q: Can I tie my short natural hair in a ponytail?

A: Yes, you can tie your hair in a ponytail, but make sure not to tie it too tightly or too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Q: Can I tie my hair in a bun?

A: Yes, you can tie your hair in a bun, but make sure to use a hair tie or scrunchie that won't pull or tug on your hair.

Q: Can I tie my hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to avoid tying your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting before tying.

Hair Care Tips for Short Natural Hair

Here are some general hair care tips that can help keep your short natural hair healthy and strong:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Deep condition your hair weekly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, or use them sparingly and with a heat protectant.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote hair growth and overall health.

By following these tips and using the techniques outlined in this article, you can easily tie your short natural hair in a variety of ways that are both practical and stylish.
