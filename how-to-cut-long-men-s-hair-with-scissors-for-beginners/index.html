How To Cut Long Men s Hair With Scissors For Beginners

Written by Christine Jun 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a beginner at cutting men's hair, it can be intimidating to approach long hair. However, with the right tools, techniques and practice, you can master the skill of cutting long men's hair with scissors. In this article, we'll walk you through step-by-step instructions to achieve a professional-looking haircut, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools:

  • Scissors: Make sure you have a good pair of hairdressing scissors that are sharp and have a comfortable grip.
  • Comb: A wide-tooth comb is ideal for detangling the hair before cutting.
  • Clips: You'll need clips to hold the hair in place while you work on different sections.
  • Spray bottle: Fill a spray bottle with water to keep the hair damp while you cut.
  • Cape or towel: Use a cape or towel to protect your client's clothes from hair clippings.

Preparing the Hair

Before you start cutting, you'll need to prepare the hair:

  1. Wash and dry the hair: Make sure the hair is clean and dry before cutting.
  2. Detangle the hair: Use a wide-tooth comb to gently remove any tangles or knots from the hair.
  3. Trim any split ends: If the hair has split ends, trim them with scissors before starting the haircut.

Sectioning the Hair

Sectioning the hair will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any sections:

  1. Divide the hair in half: Use a comb to make a horizontal parting at the back of the head, from ear to ear. Clip the top section of hair out of the way.
  2. Divide the bottom section: Make a vertical parting down the middle of the head, from the crown to the nape of the neck. Clip one half out of the way.
  3. Start cutting: Begin cutting the hair in small, even sections, starting at the back of the head and working your way forward.

Cutting the Hair

Now it's time to start cutting:

  1. Take a small section of hair: Use your comb to take a small section of hair from the bottom of the hairline.
  2. Hold the hair between your fingers: Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about an inch or two from the ends.
  3. Cut the hair: Use your scissors to cut the hair in a straight line. Make sure you cut the hair evenly and don't take off too much at once.
  4. Repeat: Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting them in a straight line. Work your way around the head, cutting the hair to the desired length.

Blending the Hair

Once you've cut all the hair to the desired length, it's time to blend:

  1. Comb the hair: Comb the hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Take a small section of hair: Use your comb to take a small section of hair from the top of the hairline.
  3. Blend the hair: Hold the hair between your fingers and use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle. This will blend the shorter hair with the longer hair and create a seamless transition.
  4. Repeat: Continue taking small sections of hair and blending them until the hair is evenly blended.

Finishing Touches

Once you've cut and blended the hair, it's time to add the finishing touches:

  1. Check for any uneven sections: Comb through the hair and check for any sections that are longer or shorter than the rest. Trim any uneven sections with your scissors.
  2. Remove any stray hairs: Use your scissors to remove any stray hairs around the hairline and ears.
  3. Style the hair: Style the hair as desired using a blow dryer, gel, or other styling products.

Tips for Cutting Long Men's Hair

  • Start with small cuts: It's better to take off a little at a time than to cut too much and have to start over.
  • Keep the hair damp: Use a spray bottle filled with water to keep the hair damp while you cut.
  • Don't cut too much at once: It's better to take off a little at a time than to cut too much and have to start over.
  • Use the right scissors: Make sure you have a good pair of hairdressing scissors that are sharp and have a comfortable grip.
  • Practice makes perfect: The more you practice cutting hair, the better you'll get at it.

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and the style you're trying to achieve. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, but it can be difficult to achieve a professional-looking haircut on your own. It's best to go to a professional hairstylist for a haircut, especially if you're not experienced at cutting hair.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting hair, don't panic. It's better to stop and assess the situation than to keep cutting and make it worse. You can always trim off more hair later if needed.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, too often.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when wet.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Conclusion

Cutting long men's hair with scissors can seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools, techniques, and practice, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut. Remember to start with small cuts, keep the hair damp, and use the right scissors. If you make a mistake, don't panic â you can always trim off more hair later. And don't forget to take care of your hair after cutting it to keep it healthy and looking great.
