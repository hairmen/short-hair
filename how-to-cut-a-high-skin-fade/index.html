How To Cut A High Skin Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a stylish new haircut? A high skin fade might be just what you need. This trendy haircut is popular with men of all ages and hair types, and it's easy to see why. The high skin fade is a bold and modern look that can be customized to suit your personal style. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut a high skin fade step by step.

Description

A high skin fade is a type of haircut where the hair gradually tapers from long to short, with the shortest hair at the sides and back of the head. The hair on top can be any length you like, but it's usually longer than the sides. The skin fade refers to the fact that the hair is cut very close to the skin at the sides and back of the head, giving the appearance of a fade.

How to

To cut a high skin fade, you'll need a few tools:

  • Clippers
  • Guards
  • Shears
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape
  • Mirror

Follow these steps to cut a high skin fade:

Step 1: Choose the Right Guard

Start by choosing the right guard for your clippers. The guard you choose will determine how short the hair at the sides and back of your head will be. For a high skin fade, you'll want to start with a longer guard, like a #4 or #5.

Step 2: Cut the Sides and Back

Using your clippers and the guard you selected, start cutting the hair at the sides and back of your head. Work your way up the head, gradually switching to shorter guards as you go. Be sure to blend the hair as you go, creating a smooth transition from longer to shorter hair.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Once you've cut the sides and back of your head, it's time to move on to the top. Use your shears to cut the hair on top to your desired length. You can leave it long or cut it short – it's up to you.

Step 4: Blend the Top and Sides

To create a seamless transition from the top to the sides, use your shears to blend the hair at the top of your head with the shorter hair at the sides. Be sure to blend the hair evenly, creating a smooth and natural look.

Step 5: Clean Up the Edges

Finally, use your clippers and shears to clean up the edges around your hairline and ears. Be sure to take your time and create a neat and tidy finish.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting a high skin fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to shorter guards gradually
  • Use your shears to blend the hair at the top of your head with the shorter hair at the sides
  • Take your time and be patient – a high skin fade requires precision and attention to detail
  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles
  • Practice makes perfect – don't be afraid to experiment with different techniques and styles

FAQ

Is a high skin fade suitable for all hair types?

Yes, a high skin fade can be customized to suit any hair type or texture.

How often should I get a high skin fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out. Generally, you should plan to get a high skin fade every 2-3 weeks to maintain the look.

Can I cut a high skin fade at home?

Yes, with the right tools and techniques, you can cut a high skin fade at home. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's best to leave it to a professional.

Hair Care

To keep your high skin fade looking sharp, you'll need to take good care of your hair. Be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, and use styling products to keep it looking neat and tidy. Consider using a pomade or wax to add texture and hold to your hair.

Now that you know how to cut a high skin fade, it's time to give it a try. Follow these steps and tips to create a stylish and modern look that's sure to turn heads.
