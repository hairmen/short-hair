What Can Be The Reason Of Excessive Hair Fall

Written by Aprilia Jun 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Excessive hair fall is a common problem that affects both men and women. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, hormonal changes, poor diet, and certain medical conditions. In this article, we will explore the different reasons why you may be experiencing excessive hair fall and what you can do to prevent it.

Genetics

One of the most common reasons for excessive hair fall is genetics. If hair loss runs in your family, then you may be genetically predisposed to it. In men, this is known as male pattern baldness, which usually starts with a receding hairline and thinning hair on the crown of the head. Women can also experience genetic hair loss, which is known as female pattern baldness. This usually results in thinning hair on the top and sides of the head.

Stress

Stress is another common cause of hair fall. When you are under stress, your body releases a hormone called cortisol, which can affect the growth cycle of your hair follicles. This can lead to excessive shedding and thinning of the hair. Stress can also cause you to pull or twist your hair, which can further damage the hair follicles.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also contribute to hair fall. Women, in particular, may experience hair loss due to hormonal changes during pregnancy, menopause, or after stopping birth control pills. Thyroid imbalances can also cause hair loss, as the thyroid gland regulates the body's metabolism and affects hair growth.

Poor Diet

Your diet can also affect the health of your hair. If you are not getting enough nutrients, such as protein, iron, and vitamins, your hair may become weak and prone to breakage. This can lead to excessive shedding and hair fall. Additionally, consuming too much sugar and processed foods can cause inflammation in the body, which can also contribute to hair loss.

Medical Conditions

There are several medical conditions that can cause hair fall, such as alopecia areata, which is an autoimmune disorder that causes patchy hair loss, and scalp infections, which can damage the hair follicles. Certain medications, such as chemotherapy drugs, can also cause hair loss as a side effect.

Prevention Tips

There are several things you can do to prevent excessive hair fall. Eating a balanced diet that is rich in protein, iron, and vitamins can help promote healthy hair growth. Avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools can also prevent damage to the hair follicles. Additionally, managing your stress levels and practicing relaxation techniques, such as yoga or meditation, can help reduce cortisol levels and prevent hair fall.

FAQs

How much hair fall is normal?

It is normal to lose between 50 and 100 hairs per day. However, if you are experiencing excessive shedding or thinning of the hair, you may be experiencing hair fall.

Can hair fall be reversed?

Depending on the cause of the hair fall, it may be reversible. For example, if hair loss is caused by poor nutrition, improving your diet can help promote healthy hair growth. However, genetic hair loss may not be reversible without medical intervention.

What can I do to promote hair growth?

Eating a balanced diet, practicing good hair care habits, and managing stress levels can all promote healthy hair growth. Additionally, taking supplements, such as biotin, can also help support hair growth.

Excessive hair fall can be a frustrating and stressful experience, but there are many things you can do to prevent it. By understanding the different causes of hair fall and taking steps to promote healthy hair growth, you can maintain a full and healthy head of hair.
