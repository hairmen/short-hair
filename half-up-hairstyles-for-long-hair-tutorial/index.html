Half Up Hairstyles For Long Hair Tutorial

Written by Robby Aug 18, 2023 · 5 min read

Long hair can be difficult to manage, especially when you want to keep it out of your face but still want to show off its length. One solution is the half-up hairstyle, which is both practical and stylish. In this tutorial, we’ll show you how to create different half-up looks for long hair, including braids, twists, and bun variations.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

Before we get started, here are some tools you’ll need:

  • Comb
  • Hair elastics
  • Bobby pins
  • Hairspray

Now, let’s begin!

Braided Half-Up

Start by brushing your hair and separating the top section from the bottom. Take a small section of hair from the crown and braid it all the way down. Secure the braid with an elastic.

Next, take two small sections of hair from each side of your head and tie them together at the back with an elastic. Then, take the braid and wrap it around the elastic to hide it, securing it with bobby pins.

You can leave the rest of your hair down or curl it for a more polished look. Finish with hairspray.

Twisted Half-Up

Part your hair down the middle and take a small section from the front on one side. Twist it away from your face and secure it at the back with a bobby pin. Repeat on the other side.

Take another section of hair from each side, this time a little bit further back, and twist them as before. Secure them at the back with bobby pins.

Finally, take the remaining hair at the back and tie it into a low ponytail or bun. Finish with hairspray.

Braided Crown

Start by parting your hair down the middle and braiding a small section on one side. Secure it with an elastic and repeat on the other side.

Take the two braids and cross them at the back of your head, securing them with bobby pins. Tuck the ends of the braids under the opposite braid to hide them.

You can leave the rest of your hair down or curl it for a more polished look. Finish with hairspray.

Step by Step

Here are the steps for each half-up hairstyle:

Braided Half-Up

  1. Brush your hair and separate the top section from the bottom.
  2. Braid a small section of hair from the crown and secure it with an elastic.
  3. Take two small sections of hair from each side of your head and tie them together at the back with an elastic.
  4. Wrap the braid around the elastic to hide it and secure it with bobby pins.
  5. Leave the rest of your hair down or curl it for a more polished look. Finish with hairspray.

Twisted Half-Up

  1. Part your hair down the middle and take a small section from the front on one side.
  2. Twist it away from your face and secure it at the back with a bobby pin. Repeat on the other side.
  3. Take another section of hair from each side, this time a little bit further back, and twist them as before. Secure them at the back with bobby pins.
  4. Tie the remaining hair at the back into a low ponytail or bun. Finish with hairspray.

Braided Crown

  1. Part your hair down the middle and braid a small section on one side. Secure it with an elastic and repeat on the other side.
  2. Cross the two braids at the back of your head and secure them with bobby pins.
  3. Tuck the ends of the braids under the opposite braid to hide them.
  4. Leave the rest of your hair down or curl it for a more polished look. Finish with hairspray.

Tips

Here are some tips to make your half-up hairstyle look its best:

  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color for a more natural look.
  • Experiment with different braids and twists to find the style that suits you best.
  • Don’t worry if your hairstyle looks a little messy – it’s meant to be relaxed and effortless.
  • Use hairspray to keep your hairstyle in place all day.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about half-up hairstyles:

Can I do a half-up hairstyle on short hair?

Yes, you can! However, you may need to use more bobby pins to secure your hair in place.

Can I wear a half-up hairstyle to a formal event?

Yes, you can dress up your half-up hairstyle with accessories like jeweled hair clips or headbands.

How do I prevent my hair from slipping out of the bobby pins?

Try spraying the bobby pins with hairspray before using them, or criss-crossing them to create a tighter hold.

Hair Care

Here are some tips to keep your long hair healthy and shiny:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from drying out.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, or use a heat protectant spray before using them.
  • Regularly trim your hair to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can create beautiful half-up hairstyles for your long hair. Whether you’re running errands or attending a special event, these hairstyles will keep your hair out of your face and looking fabulous!
