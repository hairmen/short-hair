How Can I Stop Hair Loss From Psoriasis

Written by William Aug 10, 2023 · 4 min read

If you suffer from psoriasis, you know how frustrating and uncomfortable it can be. Not only does it affect your skin, but it can also cause hair loss. In this blog post, we’ll explore how you can stop hair loss from psoriasis and provide tips on how to care for your hair.

Description

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune disease that causes skin cells to grow too quickly, leading to thick, scaly patches on the skin. These patches can occur anywhere on the body, including the scalp. When psoriasis affects the scalp, it can cause hair loss.

How to Stop Hair Loss from Psoriasis

If you’re experiencing hair loss due to psoriasis, there are several things you can do to stop it:

1. Treat Your Psoriasis

The first step in stopping hair loss from psoriasis is to treat your psoriasis. There are several treatment options available, including topical creams, oral medications, and light therapy. Talk to your doctor about which treatment option is best for you.

2. Use a Gentle Shampoo

When you have psoriasis on your scalp, it’s important to use a gentle shampoo that won’t irritate your skin. Look for a shampoo that is specifically designed for sensitive skin or psoriasis. Avoid shampoos that contain harsh chemicals or fragrances.

3. Don’t Scratch Your Scalp

Scratching your scalp can further irritate your skin and lead to hair loss. If you feel the urge to scratch, try using a cold compress or a soothing cream instead. If you have severe itching, talk to your doctor about a prescription medication.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons, can damage your hair and make hair loss worse. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

5. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Make sure you’re getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet. Foods that are good for hair health include salmon, eggs, spinach, and nuts.

Step by Step

1. Treat your psoriasis
2. Use a gentle shampoo
3. Don’t scratch your scalp
4. Avoid heat styling
5. Eat a healthy diet

Tips

- Keep your scalp moisturized
- Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun
- Don’t use harsh chemicals on your hair
- Be patient – it may take time to see results

FAQ

Q: Can hair loss from psoriasis be permanent?

A: Hair loss from psoriasis is usually temporary. Once you treat your psoriasis, your hair should start to grow back.

Q: Will hair grow back in areas where I’ve lost it due to psoriasis?

A: In most cases, hair will grow back in areas where it was lost due to psoriasis. However, it may take several months for hair to fully regrow.

Q: Can stress make psoriasis and hair loss worse?

A: Yes, stress can worsen psoriasis and hair loss. Try to manage your stress levels through relaxation techniques, such as meditation or yoga.

Hair Care

When you have psoriasis, it’s important to take extra care of your hair. Here are some tips:

- Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair
- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
- Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails
- Don’t wash your hair too often – aim for every other day
- Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized

If you’re experiencing hair loss due to psoriasis, don’t be discouraged. With the right treatment and hair care routine, you can stop hair loss and promote hair growth. Talk to your doctor about the best treatment options for you and be patient – it may take time to see results.
