How To Fix Frizzy Front Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Sep 17, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're someone who struggles with frizz in the front of your hair, you're not alone. Frizz can be caused by humidity, genetics, or damage from heat styling tools. But don't worry, there are ways to tame that frizz and get the smooth, sleek look you're after. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for fixing frizzy front hair.

How to Fix Frizzy Front Hair

Here are some steps you can take to fix frizzy front hair:

Step 1: Use a Smoothing Shampoo and Conditioner

The first step to fighting frizz is to use a smoothing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, which can help smooth the hair cuticle and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Apply a Frizz-Control Product

After washing your hair, apply a frizz-control product to the front section. This could be a serum, cream, or oil. Be sure to apply the product evenly and avoid using too much, as this can weigh down your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry with a Round Brush

Next, use a round brush and blow dryer to smooth out the front section of your hair. Starting at the roots, use the brush to pull the hair taut and direct the heat from the dryer down the hair shaft. Continue until the front section is completely dry and smooth.

Step 4: Touch Up with a Flat Iron

If you still have some frizz after blow drying, you can use a flat iron to touch up the front section. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using the flat iron to avoid further damage to your hair.

Tips for Fighting Frizz

Here are some additional tips for fighting frizz:

Tip 1: Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to frizz. Try washing your hair every other day, or even every third day, to help maintain its natural moisture.

Tip 2: Use a Microfiber Towel

Using a regular towel to dry your hair can create friction and lead to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry.

Tip 3: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce friction and prevent frizz. Silk is also gentler on your hair than cotton, which can cause breakage and tangles.

FAQ

Q: Can genetics cause frizzy hair?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in frizzy hair. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you may be more prone to frizz.

Q: Can heat styling tools cause frizz?

A: Yes, using too much heat on your hair can damage it and lead to frizz. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is key to preventing frizz. Be sure to use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. Additionally, getting regular trims can help prevent split ends, which can also contribute to frizz.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to tame that frizz and get the smooth, sleek look you're after.
