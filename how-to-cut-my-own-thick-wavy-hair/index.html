How To Cut My Own Thick Wavy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever been frustrated with the amount of money you spend on haircuts? Or maybe you just can't seem to find a stylist who understands your thick wavy hair? Whatever the case may be, learning how to cut your own hair can be a game-changer. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own thick wavy hair.

Description

Thick wavy hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it looks beautiful when styled correctly, it can be difficult to manage and style on a daily basis. Many hair stylists may not understand the unique needs of thick wavy hair, which is why cutting your own hair may be a better option. By learning how to cut your own hair, you can save money and ensure that your hair looks the way you want it to.

How to Cut My Own Thick Wavy Hair

Cutting your own hair can seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Here are the steps to cutting your own thick wavy hair:

Step by Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help remove any tangles and make it easier to cut.
  2. Use a comb to part your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck.
  3. Divide your hair into sections, starting from the back and working your way forward. Secure each section with a hair tie or clip.
  4. Start with the back sections of your hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off any split ends or damaged hair.
  5. Gradually work your way forward, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Be sure to cut evenly on both sides.
  6. Once you have finished cutting, use a mirror to check for any uneven spots. Make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Thick Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own thick wavy hair:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. This will make the cutting process easier and more precise.
  • Start with small cuts. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so it's okay to take a break and come back to it later.
  • Don't be too hard on yourself. It may take a few tries to get the hang of cutting your own hair, so don't get discouraged if it doesn't turn out perfectly the first time.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own thick wavy hair:

  • Can I cut my hair when it's wet or dry? It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be difficult to cut evenly.
  • How often should I cut my hair? It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Should I cut my hair all at once or in sections? It's best to cut your hair in sections, starting from the back and working your way forward.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Wavy Hair

Cutting your own hair is just one part of maintaining healthy and beautiful hair. Here are some hair care tips for thick wavy hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and learning how to cut your own hair, you can save money and ensure that your thick wavy hair looks its best.
