What Is 50S And 60S Music Called

Written by Petter Nov 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about what is 50s and 60s music called. If you're a fan of the classic sounds from the 1950s and 1960s, you might be wondering what this type of music is actually called. In this post, we'll explore the different names for this genre and give you some tips on how to find and enjoy it today.

What is 50s and 60s Music?

Before we dive into the different names for this type of music, let's first define what it actually is. 50s and 60s music refers to the popular music that was produced and enjoyed during these two decades. This includes genres like rock and roll, doo-wop, R&B, and soul, as well as pop and country music.

What is the Music Called?

Now, onto the big question: what is 50s and 60s music called? There are a few different names for this genre, depending on who you ask and what specific sub-genre of music you're talking about. Here are some of the most common names:

  • Oldies music
  • Classic rock
  • Golden oldies
  • Old school music
  • Old-time rock and roll
  • 60s soul
  • 50s rock
  • Doo-wop

As you can see, there are quite a few different names for this type of music. Some people might use one term more than others, depending on their age or personal preferences. However, all of these names refer to the same general type of music.

How to Find and Enjoy 50s and 60s Music

Now that you know what this type of music is called, you might be wondering how to find and enjoy it. Here are some tips:

  • Listen to oldies radio stations: Many radio stations still play oldies music, so try tuning in to one of these stations to hear some classic tunes.
  • Search for oldies playlists on streaming services: Spotify, Apple Music, and other streaming services have plenty of oldies playlists to choose from. Try searching for keywords like "50s music" or "classic rock" to find some great options.
  • Check out oldies concerts and events: Many artists from the 50s and 60s are still performing today, so keep an eye out for concerts and events featuring these musicians.
  • Browse online forums and communities: There are plenty of online communities dedicated to oldies music, where you can connect with other fans and discover new artists and songs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about 50s and 60s music:

What are some popular artists from this era?

Some of the most popular artists from the 50s and 60s include Elvis Presley, The Beatles, Chuck Berry, Roy Orbison, Aretha Franklin, and The Supremes.

What's the difference between 50s and 60s music?

The main difference is the time period in which the music was produced. 50s music tends to be more rock and roll and doo-wop, while 60s music includes more soul and folk influences.

Is oldies music still popular today?

Yes, oldies music is still popular today, especially among older generations. Many younger people also enjoy this type of music as well, as it has a timeless quality that appeals to people of all ages.

Hair Care While Listening to 50s and 60s Music

Finally, if you really want to get into the spirit of 50s and 60s music, why not try styling your hair in a retro-inspired look? Here are some tips:

  • For women, try a classic updo or a bouffant hairstyle.
  • For men, slick back your hair or try a pompadour.
  • Use hair products like hairspray, gel, and mousse to keep your hair in place.
  • Accessorize with headbands, scarves, or hair clips for an extra retro touch.

Now that you know what 50s and 60s music is called, as well as how to find and enjoy it, you can start exploring this classic genre for yourself. Whether you're a lifelong fan or just discovering this music for the first time, there's something special about the sounds of the past that still resonate today.
