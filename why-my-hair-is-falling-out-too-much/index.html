Why Is My Hair Falling Out Too Much

Written by Aprilia Oct 06, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you experiencing excessive hair loss? Losing 50-100 hairs per day is considered normal, but if you notice more than that, it may be time to take action. Hair loss can affect both men and women and can be caused by various factors such as genetics, stress, hormonal changes, and poor nutrition. In this blog post, we will discuss the possible reasons why your hair is falling out too much and what you can do about it.

Description

Hair loss can be a frustrating and distressing experience for anyone. It can affect your confidence and self-esteem, and can even lead to depression. Hair loss can occur gradually or suddenly, and can be temporary or permanent. If you are experiencing excessive hair loss, it is important to identify the underlying cause and seek appropriate treatment.

How to Identify Hair Loss?

Before we dive into the possible causes of hair loss, let's first talk about how to identify it. You may notice more hair fall than usual while washing or brushing your hair. You may also notice thinning hair, bald spots, or receding hairline. If you are unsure whether you are experiencing hair loss, consult a dermatologist or a trichologist.

Step by Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss

Step 1: Improve Your Diet

One of the most common reasons for hair loss is poor nutrition. Your hair needs essential vitamins and minerals to grow and stay healthy. Include foods rich in protein, iron, zinc, and vitamins A, C, and E in your diet. Avoid crash diets and opt for a balanced and healthy diet.

Step 2: Manage Stress

Stress is a major contributor to hair loss. When you are stressed, your body produces cortisol, a hormone that can affect your hair growth cycle. Practice stress-reducing techniques such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.

Step 3: Avoid Heat and Chemical Treatments

Excessive heat and chemical treatments can damage your hair and lead to hair loss. Avoid using hot tools such as straighteners and curling irons, and limit the use of hair products that contain harsh chemicals.

Step 4: Use Gentle Hair Care Products

Choose hair care products that are gentle and free of sulfates and parabens. Avoid using shampoos that contain harsh ingredients that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption
  • Consult a healthcare professional if you are experiencing sudden hair loss

FAQ

Q: Can genetics cause hair loss?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in hair loss. If your family members have a history of hair loss, you may be more prone to it.

Q: Can hormonal changes cause hair loss?

A: Yes, hormonal changes such as pregnancy, menopause, and thyroid disorders can cause hair loss.

Q: Can hair loss be reversed?

A: It depends on the underlying cause of hair loss. In some cases, hair loss can be reversed with appropriate treatment.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to prevent hair loss. Use gentle hair care products, avoid excessive heat and chemical treatments, and eat a balanced and healthy diet. If you are experiencing excessive hair loss, consult a healthcare professional to identify the underlying cause and seek appropriate treatment.
