How To Cut Coarse Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts for your coarse hair? Learning how to cut your own hair can save you time and money in the long run. In this article, we’ll go over the step-by-step process of cutting coarse men’s hair, as well as tips and tricks for a successful cut. Let’s get started!

How to Cut Coarse Men's Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you’ll need to gather the necessary tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Water spray bottle
  • Mirror

Make sure your clippers and scissors are sharp to avoid pulling or damaging your hair. You can find all of these tools at your local beauty supply store or online.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair and comb it out to remove any tangles. Spray your hair with water to make it damp, but not dripping wet. This will make it easier to cut and prevent the clippers from getting clogged with hair.

Step 3: Choose Your Guard Size

Decide on the length you want your hair to be and choose the appropriate guard size for your clippers. If you’re not sure, start with a longer guard and work your way down as needed.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin by using the clippers with the guard to cut the sides and back of your hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, moving the clippers in an upward motion against the direction of hair growth. Make sure to use gentle pressure and avoid pressing too hard to prevent skin irritation or nicks.

Next, use your scissors to trim the top of your hair. Take small sections at a time and cut at an angle to create texture and movement. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as coarse hair can be difficult to manage if too short.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

Use your clippers without a guard to blend the sides and back into the top of your hair. Use a sweeping motion and be mindful of any lines or uneven areas. Take your time and use the comb to guide your clippers for a seamless blend.

Step 6: Clean Up Your Neckline

Use your clippers to clean up the hair on your neckline. Start at the center and work your way outwards. Use a trimmer or razor to create a clean line along your hairline.

Tips for Cutting Coarse Men's Hair

Here are a few tips to help you achieve a successful cut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down as needed.
  • Take your time and don’t rush.
  • Use the comb to guide your clippers for a seamless blend.
  • Be mindful of your hair’s natural growth patterns.

FAQs

1. How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. As a general rule, aim to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks.

2. Can I cut my hair with just scissors?

While it’s possible to cut your hair with just scissors, it’s easier and more efficient to use clippers for an even cut.

Hair Care for Coarse Men's Hair

Proper hair care is essential for healthy, manageable hair. Here are a few tips for caring for your coarse hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip it of its natural oils.

By following these tips and our step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to cut your own coarse men’s hair like a pro. Happy cutting!
