How To Cut My Own Naturally Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a curly girl like me, you know that finding a good hair stylist who understands your curls can be a challenge. But what if I told you that you could cut your own naturally curly hair at home? With a little bit of knowledge and the right tools, you can achieve salon-quality results while saving time and money. In this article, I'll share my tips and tricks for how to cut my own naturally curly hair.

How to Cut My Own Naturally Curly Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's talk about the tools you'll need.

What You'll Need:

  • Sharp hair cutting shears
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Now that you have your tools, it's time to get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own naturally curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and detangle your hair

Start with clean, detangled hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently remove any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use your comb to create sections in your hair. Start with a middle part and then create two more sections on each side of your head.

Step 3: Cut the length

Take one section of hair and twist it tightly. Use your hair cutting shears to snip off the ends. Repeat this process with each section until you've cut the desired length.

Step 4: Add layers (optional)

If you want to add layers to your hair, take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Use your shears to cut into the hair at a slight angle, creating a layered effect. Repeat this process with each section until you've achieved the desired look.

Step 5: Check for balance

Once you've finished cutting, use your mirror to check for balance. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is even on both sides.

Tips for a Successful DIY Haircut

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with these tips, you'll be a pro in no time:

Tip 1: Start with small snips

It's better to start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Tip 2: Use sharp shears

Dull shears can cause split ends and damage your hair. Invest in a good pair of hair cutting shears and keep them sharp.

Tip 3: Cut in a well-lit area

Make sure you're cutting your hair in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing.

FAQs

Can I cut my own naturally curly hair if it's really long?

Yes, you can cut your own naturally curly hair no matter what length it is. Just remember to start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length.

Do I need to wet my hair before cutting it?

Yes, it's best to cut your hair when it's wet so you can see the natural curl pattern and avoid any surprises when your hair dries.

Is it possible to cut my own naturally curly hair without creating frizz?

Yes, if you follow the steps above and use sharp hair cutting shears, you can achieve a salon-quality haircut without creating frizz.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After you've finished cutting your own naturally curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

Tip 1: Deep condition your hair

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Tip 2: Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair, so try to avoid it as much as possible. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Tip 3: Embrace your curls

Your natural curls are beautiful, so don't be afraid to rock them. Use a curl-enhancing product to define your curls and keep them looking their best.

And there you have it, a complete guide on how to cut your own naturally curly hair. With a little bit of practice and patience, you can achieve salon-quality results at home. Happy cutting!
