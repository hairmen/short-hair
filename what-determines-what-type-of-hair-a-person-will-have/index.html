What Determines What Type Of Hair A Person Will Have

Written by Frank Nov 16, 2023 · 3 min read

Table of Contents:

  • How to Determine Hair Type
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQs

Description

Have you ever wondered why your hair has a certain texture or why it behaves a certain way? The answer lies in your genes. A person's hair type is determined by their genetic makeup, which controls the shape and structure of the hair follicles.

There are several factors that determine a person's hair type, including:

  • Genetics
  • Ethnicity
  • Diet
  • Environmental factors

How to Determine Hair Type

The first step in understanding your hair type is to determine its texture. Hair texture is determined by the diameter of the individual hair strands. The three main hair textures are:

  • Straight
  • Wavy
  • Curly

Once you have determined your hair texture, you can then determine your hair type based on its thickness and porosity. Hair thickness refers to the overall quantity of hair on your head, while porosity refers to your hair's ability to absorb and retain moisture.

Types of Hair Thickness

There are three types of hair thickness:

  • Fine
  • Medium
  • Thick

Types of Hair Porosity

There are three types of hair porosity:

  • Low porosity
  • Normal porosity
  • High porosity

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to determine your hair type:

  1. Wash and condition your hair, and let it air dry without any styling products.
  2. Determine your hair texture by examining the shape of your hair strands. Straight hair strands are cylindrical, wavy hair strands are oval-shaped, and curly hair strands are flat.
  3. Determine your hair thickness by looking at the overall amount of hair on your head. If you can see your scalp, you have fine hair. If you can see some scalp, you have medium hair. If you can't see your scalp, you have thick hair.
  4. Determine your hair porosity by taking a strand of hair and placing it in a glass of water. If it sinks to the bottom quickly, you have high porosity hair. If it takes a while to sink, you have normal porosity hair. If it floats on top, you have low porosity hair.

Tips for Hair Care

Knowing your hair type is important for choosing the right hair care products and styling techniques. Here are some tips for caring for each hair type:

Straight Hair

  • Use lightweight products to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Wash your hair every other day to avoid oil buildup.

Wavy Hair

  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying to enhance your natural waves.
  • Avoid heavy products that can weigh down your waves.
  • Wash your hair every two to three days to avoid dryness.

Curly Hair

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Wash your hair once or twice a week to avoid dryness.

FAQs

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, hormonal changes, diet, and environmental factors can all affect your hair type.

Q: Is it possible to have more than one hair type?

A: Yes, it is common for people to have a combination of hair types, such as wavy and thick or curly and fine.

Q: How do I know if my hair is damaged?

A: Signs of damaged hair include split ends, breakage, dryness, and lack of shine.

Understanding your hair type is the first step in achieving healthy, beautiful hair. By using the right hair care products and techniques, you can make the most of your natural hair texture and keep your hair looking its best.
