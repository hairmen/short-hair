How To Use Thinning Scissors For Long Hair

Written by Daniel Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning scissors are a great tool to use when you want to add texture and volume to your long hair. They can help you achieve that perfect layered look without having to cut your hair too short. However, using thinning scissors can be a bit tricky, especially if you have never used them before. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning scissors for long hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of scissors that have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth are designed to remove excess hair and create a softer, more natural look. Thinning scissors are commonly used to thin out thick hair, remove bulk, and add texture to hair. They are also a great tool for creating layers in long hair.

How to

Before using thinning scissors on your long hair, it is important to make sure that your hair is clean and dry. You should also have a clear idea of the style you want to achieve, and the amount of hair you want to remove. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning scissors for long hair:

Step by Step

  1. Start by sectioning your hair. Use clips to separate the top layer of your hair from the bottom layer. This will make it easier to work on one section at a time.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the section with your fingers at the desired length you want to cut.
  3. Using your thinning scissors, make small, quick cuts into the section of hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up. Be sure to keep the scissors angled downward as you cut.
  4. Repeat this process on the rest of the bottom layer of your hair.
  5. Once you have finished the bottom layer, release the top layer of your hair and repeat the process.
  6. When you have finished thinning out all of your hair, use a comb to blend the layers together. This will create a natural, textured look.

Tips

  • Always use sharp thinning scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Work on small sections of hair at a time to ensure even results.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. It's better to make several small cuts than one big cut.
  • If you're not sure how much hair to remove, start with a small amount and work your way up.
  • Remember to blend the layers together for a natural look.

FAQ

Q: Can thinning scissors be used on any type of hair?

A: Thinning scissors can be used on most hair types, but they are particularly effective on thick, heavy hair.

Q: Can thinning scissors damage my hair?

A: Thinning scissors can damage your hair if they are not used properly or if they are not sharp. It's important to use sharp scissors and to take your time when using them.

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on my own hair?

A: It is possible to use thinning scissors on your own hair, but it can be difficult to achieve even results. It's best to have someone else do it for you.

Hair Care

After using thinning scissors on your long hair, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and promote healthy growth. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Using thinning scissors for long hair can be a great way to add texture and volume to your hair. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect layered look without having to cut your hair too short.
