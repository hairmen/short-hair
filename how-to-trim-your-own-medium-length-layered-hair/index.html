How To Trim Your Own Medium Length Layered Hair

Written by Robby Jun 12, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of constantly going to the salon to get your hair trimmed? Trimming your own medium length layered hair may seem daunting at first, but it's actually quite simple with the right technique. In this article, we'll go over step by step how to trim your own hair at home.

Description

Medium length layered hair is a popular hairstyle, but it requires regular maintenance to keep it looking its best. Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks is recommended to keep split ends at bay and maintain the shape of your layers. By trimming your own hair at home, you can save time and money while achieving salon-worthy results.

How to Trim Your Own Medium Length Layered Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water.

Step 2: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair before you start trimming. This will help you see the length and shape of your hair more clearly.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, separate your hair into four sections - two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section out of the way with your hair clips.

Step 4: Trim the Back

Starting with the back sections, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors vertically and trim the ends to your desired length. Repeat this process with each section, working your way around the back of your head.

Step 5: Trim the Front

Once you've finished the back sections, unclip the front sections and repeat the same process. Take small sections of hair, comb them straight down, and trim the ends to your desired length.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

To blend the layers and create a seamless look, take small sections of hair from the top and bottom layers and trim them at an angle. This will help create a gradual, layered effect.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished trimming, check your work in a mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your hair is even and the layers are blended.

Tips

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Don't cut too much at once - it's better to trim a little at a time than to cut off too much.
  • If you're unsure about trimming your own hair, start with small sections and work your way up.

FAQ

Can I trim my own hair with regular scissors?

No, it's important to use sharp hair cutting scissors to prevent split ends.

How often should I trim my medium length layered hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your layers and prevent split ends.

Is it easy to trim my own hair?

With the right tools and technique, trimming your own hair can be easy and save you time and money.

Hair Care

After trimming your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, avoid using heat styling tools too often, and protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.

Trimming your own medium length layered hair may seem intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, it can be easy and rewarding. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a salon-worthy trim at home.
