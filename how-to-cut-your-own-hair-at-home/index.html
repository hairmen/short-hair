How To Cut Your Own Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the salon to get your hair cut? Or maybe you just don't have the time to make an appointment. Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair at home can be a great solution. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own hair at home in a few easy steps.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Tips for a Great Haircut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Your Haircut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • A handheld mirror or a larger mirror you can position behind you

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can create uneven cuts and damage your hair.

Preparing Your Hair

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to prepare your hair for the cut. Follow these steps:

  1. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would.
  2. Brush or comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Part your hair in the middle or wherever you normally part it.
  4. Clip or tie back any sections of hair you don't want to cut, such as the top layer or your bangs.

Cutting Your Hair

Now that your hair is prepped, it's time to start cutting. Follow these steps:

  1. Start with small sections of hair, about 1 inch wide.
  2. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and slide your fingers down to the length you want to cut. For example, if you want to cut off 1 inch, slide your fingers down to 1 inch.
  3. Hold the section of hair taut and cut across your fingers in a straight line.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way around your head.
  5. When you're finished, check your hair in the mirror to make sure it's even. If it's not, make small adjustments until it looks even.

Remember, it's always better to cut less than to cut too much. You can always go back and trim more later if needed.

Tips for a Great Haircut

Here are a few tips to help you get the best haircut possible:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Check your hair in the mirror frequently to make sure it's even.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it may look longer than it actually is when it dries.
  • Trim your hair regularly, about every 6-8 weeks, to prevent split ends.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair at home:

  • Can I cut my own hair if it's curly? Yes, but it may be more difficult to cut evenly. Consider cutting your hair while it's dry instead of wet to avoid any surprises.
  • Should I cut my hair in layers? If you want a layered look, you can cut your hair in layers. However, it may be easier to achieve this look with the help of a professional stylist.
  • Can I cut my own bangs? Yes, but be careful not to cut them too short. It's always better to start with a longer length and trim more if needed.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

Cutting your own hair at home can be a great way to save money and time. With the right tools and a little patience, you can achieve a great haircut in the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time, cut small sections of hair at a time, and check your hair frequently in the mirror. Happy cutting!
