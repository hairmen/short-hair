How To Wash Hair After Japanese Straightening Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've just had your hair Japanese straightened, congratulations on your sleek, shiny new locks! Maintaining the health and beauty of your hair after this treatment requires some extra care and attention, starting with how you wash it. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through how to properly wash your hair after Japanese straightening.

Step 1: Wait 72 Hours

The first and most important step when it comes to washing your hair after Japanese straightening is to wait at least 72 hours after the treatment. This waiting period is necessary to allow the hair cuticles to fully close and lock in the treatment, ensuring that your hair remains straight and smooth for as long as possible. Avoid getting your hair wet during this time, and avoid any activities that might cause excessive sweating.

Step 2: Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

When it's finally time to wash your hair, make sure to use a sulfate-free shampoo. Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage to the hair cuticles, which is the opposite of what you want when you're trying to maintain your Japanese straightening. Look for shampoos that are specifically labeled as sulfate-free, or that use gentle, natural surfactants like coconut oil or aloe vera.

Step 3: Apply Conditioner

After shampooing, apply a high-quality conditioner to your hair. This will help to restore any moisture that may have been lost during the straightening process, and will also help to detangle your hair and make it easier to comb through. Be sure to use a conditioner that is specifically formulated for dry or damaged hair, as this will provide the most benefits.

Step 4: Dry Your Hair Gently

When it's time to dry your hair, be gentle! Use a soft, microfiber towel to pat your hair dry, rather than rubbing it vigorously with a rough towel. This will help to prevent breakage and damage to your hair. Avoid using a hair dryer if possible, as the heat can cause damage to the hair cuticles and undo all of the hard work you put into your Japanese straightening.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Finally, avoid using any heat styling tools on your hair for at least the first few weeks after your Japanese straightening treatment. This includes blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. Heat can cause damage to your hair and undo all of the hard work you put into your straightening treatment. If you absolutely must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Tips for Washing Hair After Japanese Straightening

  • Be gentle! Avoid excessive rubbing or tugging on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently. Try to limit your hair washing to every other day, or even every three days, to prevent excessive drying and damage.
  • Invest in a high-quality leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, which can be drying to your hair.
  • Consider investing in a silk or satin pillowcase, which can help to reduce friction and minimize damage to your hair while you sleep.

FAQs

Can I use a regular shampoo after Japanese straightening?

No, it's best to use a sulfate-free shampoo after Japanese straightening to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils and causing damage to the hair cuticles.

How long do I need to wait before washing my hair after Japanese straightening?

You should wait at least 72 hours after Japanese straightening before washing your hair to allow the hair cuticles to fully close and lock in the treatment.

Can I use a hair dryer after Japanese straightening?

Avoid using a hair dryer if possible, as the heat can cause damage to the hair cuticles and undo all of the hard work you put into your Japanese straightening. If you absolutely must use a hair dryer, use it on the cool setting and be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Hair Care After Japanese Straightening

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the health and beauty of your hair after Japanese straightening. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid excessive heat styling
  • Use a high-quality leave-in conditioner
  • Avoid products containing alcohol
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase

By following these tips and using the right hair care products, you can keep your hair looking sleek, shiny, and healthy after Japanese straightening.
