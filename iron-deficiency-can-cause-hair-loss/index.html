Iron Deficiency Can Cause Hair Loss What You Need To Know

Written by Daniel Nov 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Iron is an essential mineral that aids in the formation of hemoglobin, a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout the body. Without enough iron, your body can't produce enough hemoglobin, leading to iron deficiency anemia. While most people know that iron deficiency can cause fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath, many aren't aware that it can also result in hair loss.

How Iron Deficiency Causes Hair Loss

When your body experiences iron deficiency, it prioritizes oxygen delivery to essential organs like the heart and brain, leaving less oxygen available for other functions, including hair growth. Without adequate oxygen, hair follicles can become weak and brittle, leading to hair loss.

Iron is also necessary for the production of collagen, a protein that helps to strengthen hair follicles and promote hair growth. Without enough iron, collagen production may decrease, leading to weak hair that is prone to breakage and hair loss.

How to Know If You Have Iron Deficiency

Iron deficiency anemia is a common condition, affecting around 1 in 5 women and 1 in 10 men worldwide. Symptoms of iron deficiency anemia include:

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Headaches
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Pale skin
  • Brittle nails
  • Craving for non-food items like dirt or ice

If you are experiencing any of these symptoms, it's important to see your doctor for a blood test to check your iron levels.

How to Treat Iron Deficiency Hair Loss

The good news is that iron deficiency hair loss is reversible with proper treatment. If you are diagnosed with iron deficiency anemia, your doctor may recommend iron supplements to boost your iron levels. You may also be advised to increase your intake of iron-rich foods, such as:

  • Red meat
  • Poultry and fish
  • Beans and lentils
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Leafy greens like spinach and kale
  • Iron-fortified cereals and breads

It's important to note that taking too much iron can be harmful, so it's best to follow your doctor's recommendations for supplementation and dietary changes.

Preventing Iron Deficiency Hair Loss

The best way to prevent iron deficiency hair loss is to maintain adequate iron levels through a healthy diet and appropriate supplementation if needed. Eating a varied diet that includes iron-rich foods and taking a daily multivitamin can help ensure that you are getting enough iron to support healthy hair growth.

If you are at risk for iron deficiency anemia, such as women who experience heavy menstrual bleeding or people with gastrointestinal disorders that affect iron absorption, it's important to work with your doctor to monitor your iron levels and prevent deficiencies before they lead to hair loss.

Final Thoughts

If you are experiencing hair loss, iron deficiency may be the culprit. By maintaining adequate iron levels through a healthy diet and supplementation if needed, you can support healthy hair growth and prevent iron deficiency anemia from affecting your overall health and well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can iron supplements cause hair loss?

No, iron supplements are actually used to treat hair loss caused by iron deficiency anemia. However, taking too much iron can be harmful, so it's important to follow your doctor's recommendations for supplementation.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after iron deficiency?

It can take several months for hair to grow back after iron deficiency anemia is treated, as hair grows slowly at a rate of about half an inch per month. However, with proper treatment and maintenance of iron levels, hair should eventually return to its normal growth pattern.

Can iron deficiency cause permanent hair loss?

If left untreated for a long period of time, severe iron deficiency anemia can lead to permanent hair loss. However, with proper treatment and maintenance of iron levels, most cases of iron deficiency hair loss are reversible.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair Growth

While iron is important for healthy hair growth, there are other things you can do to promote strong, healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments like relaxers and perms
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle wet hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Get plenty of rest and manage stress levels

By taking care of your hair and maintaining healthy iron levels, you can promote strong, healthy hair growth and prevent iron deficiency hair loss.
