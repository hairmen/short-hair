Why Isn t The Top Layer Of My Hair Curly

Written by Oliver Sep 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of those people who have curly hair, but the top layer remains stubbornly straight? This can be frustrating, especially if you want your hair to have a uniform look. In this blog post, we will explore the reasons why the top layer of your hair might not be curly and what you can do to change that.

Description

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can be challenging to manage. If you have curly hair, you might have noticed that the top layer is straight while the rest of your hair is curly. This can be due to several reasons, such as genetics, hair care practices, or heat damage.

How to

If you want to make the top layer of your hair curly, there are several things you can try:

1. Use a diffuser

A diffuser is an attachment that you can use with your hairdryer to enhance your natural curls. It works by spreading the heat evenly, so your curls get a uniform look. To use a diffuser, apply a styling product to your hair, then flip your head over and diffuse your hair from the bottom up.

2. Change your hair care routine

If you are not already doing so, try using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it dry and frizzy. Also, avoid using hot tools on your hair regularly and let your hair air-dry as much as possible.

3. Get a haircut

If your hair is damaged, you might need to get a haircut to remove the split ends. Split ends can prevent your hair from curling correctly, so trimming them can make a significant difference.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to make the top layer of your hair curly:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Gently massage your scalp to stimulate blood flow and remove any buildup.

Step 2: Apply a styling product

Apply a styling product to your hair, such as a curl cream or gel. Make sure to distribute it evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Flip your head over

Flip your head over, so your hair is hanging down. This will help you get more volume and encourage your curls to form.

Step 4: Diffuse your hair

Attach a diffuser to your hairdryer and diffuse your hair from the bottom up. Use a low heat setting and don't touch your hair while you're diffusing it.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

Once your hair is dry, finish with a light hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you make the top layer of your hair curly:

1. Don't over-manipulate your hair

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz and disrupt your curls. Try to avoid running your fingers through your hair or brushing it too often.

2. Use a satin pillowcase

Satin pillowcases are gentle on your hair and can prevent breakage. They also help your hair retain moisture, which is essential for maintaining healthy curls.

3. Protect your hair from heat

If you must use hot tools on your hair, make sure to use a heat protectant spray first. This will help prevent heat damage and keep your curls looking healthy.

FAQ

Q: Why isn't the top layer of my hair curly?

A: The top layer of your hair might not be curly due to genetics, hair care practices, or heat damage.

Q: Can I make the top layer of my hair curly?

A: Yes, you can make the top layer of your hair curly by using a diffuser, changing your hair care routine, or getting a haircut.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Most people with curly hair can go 2-3 days between washes, but some might need to wash their hair every day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Hair Care

It's essential to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy, curly hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Avoid shampoos that contain sulfates and other harsh ingredients. Instead, look for a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Condition your hair regularly

Conditioning your hair is essential for keeping it hydrated and healthy. Use a conditioner that is formulated for curly hair and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Deep condition once a week

Deep conditioning can help repair damaged hair and restore moisture. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

The sun can damage your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from harmful UV rays.

5. Trim your hair regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

In conclusion, the top layer of your hair might not be curly due to several reasons, but there are things you can do to change that. By using a diffuser, changing your hair care routine, or getting a haircut, you can make the top layer of your hair curly and achieve a uniform look. Remember to take good care of your hair by using gentle products, conditioning regularly, and protecting it from the sun.
