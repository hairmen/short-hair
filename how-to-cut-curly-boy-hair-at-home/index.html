How To Cut Curly Boy Hair At Home A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 08, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have a curly-haired little boy, you know that regular haircuts are a necessity to keep those tresses under control. However, taking your child to the salon every few weeks can be expensive and time-consuming. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut curly boy hair at home, so you can save money and keep your child looking sharp.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Curly Boy Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Boy Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Curly Boys

How to Cut Curly Boy Hair at Home

Cutting curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it’s actually quite simple. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Before you begin, make sure your child’s hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and it’s harder to see the natural shape of the curls when they’re wet.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your curly boy’s hair:

  1. Start at the back of the head. Use the comb to part the hair down the middle, from the nape of the neck to the crown of the head.

  2. Take a small section of hair from one side of the part. Hold it between your fingers and trim the ends with the scissors. You can use your child’s previous haircut as a guide for length, or simply trim off the split ends.

  3. Repeat on the other side of the part.

  4. Continue working your way up the back of the head, taking small sections of hair and trimming the ends.

  5. Once you reach the crown of the head, move to the sides. Use the comb to separate a section of hair at the temple, then hold it between your fingers and trim the ends.

  6. Continue working your way around the head, taking small sections of hair and trimming the ends.

  7. Once you’ve finished the sides, move to the top of the head. Use the comb to create a part down the middle, then work your way outwards, trimming the ends of each section.

  8. Finally, trim any stray hairs around the ears and neckline.

  9. Use the comb to check for any uneven spots, and make any necessary adjustments.

  10. Finish by styling your child’s hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Curly Boy Hair

Here are a few tips to make the process of cutting curly hair at home easier:

  • Start with small snips. It’s easier to take off more hair later than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Don’t pull the hair too tightly. Curly hair can spring up when it’s released, which can lead to uneven cuts.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from different angles.
  • If your child has very tight curls, consider leaving the hair a bit longer than you normally would. Tight curls can appear shorter than they actually are, and you don’t want to accidentally give your child a buzz cut.

FAQ

Here are a few common questions about cutting curly boy hair:

How often should I cut my child’s hair?

It depends on how quickly your child’s hair grows and how long you want the style to last. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good timeframe for a trim.

What if I mess up?

Don’t panic! Hair grows back, and there are always ways to fix a bad haircut. If you’re really unhappy with the results, take your child to a professional stylist to have it fixed.

Can I use clippers to cut curly hair?

Clippers can be used to trim the neckline and around the ears, but it’s not recommended to use them to cut the rest of the hair. Clippers can create blunt, choppy lines that don’t work well with curly hair.

Hair Care for Curly Boys

Once you’ve cut your child’s hair, it’s important to take care of those curly locks to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner made specifically for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it’s dry. Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle when it’s wet.
  • Don’t rub the hair dry with a towel. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water and let it air dry.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to keep those curls looking bouncy and defined.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your curly boy’s hair at home and keep him looking stylish and well-groomed.
