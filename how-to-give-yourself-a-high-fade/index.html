How To Give Yourself A High Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the barbershop every week for a simple high fade? With the right tools and techniques, it's possible to give yourself a high fade at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the step-by-step process of achieving a perfect high fade with relaxed English language.

Description

A high fade is a trendy and stylish haircut that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back while leaving the hair on top longer. The high fade is a versatile haircut that can be worn in various styles, including a slick back, a side part, a messy top, or a pompadour.

While getting a high fade at the barbershop is convenient, it can be expensive in the long run. By learning how to give yourself a high fade, you can save money and achieve the desired look at home. All you need is the right equipment, some patience, and a steady hand.

How to Give Yourself a High Fade

The key to giving yourself a high fade is to use the right tools and follow a precise technique. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers with different guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair gel or pomade
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before starting the haircut, make sure to wash your hair and dry it completely. This will make it easier to cut and style.

Step 2: Choose the Right Guard Size

Start by deciding on the length of the fade. For a high fade, you'll want to use a shorter guard size on the sides and back and gradually increase the length as you move towards the top. A good starting point is a #2 or #3 guard size.

Step 3: Begin Cutting the Sides and Back

Attach the desired guard size to your clippers and start cutting the hair on the sides and back. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using a scooping motion. Make sure to blend the hair evenly, and don't forget to trim the hair around the ears and neckline.

Step 4: Switch to a Higher Guard Size

Once you've finished cutting the sides and back, switch to a higher guard size and blend the hairline. Use a scooping motion to blend the hairline evenly, starting from the bottom and moving up.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Using scissors, cut the hair on top to the desired length. You can use a comb to section the hair and make sure it's even. If you're going for a messy look, you can use your fingers to style the hair.

Step 6: Blend the Sides and Top

Using a shorter guard size, blend the hair on the sides with the hair on top. Make sure to blend the hair evenly, using a scooping motion. You can switch to a higher guard size as you move towards the top.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Using a trimmer or razor, clean up the edges around the ears and neckline. Make sure the lines are straight and even.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade to your hair and style it as desired. You can slick it back, create a side part, or leave it messy.

Step 9: Check Your Work

Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure everything is even. Make any necessary adjustments to achieve the desired look.

Step 10: Clean Up

Once you're done, clean up your workspace and tools. Make sure to sanitize your clippers and scissors before storing them away.

Tips for a Perfect High Fade

  • Start with a longer guard size and gradually decrease the length to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a scooping motion when cutting the hair to blend it evenly.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Use a mirror to check your work and make sure everything is even.
  • Experiment with different styles and lengths to find the perfect high fade for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I give myself a high fade if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still give yourself a high fade if you have curly hair. Make sure to use a guard size that's suitable for your hair texture and take your time when cutting the hair.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always adjust the length or blend the hair to fix the mistake. If you're unsure, it's best to consult a professional barber.

Hair Care After a High Fade

After getting a high fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Apply a light hair oil or serum to add shine and prevent frizz.
  • Visit a barber every 2-3 weeks to maintain the length and shape of your fade.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can give yourself a perfect high fade at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll achieve a stylish and trendy haircut in no time.
