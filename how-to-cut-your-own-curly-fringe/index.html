How To Cut Your Own Curly Fringe A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of paying salon prices for a simple haircut? Do you have curly hair and struggle to find a stylist who knows how to cut it properly? Look no further! In this article, we will guide you through how to cut your own curly fringe, saving you time and money. Let's get started!

Description

A curly fringe, also known as bangs, is a trendy and stylish way to frame your face. It can be difficult to cut curly hair, but with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve a salon-worthy cut at home. Whether you want a dramatic chop or just a subtle trim, this guide will teach you how to cut your own curly fringe.

How to Cut Your Own Curly Fringe

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to hold back the rest of your hair.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Determine how long you want your fringe to be. If you're unsure, start with a longer length and trim gradually. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't add hair back!

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section off the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape. The point of the triangle should be at the center of your forehead, and the base should be at the top of your ears. Clip back the remaining hair.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Using your scissors, begin cutting your hair. Cut vertically into the hair, rather than horizontally, to create a softer look. Cut small sections at a time and remember to keep the scissors angled downwards to create a layered effect.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After every few snips, release the hair and check your progress in the mirror. Make sure both sides are even and adjust as necessary.

Step 6: Finish the Cut

Once you've achieved your desired length, use your scissors to add texture and movement to your fringe. This will help prevent your hair from looking too blunt or heavy.

Tips

- Always cut less than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more if necessary.

- Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.

- Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my curly fringe?

A: It's recommended to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh and healthy.

Q: Can I cut my curly fringe when it's wet?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Hair Care

After cutting your curly fringe, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance your curls and prevent frizz. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.

Congratulations, you've learned how to cut your own curly fringe! With a little practice and patience, you'll be able to achieve the perfect cut every time. Enjoy your new look!
