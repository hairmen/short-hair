How To Dread Short Hair White Guy A Step By Step Guide

If you're a white guy with short hair and you've been wanting to get dreadlocks, you might be wondering if it's even possible. The good news is that it is! In this article, we'll walk you through how to dread short hair as a white guy. We'll cover everything from how to start your dreads to how to care for them, so you can rock your new hairstyle with confidence.

  • How to Dread Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Dread Short Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that dreading short hair can take some time and patience. Depending on your hair texture, it might take several months to a year for your dreads to fully develop. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long to start dreading.
  • You'll need to wash your hair less frequently once you start dreading, as overwashing can cause your dreads to unravel.
  • You'll need to be gentle with your hair, as rough handling can cause breakage and damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into small squares or rectangles. You can use a comb or your fingers to do this. The size of the sections will determine the size of your dreads, so choose accordingly.

Step 2: Backcomb Your Hair

Next, use a backcombing technique to create knots in each section. This involves combing your hair backwards towards your scalp, which will create tangles and knots. Use a dread comb or a regular comb with small teeth to do this.

Step 3: Twist Your Hair

Once you've backcombed each section, twist the hair in the same direction until it starts to coil on itself. This will help to tighten the knots and create dreads.

Step 4: Secure Your Dreads

Use rubber bands or hair ties to secure the ends of each dread. This will help to keep the coils in place while your hair locks up.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreads

As your dreads start to mature, you'll need to maintain them to keep them healthy and looking their best. This will involve regular washing and conditioning, as well as palm rolling and re-twisting as needed.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are some tips to help you care for your dreads:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup and keep your dreads clean.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils, as these can cause your dreads to become greasy and attract dirt.
  • Palm roll your dreads regularly to help them tighten up and maintain their shape.
  • Re-twist any loose strands or sections as needed to keep your dreads looking neat.

FAQs

Can I still wash my hair with dreads?

Yes, you can still wash your hair with dreads, but you'll need to be careful not to overwash. Use a residue-free shampoo and wash your hair once or twice a week, depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

Will dreads work on all hair types?

Dreads can work on most hair types, but they may take longer to form on straight or fine hair. If you have straight hair, you may need to use a different technique, such as twist and rip, to create your dreads.

How long will it take for my dreads to form?

The time it takes for your dreads to form will depend on your hair type and the method you use. On average, it can take anywhere from several months to a year for your dreads to fully mature.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you have your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are some hair care tips for dreads:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your dreads.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid snagging and pulling on your dreads while you sleep.
  • Avoid swimming in chlorinated water, as this can cause your dreads to become brittle and break.
  • Be gentle when washing and conditioning your dreads, and avoid scrubbing or twisting them too vigorously.

With these tips and techniques, you can successfully dread short hair as a white guy. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and take good care of your dreads to keep them looking their best.
