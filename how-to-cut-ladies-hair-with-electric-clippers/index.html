How To Cut Ladies Hair With Electric Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to cut your own or someone else's hair at home, using electric clippers can be a quick and easy way to achieve a professional-looking cut. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut ladies hair with electric clippers, from choosing the right tools to finishing touches.

How to Cut Ladies Hair with Electric Clippers

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

Before you begin cutting, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools. Look for clippers that are specifically designed for women's hair, as they tend to be lighter and easier to handle than men's clippers. Also, consider the length of the blade â shorter blades are better for precise cuts, while longer blades are better for longer hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure the hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the natural flow of the hair and ensure a more even cut. If the hair is particularly tangled, you may want to use a detangling spray or conditioner to make it easier to work with.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using hair clips or elastics. Start by sectioning off the top of the head, leaving the sides and back loose. This will allow you to focus on one section at a time and ensure a more even cut.

Step 4: Start at the Bottom

Using the clippers with the appropriate blade length, start at the bottom of the hair and work your way up. Use a comb to guide the clippers and ensure an even cut. Don't try to cut too much hair at once â it's better to take small sections and work slowly to avoid any mistakes.

Step 5: Move to the Sides and Back

Once you've finished the bottom section, release the next section and repeat the process. Work your way around the sides and back of the head, taking your time to ensure an even cut. Don't forget to blend the sections together for a seamless finish.

Step 6: Trim the Bangs

If the hair has bangs, trim them using scissors or a smaller blade length on the clippers. Start by cutting them longer than you want them to be, and then gradually trim them to the desired length. Remember to cut them straight across for a classic look, or at an angle for a more modern style.

Step 7: Check for Any Uneven Spots

Once you've finished cutting the hair, take a step back and check for any uneven spots. Use the clippers or scissors to make any necessary adjustments, taking care not to overdo it and create any bald spots.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Once you're happy with the cut, style the hair as desired using a blow dryer, straightener, or curling iron. Use a little bit of product to keep the hair in place, such as hairspray, mousse or gel.

Tips for Cutting Ladies Hair with Electric Clippers

  • Always start with a longer blade length and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb to guide the clippers and ensure an even cut.
  • Take your time and work slowly to avoid any mistakes.
  • Check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.

FAQs about Cutting Ladies Hair with Electric Clippers

Can I cut my own hair with electric clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with electric clippers. Just make sure you have the right tools and take your time to avoid any mistakes.

What blade length should I use for short hair?

For short hair, a blade length of 1/8 inch to 1/4 inch is recommended. This will give you a close cut without going too short.

What blade length should I use for long hair?

For long hair, a blade length of 1/2 inch to 1 inch is recommended. This will give you a longer cut without sacrificing length.

Hair Care After Cutting Ladies Hair with Electric Clippers

After cutting your hair with electric clippers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair moisturized, and avoid using too much heat when styling to prevent damage. Regular trims every 6-8 weeks will also help maintain the shape and style of your cut.
