Why Does My Hair Always Go Down

Written by Luffy Jul 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your hair constantly falling flat and losing its volume? Do you feel like no matter what you do, your hair always ends up looking lifeless and limp? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with hair that won't hold a style or maintain its volume. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why your hair always goes down and provide tips and tricks for maintaining volume and lift.

  • Reasons Why Your Hair Always Goes Down
  • How to Maintain Volume in Your Hair

Reasons Why Your Hair Always Goes Down

There are several reasons why your hair may be falling flat and losing its volume.

1. Genetics

Unfortunately, some people are just born with hair that is naturally thin and flat. If your hair is genetically predisposed to being limp, it may be difficult to achieve volume and lift.

2. Product Buildup

Using too many hair products or failing to properly wash them out can leave a buildup on your hair, making it heavy and difficult to style. This can cause your hair to fall flat and lose its volume.

3. Heat Damage

Using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can damage your hair, causing it to become brittle and more prone to breakage. This can lead to thinning and flatness over time.

4. Overwashing

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and lacking in volume. Additionally, using hot water can open up the hair cuticle, making it more difficult to achieve lift and body.

How to Maintain Volume in Your Hair

If you're struggling to maintain volume and lift in your hair, there are a few things you can do to help.

1. Use Lightweight Products

When it comes to styling products, less is often more. Look for lightweight products that won't weigh your hair down, like mousse or sea salt spray. Avoid heavy creams or oils that can make your hair look greasy and flat.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Drying your hair upside down can help to create lift and volume at the roots. Use a round brush to create tension and lift as you blow dry.

3. Sleep with Your Hair in a High Bun

Sleeping with your hair in a high bun can help to maintain volume and lift overnight. Just be sure to use a scrunchie or other soft hair tie to avoid creating creases in your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Achieving Volume

If you're looking for a step-by-step guide to achieving volume and lift in your hair, follow these simple steps:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Begin by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid product buildup.

2. Apply Styling Product

Apply a lightweight styling product, like mousse or sea salt spray, to your hair. Be sure to distribute evenly throughout your hair.

3. Blow Dry Upside Down

Flip your head upside down and blow dry your hair, using a round brush to create lift and tension at the roots.

4. Finish with Hairspray

Once your hair is dry, finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips and Tricks for Lifting Your Hair

Here are a few additional tips and tricks for lifting your hair:

1. Tease at the Roots

Teasing your hair at the roots can help to create lift and volume. Just be sure to use a comb or brush to smooth the top layer of hair.

2. Use a Root Lifter

A root lifter product can help to create lift and volume at the roots of your hair. Apply it directly to your roots before blow drying.

3. Try Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can help to absorb excess oil and add texture to your hair, making it easier to style and lift.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I achieve volume in my hair if it's naturally thin?

Yes, you can still achieve volume in thin hair. Look for volumizing products and try blow drying your hair upside down to create lift at the roots.

2. How often should I wash my hair?

It's best to wash your hair every two to three days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

3. Can heat styling tools damage my hair?

Yes, using heat styling tools too frequently can lead to heat damage and breakage. Be sure to use a heat protectant product and avoid using high heat settings.

Hair Care for Volume and Lift

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining volume and lift in your hair. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for volumizing and adding lift to your hair.

2. Avoid Overwashing

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and lacking in volume. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days.

3. Use a Hair Mask

Using a hair mask once a week can help to nourish and strengthen your hair, making it easier to style and maintain volume.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve volume and lift in your hair and say goodbye to flat, lifeless locks. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid damaging it with excessive heat styling or product buildup.
