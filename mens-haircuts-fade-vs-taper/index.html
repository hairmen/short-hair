Mens Haircuts Fade Vs Taper What s The Difference

Written by Frank Oct 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to mens haircuts, there are many different styles to choose from. Two of the most popular styles are the fade and the taper. While they may sound similar, these two haircuts have distinct differences that can make a big impact on your overall look. In this article, we'll break down the differences between a fade and a taper, and give you some tips on how to choose the right one for you.

Description

So, what exactly is the difference between a fade and a taper? The main difference is in how the hair is cut on the sides and back of the head. With a fade, the hair gradually gets shorter as it goes down towards the neckline. This creates a faded, or "blended" look. With a taper, the hair is cut to a shorter length at the neckline, and gradually gets longer as it goes up towards the top of the head. This creates a tapered, or "layered" look.

Another key difference between these two haircuts is the length of the hair on top. With a fade, the hair on top can be any length, from very short to quite long. With a taper, the hair on top is usually kept relatively short, and is styled with a bit of volume on the crown of the head.

How to Get a Fade or Taper Haircut

If you're interested in getting a fade or a taper haircut, there are a few things to consider. First, think about the length and style of your hair. Do you want to keep your hair short on top, or do you prefer a longer style? Are you looking for a dramatic, high-contrast fade, or a more subtle, gradual fade?

Next, consider your face shape and hair type. A fade or taper can look great on almost any face shape, but it's important to choose a style that flatters your features. If you have a round face, for example, a high-contrast fade with a longer top can help elongate your face. If you have thin or fine hair, a taper can add volume and texture.

Finally, choose a skilled barber or hairstylist who can help you achieve the look you want. Look for someone who has experience with fades and tapers, and who can give you personalized advice based on your hair type and face shape.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Fade or Taper Haircut

Here's a basic step-by-step guide to getting a fade or taper haircut:

  1. Choose the length and style of your hair on top.
  2. Choose the length of the hair on the sides and back of your head.
  3. Tell your barber or hairstylist which style you want (fade or taper), and how dramatic you want the transition to be.
  4. Relax and enjoy the haircut!

Tips for Maintaining Your Fade or Taper Haircut

Once you've got your new fade or taper haircut, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Visit your barber or hairstylist regularly for touch-ups.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Style your hair with a quality hair product, such as a pomade or wax, for a polished look.
  • Consider using a hair trimmer to keep the sides and back of your head looking neat between appointments.

FAQs About Mens Haircuts Fade vs Taper

Q: Which is better, a fade or a taper?

A: There's no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, as the best haircut for you will depend on your personal style, face shape, and hair type. Both fades and tapers can look great when done well, so it's a matter of personal preference.

Q: How often do I need to get a fade or taper haircut?

A: This will depend on how quickly your hair grows, but most people will need to get a touch-up every 4-6 weeks to keep their haircut looking sharp.

Q: Can I get a fade or taper with curly hair?

A: Absolutely! Fades and tapers can look great on all hair types, including curly hair. Just be sure to choose a barber or hairstylist who has experience working with curly hair.

Hair Care Tips for Men with Faded or Tapered Haircuts

If you have a fade or taper haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are a few hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as blow dryers or straighteners, too frequently, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to give your hair a deep conditioning treatment.

With these tips and tricks, you can keep your fade or taper haircut looking sharp and stylish. Whether you prefer a dramatic fade or a subtle taper, these haircuts are a great way to update your look and stay on-trend.
