How To Cut Little Boy Hair Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the salon for your little boy's haircut? Do you want to try cutting your little boy's hair at home with scissors? Cutting little boy hair scissors is not as difficult as it seems. With the right tools and techniques, you can give your little boy a fresh new haircut without leaving the comfort of your home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting little boy hair scissors, from preparation to aftercare.

How to Prepare for Cutting Little Boy Hair Scissors

Before you start cutting little boy hair scissors, there are some preparations you need to make. Here are the things you need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Hair clips (optional)

Once you have all the tools, you need to prepare your little boy for the haircut. Here are the steps:

  1. Wash your little boy's hair and dry it with a towel.
  2. Put the haircutting cape or towel around your little boy's neck to protect their clothes from hair.
  3. Use hair clips to keep the hair out of your little boy's face.
  4. Use a spray bottle to wet the hair. Wet hair is easier to cut and style.

How to Cut Little Boy Hair Scissors - Step by Step

Now that you have prepared your little boy for the haircut, it's time to start cutting. Here are the steps to follow to cut little boy hair scissors:

  1. Start by combing the hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Decide on the length of the hair you want to cut. You can use clippers to trim the sides and back, leaving the top longer for styling.
  3. Use your fingers or a comb to hold the hair and cut it with scissors. Start at the back and work your way towards the front.
  4. Take small sections of hair and cut them one at a time.
  5. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle for a layered look.
  6. Check the length of the hair as you go to ensure it's even.
  7. Trim the hair around the ears and neckline.
  8. Style the hair using a comb or your fingers.
  9. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs.
  10. Remove the haircutting cape or towel and shake off any loose hair.

Tips for Cutting Little Boy Hair Scissors

Cutting little boy hair scissors can be challenging, especially if your little boy is moving around. Here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Use a low stool or chair to make it easier to reach your little boy's head.
  • Use a comb to hold the hair in place while cutting.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure even length.
  • Take breaks if your little boy gets restless or uncomfortable.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the head.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting little boy hair scissors:

How often should I cut my little boy's hair?

It depends on how fast their hair grows. On average, you should cut your little boy's hair every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim the hair shorter or fix it during the next haircut.

Should I use clippers or scissors?

It depends on the style you want to achieve. Clippers are great for short haircuts, while scissors are better for longer hair.

Hair Care after Cutting Little Boy Hair Scissors

After cutting little boy hair scissors, it's important to take care of the hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Shampoo and condition the hair regularly.
  • Use a comb or brush to style the hair.
  • Trim any stray hairs to maintain the style.
  • Use a hair product, such as gel or pomade, to style the hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, on little boy's hair.

Cutting little boy hair scissors may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, it's a simple process. Follow our step-by-step guide, and you'll be able to give your little boy a fresh new haircut in no time.
