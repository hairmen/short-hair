How To Take Care Of Frizzy Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 27, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have frizzy curly hair, you know how frustrating it can be to manage. Frizz can make your hair unruly and difficult to style, and curly hair can easily become dry and brittle. But fear not! With the right hair care routine, you can keep your frizzy curly hair looking healthy, shiny, and beautiful. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to take care of frizzy curly hair, along with some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

How to Take Care of Frizzy Curly Hair

The key to managing frizzy curly hair is to keep it moisturized and nourished. Curly hair is naturally drier than straight hair, so it needs extra hydration to keep it healthy. Here are some things you can do to take care of your frizzy curly hair:

1. Shampoo less often

Shampooing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Instead, try to shampoo your hair every other day or every few days. You can use a dry shampoo on the days you don't shampoo to keep your hair looking fresh.

2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates are harsh detergents that can further strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for a shampoo that is sulfate-free and formulated for curly hair.

3. Condition your hair regularly

Conditioning your hair is essential for keeping it moisturized and healthy. Use a conditioner that is specifically formulated for curly hair, and apply it generously after shampooing. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

4. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can provide extra moisture and help to tame frizz. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair after showering, and comb it through with your fingers.

5. Don't towel-dry your hair

Towel-drying your hair can cause friction and lead to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze the water out of your hair. Don't rub your hair vigorously with the towel.

6. Use a diffuser when blow-drying

Blow-drying your hair can also cause frizz if you don't use the right technique. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair gently and evenly. Hold the diffuser close to your scalp and move it around in circular motions to lift your curls and add volume.

7. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and opt for heatless styling methods like braids and twists.

8. Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. If you're going to be spending time in the sun, wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF.

9. Get regular trims

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, or whenever you notice your ends starting to look dry and frayed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to taking care of your frizzy curly hair:

Step 1: Shampoo your hair every other day or every few days using a sulfate-free shampoo.

Step 2: Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair after shampooing, and leave it in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after showering.

Step 4: Gently squeeze the water out of your hair using a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.

Step 5: Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair gently and evenly.

Step 6: Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.

Step 7: Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

Step 8: Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Tips for Managing Frizzy Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing your frizzy curly hair:

1. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can reduce friction and prevent frizz. Silk also helps to keep your hair hydrated, so you wake up with softer, smoother curls.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair than a brush, and it can help to detangle your curls without causing breakage or frizz.

3. Experiment with different hairstyles

There are many different hairstyles that work well for frizzy curly hair, from braids and twists to buns and ponytails. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

4. Use a hair mask once a week

A hair mask can provide extra nourishment and hydration for your hair. Use a hair mask once a week to keep your curls looking healthy and shiny.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my frizzy curly hair?

A: You should shampoo your hair every other day or every few days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my frizzy curly hair?

A: It's best to use products that are specifically formulated for curly hair, as these products are designed to provide extra moisture and nourishment.

Q: How can I reduce frizz in my curly hair?

A: You can reduce frizz in your curly hair by using a sulfate-free shampoo, conditioning regularly, using a leave-in conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and protecting your hair from the sun.

Q: Can I brush my curly hair?

A: It's best to use a wide-tooth comb on your curly hair to avoid causing breakage and frizz.
