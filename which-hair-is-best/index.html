Which Hair Is Best A Comprehensive Guide To Relaxed Hair

Written by Eveline Jun 27, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of dealing with unruly and unmanageable hair? Do you want to achieve a sleek and polished look? If so, relaxers may be the solution for you. But which hair is best for a relaxed style? In this article, we will explore the different types of hair and provide tips for achieving the perfect relaxed look.

Table of Contents

  • Types of Hair
  • How to Relax Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Relaxing Your Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Relaxed Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Relaxed Hair

Types of Hair

Before deciding which hair is best for a relaxed style, it is important to understand the different types of hair. There are three main types of hair: straight, wavy, and curly.

Straight hair is the easiest type to relax, as it requires the least amount of processing. Wavy hair can also be relaxed, but it may require a bit more processing time. Curly hair, on the other hand, can be difficult to relax and may require multiple treatments to achieve the desired effect.

It is important to note that not all hair types are suitable for relaxing. If your hair is damaged or overly processed, it may not be able to handle the chemicals in a relaxer. Additionally, if your hair is already thin or fine, relaxing it may cause breakage or damage.

How to Relax Your Hair

Relaxing your hair involves using chemicals to break down the protein bonds that give your hair its natural texture. While this process can be done at home, it is important to follow the instructions carefully and use caution to avoid damaging your hair or scalp.

The first step in relaxing your hair is to choose the right product. There are many different types of relaxers available, including lye and no-lye options. Lye relaxers are more harsh and can cause more damage to your hair, while no-lye relaxers are gentler but may not be as effective.

Step-by-Step Guide to Relaxing Your Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before applying the relaxer, you should prepare your hair by washing it and detangling it thoroughly. You should also apply a protective cream or oil to your scalp and hairline to prevent irritation or burns.

Step 2: Mix the Relaxer

Follow the instructions on the relaxer kit to mix the chemicals together. Be sure to wear gloves and avoid getting the mixture on your skin or eyes.

Step 3: Apply the Relaxer

Starting at the back of your head, apply the relaxer to your hair, working from the roots to the tips. Be sure to apply the relaxer evenly and avoid overlapping onto previously treated hair.

Step 4: Rinse and Neutralize

After the recommended processing time, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water to remove the relaxer. Then, apply a neutralizing shampoo to stop the chemical process and restore your hair's pH balance.

Step 5: Condition and Style

Once you have neutralized your hair, apply a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Then, style your hair as desired, using heat protectant products to avoid damage from hot tools.

Tips for Maintaining Relaxed Hair

Now that you have achieved the perfect relaxed look, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and beauty. Here are some tips for maintaining relaxed hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair's natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I relax my hair?

A: It is generally recommended to wait at least 6-8 weeks between relaxer treatments to avoid over-processing your hair. However, the frequency of your treatments will depend on your hair type and the strength of the relaxer.

Q: Can I relax my hair if it is already colored or highlighted?

A: Yes, but you should be careful to avoid overlapping the relaxer onto previously treated hair to avoid damage or breakage.

Hair Care for Relaxed Hair

Finally, it is important to take care of your hair to keep it looking and feeling its best. Here are some tips for caring for relaxed hair:

  • Wash your hair no more than once a week to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can cause buildup and damage.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using tight hair ties or barrettes, as they can cause breakage and damage.
  • Protect your hair from the elements by wearing a hat or using a scarf.

Now that you know which hair is best for a relaxed style, you can achieve the sleek and polished look you've always wanted. Just remember to follow the instructions carefully and take care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
