Why Is Only The Bottom Part Of My Hair Curly

Written by Petter Jul 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who has noticed that only the lower portion of your hair is curly, you may be wondering why this is happening. It can be frustrating to have hair that isn't uniform in texture, but there are actually a few reasons why this can occur. In this blog post, we'll dive into the science behind this phenomenon and offer some tips on how to style and care for your curly hair.

Table of Contents

What Causes Curly Hair?

Before we get into why only the bottom part of your hair may be curly, let's first discuss what causes curly hair in general. The shape of your hair follicles is what determines whether your hair is straight, wavy, curly, or coily. Follicles that are more oval in shape tend to produce curly hair, while those that are more round produce straight hair.

Additionally, the amount of disulfide bonds in your hair plays a role in its texture. Curly hair has more disulfide bonds than straight hair, which creates more kinks and curls in the hair shaft.

Why is Only the Bottom Part of My Hair Curly?

Now that we know what causes curly hair, let's talk about why only the bottom part of your hair may be curly. There are a few reasons why this can happen:

Hair Damage

If you frequently use heat tools, color your hair, or use chemical treatments like relaxers or perms, you may be damaging the upper portion of your hair. This damage can cause the hair to lose its curl pattern, resulting in straighter hair at the top and curlier hair at the bottom.

Weight

The weight of your hair can also play a role in its texture. If your hair is longer, the weight of the hair at the top may be pulling the curls out, resulting in straighter hair at the top and curlier hair at the bottom.

Natural Variation

Finally, it's important to remember that natural variation in hair texture is completely normal. Even if you haven't experienced hair damage or weight issues, it's possible that your hair simply grows in a way that creates a different texture at the top and bottom.

How to Style Curly Hair

If you're looking to enhance your natural curls, there are a few styling techniques you can try:

Use a Diffuser

A diffuser attachment on your hair dryer can help enhance your curls without creating frizz. Use a low heat setting and scrunch your hair as you dry it to encourage curl formation.

Apply Products

Using products specifically designed for curly hair can help define your curls and reduce frizz. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil.

Avoid Brushing

Brushing curly hair can disrupt the curl pattern and create frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

Tips for Caring for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires some extra TLC to keep it healthy and defined:

Avoid Over-Shampooing

Shampooing too frequently can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can make it dry and frizzy. Try washing your hair every other day or every few days instead.

Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help keep curly hair hydrated and healthy. Look for products that contain ingredients like avocado oil or honey.

Avoid Heat

Excessive heat can damage curly hair and cause it to lose its curl pattern. Try to air dry your hair as much as possible and limit your use of heat tools like flat irons and curling wands.

FAQs

Can I Make the Top Part of My Hair Curly?

If you're looking to enhance the curl pattern at the top of your hair, you can try using a curl-enhancing product and diffusing your hair upside down to encourage volume and curl formation.

Should I Cut My Hair to Make it More Uniform?

If you're unhappy with the variation in your hair texture, you may want to consider getting a haircut that is specifically designed for curly hair. A curly cut can help create a more uniform shape and enhance your natural curls.

Can Curly Hair Become Straight?

If you have naturally curly hair, it's unlikely that it will become completely straight without the use of chemical treatments or heat tools. However, damage to the hair can cause it to lose its curl pattern and become straighter.


Overall, if only the bottom part of your hair is curly, it's important to remember that this is completely normal and can be attributed to a variety of factors. By properly caring for and styling your hair, you can enhance your natural curls and create a look that you love.
