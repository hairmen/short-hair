Why Did My Hair Get Curlier As I Get Older

Written by Bardi Nov 01, 2023 · 4 min read

As we age, our bodies go through various changes, and one of those changes can be in our hair. If you've noticed that your hair has gotten curlier over time, you may be wondering why this is happening. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this phenomenon, and provide tips for taking care of your newly curly locks.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Many people experience changes in their hair as they age. For some, this means that their hair becomes thinner or grayer. For others, it means that their hair becomes curlier. The reason for this change is due to the shape of our hair follicles.

Hair follicles are small sacs located within our skin that produce hair. The shape of these sacs determines the texture of our hair. Straight hair has round hair follicles, while curly hair has oval-shaped hair follicles. As we age, our hair follicles can change shape, becoming more oval-shaped. This change causes hair to become curlier.

How to

If you're experiencing changes in your hair texture, there are a few things you can do to embrace your new curls:

  • Learn how to style curly hair: Curly hair requires a different approach to styling than straight hair. Look up tutorials online, or consider visiting a stylist who specializes in curly hair.
  • Invest in curly hair products: Products specifically designed for curly hair can help enhance your natural curls and keep them looking healthy.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying: A diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help enhance your curls and prevent frizz.

Step by step

If you're new to curly hair, here are some steps to help you embrace your new texture:

  1. Start with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing product to damp hair, such as a curl cream or gel.
  3. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair, or let it air dry.
  4. When your hair is dry, use a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and tame any frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for taking care of curly hair:

  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your curls looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage curly hair.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause hair to become curlier?

A: No, stress does not directly cause hair to become curlier. However, stress can cause hair loss or damage, which can affect the texture of your hair.

Q: Can hormone changes cause hair to become curlier?

A: Yes, changes in hormone levels can affect the shape of your hair follicles, causing your hair to become curlier.

Q: Can medication cause hair to become curlier?

A: Yes, some medications can affect the texture of your hair. Talk to your doctor if you're experiencing changes in your hair texture after starting a new medication.

Hair Care

Regardless of your hair texture, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Protect your hair from heat and sun damage
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful, no matter what its texture may be.
