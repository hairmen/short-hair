How To Cut Back And Sides Of Men s Hair

Written by Luffy Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the barbershop every month to get your hair cut? Why not try cutting it yourself? With a little practice and patience, you can learn how to cut the back and sides of your hair at home. In this post, we will show you the steps to achieve a professional-looking haircut without leaving your house. Let's get started!

How to:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Hair clips
  • Cape or towel

Make sure your clippers have different guard sizes to adjust the length of your hair. It's also important to keep your tools clean and sharp for better results.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, then dry it completely before you start cutting. Wet hair can be harder to cut and may result in uneven lengths. Use a towel to remove excess water and let your hair air-dry or use a blow dryer.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start by separating the top part of your hair from the sides and back. This will make it easier to cut each section without mixing them up.

Step 4: Trim the Sides

Use the clippers with the appropriate guard size to trim the sides of your hair. Start from the bottom and work your way up to the desired length. Use a comb to guide the clippers and make sure the hair is even all around. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the Back

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky. Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head or ask someone to help you. Comb your hair straight down and trim the ends with scissors. Start from the bottom and work your way up, using a comb as a guide. Make sure both sides are even.

Step 6: Blend the Top

Blend the top of your hair with the sides and back using scissors. Comb your hair to the front and trim the ends. Use a point-cutting technique to avoid blunt lines and achieve a natural look. Cut small sections at a time and check the length as you go.

Step 7: Check for Uneven Parts

After cutting your hair, check for any uneven parts or missed spots. Use the clippers or scissors to fix any mistakes and make sure your hair looks even all around.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. Use a hair product of your choice to add texture and hold. You can also use a blow dryer and a brush to create different styles.

Tips:

  • Start with a longer guard size and adjust it as you go to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a cape or towel to prevent hair from sticking to your clothes.
  • Practice on a small section of your hair before cutting the whole head.
  • Don't rush and take your time to achieve the desired look.

FAQ:

Can I cut my hair wet?

No, it's best to cut your hair when it's dry to avoid uneven lengths.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic, mistakes happen. You can use a longer guard size to fix it or go to a professional if needed.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and style, but on average, every 4-6 weeks is recommended.

Hair Care:

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and protect it from the sun and pollution. Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and promote hair growth.

Cutting your hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these steps and tips, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut without leaving your house. Practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to try different styles and techniques.
