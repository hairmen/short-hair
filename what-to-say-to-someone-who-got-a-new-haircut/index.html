What To Say To Someone Who Got A New Haircut

Written by Frank Jul 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Getting a new haircut can be a big deal, especially if it's a drastic change. It's always nice to receive compliments and positive feedback on your new 'do. If you're not sure what to say to someone who just got a new haircut, don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll cover how to compliment someone's new haircut, what to avoid saying, and some tips on how to maintain healthy hair.

Description

Getting a new haircut can be a significant change, and it's always nice to have someone notice and compliment your new look. Knowing what to say to someone who just got a new haircut can be challenging, especially if you're not sure what they're looking for in terms of feedback. In this article, we'll cover some tips and tricks to help you give the best compliments and feedback to someone who just got a new haircut.

How to Compliment Someone's New Haircut

When complimenting someone's new haircut, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Be Specific

Instead of saying, "I like your new haircut," try to be more specific. For example, "Your new haircut really suits your face shape," or "I love the way your new haircut brings out your eyes."

Be Genuine

It's essential to be genuine when complimenting someone's new haircut. If you're not a fan of their new look, it's okay to keep your feedback brief and polite. For example, "Your new haircut looks very trendy," or "Your new haircut is very stylish."

Be Enthusiastic

It's always nice to receive enthusiastic feedback on your new haircut. Try to be positive and upbeat when complimenting someone's new look. For example, "Wow, your new haircut is incredible! I love it!" or "Your new haircut is so cool, I wish I could pull it off."

What to Avoid Saying

When it comes to giving feedback on someone's new haircut, there are a few things you should avoid saying:

Comparing Them to Someone Else

Avoid comparing someone's new haircut to someone else, even if it's meant as a compliment. For example, "Your new haircut reminds me of Jennifer Aniston's hair." This can come across as insincere and diminish the person's unique style.

Being Negative

Avoid being negative or critical of someone's new haircut. If you don't like their new look, it's okay to keep your feedback brief and polite. For example, "Your new haircut is different, but it looks great on you."

Asking if They Like It

Avoid asking if someone likes their new haircut. They may feel pressured to give a positive response, even if they're not entirely satisfied with their new look.

Step by Step

Here are some steps to follow when complimenting someone's new haircut:

Step 1: Observe Their New Look

Take a moment to observe their new haircut and try to find something you genuinely like about it.

Step 2: Be Specific

When complimenting their new look, be specific and try to find something unique and positive to say.

Step 3: Be Enthusiastic

Be positive and enthusiastic when complimenting their new haircut. It will make them feel good and confident about their new look.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

Be Mindful of Their Feelings

Getting a new haircut can be a vulnerable experience. Be mindful of their feelings and try to be supportive and positive.

Ask Questions

Ask questions about their new haircut and show genuine interest. For example, "Who inspired your new look?" or "How did you decide on this new style?"

Encourage Them to Try Something New

If they're hesitant about trying a new hairstyle, encourage them to step out of their comfort zone and try something new. It can be a transformative experience.

FAQ

What if I don't like their new haircut?

If you don't like their new haircut, it's okay to keep your feedback brief and polite. For example, "Your new haircut is different, but it looks great on you."

What if they don't like their new haircut?

If they don't like their new haircut, be supportive and try to find something positive to say. Encourage them to talk to their hairstylist and make any necessary adjustments.

How can I maintain healthy hair?

To maintain healthy hair, make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, use heat protectant when styling with heat tools, and avoid using harsh chemicals and dyes.

Hair Care

Maintaining healthy hair is essential. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair 2-3 times a week to remove dirt and oil buildup.

Use Heat Protectant

Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using any heat tools to prevent heat damage.

Avoid Harsh Chemicals and Dyes

Avoid using harsh chemicals and dyes that can damage your hair. Opt for natural and organic hair care products instead.

Conclusion

Knowing what to say to someone who just got a new haircut can be challenging, but with these tips and tricks, you'll be able to give the best compliments and feedback. Remember to be specific, genuine, and enthusiastic when complimenting their new look. Be mindful of their feelings and encourage them to step out of their comfort zone and try something new. Maintaining healthy hair is essential, so make sure to follow our hair care tips to keep your hair looking its best.
