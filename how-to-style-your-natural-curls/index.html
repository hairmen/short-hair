How To Style Your Natural Curls A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're blessed with natural curls, it's important to know how to style them so they look their best. Whether you have tight ringlets or loose waves, there are plenty of styling options that can help you enhance your curls and keep them looking healthy and vibrant. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to style your natural curls, share tips for maintaining them, and answer some of the most frequently asked questions about curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Your Curls
  • Tips for Maintaining Curls
  • Curly Hair FAQ
  • Hair Care for Curls

How to Style Your Curls

The key to styling natural curls is to embrace their texture and work with it, rather than trying to force them into a style they're not meant for. Here's a step-by-step guide to styling your curls:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Conditioned Hair

The foundation of any good hairstyle is healthy, clean hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair to keep your curls hydrated and defined. Rinse your hair thoroughly and gently towel-dry it, being careful not to rub too hard and cause frizz.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner or Curl Cream

To help define your curls and keep them looking bouncy, apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream throughout your hair. Focus on the ends and mid-shaft of your hair, avoiding the roots. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Use a Diffuser to Dry Your Hair

A diffuser attachment for your blow dryer can be a game-changer for curly hair. It helps distribute heat evenly and reduces frizz while enhancing your curls. Use a low heat setting and scrunch your hair with the diffuser, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 4: Scrunch in a Styling Gel or Mousse

To add even more definition to your curls, apply a styling gel or mousse to your hair. Scrunch it in gently, being careful not to disrupt the curl pattern. You can also use a curling wand or iron to touch up any areas that need more definition.

Step 5: Finish with a Curl-Enhancing Spray or Oil

To keep your curls looking fresh and hydrated throughout the day, finish with a curl-enhancing spray or oil. This will help lock in moisture and add shine to your curls. Use a light hand and focus on the ends and mid-shaft of your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Curls

Now that you know how to style your natural curls, it's important to know how to maintain them. Here are some tips to keep your curls looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your curls.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and disrupt your curl pattern.
  • Protect your curls from sun damage by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

Curly Hair FAQ

Here are some of the most frequently asked questions about curly hair:

What's the best way to detangle curly hair?

The best way to detangle curly hair is to use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Start at the ends and work your way up, gently separating any knots or tangles. Avoid using a brush, as this can disrupt your curl pattern and cause frizz.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It's best to wash curly hair once or twice a week, depending on your hair type and lifestyle. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness, while under-washing can lead to buildup and scalp irritation.

What's the best haircut for curly hair?

The best haircut for curly hair is one that takes your curl pattern and face shape into account. Layers can help enhance your curls and add volume, while a blunt cut can weigh them down. Consult with a stylist who specializes in curly hair to find the best haircut for you.

Hair Care for Curls

Curly hair requires a little extra TLC to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips specifically for curls:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Wrap your hair in a microfiber towel or old t-shirt instead of a regular towel to reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using products with alcohol, as they can dry out your curls.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to style your natural curls with confidence and keep them looking their best. Remember to embrace your curls and work with their unique texture, rather than trying to force them into a style they're not meant for. Happy styling!
