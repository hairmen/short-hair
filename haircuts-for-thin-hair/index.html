Haircuts For Thin Hair How To Get Fuller Thicker Hair

Written by Oliver Aug 30, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have thin hair, it can be frustrating to find the right haircut that doesn’t make your hair look even thinner. But don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this article, we’ll share with you the best haircuts for thin hair that will help you achieve fuller and thicker hair. We’ll also provide you with some tips and tricks on how to take care of your thin hair.

  • How to Choose the Right Haircut for Thin Hair

  • Tips and Tricks for Taking Care of Thin Hair

How to Choose the Right Haircut for Thin Hair

When it comes to choosing the right haircut for thin hair, it’s important to keep in mind that the goal is to make your hair look fuller and thicker. Here are some things to consider:

Length

The length of your hair can have a big impact on how thin or full it looks. Generally, shorter hairstyles tend to make hair look fuller, while longer styles can weigh hair down and make it look thinner. However, if you prefer longer hair, there are some styles that can work well for thin hair, such as long layers or a blunt cut with face-framing layers.

Layers

Layering can add volume and texture to thin hair, but it’s important to choose the right type of layers. Avoid choppy or blunt layers, as they can make thin hair look even thinner. Instead, opt for long, subtle layers that blend seamlessly into the rest of your hair.

Bangs

Bangs can work well for thin hair, but it’s important to choose the right type of bangs. Avoid heavy or blunt bangs, as they can make hair look thinner. Instead, opt for wispy or side-swept bangs that add texture and volume.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Haircut for Thin Hair

Now that you know what to look for in a haircut for thin hair, here’s a step-by-step guide to getting the perfect haircut:

Step 1: Do Your Research

Before heading to the salon, do some research on hairstyles for thin hair. Look for styles that you like and that will work well with your hair type and face shape.

Step 2: Consult with Your Stylist

When you arrive at the salon, consult with your stylist about your hair type and the style you’re interested in. They can provide you with recommendations and advice on what will work best for you.

Step 3: Choose the Right Length

Based on your consultation with your stylist, choose the right length for your hair. Remember, shorter styles tend to make hair look fuller, while longer styles can weigh hair down and make it look thinner.

Step 4: Choose the Right Layers

If you decide to add layers, choose long, subtle layers that blend seamlessly into the rest of your hair. Avoid choppy or blunt layers that can make thin hair look even thinner.

Step 5: Choose the Right Bangs

If you decide to get bangs, choose wispy or side-swept bangs that add texture and volume to your hair.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

After your haircut, style your hair using products that are specifically designed for thin hair. We’ll cover some of our favorite products in the next section.

Tips and Tricks for Taking Care of Thin Hair

Here are some tips and tricks for taking care of thin hair:

Use the Right Products

Use products that are specifically designed for thin hair, such as volumizing shampoo and conditioner, root-lifting spray, and dry shampoo. Avoid heavy or greasy products that can weigh hair down and make it look thinner.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners, as they can damage thin hair and make it look even thinner. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and choose the lowest heat setting possible.

Avoid Over-Washing

Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it look thinner. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every three days.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, as they can pull on thin hair and make it look thinner. If you must wear your hair up, opt for looser styles, such as a messy bun or a loose braid.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can thin hair be thickened?

A: While you can’t change the thickness of your hair, there are things you can do to make it look fuller and thicker. Choosing the right haircut, using the right products, and taking care of your hair can all help to make it look thicker.

Q: What is the best haircut for thin hair?

A: The best haircut for thin hair depends on your hair type and face shape. Generally, shorter hairstyles with long, subtle layers work well for thin hair.

Q: How often should I get a haircut if I have thin hair?

A: It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks if you have thin hair, as this can help to prevent split ends and breakage.

Here are some of our favorite hair care products for thin hair:

Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, such as TRESemmé Thick & Full Shampoo and Conditioner, to add volume and thickness to your hair.

Dry Shampoo

Use a dry shampoo, such as Batiste Dry Shampoo, to absorb excess oil and add volume to your hair between washes.

Heat Protectant Spray

Use a heat protectant spray, such as TRESemmé Thermal Creations Heat Tamer Spray, to protect your hair from heat damage when using heat styling tools.

Texturizing Spray

With these tips and products, you can achieve fuller and thicker hair, even if you have thin hair. Remember, the key is to choose the right haircut, use the right products, and take care of your hair.
