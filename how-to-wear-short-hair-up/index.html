How To Wear Short Hair Up Tips Tricks And Styles

Written by William Aug 28, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have short hair, you might think styling options are limited. But fear not! There are plenty of ways to wear short hair up, whether you're going for a casual or formal look. In this post, we'll share some tips, tricks, and styles to inspire your next hairdo.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs

How to Wear Short Hair Up

Before we dive into specific styles, let's talk about some general tips for wearing short hair up:

  • Use bobby pins and hair ties that match your hair color for a more seamless look.
  • Don't be afraid to use hair accessories, like headbands or clips, to add interest to your updo.
  • Experiment with different products, like texturizing spray or mousse, to add volume and hold to your hair.

Now, let's explore some popular ways to wear short hair up:

1. The Half-Up Top Knot

This style is perfect for when you want to keep some hair down but still get it out of your face. To achieve the half-up top knot:

  1. Start by pulling the top half of your hair up into a high ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail and wrap it around itself to create a bun.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  4. Leave the rest of your hair down or style it as desired.

2. The Messy Bun

A messy bun is a classic updo that works well on short hair. To create a messy bun:

  1. Pull your hair up into a high ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail and wrap it around itself to create a bun.
  3. Loosen some strands around your face and at the back of your head to create a messy, relaxed look.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins.

3. The Braided Updo

Braids can add interest and texture to short hair. To create a braided updo:

  1. Start by braiding a small section of hair near your temple.
  2. Continue braiding around your head, incorporating more hair as you go.
  3. When you reach the back of your head, secure the braid with bobby pins.
  4. Repeat on the other side.
  5. Gather the remaining hair at the nape of your neck and twist it into a bun or knot.
  6. Secure with bobby pins.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're more of a visual learner, here's a step-by-step guide to creating a simple updo on short hair:

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Section off the top half of your hair and secure it with a hair tie or clip.
  3. Twist the bottom half of your hair into a bun or knot at the nape of your neck.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  5. Take the top half of your hair and tease it gently with a comb or brush to add volume.
  6. Smooth the teased section over the bun and secure with bobby pins.
  7. Use hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Success

Here are a few more tips to help you rock your short hair updo:

  • Start with clean, dry hair for best results.
  • Use hairspray or texturizing spray to add hold and volume to your hair.
  • Don't worry if your updo looks a little messy or undone - that's part of the charm!
  • Experiment with different styles and accessories to find what works best for you.

FAQs

Can I wear short hair up for formal occasions?

Absolutely! There are plenty of elegant updos that work well on short hair. Just add some accessories, like a sparkly hair clip or statement earrings, to dress up the look.

What if my hair is too short for an updo?

If your hair is too short to pull back into a bun or ponytail, you can still create a faux updo by teasing and pinning sections of hair at the crown of your head.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair requires a different approach to hair care than longer locks. Here are a few tips to keep your short hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of natural oils.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, like flat irons or curling wands, too often.
  • Invest in regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to wear your short hair up in a variety of stylish and effortless ways. Whether you're running errands or attending a wedding, there's an updo that's perfect for you.
