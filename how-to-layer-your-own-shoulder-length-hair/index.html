How To Layer Your Own Shoulder Length Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new look for your shoulder length hair, layering is a great option. Layering your hair can add volume, texture, and movement to your locks. While going to a stylist is always an option, layering your own hair at home can save you money and give you more control over the final result. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps to layer your own shoulder length hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Your Own Shoulder Length Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Layering

How to Layer Your Own Shoulder Length Hair

The first step in layering your own shoulder length hair is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need sharp scissors, a comb, and clips to section your hair. It's important to use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut and prevent split ends.

Next, it's important to decide on the length and style of your layers. Take into consideration the shape of your face and the thickness of your hair. If you have thin hair, shorter layers can add volume. If you have thick hair, longer layers can help to thin it out.

Once you've decided on the length and style of your layers, it's time to start cutting. It's best to start with clean, dry hair. Begin by sectioning your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Clip each section to keep it separate.

Step by Step Guide

1. Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors at a slight angle and cut into the hair, creating a soft, feathered effect.

2. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head. Be sure to cut each section to the same length to create an even, balanced look.

3. Repeat this process on the side sections of your hair, starting from the bottom and working your way up.

4. Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Take small sections of hair and cut at an angle, creating longer layers towards the front of your face. This will help to frame your face and create a more dynamic look.

Tips for Layering Your Own Hair

- Take your time and work slowly. It's better to take small sections of hair and cut gradually than to cut too much off at once.

- Use a mirror to check the back of your head and ensure that the layers are even.

- If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, start with small trims and work your way up to layering.

- Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member if you're having trouble cutting the back sections of your hair.

FAQs

Q: How often should I layer my shoulder length hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's recommended to get a trim and layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Q: Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, layering can be a great option for curly hair. Be sure to cut each section of hair when it's dry, as curly hair can shrink up when it dries.

Hair Care After Layering

After layering your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain healthy locks. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims can also help to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers. With a little bit of care and maintenance, you can enjoy your newly layered shoulder length hair for months to come.
