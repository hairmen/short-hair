Do They Pluck Your Eyebrows Before Microblading

Written by Daniel Jun 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're considering microblading, you're probably wondering about the process and what to expect. One question that often comes up is whether or not your eyebrows will be plucked before the procedure. In this article, we'll answer that question and provide you with some helpful tips and information to prepare for your microblading appointment.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Prepare for Microblading
  • Step-by-Step Microblading Process
  • Tips for Before and After Microblading
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Eyebrow Hair Care After Microblading

Description

Microblading is a semi-permanent cosmetic tattooing technique that involves creating natural-looking eyebrow hair strokes using a small handheld tool. This technique can help fill in sparse or thin eyebrows, create a more defined shape, and save time on daily eyebrow makeup application.

How to Prepare for Microblading

Before your microblading appointment, it's important to avoid certain activities that can affect your skin and eyebrows. These include:

  • Waxing or tweezing your eyebrows
  • Using exfoliating products or treatments
  • Getting a chemical peel
  • Using retinol or other skincare products that can thin the skin

You should also avoid alcohol and caffeine for at least 24 hours before your appointment, as they can affect your skin's sensitivity.

Step-by-Step Microblading Process

When you arrive at your microblading appointment, the technician will begin by discussing your desired eyebrow shape and color with you. They will then clean your eyebrows and apply a numbing cream to minimize any discomfort during the procedure.

Next, the technician will use a small handheld tool to create hair-like strokes in your eyebrows, using a special microblading pigment. The process usually takes about 1-2 hours, depending on the complexity of the design.

After the procedure, you may experience some redness, swelling, or discomfort in the eyebrow area. This is normal and should subside within a few days.

Tips for Before and After Microblading

To ensure the best results from your microblading procedure, follow these tips:

  • Avoid sun exposure and tanning for at least 2 weeks before and after your appointment
  • Avoid sweating and exercise for at least 24 hours after the procedure
  • Avoid applying makeup or skincare products to the eyebrow area for at least 10 days after the procedure
  • Apply a healing ointment to the eyebrow area as directed by your technician

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions and answers about microblading:

Do they pluck your eyebrows before microblading?

It depends on the technician and the desired eyebrow shape. Some technicians may choose to pluck a few stray hairs to create a more defined shape, while others may not pluck at all.

How long does microblading last?

Microblading typically lasts 1-3 years, depending on individual factors such as skin type and lifestyle.

Is microblading painful?

Some discomfort is normal during the procedure, but most technicians use a numbing cream to minimize any pain.

Eyebrow Hair Care After Microblading

After your microblading procedure, it's important to take care of your new eyebrow hair. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid picking or scratching at the eyebrow area
  • Avoid swimming or exposure to water for at least 10 days after the procedure
  • Use a gentle cleanser and avoid scrubbing the eyebrow area
  • Apply sunscreen to the eyebrow area when exposed to the sun

In conclusion, whether or not your eyebrows will be plucked before microblading depends on the technician and your desired eyebrow shape. To prepare for your appointment, avoid certain activities and follow the aftercare instructions provided by your technician. With proper care, your new eyebrows can last for years and enhance your natural beauty.
