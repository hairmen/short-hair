Why Does My Hair Feel So Thin After Extensions

Written by Eveline Jun 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've recently gotten hair extensions and noticed that your hair feels thinner, you're not alone. Many people experience this after getting extensions, and it can be a frustrating and confusing experience. In this post, we'll explore some of the reasons why your hair may be feeling thin after extensions, as well as some tips for how to care for your hair during and after the extension process.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Description:

Hair extensions can be a great way to add length and volume to your hair. However, they can also cause damage to your natural hair if not installed or maintained properly. One of the most common complaints after getting extensions is that the hair feels thinner than before. This can be caused by a number of factors, including:

  • The weight of the extensions
  • The installation method
  • The type of extensions used
  • Poor maintenance

In the next sections, we'll explore each of these factors in more detail.

How To:

If you're experiencing thinning hair after getting extensions, there are a few things you can do to help restore your hair's thickness:

  • Remove the extensions: If the extensions are causing too much damage to your natural hair, it may be best to remove them and give your hair a break.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo: Look for a shampoo that is designed to add volume to your hair. This can help make your hair feel fuller and thicker.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Try to avoid using them as much as possible.
  • Get regular trims: Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair look thinner.

Step By Step:

If you're considering getting hair extensions, here are some steps you can take to ensure that they don't cause your hair to thin:

  • Choose a reputable salon: Do your research to find a salon that has experience with extensions and uses high-quality products.
  • Consult with a stylist: Talk to your stylist about the type of extensions that would work best for your hair type and lifestyle.
  • Opt for a lighter weight: If you're concerned about the weight of the extensions, ask your stylist to use a lighter weight option.
  • Maintain your extensions: Follow your stylist's instructions for maintenance, which may include regular brushing and conditioning.

Tips:

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair after getting extensions:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb: This can help prevent tangles and breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can put stress on your natural hair and cause it to break.
  • Avoid chemical treatments: Chemical treatments like coloring and relaxing can weaken your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

FAQs:

Q: Will my hair grow back after getting extensions?

A: Yes, your hair will continue to grow as normal after getting extensions. However, if the extensions are causing damage to your natural hair, it may take some time for it to recover.

Q: How long do extensions typically last?

A: The lifespan of extensions can vary depending on the type of extensions and how well they are maintained. Some extensions can last up to six months or longer, while others may need to be replaced every few weeks.

Hair Care:

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the health and thickness of your hair, whether you have extensions or not. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly: This can help remove buildup and keep your hair healthy.
  • Use a conditioner: Conditioner can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent breakage.
  • Eat a balanced diet: A diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help promote healthy hair growth.
  • Protect your hair from heat: Use heat protectant products and avoid excessive heat styling.
  • Get regular trims: Trimming your hair regularly can help prevent split ends and breakage.

In conclusion, if you're experiencing thinning hair after getting extensions, it's important to understand that this can be caused by a variety of factors. By taking proper care of your hair and choosing the right type of extensions, you can help prevent thinning and maintain the health and thickness of your natural hair.
