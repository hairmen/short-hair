How To Get Your Hair To Lay Flat Top Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Oct 30, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have unruly hair that just won't lay flat, you know how frustrating it can be. But don't worry – there are plenty of easy tips and tricks you can use to get your hair to behave. In this article, we'll go over how to get your hair to lay flat, step by step. We'll also answer some common questions about hair care, so you can keep your locks looking their best.

Step 1: Start with a Good Haircut

The first step to getting your hair to lay flat is to start with a good haircut. Talk to your stylist about getting a cut that is flattering to your face shape and hair texture. A layered cut can help reduce volume and make your hair easier to manage.

Another option is to get a blunt cut, which will create a sleek, straight look. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, consider getting a keratin treatment or other smoothing treatment to help tame your locks.

Step 2: Blow Dry Your Hair Correctly

Blow drying your hair correctly is key to getting it to lay flat. Start by applying a heat protectant product to your hair, then use a round brush to blow dry your hair in sections. Aim the nozzle of your blow dryer downward to help smooth your hair and prevent frizz.

For even better results, use a boar bristle brush, which will help distribute your hair's natural oils and create a smooth, shiny finish. Make sure to blow dry your hair completely before moving on to the next step.

Step 3: Use the Right Products

Using the right products can make all the difference when it comes to getting your hair to lay flat. Look for a smoothing serum or cream that will help tame flyaways and keep your hair looking sleek. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends and avoiding the roots.

You can also use a styling cream or mousse to help control volume and keep your hair in place. Just be careful not to use too much, which can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Step 4: Flat Iron Your Hair

Using a flat iron is one of the most effective ways to get your hair to lay flat. Start by applying a heat protectant spray to your hair, then use the flat iron to straighten your hair in sections. Make sure to use a high-quality flat iron with ceramic plates, which will help distribute heat evenly and reduce the risk of damage.

For even better results, use a comb or brush to guide your hair through the flat iron. This will help create a sleek, straight look that will last all day.

Step 5: Maintain Your Style

Once you've gotten your hair to lay flat, it's important to maintain your style. Use a light hairspray or finishing spray to help hold your hair in place without weighing it down. Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz and undo all your hard work.

You can also use dry shampoo to help extend the life of your style between washes. Just make sure to use a high-quality product that won't leave any residue or buildup on your hair.

Tips for Getting Your Hair to Lay Flat

Tip #1: Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help reduce friction and prevent frizz.

Tip #2: Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Tip #3: Consider getting a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Most people can get away with washing their hair every other day or a few times a week. If you have very oily hair, you may need to wash it more often.

Q: How can I prevent split ends?

A: Regular trims and using a leave-in conditioner can help prevent split ends. Avoid using hot tools on high heat and be gentle when brushing your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Locks

To keep your hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips for healthy, beautiful locks:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or using a heat protectant spray.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can cause damage and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can get your hair to lay flat and keep it looking its best. Whether you have curly, wavy, or straight hair, these techniques will help you achieve a sleek, polished look that will turn heads.
