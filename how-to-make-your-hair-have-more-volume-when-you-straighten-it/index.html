How To Make Your Hair Have More Volume When You Straighten It

Do you have flat, lifeless hair that just won't seem to hold any volume? Straightening your hair can make it look sleek and shiny, but it can also make it look even flatter. However, with a few simple tricks and techniques, you can make your hair look voluminous and full even when it's straight. In this article, we'll show you how to make your hair have more volume when you straighten it.

How to Make Your Hair Have More Volume When You Straighten It

If you want to make your hair look voluminous when you straighten it, there are a few things you can do. Here are some tips:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start straightening your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. If your hair is wet or damp, it will be harder to straighten and you'll be more likely to damage it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean your hair, and let it air dry or blow-dry it on a low heat setting.

2. Use a heat protectant

Straightening your hair can be damaging, especially if you do it frequently. To protect your hair from heat damage, use a heat protectant spray or serum before you start straightening. This will help to minimize damage and keep your hair looking healthy.

3. Use a high-quality straightener

The quality of your straightener can make a big difference in how your hair looks. Look for a straightener that has ceramic plates, as these will distribute heat evenly and reduce the risk of damage. You should also choose a straightener with adjustable heat settings so you can control the temperature.

4. Section your hair

Sectioning your hair can make it easier to straighten and give you more control over the styling process. Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties, and work on one section at a time. This will help to prevent tangles and ensure that each section is straightened evenly.

5. Use a comb

Using a comb while you straighten your hair can help to add volume and reduce frizz. Comb through each section of hair before you straighten it, and use the comb to lift the hair at the roots as you straighten. This will create more volume and prevent your hair from looking flat.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to making your hair look voluminous when you straighten it:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties.
  4. Use a high-quality straightener with ceramic plates and adjustable heat settings.
  5. Comb through each section of hair before you straighten it.
  6. Straighten each section of hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  7. Use the comb to lift the hair at the roots as you straighten.
  8. Repeat until all sections of hair are straightened.
  9. Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve voluminous, straight hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add thickness to your hair.
  • Blow-dry your hair upside down to create more volume at the roots.
  • Don't over-straighten your hair. Leave some natural texture to create volume.
  • Use a dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair between washes.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair, such as oils and serums.

FAQ

Can I use a straightening brush to add volume to my hair?

Yes, a straightening brush can be a great tool for adding volume to your hair. Use it to lift your hair at the roots as you straighten, and brush through each section of hair to create a smooth, voluminous look.

How often should I straighten my hair?

It's best to limit how often you straighten your hair to avoid damage. Try to only straighten your hair once or twice a week, and use a heat protectant spray or serum each time.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important to keep it looking healthy and voluminous. Here are some tips:

  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet or damp hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and techniques, you can make your hair look voluminous and full even when it's straight. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
