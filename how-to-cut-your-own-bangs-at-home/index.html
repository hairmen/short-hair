How To Cut Your Own Bangs At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own bangs at home can seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple and cost-effective way to freshen up your look. In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps to cut your own bangs at home, as well as some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Bangs at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Bangs

How to Cut Your Own Bangs at Home

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to hold back sections of your hair.

When it comes to cutting your own bangs, it’s important to take your time and be patient. Start with small cuts and work your way up to the length you want. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own bangs at home:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off the front portion of your hair that you want to cut into bangs. Use a comb to create a triangle-shaped section, with the top point of the triangle at the center of your forehead.

Step 2: Determine Your Bangs Length

Decide how long you want your bangs to be. Keep in mind that hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so it’s best to start longer than you want them to end up.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Starting from the center of your forehead, hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Work your way outwards, cutting small sections at a time. Make sure to keep the hair taut and use the comb to keep the hair even.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you’ve cut your bangs, check to make sure they’re even. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully, taking small sections at a time.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs

Here are some tips to make cutting your own bangs easier:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and a jagged cut.
  • Start with longer bangs and work your way up to the length you want.
  • Don’t cut your bangs straight across. Instead, cut them at a slight angle for a more natural look.
  • Don’t cut your bangs when they’re wet. They tend to shrink up when they dry, so it’s best to cut them when they’re dry.
  • Take your time and be patient. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.

FAQs

Can I cut my own bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that your hair will shrink up when it dries, so it’s best to start longer than you want them to end up.

How often should I cut my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the length of your bangs. On average, it’s recommended to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Should I use regular scissors or hair-cutting scissors?

Hair-cutting scissors are recommended because they’re sharper and will give you a cleaner cut.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Bangs

After cutting your own bangs, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent split ends and breakage. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week and avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.

Cutting your own bangs at home is a great way to save money and freshen up your look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut in the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and follow these steps and tips for a great result every time.
