How To Cut Hair Shorter In The Back Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Nov 21, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re looking to change up your hairstyle, one easy way to do so is by cutting your hair shorter in the back. Whether you’re going for a classic bob or a trendy pixie cut, this technique can help you achieve the look you want. In this article, we’ll walk you through how to cut hair shorter in the back step by step, as well as provide some tips and tricks for making the process as smooth as possible.

How to Cut Hair Shorter in the Back: Step by Step

If you’re not a professional hair stylist, cutting your own hair can be a little intimidating. However, with the right tools and some basic knowledge, it’s definitely doable. Here’s how to cut hair shorter in the back:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you’ll need the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (if you’re going for a very short cut)
  • Hair ties (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp – dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve the look you want.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair as usual. If you have curly or wavy hair, consider straightening it before you start cutting. This can help you get a more even cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, separate your hair into three sections – one on top and one on each side. Clip the top section up and out of the way.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Section

Start with the bottom section. Use your comb to comb the hair straight down, and then cut straight across. If you want a slightly angled cut, you can cut at a slight angle.

Step 5: Cut the Side Sections

Next, work on the side sections. Comb the hair straight down, and then cut at an angle to create a gradual slope from the back to the front.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut the side sections, unclip the top section and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to blend the layers, creating a seamless transition between the top and bottom sections.

Step 7: Check Your Work

After you’ve finished cutting, take a look at your hair in the mirror. Make sure the back is even and that the layers are blended nicely. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them up.

Tips for Cutting Hair Shorter in the Back

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you cut your hair shorter in the back:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter. You can always take more off, but you can’t put it back on!
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve the look you want.
  • Take your time. Cutting your hair too quickly can result in an uneven cut.
  • If you’re unsure about cutting your hair yourself, consider going to a professional stylist.

Frequently Asked Questions about Cutting Hair Shorter in the Back

Here are some common questions people have about cutting their hair shorter in the back:

How much hair should I cut?

This depends on the look you’re going for. If you’re going for a classic bob, you may want to cut off several inches. If you’re going for a shorter, edgier style, you may only need to cut off an inch or two.

Can I cut my hair shorter in the back with clippers?

Yes, you can use clippers to cut your hair shorter in the back. However, keep in mind that this will result in a very short cut, so make sure it’s the look you’re going for.

What if I mess up?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don’t panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to have it fixed.

Hair Care Tips for Shorter Hair

Once you’ve cut your hair shorter in the back, it’s important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily cut your hair shorter in the back and achieve the look you want. Whether you’re going for a classic bob or a trendy pixie cut, this technique can help you take your hair game to the next level.
