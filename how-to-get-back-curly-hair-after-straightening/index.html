How To Get Back Curly Hair After Straightening

Are you tired of having straight hair after relaxing it? Do you miss your natural curls? If you're looking for ways to restore your curly locks, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll give you tips on how to get back curly hair after straightening.

How to Get Back Curly Hair After Straightening

It's important to note that the process of getting back your curly hair after straightening may take time, patience, and effort. Here are some methods you can try:

1. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair is crucial to restoring moisture and elasticity to your curls. Use a deep conditioner that contains natural oils such as coconut, avocado, or jojoba oil. Apply the deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it off.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help restore the strength and structure of your hair. Look for a protein treatment that contains hydrolyzed keratin, collagen, or silk amino acids. Apply the protein treatment to your hair and leave it on for the recommended time before rinsing it off.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it harder to restore your curls. Avoid using flat irons, curling irons, or blow dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Use a Diffuser

When you do need to dry your hair, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. This will help minimize frizz and enhance your natural curls.

5. Finger Coil Your Hair

Finger coiling is a technique that can help restore your curls. After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner and then use your fingers to coil small sections of your hair around themselves. Leave the coils in place until your hair is dry, then gently separate them to reveal your curls.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Maintaining your curly hair requires some effort, but it's worth it. Here are some tips to help you keep your curls healthy:

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Satin pillowcases can help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep. They also help your curls retain their shape.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness. Try to wash your hair no more than twice a week.

3. Use a Leave-in Conditioner

Leave-in conditioners can help keep your hair moisturized and prevent tangling.

4. Avoid Brushing Your Hair

Brushing your hair can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls.
