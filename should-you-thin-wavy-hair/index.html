Should You Thin Wavy Hair

Written by Petter Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have wavy hair, you may be wondering if you should thin it out. Thinning hair is a common hair styling technique, but is it right for wavy hair? In this article, we will explore the pros and cons of thinning wavy hair and give you some tips for maintaining your wavy locks.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Thin Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Thinned Wavy Hair
  • FAQs

Description

Thinning hair involves removing some of the hair strands to create a lighter, more manageable hairstyle. It is often recommended for people with thick, voluminous hair. However, wavy hair is different from straight or curly hair, and thinning it out may not be the best option for everyone.

Wavy hair is naturally voluminous and has a lot of body. Thinning it out can reduce the volume and make it look flat. On the other hand, if your wavy hair is very thick and unmanageable, thinning it out can make it easier to style and maintain.

How to Thin Wavy Hair

Thinning wavy hair can be done in several ways:

  • Thinning scissors: These are special scissors that have one serrated edge and one straight edge. They are used to remove some of the hair strands without cutting them too short.
  • Razor cutting: This technique involves using a razor to slice through the hair strands at an angle. It creates a softer, more natural look.
  • Texturizing: This involves creating small, choppy layers throughout the hair. It reduces the weight of the hair and makes it easier to style.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you want to thin out your wavy hair, here is a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Towel dry your hair and comb it out.
  3. Section off the hair you want to thin. This can be the top layer, the sides, or the back.
  4. Use your thinning scissors, razor, or texturizing shears to remove some of the hair strands. Be careful not to cut too much at once.
  5. Repeat on the other sections of hair you want to thin.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Thinned Wavy Hair

After thinning out your wavy hair, you may need to adjust your hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a lightweight, volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help maintain the volume in your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy, oil-based products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush that can flatten your waves.
  • Avoid heat styling tools that can damage your hair and make it look flat.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your thinned hair.

FAQs

Is it bad to thin out wavy hair?

No, it is not bad to thin out wavy hair. However, it may not be the best option for everyone. Thinning out your hair can reduce the volume and make it look flat. On the other hand, if your wavy hair is very thick and unmanageable, thinning it out can make it easier to style and maintain.

How often should you thin out wavy hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes unmanageable. Generally, you should only thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks or as needed.

Hair Care for Wavy Hair

Whether you decide to thin out your wavy hair or not, it is important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can dry out your hair and make it frizzy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to help hydrate your hair and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can break the waves and create frizz.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your natural waves.

In conclusion, whether or not you should thin out your wavy hair depends on your personal preference and the texture of your hair. If you have very thick, unmanageable hair, thinning it out can make it easier to style and maintain. However, if you love the volume and body of your wavy hair, you may want to avoid thinning it out. Whatever you decide, remember to take good care of your hair and embrace your natural texture.
