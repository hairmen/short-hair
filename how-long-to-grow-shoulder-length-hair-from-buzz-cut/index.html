How Long Does It Take To Grow Shoulder Length Hair From A Buzz Cut

Written by Oliver Nov 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your buzz cut and want to grow your hair out to shoulder length? Well, you're not alone. Many people are growing out their hair for various reasons, and it can be frustrating to wait for your hair to grow to your desired length. In this article, we'll discuss how long it takes to grow shoulder length hair from a buzz cut, how to do it, and some tips to make the process easier.

How to Grow Shoulder Length Hair from a Buzz Cut

The first step in growing out your hair is to stop cutting it. It may seem obvious, but many people make the mistake of trimming their hair too often and slowing down the growth process. Let your hair grow out naturally for a few months before attempting any styles or cuts.

Once your hair has grown out to a few inches in length, start styling it in a way that will encourage growth. Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and slow down growth. Instead, opt for natural hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails.

As your hair continues to grow, you may want to consider getting regular trims to keep your ends healthy. Split ends can cause your hair to break and stunt growth, so it's important to keep them in check. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

How Long Does It Take to Grow Shoulder Length Hair?

The length of time it takes to grow shoulder length hair from a buzz cut varies from person to person. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so it can take anywhere from 12-18 months to grow shoulder length hair from a buzz cut. However, factors like genetics, age, and health can affect how fast your hair grows.

It's important to be patient and not get discouraged if your hair isn't growing as fast as you'd like. Keep up with healthy hair habits like eating a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and avoiding damaging styling tools.

Tips for Growing Out Your Hair

Here are some tips to make the process of growing out your hair easier:

  • Be patient: Growing out your hair takes time, so don't expect immediate results.
  • Avoid heat styling tools: Heat can damage your hair and slow down growth, so try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Eat a healthy diet: A balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can promote hair growth and overall hair health.
  • Stay hydrated: Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Get regular trims: Regular trims can help keep your ends healthy and prevent breakage.

FAQs

Can I speed up hair growth?

While there's no guaranteed way to speed up hair growth, there are some things you can do to promote healthy hair growth. Eating a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and avoiding damaging styling tools can all help promote hair growth.

What should I do if my hair isn't growing?

If you're not seeing any growth in your hair, it may be due to genetics, age, or an underlying health condition. Talk to your doctor if you're concerned about your hair growth.

Should I take hair growth supplements?

Hair growth supplements may help promote healthy hair growth, but they're not a guaranteed solution. Talk to your doctor before taking any supplements.

Hair Care for Growing Out Your Hair

As you're growing out your hair, it's important to take good care of it to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Get regular trims to keep your ends healthy.

By following these tips and being patient, you can grow out your hair from a buzz cut to shoulder length in no time. Remember to take good care of your hair and avoid damaging styling tools to promote healthy growth.
