What Do The Numbers Mean In A Haircut

Written by Christine Nov 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to getting a haircut, you may have heard your barber or stylist mention numbers such as "2" or "4." These numbers actually have a specific meaning and can determine the length of your hair. In this blog post, we'll explain what the numbers mean in a haircut and how they can affect your overall look.

Table of Contents

  • What Do the Numbers Mean?
  • How to Communicate with Your Barber or Stylist
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Getting a Great Haircut

What Do the Numbers Mean?

The numbers in a haircut refer to the guard size on the clippers that your barber or stylist is using. The guard size determines the length of the hair that is left on your head after the clippers pass over it. The lower the number, the shorter the hair will be. For example, a "0" guard size will leave your hair very short or even bald, while a "8" guard size will leave your hair much longer.

It's important to note that the guard sizes may vary slightly depending on the brand of clippers being used, but generally, the numbers will be the same across different brands.

How to Communicate with Your Barber or Stylist

Now that you know what the numbers mean, it's important to be able to communicate with your barber or stylist about the length of hair you want. It's always a good idea to bring a picture of the style you're looking for to help illustrate your desired length and look.

You can also simply tell your barber or stylist the guard size you want, such as "I want a number 4 on the sides and back and a scissor cut on top." This will help ensure that they understand exactly what you're looking for.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're getting a clipper cut, here's a step-by-step guide to help you understand the process:

1. Your barber or stylist will start by selecting the guard size they will use on the clippers.

2. They will then begin cutting your hair with the clippers, starting at the back and moving towards the top of your head.

3. After the sides and back are cut, your barber or stylist may switch to scissors to cut the top of your hair to your desired length.

4. Once the cut is complete, your barber or stylist will use a comb and scissors to blend the different lengths of hair together and create a seamless look.

Tips for Getting a Great Haircut

Here are some tips to help you get the best haircut possible:

- Bring a picture of the style you want to help your barber or stylist understand your desired look.

- Be clear in your communication about the length of hair you want.

- Consider the shape of your face and head when choosing a hairstyle.

- Don't be afraid to ask questions or make adjustments during the cut if needed.

FAQ

Q: Can I use the same guard size all over my head?

A: It's possible, but it may not create the most flattering look. Your barber or stylist can help you determine the best guard sizes to use for your desired style.

Q: Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

A: It's possible, but it can be difficult to achieve an even and polished look without professional training.

Hair Care after Your Haircut

After your haircut, it's important to practice good hair care to maintain your style. This includes regular washing and conditioning, using a heat protectant if using hot tools, and trimming split ends as needed.

Now that you know what the numbers mean in a haircut, you can confidently communicate with your barber or stylist and achieve your desired look. Remember to keep up with regular hair maintenance to keep your style looking fresh and polished.
