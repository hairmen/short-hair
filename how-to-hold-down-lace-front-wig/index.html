How To Hold Down Lace Front Wig A Comprehensive Guide

If you're new to wearing wigs, you might be wondering how to hold down a lace front wig. Don't worry - you're not alone! Lace front wigs are a popular choice for many people, but keeping them in place can be a challenge. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to hold down your lace front wig, as well as some tips and tricks for keeping it looking great.

How to Hold Down a Lace Front Wig

The first thing you need to know about holding down your lace front wig is that there are a few different methods you can use. Some people prefer to use wig glue, while others prefer wig tape. Whichever method you choose, the goal is to keep the wig securely in place so it doesn't shift, slide, or come off altogether.

Wig Glue

Wig glue is a popular choice for holding down lace front wigs because it creates a strong, long-lasting bond. However, it can be difficult to remove and may cause damage to your natural hairline if used improperly. To use wig glue, follow these steps:

  1. Clean your forehead and the area where the wig will be attached with rubbing alcohol.
  2. Apply a thin layer of wig glue to your forehead using a cotton swab or brush.
  3. Wait for the glue to become tacky (usually 30-60 seconds).
  4. Carefully place the wig onto your head, starting at the front and working your way back.
  5. Use a comb or brush to smooth out any bumps or lumps.

To remove wig glue, use a wig adhesive remover or rubbing alcohol. Never pull the wig off your head, as this can cause damage to your natural hairline.

Wig Tape

Wig tape is another popular option for holding down lace front wigs. It's easy to use and doesn't require any special tools or products. To use wig tape, follow these steps:

  1. Clean your forehead and the area where the wig will be attached with rubbing alcohol.
  2. Cut the wig tape into small strips, about 2-3 inches long.
  3. Peel off the backing from one side of the tape and apply it to your forehead.
  4. Peel off the backing from the other side of the tape and carefully place the wig onto your head.
  5. Use a comb or brush to smooth out any bumps or lumps.

To remove wig tape, gently peel it off your skin. You can also use rubbing alcohol to help loosen the adhesive.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to holding down your lace front wig, using either wig glue or wig tape:

  1. Clean your forehead and the area where the wig will be attached with rubbing alcohol.
  2. Apply the wig glue or wig tape according to the instructions above.
  3. Carefully place the wig onto your head, starting at the front and working your way back.
  4. Use a comb or brush to smooth out any bumps or lumps.

That's it - you're ready to go! With the right technique and a little practice, you'll be able to hold down your lace front wig with ease.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips and tricks for holding down your lace front wig:

  • Use a wig cap to help keep your natural hair in place and prevent it from poking through the wig.
  • Apply a small amount of hairspray to your natural hairline before attaching the wig. This will help the wig stick better.
  • Avoid using too much glue or tape, as this can cause the wig to look unnatural and feel uncomfortable.
  • If you have sensitive skin, test the glue or tape on a small area first to make sure you don't have an allergic reaction.

FAQ

How long will the wig stay in place?

The length of time your wig will stay in place depends on a few factors, including the type of adhesive you use, how well it's applied, and how active you are. Wig glue can last for up to two weeks, while wig tape usually lasts for a few days.

Can I swim or shower with my wig on?

We don't recommend swimming or showering with your wig on, as this can cause damage to the wig and the adhesive. If you need to wash your wig, remove it first and follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning.

How do I remove the wig without damaging it?

To remove the wig, gently lift it off your head, starting at the back and working your way forward. Use a wig adhesive remover or rubbing alcohol to help loosen the adhesive. Do not pull the wig off your head, as this can cause damage to the wig and your natural hairline.

Hair Care

Taking care of your lace front wig is important to keep it looking great and lasting as long as possible. Here are a few tips for caring for your wig:

  • Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair.
  • Wash the wig regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Allow the wig to air dry on a wig stand or mannequin.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons or flat irons, as these can damage the wig.

By following these tips, you'll be able to hold down your lace front wig and keep it looking great for as long as possible.
