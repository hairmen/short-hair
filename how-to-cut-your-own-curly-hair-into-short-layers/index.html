How To Cut Your Own Curly Hair Into Short Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that finding the right haircut can be a challenge. While some people prefer long, flowing locks, others opt for short, layered cuts. If you're in the latter camp, you might be wondering how to cut your own curly hair into short layers. The good news is that it's actually easier than you might think! In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step, so you can achieve the perfect haircut from the comfort of your own home.

Description

Cutting your own curly hair into short layers can be a great way to refresh your look without having to visit the salon. Short layers can add volume and texture to your curls, giving them a bouncy, playful look. Plus, cutting your own hair can be a fun and empowering experience!

How To

Before you get started, gather all the tools you'll need. You'll want to have a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and some hair clips on hand. You may also want to have a handheld mirror so you can see the back of your head.

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair so that you can see how your curls naturally fall. Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, and then let it air dry or use a diffuser to dry it.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections

Use your comb to divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle, and then divide each side into two sections (front and back). Use your hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the back sections first

Starting with the back sections, use your comb to separate a small section of hair. Hold the hair between your fingers, and then use your scissors to cut off the desired length. Repeat this process for each section, working your way up to the top of your head.

Step 4: Move on to the front sections

Once you've finished cutting the back sections, move on to the front sections. Follow the same process, starting with the bottom sections and working your way up. Be sure to cut each section to the same length as the corresponding section in the back.

Step 5: Blend the layers

Once you've finished cutting all the sections, it's time to blend the layers together. Use your scissors to carefully blend the ends of each section together. This will create a seamless, layered look.

Step 6: Trim any stray hairs

Finally, use your scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. Take your time and be careful not to cut off too much.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you cut your own curly hair into short layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's better to cut off too little than too much!
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair can take longer than you think.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting your own curly hair into short layers:

Can I cut my own hair if it's very curly?

Yes, you can! In fact, cutting your own curly hair can be easier than cutting straight hair, since the curls will hide any imperfections.

How short should I go?

The length of your layers will depend on your personal preference. If you're not sure, start with a longer length and work your way shorter until you find the perfect length.

What if I mess up?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! Remember, hair grows back. You can always visit a salon to have your hair fixed, or you can try again on your own.

Hair Care

After you've cut your own curly hair into short layers, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid using a hairbrush, which can damage your curls. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, which can dry out your curls and cause damage.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut your own curly hair into short layers like a pro. Have fun and experiment with different styles until you find the perfect look for you!
