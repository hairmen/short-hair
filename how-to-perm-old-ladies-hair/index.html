How To Perm Old Ladies Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, our hair loses its natural bounce and volume, making it difficult to style. Perming is a great way to add volume and texture to old ladies' hair. In this article, we will guide you through the process of perming old ladies' hair, step by step. We will also provide you with some tips, frequently asked questions and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Perm Old Ladies Hair
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Perming Old Ladies Hair
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care Advice

How to Perm Old Ladies Hair

Perming old ladies' hair is a process that involves the use of chemicals to break down the hair's natural structure and reshape it. The process should be done by a professional hair stylist to ensure that the hair is not damaged. Before perming, the hair should be washed and dried thoroughly.

The perming solution is then applied to the hair, and the hair is wrapped around perm rods of different sizes, depending on the desired curl pattern. The hair is left to process for a specific amount of time, depending on the hair's texture and condition. After processing, the perm rods are removed, and a neutralizing solution is applied to stop the chemical process.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to perm old ladies' hair:

  1. Wash and dry the hair
  2. Section the hair and wrap around perm rods of varying sizes
  3. Apply the perming solution to each section of hair, making sure to saturate the ends
  4. Cover the hair with a plastic cap and leave to process for the recommended time
  5. After processing, rinse the hair thoroughly with warm water
  6. Apply the neutralizing solution to the hair, making sure to cover every strand
  7. Leave the neutralizer on for the recommended time
  8. Remove the perm rods and rinse the hair thoroughly with warm water
  9. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to the hair and leave on for 5-10 minutes
  10. Rinse the hair thoroughly with cool water and style as desired

Tips for Perming Old Ladies Hair

Here are some tips to follow when perming old ladies' hair:

  • Choose a perm solution that is gentle and formulated for old ladies' hair
  • Use perm rods of varying sizes to create a natural-looking curl pattern
  • Do not over-process the hair, as this can lead to damage and breakage
  • Use a neutralizing solution that is specifically formulated for the perm solution you are using
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment to the hair after perming to restore moisture and prevent breakage

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about perming old ladies' hair:

  • Is perming old ladies' hair safe?

    • Perming old ladies' hair is safe when done by a professional hair stylist who uses gentle products and follows the correct procedure.

  • How long does a perm last?

    • A perm can last for up to 6 months, depending on how well the hair is cared for.

  • Can I perm my hair if I have color-treated hair?

    • Yes, but it is important to wait at least 2 weeks after coloring the hair before perming.

Hair Care Advice

Here are some tips for caring for permed old ladies' hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools on permed hair as this can cause damage
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to the hair daily to keep it moisturized
  • Avoid washing the hair too often, as this can cause the perm to lose its shape
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage

Perming old ladies' hair can add volume and texture, making it easier to style. By following the steps outlined in this article and taking proper care of the hair after perming, you can achieve beautiful, long-lasting curls.
