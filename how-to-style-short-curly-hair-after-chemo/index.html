How To Style Short Curly Hair After Chemo Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Nov 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dealing with hair loss after chemotherapy can be a difficult and emotional experience. For many women, hair is a symbol of femininity and losing it can feel like losing a part of themselves. However, once the hair starts to grow back, styling it can be a challenge. In this article, we will provide you with some tips and tricks on how to style short curly hair after chemo.

Hair Care

The first step in styling short curly hair after chemo is to take care of your hair. Chemotherapy can weaken your hair and make it more prone to damage, so it is important to be gentle with it. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for colored or damaged hair. Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and flat irons, as they can further damage your hair. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on a low setting to dry it.

How to Style Short Curly Hair After Chemo

Short curly hair can be styled in many different ways, depending on your personal style and preferences. Here are some popular options:

Curls

Embrace your natural curls by using a curl enhancing product, such as a mousse or gel, to define your curls. Scrunch your hair while it is wet to encourage the curls to form, and then let it air dry or use a diffuser on a low setting. Once your hair is dry, you can use your fingers to shape your curls and add volume.

Pixie Cut

A pixie cut is a short, cropped hairstyle that can be very flattering for women with short curly hair. This style is low maintenance and easy to style. Simply apply a styling cream to your hair and use your fingers to create texture and shape.

Bob

A bob is a classic hairstyle that can be adapted to suit short curly hair. You can opt for a chin-length bob or a longer bob that falls just below your ears. Use a curl enhancing product to define your curls and create volume. You can also add bangs to frame your face.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to style short curly hair after chemo:

  1. Wash and condition your hair using a mild shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for colored or damaged hair.
  2. Apply a curl enhancing product, such as a mousse or gel, to your hair while it is still wet.
  3. Scrunch your hair with your fingers to encourage the curls to form.
  4. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on a low setting to dry it.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to shape your curls and add volume.
  6. Alternatively, you can use a styling cream to create texture and shape.
  7. If you want to try a pixie cut, apply a styling cream to your hair and use your fingers to create texture and shape.
  8. If you want to try a bob, use a curl enhancing product to define your curls and create volume. You can also add bangs to frame your face.
  9. Finish with a hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks on how to style short curly hair after chemo:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as a brush can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and flat irons, as they can further damage your hair.
  • Try different products to find what works best for your hair.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what suits you best.
  • Be patient - it may take some time for your hair to grow back and for you to find a style that you love.

FAQs

How long does it take for hair to grow back after chemo?

It can take several months for hair to start growing back after chemo, and it may take up to a year for it to fully regrow. The rate of regrowth can vary depending on the individual.

Can I dye my hair after chemo?

It is generally recommended to wait at least six months after chemo before dyeing your hair. This is because your hair may be weaker and more prone to damage, and the chemicals in hair dye could cause further damage. Talk to your doctor or a hairstylist for advice on when it is safe to dye your hair.

What can I do to promote hair growth?

Eating a healthy diet and taking good care of your hair can help promote hair growth. You can also talk to your doctor about hair growth treatments, such as minoxidil.

In conclusion, styling short curly hair after chemo can be a challenge, but it is possible. By taking good care of your hair and experimenting with different styles and products, you can find a look that makes you feel confident and beautiful.
