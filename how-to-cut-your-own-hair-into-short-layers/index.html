How To Cut Your Own Hair Into Short Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 02, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon just to maintain your short layered haircut? Well, we have good news for you! With a little bit of practice and patience, you can learn how to cut your own hair into short layers at home. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of cutting your own hair into short layers, including tips and tricks for a flawless finish. So, let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair into Short Layers
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care after Cutting

How to Cut Your Own Hair into Short Layers

Before we dive into the step by step guide, it’s important to understand the basics of cutting your own hair into short layers. First and foremost, you will need to invest in a good quality pair of hair cutting scissors. You should also have a comb, hair clips, and a handheld mirror to help you see the back of your head.

It’s also important to note that cutting your own hair can be a bit tricky, especially if you’re new to it. So, don’t be too hard on yourself if it doesn’t turn out perfectly the first time. Practice makes perfect!

Step by Step Guide

Now, let’s get into the nitty-gritty of how to cut your own hair into short layers:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it’s clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to make the cuts. It’s also a good idea to style your hair the way you normally would, so you can get a better idea of how it will look once it’s cut.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start cutting the back

Take one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Hold it between your index and middle fingers, and cut straight across. This will be your guide for the rest of the cuts.

From there, take small sections of hair and comb them straight down. Use the guide you just created to cut each section at the desired length. Continue this process until you have cut all of the back sections.

Step 4: Move to the sides

Next, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair, comb it straight down, and use the guide you created to cut it at the desired length. Continue this process until you have cut both side sections.

Step 5: Cut the front sections

Finally, move on to the front sections. These sections are a bit trickier, so take your time. Comb each section straight down and use your guide to cut them at the desired length. Make sure to cut them at an angle, so they blend in with the rest of your hair.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect short layered haircut:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can’t undo a cut that’s too short.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don’t be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.
  • Use a handheld mirror to help you see the back of your head.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair is heavier and can look shorter once it dries.
  • Watch tutorials online to get a better idea of the process.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair into short layers:

  • Can I cut my own hair into short layers if I have curly hair? Yes, you can! Just make sure to cut your hair when it’s dry, so you can see how it will look once it’s styled.
  • How often should I cut my hair? It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks. However, if you’re cutting your own hair, you can do it more often if needed.
  • What should I do if I make a mistake? Don’t panic! Mistakes happen. If you make a mistake, try to blend it in with the rest of your hair or go to a professional for help.

Hair Care after Cutting

Congratulations, you’ve successfully cut your own hair into short layers! Now, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain your new style. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. You can also use hair masks and oils to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to cut your own hair into short layers like a pro. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don’t be afraid to try again if it doesn’t turn out perfectly the first time. Good luck!
