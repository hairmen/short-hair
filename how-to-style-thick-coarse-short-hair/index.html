How To Style Thick Coarse Short Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Aug 29, 2023 · 5 min read

Thick and coarse hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it can be voluminous and full of life, but on the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. If you have short hair, it can be even more challenging to style thick and coarse hair. However, with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a variety of stylish looks. In this guide, we'll show you how to style thick coarse short hair, step by step.

Description of Thick Coarse Short Hair

Thick and coarse hair is characterized by its thickness, texture, and density. It can be challenging to style because it tends to be resistant to heat and moisture. Short hair can make styling even more difficult, as there is less hair to work with. However, with the right techniques and products, you can create a variety of styles, from sleek and smooth to voluminous and textured.

How to Style Thick Coarse Short Hair

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition

The first step in styling thick coarse short hair is to start with a clean slate. Shampoo and condition your hair, using products that are designed for your hair type. Look for products that are moisturizing and nourishing, as thick and coarse hair tends to be dry and brittle.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After shampooing and conditioning, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair. This will help to add moisture and make your hair more manageable. Look for a leave-in conditioner that is lightweight and non-greasy, as heavy products can weigh down your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush. This will help to smooth your hair and create volume. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends, using the brush to lift and separate your hair. Use a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 4: Use a Styling Product

Once your hair is dry, apply a styling product that is designed for your hair type. Look for products that are lightweight and provide hold, such as a mousse or a gel. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers to work it through your strands.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Now it's time to style your hair. There are a variety of styles you can create, depending on your preferences. For a sleek and smooth look, use a flat iron to straighten your hair. For a textured and voluminous look, use a curling iron to create loose waves. You can also try a messy, tousled look by using your fingers to create texture and volume.

Tips for Styling Thick Coarse Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you style your thick coarse short hair:

  • Use heat protectant products to avoid damaging your hair
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair before styling
  • Experiment with different products and styling techniques to find what works best for you
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair

FAQs About Styling Thick Coarse Short Hair

What are the best haircuts for thick coarse short hair?

The best haircuts for thick coarse short hair are those that provide texture and movement. Short layered haircuts are a great option, as they can help to thin out your hair and make it more manageable. Pixie cuts and bobs are also good choices, as they can be styled in a variety of ways.

How often should I wash my thick coarse short hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently, while if you have dry hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week. If you exercise regularly, you may need to wash your hair more frequently to remove sweat and dirt.

What should I do if my thick coarse short hair is frizzy?

If your hair is prone to frizz, try using a smoothing serum or oil to tame flyaways. You can also try blow drying your hair with a round brush to smooth your strands. Avoid using hot tools on high heat, as this can exacerbate frizz.

Hair Care for Thick Coarse Short Hair

To keep your thick coarse short hair looking its best, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to add moisture to your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools on high heat, as this can damage your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as bleach or relaxers, as they can damage your hair

With these tips and techniques, you can style your thick coarse short hair in a variety of ways. Whether you prefer a sleek and smooth look or a messy and textured style, you can achieve it with the right products and tools.
