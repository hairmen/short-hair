Do You Start Dreads With Wet Or Dry Hair The Ultimate Guide

Written by Christine Nov 29, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Starting dreadlocks is a big decision that requires careful consideration and preparation. One of the most commonly asked questions by people who want to start dreadlocks is whether they should start with wet or dry hair. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of starting dreadlocks with wet or dry hair, and provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start your own dreadlocks.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Start Dreadlocks
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Dreadlocks
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Description

Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that involves the matting and twisting of hair into rope-like strands. This style has been around for centuries and is associated with various cultures and traditions. Starting dreadlocks can be a lengthy process that requires patience and dedication. One of the first decisions you'll need to make when starting dreadlocks is whether you should start with wet or dry hair.

How to Start Dreadlocks

Before you start your dreadlocks, it's important to understand that the process can take anywhere from several months to a few years, depending on your hair type and the method you choose. Here are some steps you can follow to start your own dreadlocks:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Choose a method: There are several methods for starting dreadlocks, including the backcombing method, the twist and rip method, and the neglect method. Research each method and choose the one that works best for you.
  2. Prepare your hair: Wash your hair thoroughly and let it air dry. It's important to have clean, dry hair before starting the dreadlocking process.
  3. Section your hair: Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the method you choose.
  4. Start the dreadlocking process: Depending on the method you choose, you may need to backcomb, twist and rip, or simply let your hair mat and tangle naturally. Follow the instructions for your chosen method carefully.
  5. Maintain your dreadlocks: Once your dreadlocks have formed, you'll need to maintain them regularly. This may include washing, separating, and palm rolling your dreadlocks to keep them looking neat and tidy.

Tips for Starting Dreadlocks

Here are some tips to help you start your dreadlocks:

  • Be patient: Starting dreadlocks can be a lengthy process, so be prepared to wait several months or even years for your hair to fully lock up.
  • Use the right products: There are many products on the market that are specifically designed for dreadlocks. Use a residue-free shampoo and conditioner, and consider using locking gel or wax to help your hair lock up faster.
  • Maintain your dreadlocks: Regular maintenance is crucial for keeping your dreadlocks looking neat and tidy. Wash your hair regularly, and use a dreadlock-specific shampoo and conditioner.
  • Be prepared for some discomfort: Starting dreadlocks can be uncomfortable and even painful, especially if you're using the backcombing or twist and rip methods. Be prepared for some discomfort, but remember that it will be worth it in the end.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks:

  • Do you start dreadlocks with wet or dry hair? There's no right or wrong answer to this question. Some people prefer to start with wet hair because it's easier to work with, while others prefer to start with dry hair because it's less prone to breakage.
  • How long does it take to start dreadlocks? The length of time it takes to start dreadlocks depends on your hair type and the method you choose. It can take anywhere from several months to a few years to fully lock up your hair.
  • Can you start dreadlocks with short hair? Yes, you can start dreadlocks with short hair. However, it may take longer for your hair to fully lock up, and you may need to use a different method than someone with longer hair.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once your dreadlocks have formed, it's important to take care of them properly to keep them looking neat and tidy. Here are some tips for hair care for dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Use a residue-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Separate your dreadlocks: Use your fingers to separate any matted or tangled strands of hair to keep your dreadlocks looking neat and tidy.
  • Palm roll your dreadlocks: Use your palms to roll your dreadlocks regularly to help them lock up and stay tight.
  • Avoid using products with residue: Products with residue can cause buildup and make your dreadlocks look dirty and unkempt. Stick to residue-free products.

Starting dreadlocks can be a challenging process, but with patience and dedication, you can achieve the look you want. Whether you choose to start with wet or dry hair, remember to take care of your dreadlocks properly to keep them looking healthy and beautiful.
