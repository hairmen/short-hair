How To Fade Hair Men s Haircut

Written by Luffy Sep 04, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and modern haircut, a fade is a great option. Fades are popular among men for their clean and sharp look, and they can be customized to suit different hair types and styles. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to fade hair men's haircut, including tips and hair care advice.

Description

A fade haircut involves gradually tapering the length of hair from the top of the head down to the sides and back. The result is a smooth and seamless transition from longer hair to shorter hair, creating a gradient effect. Fades can be high, medium, or low, depending on how high up the head the fade starts. They can also be combined with other styles, such as a buzz cut or a pompadour.

How to Fade Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Decide on the Type of Fade

Before you start cutting, you need to decide on the type of fade you want. High fades start near the top of the head and fade down to the sides and back. Medium fades start around the temples and fade down to the sides and back. Low fades start at the hairline and fade up to the sides and back. Choose the type of fade that suits your face shape and hair type.

Step 2: Cut the Top of the Hair

Start by cutting the top of the hair to the desired length. Use scissors or clippers to trim the hair evenly, making sure to leave enough length for the fade to blend in. If you want a textured look, use scissors to cut the hair using a point-cutting technique.

Step 3: Choose the Guard Size

Next, choose the guard size for your clippers. The guard size will determine how short the hair will be on the sides and back. Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually to achieve a smooth transition.

Step 4: Start Cutting the Hair

Start cutting the hair at the bottom of the fade line, using the clippers with the chosen guard size. Move the clippers up and down in a smooth motion, making sure to blend the hair evenly. Repeat this process, gradually moving up the head and changing to a shorter guard size until you reach the desired length.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've reached the desired length, it's time to blend the hair. Use the clippers without a guard to blend the hair between the fade line and the longer hair on top. Use a light touch and blend the hair evenly to create a seamless transition.

Step 6: Finish the Haircut

Finish the haircut by trimming any stray hairs and cleaning up the hairline around the ears and neck. Use scissors to texturize the hair on top and create a polished look.

Tips for Fading Hair

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a mirror to check the symmetry of the fade from different angles.
  • Use a comb to help guide the clippers and create a smooth transition.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or a professional barber.

FAQ

What hair types are suitable for fades?

Fades can work for most hair types, including straight, curly, and wavy hair. However, it's important to consider your hair texture and face shape when choosing the type of fade.

How often should I get a fade haircut?

It depends on the length of the fade and how quickly your hair grows. Generally, fades need to be touched up every 2-4 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

Hair Care for Faded Hair

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, as they can damage the hair and make it brittle.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to add shine and prevent frizz.
  • Visit a professional hairstylist for regular trims and maintenance.

With these tips and steps on how to fade hair men's haircut, you can achieve a stylish and modern look that suits your personality and style. Remember to take care of your hair and visit a professional for guidance and advice.
