What Causes Severe Hair Shedding Understanding The Root Of The Problem

Written by Robby Jun 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Hair shedding is a natural process that occurs to everyone. On average, a person loses around 50 to 100 strands of hair per day. However, if you notice that you are losing more hair than usual, it may be a cause for concern. Severe hair shedding can be caused by various factors, and it is important to understand the root of the problem to effectively address it.

What Causes Severe Hair Shedding?

There are several factors that can cause severe hair shedding. Here are some of the most common causes:

Stress

Stress is one of the leading causes of hair shedding. When you are stressed, your body releases a hormone called cortisol, which can affect your hair growth cycle. This can result in excessive shedding and thinning of the hair.

Poor Nutrition

What you eat can also affect your hair health. A diet that lacks essential nutrients, such as protein, vitamins, and minerals, can lead to hair shedding. Iron deficiency is also a common cause of hair loss.

Hormonal Imbalance

Hormonal changes can also cause hair shedding. For instance, women may experience hair loss after giving birth or during menopause due to changes in their hormone levels.

Medical Conditions

Medical conditions, such as thyroid problems, scalp infections, and autoimmune disorders, can also cause hair shedding. If you notice sudden hair loss, it is best to consult a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

How to Address Severe Hair Shedding

If you are experiencing severe hair shedding, there are several things you can do to address the problem:

Improve Your Diet

Eating a diet that is rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Incorporate foods such as eggs, fish, nuts, and leafy greens into your diet.

Reduce Stress

Reducing stress through activities such as meditation, yoga, or exercise can help regulate your cortisol levels and promote healthy hair growth.

Consult a Professional

If your hair shedding is caused by an underlying medical condition, it is best to consult a healthcare professional. They can prescribe medication or recommend treatments to address the root of the problem.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Preventing hair shedding starts with maintaining healthy hair. Here are some tips to help keep your hair healthy:

Avoid Overstyling

Overstyling, such as using heat tools or chemical treatments, can damage your hair and lead to shedding. Limit your use of these products and opt for natural hairstyles instead.

Use Gentle Hair Products

Using harsh hair products can also damage your hair and lead to shedding. Use gentle products that are free of sulfates and parabens to promote healthy hair growth.

Protect Your Hair

Protecting your hair from environmental damage, such as UV rays and pollution, can also promote healthy hair growth. Wear a hat or use a hair protectant spray when you are exposed to these elements.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much hair shedding is normal?

On average, a person loses around 50 to 100 strands of hair per day. This is considered normal and is part of the hair growth cycle.

Can medication cause hair shedding?

Yes, certain medications can cause hair shedding as a side effect. If you notice sudden hair loss after starting a new medication, consult your healthcare professional.

Can hair shedding be reversed?

Yes, in most cases, hair shedding can be reversed by addressing the underlying cause. However, it may take some time for your hair to fully recover.

Hair Care: Taking Care of Your Hair

Remember, healthy hair starts with taking care of your body. Eat a balanced diet, reduce stress, and protect your hair from damage to promote healthy hair growth. If you are experiencing severe hair shedding, consult a healthcare professional to address the root of the problem.
