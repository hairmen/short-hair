How To Repair Damaged Hair From Bleach Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Sep 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you've ever bleached your hair, you know how it feels to have damaged and brittle strands. Bleaching can be tough on your hair, but it's not the end of the world. There are ways to repair your hair and bring it back to life. This article will guide you through the steps of repairing damaged hair from bleach, and provide you with tips and tricks to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Description

Bleaching removes the natural pigment in your hair, leaving it lighter and more prone to damage. The bleach can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. If you've bleached your hair and are now dealing with the aftermath, don't worry. With the right care and attention, you can repair your hair and bring it back to life.

How to Repair Damaged Hair from Bleach

The first step in repairing damaged hair from bleach is to cut off the damaged hair. This may sound extreme, but it's necessary to prevent further damage. Once you've cut off the damaged hair, you can start to focus on repairing the hair that's left. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Deep Conditioner

Deep conditioning treatments are essential for repairing damaged hair from bleach. Look for a conditioner that contains ingredients like keratin, amino acids, and proteins, which will help to rebuild and strengthen your hair. Leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes, or overnight if possible.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments are another great way to repair damaged hair from bleach. These treatments penetrate the hair shaft and help to rebuild the hair from the inside out. Look for a protein treatment that contains hydrolyzed keratin or collagen.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can further damage already weakened hair. Try to avoid heat styling as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray if you must use heat styling tools.

4. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to protect your hair from further damage. Look for a conditioner that contains ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil, which will help to moisturize and strengthen your hair.

5. Be Gentle When Brushing

When your hair is damaged, it's more prone to breakage. Be gentle when brushing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to repairing damaged hair from bleach:

Step 1: Cut off the damaged hair.

Start by cutting off the damaged hair. This will prevent further damage and allow you to focus on repairing the hair that's left.

Step 2: Use a deep conditioner.

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes, or overnight if possible.

Step 3: Use a protein treatment.

Apply a protein treatment to your hair, following the instructions on the packaging. Be careful not to overuse protein treatments, as too much protein can make your hair brittle.

Step 4: Avoid heat styling.

Avoid heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, or use a heat protectant spray if you must use heat styling tools.

Step 5: Use a leave-in conditioner.

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. This will help to protect your hair from further damage.

Step 6: Be gentle when brushing.

Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to gently brush your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Tips for Repairing Damaged Hair from Bleach

Here are some additional tips for repairing damaged hair from bleach:

1. Avoid harsh chemicals.

Avoid using harsh chemicals like hair dyes or relaxers, which can further damage your hair.

2. Protect your hair from the sun.

The sun can damage your hair just like it can damage your skin. Wear a hat or use a protective hair spray when you're out in the sun.

3. Eat a healthy diet.

Eating a healthy diet can help to promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to eat plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.

4. Drink plenty of water.

Drinking plenty of water can help to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

FAQs

Q: Can I repair my hair after bleaching?

A: Yes, you can repair your hair after bleaching. The key is to cut off the damaged hair and focus on repairing the hair that's left.

Q: How long does it take to repair damaged hair from bleach?

A: It can take several weeks or months to repair damaged hair from bleach. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Q: Can I bleach my hair again after repairing it?

A: It's best to avoid bleaching your hair again after repairing it, as this can cause further damage.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Damage

Preventing damage is always better than repairing it. Here are some hair care tips to help prevent damage:

1. Use a gentle shampoo.

Use a gentle shampoo that's free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals.

2. Limit heat styling.

Limit your use of heat styling tools, and use a heat protectant spray if you must use them.

3. Protect your hair from the elements.

Wear a hat or use a protective hair spray when you're out in the sun or wind.

4. Eat a healthy diet.

Eating a healthy diet can help to promote healthy hair growth.

5. Drink plenty of water.

Drinking plenty of water can help to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you can repair your damaged hair from bleach and keep it healthy and strong. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you'll see results in no time.
