Autoimmune Diseases That Cause Eyebrow Loss Understanding The Connection

Written by Eveline Sep 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Autoimmune diseases are a group of conditions where the immune system mistakenly attacks healthy cells in the body. This can lead to a range of symptoms and complications, including hair loss. In this article, we will explore the autoimmune diseases that cause eyebrow loss, their symptoms, and some tips for managing hair loss associated with these conditions.

What are the autoimmune diseases that cause eyebrow loss?

There are several autoimmune diseases that can cause hair loss, including:

  • Alopecia Areata
  • Frontal Fibrosing Alopecia
  • Discoid Lupus Erythematosus
  • Hashimoto's Thyroiditis
  • Scleroderma

Each of these conditions affects the body in different ways and can cause varying degrees of eyebrow loss. Let's take a closer look at each of them.

Alopecia Areata

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disease that causes hair loss on the scalp, face, and other parts of the body. It occurs when the immune system attacks hair follicles, resulting in patchy hair loss. In some cases, it can cause the loss of eyebrows and eyelashes.

Frontal Fibrosing Alopecia

Frontal fibrosing alopecia is a type of hair loss that typically affects women after menopause. It causes hair loss along the hairline and can also cause the loss of eyebrows and eyelashes.

Discoid Lupus Erythematosus

Discoid lupus erythematosus (DLE) is a chronic autoimmune disease that affects the skin. It causes scaly, red patches on the skin, and can cause permanent hair loss on the scalp and eyebrows.

Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

Hashimoto's thyroiditis is an autoimmune disease that affects the thyroid gland. It causes the gland to become inflamed, which can lead to an underactive thyroid. This can cause thinning hair and the loss of eyebrows.

Scleroderma

Scleroderma is a chronic autoimmune disease that causes thickening and hardening of the skin and connective tissues. It can cause hair loss on the scalp and eyebrows, as well as thinning and hardening of the skin on the face.

What are the symptoms of eyebrow loss due to autoimmune diseases?

The symptoms of eyebrow loss due to autoimmune diseases can vary depending on the underlying condition. Some common symptoms include:

  • Patchy or complete loss of eyebrows
  • Thinning or brittle hair
  • Red, scaly patches on the skin
  • Hardening or thickening of the skin
  • Swelling or inflammation in the affected area

How to manage eyebrow loss due to autoimmune diseases?

Managing eyebrow loss due to autoimmune diseases can be challenging, but there are some things you can do to help minimize the effects of hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Consult with a dermatologist or other healthcare provider to determine the underlying cause of your hair loss.
  • Consider using a brow pencil or powder to fill in areas where hair loss is most noticeable.
  • Avoid pulling or rubbing the remaining eyebrow hair, as this can cause further damage.
  • Use gentle, non-irritating skincare and hair care products to avoid exacerbating any skin conditions that may be causing hair loss.
  • Consider wearing a hat or scarf to protect your eyebrows from sun damage or harsh weather conditions.
  • Explore medical treatments, such as corticosteroid injections, to help stimulate hair growth.
