What Causes Weak Brittle Fingernails

Written by William Jun 20, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of having weak and brittle fingernails? Do you often find yourself unable to grow your nails to a desired length? If so, you’re not alone. Weak and brittle nails are a common problem among many people, and can be caused by several different factors. In this article, we will discuss the most common causes of weak and brittle fingernails and provide tips for preventing and treating them.

What Causes Weak Brittle Fingernails?

There are several factors that can contribute to weak and brittle nails. One of the most common causes is a lack of moisture. When nails become dry, they can become brittle and prone to breakage. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including frequent hand washing, exposure to harsh chemicals, and exposure to cold weather.

Another common cause of weak and brittle nails is a deficiency in certain vitamins and minerals. Vitamins such as Biotin and Vitamin E are essential for nail health and growth. If you’re not getting enough of these nutrients in your diet, your nails may become weak and brittle.

Other factors that can contribute to weak and brittle nails include certain medical conditions, such as thyroid disorders, and lifestyle factors, such as smoking and excessive alcohol consumption. If you’re experiencing persistent problems with weak and brittle nails, it’s important to talk to your healthcare provider to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

How to Prevent Weak Brittle Fingernails

If you want to prevent weak and brittle nails, there are several steps you can take. One of the most important things you can do is to keep your nails moisturized. This can be done by using a moisturizing lotion or oil on your nails and cuticles regularly.

Another important step is to avoid exposing your nails to harsh chemicals and excessive water. If you’re washing dishes or cleaning, be sure to wear gloves to protect your nails. You should also avoid using nail polish remover that contains acetone, as this can dry out your nails and make them more brittle.

Finally, it’s important to maintain a healthy diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals that are essential for nail health. This can include foods such as eggs, nuts, and leafy greens that are high in Biotin and Vitamin E.

How to Treat Weak Brittle Fingernails

If you’re already experiencing problems with weak and brittle nails, there are several treatments that can help. One of the most effective treatments is to use a nail strengthener or hardener. These products contain ingredients that can help to strengthen your nails and prevent breakage.

Another treatment option is to use a cuticle oil or cream. These products can help to moisturize your nails and promote healthy growth. You should also consider taking a supplement that contains Biotin and other essential vitamins and minerals for nail health.

Tips for Keeping Your Nails Healthy

There are several tips you can follow to keep your nails healthy and strong. One of the most important things you can do is to keep your nails trimmed and filed regularly. This will help to prevent breakage and keep your nails looking neat and tidy.

You should also avoid using your nails as tools for opening cans or packages. This can cause your nails to become weak and prone to breakage. Instead, use a proper tool for the job.

Finally, it’s important to protect your nails from the sun. UV rays can damage your nails and cause them to become weak and brittle. If you’re going to be spending time in the sun, be sure to wear sunscreen on your hands and nails.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can a lack of calcium cause weak and brittle nails?

A: While calcium is important for overall bone health, it’s not directly related to nail health. However, a deficiency in other vitamins and minerals, such as Biotin and Vitamin E, can contribute to weak and brittle nails.

Q: Is it safe to use nail hardeners and strengtheners?

A: Yes, nail hardeners and strengtheners are generally safe to use. However, it’s important to follow the instructions carefully and avoid overuse, as this can actually make your nails more brittle.

Q: Can I use coconut oil to moisturize my nails?

A: Yes, coconut oil can be a great natural moisturizer for your nails and cuticles. Simply apply a small amount to your nails and massage gently.

Conclusion

Weak and brittle nails can be a frustrating and embarrassing problem, but there are several steps you can take to prevent and treat them. By keeping your nails moisturized, avoiding harsh chemicals, and maintaining a healthy diet, you can help to keep your nails strong and healthy. If you’re experiencing persistent problems with weak and brittle nails, be sure to talk to your healthcare provider to rule out any underlying medical conditions.
