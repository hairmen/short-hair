How To Cut The Front Parts Of Your Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 26, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, cutting the front parts of your hair can make a big difference. This is a great way to add some layers and framing around your face. However, cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're not used to doing it. But don't worry, with a few tips and tricks, you can easily cut the front parts of your hair at home. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to do it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut the Front Parts of Your Hair
  • Tips for Cutting Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

How to Cut the Front Parts of Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section your hair.

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting. Then, comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to create a part in the middle of your hair. Then, section off the front parts of your hair on each side of your face. Use the hair clips to secure the sections of hair you're not working on.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Decide how much length you want to cut off. It's better to start with a small amount and cut more if you need to. Remember, you can always cut more later, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Hold the section of hair you want to cut between your fingers. Use your scissors to make small snips, cutting vertically into the hair. This will create a more natural, layered look. Take your time and be careful not to cut too much at once.

Repeat this process on the other side of your face, making sure to keep the sections of hair even.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments and trim any stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you cut your hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut too much at once.
  • Take your time and be patient.
  • Check your work as you go.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting the front parts of your hair:

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair. Just make sure to cut it when it's dry so you can see how it will look.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim more hair later to fix it. If you're really unhappy with the way it looks, you can always go to a professional stylist to fix it.

How often should I cut the front parts of my hair?

This depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to change up your hairstyle. As a general rule, you should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Make sure to use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a low heat setting on your blow dryer. Finally, don't forget to get regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.

Now that you know how to cut the front parts of your hair, you can give yourself a new look without leaving your home. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and be patient. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting your own hair in no time.
