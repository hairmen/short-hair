Does Hair Shed More When It s Growing

Written by Petter Jun 21, 2023 Â· 3 min read

As we all know, hair plays an important role in our overall appearance. It's our crowning glory and we always want to keep it healthy and shiny. But, what happens when we start to notice that our hair is shedding more than usual? Does hair shed more when it's growing? In this article, we'll answer this common question and provide some tips on how to maintain healthy hair.

Table of Contents:

  • What is hair shedding?
  • Does hair shed more when it's growing?
  • How to maintain healthy hair?
  • Tips to reduce hair shedding
  • FAQs
  • Conclusion

What is hair shedding?

Hair shedding is a natural process where hair falls out from the scalp. It's a normal cycle for hair growth and loss. On average, we lose around 50-100 hairs per day. Shedding is different from hair loss, which is when hair falls out in clumps or patches. Hair loss can be caused by various factors such as genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and medical conditions.

Does hair shed more when it's growing?

Yes, hair shedding can increase when hair is growing. When new hair strands are growing, they push out the old ones, which can result in more shedding. This is a temporary phase and should not be a cause for concern. However, if you notice excessive shedding for a prolonged period, it's best to consult a dermatologist to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

How to maintain healthy hair?

Maintaining healthy hair is important to prevent excessive shedding and hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Keep your scalp clean and moisturized
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Get enough sleep and manage stress levels
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Limit the use of heating tools on your hair

Tips to reduce hair shedding

If you're experiencing excessive hair shedding, here are some tips to reduce it:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  • Massage your scalp with essential oils
  • Take supplements such as biotin and iron
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Try a hair mask to nourish and strengthen your hair

FAQs

1. How can I tell if I'm shedding too much hair?

If you're shedding more than 100 hairs per day or notice bald patches, it's best to consult a dermatologist.

2. Can stress cause hair shedding?

Yes, stress can cause hair shedding. It's important to manage stress levels to prevent excessive shedding and hair loss.

3. Are there any medical conditions that can cause hair shedding?

Yes, medical conditions such as thyroid disorders and alopecia can cause hair shedding. It's best to consult a dermatologist if you're experiencing excessive shedding.

Conclusion

In conclusion, hair shedding is a natural process for hair growth and loss. It's normal to experience more shedding when new hair strands are growing. However, if you notice excessive shedding for a prolonged period, it's best to consult a dermatologist to rule out any underlying medical conditions. Maintaining healthy hair is important to prevent excessive shedding and hair loss. Follow the tips mentioned in this article to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
