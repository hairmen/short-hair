Which Red Hair Suits My Skin Tone

Written by Frank Jul 02, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're considering dyeing your hair red, you may be wondering which shade of red hair will suit your skin tone. Choosing the right shade is important to ensure that your hair color complements your complexion and enhances your overall look. In this article, we'll discuss how to determine your skin tone and which shades of red hair are best suited to various skin tones.

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in determining which shade of red hair suits your skin tone is to determine your skin tone. There are three basic skin tones: cool, warm, and neutral. Here's how to determine your skin tone:

Cool Skin Tone

If you have cool skin tones, your skin will have pink, red, or blue undertones. Your veins will appear blue or purple, and you may burn easily in the sun. Your skin may also appear pale or porcelain.

Warm Skin Tone

If you have warm skin tones, your skin will have yellow, peach, or golden undertones. Your veins will appear greenish, and you may tan easily in the sun. Your skin may also appear bronze or honey.

Neutral Skin Tone

If you have neutral skin tones, your skin will have a mix of cool and warm undertones. Your veins will appear both blue and green, and you may tan or burn in the sun.

Which Shades of Red Hair Suit Your Skin Tone?

Now that you've determined your skin tone, let's discuss which shades of red hair are best suited to each skin tone.

Cool Skin Tone

If you have cool skin tones, you should opt for cool shades of red hair, such as burgundy, maroon, or dark cherry. These shades will complement your skin tone and enhance your overall look. Avoid warm shades of red hair, such as copper or auburn, as they can make your skin look washed out.

Warm Skin Tone

If you have warm skin tones, you should opt for warm shades of red hair, such as copper, auburn, or strawberry blonde. These shades will complement your skin tone and enhance your overall look. Avoid cool shades of red hair, such as burgundy or maroon, as they can clash with your warm undertones.

Neutral Skin Tone

If you have neutral skin tones, you can opt for both warm and cool shades of red hair. Consider shades such as cinnamon, chestnut, or auburn. These shades will complement your skin tone and enhance your overall look.

Tips for Maintaining Red Hair

Once you've chosen your shade of red hair, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure that the color lasts as long as possible. Here are some tips for maintaining red hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent color fading
  • Avoid hot tools, such as hair straighteners and curling irons, as they can fade the color
  • Avoid swimming in chlorinated water, as it can fade the color
  • Consider using a color-depositing shampoo or conditioner to enhance and maintain the color

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about which red hair suits different skin tones:

Can cool skin tones wear warm shades of red hair?

While it's not recommended, cool skin tones can wear warm shades of red hair. However, it's important to choose a shade that complements your skin tone, such as a warm auburn or strawberry blonde.

Can warm skin tones wear cool shades of red hair?

While it's not recommended, warm skin tones can wear cool shades of red hair. However, it's important to choose a shade that complements your skin tone, such as a cool burgundy or maroon.

Can neutral skin tones wear any shade of red hair?

Yes, neutral skin tones can wear both warm and cool shades of red hair. However, it's important to choose a shade that complements your skin tone, such as chestnut or cinnamon.

Conclusion

Choosing the right shade of red hair can be daunting, but by determining your skin tone and considering the shades that complement it, you can find the perfect shade for you. Remember to take care of your red hair to ensure that the color lasts as long as possible.
