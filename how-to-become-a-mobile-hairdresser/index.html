How To Become A Mobile Hairdresser

Written by Oliver Nov 10, 2023 · 4 min read

In this year 2023, becoming a mobile hairdresser is a fantastic career choice. You can work from home, travel to client’s homes, and have a flexible schedule. In this article, we'll explain how to become a mobile hairdresser, step by step. We'll also provide helpful tips, answer frequently asked questions, and give you some hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Get Trained and Certified
  • Step 2: Get Licensed
  • Step 3: Build Your Business
  • Step 4: Set Your Prices

Step 1: Get Trained and Certified

The first step to becoming a mobile hairdresser is to get trained and certified. You'll need to learn the skills of cutting, coloring, and styling hair. You can attend a vocational school, community college, or a private hairdressing academy. There are also online courses available. Choose a program that works for you and fits your schedule.

After completing your training, you should obtain certification. This will show potential clients that you are competent and professional. You can get certified by taking an exam administered by a professional organization or association.

Step 2: Get Licensed

Before you can legally work as a mobile hairdresser, you'll need to obtain a license. Requirements vary by state, so check with your state's licensing board. In general, you'll need to pass an exam and meet certain education and experience requirements.

Some states also require continuing education to maintain your license. Make sure you stay up-to-date on the latest trends and techniques in the hairdressing industry.

Step 3: Build Your Business

Now that you're trained and licensed, it's time to build your business. You'll need to decide if you want to work as an independent contractor or start your own business. If you're starting your own business, you'll need to choose a name, register your business, and obtain any necessary permits and licenses.

You'll also need to purchase equipment, such as a portable hairdressing chair, scissors, combs, and hairdryers. You may also want to invest in a mobile salon van or trailer.

Step 4: Set Your Prices

Setting your prices can be tricky. You want to be competitive but also make a profit. Research what other mobile hairdressers in your area are charging and set your prices accordingly. Consider your experience, education, and the services you offer.

Remember to factor in travel expenses and the cost of your equipment. You may also want to offer package deals or discounts for repeat clients.

Step 5: Market Your Services

Now that you're ready to start working, you need to market your services. Create business cards, flyers, and a website. Use social media to connect with potential clients.

Offer promotions or discounts to new clients. Consider partnering with local businesses, such as hotels or event planners.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips for success as a mobile hairdresser:

  • Be reliable and punctual. Your clients are counting on you to show up on time.
  • Be professional. Dress appropriately and maintain a clean and organized workspace.
  • Stay up-to-date on the latest trends and techniques in the hairdressing industry.
  • Offer excellent customer service. Listen to your clients' needs and communicate clearly.
  • Build relationships with your clients. Repeat business is essential to your success.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about becoming a mobile hairdresser:

  • Do I need a specific vehicle to work as a mobile hairdresser?
  • Not necessarily. You can use your own car or purchase a mobile salon van or trailer.
  • How much money can I make as a mobile hairdresser?
  • It varies depending on your location, services offered, and experience. However, mobile hairdressers can make a good income.

Hair Care Advice

As a mobile hairdresser, you'll need to provide your clients with hair care advice. Here are some tips:

  • Use high-quality products.
  • Wash your hair regularly but not too often.
  • Use a heat protectant when using hot styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and excessive heat styling.

Now that you know how to become a mobile hairdresser, take the first step and enroll in a training program. With hard work and dedication, you can build a successful career as a mobile hairdresser.
