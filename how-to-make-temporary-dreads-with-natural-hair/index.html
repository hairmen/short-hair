Temporary Dreads With Natural Hair How To Make Them

Written by Bardi Aug 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you thinking of trying out dreadlocks but aren't sure if you're ready for a permanent commitment? Temporary dreadlocks are a great way to experiment with this unique hairstyle without committing to it for the long haul. In this article, we'll show you how to make temporary dreads with natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Temporary Dreads with Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Making Temporary Dreads
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Temporary Dreads

How to Make Temporary Dreads with Natural Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that this method will only work on hair that is at least 3 inches long. If your hair is shorter than that, you may want to consider using extensions to create the dreads.

The first step is to make sure your hair is clean and dry. You can either wash your hair or use dry shampoo to remove any excess oil or product buildup.

Next, section off your hair into small, equal-sized sections. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be. Use hair elastics or clips to keep each section in place.

Now it's time to start backcombing each section. Take one section at a time and use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb the hair from the tips to the roots. This will create tangles and knots in the hair, which will eventually form into dreadlocks.

Once you've backcombed each section, use a dreadlock hook or your fingers to twist the hair in a clockwise direction. This will help the hair form into a tight coil. Continue twisting until the entire section is twisted into a dreadlock.

Repeat this process for each section of hair until your entire head is covered in dreadlocks.

Step-by-Step Guide

To sum up the process, here is a step-by-step guide:

  1. Clean and dry your hair
  2. Section off your hair into small, equal-sized sections
  3. Backcomb each section with a fine-toothed comb
  4. Twist each section clockwise with a dreadlock hook or your fingers
  5. Continue twisting until the entire section is twisted into a dreadlock
  6. Repeat for each section of hair

Tips for Making Temporary Dreads

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your temporary dreadlocks:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to help keep your hair moisturized
  • Wrap your hair at night with a silk or satin scarf to help prevent frizz and breakage
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as they can weigh down your dreads and make them look greasy
  • Be patient - it can take several weeks for your dreads to fully form and mature
  • Experiment with different styles, such as updos or half-up, half-down styles, to keep your dreads looking fresh and interesting

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about temporary dreads:

How long will my temporary dreads last?

Temporary dreads can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months, depending on how well you care for them.

Can I wash my hair with temporary dreads?

Yes, you can still wash your hair with temporary dreads. Use a residue-free shampoo and be gentle when washing and drying your hair.

Can I remove my temporary dreads whenever I want?

Yes, you can remove your temporary dreads at any time. Simply use a comb to carefully unravel each dreadlock.

Hair Care for Temporary Dreads

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair while you have temporary dreads:

  • Wash your hair at least once a week with a residue-free shampoo
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to help keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and make it more difficult to form dreadlocks
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage to your dreads
  • Wrap your hair at night with a silk or satin scarf to help prevent frizz and breakage

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to make temporary dreads with natural hair and enjoy this unique hairstyle without the long-term commitment. Have fun experimenting with your new look!
