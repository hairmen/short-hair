How to Cut Perfect Side Swept Bangs - Step by Step Guide

Are you tired of constantly going to the salon to get your bangs trimmed? Do you want to learn how to cut your own side swept bangs? Look no further! In this step by step guide, we will teach you how to cut perfect side swept bangs in the comfort of your own home.

Table of Contents

  2. How to Cut Perfect Side Swept Bangs
  3. Step by Step Guide

Description

Side swept bangs are a popular hairstyle that can add dimension and style to any haircut. They are versatile and can be worn with any hair length or texture. However, getting the perfect cut can be a challenge, especially if you're doing it yourself. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look in no time.

How to Cut Perfect Side Swept Bangs

Before we get started, it's important to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also helpful to have clips to section off your hair.

Step by Step Guide

  • Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair forward and separate the section you want to cut into a triangle shape.
  • Step 2: Use your comb to create a straight part down the middle of the triangle section.
  • Step 3: Comb the hair on one side of the part forward and clip the rest of your hair back.
  • Step 4: Hold your scissors at a slight angle and begin cutting the hair. Start with small snips, and work your way across the section. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back.
  • Step 5: Repeat the process on the other side of the part, making sure the length and angle match the first side.
  • Step 6: Release the clipped hair and check your work. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure both sides are even.
  • Step 7: Style your bangs to your desired shape and length.

Tips for Cutting Perfect Side Swept Bangs

When cutting your own side swept bangs, it's important to remember a few key tips:

  • Tip 1: Start with small snips and work your way across the section. It's better to take your time and cut slowly than to make a mistake and have to start over.
  • Tip 2: Use a mirror to check your work from all angles. It's easy to miss a spot or cut too much hair if you're only looking at yourself head-on.
  • Tip 3: Cut your bangs when they are dry. Wet hair can stretch and shrink, which can affect the length and angle of your cut.
  • Tip 4: Don't cut your bangs too short. It's better to have longer bangs that you can trim later than to have bangs that are too short and take months to grow out.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own side swept bangs if I have curly or wavy hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own side swept bangs regardless of your hair texture. Just make sure to straighten your hair before cutting to ensure a more accurate cut.

Q: How often should I cut my side swept bangs?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look uneven. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Q: What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

A: Don't panic! You can always take your bangs to a professional stylist to fix any mistakes. If the mistake is minor, you can also try to blend it in with the rest of your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Side Swept Bangs

Now that you know how to cut perfect side swept bangs, it's important to take care of them to ensure they stay looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Tip 1: Don't touch your bangs too much. The oils from your fingers can make them look greasy and dull.
  • Tip 2: Use a dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh between washes.
  • Tip 3: Avoid using too much heat on your bangs. Over-styling with heat tools can cause damage and split ends.
  • Tip 4: Trim your bangs regularly to keep them looking neat and tidy.

With these tips and techniques, you can now confidently cut your own perfect side swept bangs. Say goodbye to salon appointments and hello to beautiful, stylish bangs!
