How To Make Your Natural Hair Fluffy Tips Tricks And Techniques

Are you tired of your natural hair looking flat and lifeless? Do you want to add some volume and bounce to your locks? If so, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll share some tips, tricks, and techniques for making your natural hair fluffy and voluminous.

How to Make Your Natural Hair Fluffy

Before we dive into the steps, let's talk about what exactly is fluffy hair. Fluffy hair is hair that has volume, bounce, and movement. It's not flat or weighed down by products, and it has a natural, effortless look.

To achieve fluffy hair, you'll need to follow these steps:

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

The first step to achieving fluffy hair is to use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products are designed to add volume and body to your hair, making it easier to style and fluff up. Look for products that contain ingredients like hydrolyzed wheat protein, keratin, and biotin.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Before you start blow-drying your hair, flip your head upside down. This will help add volume to your roots and create a natural lift. Use a round brush to lift your roots even more, and aim the blow dryer nozzle at your roots to add heat and set the lift.

3. Use Velcro Rollers

Velcro rollers are a great tool for adding volume and bounce to your hair. After blow-drying your hair, roll sections of hair around the rollers and secure them in place. Leave the rollers in for at least 10 minutes to set the volume, and then gently remove them.

4. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair is a great way to add volume and texture. Use a teasing comb or brush to backcomb your roots, and then smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased section. This will create a natural lift and add volume to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of how to make your natural hair fluffy, let's break it down into a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly and gently towel dry your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Volumizing Mousse

Apply a volumizing mousse to your roots and work it through to the ends of your hair. This will help add volume and body to your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Flip your head upside down and blow dry your hair using a round brush to lift your roots. Aim the blow dryer nozzle at your roots to set the lift.

Step 4: Use Velcro Rollers

Roll sections of hair around velcro rollers and secure them in place. Leave the rollers in for at least 10 minutes to set the volume, and then gently remove them.

Step 5: Tease Your Hair

Use a teasing comb or brush to backcomb your roots, and then smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased section. This will create a natural lift and add volume to your hair.

Tips for Fluffy Hair

Here are some additional tips for achieving fluffy hair:

1. Use a Root Lifter

A root lifter is a spray or foam that you apply to your roots before blow-drying. It will help add volume and lift to your roots, making your hair look fuller and more voluminous.

2. Use a Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is a great tool for adding volume and texture to your hair. Spray it at your roots to absorb oil and add lift.

3. Avoid Heavy Products

Heavy products like oils and serums can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Instead, use lightweight products that won't weigh down your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner every day?

A: Yes, you can use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner every day. However, if you have dry or damaged hair, you may want to alternate with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.

Q: Will teasing my hair damage it?

A: Teasing your hair can cause damage if you do it too often or if you use a teasing comb or brush that's too harsh. Be gentle when teasing your hair, and don't do it every day.

Q: Can I use velcro rollers on wet hair?

A: No, you should never use velcro rollers on wet hair. Wait until your hair is at least 80% dry before using velcro rollers.

Hair Care for Fluffy Hair

Now that you know how to make your natural hair fluffy, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or use a dry shampoo in between washes.

2. Use a Heat Protectant

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons can damage your hair if you don't use a heat protectant. Spray a heat protectant on your hair before using heat styling tools.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims will help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

By following these tips and techniques, you can make your natural hair fluffy and voluminous. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type and texture. With a little patience and practice, you'll have fluffy hair that's the envy of all your friends.
