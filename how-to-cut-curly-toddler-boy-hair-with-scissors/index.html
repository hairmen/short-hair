How To Cut Curly Toddler Boy Hair With Scissors

Written by Petter Nov 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a parent of a curly-haired toddler boy, you may be wondering how to cut their hair without making it look uneven or choppy. While it may seem like a daunting task, with the right tools and technique, you can give your little one a great haircut. In this article, we'll go through the steps for cutting curly toddler boy hair with scissors, as well as some tips and tricks for getting the best results.

Description

Cutting curly toddler boy hair with scissors is a delicate process that requires patience and precision. Curly hair has a tendency to shrink when dry, so it's important to cut it while it's wet and stretched out. Using scissors instead of clippers can help you control the length and shape of the haircut, giving you more flexibility to create a personalized style.

How to

Here are the steps for cutting curly toddler boy hair with scissors:

  1. Wash your child's hair and comb it straight. Make sure you detangle any knots or snarls.
  2. Dampen the hair with a spray bottle or wet comb. This will help you see the natural curl pattern and prevent the hair from shrinking up.
  3. Section the hair into small sections using hair clips or elastic bands. This will make it easier to work with and prevent you from cutting too much at once.
  4. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up, cutting the hair in small sections. Use a comb to hold the hair taut and trim the ends with scissors. Don't cut too much at once, as curly hair tends to shrink up when dry.
  5. Move to the sides and top of the head, continuing to cut small sections of hair. Use the comb to lift the hair up and cut the ends straight across. Make sure the length is even on both sides.
  6. Check the back of the head in a mirror to make sure the length is even and there are no stray hairs.
  7. Trim around the ears and neckline using small, precise cuts. This will give the haircut a clean, finished look.
  8. Style the hair as desired. You can use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define the curls and prevent frizz.
  9. Clean up any stray hairs using a brush or vacuum. You may also want to give your child a quick rinse to remove any loose hairs.
  10. Admire your handiwork! Your little one now has a fresh, stylish haircut.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting curly toddler boy hair with scissors:

  • Use sharp, high-quality scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Keep the hair damp throughout the cutting process. Dry hair can be difficult to manage and may lead to uneven cuts.
  • Don't cut too much at once. Curly hair tends to shrink up when dry, so it's better to err on the side of caution and cut less than you think you need to.
  • Use a comb to help guide your cuts and keep the hair taut. This will help you achieve a more even cut.
  • Take breaks if your child gets fussy or restless. Cutting hair can be a long process, especially with a toddler.

FAQ

What if I make a mistake while cutting my child's hair?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, try to blend the hair around the mistake to even it out. You can also take your child to a professional stylist to fix any major issues.

How often should I cut my child's hair?

This depends on how fast their hair grows and how quickly it becomes unruly. As a general rule, you should aim to cut their hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain a consistent length and shape.

Hair Care

After cutting your child's hair, it's important to care for it properly to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for curly hair care:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out the hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define the curls and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing the hair when dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair.
  • Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and maintain a healthy length.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage the hair.

By following these tips and techniques, you can give your curly-haired toddler boy a great haircut with scissors. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With a little practice, you'll become a pro at cutting your child's hair!
