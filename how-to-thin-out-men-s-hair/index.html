How To Thin Out Men s Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Nov 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning out men's hair can be a difficult task, especially if you don't have the right tools and techniques. Many men struggle with thick, unruly hair that's difficult to style and manage. In this article, we'll provide you with some tips and tricks on how to thin out men's hair, making it easier to style and maintain.

How to Thin Out Men's Hair

There are several ways to thin out men's hair, including using thinning shears, razors, and texturizing scissors. We'll go over each of these methods in more detail below.

Using Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are specially designed scissors that have teeth or notches on one blade. These teeth help to remove some of the bulk from the hair without changing the length. Here's how to use them:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, starting at the mid-length and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Repeat this process on all sections of the hair that you want to thin out.

Remember to only use thinning shears on dry hair, as wet hair can stretch and lead to uneven results.

Using a Razor

Razors can also be used to thin out men's hair. This method involves using a razor to slice into the hair, creating a softer, more textured look. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Use the razor to slice into the hair, starting at the mid-length and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Repeat this process on all sections of the hair that you want to thin out.

Be careful not to use too much pressure when using a razor, as this can lead to cut or damaged hair.

Using Texturizing Scissors

Texturizing scissors are similar to thinning shears, but they have more teeth or notches on the blade. This helps to create a more textured, layered look. Here's how to use them:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Use the texturizing scissors to cut into the hair, starting at the mid-length and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Repeat this process on all sections of the hair that you want to thin out.

Texturizing scissors can be more difficult to use than thinning shears, so it's important to practice first on a small section of hair.

Tips for Thinning Out Men's Hair

In addition to the methods listed above, there are a few other tips to keep in mind when thinning out men's hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a comb to separate the hair and make sure you're cutting evenly.
  • Don't overdo it - it's better to err on the side of caution and cut less hair than you think you need to.
  • Keep your tools sharp and clean to avoid damaging the hair.
  • If you're unsure about how to thin out your hair, consider going to a professional stylist for help.

FAQ: Thinning Out Men's Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out men's hair:

How often should you thin out men's hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Generally, you should aim to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Can you thin out men's hair with regular scissors?

No, regular scissors are not designed for thinning out hair and can lead to uneven results or damaged hair.

Will thinning out men's hair make it look thinner?

Thinning out men's hair can make it look less bulky and more manageable, but it won't necessarily make it look thinner.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Men's Hair

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep the hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.

Thinning out men's hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. By using the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a more textured, layered look that's both stylish and practical.
