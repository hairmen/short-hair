How To Make My Hair Thicker At 50 Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Oct 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, our hair naturally becomes thinner and more prone to breakage. However, this doesn't mean that you have to live with limp, lifeless hair. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for making your hair look thicker and fuller, even if you're in your 50s.

Description

Many women experience hair thinning as they enter their 50s. This can be due to a variety of factors, including hormonal changes, stress, and genetics. However, there are several things you can do to promote healthier, thicker hair.

How to Make My Hair Thicker at 50: Step by Step

1. Choose the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by selecting a shampoo and conditioner that promotes hair growth and thickness. Look for products that contain biotin, keratin, and other essential vitamins and nutrients. These ingredients can help strengthen your hair and prevent breakage.

2. Use a Thickening Spray or Mousse

Invest in a good quality thickening spray or mousse. These products can help give your hair volume and texture, making it look fuller and thicker. Apply the product to damp hair and blow-dry with a round brush for best results.

3. Try Hair Extensions

If you're looking for a more dramatic change, consider investing in hair extensions. This can be a great way to add volume and length to your hair, giving you a more youthful appearance. Look for extensions that are made from high-quality, natural hair for the best results.

4. Get Regular Haircuts

Regular haircuts can help keep your hair looking healthy and full. Ask your stylist to add layers to your hair to create volume and texture. This can help prevent your hair from looking flat and lifeless.

5. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet can also help promote hair growth and thickness. Make sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean protein. Foods that are rich in biotin, vitamin C, and iron can be especially beneficial for your hair.

Tips for Making Your Hair Look Thicker

1. Use a Volumizing Brush

Invest in a volumizing brush to help create more lift at the roots. Use the brush to lift your hair at the crown and create volume. This will help your hair look fuller and thicker.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage. Instead, opt for loose, natural styles that allow your hair to move freely. This will help prevent damage and breakage, which can make your hair look thinner.

3. Use Heat Protectant Spray

If you use hot styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray. This will help prevent damage to your hair and keep it looking healthy and full. Apply the spray to damp hair before blow-drying or using a curling iron or straightener.

FAQ

1. Can Supplements Help Make My Hair Thicker?

Yes, supplements can be beneficial for promoting hair growth and thickness. Look for supplements that contain biotin, vitamin C, and iron. These nutrients can help strengthen your hair and prevent breakage.

2. Does Stress Cause Hair Thinning?

Yes, stress can be a contributing factor to hair thinning. When we're stressed, our body produces more cortisol, which can affect our hair growth cycle. If you're experiencing hair thinning, try to reduce your stress levels through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques.

Hair Care Tips for Thicker Hair

1. Wash Your Hair Less Frequently

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, making it look dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair every other day or every three days, depending on your hair type. This will help your hair retain its natural oils and stay healthy and full.

2. Use a Hair Mask Once a Week

Use a hair mask once a week to help nourish and strengthen your hair. Look for masks that contain ingredients like keratin, avocado oil, or argan oil. Apply the mask to damp hair and leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing out.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Use a hat or scarf to protect your hair when you're outside for extended periods of time. You can also use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to help protect your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help make your hair look thicker and fuller, even if you're in your 50s. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you'll see results over time.
