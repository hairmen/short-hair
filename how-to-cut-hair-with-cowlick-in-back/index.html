How To Cut Hair With Cowlick In Back

Are you struggling to cut hair with a cowlick in the back? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will be discussing how to properly cut hair with a cowlick in the back, as well as some tips and tricks to make the process easier. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Hair with Cowlick in Back
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

A cowlick is a section of hair that grows in a circular pattern, causing the hair to stand up or stick out in a certain direction. This can be particularly difficult to deal with when cutting hair, as it can be hard to get an even cut. However, with the right technique and a little bit of patience, cutting hair with a cowlick in the back can be done successfully.

How to Cut Hair with Cowlick in Back

The key to cutting hair with a cowlick in the back is to work with the natural growth pattern of the hair. This means cutting the hair in the direction that it naturally falls, rather than trying to force it to go in a different direction. Here are some steps to follow:

Step by Step

Step 1: Start by washing and drying the hair. This will make it easier to work with and will help you see the natural growth pattern of the hair.

Step 2: Section off the hair that has the cowlick, using clips or hair ties to keep the rest of the hair out of the way.

Step 3: Comb the hair in the direction that it naturally falls, so that the cowlick is visible.

Step 4: Use scissors to cut the hair, following the natural growth pattern of the hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, taking small sections of hair at a time.

Step 5: Once you have cut all of the hair in the cowlick section, check to make sure that it is even. If there are any uneven spots, go back and trim them until the hair is even all the way around.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to ensure an even cut.
  • If you're unsure about how to cut hair with a cowlick, consider consulting a professional hairstylist.

FAQ

Q: Can you cut hair with a cowlick while it's wet or should it be dry?

A: It's generally best to cut hair with a cowlick when it's dry, as this will help you see the natural growth pattern of the hair.

Q: How often should you cut hair with a cowlick?

A: This will depend on how fast the hair grows and how noticeable the cowlick is. Generally, it's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep the hair looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care

After cutting hair with a cowlick, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep the hair moisturized.
  • Trim the hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to successfully cut hair with a cowlick in the back. Remember to take your time and work with the natural growth pattern of the hair, and you'll end up with a great-looking haircut.
