How To Start Dreads On Short Hair

Written by Christine Sep 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair and want to start dreads, you might be wondering how to go about it. Starting dreads on short hair can be a bit more challenging than starting them on longer hair, but it's definitely possible. In this article, we'll go over the steps you need to take to start dreads on short hair, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

How to Start Dreads on Short Hair

Before you start, it's important to understand that dreads are a commitment. Starting dreads on short hair will take time and patience, but the end result is worth it. Here are the steps you need to take to start dreads on short hair:

Step 1: Decide on a Method

There are different methods you can use to start dreads, such as backcombing, twisting, and interlocking. Each method has its pros and cons, so it's important to do your research and decide which one is best for you.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a rat-tail comb to section your hair into small, even squares. The size of the sections will depend on the method you're using and the thickness of your hair.

Step 3: Backcomb or Twist the Sections

For backcombing, use a metal comb to tease each section of hair towards the roots. This will create knots that will eventually form into dreads. For twisting, simply twist each section of hair tightly until it starts to coil on its own.

Step 4: Secure the Dread

Once you've backcombed or twisted a section of hair, use a rubber band or hair tie to secure it in place. Leave the rubber band or hair tie in for a few days until the dread starts to hold its shape.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreads

Once your dreads have started to form, it's important to maintain them. This includes washing them regularly with a residue-free shampoo, using a crochet hook to tighten loose hairs, and separating them as needed to prevent them from combining into one big dread.

Tips for Starting Dreads on Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you start dreads on short hair:

  • Be patient - dreads take time to form
  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup
  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid leaving behind any product
  • Consider using a crochet hook to help tighten loose hairs
  • Separate your dreads to prevent them from combining

FAQ About Starting Dreads on Short Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads on short hair:

Can you start dreads on really short hair?

Yes, you can start dreads on hair that is as short as 2-3 inches.

How long does it take for dreads to form on short hair?

It can take anywhere from a few months to a year for dreads to form on short hair, depending on the method you use and how fast your hair grows.

Do you need to cut your hair to start dreads?

No, you don't need to cut your hair to start dreads. However, it may be easier to start dreads on hair that is shorter in length.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you have dreads, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for hair care for dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten loose hairs
  • Separate your dreads to prevent them from combining
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can cause buildup
  • Cover your dreads when swimming to prevent them from getting tangled

Starting dreads on short hair can be a bit more challenging than starting them on longer hair, but it's definitely possible. With the right method and some patience, you can have beautiful dreads in no time.
