Why Is My Eyebrow Hair Falling Out After Microblading?

Microblading has become an increasingly popular cosmetic procedure in the beauty industry, but sometimes the results can be unexpected. If you've noticed that your eyebrow hair is falling out after microblading, you may be wondering why. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide some tips on how to care for your eyebrows post-microblading.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Microblading is a semi-permanent cosmetic procedure that involves using a handheld tool to create small hair-like strokes on the eyebrows. The strokes are then filled in with pigment to create a fuller, more defined look. While this procedure can provide great results, sometimes the eyebrow hairs can fall out after the procedure.

How to

If you've noticed that your eyebrow hair is falling out after microblading, it's important to take proper care of your eyebrows. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Touching Your Eyebrows

It's important not to touch your eyebrows after microblading, especially if your hands are dirty. Touching your eyebrows can cause infections or irritation, which can cause your eyebrow hairs to fall out.

2. Avoid Exposure to Sunlight

Exposure to sunlight can cause the pigment in your eyebrows to fade or change color. It's important to avoid direct sunlight or use sunscreen on your eyebrows to protect them.

3. Use a Gentle Cleanser

After microblading, it's important to use a gentle cleanser to clean your eyebrows. Harsh cleansers can cause irritation and lead to eyebrow hair loss.

Step by step

If you're considering microblading, here are the steps involved:

1. Consultation

The first step in microblading is a consultation with a professional. During this consultation, you'll discuss your desired eyebrow shape and color.

2. Mapping

Next, the professional will use a mapping tool to determine the best eyebrow shape for your face.

3. Numbing

Before the procedure, a numbing cream will be applied to your eyebrows to minimize any pain or discomfort.

4. Microblading

The professional will use a handheld tool to create small hair-like strokes on your eyebrows. The strokes are then filled in with pigment to create a fuller, more defined look.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your eyebrows after microblading:

1. Avoid Sweating

Avoid sweating for at least 10 days after microblading to prevent any irritation or infection.

2. Avoid Swimming

Avoid swimming for at least 10 days after microblading to prevent any irritation or infection.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about eyebrow hair loss after microblading:

1. Is it normal for eyebrow hair to fall out after microblading?

Yes, it is normal for some eyebrow hair to fall out after microblading. This is because the procedure involves creating small incisions in the skin, which can cause trauma to the hair follicles.

2. Will my eyebrow hair grow back?

Yes, your eyebrow hair will eventually grow back after microblading. However, it may take several months for the hair to fully grow back.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important for maintaining healthy eyebrows. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Brow Serum

Using a brow serum can help promote hair growth and keep your eyebrows healthy.

2. Avoid Over-Plucking

Over-plucking your eyebrows can lead to hair loss and thinning. It's important to only pluck stray hairs and avoid over-tweezing.
