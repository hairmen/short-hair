How To Dread Hair Naturally A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle that's low-maintenance and unique, dreadlocks may be the perfect option for you. While many people opt for synthetic or chemical methods to create dreadlocks, it's possible to achieve this look naturally. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to dread hair naturally, along with tips and tricks for maintaining your dreads.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Start with Clean Hair
  • Step 2: Section Your Hair
  • Step 3: Twist and Rip Method
  • Step 4: Palm Rolling
  • Step 5: Maintenance and Care
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you start the dreading process, it's important to start with clean hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair. Avoid using conditioner or any other products that may interfere with the dreading process.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small, even sections. Use hair ties to keep each section in place. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want to create.

Step 3: Twist and Rip Method

The twist and rip method is a popular technique for creating natural dreadlocks. To start, take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. Then, use a comb or your fingers to rip the hair in the opposite direction. Repeat this process until the entire section is tightly twisted and knotted.

Step 4: Palm Rolling

After you've twisted and ripped each section, use the palm rolling technique to further tighten and shape your dreads. Simply roll each dread between your palms in a back and forth motion. This will help to smooth out any bumps or unevenness in the dreads.

Step 5: Maintenance and Care

Once you've created your natural dreadlocks, it's important to maintain and care for them properly. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using conditioner or any other products that may leave buildup in your dreads.
  • Keep your dreads dry to prevent mold or mildew from forming.
  • Use a crochet hook to tidy up loose hairs or stray threads.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few additional tips and tricks for achieving and maintaining natural dreadlocks:

  • Start with shorter hair if possible, as longer hair can be more difficult to dread naturally.
  • Consider using a sea salt spray to help tighten and shape your dreads.
  • Be patient - it can take several months for your dreads to fully form and mature.
  • Experiment with different methods and techniques to find what works best for your hair type and texture.

FAQs

Can I create natural dreadlocks on any hair type?

Yes, it's possible to create natural dreadlocks on any hair type. However, the process may be easier or more difficult depending on the texture and thickness of your hair.

Do I need to use any products to create natural dreadlocks?

No, you don't need to use any products to create natural dreadlocks. However, some people choose to use sea salt spray or other natural products to help with the dreading process.

How often should I wash my natural dreadlocks?

It's recommended to wash your natural dreadlocks at least once a week. However, you may need to wash more or less frequently depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

Can I still style my natural dreadlocks?

Yes, you can still style your natural dreadlocks. However, keep in mind that certain styles may be more difficult to achieve with dreads. Experiment with different styles and accessories to find what works best for you.

Final Thoughts

Creating natural dreadlocks may take some time and patience, but the end result can be a unique and low-maintenance hairstyle that's all your own. With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve and maintain your natural dreads with ease.
