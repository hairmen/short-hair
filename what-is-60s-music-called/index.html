What Is 60S Music Called

Written by William Jun 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you love the music of the 1960s and wonder what it is called? In this article, we will explore what 60s music is called, the different genres that were popular during this era, and some of the most iconic songs and artists of the time.

What is 60s Music Called?

The music of the 1960s is commonly referred to as "60s music" or "the music of the 60s." This decade was a time of immense change in the world, and the music of the era reflected this with its unique sound and lyrics that challenged the status quo.

The Different Genres of 60s Music

There were several different genres of music that were popular during the 1960s. Some of the most notable genres include:

Rock and Roll

Rock and roll was one of the most popular genres of music during the 1960s. This genre was characterized by its upbeat tempo, catchy guitar riffs, and rebellious lyrics. Some of the most iconic rock and roll songs of the 60s include "Satisfaction" by The Rolling Stones and "I Want to Hold Your Hand" by The Beatles.

Folk Music

Folk music was another popular genre of music during the 60s. This genre was characterized by its acoustic sound and lyrics that often focused on social and political issues. Some of the most iconic folk songs of the 60s include "Blowin' in the Wind" by Bob Dylan and "The Times They Are a-Changin'" also by Bob Dylan.

Soul Music

Soul music was a genre that originated in African American communities in the United States during the 1950s and 60s. This genre was characterized by its emotive vocals, gospel-influenced sound, and upbeat tempo. Some of the most iconic soul songs of the 60s include "Respect" by Aretha Franklin and "I Got You (I Feel Good)" by James Brown.

Iconic Songs and Artists of the 60s

There were many iconic songs and artists during the 1960s. Some of the most notable include:

The Beatles

The Beatles were one of the most popular bands of the 60s. Their music was characterized by its catchy melodies, innovative studio techniques, and lyrics that often dealt with love and relationships. Some of their most iconic songs include "Hey Jude," "Let It Be," and "A Hard Day's Night."

Bob Dylan

Bob Dylan was one of the most influential singer-songwriters of the 60s. His music was characterized by its poetic lyrics, acoustic sound, and focus on social and political issues. Some of his most iconic songs include "Like a Rolling Stone," "Blowin' in the Wind," and "The Times They Are a-Changin'."

The Rolling Stones

The Rolling Stones were another popular band of the 60s. Their music was characterized by its blues-influenced sound, rebellious lyrics, and energetic live performances. Some of their most iconic songs include "Satisfaction," "Paint It Black," and "Jumpin' Jack Flash."

Tips for Listening to 60s Music

If you're interested in listening to 60s music, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with some of the most iconic songs and artists of the era.
  • Explore different genres of 60s music to find what speaks to you.
  • Consider listening to full albums to get a better sense of an artist's sound and style.
  • Don't be afraid to delve into the deeper cuts of an artist's discography.

FAQs

What is the most popular genre of 60s music?

Rock and roll was one of the most popular genres of 60s music.

Who were some of the most iconic artists of the 60s?

Some of the most iconic artists of the 60s include The Beatles, Bob Dylan, and The Rolling Stones.

What was the social and political climate like during the 60s?

The 60s were a time of immense social and political change, with movements for civil rights, women's rights, and anti-war protests all taking place during this era.

Hair Care Tips from the 60s

The 60s were also a time of unique fashion and beauty trends. Here are some hair care tips from the era:

  • Embrace the bouffant: The bouffant was a popular hairstyle in the 60s characterized by its voluminous, teased hair. Consider trying this style out for a retro look.
  • Use a hair scarf: Hair scarves were a popular accessory during the 60s and can be used to add a pop of color to your hair or to keep it out of your face.
  • Invest in a good hairspray: Hairspray was a staple hair care product during the 60s, as it helped to hold styles in place throughout the day.

In conclusion, 60s music was a unique and influential era in the world of music. From rock and roll to folk music and soul, there was something for everyone during this time. By exploring different genres and iconic artists of the era, you can gain a better understanding and appreciation for this important period in music history.
