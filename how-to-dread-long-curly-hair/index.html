How To Dread Long Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to make a bold and unique statement with your hair, dreadlocks are an excellent choice. While traditionally associated with certain cultures and lifestyles, dreads have become increasingly popular in recent years as a fashion statement. However, if you have long curly hair, you may be wondering how to make the transition to dreadlocks. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide to dread long curly hair, along with helpful tips and advice for maintaining your new style.

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as dreads, are a hairstyle that involves twisting and matting sections of hair to create rope-like strands. Dreads can be created with any type of hair, but they are particularly suited to curly and coily hair textures. Dreads can be styled in a variety of ways, from loose and natural to tightly packed and sculpted.

How to Dread Long Curly Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

The first step in dreading long curly hair is to prepare your hair for the process. This involves washing your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue that may be present. It's also a good idea to deep condition your hair to soften and moisturize it, as this will make it easier to work with.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Once your hair is clean and conditioned, it's time to divide it into sections. Use a comb to separate your hair into small, manageable sections, and secure each section with a hair tie or clip. The size of your sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want to create.

Step 3: Start the Dreading Process

Now it's time to start the dreading process. There are several methods you can use to create dreads, but one of the most popular methods for long curly hair is the twist and rip method. To use this method, take a small section of hair and divide it into two strands. Twist each strand in opposite directions, then gently pull them apart to create a "ripped" look. Continue twisting and ripping each section of hair until your entire head is covered in dreads.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreads

Once your dreads are in place, it's important to maintain them properly to keep them looking fresh and healthy. This involves regular washing and conditioning, as well as periodic maintenance to tighten any loose or frizzy hairs. You can also use a variety of products, such as dread wax or sea salt spray, to help enhance and maintain your dreads.

Tips for Dreadlocking Long Curly Hair

Here are a few helpful tips to keep in mind when dreadlocking long curly hair:

  • Be patient: Dreading long curly hair can take time, so be patient and don't rush the process.
  • Use a good clarifying shampoo: A clarifying shampoo will help remove any buildup or residue that may interfere with the dreading process.
  • Condition your hair regularly: Regular conditioning will help keep your hair soft and moisturized, which will make it easier to work with.
  • Don't over-tighten your dreads: Tightening your dreads too much can cause damage to your hair and scalp, so be gentle when maintaining them.
  • Experiment with different styles: Dreads can be styled in a variety of ways, so don't be afraid to experiment with different looks and accessories.

FAQ about Dreadlocking Long Curly Hair

Q: Can you dread long curly hair without cutting it?

A: Yes, you can dread long curly hair without cutting it. In fact, many people with long curly hair choose to dread their hair as a way of embracing and celebrating their natural texture.

Q: Do you have to use wax to dread your hair?

A: No, you don't have to use wax to dread your hair. While some people find that wax helps to hold their dreads in place and enhance their texture, it's not necessary for the dreading process.

Q: How long does it take to dread long curly hair?

A: The amount of time it takes to dread long curly hair can vary depending on the length and thickness of your hair, as well as the method you use to create your dreads. On average, it can take anywhere from several hours to several days to complete the dreading process.

Hair Care for Dreadlocked Long Curly Hair

Maintaining healthy hair is important whether you have dreads or not. Here are a few tips to keep your hair healthy and strong:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Regular washing will help keep your hair and scalp clean and healthy.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo: Sulfates can be harsh on your hair and scalp, so look for a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo instead.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly: Deep conditioning will help keep your hair soft and moisturized, which is especially important for dreads.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools: Heat can cause damage to your hair and scalp, so try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair at night: Sleeping with a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can successfully dread long curly hair and enjoy a unique and stylish look. Remember to be patient, take good care of your hair, and have fun experimenting with different styles and accessories!
