How To Layer Wavy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Nov 23, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Layering is a great way to add volume and movement to your wavy hair. It can also help to reduce the weight of your hair, making it more manageable and easier to style. In this article, we will go over the steps you can take to layer your wavy hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Wavy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Layered Wavy Hair

How to Layer Wavy Hair

Layering wavy hair involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create movement and texture. The layers are usually cut in a way that follows the natural wave pattern of the hair. This technique helps the waves to cascade down, giving the hair a more voluminous and dynamic appearance.

When layering wavy hair, it's important to work with a stylist who understands the unique characteristics of wavy hair. A professional stylist can help you achieve the best results and ensure that your hair looks healthy and natural.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to layering wavy hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use clips to hold each section in place.
  3. Start with the bottom section of your hair. Hold the hair at a 90-degree angle away from your head and cut the ends. This will create the first layer.
  4. Move on to the next section of hair above the first layer. Hold the hair at a 45-degree angle away from your head and cut the ends. This will create the second layer.
  5. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way up to the top of your head.
  6. Blend the layers together by using a razor or scissors to gently feather the ends.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Layering Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when layering wavy hair:

  • Work with a stylist who has experience with wavy hair.
  • Be clear about the length and style you want to achieve.
  • Use a razor or scissors to create feathery ends.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Be patient. Layering takes time and precision.

FAQs

Q: Will layering my wavy hair make it frizzy?

A: Layering can actually help to reduce frizz by removing some of the weight from your hair. However, it's important to use the right styling products and techniques to keep your hair looking smooth and defined.

Q: How often should I get my wavy hair layered?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to maintain your style. Generally, it's a good idea to get your hair layered every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care for Layered Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to help you care for your layered wavy hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair to enhance your natural waves.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your waves and reduce frizz.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your style and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautifully layered wavy hair that is healthy, voluminous, and full of movement.
