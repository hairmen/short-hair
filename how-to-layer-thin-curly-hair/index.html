How To Layer Thin Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thin curly hair, you may find it difficult to achieve the fullness and volume you desire. One solution to this problem is to layer your hair. Layering can help add volume and texture to your curls, giving you a more voluminous and defined look. In this step-by-step guide, we will walk you through the process of layering thin curly hair.

How to Layer Thin Curly Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's first discuss what layering means for thin curly hair. Layering involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create depth and texture. This technique can help thin hair appear fuller and more voluminous.

Who is Layering Suitable For?

Layering is suitable for anyone who has thin curly hair and wants to add volume and texture to their locks. However, if you have very fine hair, it may be best to avoid layering as it can make your hair look even thinner.

When Should You Layer Your Hair?

You can layer your hair at any time, but it's best to do it when your hair is dry and styled in its natural state. This will help your stylist determine how to cut your hair to achieve the desired look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, let's dive into the step-by-step guide for layering thin curly hair.

Step 1: Consultation

The first step is to schedule a consultation with your hairstylist. This is important because your hairstylist needs to understand your hair type, texture, and desired style before they start cutting your hair. During the consultation, you can discuss the different layering techniques and decide which one is best for you.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair in its natural state. This means letting your hair air dry or using a diffuser on a low heat setting. Avoid using a brush or comb as this can cause your curls to lose their shape and definition.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections using hair clips or scrunchies. This will make it easier for your hairstylist to cut your hair evenly.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Your hairstylist will start cutting your hair at the back, working their way towards the front. They will cut your hair in layers, taking small sections at a time. They will also use a technique called point cutting, which involves cutting the hair at an angle to create texture and movement.

Step 5: Check the Length

After each section is cut, your hairstylist will check the length to ensure it's even. They will also check how the layers blend together to create a seamless look.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is cut, your hairstylist will style it using a diffuser or a curling iron. They may also use styling products to enhance your curls and add volume.

Tips for Layering Thin Curly Hair

Layering thin curly hair can be tricky, but these tips can help you achieve the best results.

Tip 1: Avoid Over-Layering

Over-layering can make thin hair look even thinner. It's best to stick to a few layers and focus on creating volume and texture through styling.

Tip 2: Use a Diffuser

A diffuser can help enhance your curls and add volume. It's best to use a diffuser on a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Tip 3: Use the Right Products

Using the right products can help enhance your curls and add volume. Look for products that are specifically designed for thin curly hair, such as volumizing sprays and mousses.

FAQs

Q: Will layering make my hair look shorter?

A: Layering can make your hair appear shorter, but it can also create volume and texture, making your hair look fuller and more voluminous.

Q: Can I layer my hair at home?

A: We recommend getting your hair layered by a professional hairstylist to ensure that it's done correctly.

Q: How often should I get my hair layered?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers lose their shape. We recommend getting your hair layered every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care for Thin Curly Hair

To keep your thin curly hair healthy and voluminous, it's important to follow a proper hair care routine. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that's specifically designed for thin hair. Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Tip 2: Condition Your Hair

Condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and nourished. Look for conditioners that are specifically designed for thin curly hair.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage your hair and cause it to become brittle and thin. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray.

Tip 4: Protect Your Hair at Night

Protect your hair at night by sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase. This will help prevent breakage and frizz.

Now that you know how to layer thin curly hair, you can achieve the voluminous and defined look you desire. Remember to follow a proper hair care routine to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
