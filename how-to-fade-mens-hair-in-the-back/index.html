How To Fade Men s Hair In The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new look, one great way to switch up your hairstyle is by getting a fade. A fade is a popular haircut style that involves gradually tapering the hair from short to long, creating a smooth and seamless transition. While fades can be done all around the head, one of the most popular areas to fade is in the back. In this article, we'll show you how to fade men's hair in the back, step-by-step.

How to:

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

The first step to fading your hair in the back is to gather your materials. You'll need a pair of clippers, a comb, and a mirror. If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, you may want to enlist the help of a friend or family member.

Step 2: Decide on Your Fade Length

The next step is to decide on your fade length. Do you want a short fade or a long fade? A low fade or a high fade? Take some time to think about what length and style will work best for you.

Step 3: Start with the Clippers

Once you've decided on your fade length, it's time to start with the clippers. Begin by using a longer guard to create a guide line for your fade. Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to cut the hair to your desired length. Work your way around the back of your head, cutting the hair to the same length all the way around.

Step 4: Switch to a Shorter Guard

Once your hair is all cut to the same length, switch to a shorter guard on your clippers. Use the shorter guard to cut the hair a little shorter than the previous length. This will create the gradual fade effect.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

After you've cut the hair with the shorter guard, it's time to blend the hair. Use the clippers without a guard to blend the shorter hair into the longer hair. Use a flicking motion to create a smooth transition between the two lengths.

Step 6: Repeat as Necessary

Continue using shorter guards and blending the hair until you've achieved the desired fade length. Take breaks and step back to look at your work to ensure you're achieving the look you want.

Tips:

- Take your time and work slowly to avoid mistakes.
- Use a mirror to check your work from all angles.
- Remember that hair grows back, so don't stress too much if you make a mistake.
- Experiment with different fade lengths and styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ:

Q: How often should I get a haircut to maintain my fade?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your fade. On average, most men need to get a haircut every 2-4 weeks to maintain their fade.

Q: Can I fade my own hair or should I go to a barber?

A: It's possible to fade your own hair, but it can be tricky. If you're not confident in your skills, it's best to go to a barber to ensure you get the look you want.

Hair Care:

After you've achieved your fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid over-washing your hair, and use a hair mask once a week to deep condition.

Now that you know how to fade men's hair in the back, it's time to give it a try. Remember to take your time, experiment with different lengths and styles, and most importantly, have fun with your new look!
