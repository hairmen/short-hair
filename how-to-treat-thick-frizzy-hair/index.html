How To Treat Thick Frizzy Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Nov 17, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have thick frizzy hair, you know the struggle of trying to tame your mane. It can be frustrating to deal with hair that seems to have a mind of its own. But fear not, there are ways to manage your frizz and make your hair look its best. In this article, we will discuss how to treat thick frizzy hair and provide you with tips and tricks to help you achieve the hair of your dreams.

Table of Contents

  • How to Treat Thick Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Frizzy Hair

How to Treat Thick Frizzy Hair

First, it’s important to understand why your hair is frizzy. Frizz occurs when your hair is dehydrated, often due to external factors like humidity, harsh hair products, and heat styling. To treat frizz, you need to start by hydrating your hair.

One of the best ways to hydrate your hair is to use a deep conditioning treatment. Look for a product that contains natural oils like coconut, argan, or jojoba oil. These oils will help to nourish your hair and prevent frizz.

Another way to treat frizz is to use a leave-in conditioner. This type of conditioner is designed to be left in your hair after you shower, providing extra hydration and protection. Look for a leave-in conditioner that contains ingredients like shea butter or glycerin, which will help to lock in moisture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know how to treat frizz, let’s go over a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the best results:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a hydrating shampoo. Look for a product that contains natural oils or ingredients like honey, which is known for its moisturizing properties.
  2. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the treatment on for the recommended amount of time, usually 5-10 minutes.
  3. Rinse out the deep conditioning treatment with cool water. This will help to seal the cuticle and lock in moisture.
  4. Towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub your hair too vigorously as this can cause damage and frizz.
  5. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly.
  6. Blow dry your hair using a diffuser attachment. This will help to minimize frizz and add volume to your hair.
  7. Style your hair as desired using heat styling tools. Remember to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you manage your thick frizzy hair:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to dry your hair, as these materials are gentler on your hair and help to minimize frizz.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and cause frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment, which can help to smooth and tame frizzy hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about treating thick frizzy hair:

  • Can I use a hair mask instead of a deep conditioning treatment? Yes, a hair mask can be a great alternative to a deep conditioning treatment. Look for a product that contains nourishing ingredients like avocado oil or honey.
  • Can I skip the leave-in conditioner? While you can skip the leave-in conditioner, it’s recommended for those with thick frizzy hair to use one to help lock in moisture and prevent frizz.
  • How often should I deep condition my hair? It’s recommended to deep condition your hair once a week, or as needed depending on the condition of your hair.

Hair Care for Thick Frizzy Hair

In addition to following the tips and tricks we’ve outlined, it’s important to take good care of your hair to prevent frizz. Here are some additional hair care tips:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain sulfates, as these can dry out your hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools to prevent damage and frizz.
  • Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends, which can contribute to frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve healthy, beautiful hair that is free from frizz. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, as it may take some time to see results. With a little effort, you can have the hair of your dreams!
