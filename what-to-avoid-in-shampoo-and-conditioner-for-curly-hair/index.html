What To Avoid In Shampoo And Conditioner For Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it can be beautiful and unique, but on the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and prone to breakage. One of the most important things to consider when taking care of your curly hair is the type of shampoo and conditioner you use. In this guide, we will go over what to avoid in shampoo and conditioner for curly hair, as well as provide tips on how to properly care for your curls.

What to Avoid in Shampoo and Conditioner for Curly Hair

When it comes to choosing a shampoo and conditioner for curly hair, there are certain ingredients you should avoid. These ingredients can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Here are some of the most common ingredients to steer clear of:

Sulfates

Sulfates are harsh detergents that are commonly found in shampoos. They can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy. Look for sulfate-free shampoos that are gentle on your curls.

Alcohol

Alcohol is a common ingredient in hair products, but it can be drying and damaging to curly hair. Look for products that are alcohol-free or have low levels of alcohol.

Silicones

Silicones are often used in conditioners to create a smooth, shiny finish, but they can also weigh down your curls and make them look greasy. Look for silicone-free conditioners that provide moisture without weighing down your hair.

Parabens

Parabens are preservatives that are commonly used in hair products, but they have been linked to hormone disruption and other health concerns. Look for paraben-free shampoos and conditioners that are gentle on your hair and your body.

How to Properly Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair can be a bit more complicated than caring for straight hair, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful. Here are a few tips for properly caring for curly hair:

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and damage. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently detangling any knots along the way.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be damaging to curly hair, so try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Protect Your Hair While Sleeping

Sleeping on a cotton pillowcase can cause friction and damage to curly hair. Try sleeping on a satin or silk pillowcase, or wear a silk or satin bonnet to protect your curls while you sleep.

Step-by-Step Guide to Washing Curly Hair

Washing curly hair requires a bit more care than washing straight hair. Here is a step-by-step guide to properly washing your curly hair:

Step 1: Wet Your Hair

Start by wetting your hair thoroughly with warm water. Use your fingers to gently massage your scalp and loosen any dirt or buildup.

Step 2: Apply Shampoo

Apply a small amount of shampoo to your scalp, and work it into a lather. Be gentle, and avoid scrubbing your hair too vigorously.

Step 3: Rinse Thoroughly

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water, making sure to remove all of the shampoo.

Step 4: Apply Conditioner

Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the conditioner evenly through your hair.

Step 5: Let It Sit

Allow the conditioner to sit on your hair for a few minutes, so it can penetrate and moisturize your curls.

Step 6: Rinse with Cold Water

Rinse your hair thoroughly with cold water to seal the cuticle and lock in moisture.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips for maintaining healthy, beautiful curly hair:

Avoid Brushing Your Hair When It's Dry

Brushing curly hair when it's dry can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Avoid Over-Washing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair no more than two or three times a week.

Use a Deep Conditioner

Using a deep conditioner once a week can help keep your curls healthy and moisturized. Look for a product that is specifically designed for curly hair.

Frequently Asked Questions About Curly Hair

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It's best to wash curly hair no more than two or three times a week. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle.

Q: Should I use a leave-in conditioner?

A: Yes, using a leave-in conditioner can help keep your curls moisturized and healthy. Look for a product that is specifically designed for curly hair, and apply it after washing your hair.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low to prevent damage.

By avoiding certain ingredients in your shampoo and conditioner, and following these tips for caring for curly hair, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and use products that are specifically designed for curly hair to avoid damage and breakage.
