Why Am I Shedding Short Hairs Understanding The Causes And Finding Solutions

Written by Daniel Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Shedding of hair is a natural process. On average, people lose around 50 to 100 strands of hair daily. However, if you are shedding short hairs, it might be a cause for concern. Short hair shedding is often noticeable as they are easier to spot on your clothes, pillows, and hairbrush. In this article, we will discuss the reasons why you might be shedding short hairs and provide solutions for hair loss.

What Causes Short Hair Shedding?

There could be several reasons why you are shedding short hairs. Some of the common causes include:

1. Telogen Effluvium

Telogen Effluvium is a condition where hair falls out after a stressful event. This could be due to pregnancy, surgery, or even a sudden change in diet. In this condition, short hairs are more likely to fall out as they are in the resting phase of the hair growth cycle.

2. Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can cause short hair shedding. This is commonly seen in women during menopause or after giving birth. Hormonal imbalances can also cause hair thinning and baldness.

3. Nutritional Deficiencies

Your hair needs proper nutrition to grow and stay healthy. A deficiency in vitamins and minerals like iron, zinc, and biotin can cause short hair shedding.

4. Hair Products

Using harsh hair products or styling tools can damage your hair and cause short hair shedding. Chemical treatments like coloring, perming, and straightening can also weaken your hair and cause it to fall out.

How to Prevent Short Hair Shedding?

Here are some tips to prevent short hair shedding:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eat a diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth. Include foods like eggs, spinach, and nuts in your diet.

2. Avoid Harsh Hair Products

Avoid using harsh hair products and styling tools. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner and limit the use of heat styling tools.

3. Manage Stress

Stress can cause short hair shedding. Practice stress-management techniques like meditation and yoga to reduce stress levels.

4. Take Supplements

If you have a nutritional deficiency, taking supplements can help prevent short hair shedding. Consult a doctor before taking any supplements.

5. Seek Professional Help

If your short hair shedding persists, seek professional help. A dermatologist or trichologist can diagnose the underlying cause of your hair loss and provide appropriate treatment.

FAQ About Short Hair Shedding

Q. Is short hair shedding normal?

A. Shedding of hair is normal. However, if you are shedding short hairs in large amounts, it could be a cause for concern.

Q. Can stress cause short hair shedding?

A. Yes, stress can cause short hair shedding. This is known as Telogen Effluvium.

Q. Can nutritional deficiencies cause short hair shedding?

A. Yes, nutritional deficiencies can cause short hair shedding. Deficiencies in vitamins and minerals like iron, zinc, and biotin can cause hair loss.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Short Hair Shedding

Here are some hair care tips to prevent short hair shedding:

1. Use a Mild Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a mild shampoo and conditioner can help prevent hair damage and promote hair growth.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. Heat can damage your hair and cause it to fall out.

3. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can improve blood circulation and promote hair growth.

4. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the sun and environmental damage by wearing a hat or scarf.

5. Trim Your Hair

Trimming your hair regularly can prevent split ends and breakage, which can cause short hair shedding.

In conclusion, shedding short hairs can be a cause for concern. However, with proper hair care, a balanced diet, and stress-management techniques, you can prevent short hair shedding and promote hair growth.
