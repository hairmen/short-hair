Best Way To Style Thinning Hair Tips And Tricks For A Fuller Look

Written by Frank Aug 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss or thinning hair, you're not alone. Many people, both men and women, struggle with this issue. However, just because your hair is thinning doesn't mean you can't still have a great hairstyle. In fact, with the right techniques and products, you can create a fuller, more voluminous look. Here are some tips and tricks for styling thinning hair.

How to Style Thinning Hair

1. Use volumizing products

One of the best ways to create a fuller look is to use volumizing products. This includes shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are designed to add volume and body to your hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, and panthenol, which can help strengthen and thicken your hair.

2. Consider a shorter haircut

If your hair is thinning, a shorter haircut can actually make it look fuller. Short hairstyles are often easier to manage and can create the illusion of thicker hair. Talk to your stylist about a cut that will work with your hair type and face shape.

3. Use a thickening spray

Another way to add volume to your hair is to use a thickening spray. These sprays are specifically designed to plump up hair strands and create a fuller look. Apply the spray to damp hair before blow-drying for best results.

4. Avoid heavy products

While you want to use products that add volume, you should avoid heavy products that can weigh your hair down. This includes things like heavy pomades and waxes. Instead, opt for lightweight products that won't leave your hair feeling greasy or weighed down.

5. Embrace your natural texture

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, embrace it. Curly and wavy hair can actually make your hair look fuller, so don't be afraid to let your natural texture shine. Use a curl-enhancing product to define your curls and create a more voluminous look.

Step by Step Guide to Styling Thinning Hair

Here's a step by step guide to styling thinning hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help add body and texture to your hair.

Step 2: Apply a thickening spray

Apply a thickening spray to damp hair, focusing on the roots. This will help plump up your hair strands and create a fuller look.

Step 3: Blow-dry your hair

Use a round brush to blow-dry your hair, focusing on the roots. This will help create lift and volume.

Step 4: Add texture

Use a texturizing spray or pomade to add texture to your hair. This will help create a more voluminous look.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

Finish your style with a light hold hairspray. This will help keep your hair in place without weighing it down.

Tips for Styling Thinning Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling thinning hair:

Tip 1: Don't over-style

Over-styling can actually make thinning hair look worse. Avoid using too much heat or pulling your hair too tightly.

Tip 2: Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, rather than a brush. This will help prevent breakage and damage.

Tip 3: Try a new part

Switching up your part can create the illusion of fuller hair. Try parting your hair on the opposite side or creating a zig-zag part.

Tip 4: Use a hair thickening supplement

If you're struggling with thinning hair, you may want to try a hair thickening supplement. These supplements are designed to promote hair growth and strengthen hair strands.

FAQ

Q1: Can I still color my hair if it's thinning?

A: Yes, you can still color your hair if it's thinning. However, you should avoid harsh chemicals and opt for a gentle, ammonia-free formula.

Q2: Can I use a straightener on thinning hair?

A: Yes, you can use a straightener on thinning hair, but be careful not to use too much heat or pull your hair too tightly.

Q3: How often should I wash my thinning hair?

A: It's best to wash thinning hair every other day or every three days. This will help prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Hair Care for Thinning Hair

Taking care of your hair is important, especially if you're experiencing thinning or hair loss. Here are some hair care tips for thinning hair:

Tip 1: Avoid tight hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids can cause damage and breakage, which can make thinning hair look worse.

Tip 2: Protect your hair from heat

Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners. This will help prevent damage and breakage.

Tip 3: Eat a healthy diet

A healthy diet can promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to eat plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables.

Tip 4: Consider a hair transplant

If you're experiencing significant hair loss, you may want to consider a hair transplant. This is a surgical procedure that involves transplanting hair follicles from one area of your scalp to another.

In conclusion, styling thinning hair doesn't have to be a challenge. By using the right techniques and products, you can create a fuller, more voluminous look. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid over-styling, and you'll be on your way to a great hairstyle.
