How To Cut Your Own Bangs Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of constantly going to the salon to get your bangs trimmed? Cutting your own bangs hair may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily at home. In this article, we’ll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own bangs hair, as well as some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Your Own Bangs Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Advice

How to Cut Your Own Bangs Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let’s go over some tools you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • A handheld mirror or two mirrors facing each other

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps for a successful bangs haircut:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be tricky to cut, so it’s best to work with dry hair.
  2. Part your hair in the middle and comb it straight down. This will create a natural-looking part for your bangs.
  3. Decide on the length you want your bangs to be. It’s best to start longer and gradually cut more off, rather than cutting too much at once.
  4. Section off the hair you want to cut. Use hair clips or elastics to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.
  5. Hold the section of hair straight out from your head with one hand, and use the other hand to cut the hair straight across. Make small cuts and check your progress frequently.
  6. Repeat steps 4-5 on the other side.
  7. Blend your bangs. Use your scissors to cut at an angle on the ends of your bangs to blend them into the rest of your hair.
  8. Check your work. Use a handheld mirror or two mirrors facing each other to check the back of your head.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs

Here are some tips to help you get the perfect bangs:

  • Start longer than you think you want. You can always cut more off, but you can’t put hair back on.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Take your time. It’s better to go slowly and check your progress frequently than to rush and make a mistake.
  • Don’t cut your bangs too short. It’s better to have longer bangs that you can trim later than to cut them too short and regret it.
  • Watch video tutorials. If you’re a visual learner, there are plenty of tutorials online that can show you how to cut your own bangs.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own bangs:

Q: Can I cut my bangs with regular scissors?

A: Yes, you can, but it’s best to use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid split ends and uneven cuts.

Q: How often should I trim my bangs?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to get in your eyes. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good timeframe.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don’t panic! If you make a mistake, try to even out your bangs as best you can and wait for them to grow out. You can always wear your hair up or to the side to hide any mistakes.

Hair Care Advice

Now that you’ve successfully cut your own bangs, it’s important to take care of them:

  • Wash your hair regularly. Clean hair will make your bangs look fresh and bouncy.
  • Blow-dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume.
  • Use hair products sparingly. Too much product can weigh down your bangs and make them look greasy.
  • Trim your bangs regularly. Keeping them at the right length will help them look neat and tidy.

Cutting your own bangs hair can be nerve-wracking, but with this step-by-step guide and some practice, you’ll be a pro in no time. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and have fun!
