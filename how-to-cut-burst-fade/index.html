How to Cut Burst Fade: Step by Step Guide (2023) | Hair Care Tips

If you're looking to cut your hair in a trendy and stylish manner, then a burst fade might be the way to go. A burst fade is a modern haircut that is popular among men of all ages. It is a variation of the classic fade and involves cutting the hair in a way that it gradually fades away from the sides and back of the head, leaving a "burst" of longer hair on the top. In this article, we'll provide you with a step by step guide on how to cut burst fade, as well as some tips and hair care advice.

How to Cut Burst Fade

Cutting a burst fade requires some skill and precision, but it is relatively easy to do once you get the hang of it. Before you start, make sure you have the right tools, including clippers, scissors, comb, and hair products like styling cream or pomade. Here are the basic steps to follow:

Step 1: Determine the Length

The first step in cutting a burst fade is to determine the length of the hair on top. This will depend on your personal preference and the shape of your head. Use scissors or clippers to trim the hair to the desired length.

Step 2: Create the Fade

The next step is to create the fade by cutting the hair on the sides and back of the head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one, gradually blending the hair into the longer hair on top. Use a comb to guide your clippers and create a smooth transition between the different lengths.

Step 3: Define the Burst

Once you've created the fade, it's time to define the burst. Use scissors or clippers to trim the hair on top, leaving a longer section in the middle. This can be styled in a variety of ways, including slicked back, spiked up, or tousled.

Step by Step Guide

To make it easier for you to cut a burst fade, here is a step by step guide:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. This includes clippers, scissors, comb, and styling products like pomade or gel.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Decide on the length of the hair on top and trim it using scissors or clippers. Make sure it is even and neat.

Step 3: Start with the Sides

Begin cutting the sides and back of the head using a longer guard on your clippers. Gradually work your way down to a shorter guard, creating a fade effect.

Step 4: Blend the Fade

Use a comb to blend the different lengths of hair and create a smooth transition between the fade and the longer hair on top.

Step 5: Define the Burst

Use scissors or clippers to trim the hair on top, leaving a longer section in the middle. You can style it in a variety of ways, depending on your preference.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Apply some styling product like pomade or gel to your hair and style it according to your preference. You can slick it back, spike it up, or leave it tousled.

Tips for Cutting Burst Fade

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a burst fade:

  • Start with a longer guard on your clippers and work your way down gradually to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb to guide your clippers and create a smooth transition between the different lengths of hair.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting a burst fade requires precision and skill.
  • Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut a burst fade at home?

A: Yes, you can cut a burst fade at home if you have the right tools and some experience with cutting hair. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's best to visit a professional barber.

Q: How often do I need to cut my burst fade?

A: The frequency of your haircuts will depend on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your hairstyle. On average, you should get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your burst fade looking fresh.

Q: Can I style my burst fade in different ways?

A: Yes, you can style your burst fade in a variety of ways, including slicked back, spiked up, or tousled. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your burst fade looking its best, here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it and cause it to become brittle.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can cause damage and breakage.

Cutting a burst fade is a trendy and stylish way to update your look. With the right tools and some practice, you can achieve this modern haircut at home. Follow our step by step guide, tips, and hair care advice to keep your burst fade looking its best.
