How to Thin Curly Thick Hair - Tips and Tricks

If you have curly thick hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. Thick hair can be heavy and overwhelming, while curly hair can be difficult to comb through. In this article, we will discuss how to thin curly thick hair to make it more manageable and easier to style.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Curly Thick Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Curly Thick Hair

How to Thin Curly Thick Hair

Thinning curly thick hair involves removing some of the bulk without sacrificing the curls. This can be done in a few different ways:

  • Using thinning shears
  • Getting a razor cut
  • Using a hair thinning razor

Each method has its own benefits and drawbacks, so it's important to choose the one that's right for you.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to thin curly thick hair using thinning shears:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it until it's about 80% dry.
  2. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Part your hair into sections and clip them up.
  4. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up.
  5. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair at a 45-degree angle, about halfway down the length of the hair.
  6. Repeat this process all over your head, focusing on the areas where your hair is thickest.
  7. Comb through your hair again to ensure that it's even.

Alternatively, you can get a razor cut or use a hair thinning razor to achieve a similar look.

Tips for Thinning Curly Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning curly thick hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to avoid over-thinning.
  • Be careful not to thin the hair too much, as this can cause split ends and damage.
  • Use a good quality pair of thinning shears or razor to ensure a clean cut.
  • Consult a hairstylist if you're unsure about how much hair to thin.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning curly thick hair:

How often should I thin my curly thick hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Generally, you should aim to thin your hair every 3 to 6 months.

Can thinning curly thick hair damage it?

If done correctly, thinning curly thick hair should not damage it. However, if you over-thin the hair or use dull shears, it can cause split ends and breakage.

Hair Care for Curly Thick Hair

Thinning curly thick hair can make it more manageable, but it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using hair elastics or clips that can pull on the hair and cause breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay.

By following these tips and thinning your curly thick hair, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy head of hair that's easy to manage and style.
