How Do You Thin Thick Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Aug 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Thick curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it looks great when styled properly, it can be difficult to manage and style on a daily basis. One of the biggest challenges with thick curly hair is keeping it from looking too bulky and heavy. The key to achieving a more manageable style is to thin out the hair without sacrificing volume. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to thin thick curly hair.

How to Thin Thick Curly Hair

Thinning out thick curly hair involves carefully removing some of the hair strands to create a more manageable and natural-looking style. The process requires some skill, so it's best to let a professional stylist do it for you. However, if you prefer to do it yourself, there are a few techniques you can use.

1. Use Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are designed to remove some of the hair strands without cutting the hair completely. They have teeth-like blades that cut some strands while leaving others intact. To use thinning shears, start by sectioning off the hair you want to thin out. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and use the thinning shears to cut some of the strands. Repeat the process until you have achieved the desired volume.

2. Razor Cut

Razor cutting is another technique that can be used to thin out thick curly hair. It involves using a razor blade to cut some of the strands of hair. This technique requires a lot of skill, so it's best to have a professional stylist do it for you. Razor cutting can create a more natural-looking style and add texture to your hair.

3. Use a Texturizing Spray

A texturizing spray can be used to create a more natural-looking style without thinning out the hair completely. Texturizing sprays add volume and texture to the hair, making it easier to style. Simply spray the texturizing spray onto your hair and style as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're going to thin out your thick curly hair at home, here's a step-by-step guide to follow:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Section off the hair you want to thin out using clips.
  3. Use thinning shears or a razor blade to cut some of the strands of hair. Start with a small amount and gradually cut more if needed.
  4. Check your progress by releasing the sectioned-off hair and seeing if it looks more natural and manageable.
  5. Repeat the process until you have achieved the desired volume.

Tips for Thinning Thick Curly Hair

Thinning out thick curly hair can be a tricky process, but these tips can help make it easier:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors or thinning shears to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take breaks between sections to avoid fatigue and ensure accuracy.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and ensure you're cutting evenly.
  • Don't over-thin your hair, as it can make it look stringy and lifeless.

FAQs

Can you thin out curly hair?

Yes, you can thin out curly hair using thinning shears or a razor blade. The process involves carefully removing some of the hair strands to create a more manageable and natural-looking style.

How do you know if your hair needs to be thinned out?

If your hair feels heavy and bulky, it may need to be thinned out. Thinning out the hair can make it easier to manage and style, and it can also create a more natural-looking style.

Is it better to thin out curly hair when it's wet or dry?

It's best to thin out curly hair when it's dry. Wet hair can shrink up to 30%, which can make it difficult to achieve the desired length and volume. Thinning out dry hair allows you to see the final result more accurately.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Thick curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair and make it more difficult to manage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can damage the hair and cause frizz.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down the hair and make it look greasy.

Thick curly hair can be difficult to manage, but by following these tips and tricks, you can achieve a more manageable and natural-looking style.
