How To Thin Your Own Hair With Thinning Scissors

Written by Petter Nov 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning scissors are a great tool for those who have thick hair and want to reduce the volume. But, do you know how to use them correctly? In this article, we will teach you how to thin your own hair with thinning scissors in a relaxed and easy-to-understand way. Let's get started!

Description

Thinning scissors are scissors with one serrated blade and one straight blade that are used to remove some of the bulk from thick hair. They are also known as texturizing scissors or blending scissors. Thinning scissors are different from regular scissors because they don't cut all the hair at once, but rather they remove some of the hair while leaving some intact. This creates a more natural and textured look.

How to Thin Your Own Hair with Thinning Scissors

Thinning your hair with thinning scissors can be done at home, but it requires some practice and patience. Before you begin, make sure you have a good quality pair of thinning scissors, a comb, and hair clips.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair as you normally would and let it air dry or blow dry it. It's important to work with dry hair to see the true thickness and texture of your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, left, right, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Start with the Bottom Section

Start with the bottom section of your hair. Comb through it to remove any tangles and knots. Hold your thinning scissors vertically and start cutting from the ends of your hair up to about halfway up the hair strand. Make sure to cut at an angle and not straight across. Repeat this process on each section of your hair.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you have thinned out each section, blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair from each section and blend them together by cutting at an angle with your thinning scissors. This will create a more natural and textured look.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair with Thinning Scissors

Thinning your hair with thinning scissors can be tricky, so here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a comb to keep your hair taut while you cut.
  • Always hold your thinning scissors vertically.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut less than to cut too much.
  • Blend the layers together to create a more natural and textured look.

FAQs

Can I use regular scissors to thin my hair?

No, regular scissors are not designed to thin hair. They will create a blunt cut and can leave your hair looking uneven and choppy.

Can I thin my hair if it's already thin?

No, if your hair is already thin, thinning it further can make it look stringy and damaged.

How often should I thin my hair?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. On average, it's recommended to thin your hair every three to six months.

Hair Care After Thinning Your Hair

After thinning your hair with thinning scissors, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools, and try to let your hair air dry as much as possible. Finally, trim your hair regularly to keep it looking healthy and prevent split ends.
