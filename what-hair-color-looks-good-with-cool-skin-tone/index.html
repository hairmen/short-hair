What Hair Color Looks Good With Cool Skin Tone A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a cool skin tone, finding the right hair color can be a bit of a challenge. Cool skin tones are characterized by blue or pink undertones, and they look best with hair colors that complement these undertones. In this article, we'll take a look at what hair colors look good with cool skin tones, how to choose the right hair color, and some tips for maintaining your new color.

Description

When it comes to hair colors that look good with cool skin tones, there are a few options to consider. Some of the most popular hair colors for cool skin tones include:

  • Platinum blonde
  • Ash blonde
  • Icy blonde
  • Cool brown
  • Ash brown
  • Jet black
  • Cool red

These hair colors complement the cool undertones in the skin, creating a harmonious look. It's important to note that warm hair colors, such as golden blonde, copper, and warm brown, can clash with cool skin tones and make the skin look washed out.

How to Choose the Right Hair Color

When choosing a hair color, consider your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color. If you have cool undertones in your skin, look for hair colors that have cool undertones as well.

If you're not sure what your skin tone is, look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you have cool undertones. If they appear green, you have warm undertones.

Another option is to try on different colored wigs or use virtual hair color apps to see what different hair colors would look like on you before committing to a color.

Some other factors to consider when choosing a hair color include:

  • Your eye color: Some hair colors can make your eye color pop, while others can clash with it.
  • Your natural hair color: Going too far from your natural hair color can be a shock to your system and require more upkeep.
  • Your lifestyle: If you don't have a lot of time to devote to hair care, choose a hair color that is low maintenance.

Step-by-Step Guide to Coloring Your Hair

If you're new to coloring your hair, it's best to visit a professional stylist. They can help you choose the right color and ensure that your hair is properly cared for. If you're coloring your hair at home, follow these steps:

  1. Choose a hair color that complements your skin tone.
  2. Perform a patch test to make sure you're not allergic to the hair color.
  3. Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline and on your ears to prevent staining.
  4. Divide your hair into sections.
  5. Apply the hair color to each section, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  6. Leave the hair color on for the recommended amount of time.
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  8. Apply a conditioner to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Hair Color

Once you've colored your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep your color looking fresh and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for color-treated hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the color.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause the color to fade.

FAQs

Q: Can warm hair colors ever look good with cool skin tones?

A: While warm hair colors can clash with cool skin tones, there are some exceptions. For example, a warm red with cool undertones can complement cool skin tones.

Q: How often should I touch up my hair color?

A: This depends on the color and how fast your hair grows. As a general rule, it's recommended to touch up your roots every 6-8 weeks.

Q: Can I color my hair if it's already damaged?

A: It's best to avoid coloring damaged hair, as it can further damage the hair. It's important to first repair any damage before coloring your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Colored Hair

Colored hair requires special care to keep it healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause the color to fade.
  • Avoid using products that contain sulfates, as they can strip the color from your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to protect your hair from damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant for weeks to come.
