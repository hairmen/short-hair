Do Perms Suit Round Faces

Written by Daniel Oct 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Perming is a great way to add volume and texture to your hair. However, if you have a round face, you may be wondering if perms suit your face shape. In this article, we will explore this topic and provide you with tips on how to choose the best perm style for your round face.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Right Perm Style for Your Round Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Perming Your Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Perm
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Permed Hair

Description

Round faces have a soft, circular shape with a wide forehead, plump cheeks, and a rounded chin. The key to finding the right perm style for a round face is to create the illusion of length and angles. This means that you should avoid adding too much volume to the sides of your face and focus on creating more volume on the top and crown area.

How to Choose the Right Perm Style for Your Round Face

When choosing a perm style, consider the following:

  • Face shape: As mentioned earlier, you want to create the illusion of length and angles. Therefore, styles that add volume to the sides of your face, such as tight curls or spiral perms, may not be the best choice. Instead, opt for loose waves or beachy curls that add volume on top.
  • Hair type: Your hair type will also determine the type of perm that suits you. Fine hair may not hold curls as well as thicker hair, so a larger curling rod may be needed. Thick hair may need a tighter curling rod to create more defined curls.
  • Personal style: Perming is a personal choice, and it should reflect your personal style. If you prefer a more natural look, opt for loose waves. If you want a bolder look, go for tighter curls.

Step-by-Step Guide to Perming Your Hair

Perming your hair requires some preparation and a bit of skill. Here's how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and oils.
  2. Section your hair and wrap it around perm rods of your desired size. The tighter the rod, the tighter the curl.
  3. Apply a perm solution, starting from the back of your head and working your way up to the top.
  4. Leave the solution on for the recommended time, usually around 15-20 minutes.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water and apply a neutralizer. This will help set the curls.
  6. Leave the neutralizer on for the recommended time, usually around 10 minutes.
  7. Remove the perm rods and rinse your hair with cool water.
  8. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair to restore moisture and prevent damage.
  9. Dry your hair with a diffuser or air dry.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Your Perm

Once you have permed your hair, you will need to take extra care to maintain the curls. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after perming to allow the curls to set.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage to your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your curls.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about perming:

  • Is perming damaging to hair? Perming involves the use of chemicals that can cause damage to your hair if not done correctly. However, if you follow the instructions carefully and take proper care of your permed hair, you can minimize damage.
  • How long does a perm last? A perm can last up to six months, depending on your hair type and how well you take care of your curls.
  • Can I perm my hair if it's already colored? Yes, but you should wait at least two weeks after coloring your hair before perming it. This will allow your hair to recover from the coloring process.
  • What type of perm is best for fine hair? Loose waves or body waves are best for fine hair as they create volume without weighing down your hair.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Permed hair requires extra care to keep it healthy and maintain the curls. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Apply a deep conditioner once a week to restore moisture and prevent damage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as they can damage your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet as this can break your curls.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to prevent frizz.

Overall, perming can be a great way to add volume and texture to your hair, but it's important to choose the right perm style for your face shape and hair type. Follow the tips and instructions provided in this article to achieve beautiful, healthy, and long-lasting curls.
