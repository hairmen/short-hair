How To Style A Pixie Cut Without Heat A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're the proud owner of a pixie cut, you know how liberating it can be to have a low-maintenance hairstyle. However, sometimes you want to switch things up and add a little extra flair to your look. But what if you don't want to use heat styling tools that can damage your hair over time? Fortunately, there are plenty of ways to style your pixie cut without heat. Read on for some tips and tricks!

How to Style a Pixie Cut Without Heat

Before we dive into specific styling techniques, it's important to prepare your hair properly. Start with clean, dry hair, and use a lightweight, non-greasy product like a texturizing spray or mousse to add some grip and hold.

Step 1: Side Sweep

One of the easiest ways to style your pixie cut without heat is to simply sweep your hair to one side. Use a comb to create a deep side part, then smooth your hair over to the opposite side. You can leave it loose for a relaxed, tousled look, or secure it with bobby pins for a more polished finish.

Step 2: Faux Hawk

For a more edgy look, try a faux hawk. Start by applying a styling cream to your hair, then use your fingers to create a tousled, textured look. Brush the sides of your hair up and towards the center, and use bobby pins or a hair tie to secure it in place.

Step 3: Braids

Pixie cuts may be short, but that doesn't mean you can't incorporate braids into your style. For a simple look, braid a small section of hair at the front of your head and secure it with a clear elastic. Alternatively, try a French braid along one side of your head, or a fishtail braid at the back.

Step 4: Accessories

Finally, don't underestimate the power of accessories when it comes to styling your pixie cut. Headbands, scarves, and hair clips can all add a pop of color or texture to your look. Try a sleek metal headband for a modern touch, or a floral scarf for a boho vibe.

Tips for Styling a Pixie Cut Without Heat

- Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type and desired style.

- Don't be afraid to play with texture, whether that means using a sea salt spray or adding in some curls with a curling wand.

- Invest in some quality hair accessories to add some flair to your look.

- If you're struggling to get your hair to stay put, try using a strong-hold hairspray or hair wax.

FAQ

Q: Can I use heat on my pixie cut?

A: While it's possible to use heat styling tools on a pixie cut, it's generally not recommended as it can damage your hair over time.

Q: How often should I wash my pixie cut?

A: This will depend on your hair type and personal preference, but most people can get away with washing their pixie cut once or twice a week.

Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

Pixie cuts may be low-maintenance, but that doesn't mean you should neglect your hair care routine. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't strip your hair of its natural oils, and be sure to deep condition once a week. Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking neat and healthy, and protect it from the sun and environmental damage by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

In conclusion, with a little creativity and some simple styling techniques, you can achieve a variety of looks for your pixie cut without resorting to heat styling tools. Have fun experimenting and finding what works best for you!
