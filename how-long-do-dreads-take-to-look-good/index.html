How Long Do Dreads Take To Look Good

Written by William Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering getting dreadlocks, you're probably wondering how long it will take before they start to look good. The truth is, it can take anywhere from a few months to a few years for your dreads to look their best. In this article, we'll explore how long it takes for dreads to look good, as well as provide tips for maintaining and caring for your dreads.

How to Get Dreads

Before we dive into how long it takes for dreads to look good, let's briefly discuss how to get dreads in the first place. There are a few different methods for creating dreadlocks, including backcombing, twisting, and the neglect method. The neglect method involves simply letting your hair mat and tangle on its own, while the other methods require some manipulation and maintenance.

Step-by-Step Guide to Creating Dreads

If you're interested in creating dreads using the backcombing method, here's a step-by-step guide to get you started:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Backcomb each section of hair from the ends toward the roots, using a fine-tooth comb.
  4. Twist each section of backcombed hair tightly in one direction.
  5. Apply a locking gel or wax to each twisted section, and palm roll the hair until it feels tight and secure.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 for each section of hair.
  7. Let your hair air dry, or use a hair dryer on a low heat setting.

Keep in mind that this is just one method for creating dreadlocks, and there are many other techniques and variations out there.

How Long Do Dreads Take to Look Good?

Now that you know how to create dreads, let's get to the question at hand: how long do dreads take to look good? The answer is, it depends.

If you're starting with short hair, it will likely take longer for your dreads to mature and look their best. On average, it takes about six months to a year for dreads to fully lock up and develop a neat, uniform appearance. However, some people's hair may take longer or shorter than this depending on factors like hair texture, thickness, and length.

In the early stages of dreadlock formation, your hair may look messy, frizzy, and uneven. This is normal and to be expected. As your dreads continue to mature and tighten up, they'll start to take on a more defined shape and texture.

Tips for Maintaining and Caring for Your Dreads

While you're waiting for your dreads to mature, it's important to take good care of them to ensure they look their best once they do. Here are some tips for maintaining and caring for your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils, as these can prevent your dreads from locking up properly.
  • Use a specialized locking gel or wax to help your dreads hold their shape and tighten up.
  • Be gentle when handling your dreads, and avoid pulling or tugging on them excessively.
  • Get regular maintenance from a professional loctician to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about how long it takes for dreads to look good:

  • Q: Can I speed up the process of my dreads looking good?
  • A: Not really. Dreads take time to mature and develop, and there's no way to rush the process.
  • Q: How long will it take for my dreads to be fully matured?
  • A: It can take anywhere from a few months to a few years for dreads to be fully matured, depending on your hair type and how well you care for them.
  • Q: Should I cut off my dreads if they don't look good right away?
  • A: No! It's normal for dreads to look messy and uneven in the early stages of formation. Give them time to mature and they'll start to look better.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to remember that caring for your hair is key to getting the best results with your dreads. Eating a healthy diet, staying hydrated, and avoiding excessive heat styling or chemical treatments can all help keep your hair in good condition and promote healthy dreadlock formation.

In conclusion, how long it takes for dreads to look good varies from person to person, but with patience and proper care, you can achieve the look you're after.
