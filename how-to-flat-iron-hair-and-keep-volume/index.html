How To Flat Iron Hair And Keep Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a sleek, polished look, flat ironing your hair is a great option. However, many people worry that flat ironing will leave their hair looking flat and lifeless. The good news is that it is possible to flat iron your hair and keep volume. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve this look, as well as provide some tips and hair care advice to keep your hair looking healthy and voluminous.

Table of Contents

  • How to Flat Iron Hair and Keep Volume: Step-by-Step
  • Tips for Flat Ironing Hair with Volume
  • FAQs About Flat Ironing Hair with Volume
  • Hair Care Advice for Flat Ironed Hair with Volume

How to Flat Iron Hair and Keep Volume: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you flat iron your hair, it's important to start with clean, conditioned hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type, and consider using a volumizing conditioner to help give your hair some extra lift.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Before you start flat ironing your hair, it's important to protect your hair from heat damage. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, focusing on the ends where your hair is most vulnerable to damage.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to create volume. You can also flip your head upside down and blow dry your hair from the roots to create even more volume. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start flat ironing.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to flat iron your hair evenly and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 5: Flat Iron Your Hair

Starting with one section of hair, clamp the flat iron as close to the roots as possible and glide it down the length of your hair. Make sure to keep the flat iron moving smoothly and quickly, without stopping or pausing in any one spot. Repeat this process with each section of hair until your entire head is flat ironed.

Step 6: Add Volume

Once your hair is flat ironed, you can use a few tricks to add volume. Try backcombing the hair at the crown of your head, or using a volumizing powder or spray to give your roots some lift. You can also flip your hair upside down and gently massage your roots to create more volume.

Tips for Flat Ironing Hair with Volume

Here are a few additional tips to help you flat iron your hair while maintaining volume:

  • Choose a flat iron with ceramic plates, which distribute heat evenly and are less likely to cause damage to your hair.
  • Don't flat iron your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair before flat ironing, which will help prevent damage and breakage.
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair after flat ironing to add shine and prevent frizz.

FAQs About Flat Ironing Hair with Volume

Can you flat iron hair without damaging it?

Flat ironing your hair can cause damage if you don't use the right products and techniques. To minimize damage, use a heat protectant spray or serum, choose a flat iron with ceramic plates, and avoid flat ironing your hair too often.

How do you prevent flat hair when flat ironing?

To prevent flat hair when flat ironing, use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, blow dry your hair with a round brush to create volume, and use backcombing or volumizing powders or sprays to add lift after flat ironing.

How long does flat ironed hair last?

Flat ironed hair can last for several days, but it is important to avoid getting your hair wet or sweating excessively, as this can cause your hair to revert back to its natural texture.

Hair Care Advice for Flat Ironed Hair with Volume

To keep your flat ironed hair looking healthy and voluminous, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and moisturize your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can flat iron your hair and keep volume for a polished, sleek look that still has plenty of bounce and body. Just remember to use the right products, techniques, and hair care practices to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
