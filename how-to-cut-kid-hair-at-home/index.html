How To Cut Kid Hair At Home A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Aug 19, 2023 · 4 min read

As parents, we all know how difficult it can be to take our kids to the salon for a haircut. From the long wait times to the fear of a bad haircut, it's a stressful experience for both the parents and the child. That's why many parents have started to cut their kid's hair at home. Not only is it more convenient, but it's also cost-effective. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut kid hair at home.

How to Cut Kid Hair at Home

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

The first step in cutting your kid's hair at home is to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clippers, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also a good idea to have a cape or a towel to wrap around your child's shoulders to protect their clothing.

Step 2: Wash and Comb Your Child's Hair

Before you start cutting your child's hair, you should wash and comb it. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the hair is clean. Use the spray bottle to dampen the hair if it's dry or tangled. Comb the hair to remove any knots and tangles.

Step 3: Start with the Clippers

If you're using clippers to cut your child's hair, start by using the longest guard attachment. Start at the back of the head and work your way up towards the top. Use long, sweeping motions to ensure an even cut. If you're unsure about how short to cut the hair, start with a longer guard and work your way down.

Step 4: Move on to Scissors

Once you've finished with the clippers, it's time to move on to the scissors. Use the comb to section off the hair you want to cut. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut it to the desired length. Take your time and make small snips to avoid cutting off too much hair.

Step 5: Trim the Bangs

If your child has bangs, use the scissors to trim them. Start by combing the bangs straight down and cutting them to the desired length. Make sure to cut them straight across to avoid any unevenness.

Step 6: Check for Any Unevenness

Once you've finished cutting your child's hair, check for any unevenness. Use the scissors to trim any sections that are longer than others. Take your time and make sure that everything is even.

Step 7: Clean Up

After you've finished cutting your child's hair, it's time to clean up. Use a vacuum or a broom to clean up any stray hairs. Remove the cape or towel and shake it out outside to get rid of any loose hairs.

Tips for Cutting Kid Hair at Home

Cutting your child's hair at home can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and a little bit of patience, it can be done. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Start with a longer guard or longer length when cutting with clippers.
  • Make small snips with the scissors to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • Take your time and check for any unevenness.
  • Have a picture or style in mind before you start cutting.
  • Watch tutorial videos online for extra guidance.

FAQ

Can I cut my child's hair with regular scissors?

While you can technically cut your child's hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Hair scissors are designed to provide a clean cut and prevent split ends. Using regular scissors can result in a jagged cut and split ends.

How often should I cut my child's hair?

The frequency of haircuts depends on the length and style of your child's hair. On average, most children need a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain their style and keep their hair healthy.

Hair Care After Cutting Kid Hair at Home

After you've finished cutting your child's hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips to keep your child's hair healthy:

  • Wash their hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle their hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on their hair.
  • Trim their hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style and prevent split ends.

Cutting your child's hair at home can be a rewarding experience. With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can give your child a great haircut without leaving the house. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
