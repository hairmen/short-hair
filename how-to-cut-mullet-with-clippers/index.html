How To Cut Mullet With Clippers A Step By Step Guide In 2023

Written by Daniel Jun 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to achieve the ultimate business in the front, party in the back hairstyle? Look no further than the mullet. This iconic haircut has made a comeback in recent years and is a popular choice for those who want to stand out from the crowd. But how do you achieve the perfect mullet? In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut mullet with clippers.

Description

The mullet is a unique hairstyle that features short hair on the top and sides of the head, with longer hair in the back. It's a versatile haircut that can be styled in many different ways, from slicked back to curly. Cutting a mullet with clippers is a quick and easy way to achieve this look.

How to Cut Mullet with Clippers

Before you begin, you'll need a few tools:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Comb
  • Haircutting scissors
  • Haircutting cape or towel

Now, let's get started:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you begin. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the final result is even.

Step 2: Section Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into three parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Start with the sides of your head. Use clippers with the appropriate guard to trim the hair to the desired length. For a classic mullet, leave the hair on the sides short, about 1/4 inch or shorter.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Next, move to the top of your head. Use scissors to trim the hair to the desired length. For a classic mullet, leave the hair on top slightly longer than the sides, about 1-2 inches.

Step 5: Cut the Back

Finally, it's time to tackle the back of your head. Using clippers with a longer guard, trim the hair in the back to the desired length. For a classic mullet, leave the hair in the back long, about 4-6 inches.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Using scissors, blend the layers of hair to create a seamless transition from the short sides to the longer top and back. Take your time with this step to ensure that the finished product looks even and polished.

Step 7: Clean Up the Neckline

Using clippers, clean up the neckline and remove any stray hairs. This will give your mullet a clean and polished look.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard. This will help you achieve the desired length without cutting too much off at once.
  • Use a comb to help guide the clippers as you cut.
  • Take breaks as needed to step back and assess your progress. It's better to take your time and get the cut right than rush through it and make mistakes.

FAQ

How often should I get a mullet haircut?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your mullet. Generally, you should plan to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your mullet looking fresh.

Can I cut my own mullet?

Yes, you can cut your own mullet at home with clippers and a little bit of patience. However, if you're new to cutting hair, it might be worth visiting a professional stylist for your first cut.

Hair Care

Now that you've achieved the perfect mullet, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and consider using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and make it more difficult to style.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut the perfect mullet with clippers in no time. So go ahead, embrace your inner Billy Ray Cyrus, and rock that mullet with confidence!
