How To Grow Short Relaxed Hair Fast Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Oct 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short relaxed hair and are looking to grow it out quickly, you may be wondering where to start. With so many products and techniques available, it can be overwhelming to know what to do. Fortunately, there are several steps you can take to encourage healthy hair growth and get the length you desire. In this article, we'll provide you with some helpful tips and tricks for growing short relaxed hair fast.

How to

1. Keep your hair moisturized

Dry hair is more likely to break and become damaged, which can slow down the growth process. To keep your hair moisturized, use a leave-in conditioner or oil regularly, and avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Trim your hair regularly

While it may seem counterintuitive, trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks can actually help it grow faster. This is because regular trims can prevent split ends and breakage, which can slow down the growth process.

3. Use a protein treatment

Protein treatments can help strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for a protein treatment that is specifically formulated for relaxed hair, and use it according to the instructions on the package.

4. Massage your scalp

Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow and stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes each day, or invest in a scalp massager for an even more relaxing experience.

Step by Step

Step 1: Start with the right products

To encourage healthy hair growth, it's important to start with the right products. Look for a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for relaxed hair, and avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.

Step 2: Keep your hair moisturized

As mentioned earlier, keeping your hair moisturized is key to preventing breakage and promoting healthy growth. Use a leave-in conditioner or oil regularly, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

Step 3: Trim your hair regularly

While it may be tempting to skip trims in order to retain length, regular trims are actually important for healthy hair growth. Schedule a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Step 4: Use a protein treatment

To strengthen your hair and prevent breakage, use a protein treatment once a month. Look for a treatment that is specifically formulated for relaxed hair, and use it according to the instructions on the package.

Step 5: Massage your scalp

Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow and stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes each day, or invest in a scalp massager for an even more relaxing experience.

Tips

1. Protect your hair at night

Sleeping with a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase can help prevent breakage and keep your hair moisturized.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and slow down the growth process. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

3. Eat a healthy diet

A diet rich in vitamins and minerals is essential for healthy hair growth. Focus on eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

FAQ

Q: How long will it take to grow my hair out?

A: The rate at which your hair grows can vary depending on factors like genetics, age, and overall health. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so it may take several months to grow out short hair.

Q: Can I use hair growth supplements?

A: While some supplements claim to promote hair growth, it's important to talk to your doctor before taking any new supplements.

Q: Can I still color my hair while trying to grow it out?

A: It's generally safe to color your hair while trying to grow it out, but be sure to use a gentle, non-damaging hair color and avoid over-processing your hair.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential for healthy growth. In addition to the tips mentioned above, be sure to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair. If you're experiencing hair loss or other hair-related issues, be sure to talk to your doctor or a licensed hair care professional for advice. With a little patience and dedication, you can grow out your short relaxed hair and achieve the length you desire.
