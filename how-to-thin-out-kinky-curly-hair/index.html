How To Thin Out Kinky Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Thick, kinky curly hair can be a blessing, but it can also be a curse. While it’s gorgeous and unique, it can be difficult to manage and style. Thankfully, thinning out kinky curly hair can help make it more manageable and easier to style. In this guide, we’ll show you how to thin out kinky curly hair in a few simple steps.

How to Thin Out Kinky Curly Hair

Thinning out your kinky curly hair can help reduce its volume and make it more manageable. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you start thinning out your hair, make sure it’s clean and dry. Wash and condition your hair as usual, and let it air dry or blow dry it on a low heat setting. If you have product in your hair, make sure to wash it out before you start.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into 4-6 sections and clip them up. This will make it easier to work on one section at a time.

Step 3: Thin Out Your Hair with Thinning Shears

Take a small section of hair and use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, taking small snips with the shears. Be careful not to take too much hair at once, as this can result in uneven layers or thin spots.

Repeat this process on each section of your hair, making sure to work evenly on both sides of your head.

Step 4: Blend Your Layers

After you’ve thinned out your hair, it’s important to blend your layers. Take a comb and comb through your hair, making sure that the layers blend seamlessly together.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve thinned out your hair, it’s time to style it. You can wear your hair curly or straight, depending on your preference. Use a styling product to define your curls or straighten your hair with a flat iron. You can also blow dry your hair to add more volume.

Tips for Thinning Out Kinky Curly Hair

Thinning out your kinky curly hair can be a bit tricky, so here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Start slow: It’s better to take too little hair than too much. Start with small snips and work your way up.
  • Use the right tools: Thinning shears are specifically designed to thin out hair, so make sure you have a good pair.
  • Don’t thin out too much: Thinning out too much hair can result in thin spots or uneven layers.
  • Blend your layers: After you’ve thinned out your hair, it’s important to blend your layers together for a seamless look.
  • Style your hair: Once you’ve thinned out your hair, style it to your liking. You can wear it curly or straight, depending on your preference.

FAQ: Thinning Out Kinky Curly Hair

Can you thin out kinky curly hair without cutting it?

Thinning out kinky curly hair typically involves cutting some of the bulk out with thinning shears. While you can technically thin out your hair without cutting it, it’s not as effective and won’t provide the same results.

Can you thin out kinky curly hair at home?

Yes, you can thin out kinky curly hair at home. However, it’s important to be careful and start slow. If you’re not confident in your ability to thin out your hair, it’s best to visit a professional stylist.

How often should you thin out kinky curly hair?

How often you should thin out your hair depends on how quickly it grows and how much volume you want to reduce. Some people may only need to thin out their hair once a year, while others may need to do it every few months.

Hair Care for Thinned Out Kinky Curly Hair

After you’ve thinned out your kinky curly hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Protect your hair when you sleep by wearing a silk or satin bonnet or using a silk or satin pillowcase.

Thinning out kinky curly hair can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it’s a simple process. By following these steps and taking care of your hair properly, you can enjoy beautiful, manageable hair every day.
