How To Fix A Bad Hair Color Job

Written by Frank Sep 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Getting a bad hair color job can be a nightmare, and it's frustrating to be stuck with something that you don't like. But don't worry, there are ways to fix it! In this article, we'll walk you through the steps on how to fix a bad hair color job and get your hair looking great again.

How to Fix a Bad Hair Color Job

Before we get into the steps on how to fix a bad hair color job, let's talk about what could have gone wrong in the first place. Here are a few reasons why you might have ended up with a bad hair color:

  • The color wasn't left on for the right amount of time.
  • The color wasn't mixed properly.
  • The color was applied incorrectly.
  • The color wasn't suited for your hair type.

If you're dealing with any of these issues, don't worry, we've got you covered. Here's how to fix a bad hair color job:

Step 1: Don't Panic

It's easy to get worked up when something goes wrong, but try not to panic. A bad hair color job can be fixed, and getting upset won't help the situation. Take a deep breath and remind yourself that it's just hair.

Step 2: Assess the Damage

Take a good look at your hair and try to determine what went wrong. Is the color too dark, too light, or just plain wrong? This will help you decide what steps to take next.

Step 3: Decide on the Fix

Depending on what went wrong, you have a few options on how to fix a bad hair color job:

  • Color correction: This involves using a color that will counteract the unwanted color. For example, if your hair turned out too brassy, a purple toner can help neutralize the brassiness.
  • Color removal: If the color is just plain wrong, you may need to remove it completely before applying a new color. This can be done with a color remover or bleach.
  • Wait it out: If the color isn't too far off, you may be able to wait it out until it fades or grows out.

Step 4: Go to a Professional

If you're not comfortable fixing the problem yourself, or if the damage is too severe, it's best to go to a professional. They'll be able to assess the damage and recommend the best course of action.

Tips for Fixing a Bad Hair Color Job

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when fixing a bad hair color job:

  • Don't try to fix the problem immediately. Wait a few days to give your hair a break.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment to help repair any damage.
  • Be patient. Fixing a bad hair color job can take time, and it may not be perfect the first time around.

FAQs

Can I fix a bad hair color job myself?

It depends on the severity of the problem. If it's a minor issue, you may be able to fix it yourself. However, if the damage is severe, it's best to go to a professional.

How long should I wait before fixing a bad hair color job?

It's best to wait a few days to give your hair a break before attempting to fix the problem.

What should I do if the color is too dark?

If the color is too dark, you can try using a clarifying shampoo or a color remover to lighten it up.

Hair Care After Fixing a Bad Hair Color Job

After fixing a bad hair color job, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help maintain the color.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.

Fixing a bad hair color job can be a challenge, but it's not impossible. With the right steps and a little patience, you can get your hair looking great again in no time!
