How To Wear Headbands With Short Natural Hair

Written by Christine Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Headbands are an excellent way to add some style and flair to your short natural hair. They come in a variety of styles and colors, and they are perfect for days when you don't feel like doing much with your hair. But how do you wear headbands with short natural hair? In this article, we'll explore some tips and tricks for wearing headbands with short natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Headbands with Short Natural Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Wearing Headbands with Short Natural Hair
  • FAQs

How to Wear Headbands with Short Natural Hair

There are many ways to wear headbands with short natural hair. Here are a few of our favorites:

1. Wear it as a Statement Piece

If you have a bold and colorful headband, wear it as a statement piece. This means that you should keep the rest of your hair and outfit simple. This will help to draw attention to your headband and make it stand out.

2. Wear it with a Low Bun

If you have short natural hair, you can still wear a low bun. Simply gather your hair at the nape of your neck and secure it with an elastic band. Then, place your headband over your head and push it down to your forehead.

3. Wear it with a High Bun

If you have enough hair to make a high bun, you can wear a headband with it. Simply gather your hair at the top of your head and secure it with an elastic band. Then, place your headband over your head and push it down to your forehead.

4. Wear it with a Twist-Out

If you have a twist-out or braid-out, you can still wear a headband. Simply place the headband over your hairline and push it down to your forehead. This will help to keep your hair out of your face and add some style to your look.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to wear headbands with short natural hair:

  1. Choose your headband. Look for one that complements your hair color and style.
  2. Place the headband over your head. You can position it so that it sits on your forehead or further back on your head.
  3. Push the headband down to your forehead. Make sure it is secure and comfortable.
  4. Adjust your hair. You can tuck your hair behind your ears or leave it out in front of the headband.
  5. Style your hair. You can wear your hair in a bun, twist-out, or any other style that you prefer.

Tips for Wearing Headbands with Short Natural Hair

Here are some tips for wearing headbands with short natural hair:

  • Choose a headband that is the right size for your head. If it is too tight, it will be uncomfortable. If it is too loose, it will fall off.
  • Experiment with different styles of headbands. You can try thin headbands, thick headbands, leather headbands, or any other style that you like.
  • Accessorize your headband. You can add a flower, bow, or other accessory to your headband to make it even more stylish.
  • Use bobby pins to secure your headband. If your headband keeps slipping, you can use bobby pins to secure it in place.

FAQs

Q: Can I wear a headband with short natural hair?

A: Yes, you can wear a headband with short natural hair. There are many ways to style a headband with short hair, including wearing it with a bun, twist-out, or simply pushing it back on your head.

Q: How do I choose the right headband?

A: When choosing a headband, consider your hair color, style, and the occasion you will be wearing it for. Look for a headband that complements your hair and adds some style to your look.

Q: How do I keep my headband from slipping?

A: If your headband keeps slipping, try using bobby pins to secure it in place. You can also look for headbands that have a non-slip grip or are adjustable.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips for wearing headbands with short natural hair:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly. Dry hair can be difficult to style and can cause your headband to slip.
  • Protect your hair at night. Sleep with a satin or silk bonnet or pillowcase to prevent breakage and maintain your style.
  • Don't wear your headband too tight. This can cause tension on your hairline and lead to breakage.
  • Take breaks from wearing headbands. Give your hair a break from time to time to prevent damage and allow your hairline to recover.

Wearing headbands with short natural hair is a fun and stylish way to add some flair to your look. Experiment with different styles and colors to find the perfect headband for you. And remember to take care of your hair by moisturizing regularly and avoiding tight headbands.
