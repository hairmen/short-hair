How Long Does It Take For Alopecia Barbae To Grow Back

Written by Robby Jul 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're one of the many men who suffer from alopecia barbae, you know how frustrating it can be to watch your beard fall out. But the good news is that in most cases, your beard will grow back on its own. The bad news is that it can take anywhere from a few weeks to several years for your beard to fully grow back, depending on the severity of your alopecia barbae.

Description:

Alopecia barbae is a type of hair loss that affects the beard area in men. It is characterized by small, round patches of hair loss on the chin and cheeks. The cause of alopecia barbae is not fully understood, but it is thought to be an autoimmune disorder that causes the body to attack hair follicles.

How to:

If you have alopecia barbae, there are a few things you can do to help your beard grow back faster:

  • Reduce stress: Stress can exacerbate autoimmune disorders, so try to find ways to relax and manage stress.
  • Eat a healthy diet: A diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help promote hair growth.
  • Take supplements: Some supplements, such as biotin and vitamin D, have been shown to promote hair growth.
  • Be patient: Unfortunately, there is no quick fix for alopecia barbae. It takes time for hair follicles to recover and start producing hair again.

Step by Step:

If you're wondering how long it will take for your beard to grow back, here's a step-by-step breakdown:

Step 1: Shedding

First, your beard will start to shed. This can be a slow process or it can happen all at once. It's important to avoid pulling or tugging on the hairs, as this can damage the hair follicles and slow down the regrowth process.

Step 2: Resting

Once the hairs have fallen out, the hair follicles will enter a resting phase. During this time, the follicles are not producing new hairs, but are instead preparing to start the growth phase again.

Step 3: Regrowth

Finally, the hair follicles will start to produce new hairs. This can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months, depending on the severity of your alopecia barbae. The new hairs may be finer or lighter in color than your previous beard, but over time they should thicken and darken.

Tips:

While you're waiting for your beard to grow back, there are a few things you can do to help it along:

  • Avoid shaving: Letting your beard grow out can help stimulate hair growth.
  • Moisturize: Keeping the skin under your beard moisturized can help promote hair growth.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals: Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products on your beard, as these can damage hair follicles.

FAQ:

Q: Can alopecia barbae be cured?

A: There is no cure for alopecia barbae, but in most cases the hair will grow back on its own.

Q: Is alopecia barbae contagious?

A: No, alopecia barbae is not contagious.

Q: Can I use minoxidil to help my beard grow back?

A: Minoxidil is a medication used to treat hair loss, but it has not been shown to be effective for alopecia barbae.

Hair Care:

Once your beard has grown back, it's important to take good care of it to prevent future hair loss. This includes:

  • Keeping your beard clean and well-moisturized
  • Avoiding harsh chemicals and styling products
  • Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients

With time and patience, your beard will grow back after alopecia barbae. Just remember to take good care of it once it does!
