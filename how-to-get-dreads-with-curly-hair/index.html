How to Get Dreads with Curly Hair: A Step-by-Step Guide

Are you tired of your curly hair and looking for a new style? Dreadlocks might be just what you need! But, if you have curly hair, getting dreads can be a bit more challenging. Don't worry, though - with the right techniques and tips, you can get dreads with curly hair that look amazing. In this article, we'll take you through a step-by-step process of getting dreads with curly hair. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Dreads with Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

How to Get Dreads with Curly Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that getting dreads with curly hair can take some time and patience. But, the end result is definitely worth it. Here are the basic steps to get dreads with curly hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Decide on the type of dreads you want. There are different types of dreadlocks, including freeform, traditional, and crochet dreads. Do some research and decide which type of dreadlocks you want to get.

Step 2: Wash your hair. It's important to start with clean hair, so wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any products, oils, or dirt from your hair.

Step 3: Section your hair. Use a comb to section your hair into small, equal-sized parts. The size of the sections will determine the size of your dreads.

Step 4: Apply dread wax. Apply a small amount of dread wax to each section of your hair. This will help your hair lock together and form dreads.

Step 5: Twist and palm roll your hair. Use your fingers to twist and palm roll each section of your hair. This will help the hair lock together and form dreads. Repeat this process for each section of your hair.

Step 6: Use a hair dryer. Use a hair dryer on a low heat setting to dry your hair. This will help set the dreads and prevent them from unraveling.

Step 7: Maintain your dreads. To maintain your dreads, you'll need to retwist and palm roll them every few weeks. You can also use a crochet hook to tighten any loose sections.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but avoid using shampoo too often, as it can dry out your hair.
  • Use a conditioner specifically designed for dreadlocks to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol or other drying agents.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to get dreads with curly hair?

A: It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year or more to get fully matured dreads with curly hair.

Q: Can you get dreads with short curly hair?

A: Yes, you can get dreads with short curly hair. Just make sure to section your hair into small parts and use a good dread wax to help the hair lock together.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Here are some tips for taking care of your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a dreadlocks shampoo.
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as it can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a t-shirt or microfiber towel to gently squeeze out excess water.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using products that contain petroleum, as it can clog your pores and cause scalp irritation.

Getting dreads with curly hair can be a bit more challenging, but with the right techniques and tips, you can achieve a great look. Remember to be patient and take good care of your dreads, and you'll have a style that you'll love for years to come!
