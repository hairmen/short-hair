How To Do Permanent Straight Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're tired of dealing with frizzy, curly, or wavy hair, permanent straightening may be the solution for you. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to do permanent straight hair, from preparing your hair to caring for it afterwards. We'll also provide tips and answer frequently asked questions to help you achieve the sleek, straight locks of your dreams.

Table of Contents:

  1. Preparing Your Hair
  2. Choosing a Straightening Method
  3. Straightening Your Hair
  4. Aftercare for Permanent Straightened Hair
  5. Tips for Maintaining Straight Hair
  6. FAQs
  7. General Hair Care Tips

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start the straightening process, it's important to prepare your hair. Here are some steps you can take:

1. Cleanse Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue that could interfere with the straightening process. Avoid using any heavy conditioners or oils that can weigh down your hair.

2. Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage from the straightening process. Make sure to choose a high-quality product that will effectively protect your hair from heat.

3. Dry Your Hair

Dry your hair completely before straightening it. You can either air-dry it or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting. Make sure your hair is completely dry before moving on to the next step.

Choosing a Straightening Method

There are several methods you can use to permanently straighten your hair. Here are some of the most popular:

1. Japanese Straightening

Also known as thermal reconditioning, Japanese straightening uses heat and chemicals to permanently straighten hair. This method is most effective on thick, coarse, and curly hair.

2. Keratin Treatment

Keratin treatments use a protein called keratin to straighten hair. It's a more gentle option than Japanese straightening and is best for those with fine, wavy, or slightly curly hair.

3. Chemical Relaxers

Chemical relaxers use strong chemicals to break down the bonds in your hair, making it easier to straighten. This method is best for those with thick or curly hair.

Straightening Your Hair

Once you've chosen a method, it's time to straighten your hair. Here's what you need to do:

1. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to apply the straightening product evenly.

2. Apply Straightening Product

Apply the straightening product to each section of hair, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to follow the instructions on the product carefully.

3. Straighten Your Hair

Using a flat iron or hair straightener, straighten each section of hair. Make sure to use the right temperature setting for your hair type, and avoid going over each section of hair more than once.

Aftercare for Permanent Straightened Hair

After straightening your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain its straightness. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Washing Your Hair Too Often

Wash your hair no more than twice a week to prevent it from becoming dry and damaged.

2. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent friction and keep your hair smooth and straight.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends from forming.

Tips for Maintaining Straight Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you maintain your straight hair:

1. Use a Hair Serum

Apply a hair serum to your hair to add shine and prevent frizz.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun's damaging rays.

FAQs

1. Is permanent straightening safe?

Yes, if done correctly by a professional, permanent straightening is safe. However, it's important to follow the aftercare instructions carefully to prevent damage to your hair.

2. How long does permanent straightening last?

Permanent straightening can last anywhere from six months to a year, depending on the method used and how well you take care of your hair afterwards.

3. Can I color my hair after permanent straightening?

It's best to wait at least two weeks after straightening your hair before coloring it. Make sure to use a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye to prevent damage to your hair.

General Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some general tips to help you take care of your hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these steps and tips, you can achieve beautiful, straight hair that lasts. Remember to choose a method that's best for your hair type, and take good care of your hair afterwards to maintain its straightness.
