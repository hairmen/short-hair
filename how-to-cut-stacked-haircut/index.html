How To Cut A Stacked Haircut Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, a stacked bob might be the perfect option for you. This haircut features shorter layers in the back that gradually get longer towards the front, creating a stacked effect. It's a versatile haircut that can be tailored to suit different face shapes and hair types. In this article, we'll show you how to cut a stacked haircut step-by-step.

Description of a Stacked Haircut

A stacked haircut is a type of bob that has shorter layers in the back and longer layers in the front. The layers are cut at an angle to create a stacked effect, which adds volume and texture to the hair. This haircut is suitable for different hair types and face shapes, as it can be customized to suit individual preferences. It's a low-maintenance haircut that looks chic and stylish.

How to Cut a Stacked Haircut

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare the hair. Wash and dry the hair, and comb it thoroughly to remove any tangles. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section the hair.

Step 1: Section the Hair

Section the hair into four parts: two on the sides and two at the back. Clip the sections of hair that you're not working on to keep them out of the way.

Step 2: Start Cutting the Back

Start cutting the back section of the hair. Take a small section of hair from the nape of the neck and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the shortest point and gradually getting longer towards the front. Repeat this process for each section of hair in the back.

Step 3: Move to the Sides

Once you've finished cutting the back section, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair from one of the sides and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, following the same technique as the back section. Repeat this process for each section of hair on both sides.

Step 4: Cut the Front Sections

Finally, it's time to cut the front sections of the hair. Take a small section of hair from the front and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the shortest point and gradually getting longer towards the back. Repeat this process for each section of hair in the front.

Tips for Cutting a Stacked Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a stacked haircut:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts.
  • Make sure the hair is dry before cutting it.
  • Section the hair carefully to ensure even cutting.
  • Keep the hair taut when cutting to avoid uneven cuts.

FAQ: Stacked Haircut

Q: Is a stacked haircut suitable for all hair types?

A: Yes, a stacked haircut can be tailored to suit different hair types, including straight, wavy, and curly hair.

Q: How often do I need to get a stacked haircut?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the haircut. Generally, a stacked haircut can be maintained for 6-8 weeks before needing a trim.

Q: Can I style my stacked haircut in different ways?

A: Yes, a stacked haircut is versatile and can be styled in different ways, such as straight and sleek, wavy and tousled, or curly and voluminous.

Hair Care for Stacked Haircut

Maintaining a stacked haircut requires some hair care tips to keep it looking fresh and stylish. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair to prevent damage.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to add shine and control frizz.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

Now that you know how to cut a stacked haircut, you can try it out for yourself. Remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully to ensure a clean and even cut. With the right hair care, your stacked haircut will look chic and stylish for weeks to come.
