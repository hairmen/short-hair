What To Do If A Perm Is Too Curly Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jul 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Perming your hair can give you a whole new look and style, but what if your perm turns out to be too curly? Don't worry, it happens to the best of us, and there are ways to fix it. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks on what to do if a perm is too curly.

How to Tell If Your Perm Is Too Curly

Before we dive into the solutions, let's first understand how to tell if your perm is too curly. A perm that is too curly will have tight curls that are hard to manage. Your hair may also feel dry and frizzy, and it may be difficult to style. If you're experiencing any of these issues, your perm may be too curly.

What to Do If a Perm Is Too Curly: Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wait It Out

If your perm is only a few days old, it's best to wait it out. Your curls will loosen up over time, and you may find that you like the way they look once they settle.

Step 2: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

If your perm is still too curly after a few days, try using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help hydrate your hair and reduce frizz. Avoid using any products that contain sulfates, as they can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can also help to soften your curls and make them more manageable. Apply a small amount to your hair while it's still damp, and comb through with a wide-toothed comb.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help to loosen up your curls and make them more relaxed. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to avoid causing any frizz. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before blow drying to prevent any damage to your hair.

Step 5: Use a Flat Iron

If your hair is still too curly after blow drying, you can use a flat iron to straighten it out. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using the flat iron, and work in small sections to ensure that each section is properly straightened.

Tips on How to Care for Permed Hair

If you're planning on getting a perm or have already gotten one, it's important to know how to care for your permed hair. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a shampoo and conditioner that is gentle and doesn't contain any harsh chemicals. This will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent any damage.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools on your permed hair as much as possible. Heat can cause damage and breakage, which can ruin your perm.

Tip 3: Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. This is especially important for permed hair, which can be more prone to damage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I undo a perm if I don't like it?

A: Yes, you can undo a perm, but it's not recommended. Perms can be damaging to your hair, and undoing it can cause even more damage.

Q: How long does a perm last?

A: A perm can last anywhere from 3 to 6 months, depending on how well you take care of it.

Q: Can I perm my hair if it's already been dyed?

A: Yes, but it's important to wait at least two weeks between dyeing and perming your hair to avoid any damage.

Conclusion

If your perm is too curly, don't panic. There are ways to fix it, and with the right care, you can keep your permed hair looking healthy and beautiful. Be sure to follow the tips and tricks outlined in this article, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
