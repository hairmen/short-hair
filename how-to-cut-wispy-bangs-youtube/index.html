How To Cut Wispy Bangs Youtube A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're thinking of changing up your hairstyle, getting wispy bangs can be the perfect way to do it. Wispy bangs are a versatile and trendy look that can add a touch of softness and elegance to your face. However, cutting them can be tricky, especially if you're doing it for the first time. Fortunately, there are many tutorials on YouTube that can guide you through the process. In this post, we'll show you how to cut wispy bangs YouTube in relaxed English language, so you can get the perfect look you want.

Description

Wispy bangs are a type of fringe that consists of thin, airy strands of hair that frame the forehead. They are usually cut to be longer on the sides and shorter in the middle, creating a feathered effect. This style is suitable for most face shapes and hair types and can be customized to suit your preferences. Cutting wispy bangs can be done at home, but it requires some skills and preparation.

How to

To cut your wispy bangs, you'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also helpful to have some hair clips to section off your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will help you see the natural texture and flow of your hair, which is essential for cutting wispy bangs.

Step 2: Section Off Your Bangs

Use a comb to section off the hair you want to cut as bangs. This section should be about one inch wide and extend from ear to ear. Clip the rest of your hair away so that it doesn't get in the way.

Step 3: Cut the Middle

Hold the middle of your bangs between your fingers and cut it straight across. This will be the shortest part of your wispy bangs. Be sure to cut only a little bit at a time, so you don't take off too much hair.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Next, take a small section of hair from one side of your bangs and hold it at a 45-degree angle. Use your scissors to cut vertically into the hair, creating a feathered effect. Repeat this on the other side of your bangs.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Use a mirror to check your work and make any necessary adjustments. If you want your bangs to be shorter, you can always cut more hair.

Step 6: Style Your Bangs

Once you're happy with your wispy bangs, you can style them using a hairdryer, curling iron, or flat iron. Experiment with different styles to see what works best for you.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your wispy bangs:

  • Always cut your bangs when your hair is dry, so you can see exactly how much you're cutting.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut a little bit at a time, so you don't take off too much hair.
  • Make sure your bangs are even on both sides.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and lengths.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting wispy bangs:

How long should wispy bangs be?

The length of your wispy bangs depends on your face shape and personal preference. As a general rule, they should be longer on the sides and shorter in the middle.

Can I cut my own wispy bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own wispy bangs, but it requires some skills and preparation. It's always best to watch tutorials and practice on a small section of hair before cutting your entire bangs.

How often should I trim my wispy bangs?

It's recommended to trim your wispy bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and neat.

Hair Care

To keep your wispy bangs looking their best, it's essential to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using a curling or straightening iron.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

Cutting wispy bangs YouTube can be a fun and easy way to change up your hairstyle. With these simple steps and tips, you can get the perfect look you want. Remember to take your time, be patient, and practice on a small section of hair before cutting your entire bangs. Happy styling!
