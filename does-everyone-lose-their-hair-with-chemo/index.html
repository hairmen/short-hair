Does Everyone Lose Their Hair With Chemo

Written by Petter Sep 18, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Chemotherapy is a common treatment for cancer. It works by destroying cancer cells in the body. However, chemotherapy can also cause side effects, such as hair loss. In this article, we will explore whether everyone loses their hair with chemo, and what you can do to care for your hair during and after treatment.

How to Prepare for Hair Loss

Not everyone loses their hair with chemo, but it is a common side effect. The amount of hair loss can vary from person to person and depend on the type and dose of chemotherapy. It is essential to prepare for hair loss so that you can feel more in control and less anxious about the process.

One way to prepare for hair loss is to cut your hair short before starting chemotherapy. Short hair is easier to manage and less likely to get tangled. It can also be less traumatic to lose short hair than long hair. If you are not comfortable with short hair, you may want to consider a wig or hairpiece. You can shop for wigs before starting treatment to find one that matches your hair color and style.

Tips for Hair Care During Chemo

If you do lose your hair during chemotherapy, there are several things you can do to care for your scalp and hair.

First, you should wash your hair with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using hot water and harsh chemicals. You may also want to avoid blow-drying and styling your hair with heat tools. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a soft towel to pat it dry.

Second, you should protect your scalp from the sun and cold. Wear a hat or scarf when you go outside, and avoid direct sunlight. You may also want to use a moisturizer or oil to soothe your scalp and prevent dryness.

FAQ

Q: Does everyone lose their hair with chemo?

A: No, not everyone loses their hair with chemo. The amount of hair loss can vary from person to person and depend on the type and dose of chemotherapy.

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemo?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after chemo. However, it may take several months for your hair to grow back fully.

Q: Can I dye my hair during chemo?

A: It is not recommended to dye your hair during chemo because your scalp may be sensitive. Wait until after treatment to dye your hair.

Conclusion

In conclusion, not everyone loses their hair with chemo, but it is a common side effect. It is essential to prepare for hair loss, care for your hair during treatment, and be patient while waiting for your hair to grow back. With the right support and care, you can feel confident and beautiful during and after chemotherapy.
