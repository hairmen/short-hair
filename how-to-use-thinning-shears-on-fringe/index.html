How To Use Thinning Shears On Fringe A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a fringe, you know how it can be a hassle to maintain. Sometimes it gets too long, too thick, or too unruly. That's where thinning shears come in handy. Thinning shears are a great tool to use on your fringe to make it more manageable and easy to style. In this article, we will guide you on how to use thinning shears on fringe in relaxed English language.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth that are designed to remove bulk from the hair without leaving a blunt cut. They are used to create texture and movement in the hair and are commonly used on the ends of the hair to prevent split ends. Using thinning shears on your fringe can thin out the hair, making it less heavy and easier to manage.

How to use thinning shears on fringe

Using thinning shears on your fringe is relatively easy. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step 1: Choose the right thinning shears

There are different types of thinning shears available in the market. Choose one that suits your hair type and the amount of hair you want to remove. If you have thick hair, choose a shear with fewer teeth. If you have thin hair, choose a shear with more teeth.

Step 2: Wash your hair

Before using thinning shears on your fringe, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will ensure that the shears can cut through the hair smoothly.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your fringe according to the amount of hair you want to remove. If you want to remove a lot of hair, make a bigger section. If you want to remove less hair, make a smaller section.

Step 4: Hold the shears correctly

Hold the thinning shears with your dominant hand and hold the hair with your other hand. Make sure the teeth of the shears are facing up and away from your scalp.

Step 5: Cut the hair

Starting at the base of the section, cut the hair in a back-and-forth motion with the thinning shears. Make sure you don't cut too much hair at once, as this can create a choppy look. Repeat this process until you have thinned out the entire section.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Fringe

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on your fringe:

  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid a choppy look.
  • Use the right type of thinning shears for your hair type.
  • Make sure your hair is clean and dry before using thinning shears.
  • Section your hair according to the amount of hair you want to remove.
  • Don't cut too close to the roots, as this can create a hole in your hair.

FAQs

Can thinning shears damage my hair?

Thinning shears, when used correctly, should not damage your hair. However, if you use them too often or cut too much hair at once, you can create split ends and breakage.

How often should I use thinning shears on my fringe?

You should use thinning shears on your fringe only when necessary. Overusing them can make your hair look thin and wispy.

Can I use thinning shears on wet hair?

No, you should not use thinning shears on wet hair. Wet hair is more elastic and can stretch, causing the shears to cut more hair than intended. Always use thinning shears on dry hair.

Hair Care Tips for a Healthy Fringe

To keep your fringe healthy and looking its best, follow these tips:

  • Trim your fringe regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Avoid using hair ties with metal clasps, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using too much product on your fringe, as this can weigh it down.

Now that you know how to use thinning shears on your fringe, you can keep it looking its best. Remember to follow the steps carefully and use the right type of shears for your hair type. With a little practice, you'll be able to thin out your fringe like a pro!
