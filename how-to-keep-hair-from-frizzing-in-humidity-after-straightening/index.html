How To Keep Hair From Frizzing In Humidity After Straightening

If you have ever straightened your hair, you know how frustrating it can be to step outside and have your hair frizz up in the humidity. This is a common problem that many people face, but luckily there are ways to prevent it from happening. In this article, we will discuss how to keep hair from frizzing in humidity after straightening.

How to Keep Hair from Frizzing in Humidity After Straightening

The key to preventing frizz in humidity after straightening is to keep your hair hydrated and protected. Follow these steps to help keep your hair smooth and frizz-free:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

Before straightening your hair, it is important to use a heat protectant. This will help prevent damage to your hair and keep it healthy. Apply the heat protectant to your hair before using any heat styling tools.

2. Use a Good Quality Straightener

Invest in a good quality straightener that has adjustable heat settings. This will allow you to control the temperature and prevent overheating your hair. Make sure to use the lowest temperature setting that works for your hair type.

3. Use a Serum or Oil

After straightening your hair, apply a serum or oil to your hair. This will help to lock in moisture and prevent frizz. Apply a small amount to the ends of your hair and work your way up.

4. Avoid Touching Your Hair

Try to avoid touching your hair as much as possible. The oils from your hands can cause your hair to frizz. If you need to touch your hair, use a soft bristle brush or a comb.

5. Use a Humidity-Blocking Hair Spray

Finish off your hairstyle with a humidity-blocking hair spray. This will help to keep your hair in place and prevent frizz. Look for a hair spray that is specifically designed for humid conditions.

Step by Step

Here is a step by step guide to helping prevent frizz in humidity after straightening:

  1. Apply a heat protectant to your hair before straightening.
  2. Use a good quality straightener with adjustable heat settings.
  3. Apply a serum or oil to your hair after straightening.
  4. Avoid touching your hair as much as possible.
  5. Finish off your hairstyle with a humidity-blocking hair spray.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz in humidity after straightening:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before straightening.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and cause it to look greasy.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Try using a microfiber towel to dry your hair, as this can help prevent frizz.

FAQ

What causes hair to frizz in humidity?

Hair frizzes in humidity because the moisture in the air causes the hair shaft to swell and become frizzy. This is especially true for hair that has been straightened, as it is more susceptible to moisture and humidity.

Can I prevent frizz in humidity after straightening?

Yes, you can prevent frizz in humidity after straightening by using a heat protectant, a good quality straightener, a serum or oil, a humidity-blocking hair spray, and by avoiding touching your hair as much as possible.

How often should I straighten my hair?

It is recommended that you only straighten your hair once a week to prevent damage and breakage. If you must straighten your hair more often, be sure to use a heat protectant and a good quality straightener.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips to help keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using hair ties that are too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

By following these tips, you can help keep your hair healthy and prevent frizz in humidity after straightening.
