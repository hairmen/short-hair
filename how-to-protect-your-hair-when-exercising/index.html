How To Protect Your Hair When Exercising Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Aug 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

As a fitness enthusiast, you know how important it is to exercise regularly for your health and overall well-being. But, did you know that exercising can also take a toll on your hair? Sweat, friction, and heat can all damage your hair, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. In this post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to protect your hair when exercising, so you can achieve your fitness goals without sacrificing your hair.

How to Protect Your Hair When Exercising

Before we dive into the tips and tricks, it's important to understand why exercise can damage your hair. When you workout, your body temperature rises, causing you to sweat. Sweat can make your hair wet and sticky, leading to breakage and tangling. Additionally, the friction caused by your hair rubbing against your clothes or gym equipment can also damage your hair. Finally, the heat from styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons can further dry out your hair, making it more prone to breakage.

To prevent this damage, it's important to take steps to protect your hair before, during, and after your workout.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to protect your hair when exercising:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you hit the gym, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help prevent sweat from sticking to your hair and causing damage.

Step 2: Use Protective Styles

Protective styles like braids, buns, and ponytails can help keep your hair out of your face and prevent it from rubbing against your clothes or gym equipment.

Step 3: Wear a Headband

A headband can help keep your hair in place and prevent sweat from getting into your eyes. Look for a headband made from moisture-wicking material to help keep your hair dry.

Step 4: Use a Sweatband

A sweatband can help absorb sweat and prevent it from dripping down onto your hair. Look for a sweatband made from a soft, absorbent material like cotton.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair from Heat

If you're planning on using styling tools like blow dryers or flat irons after your workout, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Step 6: Rinse Your Hair

After your workout, rinse your hair with water to remove sweat and any product buildup.

Step 7: Dry Your Hair

Finally, dry your hair gently with a towel or a microfiber hair turban. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause breakage.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when protecting your hair during exercise:

  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help prevent dryness and breakage.
  • If you have long hair, consider using a hair tie that won't leave a crease in your hair.
  • Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and lead to dryness.
  • If you're swimming, wear a swim cap to protect your hair from chlorine.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about protecting your hair during exercise:

Q: Can I use dry shampoo instead of washing my hair after a workout?

A: While dry shampoo can be a quick fix, it's not a substitute for washing your hair. Sweat and product buildup can clog your hair follicles, leading to breakage and hair loss.

Q: Should I wear a hat when exercising outdoors?

A: Wearing a hat can help protect your hair from the sun's damaging rays, but it can also trap sweat and moisture against your scalp, leading to breakouts and irritation. If you do wear a hat, look for one made from breathable material like cotton.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I exercise regularly?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much you sweat. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently. However, if you have dry hair, washing it too often can strip it of its natural oils and lead to dryness and breakage.

Hair Care

In addition to protecting your hair during exercise, it's important to take care of your hair on a daily basis. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to prevent breakage when brushing your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, as this can help promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and tricks, you can protect your hair while still achieving your fitness goals. Remember, healthy hair starts from the inside out, so take care of your body and your hair will thank you!
