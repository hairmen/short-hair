How To Cut Your Own Hair At Home Diy Layered Haircut Tutorial

Written by Aprilia Jun 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

In these times of social distancing, going to a salon for a haircut may not be the best idea. However, that doesn't mean you have to live with shaggy and unruly hair. Cutting your own hair may seem daunting, but with a bit of practice and patience, you can give yourself a salon-worthy haircut at home. In this blog post, we will guide you through a step-by-step tutorial on how to cut your own hair at home using the DIY layered haircut technique.

How to Cut Your Own Hair at Home

Before you begin, it's essential to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle with water.

You can follow these steps to cut your hair at home:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's entirely dry before you begin cutting.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using the comb and hair clips. Start by parting your hair in the middle and dividing it into two sections. Then, create two more sections on each side of your head, and secure them with hair clips.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this step for all the sections at the back of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

After you've cut the bottom layer, it's time to move on to the middle layer. Unclip the sections on the sides of your head and comb them straight down. Cut them at an angle, starting from the back and moving towards the front.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, it's time to cut the top layer. Unclip the front sections of your hair and comb them straight down. Cut them at an angle, starting from the back and moving towards the front.

Tips for a Perfect DIY Layered Haircut

Here are some tips to help you achieve a salon-worthy haircut at home:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. A sharp pair of scissors will make the cutting process much easier and ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time. Cutting your hair can be nerve-wracking, but rushing can lead to mistakes. Take breaks if you need to and come back to it later.
  • Start with small cuts. You can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use a mirror. It's essential to have a clear view of what you're doing, so use a mirror to guide you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair at home using the DIY layered haircut technique. However, it's important to cut your hair when it's dry as curly hair tends to look shorter when it's wet.

How often should I cut my hair at home?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair growth.

Can I fix mistakes if I make any?

Yes, you can fix mistakes by trimming off the excess hair or adjusting the length. However, it's best to take your time and avoid making mistakes in the first place.

Hair Care after a DIY Layered Haircut

After cutting your hair at home, it's essential to take care of it to maintain healthy hair growth. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair

With these tips and our DIY layered haircut tutorial, you can give yourself a salon-worthy haircut at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and invest in a good pair of scissors. Happy cutting!
