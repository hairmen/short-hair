How To Get Rid Of Eczema In The Scalp

Written by Bardi Aug 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is a common skin condition that affects people of all ages. It can cause red, itchy, and scaly patches on the skin, including the scalp. Eczema in the scalp can be particularly uncomfortable and embarrassing, as it can lead to dandruff-like flakes and hair loss. Fortunately, there are several ways to get rid of eczema in the scalp. In this blog post, we will discuss how to treat eczema in the scalp effectively.

Description of Eczema in the Scalp

Eczema in the scalp is a type of atopic dermatitis that affects the scalp area. It causes red, itchy, and scaly patches on the scalp, which can be uncomfortable and embarrassing. Eczema in the scalp can also lead to hair loss, as the constant scratching and irritation can damage the hair follicles. It is essential to treat eczema in the scalp promptly to prevent further complications.

How to Get Rid of Eczema in the Scalp

Step 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

The first step in treating eczema in the scalp is to use a gentle shampoo. Look for a shampoo that is free from harsh chemicals and fragrances. Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out the scalp and exacerbate eczema symptoms. Instead, use lukewarm water to wash your hair.

Step 2: Apply an Over-the-Counter Cream

Over-the-counter creams, such as hydrocortisone cream, can be effective in treating eczema in the scalp. Apply the cream to the affected area twice a day, or as directed by your doctor. Hydrocortisone cream can help reduce inflammation and itching in the scalp.

Step 3: Use a Scalp Oil

Scalp oils, such as tea tree oil, can help soothe eczema in the scalp. Apply a few drops of scalp oil to the affected area and massage gently. Leave the oil in the scalp for a few hours before washing it off with a gentle shampoo. Scalp oils can help reduce inflammation and itchiness in the scalp.

Step 4: Avoid Triggers

Eczema in the scalp can be triggered by certain factors, such as stress, dry air, and allergens. Avoiding these triggers can help prevent eczema flare-ups. Try to manage your stress levels, use a humidifier in dry environments, and avoid allergens such as pollen and dust.

Tips for Treating Eczema in the Scalp

Here are some additional tips for treating eczema in the scalp:

  • Avoid scratching your scalp, as it can damage the hair follicles and lead to hair loss.
  • Wear a hat or head covering to protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather.
  • Avoid using hair styling products that contain harsh chemicals, such as hair spray and gel.
  • Consult your doctor if your eczema symptoms do not improve or worsen.

FAQ about Eczema in the Scalp

What Causes Eczema in the Scalp?

Eczema in the scalp can be caused by several factors, including stress, dry air, allergens, and genetics. People with a family history of eczema are more likely to develop eczema in the scalp.

Can Eczema in the Scalp Cause Hair Loss?

Yes, eczema in the scalp can cause hair loss. Constant scratching and irritation can damage the hair follicles, leading to hair loss.

Is Eczema in the Scalp Contagious?

No, eczema in the scalp is not contagious. It is a non-infectious skin condition that cannot be passed from one person to another.

Hair Care for Eczema in the Scalp

People with eczema in the scalp should take extra care when washing and styling their hair. Here are some hair care tips for people with eczema in the scalp:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and avoid hot water.
  • Avoid using hair styling products that contain harsh chemicals.
  • Wear a hat or head covering to protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather.
  • Avoid scratching your scalp, as it can lead to hair loss.
  • Consult your doctor if your eczema symptoms do not improve or worsen.

By following these tips and treatment options, you can effectively get rid of eczema in the scalp and prevent further complications. If your symptoms persist, consult your doctor for further guidance.
