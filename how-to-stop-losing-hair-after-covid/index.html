How To Stop Losing Hair After Covid A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

2020 and 2021 have been tough years for everyone, and one of the side effects of COVID-19 that has been reported widely is hair loss. If you are experiencing hair loss after having COVID-19, you are not alone. This guide will help you understand why hair loss after COVID happens, how to prevent it, and how to manage it if it already happened.

Why Does Hair Loss Happen After COVID?

Hair loss after COVID is a result of a condition called telogen effluvium. Telogen effluvium is a type of hair loss that happens after a stressful event, such as a major illness or surgery. The stress of COVID-19 can push hair follicles into a resting phase, which leads to hair shedding. This type of hair loss usually happens two to three months after the stressful event.

Other factors that can contribute to hair loss after COVID include:

  • High fever during COVID
  • Changes in diet and nutrition
  • Changes in medication
  • Changes in hormones

It's important to note that hair loss after COVID is usually temporary and hair will grow back within a few months. However, in some cases, the hair loss can be more severe and require medical intervention.

Preventing Hair Loss After COVID

While hair loss after COVID is usually temporary, there are some things you can do to prevent it from happening or minimize its impact:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Get enough sleep and manage stress levels
  • Avoid harsh hair treatments and styles that pull on the hair
  • Consult your doctor before making any changes to your medication

It's also important to seek medical advice if you experience severe hair loss or if your hair loss is accompanied by other symptoms.

Managing Hair Loss After COVID

If you are already experiencing hair loss after COVID, there are some treatments that can help:

  • Topical minoxidil: This is a medication that can help promote hair growth. It is applied to the scalp twice a day.
  • Platelet-rich plasma (PRP): This is a treatment that involves injecting your own blood plasma into your scalp to promote hair growth.
  • Hair transplant: If you have severe hair loss, a hair transplant may be an option. This involves transplanting hair follicles from one part of your scalp to another.

It's important to consult your doctor before starting any treatment for hair loss after COVID.

Hair Care Tips After COVID

To help your hair recover after COVID, try these tips:

  • Avoid heat styling tools and harsh chemical treatments
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that pull on your hair

Remember, your hair will grow back after COVID, but it may take some time. Be patient and take good care of your hair in the meantime.

FAQs About Hair Loss After COVID

Q: How long does hair loss after COVID last?

A: Hair loss after COVID is usually temporary and lasts for a few months. However, in some cases, it can last longer.

Q: Is hair loss after COVID permanent?

A: Hair loss after COVID is usually temporary, but in some cases, it can be permanent.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss. Telogen effluvium, the type of hair loss that happens after a stressful event, is a common condition.

Q: Is there a cure for hair loss after COVID?

A: There is no cure for hair loss after COVID, but there are treatments that can help promote hair growth.

Q: Will my hair grow back after COVID?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after COVID, but it may take some time.

By following the tips and treatments outlined in this guide, you can manage hair loss after COVID and promote healthy hair growth.
