How To Soften Natural Hair With Relaxer

Written by Bardi Jul 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with coarse and unmanageable natural hair? Relaxer might be the solution you're looking for. In this article, we will guide you through the process of softening your natural hair with relaxer. From how to apply it, to tips on maintaining your newly softened hair, we’ve got you covered.

How to Soften Natural Hair with Relaxer

To soften your natural hair with relaxer, you will need to follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before applying the relaxer, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products. Divide your hair into sections and detangle it with a wide-tooth comb. Protect your scalp and hairline by applying petroleum jelly or a protective cream.

Step 2: Mix and Apply the Relaxer

Follow the instructions on your relaxer kit to mix the relaxer. Starting from the back of your head, apply the relaxer to your hair using a brush. Make sure to avoid applying the relaxer to your scalp. Leave the relaxer on for the recommended time, as stated in the instructions.

Step 3: Rinse and Neutralize

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water to remove the relaxer. Apply a neutralizing shampoo to your hair and massage it gently. Rinse your hair again with warm water and then with cold water to close the cuticles.

Step 4: Condition Your Hair

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for 15-20 minutes. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cold water to lock in moisture.

Tips for Maintaining Softened Hair

Now that you have softened your natural hair with relaxer, it’s important to maintain its softness. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and soft. Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Tip 2: Deep Condition Your Hair Regularly

Deep condition your hair at least once a week to keep it nourished and soft. You can use a store-bought deep conditioner or make your own using natural ingredients like avocado, coconut oil, and honey.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair at Night

Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent your hair from drying out and breaking while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use relaxer on colored hair?

Yes, you can use relaxer on colored hair. However, it’s important to wait at least two weeks after coloring your hair before applying relaxer.

How often should I apply relaxer to my hair?

You should wait at least 8-10 weeks between relaxer applications to prevent over-processing and damage to your hair.

Can I straighten my hair after applying relaxer?

Yes, you can straighten your hair after applying relaxer. However, it’s important to use a heat protectant and avoid excessive heat to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care for Softened Hair

Softened hair requires special care to maintain its softness and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Avoid Over-Styling

Avoid excessive styling and heat to prevent damage to your hair. Opt for protective styles like braids and buns that require minimal manipulation.

Tip 2: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and prevent breakage. Aim to trim your hair every 8-12 weeks.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun’s harmful rays. Exposure to the sun can dry out and damage your hair.

By following these tips and techniques, you can soften your natural hair with relaxer and maintain its softness and health. Happy styling!
