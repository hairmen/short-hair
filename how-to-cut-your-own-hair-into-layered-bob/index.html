How To Cut Your Own Hair Into Layered Bob

Written by Daniel Nov 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without breaking the bank, then cutting your own hair into a layered bob may be just the thing for you. In this article, we'll be giving you a step-by-step guide on how to achieve this look from the comfort of your own home. From the necessary tools to helpful tips and FAQs, we've got you covered. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • FAQs

Tools You'll Need

Before diving in, make sure you have the necessary tools to achieve this look. Here are the essentials:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Sectioning clips (optional)

How to Cut Your Own Hair into Layered Bob

First things first, decide on the length and layers you want for your bob. Do some research and gather inspiration from various sources such as magazines, Pinterest, and Instagram. Once you have an idea in mind, it's time to start cutting.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with dry, clean hair. This will make it easier to see the shape and length you want to achieve.
  2. Part your hair down the center and comb it straight down.
  3. Clip the top part of your hair up and out of the way.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down.
  5. Hold the section between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across. This will be your guide for the rest of the haircut.
  6. Take another small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down. This time, hold it between your fingers and cut it at a slight angle towards the guide you just created. This will create the first layer.
  7. Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting them at a slight angle towards the guide. Make sure to keep the sections even in size and angle.
  8. Once you have completed one side, repeat the process on the other side.
  9. Unclip the top section of your hair and repeat the same process, but this time cut the hair at a slightly longer length than the bottom layer.
  10. Blend the layers by taking a comb and running it through your hair to check for any uneven sections. Trim any stray hairs as needed.

Helpful Tips

  • It's always better to start with a longer length and work your way shorter. You can't add hair back once it's been cut!
  • Take breaks and step back to check your progress. It's easy to get carried away and cut too much off.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, have a friend or family member help you.
  • Invest in quality scissors. Dull scissors will make it harder to achieve a clean cut and can damage your hair.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair wet?

While it's possible to cut your hair wet, it's not recommended. Wet hair tends to look longer than it actually is, which can result in cutting off more than intended.

What if I mess up?

Don't panic! Hair grows back, and there are always ways to fix a bad cut. If it's a minor mistake, try blending it in with the rest of your hair. If it's a major mistake, seek the help of a professional stylist.

Do I need to use clippers?

Clippers are optional and can be used to create a more dramatic or textured look. If you're not comfortable using clippers, stick to scissors.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your bob.

Cutting your own hair into a layered bob may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and technique, it's definitely achievable. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun with it! Who knows, you may just discover your new signature look.
