How To Cut Short Spiky Hair A Step By Step Guide From Youtube

If you're looking for a fresh and edgy haircut, short spiky hair might be the right choice for you. This hairstyle is versatile and can suit different face shapes and hair types, from straight to curly or wavy. However, cutting short spiky hair can be challenging, especially if you're doing it for the first time. Fortunately, YouTube is a great resource for learning how to cut short spiky hair like a pro. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut short spiky hair, as well as some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Cut Short Spiky Hair

Cutting short spiky hair requires some skills and techniques to achieve the desired look. While it's best to go to a professional hairdresser if you're unsure about how to do it, you can also try cutting your hair at home with the help of YouTube tutorials. Here are the basic steps on how to cut short spiky hair:

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools and Materials

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to prepare some tools and materials. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, a spray bottle with water, and a mirror. You may also want to use a hair trimmer or clippers to create shorter layers.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to start with clean and dry hair so that you can see the natural texture and shape of your hair. Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, then towel-dry it gently. Don't blow-dry your hair as it can make it frizzy and harder to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into small sections, starting from the back of your head. Clip each section with a hair clip to keep it in place while you're cutting the other sections. Leave the top section of your hair for the last step.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layers

Starting from the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut the hair to the desired length. Repeat this step for the other sections of hair, making sure that the layers are even on both sides.

Step 5: Create the Top Layers

Once you've cut the bottom layers, release the top section of your hair. Comb it straight up and hold it with your fingers. Use the scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time, starting from the front and working your way to the back. Make sure that the layers are shorter on top and longer on the sides.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you've cut your hair, you can style it to create the spiky look. Apply some hair wax or gel on your fingertips and run them through your hair. Use your fingers to create spikes or messy textures, depending on your preference. You may also want to use a blow-dryer to set the style.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed guide on how to cut short spiky hair:

Step 1: Choose the Right Haircut

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to decide on the right haircut for your face shape and hair type. Short spiky hair can come in different variations, from a classic crew cut to a textured choppy style. Look for inspiration on YouTube or social media platforms and choose a hairstyle that suits your personality and lifestyle.

Step 2: Gather Your Tools

You'll need some essential tools to cut short spiky hair, such as sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, a spray bottle with water, and a mirror. You may also want to use a hair trimmer or clippers to create shorter layers or to trim your sideburns and neckline.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, wash it with shampoo and conditioner to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. Towel-dry your hair gently and let it air-dry for a few minutes. Don't blow-dry your hair as it can make it frizzy and harder to cut.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Using a comb or your fingers, section your hair into small sections, starting from the back of your head. Clip each section with a hair clip or a butterfly clip to keep it in place while you're cutting the other sections. Leave the top section of your hair for the last step.

Step 5: Cut the Bottom Layers

Starting from the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut the hair to the desired length. Repeat this step for the other sections of hair, making sure that the layers are even on both sides. Use the comb to check if the layers are straight and consistent.

Step 6: Create the Top Layers

Once you've cut the bottom layers, release the top section of your hair. Comb it straight up and hold it with your fingers. Use the scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time, starting from the front and working your way to the back. Make sure that the layers are shorter on top and longer on the sides. Use the comb to check if the layers are blended and textured.

Step 7: Texture and Detail

If you want to create a more textured and edgy look, you can use texturizing shears or razors to thin out some sections of hair. You can also use the scissors to create some choppy or uneven layers around your crown or temples. Don't overdo it, though, as too much texture can make your hair look messy and unkempt.

Step 8: Trim Your Sideburns and Neckline

To complete your short spiky haircut, you'll need to trim your sideburns and neckline. Use a trimmer or clippers to create a clean and sharp line along your sideburns and around your ears. You can also trim your neckline to create a tapered or squared shape, depending on your preference.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Once you've cut and trimmed your hair, you can style it to create the spiky look. Apply some hair wax, pomade, or gel on your fingertips and run them through your hair. Use your fingers to create spikes or messy textures, depending on your preference. You can also use a comb or a brush to create a sleeker or more defined look. Set your style with some hairspray to make it last longer.

Tips for Cutting Short Spiky Hair

Here are some tips that can help you cut short spiky hair like a pro:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid splitting or damaging your hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair and cut less than you think you need.
  • Use the comb as a guide to create even layers and avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Don't rush and take your time to create the desired shape and texture.
  • Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find the right look for you.
  • Ask for feedback from a friend or a family member to make sure that your haircut looks good from all angles.
  • If you're not comfortable cutting your hair at home, go to a professional hairdresser for a consultation and a haircut.

FAQs

Q: Is short spiky hair suitable for all face shapes?

A: Short spiky hair can suit different face shapes, from round to square or oval. However, you'll need to choose the right haircut and styling technique to balance your facial features and create a harmonious look.

Q: How often should I cut my short spiky hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your hairstyle. On average, you may need to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it in shape and prevent split ends or breakage.
