How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the back of your head. However, with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut the back of your hair with clippers in easy-to-follow steps.

Description

Cutting the back of your hair with clippers is a simple and cost-effective way to maintain your hairstyle. With a bit of practice and patience, you can achieve a clean and even cut without having to visit a barber or hairdresser. All you need is a pair of clippers, a mirror, and some basic knowledge of hair cutting techniques.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

When it comes to cutting your hair at home, the right tools are essential. Invest in a good quality pair of clippers with an adjustable blade, so you can control the length of your hair. Look for clippers with a long cord, so you can move around more easily while cutting your hair.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel drying it until it's slightly damp. Comb your hair straight back and use hair clips to keep the top and sides of your hair out of the way, so you can focus on the back.

Step 3: Choose the Right Guard Size

Attach the desired guard size to your clippers. If you want a shorter cut, choose a smaller guard size. For a longer cut, choose a larger guard size. Make sure the guard is securely attached to the clippers before you start cutting.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the hair at the nape of your neck. Hold the clippers at a 45-degree angle and move them upward, against the direction of hair growth. Use short, even strokes to create an even cut. Repeat this process until you've cut all the hair at the back of your head.

Step 5: Blend the Sides

Once you've finished cutting the back of your hair, remove the guard from your clippers and use the bare blade to blend the sides of your hair into the back. Hold the clippers at a 90-degree angle and move them in a circular motion to create a smooth transition between the sides and the back.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Use a mirror to check the back of your head for any uneven spots or missed areas. Make any necessary adjustments with the clippers until you achieve the desired length and shape.

Tips for Cutting the Back of Your Hair with Clippers

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a comb to help guide the clippers and keep the hair in place.
  • Take breaks if you need to, to avoid fatigue and mistakes.

FAQs

Can I cut the back of my hair with scissors instead of clippers?

While it's possible to cut the back of your hair with scissors, clippers are generally easier to use and provide a more even cut. If you're new to cutting your own hair, we recommend using clippers.

How often should I cut the back of my hair?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. As a general rule, we recommend cutting your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your hairstyle.

Hair Care After Cutting

Once you've finished cutting the back of your hair with clippers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemical treatments, as these can damage your hair over time.

Cutting the back of your hair with clippers can be a simple and rewarding experience, once you get the hang of it. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without having to leave your house. Follow these steps, and you'll be well on your way to becoming your own personal hairstylist.
