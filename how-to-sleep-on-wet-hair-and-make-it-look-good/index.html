How To Sleep On Wet Hair And Make It Look Good

Written by Christine Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who prefers to shower at night, you've probably experienced the struggle of trying to style wet hair before bed. It's not always the easiest task, but it's possible to sleep on wet hair and still wake up with a great hairstyle. In this blog post, we're going to share some tips on how to sleep on wet hair and make it look good the next day.

  • How to Sleep on Wet Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Sleep on Wet Hair

Sleeping on wet hair can actually have some benefits for your hair, such as increasing moisture and reducing frizz. However, it can also lead to breakage and damage if not done correctly. Here are some steps to follow when sleeping on wet hair:

1. Start with clean hair

Make sure to wash your hair and remove any excess water before going to bed. This will prevent your hair from getting too tangled or frizzy while you sleep.

2. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil

Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your damp hair to help keep it moisturized and reduce frizz. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy in the morning.

3. Avoid tight hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles such as ponytails or braids, as these can cause breakage and damage to your wet hair. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles like a loose bun or a low ponytail.

4. Use a silk or satin pillowcase

Silk or satin pillowcases are gentler on your hair than cotton pillowcases, which can cause frizz and breakage. These fabrics also help to reduce moisture loss from your hair while you sleep.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of sleeping on wet hair, here's a step-by-step guide to follow:

1. Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Remove excess water by gently squeezing your hair with a towel.

2. Apply leave-in conditioner or hair oil

Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your damp hair, focusing on the ends. Comb through your hair to distribute the product evenly.

3. Style your hair

Gently style your hair into a loose bun or low ponytail. Avoid using any elastic bands or hair ties that could cause breakage.

4. Use a silk or satin pillowcase

Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and moisture loss from your hair while you sleep.

5. Sleep on your back

Try to sleep on your back to avoid crushing your hair while you sleep. If you can't sleep on your back, try to avoid sleeping on your wet hair.

6. Take out your hair tie in the morning

In the morning, take out your hair tie and gently comb through your hair with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb. You should now have beautiful, relaxed waves or curls.

Tips for Sleeping on Wet Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when sleeping on wet hair:

  • Don't use too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy in the morning.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners on wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • If you have long hair, try braiding it to prevent tangling while you sleep.
  • Invest in a good quality silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and moisture loss from your hair.

FAQs

Can I sleep on wet hair every night?

While it's okay to sleep on wet hair occasionally, it's not recommended to do so every night. Sleeping on wet hair can cause breakage and damage over time, so it's best to let your hair air dry before going to bed.

How can I speed up the drying process?

If you're short on time, you can use a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment to speed up the drying process. However, make sure to use low heat and avoid using hot tools on wet hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips, you can sleep on wet hair and wake up with beautiful, relaxed waves or curls. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid tight hairstyles that can cause breakage and damage. Happy sleeping!
