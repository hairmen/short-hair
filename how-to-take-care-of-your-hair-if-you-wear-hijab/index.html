How To Take Care Of Your Hair If You Wear Hijab

Written by Christine Oct 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a hijabi, you may have noticed that your hair needs extra care due to the constant covering. Wearing hijab can make your hair dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. However, with a few simple steps, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful. In this article, we will discuss how to take care of your hair if you wear hijab.

Table of Content

  1. How to Take Care of Your Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Healthy Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Products

How to Take Care of Your Hair

The first step in taking care of your hair if you wear hijab is to keep it clean. It is essential to wash your hair regularly to remove any dirt, sweat, or oils that may accumulate underneath your hijab. However, be careful not to over-wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage.

Another crucial step is to keep your hair moisturized. Wearing hijab can cause your hair to dry out quickly, so it is essential to use a good quality conditioner to keep it hydrated. You can also use natural oils, such as coconut oil or argan oil, to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

It is also important to avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hairline or cause tension on your scalp. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles that allow your hair to breathe and prevent breakage. You can try braids, twists, or buns, as these styles are gentle on your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to take care of your hair if you wear hijab:

  1. Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  2. Apply a good quality conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  3. Avoid using hot tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  4. Use natural oils, such as coconut oil or argan oil, to nourish and protect your hair.
  5. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hairline or cause tension on your scalp.
  6. Opt for loose hairstyles that allow your hair to breathe and prevent breakage.
  7. Use a satin or silk scarf to cover your hair under your hijab. This will help to prevent breakage and frizz.
  8. Avoid wearing your hijab too tightly, as this can cause tension on your scalp and lead to hair loss.
  9. Take breaks from wearing hijab to allow your hair to breathe and get some fresh air.
  10. Visit a hair stylist regularly to trim your hair and keep it healthy.

Tips for Healthy Hair

Here are some additional tips for healthy hair:

  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair health.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and relaxers, as they can damage your hair.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf when you are outside.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It is recommended to wash your hair at least once a week to keep it clean and healthy.

Q: Can I wear my hijab to bed?

A: It is not recommended to wear your hijab to bed, as it can cause tension on your scalp and lead to hair loss.

Q: Should I use a hairbrush or a comb?

A: It is recommended to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. Avoid using a hairbrush, as it can cause breakage.

Hair Care Products

Here are some hair care products that are suitable for hijabis:

  • A sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair clean without stripping it of its natural oils.
  • A moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • A natural oil, such as coconut oil or argan oil, to nourish and protect your hair.
  • A satin or silk scarf to cover your hair under your hijab and prevent breakage.

By following these simple steps and tips, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful, even when wearing hijab. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid tight hairstyles, and keep it moisturized and protected.
