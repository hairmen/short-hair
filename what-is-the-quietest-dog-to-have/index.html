What Is The Quietest Dog To Have

Written by Bardi Sep 06, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you living in an apartment or a small house and looking for a furry companion that won't disturb your neighbors? Or maybe you simply prefer a calm and peaceful environment? Whatever your reason, finding the quietest dog to have is essential. This article will guide you through the best dog breeds that fit the bill.

Top 5 Quietest Dog Breeds

Before we dive into the breeds, let's clarify that every dog is unique, and their personality and behavior are shaped by various factors, including genetics, training, and environment. However, some breeds are generally known for their calm and quiet nature:

1. Basenji

The Basenji is a small African hunting dog that doesn't bark but makes a yodel-like sound. They are independent and reserved but affectionate with their owners. Basenjis are also clean and low-shedding, making them an ideal choice for people with allergies.

2. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

This charming breed is a favorite among many due to their friendly and gentle nature. Cavaliers are not known for excessive barking and adapt well to apartment living. They enjoy human company and love to cuddle.

3. Greyhound

Despite their reputation as racing dogs, Greyhounds are laid-back and calm in the house. They are sensitive and affectionate with their families and don't require much exercise. Greyhounds also have short coats that are easy to maintain.

4. Newfoundland

Newfoundlands are massive dogs that are famous for their water rescue abilities. However, they are also known for their sweet and docile temperament. Newfs are quiet and don't bark much, but they do drool and shed a lot. They are best suited for people with large homes and yards.

5. Scottish Deerhound

The Scottish Deerhound is a tall and regal breed that is quiet and reserved. They are gentle and loyal with their families and get along with other pets. Deerhounds require moderate exercise and grooming.

How to Choose the Right Quiet Dog for You

When considering a quiet dog, you should keep in mind your lifestyle, living space, and personal preferences. Here are some factors to consider:

  • Size: Do you have enough space for a large dog, or would a smaller breed fit better?
  • Temperament: Do you prefer a dog that is more independent or one that loves to cuddle?
  • Exercise Needs: Can you commit to daily walks and playtime, or do you need a low-energy dog?
  • Grooming: Are you willing to groom your dog regularly, or do you prefer a low-maintenance coat?
  • Training: Are you willing to invest time and effort in training your dog to be quiet?

Step-by-Step Guide to Train Your Dog to Be Quiet

Training your dog to be quiet requires patience, consistency, and positive reinforcement. Here are the steps to follow:

  1. Teach the "Quiet" Command: Use a treat to get your dog's attention and say "quiet" in a calm but firm voice. Wait for a moment of silence and reward your dog.
  2. Practice: Repeat the command and reward until your dog understands what is expected of them.
  3. Ignore Barking: If your dog barks for attention, ignore them until they are quiet. Reward them when they stop barking.
  4. Be Consistent: Use the command every time your dog barks and reward them when they stop. Don't yell or punish your dog.
  5. Practice in Different Environments: Practice the command in different situations, such as when someone rings the doorbell or when you're out for a walk.

Tips for Raising a Quiet Dog

Here are some tips to help you raise a quiet and well-behaved dog:

  • Provide Enough Exercise: Make sure your dog gets enough physical and mental stimulation to prevent boredom and anxiety.
  • Be Patient: Don't expect your dog to be quiet overnight. Consistency and positive reinforcement are key.
  • Don't Reinforce Barking: Avoid rewarding your dog for barking or giving them attention when they demand it.
  • Use White Noise: Playing white noise or calming music can help soothe your dog and reduce barking.
  • Seek Professional Help: If your dog's barking is excessive or disruptive, consult a professional trainer or behaviorist.

FAQs

How much do quiet dogs bark?

Quiet dogs bark occasionally and usually for a reason, such as to alert their owners of danger or to express excitement. However, they don't bark excessively or without cause.

Can you train any dog to be quiet?

While some breeds are naturally quieter than others, any dog can learn to be quiet with proper training and socialization.

Do quiet dogs make good apartment pets?

Yes, many quiet breeds, such as Cavaliers and Basenjis, are well-suited for apartment living as long as they get enough exercise and mental stimulation.

Hair Care for Quiet Dogs

Even though quiet dogs don't bark much, they still require regular grooming to keep their coat healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Brush Your Dog: Brush your dog's coat regularly to remove loose hair and prevent matting.
  • Bathe Your Dog: Bathe your dog when needed, but not too frequently, to avoid drying out their skin.
  • Trim Nails: Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent overgrowth and discomfort.
  • Clean Ears: Clean your dog's ears with a damp cloth to prevent infections.
  • Visit the Vet: Take your dog to the vet for regular check-ups and vaccinations.

In conclusion, choosing a quiet dog requires careful consideration of various factors, including breed, size, temperament, and grooming needs. With proper training and care, a quiet dog can be a loyal and loving companion for years to come.
