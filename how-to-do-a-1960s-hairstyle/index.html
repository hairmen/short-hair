How To Do A 1960S Hairstyle Relaxed English Language Tutorial

If you're looking to channel the retro glamour of the 1960s, a classic hairstyle from the era is a great place to start. The 1960s was a decade of bold and iconic hairstyles that still influence modern trends today. From the bouffant to the beehive, these hairstyles exude confidence and sophistication.

How to Do a 1960s Hairstyle

Before we get into the specifics of how to create a 1960s hairstyle, it's important to note that there were many different styles popular during this decade. That being said, there are a few key elements that most 1960s hairstyles shared, such as volume, texture, and height.

If you want to create a 1960s hairstyle, you'll need to start by selecting the right style for your hair type and length. Some popular options include:

  • Beehive
  • Bouffant
  • Flip
  • Mod Bob
  • Wavy Pixie

Step-by-Step Guide

Once you've selected your desired 1960s hairstyle, follow these steps to achieve the look:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair.
  2. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots to add texture and lift.
  3. Part your hair as desired. For a bouffant or beehive, a center part works well.
  4. Tease the crown of your hair to add height.
  5. Smooth out the teased section and pin it in place.
  6. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to create loose waves throughout your hair.
  7. Backcomb the ends of your hair to create a flipped style, if desired.
  8. Finish the look with hairspray to hold everything in place.

Tips for Perfecting Your 1960s Hairstyle

Creating a 1960s hairstyle can be a bit tricky, but these tips can help you get the perfect look:

  • Use a fine-toothed comb to tease your hair gently. Too much teasing can damage your hair.
  • When backcombing, start at the ends of your hair and work your way up. This will create a smoother finish.
  • For a bouffant or beehive, use bobby pins to secure the teased section in place.
  • Use hot rollers instead of a curling iron for a softer, more natural look.

FAQs

Q: Can I create a 1960s hairstyle with short hair?

A: Yes, there are several 1960s hairstyles that work well with short hair, such as the wavy pixie or mod bob.

Q: How do I prevent my hair from getting too tangled when teasing?

A: Use a detangling spray or conditioner before teasing to make the process easier.

Hair Care Tips for 1960s Hairstyles

To keep your 1960s hairstyle looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture to your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your hairstyle.

With these tips and a little bit of practice, you can create a stunning 1960s hairstyle that will turn heads and make you feel like a retro queen.
