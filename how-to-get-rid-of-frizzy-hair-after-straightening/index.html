How To Get Rid Of Frizzy Hair After Straightening A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of straightening your hair only to end up with frizzy locks shortly after? Don't worry, you're not alone. Frizz is a common problem, but luckily, there are ways to combat it. In this article, we'll go over how to get rid of frizzy hair after straightening, step by step. We'll also provide some tips on how to prevent frizz in the first place, and how to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny. Let's get started!

  • How to Get Rid of Frizzy Hair After Straightening
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Frizz
  • FAQs

How to Get Rid of Frizzy Hair After Straightening

The key to getting rid of frizzy hair after straightening is to add moisture back into the hair. Straightening can strip the hair of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and brittle. When the hair is dry, it's more prone to frizz. Here are some ways to add moisture back into your hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Start with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter. These ingredients will help to hydrate the hair and prevent frizz. Be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly after using conditioner to avoid any buildup.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair. This will provide extra moisture and help to detangle your hair. Be sure to apply the conditioner evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Use a Heat Protectant Spray

Before straightening your hair, apply a heat protectant spray. This will help to protect your hair from heat damage and prevent frizz. Spray the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

4. Use a Hair Serum

After straightening your hair, apply a hair serum to the ends. Serums contain oils that help to hydrate the hair and prevent frizz. Be sure to use a small amount and avoid applying it to the roots, as this can cause your hair to become greasy.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of frizzy hair after straightening:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly and towel dry your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair. Be sure to apply it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush. This will help to smooth out your hair and prevent frizz. Be sure to dry your hair completely before straightening it.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair

Straighten your hair using a flat iron. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before straightening, and avoid going over the same section of hair multiple times.

Step 5: Apply a Hair Serum

After straightening your hair, apply a hair serum to the ends. This will help to hydrate your hair and prevent frizz.

Tips to Prevent Frizz

Here are some tips to prevent frizz in the first place:

1. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. This will help to prevent breakage and frizz.

2. Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and prone to frizz. Try to wash your hair every other day, or even every three days if possible.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz. Silk is a smooth fabric that won't rough up your hair like cotton can.

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it's already dry and frizzy?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair if it's dry and frizzy. Just be sure to follow the steps outlined above to add moisture back into your hair before and after straightening.

Q: How often should I use a hair serum?

A: It's best to use a hair serum once or twice a week. Using it too often can cause your hair to become greasy.

Hair Care for Healthy and Shiny Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny:

1. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Getting regular trims can help to prevent split ends, which can make your hair look dull and frizzy.

2. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help to keep your hair hydrated and shiny.

3. Avoid Heat Styling Too Often

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Try to limit your use of hot tools and use them on a low heat setting when possible.

By following these tips and steps, you can say goodbye to frizzy hair after straightening and hello to healthy, shiny locks. Remember to take care of your hair, and it will thank you for it!
