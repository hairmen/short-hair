Is Taper Fade Haram Exploring The Debate On Islamic Haircuts

Written by Oliver Jul 04, 2023 · 4 min read

As the world becomes more interconnected, cultural practices are often shared and exchanged. One such practice is the taper fade haircut, which has become increasingly popular among men of all ethnicities. However, for some Muslims, the question arises: is taper fade haram?

What is Taper Fade?

Taper fade is a type of haircut that features gradually shorter hair on the sides and back, which fades into longer hair on top. It creates a clean, smooth look and is often paired with a beard. Taper fade is known by many names, including the South of France haircut, Brooklyn fade, and blowout.

How to Get a Taper Fade Haircut

If you're interested in getting a taper fade haircut, there are a few steps to follow:

  1. Choose a barber who has experience with taper fades.
  2. Decide on the length of the fade you want.
  3. Sit back and let the barber do the work.
  4. Maintain your haircut by getting regular trims.

Tips for a Great Taper Fade

To ensure that your taper fade looks great, here are a few tips:

  • Communicate clearly with your barber about what you want.
  • Choose a style that complements your face shape.
  • Use hair products to style your hair.
  • Keep your hair clean and healthy.

Is Taper Fade Haram?

The debate on whether taper fade is haram centers on the interpretation of Islamic teachings on hairstyles. Some Muslims believe that it is haram to imitate non-Muslims or to adopt hairstyles that are associated with non-Muslim cultures. Others argue that as long as the haircut is modest and does not violate Islamic principles of modesty and cleanliness, it is permissible.

The Arguments Against Taper Fade

Those who argue that taper fade is haram point to the fact that it is a non-Muslim hairstyle that originated in Western culture. They believe that Muslims should not adopt hairstyles that are associated with non-Muslim cultures, as this can lead to cultural assimilation and a loss of Islamic identity.

The Arguments for Taper Fade

On the other hand, those who believe that taper fade is permissible argue that it is just a haircut and that Islamic teachings do not prohibit Muslims from adopting hairstyles from other cultures. They point to the fact that there is no clear Islamic ruling on hairstyles and that as long as a haircut is modest and does not violate Islamic principles, it is acceptable.

FAQs

Can Muslims Get Taper Fade Haircuts?

Yes, Muslims can get taper fade haircuts. However, there is debate on whether taper fade is haram or not. Ultimately, it is up to individual interpretation and personal preference.

What Other Hairstyles Are Permissible for Muslims?

Islamic teachings do not prescribe specific hairstyles for Muslims. However, it is important to uphold principles of modesty and cleanliness in one's appearance. Some Muslims choose to keep their hair short or wear a head covering, while others prefer longer hair.

Hair Care for Taper Fade Haircuts

To keep your taper fade looking great, it is important to take care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Use conditioner to keep your hair soft and smooth.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as flat irons or blow dryers.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut.

Conclusion

Ultimately, the question of whether taper fade is haram is a matter of personal interpretation and preference. While some Muslims believe that it is not permissible to adopt hairstyles from non-Muslim cultures, others argue that as long as a haircut is modest and does not violate Islamic principles, it is acceptable. Whatever your view, it is important to take care of your hair and maintain a clean and modest appearance.
