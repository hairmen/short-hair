How To Keep My Hair From Getting Poofy

Written by William Nov 28, 2023 · 3 min read

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you know how frustrating it can be when your hair gets poofy. Poofy hair can be caused by humidity, heat damage, or simply genetics. Whatever the cause, there are steps you can take to keep your hair looking smooth and sleek.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips to Prevent Poofy Hair

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Look for a shampoo and conditioner that is sulfate-free and contains moisturizing ingredients like argan oil or shea butter.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner.

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help lock in moisture and prevent frizz. Focus on the ends of your hair, which are typically the driest.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Before styling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will help prevent breakage and minimize the amount of heat you need to use to style your hair.

4. Avoid heat styling as much as possible.

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause it to become poofy. Try to air dry your hair as much as possible, and if you must use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat evenly.

5. Use a serum or oil to add shine and control frizz.

After styling your hair, apply a serum or oil to add shine and control frizz. Be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy.

Tips to Prevent Poofy Hair

1. Sleep on a silk pillowcase.

Silk pillowcases can help prevent frizz and breakage by reducing friction between your hair and the pillowcase.

2. Avoid touching your hair too much.

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz and breakage. Try to avoid playing with your hair throughout the day.

3. Wear your hair in protective styles.

Braids, twists, and other protective styles can help prevent breakage and keep your hair looking smooth and sleek.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to prevent poofy hair?

A: While a hair straightener can help smooth your hair, using it too often can cause heat damage and make your hair more prone to poofiness.

Q: What should I do if my hair still gets poofy?

A: If your hair still gets poofy despite your best efforts, try using a hair serum or pomade to tame flyaways and add shine.

Hair Care Routine

1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

4. Air dry your hair as much as possible, or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.

5. Apply a serum or oil to add shine and control frizz.

6. Sleep on a silk pillowcase.

7. Avoid touching your hair too much.

8. Wear your hair in protective styles.

By following these tips and incorporating them into your hair care routine, you can keep your hair looking smooth and sleek, even in humid weather or after heat styling.
