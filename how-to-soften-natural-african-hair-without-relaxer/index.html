How To Soften Natural African Hair Without Relaxer

If you have natural African hair, you may think that the only way to soften it is by using a relaxer. However, using a relaxer can damage your hair and lead to breakage. The good news is that there are natural ways to soften your hair without using a relaxer. In this article, we will discuss how to soften natural African hair without relaxer.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Soften Natural African Hair Without Relaxer

The first step to softening natural African hair is to understand your hair type. African hair is typically dry and brittle, which can make it difficult to manage. However, there are several things you can do to soften your hair and make it more manageable.

1. Use Natural Oils:

One of the best ways to soften natural African hair is to use natural oils. These oils can penetrate your hair shaft and moisturize your hair from the inside out. Some of the best oils for softening natural African hair include coconut oil, olive oil, jojoba oil, and avocado oil. Apply the oil to your hair and scalp and leave it on for a few hours or overnight. You can also use an oil-based hair mask to soften your hair.

2. Use a Deep Conditioner:

Another way to soften natural African hair is to use a deep conditioner. Deep conditioners are designed to penetrate your hair shaft and provide intense moisture. Look for a deep conditioner that contains natural ingredients such as honey, aloe vera, or shea butter. Apply the deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Use a Hair Steamer:

A hair steamer can help to open up your hair cuticles and allow moisture to penetrate your hair. Use a hair steamer once a week to soften your hair. You can also use a hot towel to wrap your hair after applying a deep conditioner or oil treatment.

4. Avoid Heat:

Heat can damage your hair and make it more difficult to manage. Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. Instead, opt for air drying your hair using a diffuser or towel drying your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide:

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to soften natural African hair without relaxer:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  2. Apply a natural oil such as coconut oil or olive oil to your hair and scalp.
  3. Cover your hair with a plastic cap and leave the oil on for a few hours or overnight.
  4. Rinse out the oil and apply a deep conditioner to your hair.
  5. Use a hair steamer or hot towel to help the deep conditioner penetrate your hair.
  6. Rinse out the deep conditioner and towel dry your hair.
  7. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it moisturized.
  8. Avoid using heat styling tools and opt for air drying your hair.
  9. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Softening Natural African Hair:

Here are some tips for softening natural African hair:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to help prevent breakage and dryness.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals such as hair dyes and relaxers.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes foods rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Avoid using tight hair accessories that can cause breakage.

FAQs:

Q: Can I soften my natural African hair without using any products?

A: Yes, you can soften your natural African hair without using any products. Try using a hair steamer or hot towel to help open up your hair cuticles and allow moisture to penetrate your hair.

Q: How often should I deep condition my natural African hair?

A: You should deep condition your natural African hair once a week to help keep it moisturized and soft.

Hair Care for Softening Natural African Hair:

Here are some tips for hair care for softening natural African hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid causing breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools and opt for air drying your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

By following these tips and using natural products, you can soften your natural African hair without using a relaxer. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care routine, and you will see results over time.
