How To Make Your Hair Curly Again After Keratin

If youâve recently undergone a keratin treatment, you may have noticed that your hair has become straighter and smoother. While this is great for those who prefer a sleek look, some may miss their natural curls. Fortunately, there are ways to make your hair curly again after keratin without damaging your tresses. This article will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to achieve bouncy, curly locks once more.

How to

Step 1: Wait it out

The first step in making your hair curly again after keratin is simply waiting it out. Keratin treatments typically last for three to six months, depending on your hair type and quality. During this time, your hair will gradually return to its natural state, and your curls will begin to re-emerge.

Step 2: Use a clarifying shampoo

If youâre eager to accelerate the process, you can use a clarifying shampoo to strip your hair of the keratin treatment. Clarifying shampoos are designed to remove buildup and residue from your hair, including the keratin treatment. Be sure to use a gentle, sulfate-free clarifying shampoo to avoid further damage to your hair.

Step 3: Deep condition your hair

After using a clarifying shampoo, itâs important to deep condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Look for a deep conditioner that contains nourishing ingredients such as argan oil, shea butter, or coconut oil. Apply the conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends, and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing.

Step 4: Use heat styling tools

Once your hair has been deep conditioned, you can use heat styling tools to create curls. Use a curling iron or wand to create loose, beachy curls, or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your natural curls.

Step 5: Use styling products

To help your curls hold their shape, use styling products such as mousse, gel, or hairspray. Apply the product to damp hair, then use your fingers to scrunch your curls as you blow dry. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls.

Tips

  • Be patient â it may take several months for your curls to fully return.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can disrupt your curls.
  • Avoid using products that contain sulfates or alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.

FAQ

Can I make my hair curly again after keratin without using heat styling tools?

While heat styling tools are the most effective way to create curls, you can also try using rollers or braids to create curls without heat.

Can I get a keratin treatment again after making my hair curly?

Yes, you can get a keratin treatment again if you choose to do so. However, keep in mind that repeated keratin treatments can cause damage to your hair over time.

Will making my hair curly again after keratin cause damage?

If done properly, making your hair curly again after keratin should not cause damage to your hair. However, using heat styling tools too frequently or using products that contain harsh chemicals can cause damage over time.

Hair Care

To maintain healthy, curly hair, itâs important to follow a few simple hair care tips. First, be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly with products that are appropriate for your hair type. Second, avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, and always use a heat protectant before styling. Third, trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth. Finally, eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to nourish your hair from the inside out.
