How To Start Female Dreads A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 08, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to start female dreads, you've come to the right place! Here, we'll provide you with all the information you need to get started on your journey to locked hair. From how to start your dreads to tips on keeping them healthy, we've got you covered.

Description

Dreadlocks, or dreads, are a hairstyle that has been worn by people of all different cultures for thousands of years. They're essentially matted ropes of hair that are formed through a process of twisting, backcombing, and palm rolling. While dreads are often associated with Rastafarian culture, they've become popular across the world and are worn by people of all races and backgrounds.

How to Start Female Dreads

Step 1: Decide on Your Method

There are a few different methods you can use to start your dreads, including backcombing, twisting, and the "neglect" method. Each method has its own pros and cons, so it's important to do your research and decide which one is best for you.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Before you start your dreads, it's important to wash your hair thoroughly. This will help remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup, which can make it harder for your hair to lock. Use a clarifying shampoo to get your hair as clean as possible.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, you'll need to section your hair into the size and shape of the dreads you want. You can use a comb or your fingers to do this. For thinner dreads, make smaller sections. For thicker dreads, make larger sections.

Step 4: Start Twisting, Backcombing or Neglecting

Depending on the method you've chosen, you'll either start twisting or backcombing your hair, or you'll simply start neglecting it. If you're twisting or backcombing, use a bit of product to help your hair hold its shape. If you're neglecting, simply let your hair do its own thing.

Step 5: Palm Roll Your Dreads

Once you've started your dreads, you'll need to palm roll them regularly to help them lock. To palm roll, simply take a dread in your hands and rub it between your palms, rolling it back and forth. This will help it tighten up and become more locked over time.

Tips for Starting Female Dreads

Starting dreads can be a long and sometimes frustrating process, but there are a few things you can do to help make it easier:

  • Be patient. Dreads take time to form, and it can take weeks or even months before you see any real progress.
  • Keep them clean. Clean hair locks better, so it's important to wash your dreads regularly.
  • Don't overdo it with the product. While a bit of product can help your hair hold its shape, too much can actually make it harder for your hair to lock.
  • Be gentle. Avoid pulling or tugging on your dreads, as this can cause them to break or become frizzy.

FAQ

How long does it take to start female dreads?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to start dreads, depending on your hair type and the method you use.

Do I need to cut my hair to start dreads?

No, you don't need to cut your hair to start dreads. In fact, the longer your hair is, the easier it is to start dreads.

How often should I palm roll my dreads?

You should palm roll your dreads at least once a week to help them lock and tighten up.

Hair Care for Female Dreads

Once you've started your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and looking their best.

  • Wash them regularly with a clarifying shampoo.
  • Be gentle when washing and drying your dreads.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Tie them up when sleeping or doing physical activity to avoid damage.
  • Get regular maintenance to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

Starting female dreads can be a challenging but rewarding experience. With the right method and a little patience, you can achieve the beautiful, locked hair you've always wanted. Just remember to take good care of your dreads to keep them healthy and looking their best.
