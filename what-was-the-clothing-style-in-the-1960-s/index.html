What Was The Clothing Style In The 1960S

Written by Frank Nov 06, 2023 Â· 3 min read

In the 1960s, fashion was all about breaking free from the conservative styles of the past. The youth culture of the time was rebelling against societal norms, and this was reflected in the clothing of the era. From the mini skirt to the psychedelic prints, the 1960s was a decade of bold and daring fashion statements that still influence style today.

What Were the Key Fashion Trends of the 1960s?

The 1960s was a decade of change, and this was reflected in the clothes people wore. Here are some of the key fashion trends of the era:

Mini Skirts

Invented by British designer Mary Quant, the mini skirt was a revolutionary fashion statement that took the world by storm. Short and sassy, the mini skirt was the perfect symbol of the youth culture of the 1960s.

Psychedelic Prints

The 1960s was a time of experimentation, and this was reflected in the bold, psychedelic prints that were popular at the time. From swirling patterns to bright colors, these prints were a reflection of the era's counterculture movement.

Hippie Fashion

The hippie fashion trend of the 1960s was all about peace, love, and freedom. Flowing fabrics, tie-dye prints, and fringe were all popular elements of this style. This fashion trend was a reflection of the era's anti-establishment culture.

How to Create a 1960s Look Today

If you want to create a 1960s look today, here are some tips:

Go for Bold Prints

Whether it's a psychedelic print or a bold floral pattern, the 1960s was all about bold prints. Look for pieces with eye-catching patterns that will make a statement.

Embrace the Mini Skirt

The mini skirt is a classic piece of 1960s fashion that still looks great today. Pair it with a simple top and a pair of boots for a modern take on this iconic look.

Add Some Fringe

Fringe was a popular element of the hippie fashion trend of the 1960s. Add a fringe vest or a fringe bag to your outfit for a touch of bohemian style.

FAQ

What Was the Clothing Style for Women in the 1960s?

The clothing style for women in the 1960s was all about breaking free from the conservative styles of the past. Mini skirts, bright colors, and bold prints were all popular elements of 1960s fashion for women.

What Was the Clothing Style for Men in the 1960s?

The clothing style for men in the 1960s was also influenced by the counterculture movement. Bold prints, paisley patterns, and bell-bottom pants were all popular elements of 1960s fashion for men.

What Hairstyles Were Popular in the 1960s?

The 1960s was a decade of bold and daring hairstyles. The bouffant, beehive, and pixie cut were all popular styles for women, while men often wore their hair longer and more shaggy than in previous decades.

Hair Care in the 1960s

Hair care in the 1960s was all about volume and texture. Women often used rollers and backcombing to create the iconic bouffant and beehive hairstyles. Men often used hair gel to create the popular shaggy look.

Overall, the 1960s was a decade of bold and daring fashion statements that still influence style today. Whether you're looking to create a 1960s-inspired outfit or just want to add a touch of retro style to your wardrobe, there are plenty of ways to embrace the fashion of this iconic era.
