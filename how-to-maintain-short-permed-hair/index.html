How To Maintain Short Permed Hair

Short permed hair can be a great way to add volume and texture to your hair without having to spend hours styling it every day. However, it's important to know how to maintain your short permed hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. In this blog post, we'll give you some tips on how to maintain short permed hair, including how to style it, how to care for it, and more.

How to Maintain Short Permed Hair

Step 1: Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause your perm to fade faster.

Step 2: Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons as much as possible. Heat can damage your permed hair and cause it to lose its shape and texture.

Step 3: Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet. Don't use a brush, as this can cause your hair to frizz and break.

Step 4: Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to your hair when it's damp. This will help to define your curls and keep them looking bouncy and shiny.

Tips

Tip 1: Sleep with a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase to help preserve your curls and prevent frizz.

Tip 2: Don't wash your hair too often, as this can cause your perm to fade faster. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days instead.

Tip 3: Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Q: How long does a perm last in short hair?

A: A perm can last anywhere from 3-6 months depending on your hair type and how well you take care of it.

Q: Can I color my permed hair?

A: Yes, you can color your permed hair, but it's best to wait at least 2 weeks after getting your perm to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care

When it comes to maintaining short permed hair, it's important to use the right products and techniques to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for taking care of your permed hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Detangle your hair when it's wet with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to your hair when it's damp
  • Sleep with a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

By following these tips and taking good care of your short permed hair, you can keep your curls looking bouncy, shiny, and healthy for months to come.
