How To Cut Layered Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Dec 02, 2023 · 4 min read

Layered hair is a popular hairstyle that adds texture, volume, and movement to your locks. Cutting layered hair may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look at home. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of cutting layered hair, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layered Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layered Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

How to Cut Layered Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to gather the right tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle

Once you have your tools ready, it’s time to start cutting. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be misleading, as it may appear longer than it actually is when dry.

  2. Section your hair. Use the comb to make a straight part in the middle of your hair, dividing it into two equal sections. Then, make a part from the middle of your forehead to the back of your head, dividing your hair into four equal sections. Use hair clips to secure each section of hair.

  3. Start with the bottom layer. Unclip the bottom section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut at a slight angle. This will create a subtle, layered effect.

  4. Move on to the next layer. Unclip the middle section of hair and repeat the process, cutting at the same angle as the previous layer.

  5. Repeat the process for the top layers of hair. Remember to cut at a slight angle, rather than straight across.

  6. Blend the layers. Use the comb to blend the layers together, checking for any uneven sections. If necessary, go back and trim any stray hairs.

  7. Finish with styling. Once you’re happy with the length and shape of your layered hair, style it as desired.

Tips for Cutting Layered Hair

Cutting layered hair can be tricky, especially if you’re new to hair cutting. Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect layered look:

  • Start with small snips. It’s better to cut too little than too much, as you can always go back and trim more hair.
  • Use the right scissors. Haircutting scissors are specifically designed to make precise cuts, so using regular scissors can result in uneven cuts.
  • Be patient. Cutting layered hair takes time and practice, so don’t rush the process.
  • Check for symmetry. Use a mirror to check that both sides of your hair are even and symmetrical.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own layered hair?

Yes, you can cut your own layered hair. However, it may be easier to have someone else help you, especially if you’re new to hair cutting.

How often should I cut my layered hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Can I cut layers into curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair. However, it’s important to keep in mind that curly hair will appear shorter when dry, so be conservative when cutting.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Once you’ve cut your layered hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain its shape and health. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a heat protectant when styling with heat tools.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and flatten your layers.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own layered hair at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and be patient. Happy cutting!
