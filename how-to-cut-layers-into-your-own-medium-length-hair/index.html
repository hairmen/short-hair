How To Cut Layers Into Your Own Medium Length Hair

Written by Bardi Sep 07, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and movement to your medium length hair, cutting layers can be a great option. However, heading to the salon every few months can be expensive and time-consuming. Fortunately, with a few tools and some patience, you can cut layers into your own hair at home. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to achieve a layered look that suits you.

Description

The layered haircut is a popular style that adds volume, texture, and movement to the hair. It involves cutting the hair in a way that creates different lengths throughout the hair, with shorter layers on top and longer layers underneath. This creates a soft, flowing effect that can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style.

How To

To cut layers into your own medium length hair, you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Clips or hair ties

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Comb out any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section off the hair. Use clips or hair ties to separate the hair into manageable sections. Start with the hair at the back of your head and work your way forward.

Step 3: Decide on the length of your layers. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and hold it straight up. Use the scissors to cut the hair at the desired length. Remember, it's always better to start with a longer length and work your way shorter.

Step 4: Work your way around your head, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Make sure to keep the sections even and the lengths consistent. Use the mirror to check the back of your head.

Step 5: Blend the layers. Once you've finished cutting the layers, use the scissors to blend the edges. This will create a more natural look and ensure that the layers flow seamlessly into each other.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts.
  • Remember to cut the hair at an angle to create a softer look.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut small amounts and check your progress as you go.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, start with small trims and work your way up to more dramatic cuts.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut layers into curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair. However, it's important to remember that curly hair shrinks when it dries. To avoid cutting too much hair, start with longer lengths and work your way shorter.

Q: How often should I cut my layers?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, medium length hair should be trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

To keep your layered haircut looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. This includes:

  • Using a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Using a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools.
  • Trimming your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Cutting layers into your own medium length hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and a bit of practice, it's definitely achievable. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and be patient. Good luck!
