How To Layer Hair Mens A Step By Step Guide

Layering hair is a popular technique for adding volume and texture to men's hair. But how do you do it? In this article, we'll guide you through the process of how to layer hair mens, from choosing the right hairstyle to styling your new layers.

Choosing the Right Hairstyle

The first step in layering your hair is to choose the right hairstyle. Layering works best on medium to long hair lengths and can be used to create a variety of styles, from messy and textured to sleek and polished.

Consider your hair type and face shape when choosing a hairstyle. For example, if you have thick, wavy hair, a layered hairstyle can help to thin it out and make it more manageable. If you have a round face shape, layers can help to add definition and structure.

Some popular hairstyles for men that can be layered include:

  • Long and wavy
  • Messy and textured
  • Slicked-back
  • Undercut
  • Side-parted

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start layering your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type. This will help to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that could get in the way of your haircut.

Once your hair is clean and dry, comb it through to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to section and cut your hair.

Sectioning Your Hair

To start layering your hair, you'll need to section it into manageable sections. Use clips or hair ties to separate your hair into top, middle, and bottom sections.

Start by sectioning the top layer of your hair. Use a comb to create a horizontal part from your crown to your temples. Clip the top section of your hair up and out of the way.

Next, separate the middle section of your hair. Create another horizontal part just below the first one and clip the middle section up and out of the way.

Finally, separate the bottom section of your hair. This should be the remaining hair that's left hanging down.

Cutting Your Layers

Now that your hair is sectioned, it's time to start cutting your layers. Start with the top section of your hair, as this will be the shortest layer.

Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and use scissors to cut it straight across. This will create a guide for the rest of your layers. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way from the back of your head to the front.

Move on to the middle section of your hair and repeat the process. This layer should be slightly longer than the top layer, so use your previous cut as a guide.

Finally, cut the bottom layer of your hair. This should be the longest layer and can be cut to your desired length.

Blending Your Layers

Once your layers are cut, it's important to blend them together for a seamless look. Use scissors or a razor to blend the ends of your layers together, removing any harsh lines or choppy edges.

Be careful not to over-blend your layers, as this can result in a choppy or uneven cut. Use a light touch and take your time to create a natural, textured look.

Styling Your Layers

Now that your hair is layered, it's time to style it. Use a styling product that's suitable for your hair type and the look you're trying to achieve.

For example, if you want a messy, textured look, try a matte paste or clay. For a sleek, polished look, use a pomade or gel. Apply the product to your hair and style it as desired.

Tips for Layering Men's Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you become more confident.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Consult a professional hairstylist if you're unsure about layering your hair yourself.

FAQs

Can you layer short hair?

While layering works best on medium to long hair lengths, it is possible to layer short hair. However, the layers will be shorter and less noticeable than on longer hair.

How often should you get your hair layered?

How often you get your hair layered depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. On average, most people get their hair layered every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

Now that your hair is layered, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type and avoid using too much heat on your hair when styling it.

Regular trims will also help to keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends. Consult a professional hairstylist for advice on how often to get your hair trimmed.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and textured look by layering your hair. Experiment with different styles and products to find the look that works best for you.
