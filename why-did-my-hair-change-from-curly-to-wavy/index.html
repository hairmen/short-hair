Why Did My Hair Change From Curly To Wavy

Written by Christine Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of those people who have always had curly hair, but suddenly noticed that your curls have turned into waves? Don't worry; you're not alone. Many people experience this phenomenon, and it can be quite confusing. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why your hair may have changed from curly to wavy and what you can do about it.

Description

Before we dive into the reasons why your hair may have changed from curly to wavy, it's important to understand the difference between the two hair types. Curly hair is characterized by tight, coiled curls that form ringlets or spirals. On the other hand, wavy hair has looser curls that fall somewhere between straight and curly.

If you've noticed that your hair has become less curly and more wavy over time, there are a few reasons why this may have happened. Let's take a closer look.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

The first step in understanding why your hair may have changed from curly to wavy is to determine your hair type. The most common hair types are straight, wavy, curly, and coily. To determine your hair type, you can use the following guidelines:

  • Straight hair: Hair that falls flat and has no discernible curl pattern.
  • Wavy hair: Hair that has a slight curl pattern and is often described as "beachy."
  • Curly hair: Hair that has a defined curl pattern and forms ringlets or spirals.
  • Coily hair: Hair that has a very tight curl pattern and is often referred to as "kinky."

Once you've determined your hair type, you can better understand why your hair may have changed from curly to wavy.

Step-by-Step Guide to Transitioning

If you've noticed that your hair has become less curly and more wavy, you may be wondering how to transition to your new hair type. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you:

  1. Start by using a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for wavy hair.
  2. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while it's still wet.
  3. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and make it frizzy.
  4. Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  5. Avoid using heavy products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  6. Try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves.
  7. Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for your new hair type.

By following these steps, you can transition to your new hair type and learn to love your waves.

Tips for Managing Wavy Hair

If you're new to the world of wavy hair, here are a few tips to help you manage your new hair type:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as brushes can disrupt your natural curl pattern.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Try using a curl-enhancing product, such as a mousse or gel, to define your waves.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.

FAQ

Q: Can hair texture change naturally?

A: Yes, hair texture can change naturally over time due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors.

Q: Can I get my curls back?

A: It's possible to regain your curls with proper hair care and styling techniques. Consider consulting with a stylist who specializes in curly hair for advice.

Q: Can heat styling tools cause my hair to change texture?

A: Yes, using heat styling tools can damage your hair and cause it to become frizzy or lose its curl pattern.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

If you're looking to keep your wavy hair healthy and beautiful, here are a few hair care tips to consider:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause frizz.
  • Consider using a hair oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.

By following these tips and using the right products, you can keep your wavy hair looking gorgeous and healthy.

In conclusion, if you've noticed that your hair has changed from curly to wavy, it's nothing to worry about. With the right hair care routine and styling techniques, you can embrace your new hair type and look fabulous.
