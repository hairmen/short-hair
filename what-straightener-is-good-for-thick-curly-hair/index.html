What Straightener Is Good For Thick Curly Hair The Ultimate Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thick curly hair, you know that finding the right straightener can be a challenge. You need a straightener that is powerful enough to tame your curls, but gentle enough to avoid damaging your hair. In this guide, we'll show you what straightener is good for thick curly hair and give you tips for achieving a sleek, straight look that will last all day.

Description

Thick curly hair can be beautiful, but it can also be difficult to manage. Many straighteners on the market are not designed to handle the unique challenges of thick curly hair. They may not be powerful enough to straighten your hair or may cause damage to your hair over time. That's why it's important to find a straightener that is specifically designed for thick curly hair.

How to Choose the Right Straightener for Thick Curly Hair

When choosing a straightener for thick curly hair, there are several factors to consider:

Plate Material

The plate material is one of the most important factors to consider when choosing a straightener for thick curly hair. Ceramic plates are a popular choice because they distribute heat evenly and are less likely to cause damage to your hair. Titanium plates are also a good choice because they heat up quickly and can handle thick, curly hair.

Plate Width

The plate width is another important factor to consider. If you have thick curly hair, you'll want a straightener with wider plates. This will allow you to straighten larger sections of hair at once, reducing the time it takes to achieve a sleek, straight look.

Temperature Settings

The temperature settings on your straightener are also important. You'll want a straightener with adjustable temperature settings so you can choose the right temperature for your hair type. If you have thick curly hair, you'll need a straightener that can reach high temperatures to effectively straighten your hair.

Step by Step Guide to Straightening Thick Curly Hair

Now that you have the right straightener, it's time to start straightening your hair. Here's a step by step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products designed for curly hair. This will help to soften and detangle your hair, making it easier to straighten.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Before straightening your hair, apply a heat protectant to protect your hair from heat damage.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to straighten each section of hair.

Step 4: Straighten Each Section of Hair

Starting with the bottom sections of hair, use your straightener to straighten each section of hair. Be sure to use the right temperature setting for your hair type and use even pressure as you straighten each section of hair.

Step 5: Finish with a Hair Serum

Once you've finished straightening your hair, apply a hair serum to add shine and reduce frizz.

Tips for Straightening Thick Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for achieving a sleek, straight look:

Use a Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before straightening it. This will help to reduce damage to your hair.

Don't Straighten Wet Hair

Always straighten dry hair. Straightening wet hair can cause damage to your hair and can be dangerous.

Use a High Quality Straightener

Invest in a high quality straightener that is specifically designed for thick curly hair. This will help to reduce damage to your hair and will make it easier to achieve a sleek, straight look.

FAQ

Can I straighten my hair every day?

Straightening your hair every day can cause damage to your hair. It's best to limit straightening to once or twice a week.

What if my hair is too thick to straighten?

If your hair is too thick to straighten with a traditional straightener, consider using a straightening brush or a straightening iron with wider plates.

How can I reduce damage to my hair when straightening it?

Use a heat protectant, choose the right temperature for your hair type, and use even pressure when straightening your hair to reduce damage.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for hair care for thick curly hair:

Use Products Designed for Curly Hair

Use products designed specifically for curly hair to help keep your hair healthy and strong.

Avoid Overwashing Your Hair

Overwashing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, causing damage and dryness. Try to wash your hair every other day or every three days.

Avoid Heat Styling Too Often

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair over time. Try to limit heat styling to once or twice a week.

With the right straightener and the right hair care routine, you can achieve a sleek, straight look that will last all day. Follow these tips and you'll have healthy, beautiful hair in no time.
