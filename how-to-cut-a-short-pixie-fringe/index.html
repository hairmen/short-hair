How To Cut A Short Pixie Fringe A Step By Step Guide

Are you ready to take the plunge and cut yourself a short pixie fringe? This edgy hairstyle has been gaining popularity in recent years, and for good reason! A short pixie fringe can give you a bold and confident look. But before you grab your scissors and start cutting away, let's go through some helpful tips and steps to ensure you get the perfect cut.

How to Cut a Short Pixie Fringe

A short pixie fringe is a bold and daring haircut that requires the right tools and techniques to achieve the perfect look. Before you start cutting, make sure you have a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair as you cut.

When it comes to cutting a short pixie fringe, there are a few techniques you can use. You can opt for a straight-across cut, a diagonal cut, or a choppy cut. The type of cut you choose will depend on your personal preferences and the shape of your face.

It's important to note that cutting a short pixie fringe can be a bit tricky, especially if you're doing it yourself. If you're not confident in your cutting skills, it's best to seek the help of a professional stylist.

Step-by-Step Guide

Ready to cut your own short pixie fringe? Follow these step-by-step instructions:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use a comb to part your hair where you want your fringe to be.
  2. Dampen your hair with water using a spray bottle.
  3. Using your scissors, cut your fringe to the desired length. Remember, you can always cut more hair off, but you can't put it back on!
  4. If you want a choppy look, use your scissors to make small snips in your fringe. This will give it a textured, edgy look.
  5. Style your fringe using a blow dryer and a round brush. Blow dry your hair in the direction you want it to lay.
  6. Finish off with some hairspray or styling gel to keep your fringe in place.

Helpful Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own short pixie fringe:

  • Start by cutting a little bit of hair at a time. It's better to cut too little than too much!
  • Use a comb to keep your hair in place as you cut.
  • Remember that your fringe will look shorter when it's dry, so be cautious when cutting.
  • If you're not confident in your cutting skills, seek the help of a professional stylist.

FAQs

Can I cut a short pixie fringe if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! A short pixie fringe can look great on curly hair. Just make sure to cut your hair when it's dry so you can see the length and shape more clearly.

How often do I need to trim my pixie fringe?

It's best to trim your pixie fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh and stylish.

Can I style my pixie fringe in different ways?

Yes! You can style your pixie fringe in different ways, such as slicking it back, wearing it to the side, or adding some volume with a round brush and blow dryer.

Hair Care for Your New Pixie Fringe

Now that you have a stylish new pixie fringe, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from getting too oily.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

With these tips and steps, you can confidently cut your own short pixie fringe at home. Just remember to take your time, be cautious, and seek the help of a professional if you're not confident in your skills. Good luck!
