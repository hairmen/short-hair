How To Decide Which Haircut Suits You

Written by Bardi Aug 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Deciding which haircut suits you can be a daunting task. With so many different styles and trends, it's hard to know where to start. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of choosing the perfect haircut for you. From face shape to hair texture, we'll cover everything you need to know to make an informed decision.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing a haircut is determining your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Each face shape has its own set of characteristics that can help guide your decision.

Round faces look best with haircuts that add height and length to the face. This can be achieved with layers or a side-swept fringe. Oval faces are considered the most versatile, and can pull off a variety of styles. Square faces benefit from softening angles with layers or curls. Heart-shaped faces look best with a side-swept fringe or layers that frame the face. Diamond faces look great with a chin-length bob or a side-swept fringe. Oblong faces benefit from styles that add width to the face, such as curls or layers that hit at the cheekbones.

Consider Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture plays a big role in what type of haircut will suit you best. If you have thick, coarse hair, you may want to opt for a layered cut that thins out the hair. If you have fine hair, a blunt cut can add the illusion of thickness. Curly hair benefits from layers that enhance the curl pattern, while straight hair looks great with blunt cuts or long layers.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut. If you're someone who doesn't have a lot of time to style your hair, a low-maintenance cut may be the best option. If you're someone who loves to experiment with different styles, a more versatile cut may be the way to go.

Know Your Hair Type

Knowing your hair type is crucial when choosing a haircut. Different hair types require different care and styling techniques. For example, if you have curly hair, you may want to avoid a super-short cut that will require a lot of styling to maintain. If you have straight hair, you may want to avoid a cut that requires a lot of layers, as they can make the hair look thin and wispy.

Consider Professional Help

If you're unsure about what type of haircut will suit you best, consider consulting with a professional stylist. A stylist can assess your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle to help you choose the perfect cut. They can also offer advice on how to style and maintain your new look.

Face-Framing Layers

Face-framing layers are a great way to add dimension and movement to your hair. They can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type. For example, if you have a round face, longer layers that hit at the cheekbones can help elongate the face. If you have fine hair, shorter layers can add volume and dimension.

Short Haircuts

Short haircuts can be a great option for those who want a low-maintenance style. They can also be very versatile and can be styled in a variety of ways. However, short haircuts may not be the best option for those with very thick or curly hair, as they can be difficult to manage.

Long Haircuts

Long haircuts can be very versatile and can be styled in a variety of ways. They are a great option for those who love to experiment with different styles. However, long hair can be high-maintenance and may not be the best option for those with a busy lifestyle.

Maintenance and Hair Care

Once you've chosen your perfect haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Regular trims can help keep your haircut looking fresh, and using the right products for your hair type can help enhance your style. It's also important to avoid using too much heat styling, as this can damage the hair.

FAQ

Q: How often should I get a haircut?
A: It depends on your hair type and style. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and maintain your style.

Q: Can I pull off a short haircut?
A: Short haircuts can be very flattering on a variety of face shapes and hair types. If you're unsure, consult with a professional stylist to find a short haircut that will suit you best.

Q: How do I know if a particular haircut will suit me?
A: Consider your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle when choosing a haircut. If you're unsure, consult with a professional stylist who can offer advice.

Choosing the perfect haircut can be a fun and exciting process. By considering your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, you can choose a style that will suit you best. With the right maintenance and care, your new haircut will look great and make you feel confident and stylish.
