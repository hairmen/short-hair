How To Dread Hair With Crochet Hook

Written by Petter Nov 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dreading hair has become a popular trend nowadays. It is not only a fashionable statement but also a convenient hairstyle for those who prefer low maintenance hair. There are many ways to create dreadlocks, but one of the most popular methods is using a crochet hook. In this article, we will guide you on how to dread hair with crochet hook step by step.

Description

Crochet hook dreading is a process of creating dreads by using a small hook. The process involves pulling hair through itself in a twisting motion, creating knots that eventually become dreadlocks. The best part of this method is that it works well with different hair textures and lengths. It is also a gentle process that does not involve the use of chemicals or wax, making it a healthy option for your hair.

How to Dread Hair with Crochet Hook

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

The first step is to wash your hair thoroughly with a residue-free shampoo. This will remove any oils or dirt that may prevent the hair from locking quickly. Dry your hair using a towel, but do not use a hairdryer as it may cause frizz.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections, about the size of a pencil. Use hair ties to keep them in place.

Step 3: Start the Dreadlocking Process

Take a crochet hook and start at the root of the hair section. Insert the hook into the hair and pull it through to the other side, creating a loop. Take the end of the hair section and insert it through the loop, creating a knot. Repeat this process until the entire hair section is knotted. The knots will start to tighten and form a dreadlock.

Step 4: Maintain the Dreadlocks

After creating the dreadlocks, you need to maintain them regularly. You can use a crochet hook to tighten the knots and remove any loose hair. You can also palm roll the dreadlocks to help them lock together.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you dread your hair with crochet hook:

  • Use a crochet hook with a small hook size for better control.
  • Do not make the knots too tight as it may cause discomfort and breakage.
  • Be patient as the process may take several hours depending on the length and thickness of your hair.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using hair products that may cause build-up.

FAQ

Can you use a regular crochet hook?

It is not recommended to use a regular crochet hook as it may damage your hair. Use a crochet hook specifically designed for dreading hair.

Can you dread hair that has been relaxed or chemically treated?

Yes, you can dread hair that has been relaxed or chemically treated. However, the process may take longer as the hair may be weaker.

How long will it take to dread my hair?

The time it takes to dread your hair depends on the length and thickness of your hair. It may take several hours to several days to complete the process.

Hair Care

After dreading your hair, you need to take care of it to prevent breakage and maintain healthy hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using hair products that may cause build-up or weigh down your hair.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.

Crochet hook dreading is an easy and healthy way to create dreadlocks. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and low maintenance hairstyle. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips to dread your hair with crochet hook.
