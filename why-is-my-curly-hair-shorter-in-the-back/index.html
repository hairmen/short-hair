Why Is My Curly Hair Shorter In The Back

Written by Eveline Oct 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you often find that your curly hair is shorter in the back than in the front? This can be a common concern for those with curly hair. In this article, we will discuss the reasons behind this issue and provide some tips on how to address it.

Description

Curly hair is often more prone to shrinkage than straight hair. This means that when it dries, it can appear shorter than its actual length. This is especially noticeable in the back of the head, where the hair is often more tightly coiled.

Another reason why curly hair may be shorter in the back is due to the way it is cut. Some hairdressers may cut curly hair in a way that does not take into account the natural curl pattern. This can lead to uneven lengths and a shorter appearance in the back.

How to

If you are experiencing shorter hair in the back due to shrinkage, there are a few things you can do to help elongate your curls:

  1. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can contribute to damage and breakage.
  2. Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define and hold your curls.
  3. Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it is dry, as this can lead to frizz and breakage.
  4. Try the "pineapple" method when sleeping, where you loosely gather your curls on top of your head and secure them with a scrunchie. This can help maintain curl definition and prevent flattening.

If your hair is shorter in the back due to an uneven cut, it may be time to find a hairdresser who specializes in curly hair. They will know how to cut your hair in a way that takes into account your natural curl pattern and can help create a more even appearance.

Step by step

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

Step 2: Apply product

Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair, focusing on the back where your curls may be tighter. This will help define your curls and prevent shrinkage.

Step 3: Style your hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to gently dry your curls, cupping them in the diffuser to prevent frizz. You can also air dry your hair by gently scrunching your curls upwards.

Tips

  • Avoid using heavy products like oils and butters, as they can weigh down your curls and contribute to shrinkage.
  • Try using a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause friction and lead to frizz.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques, such as finger coiling or plopping, to find what works best for your hair.
  • Be gentle when detangling your hair, using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to prevent breakage.

FAQ

Q: Can cutting my hair shorter in the back help prevent shrinkage?

A: No, cutting your hair shorter in the back will not prevent shrinkage. This is a natural characteristic of curly hair.

Q: Will using a straightening treatment help prevent shrinkage?

A: No, using a straightening treatment can actually damage your hair and lead to breakage. Embrace your natural curls and work with them, rather than trying to change them.

Q: My hair is still shorter in the back despite following these tips. What can I do?

A: If your hair is still shorter in the back, it may be time to consult a hairdresser who specializes in curly hair. They can assess your hair and provide personalized recommendations.

Hair Care

Taking care of curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve healthy, bouncy curls. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, and incorporating hydrating products like leave-in conditioners and curl creams. With a little patience and experimentation, you can learn to love your natural curls and embrace their unique beauty.
