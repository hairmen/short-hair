Haircuts For Round Faces And Long Hair A Guide For 2023

Written by Bardi Aug 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have a round face and long hair, finding the right haircut can be a challenge. But fear not, there are plenty of hairstyles that can flatter your features and highlight your long locks. In this guide, we'll explore some of the best haircuts for round faces and long hair, along with tips for styling and maintaining your new 'do.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Choose the Right Haircut
  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair
  3. Tips for Maintaining Your Haircut
  4. FAQs About Haircuts for Round Faces and Long Hair
  5. Hair Care Tips for Round Faces and Long Hair

How to Choose the Right Haircut

When considering a haircut for a round face and long hair, the goal is to create balance and elongate the face. Here are some hairstyles to consider:

Long Layers

Long layers are a great option for round faces and long hair because they add movement and texture to the hair. The layers should start around the collarbone to create length and slim down the face.

Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs can help to break up a round face and draw attention to the eyes. Make sure the bangs are long enough to sweep to the side and not cut straight across, which can make the face appear wider.

Face-Framing Layers

Face-framing layers are another great option for round faces because they help to create angles and elongate the face. The layers should start at the cheekbones and frame the face.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair

Once you've chosen your new haircut, it's important to know how to style it. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a formula designed for your hair type. For round faces, it's best to avoid styles that are too sleek or flat, as they can make the face appear wider.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Before using any heat styling tools, make sure to apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will help to prevent damage and keep your hair looking healthy.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a round brush and a blow dryer to dry your hair, focusing on the roots to create volume. For round faces, it's best to avoid a center part, which can make the face appear wider.

Step 4: Use a Curling Iron or Flat Iron

Depending on the style you're going for, you can use a curling iron or flat iron to add waves or straighten your hair. Just be sure to use a heat protectant and not apply too much heat to your hair.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

To keep your style in place, finish with a light mist of hairspray. For round faces, it's best to avoid styles that are too slick or flat, as they can make the face appear wider.

Tips for Maintaining Your Haircut

Now that you have your new haircut, here are some tips for maintaining it:

Trim Your Hair Regularly

To keep your haircut looking fresh and healthy, it's important to trim your hair regularly. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

To keep your hair looking healthy and shiny, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. This will help to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

Avoid Overstyling Your Hair

While it's fun to experiment with different hairstyles, it's important to avoid overstyling your hair. Too much heat or product can damage your hair and make it harder to maintain your new haircut.

FAQs About Haircuts for Round Faces and Long Hair

Q: What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a round face and long hair?

A: It's best to avoid styles that are too sleek or flat, as they can make the face appear wider. Additionally, avoid a center part, which can also make the face appear wider.

Q: How can I make my face appear longer?

A: Long layers and face-framing layers are great options for elongating the face. Additionally, side-swept bangs can help break up a round face and draw attention to the eyes.

Hair Care Tips for Round Faces and Long Hair

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When brushing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and keep your hair looking healthy.

Avoid Overwashing Your Hair

While it's important to keep your hair clean, overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils and leave it looking dry and damaged. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every few days.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays can damage your hair and leave it looking dry and brittle. To protect your hair from the sun, wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

With these tips and hairstyles, you can confidently rock a new look that flatters your round face and long hair. Remember to maintain your new haircut with regular trims and deep conditioning treatments, and avoid overstyling your hair. Happy styling!
