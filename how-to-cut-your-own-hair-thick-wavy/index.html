How To Cut Your Own Hair Thick Wavy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 02, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have thick wavy hair. However, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut your own hair thick wavy, as well as provide tips and answers to frequently asked questions.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Thick Wavy

Cutting your own hair thick wavy can be a challenge, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • A handheld mirror or a larger mirror to see the back of your head

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it's damp. Comb your hair to remove any tangles, and let it air-dry completely before you start cutting.


Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to cut. Start by splitting your hair down the middle from front to back. Then, make a part from ear to ear, creating four sections: two in the front and two in the back.


Step 4: Trim the Ends

Start with the front sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, pulling it straight up. Use your scissors to trim the ends, cutting straight across. Repeat this process with the rest of the front sections, then move on to the back.


Step 5: Cut the Back

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky, but it's doable. Use your handheld mirror or larger mirror to see the back of your head, and take small sections of hair to cut. Remember to cut slowly and check your progress frequently.


Step 6: Blend the Layers

If you want to add layers to your hair, start by focusing on the top layer. Take small sections of hair and hold them straight up, cutting into the ends to add texture and dimension. Blend the layers by cutting into the hair at an angle, creating a seamless transition from one layer to the next.


Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your own hair thick wavy:

  1. Gather your tools
  2. Wash and dry your hair
  3. Section your hair
  4. Trim the ends
  5. Cut the back
  6. Blend the layers

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Thick Wavy

Here are a few tips to help you achieve a great haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly
  • Check your progress frequently by looking in the mirror
  • Use a comb to create a guide when cutting long layers
  • Invest in high-quality scissors to achieve a clean cut

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if I have thick wavy hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have thick wavy hair. It may take some practice, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a great haircut.

What if I mess up my haircut?

If you're not happy with your haircut, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.


Hair Care for Thick Wavy Hair

Caring for your thick wavy hair is important to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair to prevent breakage

By following these tips and using the right technique, you can cut your own hair thick wavy and achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home.
