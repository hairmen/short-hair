How To Do 50S Curls On Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 18, 2023 · 3 min read

If you're a fan of vintage hairstyles, you've probably been admiring the classic 50s curls. These bouncy, voluminous curls were a staple during the golden era of Hollywood and are still popular today. If you have long hair, you might be wondering if you can achieve this look without chopping off your locks. The good news is that it's possible! In this article, we'll show you how to do 50s curls on long hair.

What You'll Need

Before we get started, let's gather the tools and products you'll need to achieve this hairstyle:

  • Heat protectant spray
  • Curling iron (preferably a 1-inch barrel)
  • Hairspray
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Bobby pins

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve the perfect 50s curls:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to your damp hair to protect it from heat damage. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to add volume.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use sectioning clips to hold the sections in place.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Starting with the bottom section, take a 1-inch section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron. Hold it for 10-15 seconds before releasing. Repeat this step until you've curled all your hair.

Step 4: Pin Your Curls

Once you've curled all your hair, use bobby pins to pin the curls in place. This will help them set and cool down.

Step 5: Release Your Curls

After 10-15 minutes, remove the bobby pins and release your curls. Use your fingers to loosen them up and add volume.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Use a comb to tease the hair at the crown of your head to add height and volume. Spray your hair with hairspray to set the curls and hold the shape.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect 50s Curls

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 50s curls:

  • Use a 1-inch curling iron to create tight curls that will hold their shape.
  • Pin your curls in place to help them set and cool down.
  • Tease the hair at the crown of your head to add height and volume.
  • Use hairspray to set your curls and hold the shape.

FAQs

Can I achieve 50s curls on long hair?

Yes! You can achieve 50s curls on long hair by using a curling iron and sectioning your hair into parts.

What size curling iron should I use?

Use a 1-inch curling iron to create tight curls that will hold their shape.

How do I make my curls last longer?

Use hairspray to set your curls and hold the shape. You can also pin your curls in place to help them set and cool down.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and strong, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve the perfect 50s curls on your long hair. Whether you're getting ready for a special occasion or just want to switch up your hairstyle, this vintage look is sure to turn heads.
