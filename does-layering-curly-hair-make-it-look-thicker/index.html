Does Layering Curly Hair Make It Look Thicker

Written by Daniel Aug 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are someone with naturally curly hair, you may have wondered whether or not layering your hair could make it look thicker. Layering is a popular technique that is used to add volume and texture to hair, but does it work for curly hair? In this blog post, we will explore this question and provide you with some tips on how to achieve thicker-looking hair through layering.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Layer Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Curly Hair
  • FAQs

Description

In general, layering curly hair can make it look thicker. When you have curly hair, the curls tend to clump together, which can create gaps between the hair strands. These gaps can make your hair look thinner than it actually is. By adding layers to your hair, you can create more movement and volume, which can help to fill in these gaps and make your hair look fuller and thicker.

How to Layer Curly Hair

Layering curly hair is not as simple as just cutting off some length. When layering curly hair, you need to take into account the natural curl pattern and the shape of the face. Here are some tips to help you get started:

1. Choose a Professional Stylist

When it comes to layering curly hair, it's important to find a stylist who has experience working with curly hair. Look for someone who specializes in cutting curly hair and has a portfolio of clients with similar hair types.

2. Consider the Face Shape

The shape of your face can play a big role in the type of layers that will work best for you. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to add layers that frame the face and create more length. If you have a square face, you may want to add layers that soften the angles of the jawline.

3. Work with the Natural Curl Pattern

When layering curly hair, it's important to work with the natural curl pattern. Cutting against the curl pattern can create frizz and make the hair look uneven. Your stylist should work with the natural curl pattern to create layers that enhance the curls and create more volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to layering curly hair:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's best to start with clean, dry hair when layering curly hair. This will give your stylist a better idea of how the hair naturally falls and curls.

2. Section the Hair

Section the hair into four or five sections, depending on the thickness of your hair. Use clips to secure each section.

3. Cut the Layers

Starting with the bottom section, use a razor or scissors to cut the layers. Your stylist should work with the natural curl pattern and cut each layer at a slightly different length to create movement and volume. Repeat this process for each section.

4. Blend the Layers

Once all of the layers have been cut, your stylist should blend them together to create a seamless look. This will help to avoid any harsh lines or unevenness.

Tips for Layering Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve thicker-looking hair through layering:

  • Use a diffuser when drying your hair to enhance the natural curl pattern and create more volume.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down the hair and make it look flat.
  • Consider adding highlights or lowlights to create more depth and dimension.

FAQs

Can layering curly hair make it look thinner?

If not done properly, layering curly hair can actually make it look thinner. It's important to work with a professional stylist who has experience working with curly hair to ensure that the layers are cut in a way that enhances the natural curl pattern and creates more volume.

How often should I get my curly hair layered?

The frequency of layering curly hair depends on the individual's hair growth and maintenance routine. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep the hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Layered Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, thick-looking curly hair. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools that can damage the hair and create frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance the natural curl pattern and create more volume.

Overall, layering curly hair can be a great way to add volume and texture to your hair. By working with a professional stylist and following these tips for hair care, you can achieve thicker-looking hair that is healthy and beautiful.
