Introduction

Body hair is a topic that has been debated for centuries. Some people believe that body hair is unsightly and should be removed, while others believe that it is a natural part of the human body and should be left alone. The Bible has several references to body hair, and it is important to understand what it says about the topic.

Body Hair in the Old Testament

The Old Testament has several references to body hair, and it is clear that the ancient Israelites had a different perspective on body hair than we do today. In Leviticus 19:27, it says, "You shall not round off the hair on your temples or mar the edges of your beard." This passage suggests that men should not shave their beards, and that they should leave their hair intact.

However, there are some exceptions to this rule. In Numbers 6:5, it says, "All the days of his vow of separation, no razor shall touch his head. Until the time is completed for which he separates himself to the Lord, he shall be holy. He shall let the locks of hair of his head grow long." This passage suggests that men who are making a vow to the Lord should not cut their hair.

Body Hair in the New Testament

The New Testament does not have many references to body hair, but it does have a passage that suggests that men should not have long hair. In 1 Corinthians 11:14-15, it says, "Does not nature itself teach you that if a man wears long hair it is a disgrace for him, but if a woman has long hair, it is her glory? For her hair is given to her for a covering."

It is important to note that this passage is not specifically referring to body hair, but rather the length of hair on a person's head. However, it does suggest that there were certain cultural expectations for men and women regarding their hair.

Tips for Taking Care of Body Hair

Whether you choose to remove your body hair or leave it intact, it is important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for taking care of body hair:

  • Use a gentle soap or body wash when showering
  • Trim body hair with scissors or clippers instead of shaving
  • Moisturize skin after shaving or trimming
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or products on body hair
  • Consult with a dermatologist if you have any concerns about your body hair
