How To Cut A Shaggy Haircut For Boys

Written by Robby Oct 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Shaggy haircuts are a popular choice for boys who want a casual and messy look. This style can be achieved with a little bit of effort and the right technique. In this article, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of how to cut a shaggy haircut for boys. We will also provide tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you maintain this trendy hairstyle.

Step-by-Step Guide

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Follow these steps to cut a shaggy haircut for boys:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your child's hair before you start cutting. Wet hair can be deceiving, and you might end up cutting more than you intended. Dry hair will give you a better idea of how the hair will fall after it's cut.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections starting at the crown of the head. Use the comb to create a straight line across the back of the head. The line should be about an inch above the nape of the neck.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Cut the hair in a straight line across the back of the head. Use the comb to hold the hair straight while cutting. You can use a longer or shorter length, depending on how long you want the hair to be.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Start at the bottom of one side of the head and work your way up. Cut the hair at an angle, following the shape of the head. Use the first side as a guide for the second side, making sure the lengths are even.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Take the top section and comb it straight up. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the front and working your way back. The longer the hair, the more layers you can create for a shaggier look.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Use the scissors to blend the layers together. You can do this by cutting into the hair at a 45-degree angle, making sure not to cut too much off. This will create a seamless blend between the layers.

Step 7: Check for Unevenness

Check the haircut for any unevenness or missed spots. Use the scissors to trim any areas that need it.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Style the hair by using your fingers to add texture and volume. Apply a small amount of styling product to keep the hair in place.

Tips for Cutting a Shaggy Haircut for Boys

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect shaggy haircut:

  • Start with longer hair and gradually cut more off as needed.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair if it starts to dry out while cutting.
  • Cut the hair in small sections to ensure evenness.
  • Use thinning shears to create a more textured look.
  • Don't be afraid to make mistakes - hair grows back!

FAQs

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how fast their hair grows and how short you want it to be. On average, boys should get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

What if my child won't sit still during the haircut?

Try distracting them with a toy or a movie. You can also have them hold a mirror so they can see what you're doing.

Can I cut a shaggy haircut without scissors?

No, scissors are necessary for cutting hair. Clippers are not recommended for a shaggy haircut as they can leave harsh lines.

Hair Care for a Shaggy Haircut

To keep your child's shaggy haircut looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash their hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle their hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on their hair, as it can damage the ends.
  • Trim the ends every 4-6 weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Apply a styling product to keep the hair in place.

By following these steps, you can easily cut a shaggy haircut for your child. Remember to take your time and use the right tools to achieve the desired look. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting shaggy haircuts in no time!
