Is Point Cutting Bad For Your Hair

Written by Eveline Nov 07, 2023 · 4 min read

In this day and age, there are many different hair cutting techniques that are used by hairstylists all over the world. One of the most popular techniques is point cutting. However, many people wonder if point cutting is bad for their hair. In this article, we will explore this question in detail and provide you with some tips on how to care for your hair if you have point cut hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Point Cut Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Caring for Point Cut Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Point Cut Hair

Description

Point cutting is a technique where the tips of the hair are cut at an angle, rather than straight across. This creates a textured and layered look, which is often favored by those who have fine or thin hair. By removing some of the weight from the hair, point cutting can give the appearance of more volume and movement.

However, some people worry that point cutting can be damaging to their hair. This is because the technique involves cutting into the hair shaft, which can cause split ends and other damage. So, is point cutting bad for your hair?

How to Point Cut Your Hair

If you are interested in trying out point cutting on your own hair, there are a few things you should keep in mind. First, make sure you have a sharp pair of scissors that are designed for cutting hair. Blunt scissors can cause more damage to your hair, so invest in a good pair.

Next, be sure to practice on a small section of hair before you start cutting your entire head. This will help you get a feel for the technique and ensure that you are cutting evenly.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to point cutting your hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Separate a small section of hair that you want to point cut.
  3. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and twist it slightly.
  4. Using your scissors, cut into the twisted section of hair at an angle.
  5. Repeat this process on the rest of your hair, being careful to cut evenly.

Tips for Caring for Point Cut Hair

If you have point cut hair, there are a few things you can do to care for it and keep it looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is point cutting bad for your hair?

Point cutting can be damaging to your hair if it is not done properly. It is important to use sharp scissors and to practice on a small section of hair before cutting your entire head.

What are the benefits of point cutting?

Point cutting can create a textured and layered look that can give the appearance of more volume and movement. It is often favored by those who have fine or thin hair.

How often should I get my point cut hair trimmed?

You should get your hair trimmed every 6 to 8 weeks to keep it looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care for Point Cut Hair

If you have point cut hair, it is important to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh and healthy, and avoid brushing your hair when it is wet to prevent breakage.

In conclusion, point cutting can be a great way to create a textured and layered look for your hair. However, it is important to use the proper technique and to take care of your hair properly to prevent damage. With the right care, point cut hair can look healthy and beautiful.
