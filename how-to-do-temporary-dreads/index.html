How To Do Temporary Dreads A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dreadlocks, or dreads, are a popular hairstyle that involves sectioning and twisting the hair into rope-like strands. While traditionally associated with Rastafarianism and the African diaspora, dreads have become a mainstream fashion statement. If you're interested in trying out dreads but don't want to commit to the permanent hairstyle, temporary dreads are a great option. In this article, we'll walk you through how to do temporary dreads in relaxed English language.

How to Create Temporary Dreads

To create temporary dreads, you'll need the following:

  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Hair ties
  • Hair wax or gel

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, then let it air dry or blow dry it. Avoid using any styling products, as they can make it difficult to section and twist your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into small sections. The size of each section will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be. Use clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Twist Each Section

Apply hair wax or gel to each section of hair. Use your fingers to twist each section of hair until it starts to form a rope-like strand. Continue twisting until you reach the end of the section.

Step 4: Secure the Ends

Once you've twisted each section, use a hair tie to secure the end of the dread. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Let Your Dreads Set

Once you've created all of your dreads, let them set for a few hours or overnight. This will help them hold their shape and prevent them from unraveling.

Step 6: Style Your Dreads

Once your dreads have set, you can style them however you like. You can leave them loose or tie them back into a ponytail or bun. You can also add accessories like beads or wraps to give your dreads some extra flair.

Tips for Creating Temporary Dreads

  • Use a small amount of hair wax or gel to avoid making your dreads too heavy or greasy.
  • Be patient when twisting your hair. It can take some time to get the hang of it, but practice makes perfect.
  • Experiment with different section sizes and twisting techniques to find the look that works best for you.
  • Don't leave your dreads in for too long. Temporary dreads can start to look messy and unkempt after a few weeks.

FAQ: Temporary Dreads

How long do temporary dreads last?

Temporary dreads can last anywhere from a few days to a few weeks, depending on how well you take care of them and how fast your hair grows.

Can I wash my hair with temporary dreads?

Yes, you can wash your hair with temporary dreads. However, be gentle when washing and avoid using any harsh shampoos or conditioners that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Hair Care for Temporary Dreads

While temporary dreads don't require as much maintenance as permanent dreads, you still need to take care of your hair to keep your dreads looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hair ties that are too tight, as they can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can create temporary dreads that look great and are easy to maintain. Give it a try and see how you like this popular hairstyle!
