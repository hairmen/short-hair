Does Hair Loss Slow Down After 35

Written by Frank Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, it's common to experience hair loss. In fact, according to the American Academy of Dermatology, by the age of 50, nearly half of all men and women will experience some degree of hair loss. However, many people wonder if hair loss slows down after a certain age, such as 35. In this article, we'll explore whether or not hair loss does slow down after 35, as well as provide tips on how to care for your hair as you age.

  • What Causes Hair Loss?
  • Does Hair Loss Slow Down After 35?
  • How to Care for Aging Hair

What Causes Hair Loss?

Before we can discuss whether or not hair loss slows down after 35, it's important to understand what causes hair loss in the first place. There are several factors that can contribute to hair loss, including:

  • Genetics
  • Hormones
  • Nutrition
  • Stress
  • Medical conditions

Genetics is the most common cause of hair loss, with male and female pattern baldness affecting up to 80% of men and 50% of women by the age of 70. Hormones can also play a role, with changes in estrogen, testosterone, and thyroid hormones all potentially contributing to hair loss. Poor nutrition, high levels of stress, and certain medical conditions, such as alopecia or lupus, can also cause hair loss.

Does Hair Loss Slow Down After 35?

While there is no definitive answer to whether or not hair loss slows down after 35, there is evidence to suggest that it may. One study published in the Journal of Investigative Dermatology found that hair loss rates do slow down as people age. However, this study focused on men, so it's unclear if the same holds true for women.

Additionally, it's important to note that just because hair loss may slow down after 35, it doesn't mean it will stop altogether. Hair loss can still occur at any age, so it's important to take care of your hair and scalp throughout your life.

How to Care for Aging Hair

As we age, our hair and scalp undergo changes that can make our hair more fragile and prone to damage. To care for aging hair, consider the following tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Condition regularly to keep hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently
  • Protect hair from the sun
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Reduce stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques

By taking care of your hair and scalp, you can help prevent further hair loss and keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant as you age.

FAQs

Q: Is hair loss normal as you age?

A: Yes, it's normal to experience some degree of hair loss as you age. However, excessive hair loss may be a sign of an underlying medical condition and should be evaluated by a healthcare professional.

Q: Can hair loss be reversed?

A: In some cases, hair loss can be reversed through medical treatments such as minoxidil or finasteride. However, these treatments may not be effective for everyone and can have side effects.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can contribute to hair loss by disrupting the hair growth cycle. If you're experiencing significant stress, it's important to find ways to manage it, such as through exercise or relaxation techniques.

Q: Is it true that wearing hats can cause hair loss?

A: No, wearing hats does not cause hair loss. However, wearing tight hats or headbands frequently can cause hair breakage and damage.

In conclusion, while there is some evidence to suggest that hair loss may slow down after 35, it's still important to take care of your hair and scalp throughout your life to prevent further hair loss and keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant. By following the tips outlined in this article and consulting with a healthcare professional if you have concerns about hair loss, you can help maintain a full head of hair as you age.
