How To Cut Hair To Shoulder Length At Home

Written by Petter Jul 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of paying high prices for haircuts at salons? With a few tools and some practice, you can learn how to cut your hair to shoulder length at home. Not only will you save money, but you can also customize your haircut to your liking. In this article, we will provide you a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair to shoulder length at home.

Description

The shoulder-length haircut is a popular choice among women. It is a versatile haircut that looks good on all face shapes and hair types. The shoulder-length haircut is also low maintenance and easy to style. However, getting a shoulder-length haircut at a salon can be expensive. By learning how to cut your hair to shoulder length at home, you can save money and time.

How to Cut Hair to Shoulder Length at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Haircutting comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Once you have all the necessary tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Your hair should be clean and dry before you start cutting it. Use a towel or a hair dryer to dry your hair completely.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a haircutting comb to comb your hair. Make sure your hair is tangle-free and smooth.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four equal parts. Use sectioning clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section of your hair. Use the comb to comb your hair straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across. Cut small sections at a time and check the length after each cut.

Step 5: Cut the Side Sections

Move to the side sections of your hair. Use the same technique as the back section and cut the hair straight across. Make sure the length is the same as the back section.

Step 6: Cut the Front Sections

Finally, move to the front sections of your hair. Use the comb to comb your hair straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across. Make sure the length is the same as the side sections.

Step 7: Check the Length

After cutting all sections, check the length of your hair. If you notice any unevenness, trim the hair with scissors.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with the length of your hair, style it as you desire. You can use a curling iron or a straightener to add some texture to your hair.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't add hair back.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is longer than dry hair, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. Small cuts are easier to manage and control.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair to shoulder length if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, it's important to cut curly hair when it's dry. Curly hair tends to shrink when it's dry, and cutting it when it's wet can result in a shorter haircut than intended.

What if I cut my hair too short?

If you cut your hair too short, don't panic. You can always add hair extensions or wear a wig until your hair grows back.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often. Heat can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your hair to shoulder length at home. Remember to take your time and be patient. Happy cutting!
