Can Curly Hair Be Slicked Back A Comprehensive Guide

If you have curly hair, you might have wondered if it is possible to slick it back. The answer is yes, but it requires some effort and the right technique. In this article, we will discuss how to slick back curly hair, step by step, and provide some useful tips to make the process easier. We will also answer some frequently asked questions about slicked-back curly hair and provide some advice on how to care for your curls.

How to Slick Back Curly Hair

The first thing to consider when slicking back curly hair is the type of product to use. You need something that can hold your curls in place without making them crunchy or stiff. A good option is a gel or a pomade that is specifically designed for curly hair. These products will give you the hold you need while allowing your curls to remain soft and bouncy.

Another important consideration is the length of your hair. Slicking back curly hair works best on hair that is at least shoulder-length. If your hair is shorter, you might have difficulty achieving the desired look. If you have very long curly hair, you might need to experiment with different techniques to find the one that works best for you.

Finally, you need to have some patience and be willing to experiment. Slicking back curly hair is not always easy and might require some trial and error. But with the right technique and some practice, you can achieve a sleek and sophisticated look that will turn heads.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to slick back curly hair:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair. If your hair is too dry, it will be difficult to work with. Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your curls and make them more manageable.
  2. Apply a small amount of gel or pomade to your hands and rub them together to distribute the product evenly.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, use your fingers to smooth your hair back. Use a comb to help create a neater look and remove any tangles.
  4. Continue working your way up to the top of your head, using your fingers to smooth your hair back and applying more product as needed.
  5. Once all your hair is slicked back, use a comb to create a part if desired.
  6. Finish with a light hairspray to help hold your curls in place.

Tips for Slicking Back Curly Hair

Here are some useful tips to make the process of slicking back curly hair easier:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before applying any product.
  • Apply product in small amounts to avoid making your hair too greasy or heavy.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help dry your hair without disrupting your curls.
  • Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type and curl pattern.
  • Be patient and take your time. Slicking back curly hair requires some skill and practice.

FAQs

Can you slick back short curly hair?

Slicking back short curly hair can be challenging, but it is possible. You might need to experiment with different products and techniques to find the one that works best for your hair type and length.

Can you slick back very curly hair?

Slicking back very curly hair can be tricky, but it is possible. You might need to use a stronger hold product and apply it in small amounts to avoid making your hair too heavy. You can also try using a hair straightener to help smooth your curls before slicking them back.

How do you prevent frizz when slicking back curly hair?

To prevent frizz when slicking back curly hair, use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your curls and make them more manageable. Avoid using too much product or rubbing your hair too vigorously when drying it. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help dry your hair without disrupting your curls.

Hair Care for Slicked-Back Curly Hair

After slicking back your curly hair, it is important to take care of your curls to keep them healthy and bouncy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and moisturize your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry as it can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls when they are wet.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with UV protection.

Now that you know how to slick back your curly hair, you can experiment with different styles and looks. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to try new products and techniques. With the right approach, you can achieve a sleek and sophisticated look that will turn heads.
