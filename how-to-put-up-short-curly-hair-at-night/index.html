How To Put Up Short Curly Hair At Night A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short curly hair, you know how frustrating it can be to wake up with a tangled mess in the morning. But fear not! With the right technique, you can put up your short curly hair at night to keep it looking great in the morning. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to achieve the perfect nighttime routine for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Prepare Your Hair
  • Step 2: Choose Your Method
  • Step 3: Secure Your Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Short Curly Hair at Night
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Short Curly Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start putting up your hair, you need to make sure it's clean and dry. If you've just washed your hair, wait until it's about 80% dry before putting it up. If your hair is still wet, it may not dry all the way overnight, which can lead to frizz and damage.

Once your hair is dry, you should apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep it moisturized. This will help prevent breakage and tangles while you sleep.

Step 2: Choose Your Method

There are several ways to put up short curly hair at night, depending on your hair type and personal preference. Here are a few options:

Braids:

Braids are a great option for short curly hair, as they help prevent tangles and maintain the curl pattern. Divide your hair into two or three sections and braid each section loosely. Secure the ends with a hair tie or clip.

Pineapple:

The pineapple method involves gathering your hair at the top of your head and securing it with a scrunchie or hair tie. This method works especially well for shorter hair and can help maintain volume and curls.

Bun:

You can also put your hair up in a bun, either high or low, depending on your preference. Use a hair tie or bobby pins to secure the bun in place. This method can help prevent tangling and maintain a defined curl pattern.

Step 3: Secure Your Hair

Once you've chosen your method, make sure to secure your hair properly. Use a hair tie or clip that won't leave a crease or dent in your hair. If you're using a hair tie, make sure it's not too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Make sure your hair is secure but not too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage. If you're using bobby pins, make sure they're not too tight or too loose, as this can cause discomfort while you sleep.

Tips for Maintaining Your Short Curly Hair at Night

Here are a few additional tips to help maintain your short curly hair while you sleep:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent friction and tangles.
  • Avoid using cotton pillowcases, as they can absorb moisture and cause frizz.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair in the morning.

FAQs

Q: How often should I put up my short curly hair at night?

A: This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people prefer to put their hair up every night, while others only do it a few times a week. Experiment to see what works best for you.

Q: Can I put up my short curly hair without damaging it?

A: Yes! As long as you use the right technique and don't pull your hair too tight, putting up your short curly hair at night can actually help prevent damage and breakage.

Hair Care for Short Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful short curly hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage and frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and damage.

With these tips and techniques, you can put up your short curly hair at night and wake up with beautiful, defined curls in the morning. Experiment with different methods to find what works best for your hair type and personal preference, and don't forget to take good care of your hair with proper hair care and maintenance.
