How To Do An Undercut Pixie At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a dramatic haircut that's easy to maintain and style, an undercut pixie might be just what you need. This edgy look is characterized by a short, cropped top and shaved sides and back. While it's typically done in a salon, it is possible to do an undercut pixie at home with the right tools and technique. In this article, we'll teach you how to do an undercut pixie step by step, as well as provide tips and answer frequently asked questions.

Description

The undercut pixie is a bold and daring haircut that's perfect for those who want to make a statement. It involves shaving the sides and back of the head while leaving the top long enough to style. This creates a striking contrast between the two lengths and emphasizes the facial features.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. This includes:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror
  • Towel
  • Shampoo and conditioner

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

To start, you'll want to make sure your hair is clean and dry. Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, then towel dry it. Use the comb to part your hair where you want the undercut to be.

Step 3: Clip the Top Section

Next, use the hair clips to clip the top section of your hair out of the way. This will allow you to work on the shaved sections without cutting any of the longer hair.

Step 4: Choose Your Guard Size

Choose the guard size you want to use based on how short you want the shaved sections to be. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if necessary.

Step 5: Start Shaving

Begin shaving the sides and back of your head with the clippers. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to lift the hair as you go. Be sure to use the same guard size all around to keep the length even.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

Once you've shaved the sections, use the scissors to blend the hair where it meets the longer hair on top. This will create a seamless transition between the two lengths.

Step 7: Style the Top

Unclip the top section of your hair and style it as desired. You can leave it long and tousled for a more feminine look, or slick it back for a more androgynous appearance.

Step 8: Clean Up

After you're finished, use the mirror to check for any missed spots or uneven sections. Clean up any stray hairs with the clippers or scissors, then wash your hair again to remove any loose hairs.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head for missed spots.
  • Consider having a friend or family member help you with the back of your head.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Can I do an undercut pixie on my own?

Yes, it is possible to do an undercut pixie on your own. However, it may be easier to have a friend or family member help you with the back of your head.

What tools do I need to do an undercut pixie?

You will need clippers with guards, scissors, a comb, hair clips, a mirror, a towel, and shampoo and conditioner.

How short should I go with the shaved sections?

The length of the shaved sections will depend on personal preference. It's best to start with a longer guard size and work your way down if necessary.

How do I maintain an undercut pixie?

To maintain an undercut pixie, you will need to trim the shaved sections every few weeks to keep the length even. You can also experiment with different styles and products to keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care

After you've done your undercut pixie, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using heat tools too often. Consider getting regular trims to keep the length even and prevent split ends.

With these tips and steps, you should now have a good understanding of how to do an undercut pixie at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, as it may take a few tries to get it just right. Good luck!
