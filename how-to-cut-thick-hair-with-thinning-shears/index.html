How To Cut Thick Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Frank Sep 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool for hairdressers and individuals alike. They are especially useful for people with thick hair who want to remove some of the bulk without losing length. However, using thinning shears can be tricky, and it’s important to know the proper technique to avoid any unwanted results. In this article, we’ll explore how to cut thick hair with thinning shears in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth or notches on one or both blades. They work by cutting some of the hair but leaving some behind, resulting in a more textured and layered look. This technique is perfect for people with thick hair who want to reduce some of the volume while maintaining the length. Thinning shears can also be used to blend different lengths of hair or create choppy layers.

How to Cut Thick Hair with Thinning Shears

Before using thinning shears, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can exaggerate the effects of thinning shears, and you may end up cutting more than intended.

Start by sectioning your hair into manageable parts. You can use clips or hair ties to keep the sections separate.

Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers at the point where you want to start thinning. Hold the thinning shears at a slight angle and make small snips, moving upward from the ends of the hair. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven and choppy look. Repeat this process until you’ve thinned out the desired amount of hair.

Continue with the rest of your hair, working through each section until you’ve achieved your desired look. Remember to take breaks to rest your hands and avoid fatigue.

Step by Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to cut thick hair with thinning shears:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into manageable parts.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers at the point where you want to start thinning.
  4. Hold the thinning shears at a slight angle and make small snips, moving upward from the ends of the hair.
  5. Repeat this process until you’ve thinned out the desired amount of hair.
  6. Move on to the next section and repeat until you’ve thinned out your entire head of hair.
  7. Take breaks as needed to avoid fatigue.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when using thinning shears:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work slowly.
  • Use a comb to help guide the thinning shears through your hair.
  • Use a light touch to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Don’t thin out your hair too much, as this can result in a frizzy or choppy look.
  • Consider getting your hair thinned out by a professional stylist if you’re new to using thinning shears.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting thick hair with thinning shears:

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

While thinning shears can be used on all hair types, they are most effective on thick, heavy hair.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it’s best to use thinning shears every 6-8 weeks to maintain your desired look.

Can thinning shears damage my hair?

When used correctly, thinning shears should not damage your hair. However, if used improperly or too frequently, they can cause split ends or breakage.

Hair Care

After using thinning shears on your hair, it’s important to take good care of it to avoid any damage or breakage. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot styling tools.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, especially when it’s wet.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using thinning shears correctly, you can achieve a beautiful, textured look that’s perfect for thick hair. Good luck!
