How To Add Loose Curls To Straight Hair A Beginner s Guide

Written by Frank Aug 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have naturally straight hair, you may have wondered how to add some texture and volume to your locks. Loose curls are a great way to achieve a more voluminous and playful look, and they're surprisingly easy to create! In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you add loose curls to your straight hair.

Description

Loose curls are a type of hairstyle that involves hair that is slightly wavy or curled, but not tightly coiled. This style can be achieved by using a curling iron or other heat styling tools, or by using more natural methods like braiding or twisting damp hair and letting it air dry.

How to Add Loose Curls to Straight Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start curling your hair, it's important to make sure it's clean and dry. If your hair is wet or damp, it won't hold the curl as well. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to help smooth and protect your hair, and take care not to use too many styling products that can weigh it down.

Step 2: Choose Your Tool

There are several different tools you can use to create loose curls, including curling irons, straighteners, and hot rollers. Each tool has its own pros and cons, so it's important to choose the one that works best for your hair type and the look you want to achieve.

Curling Iron: A curling iron is a great option if you want more control over the size and shape of your curls. Choose a barrel size that matches the size of the curls you want, and be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Straightener: Believe it or not, you can actually use a straightener to create loose curls! Simply clamp a small section of hair between the plates, twist the straightener 180 degrees, and pull it down the length of your hair.

Hot Rollers: Hot rollers are a great option if you want a more natural-looking curl. Simply roll small sections of hair around the hot rollers, let them cool, and then remove them for bouncy, voluminous curls.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Once you've chosen your tool, it's time to start curling! Here's a basic technique you can use:

  1. Start by dividing your hair into small sections, about 1-2 inches wide.
  2. Wrap each section of hair around the barrel of your curling iron or hot roller.
  3. Hold the hair in place for 10-15 seconds, then release it.
  4. Repeat this process until all of your hair is curled.

Remember to curl your hair away from your face for a more flattering look, and be sure to curl the ends of your hair for a more natural, tousled effect.

Step 4: Finish Your Look

Once you've curled your hair, it's time to finish your look. You can use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture, and you can also use a light hairspray to help hold your curls in place.

Tips for Adding Loose Curls to Straight Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you achieve the perfect loose curls:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Don't curl your hair too tightly, or you'll end up with more of a ringlet effect.
  • Try using different sized barrels to create a more natural-looking curl.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different techniques, like braiding or twisting your hair and letting it air dry.
  • Use a boar bristle brush to gently brush out your curls for a softer, more natural look.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about adding loose curls to straight hair:

Can I add loose curls to my hair if it's already curly?

It's definitely possible to add more definition and volume to curly hair, but you may need to use different techniques than those outlined in this article. Look for tutorials specifically designed for curly hair to get the best results.

How long will my loose curls last?

How long your curls last will depend on a variety of factors, including your hair type, the humidity, and the products you use. Generally, loose curls will last anywhere from a few hours to a few days.

Hair Care for Adding Loose Curls to Straight Hair

Adding loose curls to your straight hair can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your look. To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, be sure to:

  • Use high-quality, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too many styling products that can weigh your hair down
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you

With a little practice and patience, you'll be able to add loose curls to your straight hair like a pro!
