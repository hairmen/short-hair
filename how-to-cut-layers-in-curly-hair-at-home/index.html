How To Cut Layers In Curly Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can also be challenging to maintain. One of the best ways to add shape and definition to curly hair is by adding layers. But, going to the salon every few months can be expensive. The good news is, you can cut layers in curly hair at home with the right tools and techniques. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of cutting layers in curly hair at home.

How to Cut Layers in Curly Hair at Home

Before we get started, there are a few things you need to keep in mind:

  • Make sure your hair is dry and detangled before you start cutting.
  • Use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length.

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 2: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of the hair between your fingers, and cut a straight line. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you finish the bottom layer.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Layer

Move on to the middle layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle. This will create a soft, blended look. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you finish the middle layer.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of the hair between your fingers and cut at a steeper angle. This will create more defined layers. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you finish the top layer.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Curly Hair at Home

  • Don't cut too much hair at once. Start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • When in doubt, cut less. You can always go back and cut more later.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQs

Can you cut layers in naturally curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in naturally curly hair. In fact, adding layers can help enhance the shape and definition of curly hair.

What type of scissors should I use to cut my curly hair?

Use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Blunt scissors can cause split ends and damage to your hair.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It depends on your hair growth rate and the style you're going for. As a general rule, it's recommended to get a trim every 8-12 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Now that you know how to cut layers in curly hair at home, it's important to take care of your curls to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for curly hair care:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools. Heat can cause damage and frizz to curly hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Brushing curly hair can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair to help define your curls.
  • Avoid using towels to dry your hair. Instead, use a cotton t-shirt or a microfiber towel to gently squeeze out excess water.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut layers in curly hair at home and keep your curls looking beautiful and healthy. Remember to take your time and start with small cuts. And, most importantly, have fun experimenting with your curly hair!
