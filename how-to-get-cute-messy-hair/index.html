How To Get Cute Messy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Description:

Are you tired of your hair looking too perfect and polished? Do you want to achieve that effortless, messy look that's so popular right now? Look no further! This step-by-step guide will show you how to get cute messy hair that's perfect for any occasion.

How to:

Step 1: Start with Dirty Hair

If you want to achieve that perfect, messy look, you need to start with hair that's a little bit dirty. Clean hair tends to be too slippery and won't hold a style as well. If you're starting with clean hair, use a dry shampoo or texturizing spray to add some grit and hold.

Step 2: Add Texture

Next, you'll want to add some texture to your hair to help it hold the style. You can use a texturizing spray, sea salt spray, or even a little bit of hair wax. Apply the product to your hair and scrunch it up with your hands to create some tousled, messy texture.

Step 3: Create Loose Waves

Using a curling iron or wand, create loose waves throughout your hair. Don't worry about making them too perfect - the messier, the better. If you don't have a curling iron, you can create waves using braids. Simply braid your hair while it's damp and leave it in overnight. When you take the braids out in the morning, you'll have beautiful, beachy waves.

Step 4: Tease Your Hair

To create more volume and texture, use a teasing comb to backcomb your hair at the roots. Start at the crown of your head and work your way down, teasing small sections of hair at a time. Be gentle - you don't want to damage your hair. Once you've teased the roots, use your fingers to gently smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section.

Step 5: Add Volume

If you want even more volume, you can use a volumizing powder or mousse. Apply the product to your roots and massage it in with your fingers. This will help lift your hair and make it look fuller and more voluminous.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

To set your style in place, finish with a light mist of hairspray. Hold the can about 10 inches away from your head and spray in a sweeping motion. Don't overdo it - you want your hair to look natural and effortless, not stiff and crunchy.

Tips for Getting Cute Messy Hair

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush. Brushes can make your hair too smooth and polished.
  • Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair. Some people find that salt sprays work better than texturizing sprays, for example.
  • Don't be afraid to embrace your natural texture. If you have curly or wavy hair, work with it rather than against it.
  • For an even messier look, try pulling a few strands of hair out of your updo or ponytail.

FAQs About Cute Messy Hair

Q: Can I achieve cute messy hair with short hair?

A: Absolutely! Short hair can be even easier to style in a messy way. Use a texturizing spray and your fingers to create a tousled, beachy look.

Q: Can I get cute messy hair without using heat?

A: Yes! As mentioned earlier, you can create waves using braids. You can also try a twist-out or bantu knots to create texture without heat.

Hair Care for Messy Hair

Messy hair can be a lot of fun, but it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Don't wash your hair too often - aim for 2-3 times per week. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it more difficult to style.
  • When you do wash your hair, use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh your hair down.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve cute messy hair in no time. Have fun experimenting with different products and techniques, and don't be afraid to make it your own!
