How To Get Rid Of Brassy Hair After Bleaching

Written by Bardi Sep 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you've recently bleached your hair, you may have noticed that it has turned brassy. This is a common problem that many people face after bleaching their hair, especially if they have naturally dark hair. The good news is that there are ways to get rid of brassy hair and restore your hair's natural shine and color. In this article, we will discuss how to get rid of brassy hair after bleaching.

How to

There are several ways to get rid of brassy hair after bleaching. Some of these methods include using toners, purple shampoos, and hair masks.

1. Use a Toner

A toner is a product that helps to neutralize the brassy tones in your hair. You can find toners at your local beauty supply store or online. To use a toner, follow these steps:

  1. Shampoo your hair as you normally would.
  2. Apply the toner to your hair, making sure to saturate it evenly.
  3. Leave the toner on your hair for the recommended amount of time.
  4. Rinse the toner out of your hair with cool water.

You can use a toner once a week or as needed to keep your hair looking its best.

2. Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a product that is designed to neutralize brassy tones in blonde or bleached hair. You can find purple shampoos at your local beauty supply store or online. To use a purple shampoo, follow these steps:

  1. Wet your hair.
  2. Apply the purple shampoo to your hair and massage it in.
  3. Leave the shampoo on your hair for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Rinse the shampoo out of your hair with cool water.

You can use a purple shampoo once a week or as needed to keep your hair looking its best.

3. Use a Hair Mask

A hair mask is a deep conditioning treatment that can help to restore your hair's natural shine and color. You can find hair masks at your local beauty supply store or online. To use a hair mask, follow these steps:

  1. Shampoo your hair as you normally would.
  2. Apply the hair mask to your hair and massage it in.
  3. Leave the hair mask on your hair for the recommended amount of time.
  4. Rinse the hair mask out of your hair with cool water.

You can use a hair mask once a week or as needed to keep your hair looking its best.

Step by Step

Step 1: Shampoo Your Hair

Start by shampooing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will help to remove any buildup from styling products or hard water that may be making your hair look brassy.

Step 2: Apply a Toner

Apply a toner to your hair, making sure to saturate it evenly. Leave the toner on your hair for the recommended amount of time. Rinse the toner out of your hair with cool water.

Step 3: Use a Purple Shampoo

Wet your hair and apply the purple shampoo to your hair. Massage it in and leave it on your hair for 3-5 minutes. Rinse the shampoo out of your hair with cool water.

Step 4: Apply a Hair Mask

Apply a hair mask to your hair and massage it in. Leave the hair mask on your hair for the recommended amount of time. Rinse the hair mask out of your hair with cool water.

Tips

Here are some tips to help prevent brassy hair in the future:

  • Use a purple shampoo once a week to help neutralize brassy tones.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as they can cause damage and make your hair look brassy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look dull and brassy.

FAQ

Q: Can I use lemon juice to get rid of brassy hair?

A: While lemon juice can help to lighten your hair, it can also make it more prone to damage and breakage. It's best to use a toner, purple shampoo, or hair mask to get rid of brassy hair.

Q: How often should I use a toner or purple shampoo?

A: You can use a toner or purple shampoo once a week or as needed to keep your hair looking its best.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important, especially if you've recently bleached it. Here are some tips to help keep your hair healthy and hydrated:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools on your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

By following these tips and using the methods outlined in this article, you can get rid of brassy hair after bleaching and restore your hair's natural shine and color.
