How To Cut Your Own Hair Short Back And Sides With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money and time by cutting your own hair at home, you're not alone. With the right tools and technique, cutting your own hair can be a simple and rewarding experience. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut your own hair short back and sides with scissors, including everything from the equipment you'll need to the techniques you should use.

Table of Contents

  • Equipment Needed
  • Preparation
  • Cutting Technique
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Equipment Needed

Before you get started, you'll need to make sure you have the right equipment on-hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror (ideally one that can be positioned at different angles)
  • Hair clippers (optional, but can be helpful for achieving an even cut)

Preparation

Once you have your equipment ready, it's time to prepare your hair for cutting. Here are the steps you should follow:

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry completely. This will help you see any natural cowlicks or growth patterns that may affect how you cut your hair.
  2. Brush or comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. If you're using hair clippers, attach the desired guard to the clippers.

Cutting Technique

Now it's time to actually start cutting your hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by using your comb to create a part in your hair where you want the cut to begin. For a short back and sides cut, this will typically be on the side of your head, just above your ear.
  2. Hold the hair above the parting between your fingers and use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. It's best to start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length, to avoid accidentally cutting off too much hair.
  3. Repeat this process, working your way around your head and cutting the hair to the same length on each side.
  4. If you're using hair clippers, use them to tidy up the back and sides of your hair, making sure to blend the hair length smoothly from the bottom to the top.

Tips for Success

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Start with a longer length than you think you want, as you can always go back and cut more off if needed.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go, making sure the length on both sides is even.
  • Take breaks if you need to, as cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair short back and sides with scissors:

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

You can cut your hair wet or dry, depending on your preference. Just keep in mind that wet hair will appear longer than dry hair, so if you're cutting it wet, be sure to cut it slightly shorter than the desired length.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

It's best to use hair-cutting scissors, as they're sharper and designed specifically for cutting hair. However, if you don't have hair-cutting scissors, you can use regular scissors in a pinch.

How often should I cut my hair?

This will depend on your hair type and desired style. Generally speaking, most people need a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain their desired style.

Hair Care After Cutting

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair to remove any loose hairs.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Style your hair as desired, using a hair gel or wax if needed.

By following these steps, you should be able to successfully cut your own hair short back and sides with scissors. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it!
