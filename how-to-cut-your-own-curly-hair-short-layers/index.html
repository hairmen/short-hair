How To Cut Your Own Curly Hair Short Layers

Written by William Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair and want to give it a fresh new look, cutting it into short layers can be a great option. Not only does it add volume and texture to your hair, but it also helps to frame your face. If you're looking to cut your own curly hair into short layers, this guide will teach you how to do it step-by-step.

Description

Short layers are an excellent way to add volume and texture to curly hair. This is because the layers allow for more movement and bounce, as well as creating a more defined shape. Cutting your own curly hair into short layers can be a bit daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done.

How to Cut Your Own Curly Hair Short Layers

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. Here's how to cut your own curly hair short layers:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it dry naturally. This will help you see how your curls naturally fall and make it easier to cut them.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Clip each section to keep them separate.

Step 3: Cut the Back Layers

Start with the back sections. Take a small section of hair and twist it until it's tight. Then, cut the ends of the hair at an angle, following the shape of the twist. Continue cutting small sections of hair in this way until you've cut all the back layers.

Step 4: Cut the Front Layers

Next, move on to the front sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and pull it forward. Twist it until it's tight and cut the ends at an angle, following the shape of the twist. Repeat this process until you've cut all the front layers.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use your comb to blend them together. This will help create a seamless look and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Take a look in the mirror and make sure your layers are even. If you notice any areas that need a bit of tweaking, go ahead and trim them.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your layers, style your hair as desired. You can let your curls air dry, or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls.

Tips

Cutting your own hair can be tricky, so here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair into short layers:

Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

You can cut your curly hair when it's wet, but keep in mind that it will shrink when it dries. If you want to see how your hair will look when it's dry, it's best to cut it when it's dry.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 8-12 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own hair if I have no experience?

If you're new to cutting hair, it's best to start with small trims and work your way up. Cutting your hair into short layers can be a bit more challenging, so it's important to take your time and be patient.

Hair Care

To keep your curly hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a deep conditioner once a week to help moisturize and nourish your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

Cutting your own curly hair into short layers can be a fun way to switch up your look. By following these steps and taking good care of your hair, you can achieve a beautiful and healthy head of curls.
