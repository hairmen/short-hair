How To Cut Shorter Layers On Top Of Head A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your flat, lifeless hair? Adding shorter layers on top of your head can give you the volume and texture you crave. Cutting your own hair may seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done successfully. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps on how to cut shorter layers on top of your head.

Description

Shorter layers on top of your head can bring life to your hair by adding volume and texture. This haircut can work for any hair type, from fine hair to thick hair. Cutting shorter layers on top of your head can be a bit tricky, but it can be done with the right tools and technique. This guide will help you achieve the perfect haircut at home.

How To

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's important to work with clean, dry hair, as wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.

Step 1: Part Your Hair

Start by parting your hair down the middle. Use the comb to create a straight and even part. Then, section your hair into two parts by clipping the hair on one side away from the other.

Step 2: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how short you want your layers to be. It's important to keep in mind that hair will appear shorter once it's dry, so it's best to start with longer layers and gradually cut more if needed.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Start by cutting the first layer. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut it at the desired length. Make sure to cut straight across and avoid any jagged edges.

Step 4: Continue Cutting Layers

Continue cutting layers around your head, working from the front to the back. Make sure to keep the sections of hair even in size and length. It's important to check the length of each layer as you go to ensure they are even.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you have finished cutting all the layers, it's important to blend them together. Take the comb and comb through your hair to blend the layers together. If you notice any unevenness, use the scissors to trim any stray hairs.

Tips

- Start with longer layers and gradually cut more if needed.
- Use sharp scissors to avoid any jagged edges.
- Check the length of each layer as you go to ensure they are even.
- Comb through your hair after cutting to blend the layers together.

FAQ

Can I cut shorter layers on top of my head with curly hair?

Yes, you can cut shorter layers on top of your head with curly hair. It's important to cut your hair when it's dry to avoid any shrinkage. Use the same technique as described above, but be mindful of the curls when cutting.

How often should I cut my hair to maintain shorter layers on top of my head?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your haircut. However, the frequency of your haircuts will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Avoid heat styling as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

With this step-by-step guide, you can achieve shorter layers on top of your head at home. Remember to use the proper tools and technique, and always start with longer layers to avoid any mistakes. With a little practice, you can become a pro at cutting your own hair.
