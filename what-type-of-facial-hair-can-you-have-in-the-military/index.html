What Type Of Facial Hair Can You Have In The Military

Written by William Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are planning to join the military, you might have questions about the grooming standards that you need to follow. One of the most common questions is about facial hair. What type of facial hair can you have in the military? In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about the grooming standards in the military.

Description

The grooming standards in the military are strict, and they are designed to maintain a professional appearance. The military has different grooming standards for men and women. Men are required to shave every day, and the hair on their head and face must be neatly groomed. Facial hair is allowed in some cases, but it must be kept within certain parameters.

How to

If you are planning to grow facial hair in the military, you need to follow the guidelines set by your branch of service. Here are the guidelines for each branch:

Army

The Army allows mustaches, but they must be neatly trimmed and not extend beyond the corners of the mouth. Beards are not allowed, except for medical reasons or when a soldier is granted a waiver.

Navy

The Navy also allows mustaches, but they must be neatly groomed and not extend beyond the upper lip. Beards are not allowed, except for medical reasons or when a sailor is granted a waiver.

Air Force

The Air Force allows mustaches, but they must be neatly trimmed and not extend beyond the corners of the mouth. Beards are not allowed, except for medical reasons or when an airman is granted a waiver.

Marines

The Marines allow mustaches, but they must be neatly trimmed and not extend beyond the corners of the mouth. Beards are not allowed, except for religious reasons or when a Marine is granted a waiver.

Coast Guard

The Coast Guard allows mustaches, but they must be neatly groomed and not extend beyond the corners of the mouth. Beards are not allowed, except for medical reasons or when a Coast Guardsman is granted a waiver.

Step by Step

If you are planning to grow a mustache in the military, here are the steps you need to follow:

  1. Start by growing a full beard.
  2. Trim the beard down to a mustache.
  3. Use a trimmer to keep the mustache neatly trimmed.
  4. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs.
  5. Shave any hair that is outside the parameters set by your branch of service.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when growing facial hair in the military:

  • Follow the guidelines set by your branch of service.
  • Keep your facial hair neatly groomed at all times.
  • Shave any hair that is outside the parameters set by your branch of service.
  • Be aware that your commanding officer has the final say on your grooming standards.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about facial hair in the military:

Can I have a beard in the military?

Beards are not allowed in the military, except for medical reasons or when a soldier, sailor, airman, Marine or Coast Guardsman is granted a waiver.

Can I have a mustache in the military?

Most branches of service allow mustaches, but they must be neatly trimmed and not extend beyond the corners of the mouth.

Can I have a goatee in the military?

No, goatees are not allowed in the military.

Hair Care

To keep your facial hair looking neat and tidy, you need to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your facial hair regularly with shampoo.
  • Use a beard balm or oil to keep your beard soft and moisturized.
  • Brush your beard regularly to keep it looking neat.
  • Trim your beard regularly to keep it looking tidy.

Remember, following the grooming standards in the military is important. By keeping your facial hair neatly groomed, you can maintain a professional appearance and show your commitment to your branch of service.
