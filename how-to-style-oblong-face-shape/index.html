How To Style Oblong Face Shape Tips Tricks And Hair Care

If you have an oblong face shape, you may feel like your options for hairstyles are limited. However, there are plenty of styles that flatter your face shape and highlight your features. In this blog post, we'll give you tips and tricks for styling your oblong face shape, step-by-step instructions for creating popular hairstyles, and advice on hair care to keep your locks looking their best.

Section 1: Understanding Your Oblong Face Shape

Before we dive into hairstyles, it's essential to understand what an oblong face shape is. An oblong face is longer than it is wide, with a prominent chin and forehead. The cheekbones are typically not very noticeable, and the face has a straight, linear appearance. If you have an oblong face, your goal is to add width to your face with your hairstyle and makeup.

Section 2: How to Style an Oblong Face Shape

When it comes to styling an oblong face, you want to create the illusion of width. Here are some styles that work well:

1. Bangs

Bangs are a great way to shorten the appearance of a long forehead and add width to the face. Try side-swept bangs or blunt bangs that hit just above the eyebrows.

2. Layers

Layered haircuts can add volume and dimension to an oblong face shape. Ask your stylist for long layers that start at the cheekbones or chin.

3. Waves and Curls

Soft waves and curls can add volume and width to your face. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to create loose curls or waves.

4. Bob Haircuts

A chin-length bob can help balance out a long face. Opt for a blunt cut or a choppy texture to add volume and width.

When styling your hair, avoid hairstyles that pull all of your hair back tightly, as this can accentuate the length of your face. Instead, try a loose updo or a half-up, half-down style.

Section 3: Step-by-Step Hairstyle Tutorials for Oblong Faces

1. Side-Swept Bangs

To create side-swept bangs, start by parting your hair to the side. Take a small section of hair from the front of your hairline and comb it forward. Use scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the outer corner of one eyebrow and ending at the outer corner of the other eyebrow. Repeat on the other side of your face.

2. Layered Haircut

To create a layered haircut, ask your stylist for long layers that start at your cheekbones or chin. Blow-dry your hair with a round brush, focusing on adding volume at the crown of your head. Finish with a light hairspray.

3. Loose Waves

To create loose waves, start by applying a heat protectant to your hair. Use a curling iron to curl small sections of your hair away from your face. Once you've curled all of your hair, use your fingers to gently separate the curls and create a tousled look. Finish with a texturizing spray.

4. Chin-Length Bob

To create a chin-length bob, ask your stylist for a blunt cut that hits just below your chin. Blow-dry your hair with a round brush, focusing on adding volume at the crown of your head. Finish with a light hairspray.

Section 4: Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Maintaining healthy hair is essential for any hairstyle to look its best. Here are some tips:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are sulfate-free.

2. Limit heat styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair over time. Try to limit your use of curling irons, straighteners, and blow dryers as much as possible. When you do use them, be sure to apply a heat protectant first.

3. Get regular trims

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

Section 5: FAQ

1. Can I wear my hair in a ponytail if I have an oblong face shape?

Yes, you can wear your hair in a ponytail, but avoid pulling all of your hair back tightly. Instead, try a loose ponytail or a messy bun.

2. Can I wear a center part if I have an oblong face shape?

A center part can accentuate the length of an oblong face shape. Instead, try a side part or a zigzag part.

3. Are there any hairstyles I should avoid if I have an oblong face shape?

Avoid hairstyles that pull all of your hair back tightly, as this can accentuate the length of your face. Also, avoid hairstyles that add height to the crown of your head, such as a high ponytail or bouffant.

Now that you know how to style your oblong face shape, you can confidently create hairstyles that flatter your features. Remember to incorporate healthy hair care practices into your routine to keep your locks looking their best.
