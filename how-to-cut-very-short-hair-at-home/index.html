How To Cut Very Short Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every month just to maintain your super-short haircut? With the right tools and techniques, you can easily learn how to cut very short hair at home. Not only will this save you a lot of time and money, but it will also give you the freedom to experiment with different styles whenever you want. In this article, we'll give you all the tips and tricks you need to achieve a perfect DIY haircut.

Description

Very short haircuts are extremely popular among both men and women. They are low-maintenance, edgy, and versatile, making them a great choice for anyone who wants to make a bold statement. However, getting a short haircut at the salon can be expensive and time-consuming, especially if you have to do it every few weeks. That's why learning how to cut very short hair at home can be a game-changer.

How to Cut Very Short Hair at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own hair is to make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • A handheld mirror or a large mirror
  • A spray bottle with water
  • A clip or hair tie to hold sections of hair in place

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, as dull or dirty scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to wash and dry your hair. This will make it easier to see the natural texture and shape of your hair, and it will also remove any product buildup or dirt that could interfere with your cut. Once your hair is clean and dry, comb it straight down so that it's lying flat against your scalp.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair into four parts: front, back, and two sides. Clip or tie each section separately to keep them out of the way while you work.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair between your fingers and hold it straight up, then trim the ends with your scissors. Work your way around the back of your head, taking small sections and cutting the ends to your desired length.

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the sides. Hold a section of hair between your fingers and cut at an angle, following the natural curve of your head. This will create a gradual fade from the back to the sides.

Finally, move on to the front section. This is the trickiest part, as you want to make sure you get the length and shape just right. Start by taking a small section of hair between your fingers and cutting the ends straight across. Then, use your comb to lift the hair up and cut at an angle, creating a slight curve towards your face.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, take a look in the mirror to make sure everything looks even and balanced. Use your comb to check that the lengths are consistent, and make any final adjustments as needed.

Tips for Cutting Very Short Hair at Home

Cutting very short hair can be challenging, but with these tips, you can achieve a professional-looking cut:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more off as you go. It's easier to cut more hair off than it is to add it back on.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head as you work.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as wet hair looks longer and can result in a shorter cut than you intended.
  • Trim your hair every four to six weeks to maintain your style and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

No, it's important to use sharp, professional-grade hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair and creating uneven cuts.

How short should I cut my hair?

The length of your hair is a personal preference, but most very short haircuts are around 1/2 to 1 inch in length.

Can I cut my hair by myself?

Yes, with the right tools and techniques, you can definitely cut your own hair at home. However, if you're unsure or nervous about cutting your hair, it's always a good idea to consult a professional stylist.

Hair Care for Very Short Hair

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are some tips for caring for very short hair:

  • Wash your hair every other day to prevent it from drying out.
  • Use a lightweight, non-greasy styling product to add volume and texture.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently learn how to cut very short hair at home. Not only will you save money and time, but you'll also have the freedom to experiment with different styles and looks. Happy cutting!
