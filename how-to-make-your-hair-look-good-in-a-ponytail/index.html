How to:

Follow these steps to achieve a perfect ponytail:

Step 1: Start with clean and dry hair

Your hair should be clean and dry before you start styling it. If your hair is damp or wet, it can cause the elastic band to slip and your hair to fall out of the ponytail. Use a hair dryer to dry your hair if necessary.

Step 2: Brush your hair

Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to gather your hair into a smooth and neat ponytail.

Step 3: Choose your placement

Decide where you want to place your ponytail. A low ponytail at the nape of the neck can look chic and sophisticated, while a high ponytail on the crown of the head can give you a more youthful and playful look. A mid-level ponytail is a classic option that works well for most people.

Step 4: Gather your hair

Use your hands to gather your hair into a ponytail at the chosen placement. Make sure itâs smooth and free of bumps or lumps.

Step 5: Secure with an elastic band

Step 6: Tame flyaways

If you have any flyaways or baby hairs, use a small amount of hairspray or gel to tame them. You can also use a toothbrush or a clean mascara wand to brush them down.

Step 7: Add some volume

If you want to add some volume to your ponytail, gently pull it apart at the crown of your head. This will create a fuller and more voluminous look.

You can prevent your elastic band from slipping by wrapping a small section of hair around it to hide it, or by using bobby pins to secure it in place.
