How To Cut Thick Shoulder Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Description

Thick hair can be challenging to cut, particularly if you're dealing with shoulder-length hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can perform a professional-looking haircut on your own. This article will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut thick shoulder-length hair, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the desired outcome.

How to Cut Thick Shoulder Length Hair

If you want to cut your own thick shoulder-length hair, you'll need a few tools. These include:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Large mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut your thick shoulder-length hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it until damp. Comb out any tangles and part your hair in the middle.
  2. Section off the hair you're not working on by clipping it to the side.
  3. Take a small section of hair at the front and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and snip off the ends, holding the scissors at a slight angle.
  4. Continue working in small sections, moving towards the back of your head. Take your time and make sure each section is even.
  5. Once you've finished one side, repeat the process on the other side. Make sure to check in the mirror to ensure both sides are even.
  6. Next, trim the back of your hair. Divide your hair in half horizontally, and clip the top section to the top of your head. Take small sections of the bottom half and trim the ends, making sure to keep the hair straight.
  7. Release the top section of hair and repeat the process, working in small sections until you've trimmed all the hair in the back.
  8. Check your hair in the mirror and make any final adjustments. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them until you're happy with the result.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting thick shoulder-length hair:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a clean cut.
  • Take your time. Cutting hair is a precise process, and rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Keep the hair wet while cutting, as it's easier to manage and cut evenly.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair frequently, and make any necessary adjustments.
  • If you're not confident cutting your hair on your own, consider asking a friend or professional stylist for help.

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting thick shoulder-length hair:

  • How often should I cut my hair? It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Can I cut my hair when it's dry? It's best to cut your hair when it's damp, as it's easier to manage and cut evenly.
  • How do I know which length to cut my hair? It's best to start with a longer length and gradually trim it shorter until you achieve the desired length. You can always trim more if needed, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.

Hair Care after Cutting

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to keep your hair moisturized.

By following these tips, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home and keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
