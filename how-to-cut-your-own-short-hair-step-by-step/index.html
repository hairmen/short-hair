How To Cut Your Own Short Hair Step By Step A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Aug 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money and cut your own hair, you may be wondering how to cut your own short hair step by step. While it may seem intimidating at first, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to cut your own short hair step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Styling Your Hair
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few key tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Handheld mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can lead to uneven cuts and split ends.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll want to make sure it's clean and dry. This will give you a better idea of how your hair will look once it's cut.

Once your hair is dry, comb it straight down and part it as you normally would.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is important to ensure an even cut. Start by dividing your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section in place.

Starting with the back sections, comb your hair straight down and trim any split ends. If you want a shorter cut, use your clippers to trim the hair to the desired length.

Move on to the front sections, and comb your hair forward. Use your scissors to cut your hair at an angle, starting with the shortest pieces first. This will create a textured look that's popular with short haircuts.

Cutting Your Hair

Once you've sectioned your hair, it's time to start cutting. Work on one section at a time, and remember to cut small amounts at a time. You can always take more off, but you can't put it back on!

If you're using scissors, hold them at an angle and cut in a downward motion. This will create layers and texture. If you're using clippers, start at the bottom and work your way up, making sure to blend any harsh lines.

Styling Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to style it. Use a styling product of your choice, such as gel or mousse, to create your desired look. If you're not sure how to style your hair, try searching for tutorials online or experimenting until you find a look you like.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use sharp scissors
  • Work on one section at a time
  • Cut small amounts at a time
  • Use clippers to create shorter cuts
  • Blend harsh lines with clippers or scissors

FAQs

Q: Is it easy to cut your own short hair?

A: Cutting your own hair can be intimidating at first, but with practice and the right tools, it can be a relatively easy process.

Q: Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

A: While you can technically cut your hair with regular scissors, it's best to use sharp, professional hair-cutting scissors to ensure an even cut.

Q: How often should I cut my short hair?

A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain your short hairstyle.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair

Now that you know how to cut your own short hair step by step, you can save money and achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your own home. With practice and patience, you'll be able to perfect your technique and create a variety of short hairstyles.
