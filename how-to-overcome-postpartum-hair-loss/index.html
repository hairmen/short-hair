How To Overcome Postpartum Hair Loss

Written by Luffy Jul 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Postpartum hair loss can be a frustrating and stressful experience for many new mothers. After giving birth, it's not uncommon for women to experience hair thinning, shedding, and even bald spots. However, there are steps you can take to overcome postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. In this article, we'll go over some tips, how-tos, and FAQs to help you address postpartum hair loss.

How to Overcome Postpartum Hair Loss

There are several ways to address postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

1. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough iron, zinc, and biotin, as these nutrients are particularly important for hair health.

2. Manage stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it's important to take steps to manage stress levels. Try relaxation techniques like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.

3. Avoid tight hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns can pull on the hair and cause damage. Avoid these styles and opt for looser, more gentle hairstyles instead.

4. Be gentle with your hair

Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid using hot styling tools like hair straighteners or curling irons. Opt for a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles instead.

Step-by-Step Hair Care Routine

Here's a step-by-step hair care routine you can follow to promote healthy hair growth and address postpartum hair loss:

Step 1: Cleanse

Use a gentle shampoo to cleanse your hair and scalp, being careful not to scrub too hard or use water that's too hot.

Step 2: Condition

Use a conditioner to help detangle your hair and add moisture. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Apply a hair mask

Once a week, apply a hair mask to help nourish your hair and scalp. Look for a mask that contains ingredients like coconut oil, argan oil, or shea butter.

Step 4: Style gently

Avoid tight hairstyles and be gentle when styling your hair. Let your hair air-dry whenever possible, and avoid using hot styling tools.

Tips for Preventing and Treating Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips for preventing and treating postpartum hair loss:

1. Take a hair supplement

Consider taking a hair supplement that contains vitamins and minerals that are important for hair health, like biotin, iron, and zinc.

2. Massage your scalp

Massage your scalp gently to help improve circulation and promote healthy hair growth.

3. Use a volumizing shampoo

Use a volumizing shampoo to help add body and fullness to thinning hair.

4. Don't skip meals

Eating a balanced diet is important for healthy hair growth, so make sure you're not skipping meals or restricting your calorie intake.

FAQs about Postpartum Hair Loss

Q: When does postpartum hair loss occur?

A: Postpartum hair loss typically occurs around three to six months after giving birth.

Q: Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

A: No, postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and hair growth typically returns to normal within a few months.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, breastfeeding can contribute to postpartum hair loss due to hormonal changes in the body.

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

Here are some additional hair care tips for new moms:

1. Keep your hair tied back

Keeping your hair tied back can help prevent your baby from pulling on it, which can cause damage and hair loss.

2. Use a silk pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.

3. Wash your hair less often

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and contribute to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days instead.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can overcome postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient, as it may take some time for your hair to fully recover.
