Is It Safe To Perm Naturally Curly Hair

Description

Many people with naturally curly hair often wonder if it is safe to perm their hair. The truth is that perming hair, whether curly or straight, involves using chemicals that can damage hair if not done correctly. However, perming naturally curly hair can have benefits such as loosening curls or adding volume to hair. In this article, we will discuss how to safely perm naturally curly hair.

How to Perm Naturally Curly Hair

Before perming naturally curly hair, it is important to choose the right perm solution based on your hair type and texture. It is recommended to use a gentle perm solution specifically designed for curly hair. Make sure to read the instructions carefully and follow them step by step. It is also recommended to do a patch test to check for any allergic reactions.

Step by Step Process

1. Shampoo and condition hair the day before perming. Do not use any styling products.

2. Section hair into four parts and clip them.

3. Apply petroleum jelly around the hairline to prevent chemical burns.

4. Put on gloves and mix the perm solution as per instructions.

5. Apply the perm solution to each section of hair, starting from the bottom and working your way up.

6. Cover hair with a plastic cap and let it sit for the recommended time specified in the instructions.

7. Rinse hair with warm water for at least five minutes to remove all the perm solution.

8. Apply the neutralizer as per instructions and let it sit for the recommended time.

9. Rinse hair thoroughly with warm water.

10. Apply a deep conditioner and leave it on for 10-15 minutes.

11. Rinse hair with cool water and gently pat dry with a towel.

Tips for Perming Naturally Curly Hair

1. Don't perm your hair too often as it can make your hair dry and brittle.

2. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair to prevent breakage.

4. Avoid using hot styling tools such as curling irons or straighteners as they can damage permed hair.

5. Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.

FAQs

Q: Can I perm my hair if I have color-treated hair?

A: It is not recommended to perm color-treated hair as it can lead to breakage and damage.

Q: How long does a perm last?

A: A perm can last up to six months depending on the hair's texture and how well it's taken care of.

Hair Care for Permed Naturally Curly Hair

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.

2. Avoid using harsh chemicals such as hair dyes or bleach on permed hair.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

4. Avoid using a towel to dry your hair as it can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water.

5. Consider getting regular trims to remove split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Overall, perming naturally curly hair can be safe if done correctly and with the right products. Always follow instructions carefully and take care of your permed hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
