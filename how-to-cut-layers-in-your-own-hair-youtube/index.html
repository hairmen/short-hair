How To Cut Layers In Your Own Hair Youtube

Written by Christine Aug 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a scary experience, but with the help of YouTube, it can be done! Layers are a great way to add dimension and depth to your hair, and it's a popular style that many people want to try at home. In this article, we'll go over the steps to cut layers in your own hair using YouTube as a guide. We'll also provide tips and FAQs to help you achieve the best results.

How to

1. Find a YouTube tutorial that fits your hair type and desired style. Search for "how to cut layers in your own hair" and include your hair type, such as "curly," "straight," or "thick." 2. Gather the necessary tools, including hair cutting scissors, sectioning clips, and a comb. 3. Start with clean, dry hair. It's important to cut hair when it's dry because wet hair can shrink when it dries, resulting in uneven layers. 4. Section your hair. Divide your hair into sections using the clips. Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Pin up the top section of hair so it's out of the way. 5. Start with the bottom layer. Take a section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle. This will create a gradual layer. 6. Move up to the next section. Take the next section of hair and use the bottom layer as a guide. Cut the hair at the same angle to create a gradual layer that blends with the bottom layer. 7. Continue layering the back sections of hair. Use the same technique to cut the hair at an angle and blend the layers together. 8. Move on to the front sections of hair. Unclip the top section and use the bottom layers as a guide to create layers that frame your face. 9. Use a mirror to check the layers. Make sure the layers are even and blend well together. If necessary, go back and trim any uneven sections. 10. Style your hair as desired. Once you're happy with the layers, style your hair as you normally would.

Use a mirror to check the layers. 10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once. - Use sharp, professional-grade hair cutting scissors for the best results. - Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and stressful. - If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, practice on a wig or mannequin head first.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut layers in my own hair if it's curly? A: Yes, you can! Just make sure to find a YouTube tutorial that specifically addresses cutting curly hair. Q: Do I need to wet my hair before cutting it? A: No, it's best to cut hair when it's dry. Wet hair can shrink when it dries, resulting in uneven layers. Q: How often should I cut my own hair? A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your layers. It's generally recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain healthy, shiny locks. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage. Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.
