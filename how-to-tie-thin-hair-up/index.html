How To Tie Thin Hair Up A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your thin hair getting in your face and becoming a nuisance? Tying your hair up is a great solution, but it can be difficult to do with thin hair. Don't worry – we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll show you how to tie thin hair up in a way that's easy and stylish. Keep reading for our step-by-step guide, tips, and hair care advice.

How to Tie Thin Hair Up

Before we get started, it's important to note that tying your hair up too tightly can cause breakage and damage. Be gentle when pulling your hair back and avoid using tight elastics or hair ties.

Step 1: Brush your Hair

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to tie your hair up and will prevent breakage.

Step 2: Choose Your Style

There are many different ways to tie your hair up, even if you have thin hair. Here are a few popular styles:

  • Ponytail: Gather your hair into a high or low ponytail and secure it with an elastic. For a more polished look, wrap a small piece of hair around the elastic and secure it with a bobby pin.
  • Bun: Gather your hair into a high or low bun and secure it with an elastic. For a more voluminous bun, use a hair doughnut or sock bun to create the shape.
  • Braid: Create a simple or fishtail braid and secure it with an elastic. For a more bohemian look, pull on the sides of the braid to make it looser and messier.

Step 3: Tie Your Hair

Once you've chosen your style, it's time to tie your hair up. Use a gentle touch and avoid pulling too tightly. Here's how:

  1. Gather your hair into the desired style.
  2. Use an elastic to secure your hair in place. Avoid using elastics with metal clasps, as they can cause breakage.
  3. If desired, wrap a small piece of hair around the elastic and secure it with a bobby pin.

Tips for Tying Thin Hair Up

Here are some tips to help you tie your thin hair up without causing damage:

  • Avoid using tight elastics or hair ties. Instead, opt for gentle elastics or scrunchies.
  • Use a hair doughnut or sock bun to add volume to your bun.
  • Don't tie your hair up in the same spot every day. This can cause breakage and damage.
  • Try using a claw clip instead of an elastic for a gentler hold.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about tying thin hair up:

Can I tie my thin hair up every day?

It's best to avoid tying your hair up in the same spot every day, as this can cause breakage and damage. Instead, switch up your hairstyle and use gentle elastics or scrunchies.

What's the best way to add volume to a bun?

Using a hair doughnut or sock bun can add volume to your bun. Simply gather your hair into a ponytail, place the doughnut or bun over the ponytail, and wrap your hair around it. Secure with an elastic and bobby pins.

What's the best way to tie thin hair up without causing damage?

Avoid using tight elastics or hair ties, and be gentle when pulling your hair back. Use a claw clip or gentle elastic, and switch up your hairstyle to avoid causing breakage and damage.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Here are some tips to keep your thin hair healthy and strong:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for thin hair.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as they can cause damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to avoid tangles and breakage.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back too tightly or using tight elastics or hair ties.
  • Eat a balanced diet and take supplements if necessary to promote healthy hair growth.

Now that you know how to tie thin hair up, you can say goodbye to pesky flyaways and hello to stylish updos. Remember to be gentle when pulling your hair back, and switch up your hairstyle to avoid causing damage. Happy styling!
