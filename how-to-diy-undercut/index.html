How To Diy Undercut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a bold and edgy haircut, an undercut might be just what you need. An undercut is a hairstyle where the hair on the sides and back of the head is shaved or cut very short, while the hair on top is left longer. In this guide, we'll show you how to DIY undercut in relaxed English language. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced DIYer, you'll find everything you need to know to create a great undercut at home.

Description

The undercut is a versatile hairstyle that can be customized to suit your preferences. It's a great way to add some edge to your look, and it's also a practical option if you're looking for a low-maintenance haircut. Undercuts can be done on any hair length, texture, and color, making it a perfect fit for everyone.

How to

Here's how to DIY undercut:

Step 1: Choose your style

Before you start cutting, decide on the type of undercut you want. There are different styles to choose from, such as a disconnected undercut, a fade undercut, or a textured undercut. You can browse online for inspiration or consult with a stylist for advice.

Step 2: Gather your tools

You'll need a few tools to get started, including a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Make sure your tools are sharp and clean before using them.

Step 3: Section your hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use a comb to create a parting from the top of your head to the nape of your neck. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way so that you can work on the undercut.

Step 4: Cut the sides and back

Using your clippers, start cutting the hair on the sides and back of your head. Use a guard attachment to adjust the length of the hair you're cutting. Work your way from the bottom of your head to the top, making sure to blend the hair as you go.

Step 5: Blend the hair

Once you've cut the hair on the sides and back, use scissors to blend the hair on top with the undercut. This will create a more natural and seamless look.

Step 6: Style your hair

Once you've finished cutting, style your hair as desired. You can use a hair product like wax or pomade to add texture and hold to your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when DIY undercutting:

- Start with a longer guard attachment and work your way down to a shorter length to avoid cutting too much hair at once.

- Use a mirror to check the back of your head to make sure you're cutting evenly.

- If you're unsure about cutting, start with a small section and work your way up.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about DIY undercutting:

Can I do an undercut on curly hair?

Yes, you can do an undercut on curly hair. Just make sure to cut the hair when it's dry and use a guard attachment that's appropriate for your hair length.

How often do I need to cut my undercut?

The frequency of your undercut maintenance depends on how fast your hair grows. Generally, you can wait for two to three weeks before getting it trimmed.

Can I do an undercut on my own?

Yes, you can do an undercut on your own. However, it's recommended to have someone help you with the back of your head to ensure an even cut.

Hair Care

Maintaining an undercut is easy. Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too frequently as they can damage your hair. Also, make sure to get your hair trimmed regularly to maintain your undercut's shape.

Now that you know how to DIY undercut, you're ready to rock a cool and edgy hairstyle. Remember to take your time and start with a longer length to avoid any mishaps. Happy undercutting!
