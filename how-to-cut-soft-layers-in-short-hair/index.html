How To Cut Soft Layers In Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read
If you're looking to add some dimension and texture to your short hair, cutting soft layers can be a great option. Not only does it add movement to your hair, but it can also make it look thicker and fuller. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut soft layers in short hair, including tips and frequently asked questions.

Description

Cutting soft layers in short hair involves creating layers that blend seamlessly into one another, rather than harsh, choppy layers. This technique works best on hair that is already shoulder-length or shorter. It can help add volume and texture to thin or fine hair, and make thick hair more manageable.

How to

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a mirror in front of you and behind you, so you can see the back of your head. Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it is completely dry before you start cutting. Step 2: Divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to secure the top section of your hair out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose. Step 3: Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut at a slight angle. This will create a soft, subtle layer. Step 4: Continue to work your way up the back of your head, taking small sections of hair and cutting them at a slight angle. Make sure to blend each layer into the previous one, so there are no harsh lines. Step 5: Once you have finished the back, move on to the sides. Take small sections of hair and cut at a slight angle, working your way towards the front. Step 6: When you reach the front, take a small section of hair from each side and cut at a slight angle towards the back. This will create face-framing layers. Step 7: Once you have finished cutting, use a mirror to check the back and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair to make sure you are cutting evenly. - Use a comb to keep your sections neat and tidy. - Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven layers. - Take your time and work slowly, making sure each layer blends into the previous one.

FAQ

Q: Will cutting soft layers in short hair make it look thinner? A: No, cutting soft layers can actually make your hair look thicker and fuller. Q: Can I cut soft layers in my own hair? A: It is possible to cut soft layers in your own hair, but it can be difficult to do it evenly. It's best to have a friend or professional stylist help you. Q: How often should I cut my hair to maintain soft layers? A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to maintain your layers. Generally, every 6-8 weeks is a good timeframe for a trim.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking healthy and fresh, it's important to take good care of it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. If you do use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray. Regular trims can also help keep your hair looking neat and tidy. Cutting soft layers in short hair can be a great way to add dimension and texture. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look that works for you. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and don't cut too much hair at once.
