How To Fix My Lace Front Wig A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 07, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Lace front wigs have become increasingly popular in recent years due to their natural-looking appearance and versatility. However, sometimes these wigs can become disheveled or damaged over time, leaving you wondering how to fix them. In this post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix your lace front wig.

Description

Lace front wigs are a type of wig that has a sheer lace panel along the front hairline. This panel can be trimmed to fit your natural hairline and creates a natural-looking hairline. Lace front wigs can be made from synthetic or human hair and come in a variety of styles and colors. However, just like natural hair, lace front wigs can become tangled, frizzy, and damaged over time.

How to Fix My Lace Front Wig

Step 1: Detangle Your Wig

The first step to fixing your lace front wig is to detangle it. Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently comb through the hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Be gentle when detangling your wig to avoid damaging the hair fibers.

Step 2: Wash Your Wig

After detangling your wig, it's time to wash it. Fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of wig shampoo. Place your wig in the water and gently swish it around. Rinse your wig thoroughly with cool water and gently squeeze out the excess water.

Step 3: Condition Your Wig

Once your wig is clean, it's time to condition it. Apply a small amount of wig conditioner to the hair, avoiding the roots. Gently comb through the hair with a wide-tooth comb to distribute the conditioner evenly. Let the conditioner sit on the hair for a few minutes before rinsing it out with cool water.

Step 4: Dry Your Wig

After conditioning your wig, gently squeeze out the excess water and place it on a wig stand to air dry. Avoid using heat to dry your wig, as this can damage the hair fibers. Once your wig is completely dry, you can style it as desired.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when fixing your lace front wig:

  • Use a wig stand to air dry your wig
  • Avoid using heat to style your wig
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or in a wig box when not in use
  • Don't sleep in your wig, as this can damage the hair fibers

FAQ

How often should I wash my lace front wig?

You should wash your lace front wig every 6-8 wears, or as needed if it becomes dirty or tangled.

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig?

No, you should use a wig-specific shampoo and conditioner to avoid damaging the hair fibers.

Can I dye my lace front wig?

Yes, you can dye your lace front wig. However, it's important to use a wig-specific dye and to follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the quality and longevity of your lace front wig. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using heat to style your wig
  • Don't sleep in your wig
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or in a wig box when not in use
  • Wash and condition your wig regularly

By following these tips and steps, you can fix your lace front wig and keep it looking beautiful for years to come.
