Can You Start Dreads On Heat Damaged Hair

Written by Aprilia Nov 04, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're considering starting dreadlocks, you may be wondering if it's possible to do so on heat damaged hair. The answer is yes, but it's important to take certain factors into consideration before making the decision to start your dreadlocks.

Description

Heat damaged hair can be caused by excessive use of heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. This can lead to split ends, breakage, and overall weak and damaged hair. Starting dreadlocks on heat damaged hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible.

How To

The key to starting dreadlocks on heat damaged hair is to first restore your hair's health. This means cutting off any severely damaged ends and taking steps to prevent further damage. It's important to avoid heat styling tools and opt for protective styles such as braids or twists. Regular deep conditioning treatments can also help to restore moisture and strength to your hair.

Step by Step

1. Assess the damage: Before starting dreadlocks, assess the extent of the heat damage to your hair. If your hair is severely damaged, it may be best to wait until it's healthier before starting your dreadlocks.

2. Cut off damaged ends: If your hair is damaged, it's important to cut off any severely damaged ends before starting your dreadlocks. This will help to prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth.

3. Restore hair health: Take steps to restore your hair's health before starting your dreadlocks. This may include deep conditioning treatments, protective styling, and avoiding heat styling tools.

4. Choose a method: There are several methods for starting dreadlocks, including backcombing, twisting, and interlocking. Choose the method that works best for your hair type and texture.

5. Maintain your dreadlocks: Once you've started your dreadlocks, it's important to maintain them properly to prevent further damage. This may include regular washing, moisturizing, and re-twisting.

Tips

- Avoid heat styling tools: Heat styling tools can further damage your hair, so it's important to avoid them while starting your dreadlocks.

- Protect your hair: Opt for protective styles such as braids or twists to protect your hair while it's growing out.

- Use natural products: Natural products are gentler on your hair and can help to restore its health.

FAQ

Q: Can I start dreadlocks on relaxed hair?

A: Yes, it's possible to start dreadlocks on relaxed hair. However, it's important to take steps to restore your hair's health before starting your dreadlocks.

Q: How long does it take to start dreadlocks?

A: The time it takes to start dreadlocks depends on your hair type and the method you choose. It can take anywhere from a few hours to several months.

Hair Care

Starting dreadlocks on heat damaged hair can be challenging, but with proper care and attention, it's possible to achieve healthy and beautiful dreadlocks. Take steps to restore your hair's health before starting your dreadlocks, and maintain them properly to prevent further damage.
