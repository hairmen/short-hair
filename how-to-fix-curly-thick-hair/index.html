How To Fix Curly Thick Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 03, 2023 · 6 min read

Curly thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it’s full of volume and texture, but on the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. If you’re struggling with your curly thick hair, don’t worry – there are plenty of ways to fix it! In this article, we’ll go over some tips and tricks for taming your curly locks and achieving the perfect hairstyle.

  • How to Fix Curly Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing Curly Thick Hair
  • FAQs

How to Fix Curly Thick Hair

The first step in fixing curly thick hair is to understand your hair type. Curly hair tends to be dry and prone to frizz, so it’s important to use the right products and techniques to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips to help you get started:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair needs moisture to stay hydrated and healthy. Look for a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for curly hair, and that contain moisturizing ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, or argan oil. Avoid shampoos with sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and leave it feeling dry and brittle.

2. Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy. If you have curly hair, you should aim to wash it no more than two or three times a week. On the days you don’t wash your hair, you can use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and refresh your curls.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb or Your Fingers to Detangle Your Hair

Curls are more prone to tangling than straight hair, so it’s important to be gentle when you’re detangling your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently work out any knots or tangles, starting at the ends of your hair and working your way up.

4. Use a Leave-In Conditioner or Curl Cream

A leave-in conditioner or curl cream can help define your curls and keep them looking bouncy and shiny. Apply a small amount of product to your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends, and scrunch your hair to encourage your curls.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of managing curly thick hair, here’s a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect hairstyle:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Moisturized Hair

Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and let it air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls gently.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner or Curl Cream

Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Scrunch your hair to encourage your curls.

Step 3: Use a Diffuser Attachment on Your Hair Dryer

If you’re using a hair dryer, use a diffuser attachment to dry your curls gently without disturbing them too much. Hold the diffuser close to your hair, and use a low heat and low speed setting to avoid frizz.

Step 4: Finish with a Hair Oil or Serum

Once your hair is dry, apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to add shine and tame any frizz.

Tips for Managing Curly Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you manage your curly thick hair:

1. Sleep on a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and tangle your hair while you sleep, leading to frizz and breakage. Switch to a silk or satin pillowcase to help your hair retain its moisture and prevent tangles.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Curly hair is more prone to damage from heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands. If you must use these tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low to avoid damaging your hair.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can lead to breakage and frizz. Aim to get a trim every 8-12 weeks, or as needed.

FAQs

1. Can I Brush My Curly Hair?

It’s best to avoid brushing curly hair, as this can disrupt your curls and cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

2. How Do I Refresh My Curls on Days I Don’t Wash My Hair?

You can use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and refresh your curls on days you don’t wash your hair. You can also mist your hair with a water and conditioner mixture, and scrunch your curls to revive them.

3. How Do I Prevent Frizz in Humid Weather?

Humidity can cause curly hair to frizz, so it’s important to use a styling product with anti-humidity properties. You can also tie your hair up in a loose bun or braid to keep it contained in humid weather.

Hair Care for Curly Thick Hair

In addition to the tips and tricks we’ve covered in this article, here are some additional hair care tips for curly thick hair:

1. Deep Condition Your Hair Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy, and prevent breakage and split ends. Aim to do a deep conditioning treatment once a week or as needed.

2. Use a Heat Protectant Spray Before Heat Styling

If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage to your hair.

3. Avoid Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments like perms and relaxers can be damaging to curly hair, and can lead to breakage and dryness. If you want to change your hair texture, consider using heat styling tools instead.

By following these tips and tricks, you can learn how to fix your curly thick hair and achieve the perfect hairstyle. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and use the right products and techniques to keep it healthy and hydrated. With a little bit of patience and practice, you’ll be able to manage your curly locks like a pro!
