How To Get Healthy African American Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Nov 16, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're an African American with relaxed hair, you know how difficult it can be to maintain healthy hair. Relaxers, heating tools, and harsh chemicals can all take a toll on your hair, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. But don't worry – with the right care, you can get your hair back to a healthy, strong state. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieving healthy African American hair so you can feel confident and beautiful.

Step 1: Choose the Right Products

The first step to getting healthy African American hair is to choose the right products. Look for products that are specifically designed for relaxed hair, as they will be gentler and less likely to strip your hair of its natural oils. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals like sulfates, which can dry out your hair and scalp.

You'll also want to choose products that are moisturizing and nourishing. Look for ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil, which can help to hydrate and strengthen your hair. And don't forget to use a heat protectant spray if you plan on using heating tools, as this will help to prevent damage and breakage.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair Properly

When it comes to washing your relaxed hair, less is more. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week, and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

When washing your hair, be gentle. Don't scrub too hard, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use your fingertips to massage the shampoo into your scalp, and rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Step 3: Condition Your Hair Regularly

Conditioning is key to maintaining healthy African American hair. After shampooing, apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

For added moisture, you can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Apply the treatment to your hair, cover with a shower cap, and leave on for 20-30 minutes before rinsing.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair While Sleeping

When you sleep, your hair can become tangled and damaged. To protect your hair while you sleep, use a satin or silk pillowcase, or wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet. This will help to prevent breakage and keep your hair looking smooth and healthy.

Step 5: Reduce Heat Styling

Heat styling can be very damaging to relaxed hair. If possible, try to reduce the amount of heat styling you do, or use a lower heat setting. When you do use heating tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray, and avoid using them on wet hair.

Step 6: Trim Your Hair Regularly

To prevent split ends and breakage, it's important to trim your hair regularly. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, or whenever you notice split ends or breakage. This will help to keep your hair looking healthy and strong.

Step 7: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can be damaging to your hair, causing dryness and breakage. To protect your hair from the sun, wear a hat or scarf when you're outside, or use a hair product that contains UV protection.

Step 8: Stay Hydrated and Eat a Healthy Diet

Finally, it's important to stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote healthy hair. Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated, and eat a diet that's rich in fruits, vegetables, and protein. This will help to nourish your hair from the inside out.

Tips for Healthy African American Hair

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, as this can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using rubber bands or other hair accessories that can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can dry out your hair and scalp.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized throughout the day.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my relaxed hair?

A: Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week, using a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

Q: Can I use heating tools on my relaxed hair?

A: Yes, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray, and avoid using heating tools on wet hair.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, or whenever you notice split ends or breakage.

Q: What are some good ingredients to look for in hair products?

A: Look for ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil, which can help to hydrate and strengthen your hair.

By following these steps and tips, you can get your relaxed African American hair back to a healthy, strong state. Remember to be patient, as it may take time to see results, but with consistent care, you'll be on your way to beautiful, healthy hair.
