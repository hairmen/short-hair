How To Cut Layers On Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 07, 2023 · 6 min read
If you're looking for a way to add dimension and texture to your short hair, cutting layers is a great option. Layers can give your hair movement, volume, and a flattering shape. However, cutting layers on short hair can be tricky. In this post, we'll show you how to cut layers on short hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand way.

How to Cut Layers on Short Hair

Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Decide on the Length and Placement of Your Layers

The first step in cutting layers on short hair is to decide on the length and placement of your layers. Do you want long layers or short layers? Do you want your layers to start at your chin or higher up? Think about the overall shape you want to achieve and where you want your layers to fall.

Section Your Hair

To make it easier to cut your layers, you'll need to section your hair. Use your comb to create a part down the middle of your head, and then make a part from ear to ear. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way.

Cut Your Layers

Now it's time to start cutting your layers. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section of your hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut into the hair at a slight angle. This will create a soft, textured look. Repeat this process on all the sections of your hair, working your way up to the top section.

Blend Your Layers

Once you've cut all your layers, it's time to blend them together. Take a comb and comb through your hair, using a small scissor to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless, natural look.

Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut and blended your layers, it's time to style your hair. You can blow dry your hair with a round brush to add volume, or use a curling iron to create loose waves. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

Tips

Start with Small Sections

When cutting layers, it's important to start with small sections of hair. This will make it easier to control the length and shape of your layers.

Cut Your Layers When Your Hair is Dry

Cutting your hair when it's dry will give you a more accurate idea of how your layers will look. Wet hair can be deceiving, as it can stretch and shrink as it dries.

Use Point Cutting for a Softer Look

If you want a softer, more natural look, try using point cutting. This involves holding your scissors vertically and cutting into the hair at different angles.

Don't Cut Too Much

It's better to start with a small amount of hair and cut more if needed. Cutting too much at once can result in an uneven or unflattering look.

Take Your Time

Cutting layers on short hair can be time-consuming, but it's important to take your time and be patient. Rushing can result in mistakes and an unsatisfying end result.

FAQ

Can You Cut Layers on Very Short Hair?

Yes, you can cut layers on very short hair. However, the layers will have to be shorter and closer together to achieve the desired effect.

How Do You Decide on the Length of Your Layers?

Deciding on the length of your layers depends on your personal preference and the shape you want to achieve. Generally, shorter layers will add more volume and movement, while longer layers will create a softer, more flowing look.

Will Cutting Layers Damage Your Hair?

Cutting layers should not damage your hair as long as you use sharp scissors and take care not to cut too much. However, frequent cutting or cutting too much at once can cause damage.

How Often Should You Cut Layers on Short Hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers and keep your hair healthy.

Can You Cut Layers on Curly or Wavy Hair?

Yes, you can cut layers on curly or wavy hair. However, it's important to take the texture of your hair into consideration when deciding on the length and placement of your layers.

Hair Care

Use a Good Shampoo and Conditioner

To keep your hair healthy and strong, it's important to use a good quality shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are designed for your hair type and contain nourishing ingredients like vitamins and oils.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to avoid using heat tools like curling irons and straighteners as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray before styling.

Get Regular Trims

To maintain your layers and keep your hair looking fresh, it's important to get regular trims every 6-8 weeks. This will also help prevent split ends and breakage.

Use Hair Masks and Treatments

To keep your hair nourished and moisturized, use a hair mask or treatment once a week. Look for products that contain ingredients like coconut oil, argan oil, or shea butter.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. To protect your hair, wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray when you're outside for long periods of time. In conclusion, cutting layers on short hair can be a great way to add dimension and texture to your hair. By following these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a flattering and stylish look. Remember to take care of your hair with regular trims and nourishing treatments, and protect it from damage. Happy cutting!
