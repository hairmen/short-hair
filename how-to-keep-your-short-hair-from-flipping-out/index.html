How To Keep Your Short Hair From Flipping Out

Written by Bardi Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair, you know how frustrating it can be when your hair starts to flip out. Fortunately, there are several things you can do to prevent this from happening. In this post, we will discuss how to keep your short hair from flipping out, including tips, step-by-step instructions, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide to Keeping Short Hair from Flipping Out

How to Prevent Short Hair from Flipping Out

Short hair is prone to flipping out, especially if you have thick or curly hair. The key to preventing this is to keep your hair well-maintained and styled properly. Here are a few tips:

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

Using the right products can make a big difference in preventing your short hair from flipping out. Choose products that are designed for your hair type, such as a lightweight mousse or gel. These products will help keep your hair in place without weighing it down.

Tip 2: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help prevent it from flipping out by setting it in place. Use a round brush to smooth your hair as you blow dry it, and be sure to direct the airflow downward to keep your hair from flying up.

Tip 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent your hair from flipping out by keeping it in shape. Short hair grows quickly, so it's important to keep up with regular trims every 4-6 weeks.

Step-by-Step Guide to Keeping Short Hair from Flipping Out

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your short hair from flipping out:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type. Rinse thoroughly and gently towel dry your hair.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair, focusing on the roots and ends. Use a lightweight mousse or gel that's designed for your hair type.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair in small sections. Direct the airflow downward to keep your hair from flipping out.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

Finish by spraying your hair with a lightweight hairspray to hold it in place.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair

Here are a few tips for maintaining your short hair and preventing it from flipping out:

Tip 1: Don't Overwash Your Hair

Overwashing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and prone to flipping out. Try washing your hair every 2-3 days instead of every day.

Tip 2: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent your short hair from flipping out overnight. Silk is gentle on your hair and won't cause friction like cotton pillowcases do.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Damage

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it more prone to flipping out. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools and always use a heat protectant spray.

FAQ

Q: Can I prevent my short hair from flipping out without using styling products?

A: While using styling products can help keep your hair in place, you can also prevent your short hair from flipping out by blow drying it properly and sleeping on a silk pillowcase.

Q: How often should I trim my short hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your short hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it in shape and prevent it from flipping out.

Hair Care Advice

In addition to the tips and steps outlined above, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent it from flipping out. Here are a few extra tips:

Tip 1: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Tip 2: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Sun exposure can damage your hair and make it more prone to flipping out. Protect your hair by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Tip 3: Be Gentle When Brushing Your Hair

Brushing your hair too vigorously can cause it to flip out. Use a wide-tooth comb or a soft-bristled brush to gently detangle your hair.

By following these tips and steps, you can keep your short hair from flipping out and maintain a stylish, polished look.
