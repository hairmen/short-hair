How To Get Back Original Hair After Straightening

Written by Frank Jul 20, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Straightening your hair can be a great way to switch up your look, but if you miss your natural curls or waves, you may be wondering how to get your original hair back. Fortunately, there are several steps you can take to restore your hair's natural texture and promote healthy growth. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know to get back your original hair after straightening.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Back Original Hair After Straightening
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Restoring Original Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

How to Get Back Original Hair After Straightening

If you want to get your original hair back after straightening, you need to take a few steps to restore your hair's natural texture and promote healthy growth.

The first step is to stop using heat on your hair. Heat styling tools like flat irons and blow dryers can damage your hair and cause it to lose its natural texture. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to create natural-looking waves or curls.

You should also avoid chemical treatments like perms or relaxers, which can damage your hair and alter its texture. If you want to color your hair, choose a gentle, non-damaging hair dye that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

Another important step is to nourish your hair from the inside out. Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth and restore your hair's natural texture. Foods like salmon, eggs, spinach, and avocados are all great for promoting healthy hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you get your original hair back after straightening:

  1. Stop using heat on your hair
  2. Avoid chemical treatments like perms or relaxers
  3. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  4. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  5. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  6. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  7. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  8. Get regular trims to remove split ends and promote healthy growth
  9. Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment to nourish your hair
  10. Be patient, as it may take several months for your natural texture to fully return

Tips for Restoring Original Hair

Here are some additional tips to help restore your hair's natural texture:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent hair breakage
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as chlorine or hair spray
  • Consider taking a hair supplement to promote healthy growth and thickness
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Try using a hair serum or leave-in conditioner to add moisture and shine to your hair

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting back your original hair after straightening:

How long does it take for my natural texture to return?

It can take several months for your natural texture to fully return, depending on how often you straightened your hair and how damaged it is. Be patient and continue to take good care of your hair to promote healthy growth.

Can I still color my hair?

Yes, you can still color your hair if you want to, but choose a gentle, non-damaging hair dye that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

What should I do if my hair is still straight?

If your hair is still straight, try using a curl-enhancing product or getting a perm to help restore your natural texture. Be sure to choose a gentle, sulfate-free product that won't damage your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair healthy and promoting healthy growth:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends and promote healthy growth
  • Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment to nourish your hair

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can restore your natural texture and promote healthy growth.
