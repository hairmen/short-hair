How To Do A Disconnected Undercut At Home

Written by Eveline Nov 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and edgy hairstyle, a disconnected undercut might be the perfect choice for you. This style features short sides and a longer top that is disconnected from the sides. The best part? You can do it at home! In this blog post, we'll guide you through the steps to achieve a disconnected undercut look from the comfort of your own home.

Description

A disconnected undercut is a haircut that features short sides and a longer top that is disconnected from the sides. This style is trendy and edgy, and it can be customized to suit your personal style. The beauty of a disconnected undercut is that it can be styled in many different ways, from a slicked-back look to messy, textured hair.

How to

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving a disconnected undercut at home:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a few tools to achieve a disconnected undercut at home. You'll need a pair of hair clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you begin.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Decide on the length you want for the sides and the top of your hair. The sides should be shorter than the top, but the length can vary depending on your personal preference. Use the hair clippers to trim the sides to your desired length.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Using scissors, cut the top of your hair to the desired length. The top should be longer than the sides, and it should be disconnected from the sides. Use the comb to create a clean part between the top and the sides.

Step 4: Blend the Top and the Sides

To blend the top and the sides, use the scissors to create layers on the top of your hair. This will create a seamless transition from the top to the sides. Use the comb to blend the hair together.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've achieved the disconnected undercut look, it's time to style your hair. You can use gel or pomade to create a sleek, slicked-back look. Alternatively, you can use a texturizing spray or wax to create a messy, textured look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when doing a disconnected undercut at home:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually trim the hair to achieve your desired look.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you're cutting.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.

FAQ

Can I do a disconnected undercut on myself?

Yes, you can do a disconnected undercut on yourself. However, it may be easier to ask a friend or family member to help with the back of your head.

What products should I use to style my disconnected undercut?

You can use gel, pomade, texturizing spray, or wax to style your disconnected undercut. Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type.

How often should I trim my disconnected undercut?

You should trim your disconnected undercut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

Hair Care

To keep your disconnected undercut looking fresh, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. You can also use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

With these tips and steps, you can achieve a trendy and edgy disconnected undercut look from the comfort of your own home. Experiment with different styles and products to find the perfect look for you.
