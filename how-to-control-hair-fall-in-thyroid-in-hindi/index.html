How To Control Hair Fall In Thyroid In Hindi A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Thyroid is a common health condition that affects millions of people worldwide. One of the most common symptoms of thyroid is hair fall. Hair fall can be a distressing symptom that can affect your confidence and self-esteem. However, the good news is that there are ways to control hair fall in thyroid. In this article, we will discuss how to control hair fall in thyroid in Hindi.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Control Hair Fall in Thyroid
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Control Hair Fall in Thyroid
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thyroid Patients

Description

Thyroid is a condition in which the thyroid gland does not produce enough hormones. This can lead to a variety of symptoms, including hair fall, weight gain, fatigue, and more. Hair fall in thyroid is a common symptom that can be caused by a variety of factors, including hormonal imbalances, stress, and nutritional deficiencies.

How to Control Hair Fall in Thyroid

The first step in controlling hair fall in thyroid is to get your thyroid levels checked. If your thyroid levels are not within the normal range, your doctor may prescribe medication to help regulate your thyroid hormone levels. In addition to medication, there are several lifestyle changes you can make to control hair fall in thyroid.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to control hair fall in thyroid:

  1. Get your thyroid levels checked by a doctor.
  2. Take medication to regulate your thyroid hormone levels as prescribed by your doctor.
  3. Eat a healthy and balanced diet that includes foods rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth, such as iron, zinc, and biotin.
  4. Manage stress levels through meditation, yoga, or other relaxation techniques.
  5. Avoid harsh chemicals and hair treatments that can damage your hair.
  6. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  7. Massage your scalp regularly to increase blood flow to the hair follicles.
  8. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause damage.
  9. Get enough sleep to promote overall health and reduce stress.
  10. Consult a dermatologist or hair specialist if your hair fall persists despite making lifestyle changes.

Tips to Control Hair Fall in Thyroid

Here are some additional tips to control hair fall in thyroid:

  • Avoid using hot tools like hair straighteners and curling irons that can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Take supplements that promote hair growth, such as biotin and omega-3 fatty acids.
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption as they can damage your hair and affect your overall health.

FAQs

Q. Can thyroid cause permanent hair loss?

A. In some cases, thyroid can cause permanent hair loss. However, with proper treatment and lifestyle changes, you can control hair fall and promote hair growth.

Q. Can thyroid medication cause hair loss?

A. It is possible for some thyroid medications to cause hair loss as a side effect. However, this is not a common side effect and can usually be managed with proper medication adjustments.

Q. How long does it take for hair to grow back after thyroid treatment?

A. It can take several months for hair to grow back after thyroid treatment. However, with proper treatment and lifestyle changes, you can promote hair growth and control hair fall.

Hair Care for Thyroid Patients

Here are some additional hair care tips for thyroid patients:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and hair treatments that can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when wet, as wet hair is more prone to damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to keep your hair moisturized.

Controlling hair fall in thyroid requires a combination of medication and lifestyle changes. By following the tips and guidelines mentioned in this article, you can promote hair growth and control hair fall. If your hair fall persists despite making lifestyle changes, consult a dermatologist or hair specialist for further guidance.
