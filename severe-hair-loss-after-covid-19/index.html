Severe Hair Loss After Covid 19 Causes Symptoms And How To Treat It

Written by Luffy Jul 07, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are experiencing severe hair loss after COVID-19, you are not alone. Many people who have recovered from the virus have reported hair loss as one of the long-term effects. This type of hair loss is called telogen effluvium, which is a temporary condition that can last up to six months. In this article, we will discuss the causes, symptoms, and how to treat this condition.

Causes of Severe Hair Loss After COVID-19

The exact cause of telogen effluvium after COVID-19 is not yet clear, but it is believed to be related to the stress that the body experiences during the infection. When the body is under stress, it can push hair follicles into the resting phase of the hair growth cycle, which results in hair loss.

Additionally, some COVID-19 patients may experience nutritional deficiencies during the illness or after recovery, which can also contribute to hair loss.

Symptoms of Severe Hair Loss After COVID-19

Telogen effluvium after COVID-19 is characterized by a sudden and excessive shedding of hair from all over the scalp. You may notice more hair than usual on your pillow, in the shower, or on your clothes.

In some cases, the hair loss may be accompanied by itching, burning, or pain on the scalp.

How to Treat Severe Hair Loss After COVID-19

The good news is that telogen effluvium after COVID-19 is a temporary condition, and most people will see their hair grow back within a few months. However, there are some things you can do to help speed up the recovery process.

First, make sure you are eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals. You may also want to take a multivitamin or hair supplement to ensure you are getting all the nutrients your body needs.

Secondly, avoid any harsh hair treatments or styling products that can damage your hair or scalp. Stick to gentle shampoos and conditioners, and avoid using heat styling tools like hair dryers or flat irons.

Thirdly, consider using a hair growth serum or treatment that contains ingredients like minoxidil or biotin. These can help stimulate hair growth and improve the overall health of your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Severe Hair Loss After COVID-19

In addition to the treatments mentioned above, there are some things you can do to take care of your hair during the recovery period.

First, avoid wearing tight hairstyles like braids or ponytails, as these can put stress on your hair follicles and make the shedding worse.

Secondly, be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-toothed comb to avoid tugging or pulling.

Finally, try to reduce your stress levels as much as possible. Take time for self-care activities like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.

FAQs About Severe Hair Loss After COVID-19

Q: Is severe hair loss after COVID-19 permanent?

A: No, it is usually a temporary condition that will resolve on its own within a few months.

Q: Can hair supplements help with hair loss after COVID-19?

A: Yes, some hair supplements can help provide the nutrients your body needs for healthy hair growth.

Q: Should I see a doctor if I am experiencing severe hair loss after COVID-19?

A: It is always a good idea to consult a doctor if you are experiencing any unusual symptoms, including hair loss. They can help rule out any underlying medical conditions and recommend appropriate treatments.

Severe hair loss after COVID-19 can be a stressful and frustrating experience, but it is important to remember that it is a temporary condition. By taking care of your hair and overall health, you can help speed up the recovery process and get back to feeling like yourself again.
