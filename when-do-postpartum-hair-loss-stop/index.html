When Do Postpartum Hair Loss Stop

Written by Bardi Jun 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a new mom, you may be wondering when postpartum hair loss will stop. This is a common concern among women who have recently given birth. Postpartum hair loss is the shedding of hair that can occur after giving birth. It usually happens between three and six months after delivery.

Description:

Postpartum hair loss is a natural process that occurs after giving birth. During pregnancy, the hormone levels in your body change, which can cause your hair to become thicker and fuller. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels return to normal, which can cause your hair to shed. This shedding is temporary and usually stops within six to twelve months after delivery.

How to:

There are several things you can do to help reduce postpartum hair loss. These include:

1. Eat a balanced diet:

Eating a well-balanced diet can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins.

2. Avoid heat styling:

Avoid using hot tools such as hair dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. These can damage your hair and make postpartum hair loss worse.

3. Be gentle with your hair:

Avoid pulling your hair back tightly or using harsh chemicals on your hair. Instead, use gentle hair products and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.

4. Take care of your scalp:

A healthy scalp can help promote healthy hair growth. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing your hair.

Step by Step:

Here are some steps you can take to help reduce postpartum hair loss:

1. Consult with your doctor:

If you're concerned about postpartum hair loss, talk to your doctor. They can help determine if there's an underlying medical issue that may be causing your hair loss.

2. Be patient:

Postpartum hair loss is temporary, and your hair will eventually stop shedding. Be patient and give your hair time to recover.

3. Try hair growth supplements:

There are several hair growth supplements available that can help promote healthy hair growth. Talk to your doctor before taking any supplements.

4. Consider a new hairstyle:

If you're experiencing postpartum hair loss, consider a new hairstyle. A shorter haircut can help make your hair look fuller and thicker.

Tips:

Here are some additional tips to help reduce postpartum hair loss:

1. Massage your scalp:

Massaging your scalp can help promote healthy hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb:

Avoid using a brush on your hair, as this can cause breakage and make postpartum hair loss worse. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

3. Avoid tight hairstyles:

Avoid pulling your hair back tightly into a ponytail, bun, or braids. This can cause stress on your hair and make postpartum hair loss worse.

4. Get enough sleep:

Getting enough sleep can help reduce stress on your body, which can help reduce postpartum hair loss.

FAQ:

Q: When does postpartum hair loss start?

A: Postpartum hair loss typically starts between three and six months after delivery.

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss usually lasts between six and twelve months after delivery.

Q: Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

A: No, postpartum hair loss is temporary and your hair will eventually stop shedding.

Hair Care:

Taking care of your hair during postpartum hair loss is important. Here are some tips to help keep your hair healthy:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner:

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to help keep your hair healthy and reduce breakage.

2. Avoid heat styling:

Avoid using hot tools such as hair dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. These can damage your hair and make postpartum hair loss worse.

3. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair:

Avoid pulling your hair back tightly or using harsh chemicals on your hair. Instead, use gentle hair products and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.

4. Eat a healthy diet:

Eating a well-balanced diet can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins.

In conclusion, postpartum hair loss is a natural process that occurs after giving birth. It usually starts between three and six months after delivery and lasts between six and twelve months. There are several things you can do to help reduce postpartum hair loss, including eating a balanced diet, avoiding heat styling, being gentle with your hair, and taking care of your scalp. Remember to be patient and give your hair time to recover.
